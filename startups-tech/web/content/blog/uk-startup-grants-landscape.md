<p>Startup grant funding is one of the most searched topics in early-stage finance, and also one of the most misunderstood. This guide maps the landscape at a category level: what grants are, how they compare to equity and debt, and, crucially, how they sit alongside the two tax reliefs (R&D and SEIS/EIS) that are the real planning levers for UK tech founders.</p>

<p>It is not a scheme directory. Specific grant schemes change their terms, deadlines and award sizes constantly. A static list goes stale within months. The aim here is the framework, not the list.</p>

<h2>What startup grants are and where they fit</h2>

<p>Grants are non-dilutive, generally non-repayable funding awarded for specific activities such as R&D, product development or market expansion. They do not take equity and they do not need repaying (subject to meeting the grant conditions). They sit alongside, not instead of, SEIS/EIS equity investment and R&D tax relief. The key planning question for any grant-funded founder is not whether to apply for a grant but how that grant interacts with the other reliefs your company can claim.</p>

<h2>The main categories of UK startup grant</h2>

<p>Grant funding for UK startups clusters into three broad categories. Specific schemes, award amounts and eligibility windows change frequently, so the category map below is the stable part; check live portals for current opportunities.</p>

<h3>Innovation and R&amp;D grants</h3>

<p>These fund projects that advance science or technology. Innovate UK, the UK government's innovation agency, is the largest single source. It funds R&amp;D across sectors through competitions run on <a href="https://apply-for-innovation-funding.service.gov.uk/">the Innovate UK apply portal</a>. The Horizon Europe programme (which the UK re-associated with in late 2023) gives UK companies access to EU-level research funding. Eligibility, award sizes and consortium requirements vary by competition and should be verified directly.</p>

<h3>Regional and growth grants</h3>

<p>Local enterprise partnerships, combined authorities and devolved administrations (including the Welsh Government and Scottish Enterprise) operate their own grant programmes, often aimed at job creation, capital investment or export growth. The UK Government's <a href="https://www.find-government-grants.service.gov.uk/">Find a Grant</a> portal is the primary aggregator. Eligibility is typically location-specific and tied to headcount or capital spend commitments.</p>

<h3>Sector-specific grants</h3>

<p>Several industries have dedicated grant streams, including life sciences (through UKRI's MRC and NIHR), clean energy, advanced manufacturing and creative industries. These often involve matched funding or phased milestones. The relevant sector body or UKRI sub-council administers them.</p>

<h2>Non-dilutive vs dilutive: why founders care</h2>

<p>Every equity round gives away a share of your company. At pre-seed and seed stage, where SEIS and EIS are available, the tax relief to investors is real and significant, but you are still diluting. A grant, awarded for the same R&amp;D or product activity, lets you fund that work without giving up any equity. The trade-off is that grants are competitive, activity-constrained and slower to land than a private equity round. Most funded tech startups end up using both: grant funding for specific project costs, equity for growth capital and working capital. The decision is not either/or.</p>

<h2>The important bit: how a grant interacts with your R&amp;D tax relief</h2>

<p>Under the merged R&amp;D scheme, for accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024, <a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-rd-relief">the subsidised-expenditure restriction was removed entirely</a>. Grant funding, subsidies and customer contributions no longer reduce the qualifying R&amp;D expenditure you can claim on that spend. A grant-funded R&amp;D project can be claimed in full under the merged scheme, subject to not counting the same cost twice.</p>

<p>This is a meaningful change from the pre-April 2024 SME rules, where a grant often pushed spend onto the old RDEC route and gave a smaller benefit. A heavily grant-funded, loss-making, R&amp;D-intensive SME that the old rules would have kept off the SME route can now potentially reach <a href="/blog/research-and-development/eris-rd-intensive-30-percent">ERIS</a>.</p>

<p>For the full mechanics, including how the merged scheme handles subsidised expenditure and what ERIS means for grant-funded companies, see our dedicated post: <a href="/blog/research-and-development/startup-grants-and-rd-interaction">Startup Grants and R&amp;D Tax Relief: How They Interact</a>.</p>

<h2>Grants and SEIS/EIS together</h2>

<p>A grant does not use up your SEIS or EIS allowances. The two are separate instruments governed by different rules. A company can hold an Innovate UK grant and run a SEIS round at the same time; there is no overlap in the caps.</p>

<p>The SEIS limit is up to £250,000 in total from investors, with company gross assets of no more than £350,000 and fewer than 25 full-time-equivalent employees at the time of investment (<a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-to-use-the-seed-enterprise-investment-scheme">gov.uk: SEIS rules</a>). EIS raises can reach £5m per year and £12m over the company's lifetime (<a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-the-enterprise-investment-scheme">gov.uk: EIS limits</a>). Neither cap is affected by grant funding received.</p>

<p>The planning point is structural: grants fund activity costs; SEIS/EIS funds the round. They are complementary, not competing. For a full comparison of the two equity schemes, see <a href="/blog/seis-and-eis/seis-vs-eis-explained">SEIS vs EIS Explained</a>.</p>

<h2>Getting grant-plus-relief planning right</h2>

<p>The interaction between a specific grant, R&amp;D relief and an equity raise is not a template exercise. Grant conditions vary, accounting periods matter for the merged-scheme date test, and some grant agreements include state-aid or match-funding clauses that need checking before a round is structured. If your company is approaching a grant award alongside an R&amp;D claim or a SEIS/EIS raise, it is worth taking advice before the grant agreement is signed rather than after.</p>
