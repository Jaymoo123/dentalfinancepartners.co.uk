## Site architecture: sub-segment hubs (/for/*)

Mirrors how the field segments itself (MHA, PKF Francis Clark, Cottons, EQ all split
manufacturing vs engineering; several add food and automotive):

```
/                                  Manufacturing & engineering accountants (head family)
/for/manufacturers                 factories/product businesses; costing, stock/WIP, CA
/for/engineering-firms             precision/contract engineering; project accounting, WIP
/for/fabrication-workshops         metal fab/CNC/machine shops; machine-hour economics
/for/food-and-drink-manufacturers  food SIC family is the biggest CH sub-sector; margins/waste
/for/exporters                     zero-rating, PVA, inward processing, CBAM 2027 wedge
/calculators/*                     fleet per CALCULATORS.md (CA planner, machine-hour, payroll first)
/research/manufacturing-index      flagship asset (DATA_ASSET.md)
/blog/*                            clusters per TOPICS.md
```

BUSINESS-audience rule honoured: every /for/* page carries the owner/MD frame with
segment-specific lead-form optional fields (headcount band, factory owned/leased, annual
plant spend band, exporter yes/no, R&D activity yes/no), never the job-seeker or
engineering-career frame (the sweep showed heavy career-intent noise on "engineering
accountant" — job boards ranked in 5 of 36 queries).

2. **OPERATOR-PROBLEM** (assist + capture): capital-allowance ordering on plant spend

   WIP/stock year-end judgment, machine-hour costing, CBAM exposure, EII energy

   costing, overhead absorption — currently owned by software vendors and education
   sites; heavy student intent to filter), "how many manufacturers in the UK" (→ index
   asset). Never the success metric.
