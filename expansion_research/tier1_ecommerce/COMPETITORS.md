## Where the gaps are (honest read — narrower than any prior niche)

1. **UK-first Amazon settlement/payout reconciliation explainers at A* depth** — the SaaS
   arms write to sell software; a genuinely neutral "reconcile your settlement to the penny
   without buying anything" resource layer is thin (nentara.com is the only pure attempt,
   14 pages).
2. **Marketplace-collected-VAT edge cases** — deemed-supplier boundaries (UK-established vs
   overseas-established sellers, £135 consignments, B2B carve-outs, Amazon 'VAT on fees'
   post-Aug-2024 Luxembourg→UK switch). Firms cover it shallowly; VAT agencies cover it
   from the EU side. A UK-seller-first treatment is a real but narrow wedge.
3. **Seller unit-economics calculators tied to UK tax** (margin after Amazon fees AND VAT
   AND income/corp tax) — the calculator sites do fees; the firms do tax; nobody joins them.
4. **Data asset on the UK ecommerce company population** (SIC 47910) — no rival runs a CH-
   derived index; feasibility verified (see DATA_ASSET.md).
5. **HMRC-visibility angle** — digital-platform reporting rules mean Amazon/eBay/Etsy/Vinted
   report sellers to HMRC since Jan 2024 (first reports Jan 2025); the query family is
   consumer-press-dominated but the "what to do when the letter arrives" advisory slice fits
   our lead model.
