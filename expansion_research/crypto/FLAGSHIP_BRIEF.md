# Flagship brief: Crypto Tax Gap Index (Crypto Tax Partners)

Slug already in nav: `/research/crypto-tax-gap-index`. Audience: investors, traders, DeFi/staking, NFT, miners.

## 1. Candidate dataset (honest assessment)
There is NO recurring public UK dataset on crypto tax compliance. What exists:
- HMRC/FCA one-off research reports on cryptoasset ownership (FCA consumer research, HMRC-commissioned individuals-holding-cryptoassets research). Point-in-time, not a series.
- FOI responses on HMRC nudge letters and disclosures: sporadic, published piecemeal by third parties, not an official series we can promise to refresh.
- CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework) exchange reporting starts feeding HMRC from 2026-27, but HMRC has published no statistics from it yet.
An index needs a refreshable series; none of the above qualifies today. Confidence in this negative finding: moderate-high, worth one owner-side web check before final rejection.

## 2. Source and licensing
FCA/HMRC reports are OGL when published on gov.uk/fca.org.uk. FOI responses are reusable but availability is not under our control.

## 3-4. Asset shape and refresh
Not specified. Any Dataset JSON-LD claiming a maintained series would be an overclaim against the gold-standard bar.

## Fallback (owner option)
An annual "UK Crypto Tax Enforcement Tracker" built on our own FOI requests to HMRC (nudge letter volumes, disclosure facility usage) is defensible and differentiated, but it is a new FOI-driven programme, not a bolt-on of existing public data, and delivery depends on HMRC responses.
