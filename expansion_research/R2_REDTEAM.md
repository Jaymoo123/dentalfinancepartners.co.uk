# R2 Red Team — adversarial review of R2_NICHE_SCORES_DRAFT.md

Date: 2026-07-11. Posture: each Tier-1 pick presumed WRONG until evidence survived attack.
Method: 16 live web SERP spot-checks (2 per Tier-1 group, run 2026-07-11; note: search tool is US-indexed with "uk" appended — an imperfect Google-UK proxy, but every result set returned UK firms, so directionally usable), plus internal-consistency attack on R2A/R2C/R2D and re-read of the raw evidence tables.

## Headline finding (systemic, affects the whole draft)

**The R2A "conservative bias" claim is backwards for hospitality.** The draft asserts SERP verdicts OVERSTATE competition. Live checks show the opposite in the exact groups the draft leans on: every hospitality WEAK_FIELD verdict (pubs 1.33, hotels 1.0, takeaways 0.67) is falsified today. R2A's DDG-era snapshot systematically UNDERSTATED specialist density in trade niches. Any Tier-1 ranking that cites "weakest fields in the top demand band" is built on stale competitive data.

**Second systemic defect: head-term intent was never checked.** Two of the three biggest volume numbers in the whole draft (film accountant 1,600/mo; recruitment agency accountant 880/mo) are intent-poisoned (career-seeking and reverse-direction respectively). R2D measured demand, not *buyer* demand.

## Tier-1 verdicts

### 1. Charities & non-profits — SURVIVES-WITH-CHANGES

- **Competition understated.** Live "accountant for charities" / "charity accountants uk": 4+ dedicated exact-match brands (charityaccountant.co.uk, charityaccountants.co.uk, charityaccountingpartners.co.uk, accountantsforcharities.org.uk) plus heavyweight sector teams (Price Bailey, PKF Francis Clark, Azets, Albert Goodman, Perrys) and ICAEW in the top 10. "Only CONTESTED field (2.67)" does not hold today; treat as STRONG-adjacent. Volume claim (590 head, 2,570 cluster) is plausible — dense advertiser/brand field corroborates real demand.
- **Lead economics softer than scored.** CC31/OSCR/CFG/NCVO guidance confirms trustees select examiners largely by **word of mouth**, with an independence requirement and documented price sensitivity (charityconnect thread in R2B shows haggling over £400-£1,500 IEs). Statutory audit (£5k-£20k) is unreachable via anonymous lead form. Realistic funnel = small-charity independent examination + bookkeeping + Gift Aid, i.e. the BOTTOM of the G12 band. Lead value 4 is a stretch; 3 is honest.
- **E-E-A-T:** Google won't see the Ashfield sibling estate unless linked, but the legally required trading-name disclosure puts "Ashfield Trading Ltd" (a company whose other brands are all commercial tax sites) on a charity-sector site. Trustees who check will notice. Manageable, not disqualifying — but don't run the standard anonymised-social-proof playbook unmodified; charity buyers are the most diligence-prone audience in the estate.
- **Changes:** re-score field as STRONG-adjacent; lead value 4→3; positioning = small charities/CIOs under the audit threshold; keep churches content-only (correct call, £0-£250 WTP documented).

### 2. Hospitality — SURVIVES-WITH-CHANGES

- **The core evidence claim is FALSE.** Live checks: "accountant for pubs" top-10 = HLWA (×2), Yorkshire Accountancy, Apex, Blue Shore, Xeinadin, AIMS pub page, Innspired — not 1.33 specialists. "hotel accountant" = Perrys, Xeinadin, WR Partners, ABM, LAS, UHY, Accotax (plus an Indeed jobs result — minor career-intent contamination). "accountant for takeaways" = TAJ, Perrys, Majestic, Merranti, MSF, Auditox, AIMS, AccounTax Zone. "hospitality accountant" = Paperchase, The Hospitality Accountants, Heighten, Fusion, Rouse, Streets, Xeinadin. Every WEAK verdict fails; this is a CONTESTED-to-STRONG family.
- **Grouping is fine.** One trade family with shared ops content (tronc, VAT on food, TOMS-adjacent, seasonal payroll, 2026 rates-relief cliff); restaurants/pubs/hotels/takeaways as sub-hubs is the same architecture the estate already runs. Not a mushy multi-audience trap.
- **Lead econ holds** (G11 £75-£500/mo documented, monthly-retainer buyers who do use lead forms — small operators, not tender committees).
- **Changes:** strip "weakest fields in the top demand band" as the rank-2 justification — it is untrue; rank on demand + lead value alone and accept a CONTESTED fight. The 2026 business-rates cliff is a genuine topical wedge (surfaced in live results).

### 3. Startups & tech/SaaS — SURVIVES-WITH-CHANGES

- **SERP re-check:** "accountant for startups" = mostly generalists with startup pages + commodity players (Sleek, The Accountancy £23.70/mo, a-wise £10/mo) — CONTESTED 2.0 roughly holds. "saas accountant uk" = real UK specialists (saasaccountants.co.uk, striveX £1m-£15m ARR, Reflex, Clarkwell, RSBC, dns) — the STRONG verdict is NOT just US bias; the draft's pilot.com caveat over-discounts it.
- **Volume plausible:** 720 head + £39.81 CPC = advertisers demonstrably paying, which also answers the lead-form question — startups do submit forms (the commodity firms live on them). The VC-referral objection is real only for funded startups; the search demand is pre-seed/bootstrapped.
- **Attack that lands:** the searching segment is the LOW-fee segment (£10-£24/mo commodity war), and R2B itself flags survival churn. Lead value 3 MED is right; don't let CPC inflate it.
- **Changes:** drop the "SaaS STRONG is US-bias" caveat (falsified live); win on R&D/SEIS/EIS/share-scheme depth, not the head term.

### 4. Ecommerce sellers — SURVIVES-WITH-CHANGES (demote below care sector)

- Live check confirms and worsens the 6.67: exact-match brands (ecommerceaccountants.co.uk, yourecommerceaccountant.co.uk, Elver, Unicorn ×2), platform players (Sleek, Crunch), AND the listicle layer is owned by competitors/software (LinkMyBooks, E2E, A2X directories own "best ecommerce accountant" positions — a moat the estate can't flank with one site). Amazon FBA identical.
- Demand and lead value are real (£35-44 CPC, G8 fees documented HIGH conf). The niche is good; the *rank* is wrong. This is the single hardest organic fight in Tier 1 and it sits at #4 with an honest "hardest fight" label but no time-horizon adjustment.
- **Changes:** move to Tier-1 tail or Tier 2; if built, entry wedge must be a data asset/calculator (VAT thresholds, margin scheme, EPR fees) not head-term pages.

### 5. Crypto traders & investors — SURVIVES-WITH-CHANGES

- **"Young field, few entrenched domains" is FALSE.** Live: Andersen LLP, BKL, Charlton Baker, MJ Kane, mycryptotax, cryptoaccountants.live, certifiedcryptoaccountant — plus **Koinly and Recap running accountant directories** in the top 10. Established firms + aggregator layer = mature field.
- **Head-term intent partially DIY:** "crypto tax uk" (880, the group's best term) is heavily informational/software intent — Koinly-class tools absorb the low end; the hire-an-accountant segment is the £750-£2k complex-case tail (which is genuinely good money, R2B HIGH conf).
- Overlap CAUTION vs property is fine as written — trading/DeFi/staking positioning is distinct from property CGT; the shared token is "capital gains", not the audience.
- **Changes:** re-base demand on hire-intent terms only ("crypto tax accountant" family, not "crypto tax uk"); acknowledge directory/aggregator moat; cyclicality means don't judge the site in a bear-market window.

### 6. Recruitment agencies — KILL (as a Tier-1 pick, on today's evidence)

- **The load-bearing volume is reverse-intent.** Live top-10 for BOTH "recruitment agency accountant" AND "accountants for recruitment agencies" is dominated by Reed, Hays, Robert Half, Robert Walters, Morgan McKinley, agencycentral — i.e. agencies that recruit accountants. Only 2-3 true buyer-side results (Harold Sharp, AccounTax Zone, recruitmentaccountants.com). The 880/mo head term (76% of the group's 1,150) is mostly people hiring/being accountants, not recruitment-agency owners buying accounting. True buyer demand is plausibly <300/mo.
- **The SEO fight is against Reed/Hays domain authority on your own head term** — a worse fight than any STRONG specialist field in the draft.
- **The overlap CLEAR is circular.** R2C matched recruitment against… construction-cis (0.00) because the agency site's corpus has no recruitment impressions yet — the exact blindness that forced a JUDGMENT-EXCLUDE for marketing agencies (#48, same 0.00-vs-construction-cis artefact). The draft's "R2C metrics show no query overlap" defence proves nothing; "agency accountant" token collision with the agency site is untested, not absent.
- **Disposition:** remove from Tier 1. The niche itself (temp payroll/umbrella/IR35-other-side) is real and the trade is distinct from digital/creative agencies — park it pending (a) intent-clean volume re-pull on "accountants for recruitment agencies"-family terms only, and (b) an explicit agency-site cannibalisation test.

### 7. Creative & media industries — KILL as constructed; salvage as Tier-2 sub-niche picks

- **Demand claim is intent-poisoned.** "film accountant" (1,600 = 55% of the group's 2,900) is substantially a career/job query — ScreenSkills' production-accountant job profile ranks top-10 live, and production accountants are freelancers hired by productions through industry networks, not lead-form clients. The group's headline number collapses on inspection.
- **The flanking premise is contradicted by R2A's own data.** The draft claims "DIFFERENT specialists per sub-niche… a breadth site can flank them." R2A's dominant-specialist column shows creativeandnumbers.co.uk in niches 42, 43, 44, 45, 46 and creative.accountants in 41, 44, 45 — the SAME breadth specialists already occupy multiple sub-niches. The flank the draft proposes already exists and is entrenched. Live "accountant for musicians" confirms: Gorilla, Xeinadin, Streets Media, creative.accountants, Mark Carr, RSBC, Lanop, GoForma — 8/10 specialist-grade.
- **Lead economics are the weakest in Tier 1:** R2B scores musicians/actors/artists/authors/photographers all 2 (G2 sole-trader £150-350 SA). Only film/TV (4) and content creators (3) clear the bar, and film/TV's channel is wrong (production-side buying).
- **Disposition:** kill the 8-sub-niche breadth site. If anything ships from this family it's a narrow **content creators/OnlyFans** site (CLEAR overlap — wait: hollowaydavies already ranks for OnlyFans terms per R2A own-estate hits; that makes creators a generalist-site expansion question, not a new site). Net: nothing here belongs in Tier 1.

### 8. Care sector — SURVIVES-WITH-CHANGES

- Live "accountants for care homes": 10/10 sector-relevant results (Hawsons, RA, Clarkwell, Ryans, Mitchells, BTB, UHY Ross Brooke, carehomeaccountant.co.uk, account-ease) — denser than 3.67 suggests, but mostly small/regional firms, no aggregator moat. Beatable with A* treatment.
- Lead econ is the best-evidenced in the draft (G6 £3,000-£7,000/yr HIGH conf) and care-home operators are exactly the owner-operator profile that submits forms. Domiciliary care CONTESTED + 0 measured volume means it's content-support, not a demand leg.
- **Attack that lands:** 190/mo cluster is tiny; this site pays back per-lead, not per-visitor, and will look "failed" on traffic dashboards. CAUTION vs medical needs a hard positioning wall (care BUSINESSES/CQC/payroll, never clinicians) or it erodes the medical site's watch-window (currently in a 14/28d signal window — do not muddy it).
- **Changes:** set explicit low-traffic/high-value success criteria before build; sequence AFTER the medical watch windows close (~08-03).

## Not-recommended list — wrongly-killed check

- **Pharmacies (#19): the strongest buried candidate.** 600/mo (3× care homes), lead 4, sticky practice-purchase economics. Live check shows a real but small-firm specialist field (pharmacistaccountants.co.uk, MMBA, rxvirtualfinance, Hawsons, Lanop, Xeinadin) — comparable difficulty to care homes with 3× the demand. "Natural future medical expansion" is a fair strategic call, but on raw economics pharmacies beats at least three current Tier-1 picks (recruitment, creative, arguably crypto). **Recommendation: pull pharmacies forward** — either as an early standalone or an accelerated medical-cluster build; do not leave it in the parked list.
- **Manufacturing (#73, Tier 2):** with hospitality's WEAK verdicts falsified, manufacturing's 0.67 WEAK_FIELD is now the only near-empty field left with lead value 4. I did not live-check it (flagged: do so in R3), but risk-adjusted it belongs in Tier 1 ahead of recruitment and creative.
- **Travel agents/TOMS (#59):** the 0 measured volume is a head-term phrasing artefact risk ("accountant for travel agents" is not how this audience searches; "TOMS VAT" / "TOMS accountant" untested). R2B rates the moat genuine. Cheap R3 volume re-check before permanent burial.
- **Maritime/SED (#87):** 440/mo, genuine micro-specialism, tiny incumbents — but live SERP is gov.uk + informational guides (DIY-able claim), lead 3 LOW. Correctly excluded from Tier 1; fine as an opportunistic flyer.
- Schools (tender channel), HNW (trust-gated), taxi/couriers (£150-180 fees), management consultants ("consultant accountant" 320 has the same reverse/ambiguous-intent disease as recruitment — the fold-into-contractors-ir35 call is right): **all correctly killed.**
