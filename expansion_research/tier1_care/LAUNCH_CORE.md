## Site architecture: sub-segment hubs (/for/*)

One sector family, credible to each sub-segment (mirrors the rivals' own segmentation —
CareAxis, The Healthcare Accountant and SKN all split exactly this way):

```
/                                Care sector accountants (head family)
/for/care-homes                  residential + nursing; fee mix, occupancy, staffing %
/for/domiciliary-care            sleep-ins, travel time, hourly-rate economics — widest gap
/for/supported-living            rent/care split (VAT + housing benefit), only 1 dedicated rival
/for/childrens-homes             Ofsted-side sibling; rivals list it, nobody owns it
/for/care-startups               CQC registration finance, FVS, projections, start-up accounts
/calculators/*                   fleet per CALCULATORS.md (care-hour cost, sleep-in NMW, staffing margin first)
/research/care-provider-index    flagship asset (DATA_ASSET.md)
/blog/*                          clusters below
```

BUSINESS-audience rule honoured: every /for/* page carries the operator (owner/registered
manager) frame, segment-specific lead-form optional fields (beds/locations count, CQC
registration status, LA vs self-funder mix, sponsored-staff count), never the care-worker or
family-payer frame.

## Intent classes

1. **HIRE** (lead pages): "care home accountant(s)", "accountants for domiciliary care
   agencies", "supported living accountants", "childrens home accountants" — all verified as
   real rival-targeted terms (12 dedicated rivals build exactly these pages).
2. **OPERATOR-PROBLEM** (assist + capture): sleep-in pay rules, travel-time NMW, VAT
   exemption/recovery, RCB 2/2025 grouping, sponsorship costs, CQC FVS, LA fee negotiation,
   agency-staff cost control. Calculator/checker surfaces.
3. **DIY-INFORMATIONAL** (authority + citations): "are care home fees vat exempt" family,
   funded-nursing-care questions (rate/backdating/means-testing), "start a domiciliary care
   agency", franchise economics, "how many care homes" (→ index asset). Never the success
   metric; feeds internal links + GEO/AI-answer presence.

## Launch core (22 pages + 3 tools + 1 asset)

**Hubs (6):** homepage + the five /for/* pages above. Evidence: every dedicated rival
verified this run segments its offer this way; domiciliary and supported living are the two
segments where the dedicated tier is thinnest (2 firms each) while the query families are
broad (TOPICS.md clusters).

**Money/service pages (6):**
- CQC financial viability statement service — a rival literally sells this (cqcfinancialviability.com); productised, fixed-fee.
- Care payroll (sleep-ins, travel time, sponsored staff) — 4 payroll bureaus have care pages; no dedicated firm owns the compliance angle end-to-end.
- Care VAT review (welfare exemption, partial exemption, RCB 2/2025 health-check) — the highest-stakes advisory wedge; Kingscrest-style reclaim marketing (Cost Care) proves money moves here.
- Buying a care home (due diligence, capital allowances, structure) — brokers own valuation; nobody owns the tax/DD slice as a page family.
- Selling a care home (CGT/BADR, propco-opco) — same gap on the exit side.
- Start a domiciliary care agency: accounts, projections & CQC money paperwork — start-up cluster is large in autocomplete and owned by CQC consultants, not accountants.

**Blog clusters at launch (10 posts, 2 per hub):** VAT exemption edge cases + RCB 2/2025
explainer; sleep-in pay after Mencap + travel-time NMW; sponsorship true-cost + Employment
Allowance for agencies; FNC/CHC/LA fee-mix accounting + fee-increase negotiation with
cost-of-care evidence; agency-staff cost control + occupancy-void accounting; (children's
homes) Ofsted vs CQC money paperwork; (startups) FVS walkthrough; capital allowances on
fit-out (FA 2026 14%/40% FYA); business rates for care settings; MTD-IT for owner-operators.

**Tools (3):** care-hour cost, sleep-in NMW, staffing margin (CALCULATORS.md launch tier).

**Asset (1):** UK Care Provider Business Index (DATA_ASSET.md).
