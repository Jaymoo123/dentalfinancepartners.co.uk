# House positions outline — Pharmacies (R3)

Date: 2026-07-11. Every citation URL fetched live this run and confirmed 200 with expected
phrases (`raw/citation_checks.json`, 31/31 passing). Positioning wall honoured throughout:
pharmacy BUSINESS owners + NHS contract economics; locum pharmacists are a content audience
only; nothing clinical, nothing patient-facing (medical-site adjacency guard).

## A. VAT — the pharmacy signature topic (the strongest differentiation wedge in the niche)

1. **NHS-dispensed prescription drugs are zero-rated; most OTC retail sales are standard-rated.**
   A community pharmacy is structurally a VAT-mixed business and almost always reclaims more
   input VAT than a pure retailer expects. Cite: VAT Notice 701/57
   (gov.uk/guidance/health-professionals-pharmaceutical-products-and-vat-notice-70157).
2. **Zero-rating applies to dispensing by a registered pharmacist against a prescription** —
   services supplied by pharmacists (e.g. private services, some Pharmacy First-adjacent
   private clinics) can be exempt or standard-rated instead; the mix must be mapped SKU/service
   line by line. Cite: Notice 701/57.
3. **Partial exemption rarely bites but must be checked** where exempt supplies (e.g. certain
   medical services) exist; de minimis limits apply. Cite: VAT Notice 706
   (gov.uk/guidance/partial-exemption-vat-notice-706).
4. **Retail schemes are the practical mechanic** for splitting zero/standard-rated takings in a
   shop that can't itemise every sale; choosing the wrong scheme systematically overpays VAT.
   Cite: HMRC VAT Retail Schemes manual (gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-retail-schemes).
5. **£90,000 registration threshold is irrelevant to almost every pharmacy** (NHS turnover
   forces registration economics early); voluntary registration is normally advantageous
   because of zero-rated outputs. Cite: gov.uk/vat-registration.

## B. NHS contract economics (the "no generalist can fake this" layer)

6. **Pharmacy income is contract-driven, not till-driven**: reimbursement (Drug Tariff prices)
   + remuneration (fees/service payments) under the Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework.
   Cite: NHS England CPCF (england.nhs.uk/community-pharmacy-contractual-framework/); NHSBSA
   Drug Tariff (nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/drug-tariff).
7. **The FP34 submission cycle drives cash flow**: prescriptions submitted monthly; payment
   arrives ~2 months later, with an advance on account — working-capital planning must model
   the NHSBSA payment lag. Cite: NHSBSA submitting prescriptions
   (nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/submitting-prescriptions).
8. **Drug Tariff/Category M clawbacks and price volatility mean gross margin is set centrally
   and retrospectively adjusted** — margin variance analysis, not just bookkeeping, is the
   core monthly job. Cite: Drug Tariff (as above).
9. **Service income (Pharmacy First etc.) is a growing, separately-accounted revenue line**
   with its own fee structure and thresholds. Cite: NHS England Pharmacy First
   (england.nhs.uk/primary-care/pharmacy/pharmacy-services/pharmacy-first/).
10. **Market entry is regulated**: opening/relocating/buying a pharmacy engages the NHS
    (Pharmaceutical and Local Pharmaceutical Services) Regulations 2013 — the NHS contract,
    not the shop, is the asset. Cite: legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/349/contents.
11. **Premises must be GPhC-registered and ownership changes have regulatory steps** (incl.
    superintendent pharmacist requirements for companies). Cite:
    pharmacyregulation.org/pharmacies.

## C. Buying and selling a pharmacy (the high-value moment)

12. **Share purchase vs asset purchase is the first structuring decision**: asset deals attract
    SDLT on any property at non-residential rates (up to 5%); share deals attract 0.5% stamp
    duty on shares — but inherit history. Cite:
    gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax/nonresidential-and-mixed-use-rates; gov.uk/guidance/stamp-duty-on-shares.
13. **Goodwill dominates pharmacy pricing** (driven by the NHS contract/item volume); on a
    company purchase, corporation tax relief on goodwill is restricted and only available in
    limited cases at fixed rates. Cite:
    gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-relief-on-goodwill-and-relevant-assets.
14. **On sale, Business Asset Disposal Relief caps CGT at the BADR rate (14% for 2025/26,
    legislated to rise) on qualifying disposals up to the £1m lifetime limit** — timing a
    pharmacy sale around BADR rate steps is real money. Cite: gov.uk/business-asset-disposal-relief.
15. **Standard CGT rates (18%/24%) apply beyond BADR**; deal structuring (earn-outs, deferred
    consideration) changes when tax falls due. Cite: gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates.
16. **Valuation practice**: pharmacies price on a multiple of adjusted EBITDA / pence-per-item
    benchmarks; the dossier's calculator (purchase affordability) encodes the arithmetic, the
    positions stay method-level (no invented multiples without a cited broker source at build).

## D. Capital allowances and premises

17. **Pharmacy fit-outs (shelving, dispensary robots, refrigeration, security) are plant &
    machinery**: AIA covers up to £1m at 100%. Cite:
    gov.uk/capital-allowances/annual-investment-allowance.
18. **FA 2026 changed the writing-down landscape**: main-rate WDA falls 18%→14% with a new 40%
    first-year allowance for qualifying expenditure (ground truth in estate memory; page-level
    rates cite gov.uk/work-out-capital-allowances/rates-and-pools).
19. **Structures & Buildings Allowance (3% straight line)** applies to qualifying works on the
    building itself, distinct from fit-out plant. Cite:
    gov.uk/guidance/claiming-capital-allowances-for-structures-and-buildings.

## E. Locum pharmacists (content-only audience — no locum lead forms at launch)

20. **HMRC's Employment Status Manual has a locum-pharmacist-specific page (ESM4270)** — status
    is fact-based, and HMRC's stated position on locum pharmacists is restrictive; "everyone
    does it self-employed" is not a defence. Cite:
    gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-status-manual/esm4270.
21. **Off-payroll (IR35) applies where a locum works through their own company** and the client
    is in scope; CEST is the check tool of record. Cite:
    gov.uk/guidance/understanding-off-payroll-working-ir35; gov.uk/guidance/check-employment-status-for-tax.
22. **Self-employed status tests** (control, substitution, financial risk) decide the
    sole-trader locum question. Cite: gov.uk/employment-status/selfemployed-contractor.
23. **MTD for Income Tax hits sole-trader locums from April 2026 at £50,000+ income**
    (then £30k). Cite: gov.uk/guidance/use-making-tax-digital-for-income-tax.
24. **Cash basis is the default for unincorporated businesses** — relevant to locums and small
    owner books. Cite: gov.uk/simpler-income-tax-cash-basis.

## F. Payroll, company tax, general

25. **Employer NIC is 15% above a £5,000 secondary threshold** (estate ground truth; verified
    live on the 2026-27 rates page) — material for pharmacies' dispenser/counter-staff payrolls.
    Cite: gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2026-to-2027.
26. **Employment Allowance offsets employer NIC** for eligible employers. Cite:
    gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance.
27. **Corporation tax: 25% main rate / 19% small profits rate with marginal relief** — most
    single-store pharmacies sit in the marginal band; associated-company rules bite multi-store
    owners with separate companies. Cite: gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates.

## Deliberately out of scope (positioning wall)

- Anything clinical, prescribing, or patient-safety (GPhC professional matters beyond
  ownership/registration mechanics) — that is the medical site's adjacency zone and a
  regulated-profession credibility trap.
- Scotland/Wales/NI contract variants at v1: the CPCF citations above are England; devolved
  contract content queued post-launch (mirrors the charities pilot's England&Wales-first call).
