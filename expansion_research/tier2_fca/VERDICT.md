# R3 verdict: FCA-regulated firms accountancy (tier2_fca)

Date: 2026-07-15. R2 prior: "weak field, tiny demand, compliance moat". Question: does
moat + lead value beat tiny demand?

## VERDICT: NO-GO (as a standalone estate site)

R2's prior partially survives but the decisive new finding is that the moat faces the
wrong way and the field is not actually weak.

### Reasoning

1. **The compliance moat excludes the estate, it does not protect it.** The high-value
   services (CASS audits, PI/EMI safeguarding audits, statutory audits, anything opined
   to the FCA) legally require registered auditors. The estate operator is not one and
   cannot become one by content. The only defensible half of the niche is unwalled
   ordinary accountancy plus content/tools.
2. **The "weak field" was measured on the wrong axis.** Accountancy rivals are weak
   (2 dedicated microsites, 17 thin section pages), but the buyer-intent SERPs are
   actually owned by ~19 well-funded FCA compliance consultancies, plus law firms and
   regtech vendors, all of whom answer the same buyer's problem better than an
   accountancy handoff does. A new site fights them, not the 2 microsites.
3. **The lead-gen handoff model half-works at best.** The natural handoff for
   CASS/safeguarding-audit leads must be a registered audit firm; whether the existing
   partner (Reflex, which already markets a CASS audit page) can sign such opinions is
   unverified. For authorisation/prudential leads the right recipient is a compliance
   consultancy, an entity the estate has no deal with. Only the low-value slice
   (small brokers/IFAs/ARs wanting a normal accountant) maps cleanly to the current
   partner model.
4. **Demand is tiny and polluted.** Autocomplete surface is heavily contaminated
   (US county auditors, social-care safeguarding, Brazilian IFPR institute, consumer
   "is X regulated" checks). The clean firm-side pool (845 clusters upper bound, real
   writable set smaller) is the thinnest of any niche researched, with zero volume data
   to contradict the tiny-demand prior.
5. **Maximum YMYL exposure with the estate's weakest hand.** Faceless, no-credential
   content advising regulated financial firms on prudential compliance is the worst
   possible fit for the locked "user is not an accountant" constraint.

### CONDITIONAL path (only if all three hold, revisit then)

- Paid volume pulls (see DOSSIER TODO) show CASS/safeguarding/IFPR queries have
  materially more volume than assumed, AND
- Reflex (or a new second partner) is confirmed as a registered auditor able to take
  CASS/safeguarding audit engagements, AND
- The play is reframed as a small calculator/tool asset cluster (IFPR own-funds
  calculator, RegData calendar) bolted onto an EXISTING estate property for GEO/citation
  value, not a standalone site build.

Absent those, do not build. Better uses of the next build slot exist in every Tier-1
niche already scored above this one.
