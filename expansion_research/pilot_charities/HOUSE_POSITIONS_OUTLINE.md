## A. Registration, reporting and scrutiny (Charity Commission)

1. **Registration threshold**: a charity must register with the Charity Commission once income exceeds £5,000; CIOs must register regardless of income.
   — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/how-to-register-your-charity-cc21b (200; "5,000" confirmed)
2. **Annual return**: required for registered charities with income over £10,000, and for all CIOs; due within 10 months of financial year end.
   — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/prepare-a-charity-annual-return (200; "annual return", "10,000" confirmed)
3. **External scrutiny gate**: independent examination required once gross income exceeds £25,000.
   — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/independent-examination-of-charity-accounts-trustees-cc31 (200; "independent examination", "25,000" confirmed)
4. **Audit threshold**: statutory audit required where gross income exceeds £1m, or income exceeds £250,000 AND gross assets exceed £3.26m (governing-document/funder audit clauses can force audit below these).
   — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/charity-reporting-and-accounting-the-essentials-november-2016-cc15d (200; "independent examination", "audit" confirmed)
5. **Qualified examiner rule**: where accruals accounts are required (income over £250,000), the independent examiner must belong to a body listed in the Charities Act (ICAEW, ACCA, AAT etc.).
   — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/independent-examination-of-charity-accounts-examiners-cc32 (200)
6. **Receipts & payments vs accruals**: non-company charities under £250,000 income may prepare R&P accounts; charitable companies and all charities over £250,000 must prepare accruals accounts.
   — CC15d (as position 4)
7. **SORP**: accruals accounts must follow the Charities SORP (FRS 102). A new SORP applies for periods beginning on or after 1 January 2026 (tiered reporting) — the site launches INTO the SORP-2026 transition, which is a core content opportunity.
   — https://www.charitysorp.org/ (200; "SORP" confirmed)
8. **Charities Act 2022**: staged commencement changed rules on permanent endowment, disposals of charity land, ex gratia payments, charity names and governing-document amendments; cite the Commission's implementation plan for what is in force.
   — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-act-2022-implementation-plan (200; "Charities Act 2022", "permanent endowment" confirmed)
9. **Public register**: charity details, accounts and trustee info are publicly searchable; full register downloadable.
   — https://www.gov.uk/find-charity-information (200) and https://register-of-charities.charitycommission.gov.uk/en/register/full-register-download (200; ZIP downloads confirmed)

## B. Charity taxation (HMRC)

10. **Recognition**: charity tax reliefs require recognition by HMRC (separate from Commission registration).
    — https://www.gov.uk/charities-and-tax (200)
11. **Corporation tax/income tax**: charities pay no tax on most income and gains applied to charitable purposes; a tax return is only needed if HMRC issues a notice or there is non-exempt income.
    — https://www.gov.uk/charities-and-tax (200)
12. **Primary-purpose trading** is tax-exempt; **non-primary-purpose trading** is taxable above the small trading exemption: £8,000, or 25% of income, capped at £80,000 turnover.
    — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading (200; "trading", "80,000" confirmed)
13. **Trading subsidiary**: taxable trading is routed through a subsidiary company; the subsidiary pays corporation tax but can eliminate profit by donating it to the parent charity (corporate Gift Aid).
    — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading (200)

## C. Gift Aid family

14. **Gift Aid basic mechanics**: 25p reclaimed per £1 donated; requires a valid declaration and the donor to have paid at least the reclaimed amount in UK tax.
    — https://www.gov.uk/claim-gift-aid (200; "Gift Aid", "25p" confirmed)
15. **Declarations**: content requirements and record-keeping.
    — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/gift-aid-declarations-claiming-tax-back-on-donations (200)
16. **Donor benefit limits**: benefits to donors must stay within HMRC limits or the donation loses Gift Aid.
    — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/gift-aid-what-donations-charities-and-cascs-can-claim-on (200; "benefit" confirmed)
17. **GASDS**: top-up on small cash/contactless donations of £30 or less, on up to £8,000 of donations per tax year; no declarations needed; community-buildings rules for connected charities.
    — https://www.gov.uk/claim-gift-aid/small-donations-scheme (200; "8,000" confirmed) and https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claiming-a-top-up-payment-on-small-charitable-donations (200; "GASDS", "30" confirmed)
18. **Retail Gift Aid** (charity shops): the agency arrangement letting shops claim Gift Aid on donated-goods proceeds, with the standard/simplified operating methods.
    — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/charities-detailed-guidance-notes/chapter-3-gift-aid (200; "retail gift aid" confirmed at ch. 3.42)
19. **Donor-side relief**: higher/additional-rate donors reclaim the difference through self assessment.
    — https://www.gov.uk/donating-to-charity/gift-aid (200; "higher rate" confirmed)

## D. VAT and rates

20. **No blanket VAT exemption**: charities follow normal registration rules; reliefs exist on specific purchases (zero-rated advertising, aids for disabled people; 5% reduced rate fuel & power for non-business use); business/non-business apportionment and partial exemption are the recurring pain points. One-off fundraising events are exempt (VAT Notice 701/1).
    — https://www.gov.uk/vat-charities (200) and https://www.gov.uk/guidance/how-vat-affects-charities-notice-7011 (200; "VAT", "relief" confirmed)
21. **Business rates**: 80% mandatory charitable relief on property used wholly/mainly for charitable purposes; councils may top up to 100%.
    — https://www.gov.uk/apply-for-business-rate-relief/charitable-rate-relief (200; "80%" confirmed)

## E. CICs and structures

22. **A CIC is not a charity**: no charity tax reliefs, standard corporation tax; regulated by the CIC Regulator, not the Charity Commission.
    — https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies (200)
23. **CIC asset lock and caps**: statutory asset lock; dividend/performance-related caps for CICs limited by shares; the pack of CIC guidance chapters is the citation anchor.
    — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-how-to-form-a-cic (200; "asset lock" confirmed)
24. **CIC filing**: CIC34 community interest report filed with the accounts at Companies House (with fee).
    — same CIC guidance pack (chapter on reporting); second-pass verify the current fee figure at S5
25. **Structure choice**: charity (inc. CIO) vs CIC vs unincorporated association — the Commission's structure guidance is the canonical comparison; CIO = incorporation without Companies House.
    — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charity-types-how-to-choose-a-structure (200; "charitable incorporated organisation" confirmed)

## F. Jurisdiction and payroll

26. **Scotland is a different regime**: OSCR-regulated; ALL Scottish charities need external scrutiny regardless of size and thresholds differ. Site default scope = England & Wales, with explicit Scotland call-outs, never silently mixed.
    — https://www.oscr.org.uk/ (200; "Scottish Charity Regulator" confirmed)
27. **Charity payroll**: employer NIC 15% above a £5,000 secondary threshold (per estate ground truth, confirmed on the live employer rates pages); charities are eligible for the Employment Allowance (up to £10,500).
    — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2026-to-2027 (200; "15%", "5,000" confirmed) and https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance (200; "10,500" confirmed)
