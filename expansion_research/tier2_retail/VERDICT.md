# R3 VERDICT — Retail and independent shops accountancy (Tier-2)

Date: 2026-07-15. R2 prior: "weak field, thin differentiation".

## Verdict: CONDITIONAL, leaning NO-GO as a standalone site

R2's prior is CONFIRMED on both halves, now with evidence:

**Weak field (confirmed).** 0 dedicated UK retail-accountancy specialists among 108
fetch-verified SERP candidates; only 22 generalist firms with retail sector pages. The
adjacent money SERPs (franchise, EPOS, buy/sell a shop) are owned by directories,
software vendors and marketplaces, not accountants. A well-built niche site would face
the weakest accountancy competition of any niche researched so far.

**Thin differentiation (confirmed, and it is the binding constraint).** The usable topic
pool is roughly 150-180 clean UK provider-side clusters (vs 659 for care on the identical
method), autocomplete hire-intent demand is thin, and most of the surface (business
rates, payroll, margins, buying a business) is content the estate's generalist site could
carry. Hollowaydavies already ranks organically for "accountants for independent shops".
A retail site would differentiate by packaging, not subject matter.

**The ecommerce wall is NOT the blocker.** The bricks-and-mortar surface (retail VAT
schemes, till/EPOS reconciliation, mixed-rate food VAT, business rates, cash-business
compliance, premises, shop sale/goodwill, franchisee accounting, sub-trade pages) is
structurally distinct from the ecommerce site's online-seller territory and enough for a
launch core. Details in DOSSIER.md "THE WALL". Overlap policing is needed only on
inventory/COGS fundamentals, thresholds, MTD ITSA and sole-trader-vs-ltd.

## Conditions under which this flips to GO

1. Paid volume data (TODO list in DOSSIER.md) shows the hire-intent heads ("retail
   accountant", "accountant for shop", "convenience store accountant") carry meaningful
   UK volume. This is the main unknown; free sources cannot quantify it.
2. The estate decides sub-trade lead-gen (convenience stores especially, where SERP
   intent looked most commercial) is worth a dedicated brand rather than a generalist
   /for/* expansion.

## Recommended alternative if NO-GO stands

Spend the same effort adding a retail cluster to the generalist site: /for/retail-shops,
/for/convenience-stores, /for/franchisees plus the retail-VAT-scheme and EPOS content.
It captures the defensible surface, needs no new brand/domain/deploy, and avoids
splitting authority in a niche where our generalist already ranks.
