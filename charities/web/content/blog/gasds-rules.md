<p>The Gift Aid Small Donations Scheme (GASDS) lets your charity claim a 25% top-up on small cash and contactless donations without needing a Gift Aid declaration from each donor. It was designed for collection buckets, church plate, and contactless payment terminals where asking every donor to sign a form is simply not practical.</p>

<h2>What GASDS lets your charity claim</h2>
<p>GASDS pays a top-up equivalent to the Gift Aid rate of 25p for every £1 of qualifying donation. Your charity can claim on up to <strong>£8,000 of donations per tax year</strong>, which produces a <strong>maximum top-up of £2,000</strong>. No declaration is required from the donor. The scheme is available to charities and community amateur sports clubs (CASCs) that are recognised by HMRC for Gift Aid purposes.</p>
<p>The official HMRC guidance for the scheme is at <a href="https://www.gov.uk/claim-gift-aid/small-donations-scheme">gov.uk/claim-gift-aid/small-donations-scheme</a> and <a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claiming-a-top-up-payment-on-small-charitable-donations">gov.uk/guidance/claiming-a-top-up-payment-on-small-charitable-donations</a>.</p>

<h2>Which donations qualify</h2>
<p>A donation qualifies for GASDS when it meets all three conditions:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>The amount is £30 or less.</strong> Each individual donation must be £30 or less. A £31 donation does not qualify at all; there is no partial claim on the first £30.</li>
  <li><strong>It is paid in cash or by contactless payment.</strong> This includes coins, notes, contactless card transactions, and contactless device payments (such as a phone tap). Direct debits, cheques, and online bank transfers do not qualify.</li>
  <li><strong>No Gift Aid declaration is attached.</strong> If a donor gives you a declaration, claim Gift Aid in the normal way instead. GASDS is the route when no declaration exists.</li>
</ul>
<p>You do not need to identify individual donors. Aggregate your qualifying cash and contactless totals for the tax year and claim on that figure.</p>
<p>For the rules on Gift Aid itself, including declarations and the 25p-per-£1 mechanics, see the <a href="/guides/gift-aid-complete-guide">Gift Aid complete guide</a>.</p>

<h2>The annual limit and what the top-up is worth</h2>
<p>Your charity can claim GASDS on up to <strong>£8,000 of qualifying donations per tax year</strong>. At the 25% top-up rate, that produces a <strong>maximum top-up of £2,000</strong> per year. These figures are set by HMRC and apply per charity (or per community building where the community buildings rules apply).</p>

<h2>The matching rule: why you need some Gift Aid claims too</h2>
<p>GASDS is not a free-standing scheme. There is a <strong>matching rule</strong>: the donations on which you claim GASDS in a tax year <strong>cannot exceed 10 times</strong> the donations on which you claim Gift Aid in the same year.</p>
<p>This means you need at least some Gift Aid-eligible donations with proper declarations to support your GASDS claim. If your Gift Aid claim falls, so does your GASDS ceiling.</p>

<p>The direction matters: it is GASDS limited by Gift Aid, not the other way around. A charity that collects £8,000 in bucket donations but only has £50 of Gift Aid-eligible donations that year is limited to a GASDS claim on £500 (10 times £50).</p>
<p>Note: for donations collected on or after 6 April 2017, your charity does not need a prior Gift Aid track record to use GASDS. You just need to be making Gift Aid claims in the same year.</p>

<h2>Community buildings and connected charities</h2>
<p>Where qualifying events take place in a community building, additional £8,000 GASDS allowances can become available to connected charities or for particular buildings. The conditions include requirements around the number of events (at least 6 per year) and the number of people attending (at least 10 per event).</p>
<p>The community buildings rules add significant complexity and the sub-rules interact in ways that are easy to misread. The right approach is always to work through the HMRC guidance page directly: <a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claiming-a-top-up-payment-on-small-charitable-donations">gov.uk/guidance/claiming-a-top-up-payment-on-small-charitable-donations</a>. Do not try to estimate or apportion the allowances without reading that guidance carefully for your exact situation.</p>
<p>If the connected-charity or community-buildings position is not straightforward for your organisation, this is a good point to take professional advice. See the <a href="/services/gift-aid">Gift Aid service page</a> for how we help charities with GASDS and Gift Aid claims.</p>

<h2>How to claim and the 2-year deadline</h2>
<p>GASDS claims are submitted through HMRC's Charities Online service (the same portal used for Gift Aid). You can include GASDS alongside a Gift Aid claim or submit it separately.</p>
<p>The critical constraint is the <strong>2-year deadline</strong>: claims must be made within 2 years of the end of the tax year in which the donations were collected. Miss the window and the top-up is lost.</p>
<p>For example, donations collected in the 2023/24 tax year (ending 5 April 2024) must be claimed by 5 April 2026. Charities that run annual Gift Aid claims often bundle GASDS in the same submission, which is good practice and avoids the risk of letting a year drop off.</p>
<p>Steps to prepare a claim:</p>
<ol>
  <li>Total your qualifying cash and contactless donations for the tax year (each £30 or less).</li>
  <li>Confirm your Gift Aid donations for the same year. Your GASDS base cannot exceed 10 times that figure.</li>
  <li>Apply the lower of: your actual qualifying total, the 10x matching ceiling, and the £8,000 scheme cap.</li>
  <li>Submit via Charities Online. The 25% top-up is paid directly to the charity.</li>
</ol>

<p>The two schemes complement each other. Where a donor has given a declaration, claim Gift Aid in the usual way (there is no upper limit on Gift Aid claims). Reserve GASDS for the donations where a declaration was not practical. Together, they maximise what your charity recovers from HMRC on donations from UK taxpayers.</p>

<h2>Common failure modes</h2>
<p>These are the mistakes that most often result in lost GASDS claims or HMRC repayment demands:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Letting the 2-year window close.</strong> The deadline is firm. A charity that has never claimed GASDS but has been collecting bucket donations for several years can only recover the two most recent tax years. Earlier years are gone.</li>
  <li><strong>No Gift Aid claims in the same year.</strong> If you do not make any Gift Aid claims in a tax year, the matching rule leaves your GASDS ceiling at zero. You cannot claim GASDS that year.</li>
  <li><strong>Including donations over £30.</strong> A single contactless donation of £32 does not qualify. Amounts above £30 must be excluded from the GASDS total, not rounded down.</li>
  <li><strong>Including non-qualifying payment types.</strong> Online donations, bank transfers, cheques and direct debits do not qualify even if the donor did not provide a declaration. Only cash and contactless count.</li>
  <li><strong>Swapping the matching rule figures.</strong> The rule is GASDS base limited by 10 times the Gift Aid donation total, not the other way around. Check which direction your records support before you prepare the claim.</li>
  <li><strong>Claiming GASDS without HMRC recognition.</strong> Your charity must be HMRC-recognised for Gift Aid before it can use GASDS. Charity Commission registration alone is not sufficient (see <a href="https://www.gov.uk/charities-and-tax">gov.uk/charities-and-tax</a>).</li>
</ul>
