<h2 id="the-two-routes">The two routes from 1 April 2024</h2>
<p>From accounting periods starting on or after 1 April 2024, there is no longer a separate SME scheme. All companies claim through one of two routes:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Merged RDEC (above-the-line credit):</strong> a 20% expenditure credit available to most companies. The credit is taxable, so after corporation tax at 25% the net benefit is 20% x 75% = <strong>15p per £1</strong> of qualifying spend.</li>
  <li><strong>ERIS (Enhanced R&D Intensive Support):</strong> a more generous route for R&D-intensive loss-making SMEs. The key test is that qualifying R&D expenditure is at least <strong>30%</strong> of total company expenditure (reduced from 40% from 1 April 2024). The ERIS payable credit is approximately 26.97p per £1 of qualifying spend (1.86 x 14.5%), and it is payable even when there is no corporation tax liability. It is NOT subject to a corporation tax haircut.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="intensity-test">The 30% intensity test</h2>
<p>Divide qualifying R&D spend by total company expenditure. If the ratio is 30% or above, and the company is loss-making, ERIS applies. If it is below 30%, merged RDEC applies.</p>
<p>At the model defaults (£500,000 total expenditure, £135,000 qualifying R&D): intensity = 27%. Below the 30% threshold, so merged RDEC applies.</p>

<h2 id="what-qualifies">What qualifies as R&D expenditure</h2>
<p>Qualifying expenditure includes:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Staff costs:</strong> employment costs of employees working on the project (100% qualifies). This is usually the largest category.</li>
  <li><strong>Consumables and materials:</strong> items transformed or depleted in the R&D process (100% qualifies).</li>
  <li><strong>Software:</strong> software purchased or licenced specifically for the R&D project (100% qualifies).</li>
  <li><strong>Externally provided workers (EPW):</strong> agency workers. Up to 65% of the cost qualifies.</li>
  <li><strong>Subcontractors:</strong> unconnected subcontractors. Only 65% of the cost qualifies. Connected-party subcontractors: 100% qualifies but with additional conditions.</li>
  <li><strong>Clinical trial participants:</strong> costs of paying volunteers in clinical trials.</li>
</ul>
<p>Capital expenditure is NOT in the R&D credit calculation (it may get separate R&D allowances under different rules).</p>

<h2 id="the-paye-cap">The PAYE cap</h2>
<p>The RDEC credit is capped at 3 times the PAYE and NIC costs of employees engaged in R&D. This cap rarely applies to genuine R&D-intensive businesses but can bite if you use many contractors or subcontractors relative to employees. ERIS has the same cap principle.</p>

<h2 id="how-to-claim">How to claim</h2>
<p>R&D tax relief is claimed through the company's corporation tax return (CT600). You must submit an Additional Information Form (AIF) to HMRC before filing the CT600 for any accounting period where a claim is made. The AIF asks for a technical narrative (the project description, the scientific or technological advance sought, the uncertainties overcome) and a financial breakdown.</p>
<p>HMRC has increased enquiry activity in this area. Claims should be well-documented and prepared by someone familiar with the qualifying criteria before submission.</p>

<h2 id="what-the-model-does-not-capture">What the model does not capture</h2>
<p>The estimator gives a directional figure. It does not capture: the loss position (ERIS requires a loss-making position in the period); the PAYE cap; grants received (which reduce qualifying expenditure); connected-party rules; or the quality of the technical narrative. A specialist scopes a real claim before you submit.</p>
