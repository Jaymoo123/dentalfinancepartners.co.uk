<p>If you are running a limited company in the UK, you need an accountant. The question is not whether to hire one, but how much it will cost and what you get for your money.</p>

<p>The short answer: <strong>limited company accountant cost</strong> in 2025/26 typically ranges from £60 to £300 per month for a standard service, with annual fees for basic compliance starting around £500 and full-service packages reaching £3,000+ per year.</p>

<p>But that range is wide for a reason. What you pay depends on your turnover, transaction volume, industry, and the complexity of your affairs. A freelance consultant in Shoreditch turning over £45,000 pays very different fees to a 10-employee manufacturing company in Birmingham turning over £1.2 million.</p>

<p>This guide breaks down exactly what drives <strong>accountant costs for limited company</strong> structures, what each price tier includes, and how to decide what you actually need.</p>

<h2>What Determines the Cost of a Limited Company Accountant?</h2>

<p>Accountants do not charge a flat rate for every limited company. The price reflects the time and expertise required to handle your specific situation. Here are the main factors that push fees up or down.</p>

<h3>Your turnover and transaction volume</h3>
<p>This is the single biggest driver of cost. A company turning over £40,000 with 20 transactions a month takes far less time to process than one turning over £400,000 with 200 transactions. More transactions mean more bookkeeping, more reconciliations, and more time preparing accounts.</p>

<p>Most firms tier their pricing by turnover bands. Typical brackets look like this:</p>

<ul>
<li>Turnover up to £50,000: £60 to £100 per month</li>
<li>Turnover £50,000 to £150,000: £100 to £175 per month</li>
<li>Turnover £150,000 to £500,000: £175 to £250 per month</li>
<li>Turnover £500,000+: £250 to £400+ per month</li>
</ul>

<h3>How many employees you have</h3>
<p>If you are a single director with no employees, payroll is straightforward. You process one salary, one RTI submission, and that is it. Add a handful of staff and the payroll work multiplies. Each employee means a contract, a tax code check, monthly RTI submissions, pension enrolment, and a year-end P60.</p>

<p>Most accountants include payroll for one or two directors in their base fee. Additional employees usually add £5 to £15 per person per month.</p>

<h3>Your transaction count and bookkeeping needs</h3>
<p>Some accountants include full bookkeeping in their monthly fee. Others charge extra for it or expect you to do it yourself using software like Xero or FreeAgent. If you send your accountant a shoebox of receipts at year-end, expect to pay more than if you use cloud software and reconcile your bank feed monthly.</p>

<h3>VAT complexity</h3>
<p>Flat rate VAT is simple. Standard VAT with partial exemption or capital goods scheme adjustments is not. If you import goods, deal with reverse charge, or file monthly returns, the accountant cost for limited company structures with VAT goes up.</p>

<h3>Specialist areas: R&D, CIS, property, international</h3>
<p>If you claim R&D tax credits, work in construction under CIS, hold property in your company, or have international transactions, expect a premium. These areas require specialist knowledge and often involve additional forms and HMRC enquiries.</p>

<h2>What Do Different Price Tiers Actually Include?</h2>

<p>Let us be specific about what you get at each level. The descriptions below reflect what a typical qualified firm like Holloway Davies would include, though exact scope varies between firms.</p>

<h3>Basic compliance package: £500 to £900 per year</h3>
<p>This is the entry level. It covers the statutory minimum: preparing your annual accounts, filing your corporation tax return (CT600), and submitting your confirmation statement to Companies House. You handle your own bookkeeping and payroll.</p>

<p>This suits a contractor or sole director with simple affairs, low transaction volumes, and no employees. You will likely use software like FreeAgent or Xero yourself, and the accountant reviews the year-end figures.</p>

<h3>Standard monthly package: £1,200 to £2,400 per year (£100 to £200 per month)</h3>
<p>This is the most common tier for growing limited companies. It typically includes:</p>

<ul>
<li>Monthly or quarterly bookkeeping (you provide bank statements or use cloud software)</li>
<li>Management accounts (a look at your profit position mid-year)</li>
<li>Payroll for up to two directors</li>
<li>VAT returns (if registered)</li>
<li>Annual accounts and corporation tax return</li>
<li>Confirmation statement filing</li>
<li>Ongoing tax planning advice throughout the year</li>
</ul>

<p>This level works well for a small business in Digbeth, Birmingham turning over £80,000 to £150,000 with one or two staff and standard VAT.</p>

<h3>Full-service package: £2,400 to £4,000+ per year (£200 to £350+ per month)</h3>
<p>At this level, the accountant handles everything. Bookkeeping, payroll, VAT, management accounts, tax planning, HMRC enquiries, and advisory work. You get a dedicated point of contact who understands your business and proactively suggests tax-efficient strategies.</p>

<p>This suits companies turning over £200,000+ with multiple employees, complex VAT, or specialist needs like R&D credits. It also suits directors who want to focus on running the business and not think about compliance.</p>

<h2>Limited Company Accountant Cost by Business Type</h2>

<p>To make this concrete, here are typical annual fees for different business profiles in 2025/26.</p>

<h3>Freelance consultant or contractor (single director, no staff)</h3>
<p>Turnover £50,000 to £80,000. Low transaction volume. No VAT. Uses FreeAgent for bookkeeping. Annual fee: £600 to £900. Monthly option: £60 to £80 per month.</p>

<h3>Ecommerce business (director plus one employee)</h3>
<p>Turnover £120,000. Standard VAT. 40 to 60 transactions per month. Uses Xero with Dext for receipt capture. Annual fee: £1,500 to £2,000. Monthly: £130 to £170.</p>

<h3>Construction company (director, 3 staff, CIS)</h3>
<p>Turnover £250,000. CIS deductions, standard VAT, monthly payroll for 4 people. Requires specialist CIS knowledge. Annual fee: £2,400 to £3,200. Monthly: £200 to £270.</p>

<h3>Tech startup claiming R&D credits</h3>
<p>Turnover £180,000. Loss-making or low-profit. Claiming R&D tax credits under the merged scheme. Complex cost allocation. Annual fee: £2,800 to £4,000. Monthly: £230 to £350.</p>

<h2>What Is NOT Included in Standard Fees</h2>

<p>Knowing what is excluded is as important as knowing what is included. Common extras include:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Self assessment tax returns</strong> for directors and shareholders: typically £150 to £300 per return</li>
<li><strong>Dividend vouchers and minutes</strong>: some firms include these, others charge £50 to £100 per year</li>
<li><strong>HMRC enquiry work</strong>: if HMRC opens an investigation, this is usually charged separately at an hourly rate of £150 to £300</li>
<li><strong>Company secretarial work</strong>: changes to share structure, director appointments, or company name changes</li>
<li><strong>Business advice</strong> beyond tax compliance: strategic planning, exit preparation, or raising finance</li>
</ul>

<p>Always ask for a full scope of services in writing before you sign up. A cheap monthly fee that excludes everything you actually need is not a bargain.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Accountant for Your Limited Company</h2>

<p>Price matters, but it is not the only factor. A £50 per month accountant who misses your corporation tax deadline or files incorrect figures will cost you far more in penalties and stress than a £150 per month accountant who gets it right.</p>

<p>Here is what to look for:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Qualification</strong>: Are they ACCA or AAT qualified? Anyone can call themselves an accountant. Qualified firms carry professional indemnity insurance and follow ethical standards.</li>
<li><strong>Industry experience</strong>: Do they work with businesses like yours? A firm that specialises in contractors may not be the best fit for a retail business with stock and VAT.</li>
<li><strong>Software compatibility</strong>: Do they use Xero, FreeAgent, or QuickBooks? If you already use a platform, make sure they work with it.</li>
<li><strong>Communication</strong>: Do they respond to emails within 24 hours? Do you get a named contact or a shared inbox?</li>
<li><strong>Fixed fees vs hourly billing</strong>: Fixed monthly fees give you certainty. Hourly billing creates unpredictable costs.</li>
</ul>

<p>We recommend you speak to at least two or three firms before deciding. Ask for a proposal that spells out exactly what is included and what costs extra.</p>

<h2>Can You Reduce the Cost of a Limited Company Accountant?</h2>

<p>Yes, in several ways. But each trade-off has consequences.</p>

<p><strong>Do your own bookkeeping.</strong> If you use cloud accounting software and keep your records up to date, you reduce the time your accountant spends on data entry. This can lower your monthly fee by £30 to £60.</p>

<p><strong>Use software your accountant recommends.</strong> Most firms have preferred platforms (FreeAgent for contractors, Xero for growing businesses). Using their recommended software means fewer compatibility issues and less time spent converting data.</p>

<p><strong>Keep your affairs simple.</strong> Avoid complex share structures, multiple director loan accounts, or overseas transactions unless you genuinely need them. Every complication adds time and cost.</p>

<p><strong>Pay annually instead of monthly.</strong> Some firms offer a discount of 5% to 10% if you pay the full year upfront. Ask about this when you get a quote.</p>

<p>But do not cut corners on things that matter. Filing your accounts late costs £150 to £1,500 in Companies House penalties alone. A missed corporation tax deadline triggers interest and surcharges. A cheap accountant who misses deadlines is not cheap at all.</p>

<h2>What About Free or Very Cheap Options?</h2>

<p>You will see accountants advertising packages from £30 to £50 per month. Some are legitimate start-ups building a client base. Others are not worth the risk.</p>

<p>At that price point, the accountant is likely processing high volumes with minimal personal service. You may get a standard set of accounts with no tax planning, no proactive advice, and no one to call when something goes wrong. If HMRC opens an enquiry, you may be charged separately at a high hourly rate.</p>

<p>There is also the question of who actually does the work. Some firms outsource preparation to offshore teams with limited UK tax knowledge. The quality varies enormously.</p>

<p>If your affairs are genuinely simple (single director, no staff, no VAT, low turnover), a basic compliance package at £60 to £80 per month is realistic. Below that, read the small print carefully.</p>

<h2>Limited Company Accountant Cost: The Bottom Line</h2>

<p>For 2025/26, a realistic budget for a good limited company accountant is:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Basic compliance</strong>: £500 to £900 per year (£40 to £75 per month)</li>
<li><strong>Standard service with bookkeeping and payroll</strong>: £1,200 to £2,400 per year (£100 to £200 per month)</li>
<li><strong>Full-service with tax planning and advisory</strong>: £2,400 to £4,000+ per year (£200 to £350+ per month)</li>
</ul>

<p>The <strong>limited company accountancy costs</strong> you actually pay depend on your specific circumstances. A firm that understands your sector, communicates clearly, and files everything on time is worth the fee. One that undercuts on price but delivers poor service is a false economy.</p>
