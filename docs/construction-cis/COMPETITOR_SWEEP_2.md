## 4. Location-Page Blueprint (from sweep_3 analysis of ranking pages)

### What the SERP evidence proves

Dedicated city + CIS landing pages rank in organic positions 2-6 for every local query examined. National firms without city CIS pages (RIFT, DNS) do NOT rank for "[city] CIS accountant" despite domain authority. Directories capture some positions but deliver aggregated traffic, not direct leads.

### Specification: CIS Accountants in [City] — 2,000-2,500 words

**URL pattern:** `/cis-accountants/[city]/`

**Structure:**

1. **Local market context (200-300 words — must be CUSTOM per city, cannot be templated)**
   - Name 3-5 major live construction projects with scale (investment value, floor area, completion date)
   - Name Tier 1 contractors active on those projects (BAM, Vinci, Wates, Caddick, GMI etc.)
   - Name the supply chain geography (motorway corridor, industrial zones local subbies operate from)
   - Dominant trades for that market (Sheffield = steel/structural; Manchester = fit-out, MediaCityUK hotel pipeline)
   - Source: Glenigan, Barbour ABI press releases, local planning portals

2. **CIS services in [City] (400-500 words)**
   - Subcontractor self-assessment + CIS refund claim
   - CIS registration and verification
   - Monthly CIS return preparation (nil returns from April 2026)
   - VAT reverse charge compliance
   - Gross Payment Status application (post-FA 2026 regime)
   - Year-end accounts for construction sole traders and limited companies
   - CIS for limited companies (EPS reclaim, distinct from sole trader)
   - One sentence of local project flavour per service bullet

3. **CIS refund worked example for [City] (200-250 words)**
   - Use realistic local trade and earnings (e.g. "Leeds groundworker, £42,000 gross")
   - Show: gross income, 20% deducted (£X), allowable expenses (travel/tools/PPE), taxable profit, actual tax owed, refund due
   - Include back-years angle with combined 4-year figure
   - Link to refund estimator calculator
   - This section differentiates: no ranking competitor includes a city-specific worked example

4. **Trade-specific notes for [City] market (150-200 words)**
   - Which trades are most active locally
   - Any local compliance patterns (high proportion of unverified 30% deductions in informal supply chains)
   - CITB levy threshold (£135k turnover) for growing subbies

5. **FAQ (8-10 questions, 300 words)**
   Must include: do I need to be based in [City], how much is the average refund, can I claim 4 years back, multiple contractors, CIS + self-assessment interaction, sole trader vs limited company, GPS eligibility, how long does a refund take

6. **Local proof signals**
   - Physical address (or nearest office) in or near the city
   - Named adviser (photo + bio where brand allows — note: Trade Tax Specialists is a faceless brand per HP §12.A; format as "our [City]-area specialist" or similar)
   - Google reviews count + rating
   - Named client testimonial (trade + city + outcome + £ amount)

**Schema:** LocalBusiness + FAQPage + Review aggregate markup

**Location priority order (from SERP competition analysis):**
- Highest competition (strongest local pages needed): London, Manchester, Leeds, Birmingham
- Medium competition (good opportunity): Sheffield, Bristol, Glasgow, Edinburgh, Liverpool, Nottingham
- Lower competition (easier wins): Hull, Bradford, Derby, Leicester, Coventry, Wolverhampton, Sunderland, Middlesbrough, Preston

## 6. April 2026 First-Mover Note

Two topics where we face almost no accountant-brand competition:

1. **GPS anti-fraud changes (FA 2026 ss.62A/62B):** Only BDO and TaxAssist have published substantively on the "knew or should have known" standard, the 5-year reapplication ban and the knowledge-based penalty of 20% of payment/sums returned on the payer or return-maker. We already have `cis-april-2026-rule-changes` and `cis-gross-payment-status-guide`; wave-3 posts that touch GPS must carry the §3 corrections per house_positions.md.

2. **Nil return reinstatement (April 2026):** The contractor nil-return obligation (removed 2015, reinstated 6 April 2026) is barely covered. A CIS deadline calendar that makes this concrete would own the query.

## 8. HP Correction Flag (wave-3 mandatory)

The following error was found in the live content inventory while compiling this sweep. It contradicts house_positions.md §3 and must be corrected before wave-3 content cites the demolition contractors trade page.

**File:** `construction-cis/web/src/data/trade-types.ts`
**Trade page:** `demolition-contractors`
**Error location:** `challenges[0].body` and `faqs[1].answer`
**Error text:** "Directors face personal liability of up to 30% of tax lost" and "individual directors face personal penalties of up to 30% of tax HMRC considers lost"
**Correct position (HP §3):** FA 2004 ss.62A/62B impose a penalty of **20% of the payment / 20% of the sums the return treats as paid**, on the **payer or return-maker**. Director personal liability under ss.62A/62B is NOT a fixed percentage (30% is wrong and does not appear in either section). Where a company's deliberate behaviour produces penalties, HMRC may pursue **officers personally under the existing officer-liability rules** — this should be stated in general terms without a fixed percentage. The "30% of tax lost" framing was the pre-correction locked text, caught by wave-2 Opus QA and corrected in house_positions.md on 2026-06-12. The trade-types.ts file has NOT been updated to reflect the correction.

**Required fix (manager action, not sub-agent):** Edit `challenges[0].body` and `faqs[1].answer` in the `demolition-contractors` entry to remove "30% of tax lost" / "30% of tax HMRC considers lost" and replace with the correct formulation: "Under FA 2004 ss.62A/62B (inserted by Finance Act 2026), a person who makes a payment knowing of deliberate CIS failures in the supply chain faces a penalty of 20% of the payment. HMRC may also pursue company officers personally under the officer-liability rules."
