## CRITICAL drift watchpoints (Wave 9 prep surfaced 51 catches — 12 directly Bucket B; read all before writing)

1. **CTA 2010 Part 8ZB ends at s.356OT (20 sections, ss.356OA-356OT).** ITA 2007 Part 9A ends at s.517U (21 sections incl. s.517M private-residences carve-out which has no corporate-side equivalent).
2. **Four conditions A-D at s.356OB(4)-(7) — disjunctive** ("main purpose, or one of the main purposes"): A acquisition main-purpose; B derived-property acquisition main-purpose; C trading stock (deterministic); D development main-purpose. Multiple main purposes can coexist; only one needs to be profit-from-disposal.
3. **s.356OB(2) is chargeable-person rule; s.356OB(8) is six-month time-period definition** (Stage 2b cite refinement). NOT both in s.356OB(2) as earlier framing suggested. Same offset s.517B(2)/(8) for individuals.
4. **s.356OD is DISTINCT THREE-CONDITION framework** (not a parallel restatement of Conditions A-D): (1) 50% UK-land derivation; (2) arrangement participation; (3) main-purpose on the arrangement to deal/develop land + realise profit. Verify verbatim at write time.
5. **s.356OH / s.517H anti-fragmentation** attributes coordinated activities by associated persons to the chargeable person. Defeats the "split developer + seller + profit-recipient" planning pattern.
6. **CTA 2009 Part 14 ends at s.1175 (NOT s.1179).** Section range 1143-1175.
7. **LRR rate is 100% standard + 50% additional (= 150% total) per s.1149(8).** Sessions should use additive framing not "150% multiplicative" to surface interactions with other reliefs correctly.
8. **LRR polluter exclusion "relevant connection" is defined at s.1178** (NOT s.1150(3)). Three-pathway test: (a) connected at contamination act/omission; (b) connected at major-interest acquisition; (c) connected at remediation.
9. **LRR Conditions A-F (SIX) at s.1144** — including Condition E ("not subsidised") and Condition F ("not landfill tax"). Easily-missed exclusions for landlord-developer brownfield projects with local authority grants.
10. **LRR tax credit rate is 16% of qualifying loss per s.1154** — economically WORSE than carry-forward at marginal CT rate; sessions writing planning advice must caveat.
11. **RPDT IS IN FORCE per FA 2022 Part 2 ss.31-53.** Earlier HP framing of "repealed by FA 2024 s.81" was WRONG (Stage 2b correction — FA 2024 s.81 returns 404 on legislation.gov.uk; FA 2022 Part 2 verified "no outstanding effects" on 2026-05-25). 4% surcharge above £25m group allowance threshold. B3 + B10 must work into developer-CT cost calculation where group profits exceed £25m.
12. **s.18N(3) "let commercially" exclusion is EXPANSIVE** (Stage 1b correction) — defeats commercial-letting if let to (a) connected person; (b) spouse/civil partner of connected person; (c) relative of connected person; (d) spouse/civil partner of relative of connected person. Sibling/parent/child/uncle/aunt tenants of a connected person all engage CIHC status.
13. **Condition C trading-stock denies s.162 incorporation relief** — landlord-developers incorporating CANNOT use s.162; must route through CTA 2010 Part 22 trade-transfer for going-concern treatment OR accept CGT s.161 deemed-disposal at MV.
