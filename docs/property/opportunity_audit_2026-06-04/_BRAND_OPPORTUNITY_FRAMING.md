# PRIMARY FRAMING — brand-breadth, not technical gaps

**This is the lens for the opportunity register.** Confirmed across GSC, a non-circular taxonomy, competitor strict-survivors, and the interim01 multi-engine sitemap mine (723 competitor sitemaps): **we are saturated on technical tax and under-indexed on the broader brand surface.** The opportunity is to spread the brand seed across customer-decision, human/relatable, general-accounting, and authority content — NOT more case-law-style technical pages.

## The four brand-seed plays (priority order)
1. **Customer/commercial (22→grow):** "how much does a property accountant cost", "do I need an accountant for my buy-to-let", "is incorporation worth it and what does it cost", "switching to a property accountant". Highest conversion intent.
2. **Human/relatable (7→grow):** "common mistakes landlords make at tax time", "X legal ways to reduce tax on rental income", "Section 24 myths", anonymised case studies. Shareable, top-of-funnel, brand warmth.
3. **General accounting (18→grow):** bookkeeping for landlords, MTD software comparisons, record-keeping, cash-flow/running-your-property-business.
4. **Authority (38→grow):** Budget/Finance-Act explainers framed for landlords, "what's changing 2026/27", annual outlooks.
