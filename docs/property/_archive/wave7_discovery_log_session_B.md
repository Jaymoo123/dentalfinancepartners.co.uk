## D-1 ADJACENT_TOPIC — HMRC v Tooth [2021] UKSC 17 deliberate-test is doctrinally wider than Sch 24
**Surfaced at:** 2026-05-24, B8 step 6 (planning write)
**Detail:** Tooth is the controlling Supreme Court authority on what "deliberate" means in the tax-penalty context. Originally read into Sch 24 para 3 (the deliberate behaviour band), the case has been applied progressively wider — most importantly into TMA 1970 s.29(5) (deliberate-behaviour competent-officer test for discovery assessments) and into the deliberate limb of Sch 41 (failure-to-notify). The deliberate-test as articulated in Tooth (subjective knowing-falsity) is the single most important piece of case law spanning Bucket B's Sch 24 / s.29 discovery / Sch 41 / reasonable-excuse adjacency.
**Recommendation:** B1 (discovery limits) and B9 (reasonable excuse case law) should both cite Tooth as the cross-regime deliberate-test authority. Consider a future wave bucket on "the deliberate-test family" pulling Tooth + Hicks + adjacent doctrine into a single hub page; would naturally cluster around the §27.1-§27.3 + B1/B8/B9 pages once Wave 7 ships.
