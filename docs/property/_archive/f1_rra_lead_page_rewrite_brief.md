## 2. What is still wrong or stale after the citation back-patch

The page was written in the pre-enactment voice. Mechanical citation correction does not fix the factual frame, which is still that of a Bill in passage. Items the rewrite must fix:

1. Predicted-effect framings still everywhere. Phrases like "may lead to longer void periods", "could significantly affect net rental yields", "uncertainty created by the new tenancy regime", "many landlords are considering portfolio disposals". The Act is in force from 1 May 2026; these need to become present-tense observations grounded in the actual statutory framework, not predictions.
2. "From May 2026" as a future date. As of today (2026-05-23), the Section 21 abolition, periodic-tenancy default, Section 13 rent procedure, advance-rent ban, bidding-wars ban, pet rights, and financial penalty regime are already in force (SI 2026/421 reg.2 and reg.3 brought them in on 1 May 2026, per section 20.12 of house_positions.md). "From May 2026" framings should become "in force since 1 May 2026" or "since the 1 May 2026 commencement".
3. "Landlords considering incorporation should act before May 2026 if possible." That window has closed; this advice is stale and slightly misleading. Rewrite to current-state incorporation analysis with the post-RRA regulatory backdrop.
4. "Crystallising gains before May 2026 allows landlords to avoid..." Same problem; the rewrite should reframe to current-state CGT planning (e.g., the impact of the 12-month re-letting restriction on landlord-sale possession timing per section 20.2 / 20.11).
5. Section 21 framing is still vague. The page says Section 21 is abolished without distinguishing transitional treatment for pre-1-May-2026 Section 21 notices, which section 20.9 covers. The rewrite should give one paragraph on the transitional saving for already-served notices.
6. Missing post-enactment tax angles entirely. No mention of:
   - PRS Database registration fees and Ombudsman scheme subscription as deductible business expenses (section 20.5, 20.11).
   - 12-month landlord-sale re-letting restriction and its CGT timing interaction with the NRCGT 60-day window (section 20.2 last bullet, 20.11 third bullet).
   - Decent Homes Standard repair vs improvement classification for revenue-vs-capital tax treatment (section 20.4, 20.11).
   - Advance-rent prohibition removing the historical "12 months upfront" pattern that some landlords used as a working-capital lever (section 20.8).
   - Bidding-wars or asking-rent ceiling locking advertised-rent as the statutory cap until the first Section 13 review (section 20.8, 20.6).
   - Pet damage insurance now NOT permitted as a tenant-borne charge per the F-11 section 20.7 correction; landlord pet-damage uplift on the building or contents policy remains deductible.
7. "Rent review restrictions" detail is vague. The page mentions "once per year" but does not name the Section 13 mechanism, the 2-month notice, the tribunal challenge route, or the procedural protection that the tribunal cannot now set a rent above the landlord's proposed amount (section 20.6).
8. Section 24 and 22/42/47% framing is fine, but the rewrite should preserve and update with current-state figures (the page already lands the 22/42/47 from April 2027 correctly).
9. MTD interaction paragraph still works post-1-May-2026 commencement; the MTD ITSA 6 April 2026 threshold and tapering (50k to 30k to 20k) are correct per house positions sections 3 and 19.
10. No legislation.gov.uk citation. A page about the tax implications of an enacted Act should link to https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/26/contents at least once.
