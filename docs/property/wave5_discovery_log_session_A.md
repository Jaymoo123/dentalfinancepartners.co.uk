## D-28 | 2026-05-23 PM | SESSION_RETROSPECTIVE | Session A close-out

Six observations from the Session A retrospective across A1-A10:

1. **§16.35 per-write verification is non-negotiable on VAT briefs.** Two of the ten briefs in Bucket A carried significant statutory mis-citations (F-8 Sch 4A para 9 → Sch 6 para 9 on A8; F-9 cladding-remediation zero-rate that does not exist on A9). Both caught at write time by direct verification against legislation.gov.uk and Notice 708. Without §16.35 the wrong information would have shipped on both pages.

2. **VAT bucket is statute-led + Notice-led + Brief-led + case-law-led.** No single authority covers the whole bucket; each page draws from a different combination of VATA 1994 schedules, VAT Regulations 1995, HMRC Notices (708, 709/3, 742, 742A, 700, 719), HMRC Internal Manuals (VATSC, VATLAND, VIT, VATTOS, VCONST, PE), and CJEU/UT case law (Card Protection Plan, MEO, Vodafone Portugal, Sonder Europe).

3. **Cross-link density matters more in the umbrella page than in depth pages.** A10 has 12 internal forward-links; A1-A9 average 4-5. The pattern reflects A10's hub role and the depth pages' more focused topical narrowness.

4. **The cannibalisation discipline held throughout.** A10 was the highest cannibal-risk page (against existing landlord-vat-registration), and the explicit per-stream-classification vs operational-registration differentiation made it work without overlap.

5. **Anti-templating discipline held.** Ten VAT pages each have a distinct H2 spine. The brief authored differentiator spines were generally usable as-is; A10 added two extra H2s (Three Worked Mini-Scenarios + Most-Common Landlord VAT Mistakes) to clear the 3,000-word floor that the brief's spine alone did not reach.

6. **Worked examples are the single most-effective device on operational mechanic pages.** A2 (CGS), A3 (PE), A4 (mixed-use), A7 (reg 111), A8 (28-day), A9 (BSA 2022), A10 (three scenarios) all carry worked examples with named anonymised entities and specific figures. A1 (OTT mechanic) and A6 (conversion) carry worked figures within the FAQs. The pattern matches the depth-of-mechanic gradient.
