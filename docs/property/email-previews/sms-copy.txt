PROPERTY TAX PARTNERS, LEAD NURTURE SMS COPY SHEET
Updated 2026-07-03. Plain text reference for the two core SMS touches.
Source of truth for send logic: Property/web/src/config/lead-nurture.ts


1. FIRST SMS (key: t0_sms, sends at T0 alongside the T0 email)
   STATUS: APPROVED, KEEP AS IS. Do not reword without owner sign-off.

   Hi {firstName}, it's Property Tax Partners. Thanks for your enquiry about your property tax. Reply YES and one of our specialists will call you. Reply STOP to opt out.

   Notes:
   . 156 characters before the name is inserted, so it renders as two
     segments for most names (161 with a 5 character name). Approved in
     this form, leave it alone.
   . Leads with reply YES, opt out sits last, per owner direction
     (commit 7df4645c).


2. NEW POST-YES ACKNOWLEDGMENT SMS (sends immediately when the lead replies YES)
   STATUS: PROPOSED, reworded per owner brief of a warm confirmation.

   Great, thank you {firstName}. One of our specialists will call you shortly. Nothing to prepare. Speak soon.

   Nameless fallback (if no first name is held, drop the name and comma):

   Great, thank you. One of our specialists will call you shortly. Nothing to prepare. Speak soon.

   Notes:
   . 96 characters before the name is inserted (101 with a 5 character
     name), single segment for any realistic name. Nameless fallback is
     95 characters.
   . No dashes, no links, no exclamation marks.
   . No STOP line here by design: the lead has just opted in by replying
     YES, and the opt out instruction was already given in the first SMS.
   . "Shortly" plus "Speak soon" avoids the "soon ... then" clash in the
     owner's draft while keeping the same warm, brief tone. "Nothing to
     prepare" mirrors the email copy and lowers call anxiety.
