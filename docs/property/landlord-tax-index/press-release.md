# Press summary: UK landlord limited-company incorporations up roughly 3.5x in a decade

*A press-ready data summary. Faceless by design: attribute to "Property Tax

## The headline

The number of new UK companies incorporated to hold or let residential property
has risen roughly **3.5 times in a decade**. Companies registered under SIC code
68209 ("other letting and operating of own or leased real estate"), the code most
buy-to-let companies use, climbed from **19,688 in 2016 to 68,725 in 2025**, an
increase of about **249%**.

Across all four real-estate SIC codes, **94,367 property companies** were
incorporated in the 12 months to March 2026 (deduplicated), up from 33,007 in the
full year 2016.

## Key findings

- **New landlord companies (SIC 68209): 19,688 (2016) → 68,725 (2025), +249%.**
- **Trailing 12 months (to March 2026): 68,878** new SIC-68209 companies and
  **94,367** property companies across all four codes.
- The single busiest month on record in the series was **September 2025**, with
  6,632 new SIC-68209 companies.
- The climb tracks the phase-in of the **Section 24** restriction on mortgage
  interest relief (from 2017). Individual landlords are taxed on rental income
  while receiving only a basic-rate (20%) credit for finance costs; companies are
  not subject to Section 24 and deduct mortgage interest in full. That gap is the
  widely cited driver of incorporation.
- It coincides with a flat-to-rising housing market: the average UK house price
  was **£268,132 in March 2026** (HM Land Registry). Larger portfolios mean
  larger capital gains and stamp duty exposure, which feeds the incorporation
  decision.

## Why it is credible

- Counts are **gross** (dissolved companies stay on the Companies House register),
  so there is no survivorship distortion.
- The most recent two months are treated as **provisional** and excluded from all
  headline figures because of Companies House indexing lag.
- Every figure comes from **official open data** (Companies House Advanced Search
  API; HM Land Registry UK House Price Index) and the full method and a CSV are
  published alongside.
