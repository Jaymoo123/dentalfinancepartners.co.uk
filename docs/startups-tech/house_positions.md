## A. R&D tax relief

**1. The merged R&D scheme gives a 20% expenditure credit, above the line and taxable.**
For accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024 there is one R&D
expenditure credit at 20%. It is an above-the-line, taxable credit and it replaces
both the old SME super-deduction and the separate RDEC for those periods.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-large-companies (verified 2026-07-14).

**2. Loss-making R&D-intensive SMEs keep the SME route under ERIS (86% deduction, 14.5% payable credit).**
Enhanced R&D intensive support (ERIS) is available to a loss-making SME whose
qualifying R&D spend is at least 30% of its total expenditure. Such a company
keeps the SME route: an 86% additional deduction plus a payable credit at 14.5%.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-small-and-medium-sized-enterprises (verified 2026-07-14).

**3. First-time claimants must notify HMRC within 6 months of the period end.**
A claim notification must reach HMRC within 6 months of the end of the accounting
period for first-time claimants (or anyone who has not claimed in the prior three
years). Miss the window and the claim is invalid.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tell-hmrc-that-youre-planning-to-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief (verified 2026-07-14).

**4. The Additional Information Form is mandatory before the CT600 claim.**
A detailed Additional Information Form (AIF) must be submitted before the R&D
claim on the CT600. Claims filed without a valid AIF are removed by HMRC.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-detailed-information-before-you-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief (verified 2026-07-14).

**5. R&D advance assurance exists for small first-time claimants, as trust-building content, not a default recommendation.**
HMRC offers advance assurance to small first-time claimants. Treat it as a
content and trust anchor, not as a blanket recommendation.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/research-and-development-tax-relief-advance-assurance (verified 2026-07-14).

**6. Qualifying R&D must seek an advance in science or technology; routine development is not R&D.**
Qualifying R&D has to seek an advance in science or technology and resolve
scientific or technological uncertainty. Routine software development does not
qualify. This is the honest-positioning moat versus volume R&D mills.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird100000 (verified 2026-07-14).
Hub reference: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-rd-relief (verified 2026-07-14).

## B. SEIS and EIS

**7. SEIS lets a company raise up to £250,000, with gross assets no more than £350,000, fewer than 25 employees, within 3 years of trading.**
A company can raise up to £250,000 in total under the Seed Enterprise Investment
Scheme. At the time of the investment it must have gross assets of no more than
£350,000, fewer than 25 full-time-equivalent employees, and be within three years
of starting to trade.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-to-use-the-seed-enterprise-investment-scheme (verified 2026-07-14).

**8. SEIS investors get 50% income tax relief on up to £200,000 invested per tax year.**
An SEIS investor can claim 50% income tax relief on up to £200,000 invested per
tax year, with a 50% CGT reinvestment exemption on reinvested gains and a CGT-free
disposal of the shares after three years.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/seed-enterprise-investment-scheme-background (verified 2026-07-14).

**9. EIS lets a company raise up to £5m per year and £12m over its lifetime, higher for knowledge-intensive companies.**
Under the Enterprise Investment Scheme a company can raise up to £5m per year and
£12m over its lifetime across the venture-capital schemes (the lifetime figure is
higher for knowledge-intensive companies). Gross-asset and company-age tests apply.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-the-enterprise-investment-scheme (verified 2026-07-14).

**10. EIS investors get 30% income tax relief, up to £1m per year (£2m with knowledge-intensive companies), on a 3-year hold.**
An EIS investor can claim 30% income tax relief on up to £1m invested per tax
year, rising to £2m where the excess above £1m is invested in knowledge-intensive
companies. The shares must be held for at least three years to retain relief.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-tax-relief-for-investors (verified 2026-07-14).

**11. Advance assurance is HMRC pre-clearance for an SEIS, EIS or VCT round.**
Advance assurance is HMRC pre-clearance that a proposed share issue is likely to
qualify. It is the single highest-intent service query in this niche.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-advance-assurance (verified 2026-07-14).

## C. Share schemes

**12. EMI allows up to £250,000 of unexercised option value per employee and £3m per company; the company needs gross assets of £30m or less and fewer than 250 FTE.**
Enterprise Management Incentives (EMI) allow up to £250,000 of unexercised option
value per employee (measured over a rolling three-year window) and £3m of total
unexercised options across the company. The qualifying company must have gross
assets of no more than £30m, fewer than 250 full-time-equivalent employees, and
must not carry on excluded activities. Employees must meet the working-time
requirement.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/enterprise-management-incentives-emis (verified 2026-07-14).
Gross-assets test: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employee-tax-advantaged-share-scheme-user-manual/etassum52030 (verified 2026-07-14).

**13. ERS returns (including nil returns) and EMI grant notifications are due by 6 July after the tax year end.**
Employment-related-securities schemes must be registered, and an annual ERS return
(including a nil return where nothing happened) must be filed by 6 July following
the tax year end. EMI grant notification now runs on the same 6 July deadline.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-your-employment-related-securities-ers-return (verified 2026-07-14).

**14. EMI option grants should be valued in agreement with HMRC Shares and Assets Valuation.**
An option-grant valuation should be agreed with HMRC Shares and Assets Valuation
before or at grant. The standalone VAL231 form URL previously used now returns a
404 and is NOT cited here. The VAL231 form link must be re-verified at build; until
then, cite the EMI and ERS manual pages below. Do not publish a dead VAL231 link.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/enterprise-management-incentives-emis (verified 2026-07-14).
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-your-employment-related-securities-ers-return (verified 2026-07-14).

**15. CSOP is the £60,000-per-employee fallback once EMI limits or qualifying tests are breached.**
A Company Share Option Plan (CSOP) allows up to £60,000 of options per employee.
It is the fallback scale-up route once a company or an employee breaches the EMI
limits or qualifying tests.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/company-share-option-plan (verified 2026-07-14).

**16. A section 431 election is a 14-day joint election on restricted securities.**
Where founders or employees acquire restricted securities (common at funding
rounds), a section 431 joint election must be made within 14 days of acquisition
to be taxed on the unrestricted market value up front. Missing it is a classic
funded-startup trap.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-related-securities/ersm30450 (verified 2026-07-14).

**17. Growth shares and unapproved options are taxed under general ERS rules.**
Growth shares and unapproved options fall under general employment-related-
securities rules (income tax, and NIC where relevant, on the value acquired above
any amount paid). Use them in comparison content anchored to the ERS manual.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-related-securities/ersm30450 (verified 2026-07-14).

## D. Founder capital taxes

**18. Business Asset Disposal Relief is 18% from 6 April 2026, on a £1m lifetime limit; EMI shares qualify on the 2-year rule without the 5% personal-company test.**
Business Asset Disposal Relief (BADR) charges qualifying gains at 18% for disposals
on or after 6 April 2026. This is the current rate for this site. For history: the
rate was 14% for 2025/26 and 10% before that. The lifetime limit is £1m. EMI shares
qualify for BADR under the two-year holding rule without needing to meet the 5%
personal-company test that applies to other shares.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/business-asset-disposal-relief (verified 2026-07-14).

**19. CGT main rates on shares are 18% within the basic-rate band and 24% above it.**
For share disposals the main Capital Gains Tax rates are 18% within a taxpayer's
remaining basic-rate band and 24% above it (in force since 30 October 2024).
Source: https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates (verified 2026-07-14).

**20. A section 135 TCGA share-for-share exchange is not a disposal, subject to the s.137 anti-avoidance test.**
On a qualifying share-for-share exchange (group reorganisations and flips) section
135 TCGA treats there as being no disposal, so no immediate CGT arises. This is
subject to the section 137 anti-avoidance test and clearances. Scottish income tax
divergence is flagged for founder-salary content only, not for CGT or CT.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/capital-gains-manual/cg52521 (verified 2026-07-14).

## E. Corporation tax, extraction and payroll

**21. Corporation tax is 19% on profits up to £50,000 and 25% on profits of £250,000 or more, with marginal relief between; the limits divide by associated companies.**
The small profits rate of 19% applies to profits up to £50,000 and the main rate
of 25% applies to profits of £250,000 or more, with marginal relief tapering
between the two thresholds. The £50,000 and £250,000 limits are divided by the
number of associated companies, which matters for multi-entity founder groups.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates (verified 2026-07-14).
Marginal relief: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-marginal-relief (verified 2026-07-14).

**22. Dividend tax rates for 2026/27 are 10.75%, 35.75% and 39.35%, with a £500 dividend allowance.**
For 2026/27 the dividend ordinary, upper and additional rates are 10.75%, 35.75%
and 39.35% (FA 2026 s.4). The dividend allowance is £500.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/tax-on-dividends (verified 2026-07-14).

**23. Employer NIC is 15% above a £5,000 secondary threshold; the Employment Allowance is up to £10,500 but a solo-director company is excluded.**
Employer (secondary) National Insurance is charged at 15% on earnings above a
£5,000 secondary threshold. The Employment Allowance is up to £10,500, but a
company whose only employee paid above the threshold is a director is not eligible.
That solo-founder exclusion is the trap to flag on founder-salary content.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/national-insurance-rates-letters (verified 2026-07-14).
Employment Allowance: https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance (verified 2026-07-14).

**24. Pre-trading expenditure is allowable if incurred within 7 years before trade starts.**
Expenditure incurred within seven years before a trade starts, which would have
been allowable had the trade already begun, is treated as incurred on the first
day of trading. This is the answer to the classic pre-revenue founder question.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim46351 (verified 2026-07-14).

**25. Trading losses carry forward against future profits, so pre-profit startups should still file to bank them.**
Trading losses carry forward and set against future trading profits (subject to
the relevant-maximum rules at scale). A cash-burning pre-profit startup should
still file its return to bank the losses.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-calculating-and-claiming-a-loss (verified 2026-07-14).

**26. VAT registration is mandatory at £90,000 taxable turnover; for SaaS, place-of-supply rules may keep overseas B2B revenue out of the threshold.**
VAT registration is mandatory once rolling 12-month UK taxable turnover reaches
£90,000, or when a 30-day forward expectation crosses it. For SaaS, the B2B
reverse charge and place-of-supply rules mean overseas revenue may not count
toward the threshold. Verify specific supplies against Notice 741A at build.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/register-for-vat (verified 2026-07-14).

## G. Income tax and employee NIC (founder-salary content only)

These positions were added 2026-07-15 to close a calculator gap (the founder
dividend-vs-salary and EMI-vs-unapproved tools need the salary-side bands and
employee NIC to compute their comparison columns). All three figures were fetched
live at gov.uk on 2026-07-15. **rUK figures. Scottish income tax has its own bands,
so flag Scotland explicitly on founder-salary content only, never on CGT or CT.**

**30. Income tax for 2026/27 (rest of UK): personal allowance £12,570; basic rate 20% to £50,270; higher rate 40% to £125,140; additional rate 45% above £125,140.**
The personal allowance is £12,570. The basic rate of 20% applies from £12,571 to
£50,270, the higher rate of 40% from £50,271 to £125,140, and the additional rate
of 45% above £125,140. The personal allowance tapers by £1 for every £2 of income
over £100,000, reaching zero at £125,140. These are the rest-of-UK rates; Scottish
taxpayers have separate bands (flag on founder-salary content only).
Source: https://www.gov.uk/income-tax-rates (verified 2026-07-15).

**31. Employee (primary Class 1) NIC for 2026/27 is 8% between the primary threshold £12,570 and the upper earnings limit £50,270, then 2% above.**
An employee pays primary Class 1 National Insurance at 8% on earnings between the
primary threshold of £12,570 a year (£242 a week) and the upper earnings limit of
£50,270 a year (£967 a week), and 2% on earnings above the upper earnings limit.
This is the employee-side charge, separate from the employer 15% secondary charge
in position 23.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/national-insurance-rates-letters (verified 2026-07-15).
Thresholds: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2026-to-2027 (verified 2026-07-15).

**32. The Lower Earnings Limit for 2026/27 is £6,708 a year (£129 a week); a salary at or above it banks a qualifying year for the state pension even though no NIC is due until the primary threshold.**
The Lower Earnings Limit is £6,708 a year (£129 a week). A director salary set at or
above the LEL but below the primary threshold (£12,570) preserves a qualifying year
for state pension purposes while no employee NIC is payable. This is the mechanism
behind the common founder low-salary plan; the employer-NIC secondary threshold
(£5,000, position 23) and the Employment Allowance solo-director exclusion still
govern whether employer NIC arises.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2026-to-2027 (verified 2026-07-15).

These three positions were added after the launch QA pass surfaced figures the

**33. The EMI working-time requirement is at least 25 hours a week, or 75% of the employee's total working time.**
To hold qualifying EMI options an employee must spend at least 25 hours a week
working for the company or, if less, at least 75% of their total working time. State
this figure confidently; it is the settled statutory test, not an open flag.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/enterprise-management-incentives-emis (verified 2026-07-15).

**34. A company can submit its SEIS/EIS compliance statement (SEIS1/EIS1) only after trading for 4 months or, for SEIS, spending 70% of the money raised.**
The compliance statement cannot be filed immediately after the share issue. For SEIS,
the company must have been trading for at least 4 months OR have spent at least 70% of
the SEIS money raised on the qualifying activity. For EIS, the company must have carried
on the qualifying business activity for at least 4 months (no spend test). Only then can
the company file SEIS1/EIS1, after which HMRC issues the SEIS3/EIS3 certificates the
investors need. The EIS1 has a 2-year filing window (from the later of the tax-year end
of issue or the 4-month point); SEIS1 has no hard deadline once the 4-month/70% gate is met.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-to-use-the-seed-enterprise-investment-scheme (verified 2026-07-15).
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-the-enterprise-investment-scheme (verified 2026-07-15).

**35. Under the merged R&D scheme, subsidised expenditure (including grant funding) no longer reduces the claim.**
For accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024 the merged scheme removed the
subsidised-expenditure restriction entirely. Grant funding, subsidies and customer
contributions no longer cut down the qualifying R&D expenditure the way they did under
the old SME scheme, so a grant-funded project can be claimed in full under the merged
scheme (subject to not claiming the same cost twice). A heavily grant-funded, loss-making
R&D-intensive SME that the old rules pushed onto RDEC may now reach ERIS. This is the
concrete answer to the grant-interaction question; do not hedge it as unknown.
Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-rd-relief (verified 2026-07-15).
