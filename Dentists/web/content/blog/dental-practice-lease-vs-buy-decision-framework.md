<p>The <strong>dental practice lease buy decision</strong> ranks among the most significant financial choices you'll make as a practice owner. Whether you're establishing a new practice or relocating an existing one, the choice between leasing and purchasing premises will impact your cash flow, tax position, and long-term wealth for years to come.</p>

<p>This framework breaks down the key financial considerations to help you evaluate which option aligns with your business goals and personal circumstances.</p>

<h2>Initial Capital Requirements</h2>

<p>The upfront costs differ dramatically between leasing and buying, affecting your available working capital from day one.</p>

<h3>Buying a Practice Premises</h3>

<p>Purchasing typically requires 25-40% of the property value as a deposit, plus additional costs. For a £400,000 practice premises, expect to need:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Deposit:</strong> £100,000-£160,000 (25-40%)</li>
<li><strong>Stamp duty:</strong> £7,500 (3% on commercial property)</li>
<li><strong>Legal fees:</strong> £2,000-£4,000</li>
<li><strong>Survey costs:</strong> £1,500-£3,000</li>
<li><strong>Total upfront:</strong> £111,000-£174,500</li>
</ul>

<h3>Leasing a Practice Premises</h3>

<p>Leasing requires significantly less upfront capital but comes with ongoing commitments:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Deposit:</strong> 3-6 months' rent (typically £6,000-£12,000)</li>
<li><strong>First month's rent:</strong> £2,000-£4,000</li>
<li><strong>Legal fees:</strong> £1,500-£3,000</li>
<li><strong>Total upfront:</strong> £9,500-£19,000</li>
</ul>

<p>The lower entry cost of leasing preserves capital for equipment, marketing, and working capital during the critical early months.</p>

<h2>Monthly Cash Flow Impact</h2>

<p>Your ongoing monthly commitments vary significantly between the two options, affecting your practice's cash flow and profitability.</p>

<h3>Mortgage vs Rent Payments</h3>

<p>Using the same £400,000 property example with a £280,000 mortgage (30% deposit) at 5% interest over 20 years:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Monthly mortgage payment:</strong> £1,848</li>
<li><strong>Monthly rental equivalent:</strong> £2,000-£3,000</li>
<li><strong>Monthly saving (buying):</strong> £152-£1,152</li>
</ul>

<p>However, ownership brings additional costs that renters don't face: buildings insurance, maintenance, repairs, and commercial rates (unless passed through in the lease).</p>

<h3>Hidden Ownership Costs</h3>

<p>Property ownership includes responsibilities that can impact your monthly outgoings:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Buildings insurance:</strong> £150-£300 monthly</li>
<li><strong>Major repairs fund:</strong> £100-£200 monthly</li>
<li><strong>Commercial rates:</strong> £200-£400 monthly (varies by area)</li>
<li><strong>Property management:</strong> £50-£150 monthly (if outsourced)</li>
</ul>

<p>These additional costs can reduce or eliminate the apparent monthly saving from buying versus renting.</p>

<h2>Tax Implications</h2>

<p>The tax treatment differs significantly between leasing and buying, affecting your net position.</p>

<h3>Leasing Tax Benefits</h3>

<p>Rental payments receive straightforward tax relief:</p>

<ul>
<li>Full rent deductible against practice profits</li>
<li>Reduces corporation tax at 19% (small companies rate)</li>
<li>Simple accounting treatment</li>
<li>No capital allowances complications</li>
</ul>

<h3>Ownership Tax Considerations</h3>

<p>Property ownership creates more complex tax situations:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Mortgage interest:</strong> Deductible against rental income if property rented to practice</li>
<li><strong>Capital allowances:</strong> Available on fixtures and fittings</li>
<li><strong>Depreciation:</strong> Not tax-deductible</li>
<li><strong>Capital gains:</strong> Potentially liable on disposal (with reliefs available)</li>
</ul>

<p>Many practice owners hold property personally and rent to their practice, creating rental income subject to income tax at their marginal rate.</p>

<h2>Long-Term Wealth Building</h2>

<p>The <strong>dental practice lease buy decision</strong> significantly impacts your long-term wealth accumulation and exit strategy.</p>

<h3>Property Appreciation</h3>

<p>UK commercial property has historically provided capital growth, though past performance doesn't guarantee future returns. Over 10-15 years, property appreciation can contribute substantially to your overall wealth.</p>

<p>Example: A £400,000 property appreciating at 3% annually becomes worth £538,000 after 10 years, generating £138,000 in capital growth.</p>

<h3>Mortgage Paydown</h3>

<p>Each mortgage payment reduces your outstanding debt, building equity. Over 20 years, you'll have paid off the entire mortgage, owning a valuable asset outright.</p>

<p>By retirement, this could represent several hundred thousand pounds of additional wealth compared to renting throughout your career.</p>

<h2>Risk and Flexibility Factors</h2>

<p>Different risk profiles and flexibility needs influence the optimal choice for your situation.</p>

<h3>Market Risk</h3>

<p>Property ownership exposes you to commercial property market fluctuations. While long-term trends have been positive, short-term volatility can affect your net worth and borrowing capacity.</p>

<p>Leasing transfers this risk to the landlord, providing more predictable occupancy costs (subject to rent reviews).</p>

<h3>Business Flexibility</h3>

<p>Leasing typically offers greater flexibility for practice changes:</p>

<ul>
<li>Easier to relocate if demographics shift</li>
<li>Less capital tied up in non-productive assets</li>
<li>Simpler practice sale process</li>
<li>Reduced complexity in partnership changes</li>
</ul>

<p>Property ownership can complicate practice sales, as buyers may not want the property or may struggle with additional financing requirements.</p>

<h2>Decision Framework</h2>

<p>Use these criteria to evaluate which option suits your situation:</p>

<h3>Buy if You:</h3>

<ul>
<li>Have substantial available capital without compromising working capital</li>
<li>Plan to stay in the location for 7+ years</li>
<li>Want to build long-term wealth through property</li>
<li>Can handle property management responsibilities</li>
<li>Operate in a stable, desirable location</li>
</ul>

<h3>Lease if You:</h3>

<ul>
<li>Prefer to preserve capital for practice development</li>
<li>Value flexibility and simplicity</li>
<li>Want predictable monthly costs</li>
<li>Operate in an uncertain or declining area</li>
<li>Plan to expand to multiple locations</li>
</ul>

<h2>Financial Modeling</h2>

<p>Before making your <strong>dental practice lease buy decision</strong>, model both scenarios over your expected time horizon. Include all costs, tax implications, and potential property appreciation.</p>

<p>Key variables to consider:</p>

<ul>
<li>Property appreciation rates (be conservative)</li>
<li>Rental inflation over time</li>
<li>Interest rate changes on variable mortgages</li>
<li>Major maintenance and repair costs</li>
<li>Your marginal tax rates</li>
</ul>

<p>Professional modeling can reveal which option provides better risk-adjusted returns for your specific circumstances.</p>
