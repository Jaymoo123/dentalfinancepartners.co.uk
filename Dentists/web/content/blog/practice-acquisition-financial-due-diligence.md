<h2>Start with what the seller is actually selling</h2>
<p>Goodwill, equipment, property (or a lease), and the patient list are rarely neat boxes. You need a clear picture of what is included, what is leased, and what obligations pass to you on completion.</p>

<h2>Numbers to stress-test</h2>
<ul>
<li>NHS contract position, UDA performance, and any clawback exposure.</li>
<li>Private income by treatment type and how much depends on specific clinicians.</li>
<li>Associate and hygienist agreements — notice periods, splits, and what happens if someone leaves.</li>
<li>Lab and materials as a percentage of turnover; equipment finance and hire purchase.</li>
<li>Working capital: who owes what, and whether reported profit still leaves enough cash in the business.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Tax and structure</h2>
<p>How the transaction is structured (asset vs share purchase) changes tax and risk. Get advice early so you are not negotiating blind.</p>
