<h2>Why Choose Accountants Who Specialise in Dentistry</h2><p>Dentists face a unique set of financial circumstances that general accountants may not fully understand. From the complexities of NHS pension annual allowance charges to the VAT treatment of mixed exempt and taxable income, having an accountant who specialises in dentistry can make a significant difference to your tax position and business success <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>. Specialist dental accountants are familiar with the issues affecting dentists, whether you are a principal, partnership, company or associate <sup><a href="#ref-2" id="cite-2">[2]</a></sup>. They understand the constraints within which you work and can offer advice on a range of issues related to your dental business <sup><a href="#ref-2" id="cite-2">[2]</a></sup>.</p><p>Choosing an accountant who works with dentists regularly means you benefit from up-to-date knowledge of HMRC rules specific to the profession, including Making Tax Digital for VAT and the upcoming digital tax system for income tax <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>. The ICAEW provides technical guidance for dentists, and many specialist accountants are members of professional bodies such as NASDAL, which sets standards for dental accounting <sup><a href="#ref-3" id="cite-3">[3]</a></sup><sup><a href="#ref-2" id="cite-2">[2]</a></sup>.</p><h2>Services We Offer for Dentists and Dental Practices</h2><p>Our firm provides a full range of accounting and tax services tailored to the dental sector. We help with:</p><ul><li>Preparation and filing of annual accounts and tax returns for associates, practice owners, and limited companies.</li><li>VAT planning and compliance, including advice on exempt and taxable supplies such as cosmetic dentistry and product sales <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>.</li><li>Bookkeeping and cloud accounting setup to streamline your financial records.</li><li>Payroll and auto-enrolment pension management for practice staff.</li><li>Business structure advice, whether to trade as a sole trader, partnership, or limited company.</li><li>Practice valuation and financial due diligence for buying or selling a dental practice.</li></ul><p>We also offer proactive tax planning to help you minimise your tax liability within the law, and we stay on top of legislative changes that affect dentists <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup><sup><a href="#ref-3" id="cite-3">[3]</a></sup>.</p><h2>Tax Planning and Compliance for Associates and Practice Owners</h2><p>Tax planning is essential for dentists at every stage of their career. For associates, the first year in practice can be challenging: you must start repaying your student loan, submit your first tax return, and decide on a suitable business structure <sup><a href="#ref-4" id="cite-4">[4]</a></sup>. Our accountants guide you through these decisions, ensuring you claim all allowable expenses and make the most of your personal allowance.</p><p>For practice owners, we focus on profit extraction strategies, capital allowances on dental equipment and property, and succession planning. We also help with corporation tax planning for limited companies and ensure compliance with HMRC’s Making Tax Digital requirements <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>.</p><p>VAT is a particular area of complexity for dentists. Income from services to protect, maintain and restore oral health is exempt from VAT, but VAT is payable on income from cosmetic services, dental products, and activities such as report writing and lecturing <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>. You must register for VAT if your income from non-exempt services reaches the current threshold of £90,000 <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>. Our team ensures you correctly account for VAT and avoid penalties.</p><h2>NHS Pension and Annual Allowance Advice</h2><p>The NHS Pension Scheme presents unique tax challenges for dentists. The annual allowance, the amount your pension savings can grow each year without triggering a tax charge, is currently £60,000, but for high earners, the tapered annual allowance may reduce this to as low as £10,000 <sup><a href="#ref-1" id="cite-1">[1]</a></sup>. Many dentists face unexpected annual allowance charges, especially when their pension growth exceeds the standard allowance due to NHS pay rises or additional sessions.</p><p>Our specialist accountants calculate your pension input amounts and advise on whether you need to pay an annual allowance charge or use scheme pays to settle the tax from your pension benefits. We also help with carry forward planning to maximise your pension contributions without incurring penalties.</p><p>For associates and practice owners, we provide integrated advice that considers both your NHS pension and your private pension arrangements, ensuring you optimise your retirement savings while managing tax liabilities.</p><h2>Practice Purchase and Sale Support</h2><p>Buying or selling a dental practice is a significant financial transaction that requires expert advice. We assist with:</p><ul><li>Practice valuation and financial analysis to determine a fair price.</li><li>Due diligence on the target practice’s accounts, tax history, and contracts.</li><li>Structuring the deal to be tax-efficient, whether as an asset purchase or share sale.</li><li>Securing finance and liaising with lenders.</li><li>Post-acquisition integration and tax planning.</li></ul><p>Our team has experience working with dentists throughout the transaction lifecycle, from initial planning to completion. We also advise on goodwill valuation and the tax implications of selling a practice, including entrepreneurs’ relief (now Business Asset Disposal Relief) and capital gains tax planning.</p><h2>Client Testimonials</h2><p>We are proud of the relationships we build with our dental clients.
