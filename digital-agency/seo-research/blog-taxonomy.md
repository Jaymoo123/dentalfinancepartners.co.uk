# Blog Taxonomy — Agency Founder Finance

_Category structure, rationale, and post allocation_

---

## Design Principles

1. Categories reflect how agency founders think about their finances, not how accountants categorise tax law.
2. Each category must be large enough to hold 5–10 posts without overlap.
3. Categories must support internal linking to at least one commercial page.
4. Avoid categories that would only ever have 1–2 posts.
5. Mirror the Property Tax Partners pattern: 8–10 hardcoded category pages with descriptive intros.

---

## Category 1 — Agency Finance Essentials

**Slug:** `agency-finance-essentials`

**Who it's for:** New and early-stage agency founders getting to grips with their numbers.

**Rationale:** The highest-volume informational need. Founders who have just left employment or recently incorporated don't know how to read their P&L, what a management report looks like, or what cash conversion cycle means for an agency. This category builds trust before the sale.

**Content themes:**
- Understanding cash flow in an agency business
- What your accountant actually does for you
- Key financial metrics for agency founders
- Profit margins in agencies: what's normal?
- Bookkeeping basics for busy founders
- What to send your accountant every month
- Accounting software for marketing agencies (handles Cluster 3 keywords)
- Setting up a chart of accounts for your agency
- Finance for founders: the agency-specific curriculum

**Commercial link:** → `/services/management-accounts/`, → `/services/accounting-for-agencies/`

**Rationale:** Agency exits and M&A are increasing. Business Asset Disposal Relief (BADR/entrepreneurs' relief), MBOs, earn-outs and goodwill valuations are specialist topics with low SERP competition. High-value leads for a specialist accountant.

## Category 6 — Contractors and IR35

**Slug:** `contractors-and-ir35`

**Who it's for:** Agency founders who engage freelancers or contractors, or who are themselves operating outside IR35.

**Rationale:** IR35 is uniquely relevant to agencies. Agencies often engage large numbers of freelancers and face off-payroll working rules. Agencies that place contractors (recruitment agencies, talent agencies) also fall within the rules. This is a differentiated angle.

**Content themes:**
- IR35 for agency founders: what you need to know
- Engaging freelancers through your agency: IR35 risk assessment
- Inside vs outside IR35 for agency contractors
- Status determination statements (SDS) for agency engagements
- IR35 and creative agencies: the practical guide
- Off-payroll working rules for talent and recruitment agencies
- CEST tool limitations: what agency founders should know
- Contractor vs employee: the agency founder's decision guide

**Commercial link:** → `/agencies/recruitment-agencies/`, → `/services/accounting-for-agencies/`

## Category 9 — International Agencies

**Slug:** `international-agencies`

**Who it's for:** UK agency founders with international clients, offices, or who operate between UK and UAE.

**Rationale:** The UAE keyword signal is thin but the audience is real. Many UK agency founders have relocated to Dubai or operate across both markets. Non-dom rules, UAE corporate tax (introduced 2023), and cross-border agency income are specialist topics that nobody covers well.

**Content themes:**
- UK agency founder based in Dubai: UK tax obligations explained
- UAE corporate tax for UK agency founders
- Non-domicile tax rules for UK agency founders
- Setting up a UAE branch of your UK agency
- Invoicing international clients from your UK agency: VAT rules
- Foreign income for UK agency directors
- Transfer pricing basics for multi-entity agency groups
