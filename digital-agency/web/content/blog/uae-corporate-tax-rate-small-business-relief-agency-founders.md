<p>If you run a marketing agency, digital agency, or creative agency from Dubai, you have probably heard the good news: the UAE corporate tax rate is 9% on profits above AED 375,000. That is genuinely low by global standards. But the detail matters more than the headline.</p>

<p>What happens if your agency's profits exceed AED 375,000? Do you pay 9% on everything, or just the excess? And what is this "small business relief" everyone mentions, does it apply to your agency?</p>

<p>This article answers those questions directly. No fluff. Just the numbers and the rules as they stand for the 2025/26 tax year.</p>

<h2>The UAE Corporate Tax Rate Bands</h2>
<p>The UAE introduced corporate tax for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. The structure is simple:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>0%</strong> on taxable profits up to AED 375,000</li>
<li><strong>9%</strong> on taxable profits above AED 375,000</li>
<li><strong>Different rates</strong> (15%) apply to large multinationals under the OECD Pillar Two rules, but that will not affect most agencies</li>
</ul>
<p>If your agency makes AED 500,000 in taxable profit, you pay 0% on the first AED 375,000 and 9% on the remaining AED 125,000. That works out to AED 11,250 in tax. Not AED 45,000 (which would be 9% of the full AED 500,000). The banding is progressive, not flat.</p>
<p>This matters because many founders assume the full profit is taxed. It is not. The first AED 375,000 is tax-free every year.</p>

<h2>Does the AED 375,000 Threshold Apply to Revenue or Profit?</h2>
<p>This is the most common question we get from agency founders. The answer: it applies to <strong>taxable profit</strong>, not revenue.</p>
<p>Your taxable profit is your revenue minus allowable expenses. For an agency, those expenses typically include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Staff salaries and benefits</li>
<li>Office rent and utilities</li>
<li>Software subscriptions (Adobe, HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)</li>
<li>Freelancer and contractor costs</li>
<li>Marketing and business development spend</li>
<li>Travel and entertainment (within limits)</li>
</ul>
<p>So an agency billing AED 1.5m in revenue but spending AED 1.2m on costs has a taxable profit of AED 300,000. That is below the AED 375,000 threshold. No corporate tax is due.</p>
<p>An agency billing AED 800k with costs of AED 300k has a taxable profit of AED 500,000. The first AED 375,000 is tax-free. The remaining AED 125,000 is taxed at 9%. Total tax: AED 11,250.</p>
<p>Your revenue is not the trigger. Your profit margin is.</p>

<h2>What Is UAE Corporate Tax Small Business Relief?</h2>
<p>The UAE introduced small business relief (also called the "small business exemption") to reduce the compliance burden on smaller entities. It is available for tax periods starting on or after 1 June 2023 and initially runs for three years (until 31 May 2026).</p>
<p>If your agency qualifies, you can elect to be treated as if you are not subject to corporate tax. That means:</p>
<ul>
<li>No corporate tax return filing</li>
<li>No corporate tax liability</li>
<li>Simplified compliance</li>
</ul>
<p>But there is a catch. The relief is based on <strong>revenue</strong>, not profit. And the threshold is AED 3 million.</p>
<p>If your agency's revenue in a given tax period is AED 3 million or less, you can elect for small business relief. If your revenue exceeds AED 3 million, you cannot, regardless of your profit level.</p>
<p>This is where agency founders need to be careful. A high-revenue, low-margin agency might have profits below AED 375,000 but revenue above AED 3 million. That agency cannot use small business relief. It must file a corporate tax return and pay 9% on profits above AED 375,000.</p>

<h2>Who Qualifies for Small Business Relief?</h2>
<p>The Federal Tax Authority (FTA) has set clear conditions. Your agency qualifies for the UAE corporate tax small business relief if:</p>
<ul>
<li>Your revenue for the relevant tax period is AED 3 million or less</li>
<li>You are a resident person (i.e. your agency is incorporated in the UAE)</li>
<li>You are not part of a multinational group with consolidated revenue exceeding AED 3.15 billion</li>
</ul>
<p>If your agency meets these conditions, you can elect for the relief. It is optional, you do not have to take it. But if your profits are below AED 375,000 and your revenue is under AED 3 million, there is usually no downside to electing.</p>
<p>If your profits are above AED 375,000 but your revenue is under AED 3 million, the relief still applies. You pay no corporate tax at all. This is a significant benefit for profitable but relatively small agencies.</p>
<p>If your revenue is above AED 3 million, you cannot use the relief. You must file a return and pay 9% on profits above AED 375,000.</p>

<h2>What About Free Zone Agencies?</h2>
<p>Many agency founders in Dubai operate from a free zone (DMCC, DIFC, ADGM, Dubai Silicon Oasis, etc.). Free zone entities that are "qualifying free zone persons" can benefit from a 0% rate on qualifying income, provided they meet certain conditions.</p>
<p>But the small business relief is separate. It applies to all resident persons, including free zone entities, as long as the revenue condition is met.</p>
<p>If your free zone agency has revenue under AED 3 million, you can elect for small business relief and avoid the compliance burden of proving your income is "qualifying." This can simplify your tax position significantly.</p>
<p>If your free zone agency has revenue above AED 3 million, you need to carefully assess whether your income qualifies for the 0% free zone rate. If it does not, the 9% rate applies on profits above AED 375,000.</p>
<p>This is a complex area. If you operate from a free zone, speak to your accountant about whether the small business relief or the free zone regime is more advantageous for your specific situation.</p>

<h2>How to Elect for Small Business Relief</h2>
<p>The process is straightforward but must be done correctly. You need to:</p>
<ol>
<li>Submit a corporate tax registration application to the FTA (if you have not already)</li>
<li>In your corporate tax return for the relevant period, indicate that you are electing for small business relief</li>
<li>Maintain records demonstrating your revenue is below AED 3 million</li>
</ol>
<p>The election is made on a period-by-period basis. You can elect for one year and not the next, depending on your revenue.</p>
<p>If your revenue exceeds AED 3 million in a later period, you lose the relief for that period. You must then file a full corporate tax return and pay tax on profits above AED 375,000.</p>

<h2>Practical Examples for Agency Founders</h2>
<p>Let us run through three scenarios that cover the most common situations we see.</p>

<h3>Example 1: Small Agency, Low Profit</h3>
<p>You run a web design agency from a co-working space in Dubai Marina. Your revenue is AED 1.2m. Your costs (your salary, a junior designer, software, and office costs) total AED 1m. Your taxable profit is AED 200,000.</p>
<p>Revenue is under AED 3 million. Profit is under AED 375,000. You elect for small business relief. No corporate tax is due. No return is filed (beyond the election).</p>

<h3>Example 2: Medium Agency, High Profit</h3>
<p>You run a digital marketing agency with 12 staff. Your revenue is AED 4.5m. Your costs (salaries, rent, software, freelancers) total AED 3.8m. Your taxable profit is AED 700,000.</p>
<p>Revenue exceeds AED 3 million. You cannot use small business relief. You must file a corporate tax return. Your tax liability is 0% on the first AED 375,000 and 9% on the remaining AED 325,000. Total tax: AED 29,250.</p>

<h3>Example 3: Free Zone Agency, Borderline Revenue</h3>
<p>You run a PR agency from DMCC. Your revenue is AED 2.8m. Your costs are AED 2.2m. Your taxable profit is AED 600,000.</p>
<p>Revenue is under AED 3 million. You elect for small business relief. No tax is due, even though your profit exceeds AED 375,000. This is better than the free zone regime if your income would not qualify for the 0% rate.</p>

<h2>What Happens After May 2026?</h2>
<p>The small business relief is currently confirmed for tax periods starting before 1 June 2026. The UAE government may extend it, amend it, or let it expire.</p>
<p>If you are relying on this relief, plan for the possibility that it ends. If your revenue is close to AED 3 million, consider whether you want to keep it under that threshold or grow through it and accept the 9% rate on profits above AED 375,000.</p>
<p>We expect the FTA to provide guidance on the future of the relief in late 2025 or early 2026. Watch for announcements.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes Agency Founders Make</h2>
<p>We see the same errors repeatedly. Avoid these:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Assuming revenue is the threshold for the 0% rate.</strong> It is not. The AED 375,000 threshold is on profit. The AED 3 million threshold is for small business relief. They are different.</li>
<li><strong>Forgetting to register for corporate tax.</strong> Even if you qualify for small business relief, you must register with the FTA. You just do not need to file a full return.</li>
<li><strong>Not tracking revenue correctly.</strong> Revenue means gross income before expenses. If you bill AED 3.1m but have AED 2.9m in costs, your profit is only AED 200k. You still cannot use small business relief because your revenue exceeds AED 3m.</li>
<li><strong>Assuming free zone status automatically exempts you.</strong> It does not. Free zone entities must meet specific conditions for the 0% rate. Small business relief is a separate option.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What You Should Do Now</h2>
<p>If you run an agency in the UAE, here is your checklist:</p>
<ol>
<li>Calculate your revenue for the current financial year. Is it above or below AED 3 million?</li>
<li>Calculate your taxable profit. Is it above or below AED 375,000?</li>
<li>If your revenue is under AED 3 million, consider electing for small business relief when you file your return.</li>
<li>If your revenue is above AED 3 million, prepare to file a full corporate tax return and pay 9% on profits above AED 375,000.</li>
<li>If you are in a free zone, review whether your income qualifies for the 0% rate or whether small business relief is a better option.</li>
</ol>
<p>The UAE corporate tax system is straightforward compared to most jurisdictions. But the interaction between the profit threshold, the small business relief, and free zone rules creates traps for the unwary.</p>
<p>If your agency's revenue is close to AED 3 million or your profit is close to AED 375,000, get specific advice. A few thousand dirhams in planning can save you tens of thousands in unexpected tax.</p>
