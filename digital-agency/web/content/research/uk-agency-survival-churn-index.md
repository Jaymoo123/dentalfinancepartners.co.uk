# UK Agency Survival & Churn Index

How long UK marketing, creative, advertising and digital agencies actually last, tracked cohort by cohort from ONS Business Demography birth-cohort data, and compared against the all-industries UK average.

Of the agency-cluster enterprises born in 2019, 36.7% were still active five years later, against a 38.4% average across all UK industries. Advertising (SIC 731, which includes both advertising agencies and media representation), market research (SIC 732) and specialised design (SIC 741) each individually beat the all-industries average on their own. IT consultancy (SIC 620), the largest segment by births, sits below the all-industries average and pulls the combined weighted figure down.

Public relations (SIC 70210) cannot be isolated in this ONS release: it sits inside a broader management-consultancy group and is reported as context only, excluded from the combined agency figure to avoid diluting it with an unrelated population.

95.0% of the most recent (2023) agency-cluster birth cohort survived their first year, in line with the 93.4% all-industries figure. The gap between agencies and the wider economy opens up from year 2 onwards, not year 1.

Source: Office for National Statistics, Business Demography (Tables 5.2a-5.2e), Open Government Licence v3.0. Full data, methodology, per-segment breakdown and CSV download: https://www.agencyfounderfinance.co.uk/research/uk-agency-survival-churn-index
