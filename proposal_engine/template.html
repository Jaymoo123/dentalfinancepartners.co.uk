SECTION 03Contactability and automated follow-up

On $nurture_golive the site went live with an automated follow-up sequence, built to make sure every lead that reaches you can actually be contacted. The flow, in order: the moment an enquiry is submitted it receives an instant acknowledgement, then a short SMS and email sequence that confirms the enquirer's contact details are live, captures anything missing from the original submission, and offers a link to book a call directly. Only leads that respond to verification are passed on: the sequence acts as a quality gate in front of delivery, so a lead that never engages is never handed over, and never charged for.

SECTION 08Credits and refunds

Two credit routes apply, so leads that are not genuine or cannot be reached are not paid for.

Automatic credit, objective grounds

Within $rej_window working days of delivery you may flag a lead, with brief evidence, as (a) spam or bot-generated, (b) a duplicate of a lead delivered in the prior 30 days, (c) clearly not a property tax enquiry, or (d) having contact details that do not work (email hard-bounces and any telephone number is unobtainable or invalid). Flags are reviewed reasonably and in good faith; accepted flags are credited in full against the next invoice, or refunded within 30 days if no further invoice is due.

Dead-lead credit, evidenced pursuit

A lead whose contact details work but who never responds can also be credited, provided the lead was genuinely pursued. A dead-lead credit is valid only where you evidence $touchpoints proven contact attempts over $dead_window days from delivery, spread across telephone call, voicemail, SMS and email, supported by a contact log (call records, message copies or CRM export). Flags raised with the touchpoint log within the $dead_window-day window are reviewed in good faith and, where accepted, credited against the next invoice.

Leads are billable on delivery rather than on conversion. The credit routes above ensure spam, unreachable contacts and properly pursued non-responders are not charged.

SECTION 09Setup and how delivery works

  1. Nominate an inbox. You give us a single delivery email address. Every lead that passes contact verification (section 3) is forwarded there, including name, contact details, self-selected role and message.
  2. Consent and data protection are handled at source. The lead form carries an acknowledgement that details will be shared with the receiving firm. Data is shared controller-to-controller under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 as amended by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025. Once a lead lands in your inbox, you hold it as an independent controller.
  3. Tiering on delivery (Option B, or on request under Option A): each lead arrives with its tier and a one-line rationale.
  4. Monthly billing in arrears. At the start of each month you receive an itemised lead statement listing the previous month's delivered leads, followed by an invoice. You have 10 working days to reconcile against your own records. Payment is due within $payment_days days; credits from section 8 are applied to the same invoice cycle.
  5. No lock-in. Rolling arrangement, terminable by either side on short written notice.

Methodology. Arrival trend: Poisson regression (log-linear) fitted to the most recent eight complete calendar weeks, excluding the launch ramp so the trend is not overstated; the shaded band in Figure 1 is a 90% interval on the trend mean via the delta method. Trailing 30-day interval: nonparametric bootstrap of daily arrival counts, 5,000 resamples. Engagement values: per-lead good-faith estimates of first-year fees based on the work described in each enquiry; concentration measured as the estimated value share of the top quintile of assessed leads. The observed series is short ($n_weeks complete weeks), so interval estimates are reported alongside every point estimate.
