<p><strong>UK trainee solicitor salaries in 2025/26 range from £25,000 at small high-street firms to £60,000 or more at Magic Circle firms.</strong> Most trainees sit in the £28,000-£40,000 range. The single biggest determinant is firm type — the gap between a Magic Circle trainee and a regional high-street trainee is larger than the gap between many qualified solicitors at the same firm types.</p>

<p>This guide gives realistic salary ranges by firm type and region for 2025/26, the structural elements (SQE pay split, qualification jump, retention bonuses), and what trainees actually take home after income tax, National Insurance, pension and student loan.</p>

<h2>Trainee solicitor salary ranges by firm type — 2025/26</h2>

<h3>Magic Circle (Allen & Overy / Shearman, Clifford Chance, Freshfields, Linklaters, Slaughter and May)</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1</strong>: £50,000-£56,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 2</strong>: £55,000-£62,000</li>
<li><strong>Newly Qualified (NQ)</strong>: £125,000-£150,000</li>
</ul>

<p>The Magic Circle has historically led the market and continues to be the salary benchmark for the rest of the City. The 2024 round of NQ salary hikes (driven by US firms entering the London market aggressively) lifted the qualification jump materially.</p>

<h3>US firms in London (Kirkland & Ellis, Latham & Watkins, Sullivan & Cromwell, etc.)</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1</strong>: £55,000-£65,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 2</strong>: £60,000-£70,000</li>
<li><strong>Newly Qualified (NQ)</strong>: £150,000-£180,000+</li>
</ul>

<p>US firms typically pay marginally above Magic Circle at trainee level and significantly above at NQ. The qualification jump is the largest in the market.</p>

<h3>Silver Circle and mid-tier City firms (Hogan Lovells, Herbert Smith Freehills, Macfarlanes, Ashurst, Travers Smith)</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1</strong>: £48,000-£55,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 2</strong>: £52,000-£60,000</li>
<li><strong>Newly Qualified (NQ)</strong>: £105,000-£135,000</li>
</ul>

<h3>National full-service firms (DLA Piper UK arm, CMS, Pinsent Masons, Eversheds Sutherland)</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1 in London</strong>: £45,000-£52,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1 in regional offices</strong>: £30,000-£40,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 2 in London</strong>: £50,000-£58,000</li>
<li><strong>NQ in London</strong>: £85,000-£105,000</li>
<li><strong>NQ in regional offices</strong>: £55,000-£75,000</li>
</ul>

<p>National firms with regional offices pay a London/regional differential. The Manchester or Leeds office of a firm pays meaningfully less than the London office for the same level.</p>

<h3>Mid-market regional firms (top regional independents — Burness Paull, Mills & Reeve, Shoosmiths, Brabners)</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1</strong>: £28,000-£36,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 2</strong>: £30,000-£40,000</li>
<li><strong>NQ</strong>: £45,000-£70,000</li>
</ul>

<h3>High-street firms and small practices (2-15 fee-earners)</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Trainee year 1</strong>: £22,000-£32,000</li>
<li><strong>Trainee year 2</strong>: £24,000-£36,000</li>
<li><strong>NQ</strong>: £30,000-£50,000</li>
</ul>

<p>High-street firms are the most variable. Some pay only at National Minimum Wage levels (which for a 21+ adult is currently £11.44 per hour, around £22,300 annual for a full-time 37.5 hour week). Others pay substantially more, particularly in higher-cost-of-living areas in the South East.</p>

<h3>In-house and government legal trainees</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Government Legal Profession (GLP)</strong>: starting salary around £30,000-£35,000, rising to £40,000-£50,000 on qualification</li>
<li><strong>In-house at corporates</strong>: increasingly hire trainees directly. Salary varies widely but typically £35,000-£55,000 at trainee level for FTSE-quality employers</li>
<li><strong>Crown Prosecution Service</strong>: around £30,000 trainee, £45,000-£55,000 on qualification</li>
</ul>

<h2>What's actually in the contract</h2>

<p>Beyond the headline salary, trainee solicitor packages typically include:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Pension contribution</strong>: 3-8% employer contribution under auto-enrolment, sometimes matched higher</li>
<li><strong>Private medical insurance</strong>: standard at most City and Silver Circle firms; rare at high-street firms</li>
<li><strong>Life insurance</strong>: typically 4x base salary, standard at larger firms</li>
<li><strong>Income protection</strong>: standard at larger firms</li>
<li><strong>SQE study costs</strong>: paid by most large firms (£15,000-£25,000 in study fees and materials), often as a recoverable arrangement if the trainee leaves within 2 years of qualifying</li>
<li><strong>Salary during SQE study leave</strong>: varies — some firms pay full salary throughout; some reduce to 80-90 percent during exam periods</li>
<li><strong>Annual leave</strong>: 25-28 days standard, plus bank holidays. Larger firms increasingly offer 28-30 days</li>
<li><strong>Bonus</strong>: discretionary at most firms; structured at Magic Circle and US firms. Trainee-level bonuses typically £0-£3,000; NQ bonuses materially higher</li>
</ul>

<h2>The take-home calculation — £30,000 trainee salary 2025/26</h2>

<p>Working through a typical regional or mid-market trainee on £30,000:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Gross salary</strong>: £30,000</li>
<li><strong>Personal allowance</strong>: £12,570 (no income tax on this band)</li>
<li><strong>Income tax at 20% on £17,430</strong>: £3,486</li>
<li><strong>Employee NI at 8% on £17,430 (the band £12,570-£50,270)</strong>: £1,394</li>
<li><strong>Auto-enrolment pension at 5% (employee contribution, deducted from gross)</strong>: £1,500</li>
<li><strong>Plan 2 student loan at 9% on £2,705 (income above the £27,295 threshold)</strong>: £243</li>
</ul>

<p>Net take-home: £30,000 - £3,486 - £1,394 - £1,500 - £243 = <strong>£23,377 annually, or £1,948 per month</strong>.</p>

<p>If on Plan 5 student loan (post-Aug 2023 starters), repayments are 9% above the £25,000 threshold, so on £30,000 that's £450 — slightly more than Plan 2.</p>

<h2>The take-home calculation — £55,000 Magic Circle trainee 2025/26</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>Gross salary</strong>: £55,000</li>
<li><strong>Personal allowance</strong>: £12,570</li>
<li><strong>Income tax at 20% on £37,700</strong>: £7,540</li>
<li><strong>Income tax at 40% on £4,730 (£50,271-£55,000)</strong>: £1,892</li>
<li><strong>Employee NI at 8% on £37,700</strong>: £3,016</li>
<li><strong>Employee NI at 2% on £4,730</strong>: £95</li>
<li><strong>Auto-enrolment pension at 5%</strong>: £2,750</li>
<li><strong>Plan 2 student loan at 9% on £27,705</strong>: £2,493</li>
</ul>

<p>Net take-home: £55,000 - £7,540 - £1,892 - £3,016 - £95 - £2,750 - £2,493 = <strong>£37,214 annually, or £3,101 per month</strong>.</p>

<p>The marginal rate on the band above £50,270 is the higher-rate combination: 40% income tax + 2% NI + 9% student loan = 51% effective marginal rate. Each additional £1,000 of salary above £50,270 nets about £490 after these deductions.</p>

<h2>The qualification jump — why year 3 is the big year</h2>

<p>The single biggest salary step in a solicitor's career is the move from year-2 trainee to newly-qualified. The size of the jump varies:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Magic Circle</strong>: trainee year 2 £58k → NQ £135k = +£77k (+133%)</li>
<li><strong>US firms in London</strong>: trainee year 2 £65k → NQ £170k = +£105k (+162%)</li>
<li><strong>National firm London office</strong>: trainee year 2 £55k → NQ £95k = +£40k (+73%)</li>
<li><strong>Mid-market regional</strong>: trainee year 2 £35k → NQ £55k = +£20k (+57%)</li>
<li><strong>High-street</strong>: trainee year 2 £30k → NQ £40k = +£10k (+33%)</li>
</ul>

<p>For Magic Circle and US firm NQs, the post-qualification salary takes you into additional-rate territory immediately (above £125,140). Tax planning around the qualification jump becomes meaningful — pension contributions, salary sacrifice, the personal allowance taper at £100,000-£125,140 — all of which we cover in our <a href="/for-junior-solicitors">For junior solicitors</a> service page.</p>

<h2>SQE training contract specifics</h2>

<p>The Solicitors Qualifying Examination (SQE) replaced the LPC route from 1 September 2021. Two years of Qualifying Work Experience (QWE) under SRA-approved supervision is the practical requirement. Most firms continue to offer structured 2-year training contracts that meet the QWE requirement.</p>

<p>Salary structures under SQE:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>SQE1 study period</strong> (typically pre-contract or first 6 months of the contract): some firms pay reduced salary 80-90% of full QWE; others pay full salary</li>
<li><strong>QWE period</strong>: full salary for the duration of practical training</li>
<li><strong>SQE2 study period</strong> (typically months 18-21 of the contract): same firms with reduced SQE1 pay often reduce SQE2 study time pay similarly; full-pay firms maintain</li>
<li><strong>Qualification</strong>: NQ salary kicks in from the day SRA admission is confirmed</li>
</ul>

<p>SQE preparation costs (£15,000-£25,000) are typically funded by the employing firm at Magic Circle, US firms, Silver Circle and most National firms. Smaller regional firms and high-street firms increasingly fund SQE but the picture is more variable than at the City top tier.</p>
