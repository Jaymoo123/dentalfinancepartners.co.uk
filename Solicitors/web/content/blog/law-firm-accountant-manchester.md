<h2>Essential Services from Manchester Law Firm Accountants</h2>

<h3>SRA Compliance and Trust Accounting</h3>

<p>Every Manchester law firm handling client money must comply with <a href="/sra-compliance">SRA Accounts Rules</a>. This includes maintaining separate client and office accounts, conducting regular reconciliations, and preparing annual accountant's reports.</p>

<p>Common compliance issues we see in Manchester practices include:</p>

<ul>
<li>Inadequate client money procedures</li>
<li>Missing or incomplete client ledger records</li>
<li>Delayed reconciliations causing cash flow problems</li>
<li>Improper treatment of disbursements and VAT</li>
</ul>

<p>A <strong>law firm accountant Manchester</strong> ensures your practice meets all SRA requirements while maintaining efficient cash flow management.</p>

<h3>Partnership and LLP Tax Planning</h3>

<p>Manchester's established law firms often operate as partnerships or LLPs. The 2025/26 tax year brings significant changes with Basis Period Reform affecting how partnership profits are taxed.</p>

<p>For example, a 4-partner Manchester firm with a 31 March year-end and £800k annual profits needs careful tax planning to manage the transitional adjustments. Partners may face higher tax bills in 2024/25 as both old and new basis periods overlap.</p>

<p>Key considerations for Manchester partnerships include:</p>

<ul>
<li>Managing the transition to current year basis taxation</li>
<li>Optimising profit allocation between partners</li>
<li>Planning for potential NI changes affecting LLP members in 2026</li>
<li>Ensuring adequate cash reserves for increased tax liabilities</li>
</ul>

<p>From April 2026, Making Tax Digital for Income Tax affects sole practitioner solicitors in Manchester. This means quarterly digital submissions and maintaining digital records.</p>

<p>Manchester sole practitioners must prepare for:</p>

<ul>
<li>Compatible accounting software implementation</li>
<li>Quarterly update submissions to HMRC</li>
