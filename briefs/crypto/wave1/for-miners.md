## Search-intent class + play

HIRE lead page for a small, technically distinct segment. The page turns on the two-step nobody gets right by hand: mining rewards are taxable on receipt as income (miscellaneous, or trading income if the activity amounts to a trade), valued in sterling at receipt, and that receipt value becomes the CGT base cost for the later disposal (HP 9). DIY miners commonly report only the sale, missing the receipt-income leg, or double-count. The £1,000 trading and miscellaneous income allowance can shelter small mining receipts from income tax but does NOT remove CGT on the later disposal (HP 12). Miner frame, never investment advice. Volume is thin; the page sells getting the two-step and the hobby-vs-trade line right.

## House positions touched (docs/crypto/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 9: mining rewards taxable on receipt (misc income, or trading income if a trade), valued in sterling at receipt; that value becomes the CGT base cost (two-step). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto21150
- HP 12: £1,000 trading and miscellaneous income allowance can shelter small mining receipts from income tax but does NOT remove CGT on later disposal. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tax-free-allowances-on-property-and-trading-income and https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-receive-cryptoassets
- HP 6: selling or swapping mined tokens are taxable disposals at sterling value. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-sell-cryptoassets
- HP 3: AEA £3,000 (disposal leg). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/allowances
- HP 2: CGT 18% within remaining basic-rate band then 24% above; never flat 18%. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates
- HP 15: almost all individuals are investors, not traders; trade only in exceptional circumstances (bears on hobby-vs-trade). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250
- HP 16: trade/trader treatment not automatically better. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250
- HP 17: badges of trade. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22015
- HP 19: capital losses must be claimed (within four years of the tax-year end). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/losses

Consistency rules: UK default. Two-step always stated in full (receipt income AND later CGT). £1,000 allowance shelters income only. CGT rate band-split. AEA £3,000. Hobby-vs-trade is a facts-and-badges judgment, not a fixed threshold. No investment or price views.

## HP GAP FLAG

## Hallucination danger zones

- Always state the full two-step: income on receipt AND CGT on later disposal (HP 9). Do not report only the sale, and do not double-count (receipt value is the base cost).
- £1,000 allowance shelters small receipts from income tax only; it does NOT remove later CGT (HP 12).
- CGT rate band-split, never flat 18% (HP 2); AEA £3,000 (HP 3).
- Hobby vs trade is badges of trade on the facts (HP 15/17); no bright-line threshold, and trade treatment is not automatically better (HP 16).
- Cost deductibility is facts-dependent (HP GAP); do not promise electricity/hardware deductions.
