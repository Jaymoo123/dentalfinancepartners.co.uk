## House positions touched (docs/crypto/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 1: disposals of crypto are CGT events for almost all individuals; most holders are investors taxed under CGT. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-sell-cryptoassets
- HP 2: CGT 18% within remaining basic-rate band then 24% above; higher/additional rate 24% (from 6 April 2026); basic-rate band ceiling £37,700 (2026/27). Never flat 18%. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates
- HP 3: annual exempt amount £3,000 (frozen); most active holders exhaust it in one or two disposals. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/allowances
- HP 4: s104 pooling per token at average cost; FIFO/LIFO/specific-ID wrong for UK individuals. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22200
- HP 5: same-day rule then 30-day (bed-and-breakfast) rule override the pool; out of scope of a stateless web calculator. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22250
- HP 6: crypto-to-crypto swaps are taxable disposals at sterling market value at the swap. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-sell-cryptoassets
- HP 7: spouse/civil-partner transfers are no-gain/no-loss; a planning lever, not a sale. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/gifts
- HP 15: almost all individuals are investors, not traders; trading treatment only in exceptional circumstances. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250
- HP 19: capital losses must be claimed, normally within four years of the end of the tax year they arose. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/losses
- HP 26: SA registration deadline 5 October after the tax year of first reportable gains/income. https://www.gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment
- HP 27: gains can be reportable even when no tax is due (proceeds over the reporting threshold). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/reporting-and-paying-capital-gains-tax

Consistency rules: UK default. CGT rate always band-split. AEA £3,000. Same-day/30-day rules acknowledged but flagged as beyond any web tool. No investment or price views.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Never present CGT as a flat "18% basic rate" (HP 2). Always the band-split, with the £37,700 ceiling if a boundary is illustrated.
- AEA is £3,000 (HP 3), not a prior-year figure.
- Do not imply the web calculator produces a filing-ready number; same-day/30-day matching is out of scope (HP 5).
- Do not tell the reader to chase "trader status" (HP 16, usually a bad outcome); route the question to /for/day-traders.
- Spouse transfer is a transfer not a sale; receiving spouse inherits base cost (HP 7). Do not imply it resets the base cost to market value.
- Losses must be claimed to be usable (HP 19); do not imply they carry automatically.
- No credential claims, no named expert. No em-dashes.
