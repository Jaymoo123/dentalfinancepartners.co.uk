## House positions touched (docs/crypto/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 1: disposals are CGT events for almost all individuals; most holders taxed under CGT (the flipper baseline). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-sell-cryptoassets
- HP 2: CGT 18% within remaining basic-rate band then 24% above; never flat 18%. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates
- HP 3: AEA £3,000. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/allowances
- HP 6: NFT swaps (for crypto or other NFTs) are taxable disposals at sterling value. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-sell-cryptoassets
- HP 12: £1,000 trading and miscellaneous income allowance may shelter small creator receipts from income tax (does not remove later CGT on any tokens). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tax-free-allowances-on-property-and-trading-income
- HP 15: almost all individuals are investors; trading treatment only in exceptional circumstances. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250
- HP 16: trader status usually a BAD outcome. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250
- HP 17: badges of trade; high frequency alone not a trade. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22015
- HP 19: capital losses must be claimed (within four years of the tax-year end). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/losses
- HP 21: negligible value claims for genuinely worthless tokens/NFTs (rug pulls, dead chains). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22500
- HP 22: exchange/collection collapse is fact-specific; never promise a deduction. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22400

Consistency rules: UK default. Creator-income vs flipper-CGT split drawn explicitly. CGT rate band-split. AEA £3,000. Trader status framed as usually worse (HP 16). Negligible value requires genuine worthlessness, not just a price crash. No investment or art-price views.

## HP GAP FLAG

- Whether a specific creator's activity is a "trade/profession" (income) vs occasional CGT is a facts-and-badges judgment, not a fixed house figure. Present the split as HMRC's badges-of-trade analysis (HP 15/17) applied to facts, routing genuinely borderline cases to "speak to us". Do not assert a bright-line frequency threshold; none exists in house_positions.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do not give a bright-line rule for when a creator becomes a trader; it is badges of trade on the facts (HP 15/17, HP GAP).
- Flipper CGT rate is band-split, never flat 18% (HP 2); AEA £3,000 (HP 3).
- NFT-for-NFT and NFT-for-crypto swaps are disposals (HP 6); do not imply only cash-out is taxable.
- Do not frame trader status as desirable (HP 16).
- Negligible value needs genuine worthlessness (HP 21); a price crash alone is not a claim, and collapse is fact-specific with no promised deduction (HP 22).
- No credential claims, no named expert. No em-dashes. No investment or price views.
