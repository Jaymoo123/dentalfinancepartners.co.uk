## House positions touched

- **HP 20 (losing keys is NOT a disposal), the spine:** losing private keys is not itself a disposal; a negligible value claim may work only if the asset itself has become worthless, not merely inaccessible; DIY guides routinely blur this. Cite https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22400 (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 21 (negligible value claims for worthless tokens):** available for genuinely worthless tokens (rug pulls, dead chains), crystallising an allowable loss without a sale. Cite https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22500 (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 22 (exchange collapse is fact-specific):** FTX-class collapse may be a negligible value claim, a capital loss on the claim against the exchange, or nothing yet, depending on the facts; never promise a deduction. Cite https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22400 and https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto22500 (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 19 (losses must be claimed), for the "step people forget" section:** capital losses must be CLAIMED to be usable, normally within four years of the end of the tax year in which they arose; unreported loss years are recoverable value. Cite https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/losses (verified 2026-07-11).
