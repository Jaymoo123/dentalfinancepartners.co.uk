## House positions touched (docs/crypto/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 15: almost all individuals are investors, not financial traders, for tax; HMRC expects trading treatment only in exceptional circumstances; the badges of trade apply as for shares and high frequency alone does not make a trade. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250 and https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim20205
- HP 16: trader status is usually a BAD outcome to chase; trading profits are income taxed up to 45% plus Class 4 NIC, versus 24% CGT; it mainly matters for loss-relief edge cases; inverts the "trader status is a prize" framing. https://www.gov.uk/income-tax-rates and https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto20250
- HP 17: day-trading in shares, forex and CFDs uses the same badges-of-trade analysis; spread-betting winnings are generally outside tax (gambling, not a trade) and spread-betting losses are equally unrelievable. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22015
- HP 2 (support): CGT 18% within the basic-rate band, 24% above (the "24% CGT" side of the comparison; present with the band split). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates
- HP 3 (support): £3,000 annual exempt amount (the CGT-side allowance in the comparison table). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/allowances

Consistency rules: the headline (HP 16) is that trader status is usually WORSE; never let the page drift into implying it is a goal. The comparison uses "24% CGT" against "income tax up to 45% plus Class 4 NIC" and the CGT side always carries the band split (HP 2). Spread-bet losses are unrelievable, stated whenever the exemption is mentioned (HP 17).

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do NOT frame trader status as advantageous or a goal; it is usually worse (HP 16). This inversion is the whole point of the page.
- When stating the CGT side of the comparison, use the band split (18% within remaining basic band, 24% above; HP 2), never a flat 18%. State the trader side as income tax up to 45% plus Class 4 NIC (do not invent a single flat rate).
- HP GAP: specific Class 4 NIC rates and thresholds for 2026/27 are NOT locked in house positions. Say "plus Class 4 National Insurance" qualitatively; FLAG for Stage 2 before printing a Class 4 rate or threshold; verify at gov.uk at build time.
- Do NOT tell the reader they "can choose" their status; it is determined by the facts under the badges of trade (HP 15). The service delivers a reasoned opinion, not an election.
- Spread betting: winnings generally outside tax BUT losses equally unrelievable (HP 17); never present the exemption as a pure upside.
- Keep property CGT, EMI/share schemes and generic sole-trader Self Assessment off this page (HP 30 scope wall); this site owns crypto, day-trading and forex only.
- Do NOT give investment advice or comment on trading strategy (regulated-advice boundary); the page is about tax status, not whether to trade.
- The status checker is a scenario/triage tool ending at "speak to us", never a binding HMRC determination (CALCULATORS.md).
