## House positions touched (docs/crypto/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 10: staking rewards taxable on receipt (misc or trading income), receipt value becomes CGT base cost (two-step). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto21200
- HP 11: airdrops taxable as income only if received in return for something; genuinely unsolicited ones are not income and enter CGT at acquisition value. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto21250
- HP 12: £1,000 trading and miscellaneous income allowance can shelter small staking receipts from income tax but does NOT remove CGT on later disposal. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tax-free-allowances-on-property-and-trading-income and https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-you-need-to-pay-tax-when-you-receive-cryptoassets
- HP 13: DeFi lending/staking guidance is manual-only and transaction-specific; 2023 consultation fix not enacted; never a settled rule. Always "HMRC current view, not settled law". https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto61000
- HP 14: many DeFi deposits and LP entries are disposals under current HMRC analysis; the flagship wedge, always flagged as HMRC's current view not settled law. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/cryptoassets-manual/crypto61000
- HP 3: AEA £3,000 (for the later CGT leg). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/allowances
- HP 2: CGT 18% within remaining basic-rate band then 24% above (later disposal). https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates

Consistency rules: UK default. DeFi and LP-disposal positions (HP 13, 14) carry the "HMRC current view, not settled law" wording EVERY time. Staking is a two-step, never presented as a single event or as double taxation. £1,000 allowance shelters income only. No investment advice.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Never present a "clean, settled" DeFi rule (HP 13). Every LP/deposit-as-disposal statement carries "HMRC's current view, not settled law".
- Do not present the 2023 consultation fix as enacted law (it is not, HP 13).
- Staking two-step: income on receipt AND CGT on later disposal; do not conflate into one event, and do not call it double taxation (the receipt value becomes base cost, HP 10).
- £1,000 allowance shelters income tax on small receipts only; it does NOT remove later CGT (HP 12).
- Unsolicited airdrops are not income (HP 11); do not blanket-tax all airdrops as income.
- CGT rate band-split, never flat 18% (HP 2); AEA £3,000 (HP 3).
