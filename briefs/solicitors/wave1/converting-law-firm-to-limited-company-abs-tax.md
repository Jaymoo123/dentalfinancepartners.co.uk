## Thesis / framing

Incorporating a law firm is two parallel projects that must both succeed: (1) the SRA authorisation of the new body corporate (a recognised body if wholly lawyer-owned, or a licensed body / ABS under the Legal Services Act 2007 Part 5 if there is non-lawyer ownership), and (2) the tax-efficient transfer of the business (goodwill, WIP, debtors, fixtures) into the company. The page must explicitly DIFFERENTIATE itself from, and cross-link to, the existing `llp-conversion-tax-guide-uk-law-firms` page, which covers the EARLIER, DIFFERENT step of a general partnership converting to an LLP. This page is the FURTHER step: an existing partnership or LLP incorporating into a body corporate (a company). The single most important tax point to get right: on a related-party incorporation, the buyer-company's 6.5% goodwill relief is RESTRICTED/effectively blocked, while the seller-partners can defer their CGT via s.162 incorporation relief.

## H2 outline (aim 10-12 sections, ~3,000-3,600 words)

1. **Two steps, not one: where this fits** · explicit differentiation block up top. The partnership to LLP conversion is a separate, earlier step (cross-link `llp-conversion-tax-guide-uk-law-firms`); THIS page is incorporating an existing partnership or LLP into a body corporate (a company). Note an LLP is already a body corporate for company-law purposes but is tax-transparent (§2); incorporating into a company changes the TAX treatment (corporation tax, §3) and the regulatory authorisation.
2. **The regulatory step: SRA recognised body vs licensed body / ABS** · the new company must be authorised by the SRA before it can carry on reserved legal activities. **Recognised body** = wholly lawyer-owned/managed company or LLP. **Licensed body / ABS** = where there is non-lawyer ownership or management; licensed under **Legal Services Act 2007 Part 5 (ss.71 to 111)**, with the SRA as a designated licensing authority (s.73, Sch 10) (§1). State you cannot simply move the practice into a company and keep trading; authorisation must be in place. Cross-link the ABS explainer.
3. **The order of operations and continuity** · authorise the new body, transfer the business, novate client matters and client-account balances (client consent, SRA Accounts Rules context, §5), transfer the PII (recognised/licensed body minimum cover £3m per claim, §10), and deal with run-off on the old entity if it ceases (6-year run-off, §10). Note timing around the firm's accounting/tax year and the 6 April 2026 rate step (§3).
4. **Transferring goodwill: the seller-partners' CGT and s.162 incorporation relief** · goodwill is the big capital asset and a sale of it is a CGT event for the partners (§9). **TCGA 1992 s.162 "Roll-over relief on transfer of business"** defers the gain where a person who is not a company transfers the business as a going concern, together with the whole assets (or the whole other than cash), wholly or partly in exchange for shares in the company; the held-over gain is deducted from the base cost of the new shares and crystallises only when the shares are sold (see verified anchor block). Cash consideration restricts the relief pro rata (§9). Often paired with a later share sale to access BADR.
5. **The buyer-company's goodwill relief: the related-party trap (the load-bearing tax point)** · a company can claim **fixed-rate 6.5% per year** relief on goodwill/relevant assets acquired on/after 1 April 2019, but ONLY as part of an acquisition that also includes qualifying IP (CTA 2009 Part 8 Ch 15A, s.879A+), and the relief is RESTRICTED on **related-party** acquisitions. When partners incorporate into THEIR OWN new company, the seller and buyer are related parties, so the 6.5% relief is generally restricted/blocked (CIRD44065 confirms a full restriction where a relevant asset is acquired from a related individual or firm and the third-party-acquisition condition is not met; a partial restriction where relevant-asset cost exceeds 6x the qualifying-IP cost). FLAG this clearly: do NOT promise the company 6.5% goodwill relief on a self-incorporation. See verified anchor block.
6. **WIP and debtors on transfer** · WIP and debtors are TRADING income, not capital (§9, §4). On transfer to the company they can crystallise an income-tax charge (taxed at income rates plus Class 4) if transferred at market value above book value; the sale/transfer agreement must split goodwill (capital) from WIP/debtors (income) clearly (§9). Note WIP is recognised under FRS 102 (§4). Cross-link `wip-treatment-on-law-firm-sale`.
7. **VAT: transfer of a going concern (TOGC)** · the transfer of the business to the company can be a **TOGC** (outside the scope of VAT) if the conditions are met (going concern, same kind of business, buyer VAT-registered/registers) (§9, §6). Note the new company must register for VAT in its own right (the old registration does not automatically carry over). Cross-link the VAT registration page.
8. **Stamp duty and the share question** · transferring the business assets into the company is not a share transaction. Note that stamp duty (0.5%) applies to later transfers of SHARES in the company on a subsequent sale (a share sale is how an incorporated firm is later sold, §9), whereas the asset/business transfer on incorporation does not attract stamp duty on shares. Keep this short and accurate; do not overclaim SDLT (only relevant if the firm owns property being transferred).
9. **Capital allowances on fixtures and fit-out transfer** · if the firm owns premises/fixtures, a CAA 2001 s.198 election fixes the fixtures value between the old firm and the company (2-year limit, §8); AIA £1m and the WDA rates (main-rate 18% reducing to 14% from 1 Apr 2026 for CT, FA 2026 s.28; special rate 6%) apply going forward (§8). Keep brief; cross-link the capital-allowances page if one exists.
10. **After incorporation: how owners are now taxed** · salary plus dividend (10.75% / 35.75% from 6 April 2026, FA 2026 s.4, §3), employer NIC 15% above £5,000 (§3), s.455 on director loans (33.75% pre-6-Apr-2026 / 35.75% on/after, §3). Brief; cross-link A1 for the full extraction comparison.

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **TCGA 1992 s.162** · heading verbatim: **"Roll-over relief on transfer of business"**. Conditions (s.162(1)): *"a person who is not a company transfers to a company a business as a going concern, together with the whole assets of the business, or together with the whole of those assets other than cash"*, and the transfer is *"wholly or partly in exchange for shares issued by the company"*. Mechanism: the held-over gain is deducted from the chargeable gains on the transferred assets and is set against (deducted from the allowable cost of) the new shares, deferring tax until the shares are disposed of. Cash consideration restricts the relief pro rata (§9).
- **Legal Services Act 2007 Part 5** · heading verbatim: **"Part 5 Alternative business structures"** · sections 71 to 111 (confirmed) · s.71 "Carrying on of activities by licensed bodies"; s.72 "Licensable body"; s.73 "Licensing authorities and relevant licensing authorities"; Schedule 10 "Designation of approved regulators as licensing authorities" (the SRA is a designated licensing authority) (§1).
- **CTA 2009 Part 8 Ch 15A goodwill relief, related-party restriction** · confirmed via HMRC **CIRD44065** ("Restrictions for goodwill and relevant assets: Summary of when the restrictions apply"): relief for relevant assets (goodwill etc.) is at a **fixed rate of 6.5%** under Chapter 3 unless a restriction applies; a **FULL restriction** applies where a relevant asset is acquired from a **related individual or firm** and the **third-party acquisition condition is not met**; a **partial restriction** where the cost of the relevant assets acquired is more than **6 times** the cost of the qualifying IP assets. Relief is for acquisitions on/after 1 April 2019; no relief for goodwill acquired 8 July 2015 to 31 March 2019 (§9). CONCLUSION FOR WRITER: on a self-incorporation (partners to their own company) the parties are related, so the 6.5% relief is generally NOT available; state this plainly.
- **BADR / s.162 / TOGC** sanity per §9: BADR rate 14% (6 Apr 2025 to 5 Apr 2026) then **18% from 6 April 2026**, £1m lifetime limit; TOGC outside scope of VAT if conditions met; s.198 fixtures election 2-year limit (§8).

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative; label as not advice)

1. **Goodwill on incorporation, both sides.** Partnership goodwill valued at, say, £400,000 transferred to the new company for shares. Seller side: the partners' CGT gain is deferred under s.162 (gain rolled into the base cost of their shares). Buyer side: the company gets NO 6.5% amortisation relief on that goodwill because it is a related-party acquisition (CIRD44065 full restriction). Caption: "the relief that survives is the seller's deferral, not the company's amortisation."
2. **The WIP/debtors split.** Show £120,000 WIP and £80,000 debtors transferring: these are income, so a poorly-drafted agreement that lumps them into "goodwill" risks an income-tax mischaracterisation. Caption: "draft the agreement to split capital from income, or HMRC will."
3. **The regulatory critical path.** Sketch the sequence: apply to the SRA for the new body's authorisation (recognised body, or licensed body/ABS if non-lawyer owners), then complete the business transfer once authorised, novating client matters and client-account balances with consent. Caption: "the company cannot bill for reserved work until the SRA has authorised it."
