## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

A mixed payment (one receipt that is partly the firm's own money and partly client money) is one of the commonest day-to-day SRA Accounts Rules situations and one of the easiest to get wrong. The rule is short and strict: under **Rule 4.2** you must **allocate promptly** any funds from mixed payments to the correct client account or business account. There is no "park it in suspense and sort it later" and no "leave the whole lot in office because most of it is billed". The page must teach the reader to (1) identify a mixed receipt, (2) work out which slice is client money (apply the Rule 2.1 definition) and which is office money, (3) allocate each promptly to the right account, and (4) respect the Rule 4.3 bill-before-transfer discipline when the office slice is the firm's costs. Practical, ledger-level, breach-aware.

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **The rule: SRA Accounts Rules 2019, Rule 4.2** (https://www.sra.org.uk/solicitors/standards-regulations/accounts-rules/). CONFIRMED rule number = **Rule 4.2**. Verbatim: *"You ensure that you allocate promptly any funds from mixed payments you receive to the correct client account or business account."* CRITICAL: the rule says **"promptly"**, NOT a fixed number of days. Do NOT write "14-day rule" or any numeric deadline for the split (the 14-day rule is a VAT tax-point concept, a different topic, §6.D, do not import it here).
- **Rule 4.1 (the separation principle):** *"You keep client money separate from money belonging to the authorised body."* (the foundation the split serves).
- **Rule 4.3 (bill before transferring the firm's costs out):** verbatim: *"Where you are holding client money and some or all of that money will be used to pay your costs: (a) you must give a bill of costs, or other written notification of the costs incurred, to the client or the paying party; (b) this must be done before you transfer any client money from a client account to make the payment; and (c) any such payment must be for the specific sum identified in the bill of costs."*
- **Client money definition: Rule 2.1**, four limbs (a) regulated services, (b) on behalf of a third party, (c) as trustee/office-holder, (d) fees and unpaid disbursements held before a bill (§5). Use this to classify each slice of the mixed receipt.
- **Banking-facility prohibition: Rule 3.3** *"You must not use a client account to provide banking facilities to clients or third parties; payments into, and transfers or withdrawals from, a client account must be in respect of the delivery by you of regulated services."* (§5).
- **Reconciliation: Rule 8.3** (at least every five weeks, signed off by the COFA or a manager) (§5) - the control that surfaces an unallocated mixed-money slice.
