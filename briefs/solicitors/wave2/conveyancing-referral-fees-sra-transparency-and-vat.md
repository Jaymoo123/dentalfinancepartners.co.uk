## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

Referral fees are a fact of conveyancing economics (estate agents, mortgage brokers, comparison sites and panel managers all refer work) and, unlike in personal-injury work, they are **not banned**: the **LASPO 2012 referral-fee ban applies only to personal-injury and death claims** (s.56), so conveyancing referral arrangements remain lawful **provided they are properly disclosed**. The SRA controls them through transparency, not prohibition: the **SRA Code of Conduct paragraph 5.1** requires the firm to tell the client about any financial or other interest an introducer has in referring them, and about any relevant fee-sharing arrangement (which must be in writing); the **SRA Transparency Rules** require the firm to publish conveyancing prices including the VAT position and likely disbursements. On the accounting side, a referral or introduction fee is **consideration for a standard-rated supply of introduction services**: if the introducer is VAT-registered, the fee carries **20% VAT**, and a fee the firm itself receives for an introduction it makes is likewise its standard-rated income (§6). This page separates the regulatory question (allowed, with disclosure) from the tax question (standard-rated), and kills the LASPO confusion.

## H2 outline (aim 9-12 sections, ~3,000-3,600 words)

1. **Referral fees in conveyancing: allowed, but disclosed** · framing. State the headline: conveyancing referral fees are lawful (the LASPO ban is PI-only) but are governed by SRA transparency and disclosure, and carry VAT. Signpost the three threads: the LASPO carve-out, the SRA disclosure/transparency duties, and the VAT treatment.
2. **What counts as a referral fee** · define: a payment (or benefit) for the introduction of a client, to or from an estate agent, mortgage broker, new-build developer, comparison/lead-generation site, panel manager or other introducer. Note the SRA's presumption (Code paragraph 5.2): where it appears a payment was a referral fee, it is treated as one unless the firm shows otherwise. Distinguish a genuine referral fee from a fee-share and from a marketing/advertising spend.
3. **The LASPO ban: personal injury only, NOT conveyancing** · THE differentiator. LASPO 2012 **s.56** prohibits a regulated person paying or being paid a referral fee in connection with **"prescribed legal business"**, and s.56(4) limits "prescribed legal business" to claims (or potential claims) for **damages for personal injury or death** (or arising from circumstances involving personal injury or death), plus anything specified by regulations. Conveyancing is NOT prescribed legal business, so the LASPO ban does not reach it. State this plainly and cite the section. This is the section that owns the "are referral fees banned?" query.
4. **The SRA Code disclosure duty (paragraph 5.1)** · the core regulatory control. The firm must ensure: (5.1(a)) the client is informed of any financial or other interest the firm/introducer has in the referral; (5.1(b)) the client is informed of any fee-sharing arrangement relevant to their matter; (5.1(c)) the fee-sharing agreement is in writing; and (5.1(d)) the firm does not receive or make referral payments in respect of clients who are the subject of criminal proceedings. Frame as: disclosure to the client is mandatory, and the firm must act in the client's best interests (independence not compromised by the referral).
5. **The SRA Transparency Rules: published prices, VAT and disbursements** · the SRA Transparency Rules require firms to publish cost information for specified services including **residential conveyancing** (and probate). Rule 1.5 requires disclosure of the total/average/range of costs, the basis of charging, likely disbursements and their cost, and the **VAT treatment**. Tie this to referrals: where a referral arrangement affects what the client pays, the published-price and disclosure duties bite. Cross-link `fee-structure-for-uk-residential-conveyancing-firms`.
6. **VAT on referral fees paid to introducers** · §6. An introduction is a **supply of services**; a referral fee is consideration for that supply, **standard-rated at 20%** where the introducer is VAT-registered. The firm paying the fee can recover the input VAT (if registered and the introduction supports its taxable supplies) but cannot treat a referral fee as a disbursement to the client (it fails the eight conditions, §6, and is the firm's own cost). State this clearly.
7. **VAT on referral fees the firm RECEIVES** · §6. Where the firm itself introduces a client to a third party (e.g. a mortgage broker, surveyor or financial adviser) and is paid for it, that fee is the firm's **standard-rated income at 20%** (a supply of introduction services), included in taxable turnover towards the £90,000 registration threshold. It is not exempt and not a disbursement. Note the partial-exemption point does not arise (introduction is taxable, not exempt). Cross-link `do-uk-solicitors-charge-vat`.
8. **Accounting and bookkeeping for referral arrangements** · how to record them: referral fees paid as a marketing/cost line with recoverable input VAT (if registered); referral fees received as taxable income; the written fee-sharing agreement on file (5.1(c)); a register of introducer arrangements for SRA and audit purposes. Keep referral payments out of client account (they are office-account business, not client money, §5). Cross-link `client-money-accounting-solicitors`.
9. **Risks and red lines** · short. Independence and best-interests duty (the referral must not skew the advice); the criminal-proceedings carve-out (5.1(d)); the Transparency-Rules publication obligation; and that mislabelling a referral fee as a disbursement to the client is both a VAT error and a transparency breach. Keep proportionate, not a regulatory-defence guide.
10. **Worked sketches** · see below.
11. **Practical checklist** · (i) confirm the work is not PI/death (LASPO does not bite conveyancing); (ii) disclose the introducer's interest and any fee-share to the client (5.1); (iii) put the fee-sharing agreement in writing (5.1(c)); (iv) treat referral fees paid as standard-rated input (recover VAT if registered); (v) treat referral fees received as standard-rated income; (vi) publish prices/VAT/disbursements per the Transparency Rules; (vii) keep referral money in office account. ## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at PRIMARY SOURCE 2026-06-03)

- **LASPO 2012 referral-fee ban is PI/death only** (legislation.gov.uk LASPO 2012 Part 2, ss.56 to 60, verified 2026-06-03):
  - **s.56(1) VERBATIM:** *"A regulated person is in breach of this section if (a) the regulated person refers prescribed legal business to another person and is paid or has been paid for the referral, or (b) prescribed legal business is referred to the regulated person, and the regulated person pays or has paid for the referral."*
  - **s.56(4) VERBATIM (scope):** *"Prescribed legal business"* means business involving legal services where the legal services relate to *"a claim or potential claim for damages for personal injury or death"*, or *"any other claim or potential claim for damages arising out of circumstances involving personal injury or death"*, or business *"of a description specified in regulations made by the Lord Chancellor"*. Conveyancing is NOT within this, so the LASPO ban does not apply to conveyancing referral fees.
- **SRA Code of Conduct, referral disclosure (sra.org.uk Code of Conduct for Solicitors / for Firms, paragraph 5, verified 2026-06-03):**
  - **5.1(a) VERBATIM:** clients are informed of *"any financial or other interest which you or your business or employer has in referring the client to another person or which an introducer has in referring the client to you"*.
  - **5.1(b) VERBATIM:** clients are informed of *"any fee sharing arrangement that is relevant to their matter"*.
  - **5.1(c) VERBATIM:** *"the fee sharing agreement is in writing"*.
  - **5.1(d) VERBATIM:** *"you do not receive payments relating to a referral or make payments to an introducer in respect of clients who are the subject of criminal proceedings"*.
  - **5.2 VERBATIM (presumption):** *"Where it appears to the SRA that you have made or received a referral fee, the payment will be treated as a referral fee unless you show that the payment was not made as such."*
- **SRA Transparency Rules (sra.org.uk Transparency Rules, verified 2026-06-03):** firms must publish cost information for specified services including **residential conveyancing** (freehold/leasehold sales or purchases, mortgages/re-mortgages) and **probate** (uncontested, UK assets). **Rule 1.5** requires disclosure of the total cost or average/range, the basis of charging, likely disbursements and their cost, and the **VAT treatment** (whether VAT is included).
- **VAT on referral/introduction fees (§6, VATA 1994):** an introduction is a supply of services; a referral fee is consideration for a standard-rated supply at **20%**. Referral fee PAID to a VAT-registered introducer carries VAT (recoverable as input tax by the firm if registered and supporting taxable supplies); a referral fee RECEIVED by the firm for an introduction is the firm's standard-rated income, counting towards taxable turnover (registration threshold £90,000). A referral fee is NOT a disbursement to the client (fails the eight conditions, §6) and must not be billed as one.

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice)

1. **Estate-agent referral, disclosure and VAT.** A conveyancing firm pays an estate agent a referral fee for each instruction sent its way. The agent is VAT-registered, so the fee carries 20% VAT, which the firm recovers as input tax. The firm discloses the agent's interest to the client and keeps the written arrangement on file (5.1(c)). LASPO does not apply (this is conveyancing, not PI). Caption: "conveyancing referral fees are allowed if disclosed; the introducer's fee carries VAT, which the firm can recover."
2. **Firm receives an introduction fee.** The firm refers a buyer to a mortgage broker and is paid for the introduction. That fee is the firm's standard-rated income at 20%, declared as taxable turnover, and the client is told of the firm's interest in the referral (5.1(a)). Caption: "a fee you receive for an introduction is your standard-rated income, not a disbursement."
3. **The LASPO trap avoided.** A firm wrongly believes all referral fees are banned because it has heard of the LASPO ban. In fact LASPO s.56 catches only personal-injury/death claims; the firm's conveyancing referral arrangement is lawful with disclosure. Caption: "the referral-fee ban is personal-injury only; conveyancing referrals are permitted with disclosure."
