## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

Legal aid work is NOT VAT-exempt. For a VAT-registered firm, advice and representation funded by the Legal Aid Agency is a standard-rated supply at 20% (§6); the firm does not absorb the VAT, because the LAA pays VAT on top of the assessed fee. The practical mechanic catches firms out: you submit the claim NET of VAT, and the LAA adds VAT at source from your payment mandate, so your output VAT is funded rather than eroded. The real problem with legal aid is not the VAT rate, it is timing: fixed fees, monthly payment regimes, payments on account and slow final assessment lengthen lock-up and create a tax-point question (when is the output VAT actually due?). This page nails the VAT position first, then turns to the cash-flow and tax-point reality.

## H2 outline (aim 9-12 sections, ~2,900-3,500 words)

1. **Is legal aid work VAT-exempt? No.** Lead with the myth-buster. Legal services are standard-rated at 20% (§6); there is no exemption for publicly funded work. A VAT-registered firm accounts for output VAT on legal-aid fees just as on private work. Signpost the "LAA adds the VAT, you do not lose it" point and the cash-flow theme.
2. **How the LAA pays VAT: bill net, VAT added at source** · the mechanic. A VAT-registered firm submits its assessed bill/claim NET of VAT; the funder adds VAT according to the firm's payment mandate / VAT-registration evidence. Verbatim from anchor block. Stress that the firm must hold and evidence VAT registration for the funder to add VAT, and that the firm still accounts for the output VAT to HMRC.
3. **Self-billing and the zero-VAT-shown trap** · note the common practical issue: self-billed remittances/statements can show VAT content as zero or be hard to reconcile even though the underlying work is standard-rated. Writer: frame as a reconciliation discipline (reconcile gross receipts to your own VAT records, do not rely on the funder's statement to compute your output VAT). Attribute to practitioner experience, not a single statute.
4. **The tax point on legal-aid bills (VATA 1994 s.6, §6.D)** · when output VAT is due. The basic tax point is when the services are performed; for legal-aid work HMRC treats the basic tax point as arising on COMPLETION of the case (VATTOS8560/8570). Verbatim in anchor block. Tie to s.6(4) (earlier of invoice or payment) and the 14-day rule, and to the centrally negotiated extension HMRC operates for solicitors' legal-aid claims. Cross-link `vat-tax-point-time-of-supply-law-firm-billing`.
5. **Statutory/standard monthly payments and payments on account: do they create a tax point?** · THE nuance. A Standard Monthly Payment (SMP) or payment on account that relates to a case already COMPLETED does not itself create a fresh tax point; but where part of an SMP/POA is offset against ongoing cases or work not yet started it is a PRE-PAYMENT and creates a tax point on that part (VATTOS8560). Verbatim in anchor block. Practical effect: account for output VAT on advance/POA elements when received to the extent they pre-pay future work.
6. **The cash-flow lag of legal aid (lock-up)** · the working-capital story. Fixed fees, controlled-work reporting cycles, monthly payment regimes, and slow final assessment of certificated/high-cost work all stretch WIP days and debtor days. Tie to lock-up generally (§4) and cross-link `law-firm-cash-flow-management` and `law-firm-lock-up-reduction`. Frame POAs/SMPs as a partial mitigant, not a cure.
7. **Payments on account as a cash-flow tool (and their VAT consequence)** · POAs smooth cash flow but, per section 5, the part that pre-pays future work creates a tax point. So a POA helps cash flow but can accelerate output VAT. Net it out for the reader.
8. **Disbursements on legal-aid matters** · the eight-condition disbursement test still applies (§6). Counsel's fees on legal-aid work, experts' fees, court fees: which are genuine disbursements (outside the scope, recharged with no VAT) versus a cost component of the firm's standard-rated supply. Counsel default is a supply to the firm (§6.B) unless client-instructed. Cross-link `disbursements-vat-treatment-uk-law-firms` and `counsel-fees-vat-guide-uk-law-firms`. Writer: do NOT label every claimed outlay a disbursement.
9. **WIP and basis-period interaction for legal-aid practices** · unbilled legal-aid WIP is recognised under FRS 102 and taxed as recognised, not when the LAA pays (§4); LLPs are on the accruals basis (excluded from the cash basis, §4). So a firm can be taxed on profit it has not yet been paid for, which compounds the legal-aid cash-flow squeeze. Cross-link `wip-valuation-method-uk-law-firms`.
10. **Mixed private/legal-aid firms** · a firm doing both should not assume different VAT treatment; both are standard-rated. The difference is the billing/payment route (LAA self-billing vs private invoicing), not the VAT liability.
11. **Practical compliance + cash-flow checklist** · (i) confirm and evidence VAT registration so the LAA adds VAT; (ii) submit claims net of VAT; (iii) reconcile gross LAA receipts to your own VAT records, never rely on the remittance's VAT field; (iv) account for output VAT at the case-completion tax point, plus on the pre-payment element of any POA/SMP; (v) apply the eight-condition test to each claimed outlay; (vi) forecast lock-up around the LAA payment cycle; (vii) recognise WIP under FRS 102 and reserve tax accordingly. ## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **Legal aid work is a standard-rated taxable supply** · legal services supplied by a solicitor are standard-rated for VAT at 20% with no exemption (§6, locked; primary source VATA 1994 + VAT Notice 700). HMRC's time-of-supply manual treats solicitors' legal-aid work as a taxable supply on which output VAT is accounted (VATTOS8560/8570).
- **LAA pays VAT in addition; firm bills net (verbatim, legal aid billing guidance, verified 2026-06-03):** *"If you are a solicitor who is VAT registered when you are preparing your account, you should submit the bill net of VAT and VAT will be added at source according to the information you have provided in your payment mandate."* Source: Scottish Legal Aid Board "VAT, how it is applied on legal aid accounts" (https://www.slab.org.uk/guidance/value-added-tax-vat-how-it-is-applied-on-legal-aid-accounts/) confirming the net-bill / VAT-added-at-source mechanic. NOTE FOR WRITER: the SLAB page is Scotland; the LAA (England and Wales) operates the same self-billing model (firm submits net, VAT added per the VAT-registration mandate). Attribute the principle ("you bill net, the funder adds VAT") generally and note the firm must evidence VAT registration for VAT to be added; do not quote SLAB as if it were the LAA.
- **Tax point on legal-aid work, contracting arrangements, VATTOS8560 (verbatim, verified 2026-06-03):** *"there is a basic tax point for each case on completion"*; on Standard Monthly Payments: *"to the extent that an SMP relates to a case that has been completed ... the payment itself does not create a tax point"*, but *"if any part of the SMP is to be offset against ongoing cases, or work that has not yet commenced, it represents a pre-payment for those supplies and so creates a tax point"* (https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-time-of-supply/vattos8560).
- **Tax point on non-contracting legal-aid work, VATTOS8570 (verbatim, verified 2026-06-03):** *"the basic tax point occurs when all the work, except the invoicing, has been completed"*; and *"tax must be accounted for on the basis of the normal tax point rules and adjusted later in the event of any subsequent reduction in the fee"* (i.e. account on the normal tax point, then adjust if the assessed fee is reduced) (https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-time-of-supply/vattos8570). HMRC operates a centrally negotiated extension to the 14-day rule for solicitors' legal-aid claims.
- **General time of supply, VATA 1994 s.6 (§6.D, verified 2026-06-03):** basic tax point when services performed (s.6(3)); earlier of VAT invoice or payment received overrides (s.6(4)); 14-day rule (s.6(5)). Standard rate **20%** since 4 January 2011.
- **Disbursement test (§6):** all eight conditions in VAT Notice 700 §25.1.1 must be met for an outlay to be a disbursement (outside scope, no VAT on recharge); otherwise it is part of the firm's standard-rated supply. Counsel's fee defaults to a supply to the firm (§6.B).
- **WIP / basis period (§4):** WIP recognised under FRS 102; LLPs excluded from the cash basis (accruals applies); tax-year basis from 2024/25.

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice)

1. **Net bill, VAT added at source.** A VAT-registered firm completes a legal-aid matter and submits its assessed bill net of VAT. The funder adds 20% VAT per the firm's VAT mandate and pays gross. The firm accounts for that 20% as output VAT to HMRC. Caption: "the VAT is funded on top of your fee, not taken out of it, but you still owe it to HMRC."
2. **Completion tax point.** Output VAT on a legal-aid case is due for the VAT period in which the case completes (the basic tax point), even if final assessment and payment come later. Caption: "the tax point is completion, not the day the LAA pays you."
3. **POA pre-paying future work.** The firm receives a monthly payment, part of which relates to a completed case (no new tax point) and part of which is offset against ongoing work (a pre-payment, so a tax point arises on that part when received). Caption: "split a monthly payment between completed work and pre-paid work; the pre-paid part carries a tax point now."
4. **Lock-up squeeze.** A certificated matter runs 14 months before final assessment; the firm recognises WIP and is taxed on profit under FRS 102 long before the LAA settles. Caption: "you can pay tax on legal-aid profit before you are paid, so reserve and forecast for it."
