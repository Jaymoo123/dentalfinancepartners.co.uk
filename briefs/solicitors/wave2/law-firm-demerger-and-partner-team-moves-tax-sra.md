## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

A demerger or partner team move is the mirror image of a merger, and it is a tax decision and a regulatory decision at the same time. On the **tax** side, the same income-vs-capital line governs: **WIP and debtors moving with the team are trading income** (taxed at income-tax rates plus Class 4 NIC for the individual partners, ITTOIA 2005 ss.182 to 185 on cessation/transfer of WIP), while **goodwill is a capital asset** (CGT, with Business Asset Disposal Relief in play on a qualifying disposal). On the **regulatory** side, you cannot simply move client matters and client-account balances to the new firm: each affected client must be told and must **consent** to the transfer of their file and money, and any new entity the team forms needs **SRA authorisation** before it can practise. The **capital account** of each departing partner has to be valued and settled under the partnership/LLP agreement, and **restrictive covenants** (non-compete, non-solicitation of clients and staff) shape what the leavers can do and take. Get the deal documents to split goodwill (capital) from WIP/debtors (income) cleanly, treat client consent and SRA authorisation as completion conditions, and value the capital account by the agreement.

## H2 outline (aim 9-12 sections, ~3,000-3,500 words)

1. **Demerger, team move, split: what we mean** · define the three close cousins. A **demerger / split** (a department or a group of equity partners hiving off to form or join another firm), a **lateral team move** (a group of fee-earners and their matters moving between firms, the leavers usually employees or fixed-share members rather than full equity), and a **partner exit** that takes work with it. Frame as the reverse of `merging-two-uk-law-firms-tax-and-sra`: instead of combining, you are separating, and the same income/capital and regulatory tests apply in reverse. Signpost the four threads: WIP/goodwill apportionment, client consent, capital settlement, covenants.
2. **The income-vs-capital line, in reverse** · the spine of the tax analysis (§9, §4). On a split, work and value leave the firm. **Goodwill is capital** (a CGT asset); **WIP and debtors are trading income** (ITTOIA 2005 ss.182 to 185, brought into account as an income receipt on cessation/transfer, taxed at income-tax rates plus Class 4 NIC for individual partners). The documents must split any consideration or apportionment between goodwill (capital) and WIP/debtors (income), exactly as on a merger, because the latter is taxed at up to ~47% and the former potentially at the BADR rate. State that on many team moves there is **no purchase of goodwill at all** (the team simply leaves and the old firm keeps its goodwill), which changes the picture.
3. **Apportioning WIP and debtors when matters move** · the practical mechanics. When live matters transfer to the new firm mid-retainer, the WIP and any billed-but-unpaid debtors built up at the **old** firm belong to the old firm and are its trading income; work done after the move is the new firm's. The transferring documents should record the **WIP cut-off** (value of unbilled time and disbursements at the transfer date), how pre-transfer debtors are collected and accounted for, and who bills what. Caution against treating a WIP transfer as if it were capital. Cross-link `wip-treatment-on-law-firm-sale` and `wip-valuation-method`.
4. **Goodwill on a demerger** · the capital side (§9). Where a demerging group genuinely **acquires** goodwill (e.g. it buys out a book of business or a brand), that is a capital disposal for the transferring firm/partners: CGT at the standard 18% / 24% rates (from 30 October 2024), or the BADR rate on a qualifying disposal of the whole or part of the business. Often, though, a team move involves **no goodwill payment** (the leavers cannot take the firm's goodwill, and personal goodwill in a professional firm is hard to value/transfer), so be honest that the headline asset on a split is frequently WIP and clients, not a goodwill cheque.
5. **BADR and the standard CGT rates on a qualifying exit** · the relief detail (§9). Where a partner/member disposes of **the whole or part of the business** (a genuine demerger of a slice of the partnership can qualify), BADR cuts qualifying gains up to the **£1m lifetime limit** to **14% for disposals 6 April 2025 to 5 April 2026, then 18% from 6 April 2026** (10% to 5 April 2025). The qualifying conditions must be met **throughout the two years to disposal** (trading business; for a partnership/LLP, a genuine partner/member disposing of the whole or part of the business). Gains above the £1m limit or non-qualifying gains are at the standard **18% / 24%** (from 30 October 2024), after the **£3,000** annual exempt amount (2025/26 and 2026/27). The 6 April 2026 step to 18% is a live timing lever for a late-2025/26 completion. A partner caught by the salaried member rules (§2.A) may not have a BADR-qualifying interest.
6. **Settling the departing partner's capital account** · the money owed out (§2). A partner/member is taxed on their **allocated profit share, not drawings**, so on exit the firm must (a) finalise the leaver's profit share to the exit date, (b) value and repay their **capital account** (capital contributed plus undrawn profits, less any drawings and amounts owed back) per the partnership/LLP agreement, and (c) handle any tax reserve held for the leaver. Note the **basis-period reform** wrinkle: a partner leaving may have a cessation event interacting with any remaining transition-profit spreading (§4) and overlap relief from their own records. Where the leaver borrowed to fund a capital buy-in, the qualifying-loan interest relief (ITA 2007 ss.398 to 412) stops when they cease to be a partner and the capital comes out. Keep the capital settlement and the tax settlement distinct.
7. **SRA: client consent to transfer matters and balances** · the core regulatory gate (§5). You cannot move a client's file or client-account money to the new firm without the **client's instruction/consent**. Each affected client must be told of the move and asked whether they want their matter to transfer (to the new firm), remain (with the old firm) or go elsewhere. Client money moves only on that authority, under the SRA Accounts Rules 2019: it stays in a separate client account (Rule 3), every transfer must relate to the delivery of regulated services (Rule 3.3), the account is reconciled at least every five weeks signed off by the COFA or a manager (Rule 8.3), and an accountant's report may be due (Rule 12). Treat the client-balance transfer as a regulated client-money exercise with an audit trail, not an internal book transfer.
8. **SRA: authorising the new entity and notifying changes** · the other regulatory gate (§1). If the departing team forms a **new LLP or company**, that entity needs **SRA authorisation as a recognised body before it can practise**; any non-lawyer owner or manager triggers **ABS / licensed-body** authorisation under Part 5 of the Legal Services Act 2007 (the SRA is the licensing authority). The new firm needs a designated **COLP and COFA**. The old firm notifies the SRA of its change in managers/owners and updates its COLP/COFA. Treat authorisation as a completion condition: the team cannot start billing through an unauthorised entity. Cross-link `law-firm-partnership-tax-guide` and the structure pages.
9. **Restrictive covenants and what the leavers can take** · the protection layer. Partnership/LLP and employment agreements usually contain **restrictive covenants**: non-compete (for a limited period/area), non-solicitation of clients, non-dealing, and non-poaching of staff. They are enforceable only so far as they protect a legitimate business interest and go no further than reasonable. This shapes which clients the team may approach, whether the move can be announced, and the risk of a dispute. Flag that the **client's free choice of solicitor** (a regulatory principle) sits alongside, and can cut across, a non-solicitation covenant. This is legal-practice territory, so keep it as a flag to take legal advice, not a covenant-drafting guide.
10. **PII, run-off and continuity of cover** · the insurance thread (§10). On any structural change check PII: the **old firm** may need run-off cover if an entity ceases (six years of run-off on cessation, on the SRA Minimum Terms); the **new firm** needs its own PII on the Minimum Terms (minimum sum insured £3m for a recognised/licensed body, £2m for a sole practitioner/partnership) before it practises. Make sure no matter falls into an uninsured gap during the transition.

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; from HP, pre-verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **WIP / debtors = income; goodwill = capital (§9)** · A partnership/LLP has no shares, so a transfer of its business is an asset/business transfer. **Goodwill is a capital asset** (CGT, not income tax). **WIP and debtors are trading income** (income-tax rates + Class 4 NIC if brought into account as a trading receipt), so the documents must split goodwill (capital) from WIP/debtors (income). Statutory anchor for WIP-on-cessation as income: **ITTOIA 2005 ss.182 to 185** (valuation of WIP / trading stock on cessation, brought in as an income receipt, not capital).
- **BADR (§9)** · reduced CGT on qualifying gains up to a **£1m lifetime limit per individual**. Rate **10%** to 5 Apr 2025; **14%** 6 Apr 2025 to 5 Apr 2026; **18%** from 6 Apr 2026. Conditions held throughout the **2-year period to disposal**: trading business; for an asset sale of a partnership/LLP interest, a genuine partner/member disposing of the **whole or part of the business**. A salaried-member-rule-caught member (§2.A) may not have a qualifying interest. Anchor: TCGA 1992 ss.169H to 169S; rate schedule gov.uk/business-asset-disposal-relief.
- **Standard (non-BADR) CGT (§9)** · individuals **18%** within the basic-rate band, **24%** above, on **all** chargeable assets (including goodwill) **from 30 October 2024**; annual exempt amount **£3,000** (2025/26 and 2026/27). Anchor: gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates.
- **Capital account / member taxed on profit share not drawings (§2)** · ITTOIA 2005 s.863 (LLP transparency), s.850 (allocation). Qualifying-loan interest relief on a capital buy-in: ITA 2007 ss.398 to 412, available only while a partner and the capital stays in (so it ends on exit).
- **Basis period reform (§4)** · tax-year basis from **2024/25** (transition year 2023/24); transition profits spread **20% per year across 2023/24 to 2027/28** (balance in the fifth year); on permanent cessation the remaining balance is brought into charge in the cessation year. A departing partner computes overlap relief from their own records. Anchor: Finance Act 2022 s.7 and Schedule 1.
- **SRA client consent + client money on transfer (§5)** · transferring a client's matter and client-account money to the new firm needs the client's instruction/consent. SRA Accounts Rules 2019: Rule 3 (separate client account), Rule 3.3 (every transfer must relate to the delivery of regulated services, no banking facility), Rule 8.3 (reconciliation at least every five weeks, COFA/manager sign-off), Rule 12 (accountant's report if client money held; exemption average not exceeding £10,000 AND maximum not exceeding £250,000, do NOT write "£250"). Anchor: sra.org.uk SRA Accounts Rules 2019.
- **SRA authorisation of the new entity (§1)** · a new LLP/company needs SRA authorisation as a recognised body before practising; non-lawyer owners/managers trigger ABS / licensed-body authorisation under **Part 5 of the Legal Services Act 2007** (SRA is licensing authority); a designated COLP and COFA are required. Anchor: Legal Services Act 2007 (c.29) Part 5; SRA Authorisation of Firms Rules.
- **PII (§10)** · run-off cover **six years** on cessation; minimum sum insured **£3m** for a recognised/licensed body (LLP, company, ABS), **£2m** for a sole practitioner/partnership; no monetary limit on defence costs. Anchor: SRA Indemnity Insurance Rules + Minimum Terms and Conditions.
- **Restrictive covenants** · enforceable only so far as they protect a legitimate business interest and are reasonable in scope; the client's free choice of solicitor is a regulatory principle that can cut across a non-solicitation covenant. Treat as a flag to take legal advice (no covenant-drafting on the page).

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice; no real figures presented as a quote)

1. **A team move with live matters and WIP.** A group of fee-earners moves from Firm A to Firm B and their clients choose to transfer. At the move date, Firm A has unbilled WIP and billed-but-unpaid debtors on those matters. Show: that WIP and those debtors are Firm A's **trading income** (taxed at income rates + Class 4 NIC for A's partners), recorded at a clear WIP cut-off; work after the move is Firm B's; each client must **consent** to their file and any client-account balance transferring, with the money moved under the Accounts Rules and an audit trail. Caption: "the work built up before the move is the old firm's income; the clients still have to say yes."
2. **A demerger of a department, partial business disposal.** Two of six equity partners hive off the firm's commercial-property team into a new LLP, taking goodwill attributable to that book. Show: goodwill is a **capital** disposal for the leaving partners (CGT; BADR at 14% to 5 April 2026 on qualifying gains up to the £1m lifetime limit if it is a disposal of part of the business and the two-year conditions are met, then 18% from 6 April 2026); WIP/debtors transferring are **income**; the new LLP needs **SRA authorisation** before it can practise; clients consent to transfer. Caption: "goodwill is capital and may get BADR; WIP is income; the new firm needs authorising first."
3. **Settling a departing partner's capital account.** A partner leaves to join another practice. Show: finalise their profit share to the exit date (taxed on the allocation, not drawings); value and repay their capital account per the LLP agreement; the qualifying-loan interest relief on their original buy-in ends when the capital comes out; check the basis-period transition-profit and overlap-relief position; observe any restrictive covenant on which clients they may approach. Caption: "the capital account is settled by the agreement; the tax follows the profit share, not the cash drawn."

## How this DIFFERENTIATES from the nearest existing page
