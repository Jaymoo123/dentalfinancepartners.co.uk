## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

A CFA is a no-win-no-fee agreement (CLSA 1990 s.58): fees are payable "only in specified circumstances", and the firm can charge a success fee (a percentage increase on its base costs) capped at 100% by the CFA Order 2013, with a 25% cap on the success fee taken from a personal-injury claimant's damages. The legal framework is the easy half. The hard half is accounting and tax: because the fee is contingent, the firm should NOT recognise revenue (or the success fee) until it is PROBABLE the firm will win and the amount can be measured reliably (FRS 102 Section 23), and the VAT tax point does not arise until the firm issues a bill/VAT invoice or receives payment, which on a CFA is typically at successful conclusion (§6.D). Until then, WIP on a CFA matter sits at a value that reflects the contingency, not full charge-out rate. The page sets out the s.58 limits, then the revenue-recognition and VAT-timing rules that actually drive the firm's tax.

## H2 outline (aim 9-12 sections, ~2,900-3,500 words)

1. **What a CFA is, and why its accounting is different** · lead: a CFA is no-win-no-fee under s.58; fees are payable only in specified circumstances. That contingency is what makes the accounting and VAT timing non-obvious. Signpost the success-fee caps and the revenue-recognition/tax-point answer.
2. **The statutory framework: CLSA 1990 s.58** · the legal anchor. A CFA provides for fees payable only in specified circumstances; a CFA "provides for a success fee" if it increases the fee in specified circumstances; the success-fee percentage must not exceed the maximum specified by order of the Lord Chancellor. Verbatim in anchor block. Note CFAs are enforceable only if they satisfy s.58 conditions.
3. **The success-fee cap: 100% of base costs (CFA Order 2013)** · the maximum percentage increase for the purposes of s.58(4)(c) is 100% (CFA Order 2013 art 3). So the success fee can at most double the base costs. Verbatim in anchor block.
4. **The 25% personal-injury damages cap** · in PI proceedings at first instance the success fee deducted from the client's damages is capped at 25% of general damages (pain, suffering, loss of amenity) and past pecuniary loss, net of CRU recoveries, excluding future pecuniary loss (CFA Order 2013 art 5). Verbatim in anchor block. Note the post-LASPO position: since 1 April 2013 the success fee is generally NOT recoverable from the losing party and comes out of the client's damages (subject to the cap), so this is a deduction from damages, not an inter-partes recovery.
5. **Revenue recognition on a contingent matter (FRS 102 Section 23)** · THE accounting differentiator. Revenue is recognised when it can be measured reliably AND it is probable the economic benefits will flow to the firm (FRS 102 Section 23). On a CFA the right to be paid is contingent on success, so until success is probable and the amount measurable, revenue (and the success fee) should NOT be recognised. Verbatim in anchor block. Contrast with standard time-recorded work where revenue accrues as work is done.
6. **WIP on CFA matters (§4)** · how unbilled CFA work sits on the balance sheet. WIP is recognised under FRS 102 (§4), but on a contingent matter the reliable-measurement / probable-inflow test means CFA WIP is carried at a value that reflects the contingency (often little or nothing until the outcome is probable), not full charge-out rate. Tie to the FRS 102 FAQ in the existing WIP page: "if the outcome of a matter cannot be measured reliably (for example, in conditional fee agreements), a more conservative approach may be justified." Cross-link `wip-valuation-method-uk-law-firms`. Do NOT recognise full WIP on a speculative CFA.
7. **The VAT tax point on a CFA (§6.D)** · when output VAT becomes due. The basic tax point is performance, overridden by the earlier of a VAT invoice/bill or payment (VATA 1994 s.6, §6.D). On a CFA, no bill is rendered until the win, so the tax point typically arises at successful conclusion (when the firm bills, or is paid). Output VAT on the base fee AND the success fee is accounted at that point; legal services are standard-rated at 20% (§6). Cross-link `vat-tax-point-time-of-supply-law-firm-billing`.
8. **Is the success fee subject to VAT? Yes** · the success fee is part of the firm's standard-rated supply of legal services, not a separate exempt or outside-the-scope payment, so VAT is charged on it at 20% (§6). Where it is deducted from the client's damages, the 25% PI cap is the cap INCLUSIVE of VAT in practice (frame carefully: the cap is on the amount taken from the client; the success fee plus its VAT must fit within the cap). Writer: be precise that the CFA Order cap is on the success fee deducted from damages; account for the VAT within that.
9. **Tax timing: paying tax on profit you have not yet earned (or won)** · because revenue is not recognised until success is probable, a firm should not be taxed on speculative CFA WIP; but once the matter is won and recognised, a large slug of revenue (base + success fee) crystallises in one period (§4 basis-period/accruals; LLPs on accruals). Plan for the lumpy, contingent profit profile. Cross-link `law-firm-cash-flow-management`, `law-firm-partner-tax-reserving-payments-on-account`.
10. **Disbursements and ATE on CFA matters** · disbursements on a CFA still follow the eight-condition test (§6); ATE (after-the-event) insurance premiums and litigation funding interact (cross-link `disbursement-and-litigation-funding-cash-flow-tax`). Counsel on a CFA (a barrister's own CFA) is a supply to the firm by default (§6.B).
11. **Practical checklist** · (i) ensure the CFA satisfies s.58 conditions; (ii) keep the success fee within 100% of base costs and, for PI, within the 25% damages cap; (iii) recognise revenue only when success is probable and measurable (FRS 102 s.23); (iv) carry CFA WIP conservatively, reflecting the contingency; (v) account for output VAT (base + success fee) at the tax point on the win; (vi) reserve for the lumpy profit recognition; (vii) treat disbursements and ATE correctly. Inline CTA (segment: PI/litigation firm running CFAs).

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **Courts and Legal Services Act 1990 s.58 (CFA framework), verified 2026-06-03:**
  - Definition (verbatim): *"A conditional fee agreement is an agreement with a person providing advocacy or litigation services which provides for his fees and expenses, or any part of them, to be payable only in specified circumstances."*
  - Success fee (verbatim): *"A conditional fee agreement provides for a success fee if it provides for the amount of any fees to which it applies to be increased, in specified circumstances, above the amount which would be payable if it were not payable only in specified circumstances."*
  - Cap reference, s.58(4)(c) (verbatim): the percentage increase *"must not exceed the percentage specified in relation to the description of proceedings to which the agreement relates by order made by the Lord Chancellor."*
  - Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1990/41/section/58
- **CFA Order 2013 (SI 2013/689), success-fee caps, verified 2026-06-03:**
  - Art 3 (verbatim): *"the percentage specified for the purposes of section 58(4)(c) of the Act is 100%"* (the maximum success fee is 100% of base costs).
  - Art 5(1) (verbatim): the cap on the amount of the success fee that may be deducted is *"in proceedings at first instance, 25%; and ... in all other proceedings, 100%"*.
  - Art 5(2) (verbatim): the 25% applies to *"general damages for pain, suffering, and loss of amenity; and ... damages for pecuniary loss, other than future pecuniary loss"*, calculated *"net of any sums recoverable by the Compensation Recovery Unit of the Department for Work and Pensions"*.
  - Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/689 (arts 3, 5). NOTE FOR WRITER: art 5's 25% cap is the cap on the success fee taken from a PI claimant's damages; LASPO (from 1 April 2013) generally ended inter-partes recoverability of the success fee, so it is funded from the client's damages within this cap.
- **FRS 102 revenue recognition (§4):** revenue from services is recognised when the outcome can be estimated reliably AND it is probable that the economic benefits will flow to the entity (FRS 102 Section 23). On a contingent CFA, recognition is deferred until success is probable and the amount measurable. (Restated from §4; FRS 102 Section 23.)
- **WIP (§4):** WIP recognised under FRS 102; LLPs excluded from the cash basis (accruals applies); tax-year basis from 2024/25. Conservative WIP carrying value for contingent matters.
- **VAT (§6, §6.D):** legal services standard-rated at 20%; tax point per VATA 1994 s.6 (basic tax point on performance, earlier of invoice or payment, 14-day rule); on a CFA the bill/tax point typically arises at successful conclusion. The success fee is part of the standard-rated supply.

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice)

1. **Success-fee cap.** A firm runs a matter on a CFA with base costs and a 100% success fee; the success fee cannot exceed 100% of base costs (CFA Order art 3), so at most it doubles the base costs. Caption: "the success fee is capped at 100% of base costs."
2. **PI damages cap.** In a PI claim won at first instance, the firm's success fee deducted from the client's damages is capped at 25% of general damages plus past pecuniary loss, net of CRU recoveries, excluding future loss (art 5). Caption: "the PI success fee taken from damages is capped at 25% of the right slice of damages."
3. **Revenue recognition deferred.** A speculative CFA matter runs for two years; under FRS 102 s.23 the firm recognises no revenue and carries WIP conservatively until success becomes probable and measurable, then recognises base costs plus success fee in the period of the win. Caption: "no revenue until the win is probable; then it lands in one period."
4. **VAT at conclusion.** On a successful CFA the firm renders its bill; the tax point arises then, and output VAT at 20% is due on the base fee and the success fee for that VAT period. Caption: "the VAT tax point on a CFA is the bill at the end, not the work along the way."
