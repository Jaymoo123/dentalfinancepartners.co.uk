## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

PII is mandatory for every SRA-regulated firm, the premium is one of the largest single fixed costs in the year, and for most firms it falls due as a lump at the 1 October renewal (§10 / cross-link the renewal-cycle page). Premium finance lets a firm spread that lump into monthly instalments through a finance provider, at the cost of interest. The page does three jobs: it explains how premium finance works and where it fits the renewal calendar; it states the deductibility cleanly (the PII premium is an allowable trade expense under the wholly-and-exclusively test, ITTOIA 2005 s.34 / CTA 2009, §10; and the finance/interest cost is deductible where the borrowing is for the purposes of the trade, which premium finance for a mandatory business insurance generally is, BIM45690); and it sets out the VAT position (PII is VAT-exempt insurance, §10, so there is no input VAT to reclaim on the premium, and premium-finance interest is itself an exempt finance cost, so no VAT to recover there either). Honest framing: financing spreads the cost at an interest price; it does not change the deductibility of the premium, and the interest is generally deductible too because, unlike a personal-tax loan, this borrowing IS for the trade.

5. **Deductibility of the finance/interest cost (THE distinguishing tax section)** · interest on borrowing is allowable to the extent the borrowing is wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the trade (ITTOIA 2005 s.34; BIM45690: interest on money borrowed and used for business purposes is allowable; not allowable if used for private purposes). Premium finance funds a MANDATORY BUSINESS insurance, so the borrowing is for the trade and the interest is generally a deductible business expense. Premium-finance interest is itself an exempt financial supply (the provision of credit, VATA 1994 Schedule 9 Group 5, §6.C), so there is no input VAT on the finance cost to recover either. ## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; locked in HP §10 / §6.C / §10 + BIM45690 verbatim 2026-06-03)

- **PII premium deductible (§10):** PII premiums are an allowable trading expense (wholly and exclusively, ITTOIA 2005 s.34 / CTA 2009 for companies), deducted at firm level for a partnership/LLP (before allocation) and at company level for a company; run-off premiums likewise deductible (timing per FRS 102). Apportion any mixed personal/professional cover. (Locked §10.)
- **PII VAT-exempt (§10):** PII premiums are VAT-exempt insurance (VATA 1994 Schedule 9 Group 2), so there is no input VAT to reclaim on the premium. (Locked §10.)
- **Finance interest deductible because the borrowing is for the trade (HMRC BIM45690, verbatim 2026-06-03):** "When the business is funded using borrowed money and that money is used for business purposes the interest is allowable as a deduction in computing the trade profits. The interest is not allowable as a deduction if the funds are being used for private purposes." Premium finance funds a mandatory business insurance, so the borrowing is for the trade and the interest is generally deductible. CONTRAST a personal-tax loan (not deductible, see `tax-loans-for-law-firm-partners-funding-the-bill`).
- **Lending/credit is an exempt financial supply (§6.C):** the provision of credit is an exempt supply (VATA 1994 Schedule 9 Group 5), so there is no input VAT on premium-finance interest to recover.
- **PII MTC cover levels (§10):** minimum sum insured £3 million per claim for a relevant recognised body or relevant licensed body (LLPs, companies, ABSs); £2 million per claim "in all other cases" (sole practitioners and partnerships); no monetary limit on defence costs; run-off cover six years on cessation. State this way round; do NOT reverse the £3m/£2m. (Locked §10; SRA Indemnity Insurance Rules + MTC.)
- **Renewal timing (cross-link the renewal-cycle page):** most SRA-regulated firms renew on 1 October; the premium typically falls due then. (Per the existing `pii-renewal-cycle-uk-law-firms` page; the 1 October date and run-off detail live there.)
- **IPT (writer note):** do NOT invent an Insurance Premium Tax rate or assert it applies to PII; keep any IPT mention general and tell the reader to confirm with the broker. PII follows the insurance-exemption logic for VAT (no input VAT to reclaim).

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice; date-tag)

1. **Spreading the October premium (premium deductible, interest deductible).** A firm finances its 1 October PII premium over twelve months. The premium is an allowable trade expense (§10); the finance interest is also deductible because the borrowing funds a mandatory business cost (BIM45690). Caption: "the premium is deductible, and so is the interest, because the borrowing is for the trade."
2. **Contrast with a personal tax loan.** The same firm's partner finances their personal tax bill with a tax loan: that interest is NOT deductible (personal liability, see the tax-loans page). The PII premium finance is different because it funds a trade cost. Caption: "premium finance interest is deductible; personal-tax-loan interest is not. The difference is whether the borrowing is for the trade."
3. **No VAT to reclaim.** The firm looks for input VAT on the PII premium and the finance and finds none: PII is exempt insurance and the finance is exempt credit (§10, §6.C). Caption: "there is no VAT to recover on either the premium or the finance."
4. **Financing run-off on cessation.** A retiring sole practitioner finances the six-year run-off premium (§10); it is a single large premium that premium finance can spread, deductible as a trade expense (timing per FRS 102). Caption: "run-off is a large one-off premium that can also be financed, and it stays deductible."
