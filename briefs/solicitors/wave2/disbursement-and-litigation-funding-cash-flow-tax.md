## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

Litigation ties up a firm's cash for years: disbursements (court fees, experts, counsel, searches) are paid out long before the matter resolves, and on CFA/DBA work the firm's own fees may not crystallise until the win. Matter-level funding products bridge that gap. Disbursement funding/loans cover the case outlays; third-party litigation funding finances the case in return for a share of the recovery; ATE insurance protects the client (and sometimes the firm) against adverse costs and disbursement risk. The accounting questions that follow: the funding FEE or interest is generally a deductible financing cost where it is wholly and exclusively for the trade, the underlying disbursements still have to pass the eight-condition VAT test (funding does not change their VAT character), and the SRA Accounts Rules govern how funded money flows through client/office account. The page maps the products to their cash-flow role, then the tax/VAT/SRA treatment.

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **Deductibility, wholly and exclusively (§§3, 4, 10):** a trading expense (including interest/finance costs) is deductible if incurred wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the trade (ITTOIA 2005 s.34 for unincorporated firms; CTA 2009 for companies). Finance costs of borrowing for the trade are deductible. So where the FIRM borrows to fund case outlays/working capital, the funding interest/fee is a deductible business expense; where the CLIENT borrows, it is not the firm's expense. (Locked §10 wholly-and-exclusively; §3/§4 trade-profit computation.)
- **Disbursement VAT test (§6, §6.A):** an outlay is a disbursement (outside the scope of VAT, recharged with no VAT) only if all eight conditions in VAT Notice 700 §25.1.1 are met; otherwise it is part of the firm's standard-rated supply at 20%. Funding does NOT change this. Post-Brabners (R&C Brief 6 (2020), §6.A), a search fee the firm interprets in its advice is part of its taxable supply. (Locked §6/§6.A.)
- **Lending is an exempt financial supply (§6.C):** the provision of credit/finance is an exempt supply (VATA 1994 Schedule 9 Group 5), so there is no input VAT on funding interest to recover; the firm's onward charge for its legal services remains standard-rated.
- **SRA Accounts Rules 2019 (§5):** client money goes to a client account (Rule 2/3); the client account must not be used to provide banking facilities and every movement must relate to the delivery of regulated services (Rule 3.3); reconciliation at least every five weeks (Rule 8.3). (Locked §5; SRA Accounts Rules 2019.)
- **WIP / basis period (§4):** WIP recognised under FRS 102; LLPs excluded from the cash basis (accruals); tax-year basis from 2024/25. Funding does not change revenue recognition.
- **ATE / LASPO context:** since 1 April 2013 (LASPO 2012) ATE premiums are generally NOT recoverable from the losing party (limited exceptions). ## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice)

1. **Firm-borrowed disbursement funding (deductible).** A PI firm borrows to fund medical reports and court fees across its CFA caseload, repaid on settlements. The interest/fee on that borrowing is wholly and exclusively for the trade, so it is a deductible business expense; the disbursements themselves still follow the eight-condition VAT test. Caption: "when the firm borrows for its own case outlays, the finance cost is deductible."
2. **Client-borrowed funding (not the firm's expense).** The client takes a disbursement loan in the client's own name; the loan and its cost are the client's, not the firm's, so there is no firm-level deduction, and the funded money the firm holds for the client is client money under the SRA rules. Caption: "if the client borrows, it is the client's cost, not yours, and the money is client money."
3. **Funded disbursement, unchanged VAT.** A funded court fee that meets all eight conditions stays outside the scope of VAT; a funded search fee the firm interprets in its advice is part of the firm's standard-rated supply (post-Brabners), regardless of the funding. Caption: "funding never changes whether something is a true disbursement."
4. **ATE capping the downside.** ATE insurance covers adverse costs and own disbursements if the case is lost, making the CFA and disbursement funding viable; the premium is generally not recoverable from the loser post-LASPO. Caption: "ATE caps the downside but the premium usually comes from the client's recovery."
