## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

The apprenticeship levy is a payroll charge of 0.5% of an employer's annual pay bill, reduced by a £15,000 allowance, so in practice only employers with a pay bill over £3 million pay it. Larger law firms therefore pay it; smaller firms do not, but can still access apprenticeship funding through government co-investment or a levy TRANSFER from a larger firm. Levy money sits in an apprenticeship service account and can be spent on approved apprenticeship training, including the level 7 solicitor apprenticeship that takes a recruit to qualification via the SQE. The catch the page must be honest about: government funding for the level 7 solicitor apprenticeship has been restricted from January 2026 (broadly to younger apprentices and specific eligibility groups), so the funding route is in flux and date-sensitive. The page explains the levy mechanics precisely, distinguishes the levy from the separate question of whether SQE costs are tax-deductible (covered on the sibling page), and frames the firm's planning decision.
