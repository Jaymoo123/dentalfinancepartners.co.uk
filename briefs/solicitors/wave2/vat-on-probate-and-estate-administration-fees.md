## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

The firm's probate and estate-administration **fee is a standard-rated supply of legal services at 20%**: there is no VAT exemption for probate work (the same as conveyancing or litigation, §6). The real work is on the **outlays**: a payment the firm makes during an estate is a true disbursement (outside the scope of VAT, recharged with no VAT) only if **all eight conditions** in VAT Notice 700 §25.1.1 are met. The page must apply that test to the specific outlays probate generates: the **probate application (grant) fee** and **office copies** (clean disbursements, the client/estate is liable, pure conduit), **s.27 Trustee Act 1925 statutory advertisements** (a genuine disbursement where the firm pays them as agent for the PRs and itemises the exact cost), Land Registry fees on an assent/transfer (disbursement), versus standard-rated recharges (the firm's own bank/CHAPS fees, photocopying, postage, and any valuation or search the firm interprets in its own advice, the Brabners point). Honest, condition-by-condition, billing-grade.

## H2 outline (aim 9-12 sections, ~2,900-3,400 words)

1. **Is there VAT on probate fees? Yes, at 20%** · lead with the answer: the firm's probate/estate-administration fee is a standard-rated supply of legal services (VATA 1994), 20% since 4 January 2011. There is no exemption for probate. State that an unregistered firm under the £90,000 registration threshold (from 1 April 2024, §6) does not charge VAT, but most firms doing probate are registered. Then pivot: the hard part is the outlays.
2. **The eight conditions: the disbursement test** · the core anchor. A payment is a disbursement (outside VAT) only if ALL EIGHT conditions (VAT Notice 700 §25.1.1) are met: acted as the client's agent; client received and used; client responsible for paying; client authorised; client knew a third party would supply; separately itemised; recover only the exact amount; clearly additional to the firm's own supply. Fail one and it is part of the firm's standard-rated supply. Cross-link `disbursements-vat-treatment-uk-law-firms` for the general treatment; this page applies it to probate. Note "the client" in a probate matter is the PR/estate.
3. **The probate application (grant) fee, a clean disbursement** · the probate registry application fee is a statutory court fee the estate is liable for, paid by the firm as a pure conduit. It passes the eight conditions and is a genuine disbursement, recharged with no VAT. State the current figures (date-tagged): application fee **£300** for estates over £5,000, no fee at £5,000 or below; **office/additional copies of the grant £16 each** (gov.uk, verified 2026-06-03). Office copies are likewise a clean disbursement. Note court/tribunal fees generally are clean disbursements (§6).
4. **s.27 Trustee Act 1925 statutory advertisements** · the probate-specific one. PRs place statutory notices (in the Gazette and a local newspaper for land) under s.27 Trustee Act 1925 to protect themselves against unknown creditors/claimants before distributing. Where the firm pays the Gazette/newspaper for these notices **as agent for the PRs**, itemises the exact cost and meets the eight conditions, the advertising cost is a **genuine disbursement** (outside VAT, recharged at cost). Quote the s.27 mechanic (notice in the Gazette, not less than two months, distribute having regard only to claims of which the PRs had notice) so the reader understands what the outlay is for. Caution: the notice cost is the third party's charge to the estate; the firm cannot add its own margin and still call it a disbursement (condition 7, exact amount only).
5. **Other estate outlays: disbursement or recharge?** · run the common list against the test. Genuine disbursements (where pure conduit, client liable, itemised): **Land Registry fees** on an assent/transfer of the deceased's property, **statutory/court fees**, IHT paid to HMRC (a tax the estate owes, not a supply at all), bankruptcy/winding-up search fees obtained for the PRs' protection where passed through unused. Standard-rated recharges: the firm's own **bank/CHAPS/telegraphic-transfer fees** (the bank supplies the firm, not the estate, §6.A), **photocopying, postage, courier, ID checks**, and any **valuation or asset search the firm commissions and interprets in its own advice** (the Brabners principle, §6.A).
6. **The Brabners principle applied to probate** · whether a third-party report (a property valuation, a financial-asset search, a genealogist/missing-beneficiary report) is a disbursement turns on **how the firm uses it**, not how it was obtained. If the firm interprets the result and folds it into its advice to the PRs, it is part of the firm's standard-rated supply and VAT is due; if it passes the raw result to the PRs unused and the eight conditions are met, it can be a disbursement. The 1991 postal-search concession was withdrawn from **1 December 2020** (Revenue and Customs Brief 6 (2020)). Cross-link `disbursements-vs-recharges-conveyancing-vat`.
7. **Genealogist / heir-tracing and missing-beneficiary costs** · a probate edge case worth its own paragraph: an heir-hunter or genealogist instructed to trace beneficiaries. If the firm instructs and uses the report in its own advice, treat as a recharge (standard-rated). If the PRs instruct the genealogist directly (or the firm pays purely as agent and passes it through unused), it may be a disbursement. Document the agency relationship.
8. **Itemising the bill correctly** · how to present the estate bill so the VAT treatment is defensible: the firm's professional fee (plus 20% VAT) shown separately from disbursements (each itemised at the exact amount, no VAT) and from standard-rated recharges (with 20% VAT added). The SRA Transparency Rules also require clear costs information on probate. Keep evidence for each disbursement (agency, client liability, authority, itemisation, exact amount).
9. **Interaction with the estate's client account** · the firm pays these outlays either from estate client money (where held and the purpose is met, §5 Rule 5.1) or from office account and recharges. Note IHT and the probate fee are typically paid from estate funds in client account; the firm's fee transfers to office account only after a bill is delivered (§6, §5). Cross-link `probate-estate-administration-client-account-sra`.
10. **Common probate VAT mistakes** · (i) treating the firm's own bank/transfer charges as disbursements (they are recharges); (ii) adding a margin to a disbursement (breaks condition 7); (iii) treating a valuation/search the firm interprets as VAT-free (Brabners); (iv) forgetting that the firm's fee is standard-rated even when most outlays are not; (v) failing to itemise. Inline CTA to lead form (segment: probate/private-client firm wanting a disbursements and billing VAT review).
11. **Worked sketches** · see below.

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **Legal services standard-rated at 20%** · VATA 1994; no exemption for probate; 20% since 4 January 2011. Registration threshold **£90,000** (from 1 April 2024), deregistration **£88,000**. §6. Do NOT use the old £85,000 figure.
- **The eight disbursement conditions** · VAT Notice 700 §25.1.1 / HMRC manual VTAXPER39000 (https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-taxable-person/vtaxper39000): (1) acted as agent of the client when paying the third party; (2) client actually received and used the goods/services; (3) client was responsible for paying the third party; (4) client authorised the payment; (5) client knew the goods/services would be provided by a third party; (6) the outlay is separately itemised on the invoice; (7) recover only the exact amount paid; (8) the goods/services are clearly additional to the firm's own supply. ALL eight must be met. §6.
- **Probate application fee + office copies** · gov.uk (https://www.gov.uk/applying-for-probate/fees), verified 2026-06-03: application fee **£300** if the estate is over £5,000; **no fee** if the estate is £5,000 or less; **extra/office copies of the probate document £16 each**. These are statutory fees the estate is liable for, treated as clean disbursements where the eight conditions are met.
- **s.27 Trustee Act 1925 (statutory advertisements)** · legislation.gov.uk (https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/Geo5/15-16/19/section/27). VERIFIED VERBATIM operative wording: trustees/PRs may "give notice by advertisement in the Gazette, and in a newspaper circulating in the district in which the land is situated" of intention to distribute, requiring "any person interested to send ... within the time, not being less than two months, fixed in the notice ... particulars of his claim"; thereafter they "may convey or distribute the property ... having regard only to the claims ... of which the trustees or personal representatives then had notice". The advertising cost paid as agent for the PRs, itemised at the exact amount, is a genuine disbursement.
- **Brabners / search-and-report rule** · ***Brabners LLP v HMRC* [2017] UKFTT 0666 (TC), TC06093** + **Revenue and Customs Brief 6 (2020)** (postal-search concession withdrawn 1 December 2020). A third-party report the firm uses/interprets in its own advice is part of the firm's standard-rated supply; pure pass-through unused can be a disbursement. §6.A.
- **CHAPS / bank fees** · standard-rated recharges (the bank supplies the firm, not the client). §6.A.
- **IHT** · a tax the estate owes paid to HMRC; not a supply, paid from estate funds, not the firm's income. (Context only; do not analyse IHT computation, this is an accountancy/VAT page.)

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice; no real figures presented as a quote)

1. **The standard estate bill, correctly split.** The firm bills an estate for administering it. Show the three buckets: (a) the firm's professional fee, standard-rated at 20%; (b) genuine disbursements at cost, no VAT, each itemised, the £300 probate application fee, a handful of £16 office copies, the Gazette and local-newspaper s.27 notice costs, the Land Registry assent fee; (c) standard-rated recharges with 20% VAT added, the firm's CHAPS fees on distributions and its ID-check/postage costs. Caption: "fee and recharges carry VAT; clean disbursements do not."
2. **The s.27 advertisement disbursement.** The PRs ask the firm to place statutory notices to protect them before distributing the residue. The firm pays the Gazette and a local newspaper, itemises each at the exact cost, and recharges with no VAT (it acted as the PRs' agent, the cost is clearly additional to its own service, the eight conditions are met). Caption: "s.27 notices paid as agent and passed through at cost are a disbursement, do not add a margin."
3. **The valuation that is a recharge, not a disbursement (Brabners).** The firm commissions a probate valuation of the deceased's investment portfolio, interprets it and uses it in advising the PRs on the IHT position and distribution. Because the firm uses the result in its own advice it is a cost component of the firm's standard-rated supply, so VAT applies on the recharge, not a disbursement. Caption: "if you interpret it in your advice, it is part of your taxable supply."
