## The honest thesis (lead and hold)

A DBA pays the firm a percentage of what the client recovers, not a fee for time. The DBA Regulations 2013 cap that percentage, and the caps are expressly INCLUSIVE of VAT: 25% for personal-injury claims at first instance, 35% for employment matters, and 50% for all other claims. "Including VAT" is the detail firms miss: the cap is the ceiling on the total the firm can take, VAT and all, so the firm's net-of-VAT fee is the capped percentage with the VAT carved out of it, not added on top. On the accounting side, because the fee is contingent on recovery, revenue is recognised only when recovery is probable and measurable (FRS 102 Section 23), and the VAT tax point arises when the firm bills or is paid (typically at recovery). The page sets out the three caps precisely, the "including VAT" mechanic, and the recognition/tax timing.

## VERIFIED ANCHOR BLOCK (quote/paraphrase only from this; verified at primary source 2026-06-03)

- **DBA Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/609), reg 4 (non-employment matters), verified 2026-06-03:**
  - Personal injury, first instance (verbatim): a damages-based agreement *"must not provide for a payment above an amount which, including VAT, is equal to 25% of the combined sums"* (general damages and past, non-future pecuniary loss, net of social-security/CRU recoveries).
  - All other (non-employment) claims, first instance (verbatim): a damages-based agreement *"must not provide for a payment above an amount which, including VAT, is equal to 50% of the sums ultimately recovered by the client"*.
  - Both caps are expressly *"including VAT"* and apply at first instance.
  - Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/609/regulation/4
- **DBA Regulations 2013, reg 7 (employment matters), verified 2026-06-03:**
  - (Verbatim): a damages-based agreement *"must not provide for a payment above an amount which, including VAT, is equal to 35% of the sums ultimately recovered by the client"*.
  - Source: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/609/regulation/7
- **In force:** SI 2013/609 came into force 1 April 2013 (under the Courts and Legal Services Act 1990 s.58AA, as amended by LASPO 2012). Tag accordingly.
- **FRS 102 revenue recognition (§4):** service revenue recognised when the outcome can be estimated reliably AND it is probable economic benefits will flow (FRS 102 Section 23); on a contingent DBA, recognition deferred until recovery probable and measurable.
- **WIP (§4):** WIP recognised under FRS 102; LLPs excluded from the cash basis (accruals); tax-year basis from 2024/25.
- **VAT (§6, §6.D):** legal services standard-rated at 20%; the DBA payment is the firm's fee for its standard-rated supply; tax point per VATA 1994 s.6 (basic tax point on performance, earlier of invoice or payment, 14-day rule), typically at recovery. Because the cap is VAT-inclusive, the output VAT is the VAT fraction of the capped payment (1/6 at 20%).

## Worked-example sketches (illustrative only; label as not advice)

1. **The three caps.** PI claim won at first instance: payment capped at 25% including VAT of the qualifying damages. Employment claim: 35% including VAT of sums recovered. Commercial claim: 50% including VAT of sums recovered. Caption: "25% PI, 35% employment, 50% other, each including VAT."
2. **"Including VAT" carve-out.** On a 50%-including-VAT cap, the firm's total take (fee plus VAT) cannot exceed 50% of the recovery. If the firm takes the full 50%, the VAT element is 1/6 of that (at 20%), and the firm's net fee is the remaining 5/6. The firm cannot charge 50% and add VAT on top. Caption: "the cap is the lid on fee-plus-VAT; carve the VAT out, do not add it on."
3. **Revenue recognition deferred.** A commercial DBA matter runs while recovery is uncertain; under FRS 102 s.23 no DBA revenue is recognised and WIP is carried conservatively until recovery is probable and measurable, then the capped percentage is recognised in the recovery period. Caption: "no DBA revenue until recovery is probable; then it lands at once."
4. **Output VAT at the tax point.** On recovery the firm bills its DBA payment; the tax point arises, and output VAT is 1/6 of the VAT-inclusive capped payment (at 20%), due for that VAT period. Caption: "account for the VAT fraction of the capped payment when you bill at recovery."
