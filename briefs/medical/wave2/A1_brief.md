## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** Most GPs draw a profit share from the practice but have never seen how the money actually arrives. This page opens up the engine room of NHS GP income: the Global Sum as the core per-registered-patient payment, the Carr-Hill formula that turns a raw list into weighted patients (so two practices of the same headcount can be funded very differently), the Statement of Financial Entitlements that governs it, and where GMS, PMS and APMS contracts diverge. The whole point is to connect that funding structure to what a partner actually takes home, because Global Sum is practice trading income, not a partner's pay packet.

- **H2: The three NHS GP contract types: GMS, PMS and APMS**
  - H3: GMS (General Medical Services). Nationally negotiated between NHS England and the BMA General Practitioners Committee; governed by the GMS contract regulations and the Statement of Financial Entitlements. The default national contract.
  - H3: PMS (Personal Medical Services). Locally agreed variant, historically with a locally negotiated baseline; broadly similar funding building blocks but a local agreement.
  - H3: APMS (Alternative Provider Medical Services). Contracts with alternative providers (which can include companies and other organisations), time-limited and locally commissioned.
  - Tie-back: whichever contract, the core per-patient funding logic (a weighted list) is the same family; this page uses GMS as the worked example.
- **H2: The Global Sum: the core per-patient payment**
  - Define the Global Sum as the main core funding payment, calculated as a national price per weighted patient multiplied by the practice's weighted list size, paid monthly.
  - State that the per-weighted-patient amount is uplifted annually and set in the Statement of Financial Entitlements (see the figures note in section 5: hedge, do not lock a single number as permanent).
  - H3: Registered list vs weighted list. The headcount on the practice list is the registered (raw) population; the Global Sum is paid on the weighted population, which is the raw list adjusted by the Carr-Hill formula.
  - H3: How the Global Sum is paid and reconciled. Monthly payments via the commissioner; list changes feed through; explain that the practice should reconcile what it receives (signpost Cluster C reconciliation work only by theme, do NOT link to it, it is a different Wave 2 cluster).
- **H2: The Carr-Hill formula: how a raw list becomes weighted patients**
  - Purpose: the formula adjusts the raw list to reflect relative workload and need, so funding follows need rather than a flat per-head rate.
  - H3: The factors. Age and sex of patients, additional needs (morbidity and mortality), list turnover (newly registered patients cost more in the first year), the nursing and residential home factor, the rurality / geography adjustment, and a staff market-forces (cost) adjustment. Frame these as the recognised Carr-Hill factors; keep it conceptual, do not invent weightings.
  - H3: Why two same-size practices are funded differently. A worked, illustrative comparison: an older, higher-morbidity, high-turnover list weights up; a young, stable, low-need list weights down. Keep numbers illustrative and clearly hedged, no fabricated precise figures.
  - H3: Known criticisms (kept brief and neutral). The formula is widely debated as not fully capturing deprivation; note this is a live policy discussion, not advice. Do not editorialise.
- **H2: The Statement of Financial Entitlements (SFE): the rulebook for the money**
  - Explain that the SFE Directions set out the detailed payment entitlements (Global Sum, QOF, enhanced and other payments) and are reissued/amended, with the per-patient and per-point values uplifted in line with the annual contract settlement.
  - Practical point for partners: this is why the practice's core funding line moves each April, and why "last year's figure" is never the safe figure to budget on. Signpost the SFE as the source of truth (URL in section 5).
- **H2: GMS funding is practice income, not your pay (how it reaches a partner)**
  - Core teaching link: the Global Sum lands in the practice as trading income; expenses (staff, premises, running costs) are met; what remains is profit, shared between partners under the profit-sharing agreement; partners draw against expected profit and are trued up at year-end.

  - VAT note (light): core NHS GMS income is outside the scope of VAT (link by theme to VAT only via `gp-vat-registration`; keep to one sentence, anchor §6).

## 5. Exact HP positions, dates and verified anchors

**HP positions to use (anchor §3 unless noted):**
- Three contract types: **GMS** (nationally negotiated, governed by the Statement of Financial Entitlements), **PMS** (locally agreed), **APMS** (alternative providers). (§3)
- Core funding is the **Global Sum**, a per-patient payment **weighted by the Carr-Hill formula** (age, sex, morbidity/additional needs, list turnover, nursing/residential home, geography/rurality, staff market forces, giving weighted patients). (§3)
- Other core lines named (not taught here): **QOF**, **enhanced services**, **PCN / Network Contract DES** (ARRS), **dispensing**. (§3, link A2/A3.)
- **Partner income is a profit share, drawings trued up at year-end, taxed on profit share not drawings.** (§3, §1)
- **There is no single national per-patient value; figures are weighted and uplifted annually.** (§3, and the §3 VERIFY flag.)
- Company cannot hold a GMS/PMS contract; company income not pensionable. (§2.C, light touch.)
- Core NHS GMS income is outside the scope of VAT. (§6, one sentence.)
