## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** Most GP partners know their practice belongs to a primary care network and that money arrives through it, but few have seen how the Network Contract DES actually channels that money or how it lands in the practice accounts. This page maps the flow end to end: the Network Contract DES as a voluntary Directed Enhanced Service the member practices sign up to on top of their core GMS or PMS contract, the named funding streams (core PCN funding, the staff reimbursements under ARRS, enhanced access, the capacity and access payments and the Investment and Impact Fund), the nominated payee that receives the money on the network's behalf, and how it is then recognised in the practice and the partners' profit share. It stays at the funding-flow altitude and hands off to sibling B2 (ARRS employment and reimbursement detail) and B3 (clinical director and leadership pay).

**How this differs from existing pages and the two sibling picks.**
- Differs from Cluster A (`how-gms-funding-works-global-sum-carr-hill-explained`, `enhanced-services-gp-practice-income-tax`): Cluster A is the practice's own core GMS funding (Global Sum, Carr-Hill, QOF, practice-level enhanced services). This page is the separate network layer that sits on top, paid under a different agreement (the Network Contract DES) to the PCN, not the individual practice.
- Differs from sibling B2 (`arrs-reimbursement-employing-pcn-staff-tax`): B2 is the employment and reimbursement mechanics of ARRS staff (who employs them, the payroll, VAT and Employment Allowance). ## 4. Section-by-section outline (target 2,800 to 3,500 words)

- **H1: PCN Funding Explained: The Network Contract DES and How the Money Flows**
- **H2: What a primary care network is, and why funding flows differently**
  - A PCN is a group of neighbouring practices, typically covering around 30,000 to 50,000 patients, that come together to deliver services at scale. State plainly that a PCN is usually a collaboration defined by the DES, not automatically a separate legal entity (this matters for who holds the money and for VAT, signpost B2).
  - Distinguish two layers: the practice's own core contract funding (Cluster A: Global Sum, QOF, practice-level enhanced services, link `how-gms-funding-works-global-sum-carr-hill-explained`) and the network layer on top (this page). Same partners, two separate funding routes.
- **H2: The Network Contract DES: a Directed Enhanced Service the practices opt into**
  - Define a Directed Enhanced Service: a nationally directed, voluntary extension to the core GMS or PMS contract that practices choose to sign up to. The Network Contract DES is the vehicle through which practices participate in a PCN and draw the network funding.
  - State it is participation-based: a practice that signs up earns a network participation payment per registered patient (frame as a per-patient payment uplifted annually, see the figures note in section 5).
  - Tie-back: because it is an enhanced service on top of the core contract, the money and the obligations are additional to (and accounted for separately from) Global Sum and QOF.
- **H2: The funding streams inside the Network Contract DES**
  - Open with the picture: the DES carries several distinct streams, each with its own basis and its own rules. Then take them one at a time.
  - H3: Core PCN funding. A per-registered-patient (and partly per-adjusted-population) payment that funds the running of the network, and which now also rolls in the clinical director, leadership and management funding (this was combined into core PCN funding, giving networks more autonomy over how they are led; verify the current-year position and link B3 for how that pay is then taxed). Frame the per-patient figure as uplifted annually, do not lock it.
  - H3: Staff reimbursements (the Additional Roles Reimbursement Scheme, ARRS). The largest stream by value nationally. Describe it in one paragraph as a reimbursement of the actual employment cost of defined additional roles, up to a maximum per role, claimed by the network. State clearly it is a reimbursement, not free money or a grant, and hand the whole employment and accounting question to B2 (link `arrs-reimbursement-employing-pcn-staff-tax`).
  - H3: Enhanced access. Funding to deliver appointments outside core hours across the network, paid per adjusted population.
  - H3: Capacity and access payments. A support component and an improvement (achievement-linked) component.
  - H3: The Investment and Impact Fund (IIF). An achievement-based fund tied to a small set of indicators.
  - H3: Care home and other premia. Note the existence of additional premia (for example a per-bed care home premium) without locking figures.
  - Throughout: every per-patient, per-population or per-role figure is uplifted annually and set in the contract documents, so frame as current-year and confirm at source (section 5).
- **H2: How the money actually reaches the practices: the nominated payee**
  - Explain that the network nominates a payee to receive the DES funding on its behalf. This is commonly a lead (host) practice, but can be a GP federation or another legal entity the practices have set up.
  - The payee receives the funding, then the network distributes it between the member practices (or spends it directly, for example on shared staff) under the network agreement. State that the network agreement is the document that governs how the money is shared.
  - Flag the planning point neutrally: how the money sits in the payee, and how it moves between practices, has tax and VAT consequences (especially for shared staff), which is exactly what B2 covers (link `arrs-reimbursement-employing-pcn-staff-tax`).
- **H2: How PCN income is recognised in the accounts**
  - Map the streams onto the practice accounts: participation and distributed core funding as network income; ARRS handled as a reimbursement against the matching staff cost (so it should largely net off rather than inflate profit, signpost B2 for the detail); achievement funds (IIF, improvement payment) recognised when reasonably certain.
  - State the §1 rule plainly: whatever lands in the practice is trading income, and a partner is taxed on their share of practice profit, not on drawings (link `gp-partnership-tax-complete-guide`, `gp-partnership-profit-sharing-tax-planning`).
  - Signpost `gp-accounting-guide` and `gp-bookkeeping-guide-uk` for the bookkeeping mechanics; do not re-teach them.
- **H2: VAT and pension in one breath (light, with signposts)**
  - VAT (one short paragraph): core NHS network income to the practices is outside the scope of VAT, but the way shared ARRS staff are employed and recharged between practices can create a standard-rated supply of staff. Do not teach it here; this is B2's job (link `arrs-reimbursement-employing-pcn-staff-tax`). Anchor §6.
  - Pension (one short paragraph): NHS-derived profit is pensionable for a partner via the Type 1 certificate; a company cannot hold the contract and company income is not pensionable. Clinical director and leadership pay is a special case, and whether it is pensionable depends on how it is paid (link B3, `pcn-clinical-director-payments-tax`). Anchor §2.C.

## 5. Exact HP positions, dates and verified anchors

**HP positions to use (anchor §3 unless noted):**
- PCN / Network Contract DES funding (including ARRS) is one of the named NHS GP income streams, sitting alongside Global Sum, QOF and enhanced services. (§3)
- The Network Contract DES is a voluntary Directed Enhanced Service that member practices sign up to on top of their core GMS or PMS contract. (§3, verified at source below.)
- ARRS is a reimbursement of defined additional-role employment costs up to a maximum, claimed by the network; it is not a grant. (§3, verified below; detail in B2.)
- Whatever PCN income reaches the practice is trading income; a partner is taxed on profit share, not drawings. (§3, §1.)
- There is no single fixed per-patient PCN figure; figures are uplifted annually, confirm the current contract documents. (§3 VERIFY framing applied to PCN streams.)
- Core NHS income is outside the scope of VAT, but shared-staff recharges can be standard-rated (light touch, detail in B2). (§6.)
- NHS-derived profit is pensionable via the Type 1 certificate; company income is not pensionable; clinical-director pay pensionability depends on route. (§2.C, detail in B3.)
