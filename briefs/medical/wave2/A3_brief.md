## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** Beyond the core Global Sum and QOF, a GP practice can earn a long tail of extra income from enhanced services: nationally directed schemes (DES) the practice can opt into, and locally commissioned services (LES, sometimes called locally commissioned or local enhanced services) that vary by area and ICB. This page explains what these streams are, who commissions them and how the practice decides whether a service is worth signing up to, then covers the part competitors skip: how this income is taxed as ordinary practice trading profit and where the VAT line falls (most is NHS care and outside scope, but some clinic-style work can be standard-rated). The aim is a partner who can look at a list of enhanced-service offers and understand the income, the tax and the decision.

**How this differs from existing pages and the two sibling picks.**
- Differs from `gp-tax-deductions-complete-list-2026` and `gp-partnership-tax-complete-guide`: those are about deductions and partnership tax mechanics. This page is about a specific income stream (enhanced/local services) and how that income is taxed, linking back for the deduction and profit-share detail.
- Differs from sibling A1 (`how-gms-funding-works-global-sum-carr-hill-explained`): A1 is the core Global Sum and Carr-Hill. This page is the optional income on top of the core, and is explicit that enhanced services are not part of the Global Sum.
- Differs from sibling A2 (`qof-income-gp-practice-accounting-explained`): A2 is QOF (the quality framework). Enhanced services are a different thing (DES/LES), commissioned and paid differently. This page states the distinction and links A2 so a reader does not conflate QOF with enhanced services.
- Note on scope: the **PCN / Network Contract DES** is itself a directed enhanced service, but the dedicated PCN and ARRS deep-dives are a separate Wave 2 cluster. This page mentions the Network Contract DES only as one example of a DES and does NOT link to or pre-empt the PCN cluster.

  - H3: What a DES is. Nationally agreed/directed services that commissioners must offer to all practices in England; the practice chooses whether to sign up. Funding and specification are set nationally.
  - H3: Examples. Use real, current-family examples cautiously (childhood and seasonal vaccination and immunisation programmes, the Network Contract DES delivered through PCNs, and other nationally directed schemes), framed as "examples, the DES list changes year to year". Do NOT assert a fixed roster as permanent.
  - H3: The Network Contract DES (named once). State it is the directed enhanced service that funds primary care networks and the ARRS, and point the reader to the dedicated PCN material on the site in general terms only (do NOT link a Wave 2 PCN slug; it is not yet live).
- **H2: Local Enhanced Services / Locally Commissioned Services (LES / LCS)**
  - H3: What a LES is. Services a local commissioner (the ICB, often after consulting the LMC) develops to supplement the core contract; not nationally agreed, so scope and funding vary across the country. The practice chooses whether to sign up.
  - H3: Examples. Minor surgery, contraceptive and coil/implant fittings, phlebotomy, near-patient testing, ambulatory blood-pressure monitoring, some mental-health and substance-misuse work, wound care and similar, framed as "typical examples, locally variable". Do NOT present a national fixed list.
  - H3: How a LES is priced and paid. Locally negotiated, often per-item or per-patient or as a block; paid by the commissioner. The practice should model whether the fee covers the staff time and overhead (the planning point).
- **H2: DES versus LES versus QOF versus core (a clear comparison)**
  - Short comparison so the reader does not conflate them: core Global Sum (per weighted patient, automatic), QOF (quality points, opt-in), DES (nationally directed, opt-in), LES (locally commissioned, opt-in, variable). Keep it tight; link A1 and A2.
- **H2: How enhanced-services income is taxed**
  - It is ordinary practice **trading income**: part of the partnership's taxable trading profit, allocated to partners and taxed on each partner's profit share with Class 4 NIC, via SA800 to SA104 (anchor §1, §8; link `gp-partnership-tax-complete-guide`).
  - State plainly: there is no separate tax regime for enhanced services; it is taxed as profit when earned. Reference deductible costs (the staff and consumables to deliver the service) by link to `gp-tax-deductions-complete-list-2026`, do not re-teach the deductions list.
  - H3: Timing and accruals. Where a service is delivered in one period but paid in the next, it should be accrued like other practice income (light touch; link `gp-accounting-guide`).
- **H2: The VAT line on enhanced and private-feeling services**
  - Most enhanced services are NHS-commissioned medical care and so are exempt / outside the scope of VAT, and NHS income does not count toward the registration threshold (anchor §6).
  - But the watch-item: where a practice does work whose primary purpose is not the protection, maintenance or restoration of health (for example some cosmetic or purely administrative or third-party-report work it picks up alongside NHS services), that can be standard-rated. Keep this to the §6 principal-purpose framing and link `gp-vat-registration` for the detail; do not over-claim.
- **H2: Should the practice sign up? (a decision lens, not advice)**
  - A neutral framework: does the fee cover the marginal cost (staff time, consumables, room, indemnity for private elements); does it fit the practice's capacity; does it have set-up cost; is it pensionable (NHS-commissioned work feeding partner profit is, company income is not, anchor §2.C). ## 5. Exact HP positions, dates and verified anchors

**HP positions to use (anchor §3 unless noted):**
- Enhanced services are an income stream **beyond core GMS**, alongside QOF and PCN/Network Contract DES funding. (§3)
- Enhanced-services income is **practice trading income**, taxed on the partner's profit share. (§3, §1, §8)
- VAT: NHS medical care is exempt / outside scope and does not count to the registration threshold; non-therapeutic, cosmetic-only or third-party-report work can be standard-rated (the principal-purpose test). (§6)
- NHS-commissioned profit is pensionable for a Type 1 partner; company income is not. (§2.C, light touch.)
