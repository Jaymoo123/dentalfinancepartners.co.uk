## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** Most GP partnerships run a **mutual assessment period**, a trial stretch (often around six to twelve months, but practice-specific) during which the incoming GP and the existing partners decide whether to offer and accept parity or permanency. This page explains what the mutual assessment period is, and, the substance of the page, the **financial due diligence** an incoming GP should do during it: read the accounts, understand the premises position and last-man-standing risk, check the contract and list size, read the partnership deed, understand drawings versus profit, and identify any outstanding liabilities. It is the "look before you leap" page for joining a partnership, framed around the trial period that gives you the window to do it.

**How this differs from `becoming-gp-partner-financial-implications` (the named broad page) and the siblings.**
- **Differs from `becoming-gp-partner-financial-implications`.** That page explains the **consequences** of becoming a partner (status change, drawings versus profit, NIC, pension, premises risk) and ends with a deed-signing checklist. This page is the **due diligence process during the trial period**: it is structured around the mutual assessment period as a concept (which the broad page does not cover) and gives a practical, ordered DD checklist of documents to obtain and questions to ask **before** you commit, rather than explaining what each consequence is. Where the broad page says "understand last-man-standing risk", this page says "during the mutual assessment period, get the lease and the property-partnership deed, and check who is on the title". ## 4. Section-by-section outline (target 2,800 to 3,500 words)

- **H1: The Mutual Assessment Period: What to Check Financially Before Becoming a GP Partner**
- **H2: What the mutual assessment period is**
  - Define it: a **trial period** before a partner is offered parity or made permanent, during which both sides assess fit (clinical, collegiate and financial). State it is set by the practice and the deed, commonly several months to around a year, but **practice-specific, so do NOT lock a standard length**. Explain its purpose for both sides and that it is the natural window to do your financial due diligence. Distinguish it briefly from **parity** (the route to a full share, covered in E1) and from a **salaried trial**; link E1 (`buying-into-gp-partnership-capital-parity-explained`) for parity. Note that during a mutual assessment period the new GP may be salaried or on a reduced share; the deed and offer letter set the basis.
- **H2: Why due diligence matters as much for the joiner as the practice**
  - Frame the asymmetry: the practice is assessing you, but you are also buying into a business with real liabilities and a long-term premises commitment, and (anchor §4) on entry you take a share of tangible assets, premises and capital accounts. State that the cost of poor diligence is years of exposure, and that the mutual assessment period exists precisely so both sides can find out. One sentence linking the broad `becoming-gp-partner-financial-implications` page for the consequences.
- **H2: Check 1: The accounts**
  - H3: Which accounts to obtain. Ask for at least the last two to three years of **partnership accounts** and, where relevant, the **property-partnership accounts**. Anchor §1 (the partnership files an SA800; profit flows to each partner's SA104). Link D1 (`reading-gp-partnership-accounts-current-capital-accounts`) for how to read them.
  - H3: What to look for. The **profit trend** (is it stable, rising, falling, and why), the **profit per partner / per session**, the split between NHS and private income, reliance on any single income stream (for example a large enhanced service or a PCN role), and the **expenses** profile (staff costs, locum reliance, finance costs). Keep this DD-focused, no fabricated figures; everything illustrative if used.
  - H3: Drawings versus profit and the current accounts. Anchor §1 (taxed on profit share, not drawings): check that partners' **drawings are not running ahead of profit** and look at the **current-account balances** (are partners overdrawn, is there undrawn profit funding the practice). Link sibling-cluster `gp-partner-drawings-vs-profit-tax-reserving` for the drawings-versus-profit detail.
- **H2: Check 2: The premises position and last-man-standing risk**
  - H3: Are the premises owned, leased or reimbursed. Establish how the premises are held (owner-occupied via a **separate property partnership or LLP**, leased from a third party, or NHS/PropCo-owned) and how income support flows (**notional rent** for owner-occupiers, the legacy **cost rent** scheme), anchor §4. Note amounts are District-Valuer / surveyor assessed and property-specific, so do NOT lock a figure.
  - H3: What documents to read. The **lease**, the **property-partnership deed**, the title, any **mortgage / borrowing** secured on the premises, the **last notional-rent review** and any rent-review or break clauses. State these are core DD documents.
  - H3: Last-man-standing exposure. Explain the check: if you take a premises share or sign the lease, what happens when partners leave and are not replaced. Anchor §4 (last-man-standing is a real planning point). Link `gp-partnership-last-man-standing-premises-risk` and `gp-surgery-premises-own-vs-rent-tax-guide`; do NOT re-teach them.
- **H2: Check 3: The contract and the list**
  - Explain the income-side DD: which **NHS contract** the practice holds (GMS, PMS or APMS) and whether it is stable, the **registered list size** and its trend, the **patient demographics** as they affect weighted funding, dependence on **QOF, enhanced services and PCN/Network Contract DES** income, and any **dispensing** income. Keep figures hedged and date-tagged where mentioned; state there is no single national per-patient value (light §3 cross-ref). Link `gp-practice-income-pcse-statement-reconciliation` for checking the income is being paid correctly, and the broad `gp-financial-planning` page for the wider picture.
- **H2: Check 4: The partnership deed**
  - The single most important document. List what to read for: **profit-sharing** method and the **parity timetable** (link E1), the **buy-in and buy-out** terms (link E1), **drawings** and how the year-end true-up works, **capital accounts** and how capital is returned on exit (light §4; link Cluster F retirement page `retiring-from-gp-partnership-tax-capital-account` for the exit detail), **decision-making and majority** provisions, **restrictive covenants**, **maternity / paternity / sickness** provisions, **expulsion and dispute** clauses, and the **premises** provisions including last-man-standing. State that the deed should be read by a **specialist solicitor and a medical accountant together**. Anchor §1 (taxed on profit share) where relevant.
- **H2: Check 5: Drawings, working capital and cash flow**
  - Explain that as a self-employed partner your cash flow changes: drawings against anticipated profit, payments on account, and the year-end true-up (anchor §1; link the broad page and `gp-partner-drawings-vs-profit-tax-reserving`). DD point: understand the practice's **working-capital position** and whether you will be asked to fund a share of it, and budget for **tax reserving** from day one. No fabricated figures.
- **H2: Check 6: Outstanding liabilities and contingent risks**
  - List the liabilities to surface: **practice borrowings** and any personal guarantees, **premises mortgage** and lease commitments, **dilapidations** exposure on a leased surgery, **employment liabilities** (staff redundancy / TUPE-type exposure on a merger), any **pension or tax** issues in the accounts, outstanding **NHS clawbacks** or income disputes, and any **CQC** or regulatory history (a standard transaction DD item, §10 light). State that an incoming partner can inherit a share of these. Link the broad page once for the risk picture.
- **H2: Check 7: Pension and personal tax (signpost, do not deep-dive)**
  - One short section: confirm how partnership profit will affect your **NHS pension** (Type 1 certification) and whether higher profits risk the **annual-allowance taper**, and that you will move to **Self Assessment** with payments on account. ## 5. Exact HP positions, dates and verified anchors

**HP positions to use:**
- A GP partner is **self-employed**; the partnership files an **SA800** and each partner's share flows to the **partnership pages (SA104)**, taxed as trading income with Class 4 NIC; **taxed on profit share, not drawings**; drawings are set against anticipated profit and trued up at year-end. (§1) Use in the accounts, drawings and deed checks.
- On entry, what changes hands is a share of **tangible assets and any owned premises through the capital accounts**; **NHS goodwill cannot be sold** (one sentence, link the complete guide / E1; do NOT re-teach). (§4)
- **Premises** often held in a **separate property partnership or LLP**; income support via **notional rent** (current-market-rent, District-Valuer assessed) or legacy **cost rent**; amounts property-specific, do NOT lock a figure. **Last-man-standing** is a real planning point. (§4; link the two premises pages.)
- Light §3 cross-ref: practice income is GMS/PMS/APMS, Global Sum weighted by Carr-Hill, plus QOF, enhanced services and PCN/Network Contract DES; **no single national per-patient value**, figures uplift annually. Keep hedged; do NOT lock a Global Sum or QOF figure.
- Light §10 cross-ref: **CQC** registration/inspection history is a standard transaction DD item.

**DO NOT** lock a standard mutual assessment period length, a standard parity period, or any buy-in / income figure (all practice-specific; §4 premises and parity vary). Do NOT re-teach the broad financial implications, the buy-in/parity mechanics (E1), interest relief (E3), last-man-standing, the annual-allowance taper or Type 1 certification: link out. Do NOT give legal advice on the deed (frame as "have it read by a specialist solicitor and a medical accountant").
