## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** Since 1 October 2023 a doctor can draw between 20% and 100% of their accrued NHS pension while staying in the same job and continuing to build new pension in the 2015 scheme, provided they reduce pensionable pay or commitment by at least 10% for the first 12 months. This page explains how partial (flexible) retirement actually works for doctors, the conditions, how it interacts with the annual allowance, and where it sits next to taking benefits early with an actuarial reduction. It is a planning page for consultants and GP partners thinking about winding down without fully stopping.

## 4. Section-by-section outline (target 2,800 to 3,500 words)

- **H1: NHS Pension Partial Retirement for Doctors: Drawing 20 to 100 Percent While Still Working**
- **H2: What partial (flexible) retirement is**
  - Draw some or all of your accrued NHS benefits while staying employed and continuing to accrue in the 2015 scheme. Contrast with full retirement (you stop) and retire-and-return (you leave and come back). Note this is also called flexible retirement / drawdown.
- **H2: The headline rules (locked figures)**
  - H3: How much you can draw. A minimum of 20% and a maximum of 100% of your accrued benefits.
  - H3: How many times. In up to two drawdown events.
  - H3: The 10% reduction condition. You must agree a new working arrangement that reduces your pensionable pay (or commitment) by at least 10%, and keep it at the reduced level for at least 12 months, or you lose eligibility for the benefits taken.
  - H3: Minimum age. From age 55 (minimum pension age), with employer agreement.
  - H3: The 1 October 2023 change. Partial retirement was already available to 2008 Section and 2015 Scheme members; from 1 October 2023 the regulations changed so 1995 Section members can take it too. This is the key recent development.
- **H2: Re-accrual: you keep building pension in the 2015 scheme**
  - After partial retirement you continue as an active member accruing in the 2015 scheme (1/54th CARE). Explain that you are drawing old benefits and building new ones at the same time. Cross-ref §2 / AA pillar for the 2015 mechanics.
- **H2: How partial retirement interacts with the annual allowance**
  - Continuing accrual means continuing pension input, so a doctor can still generate an annual allowance charge after partial retirement; the taper still applies to high earners.
  - H3: The Scheme Pays timing point. Once you have taken ALL your benefits you can no longer make a Scheme Pays election, so a doctor with a likely AA charge should mind the sequencing. Link A2.
  - H3: Reducing hours can change taper exposure. A 10%+ reduction in pensionable pay may lower threshold/adjusted income; treat as individual, no blanket claim. Link the taper calculator.
- **H2: How partial retirement interacts with McCloud**
  - Taking benefits is the point at which the Deferred Choice Underpin choice for the remedy period is offered, so a partial-retirement event can trigger your McCloud choice. Link A1. Keep it factual.
- **H2: Partial retirement vs early retirement (the actuarial-reduction route)**
  - H3: Early retirement before normal pension age. Minimum pension age 55; NPAs are 60 (1995), 65 (2008), State Pension Age (2015). Taking benefits before your NPA applies a permanent actuarial reduction set by NHSBSA/GAD factors. Do NOT lock a fixed percentage (GAD revises factors); point to the NHSBSA factor tables.
  - H3: ERRBO (Early Retirement Reduction Buy Out). In the 2015 scheme, ERRBO lets a member pay extra to buy out part of the reduction so they can retire earlier without the full cut. Keep it brief and factual.
  - H3: Which route suits whom. Partial retirement keeps you working and accruing; early retirement is a full or partial exit with a permanent reduction. Frame as individual.

## 5. Exact HP figures, dates and statutory anchors to cite (all verified 2026-06-03)

Quote these exactly:
- Partial (flexible) retirement available across all sections **from 1 October 2023** (the change extended it to 1995 Section members; it was already available to 2008/2015 members).
- Draw **20% to 100%** of accrued benefits, in **up to two** drawdown events.
- Must reduce pensionable pay/commitment by **at least 10%**, held for **at least 12 months**, or eligibility for the benefits taken is lost.
- Minimum pension age **55** (with employer agreement).
- Continue to **re-accrue in the 2015 scheme** (1/54th CARE) after partial retirement.
- Early retirement before NPA = **permanent actuarial reduction** via NHSBSA/GAD-set factors (do NOT lock a fixed %). NPAs: **60 (1995), 65 (2008), State Pension Age (2015)**.
- **ERRBO** (Early Retirement Reduction Buy Out) in the 2015 scheme can buy out part of the reduction.
- Supporting AA figures (link out, do not re-teach at length): standard AA **£60,000** (2025/26); taper where threshold income **> £200,000 AND** adjusted income **> £260,000**, £1 for every £2, down to a **£10,000** floor.
