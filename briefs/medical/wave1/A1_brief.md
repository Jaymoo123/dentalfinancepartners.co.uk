## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** The retrospective half of McCloud is finished: eligible doctors had their 1 April 2015 to 31 March 2022 service rolled back into the 1995 or 2008 legacy section on 1 October 2023, so there is nothing to do now. The live half is the Deferred Choice Underpin, the decision (legacy benefits or 2015 benefits for the remedy period) that a doctor makes at the point of retirement, plus any annual-allowance and tax reconciliation that the rollback triggers. This page tells a doctor exactly what was done automatically, what they choose later, who is eligible, and how the NHS Digital Remedy Service and HMRC route handles the tax clean-up.

## 4. Section-by-section outline (target 2,800 to 3,500 words)

- **H1: The McCloud Remedy and Your NHS Pension: What Doctors Need to Do at Retirement**
- **H2: McCloud in one paragraph (what it is and why it happened)**
  - The 2015 transitional protections were held to be age-discriminatory in the *McCloud and Sargeant* litigation. The remedy unwinds that discrimination for the remedy period.
  - State plainly: the mandatory, retrospective part is complete; the remaining live elements are the in-retirement choice and any tax reconciliation. (Frame as generally neutral-to-positive: a member gets the better of two options.)
- **H2: Are you affected? McCloud eligibility for doctors**
  - Two conditions, both required: joined the NHS Pension Scheme on or before 31 March 2012 AND were an active member on 1 April 2015.
  - H3: Who is in scope. Currently working, already retired, left the NHS, or deceased members (estates) can all be affected.
  - H3: Who is out of scope. A doctor who first joined on or after 1 April 2012 (no legacy service), or who was not active on 1 April 2015. Note: still read the rest if unsure, eligibility is about scheme dates, not job title.
- **H2: The remedy period: 1 April 2015 to 31 March 2022**
  - Define the remedy period precisely. Service in this window is what the remedy re-treats.
  - From 1 April 2022 everyone accrues in the 2015 scheme regardless of protection (cross-ref §2, link to AA guide).
- **H2: What has already happened automatically (rollback, 1 October 2023)**
  - On 1 October 2023, remedy-period service for eligible members was rolled back into their legacy section (1995 or 2008). No member action was needed for this step.
  - H3: What rollback means in practice. Remedy-period benefits are currently held on legacy terms by default until the member chooses otherwise at retirement.
  - H3: Remediable Service Statements (RSS). Members receive statements showing both sets of figures to support the later choice.
- **H2: The Deferred Choice Underpin: the decision you make at retirement**
  - The core message: most doctors do NOT choose now. The choice (1995/2008 legacy benefits or 2015 benefits for the remedy period) is made when you apply to take your pension.
  - H3: Why deferred and not now. You only have to commit when the numbers are known, which is at retirement.
  - H3: Already retired or retiring before the new process. Those members make the choice retrospectively, after retirement, supported by their remedial service statement.
  - H3: How to think about the choice (general, non-advisory). 1/80th plus automatic lump sum and NPA 60 (1995) vs 1/54th CARE and a different normal pension age (2015); the right answer depends on individual service, earnings shape and retirement age. State that this is individual and a doctor should take advice. No fixed "legacy always wins" claim.
- **H2: The tax side: why McCloud can re-open old annual-allowance years**
  - Because remedy-period pension input is recomputed on the rolled-back basis, the pension input amounts for 2015/16 to 2021/22 can change, which can change historic annual-allowance positions (charges that were paid, or new charges, or refunds).
  - H3: The NHS Digital Remedy Service and HMRC reconciliation. HMRC built a digital service so members can submit revised pension savings information and have over-paid or under-paid AA tax reconciled rather than amending years individually.
  - H3: Compensation and the Cost Claim Back Scheme. Members who incurred costs (for example professional fees or interest) because of the remedy may be able to claim; keep this factual and brief.
  - H3: Interaction with Scheme Pays. A revised historic charge may be settled via Scheme Pays. Do not explain the mechanics here; link to sibling A2.

  - Confirm eligibility against the two dates. Keep remedial service statements. Do not try to make the choice early. Flag any historic AA charge for review (link A2). Factor McCloud into retirement-timing conversations (link A3). Get the calculation checked before you commit at retirement.

## 5. Exact HP figures, dates and statutory anchors to cite (all verified 2026-06-03)

- Rollback to the legacy 1995/2008 section: **from 1 October 2023** (automatic, no member action).

- Scheme section detail for the choice paragraph only: 1995 = 1/80th + automatic 3x lump sum, NPA 60; 2008 = 1/60th, NPA 65; 2015 = CARE 1/54th, NPA = State Pension Age (or 65 if later). (HP §2.)
