## 3. Angle (2 to 3 sentences) + differentiation

**Angle.** When a doctor breaches the annual allowance, they can pay the resulting tax charge out of the NHS pension itself (Scheme Pays) rather than from their own pocket, in exchange for a permanent reduction in benefits. This page is the dedicated mechanics deep-dive: mandatory versus voluntary Scheme Pays, the precise test (charge over £2,000 AND NHS scheme input over the standard £60,000 allowance), the hard 31 July election deadline with a worked timeline, how the benefit reduction is applied via NHSBSA/GAD factors, and how to revise an election. It is the operational "how do I actually settle this charge" companion to the broader minimise-the-charge overview.

## 4. Section-by-section outline (target 2,800 to 3,500 words)

- **H1: NHS Pension Scheme Pays for Doctors: Deadlines, the Mandatory Test and How It Reduces Your Pension**
- **H2: What Scheme Pays is (in one paragraph)**
  - A facility letting the NHS scheme settle your annual allowance charge in exchange for a permanent actuarial reduction in your benefits. You are not waiving the tax, you are funding it from the pension instead of from cash.
  - One-line link to the AA pillar for how a charge arises; do not re-teach the £60k limit here.
- **H2: Mandatory versus voluntary Scheme Pays (the key distinction)**
  - H3: Mandatory Scheme Pays. Available where BOTH conditions are met: the annual allowance charge for the tax year exceeds £2,000 AND the pension input amount in the NHS scheme alone exceeds the standard £60,000 annual allowance. Where mandatory applies the scheme MUST accept the election for the qualifying portion.
  - H3: Voluntary Scheme Pays. Where the charge is driven only by the taper (input below £60k but the lower tapered allowance is breached), or the charge is £2,000 or less, mandatory does not apply; the NHS scheme offers voluntary Scheme Pays at its discretion, on its terms. This matters specifically for tapered high earners.
  - H3: The split-charge trap. A charge can be part-mandatory, part-voluntary; explain that the mandatory limb only covers the portion driven by input above £60k.
- **H2: The numbers test, worked out**
  - H3: The £2,000 threshold. It is the charge, not the excess, that must exceed £2,000.
  - H3: The £60,000 input test. It is the NHS scheme input amount alone (not aggregated across all your pensions) that must exceed £60,000 for the mandatory limb.
  - H3: Why tapered doctors often fall into voluntary-only. Worked illustration of a consultant whose input is, say, £48,000 but whose tapered allowance is £20,000: there is a charge, but because input is below £60k the mandatory route is unavailable; voluntary Scheme Pays may still be offered.
- **H2: The deadline: 31 July, with a worked timeline**
  - The election deadline is 31 July in the year following the year in which the tax year ends (FA 2004 s.237BA).
  - H3: Worked example (lock the HP example). A 2025/26 charge must be elected by 31 July 2027. Walk the timeline: tax year ends 5 April 2026, the "year following" ends 5 April 2027, deadline 31 July 2027.
  - H3: The extended deadline where the scheme is late. Where the scheme administrator notifies a revised pension input amount on or after a date that makes the standard deadline unfair, s.237BA extends the limit to the earlier of three months from that notification or six years from the end of the original tax year. (Verified at s.237BA, see anchors.)
  - H3: The absolute backstop. No election can be made once you have become actually entitled to all your benefits under the scheme (taken everything). Time it before full retirement.
- **H2: How the benefit reduction works (scheme factors)**
  - The amount paid is deducted and your benefits are permanently reduced using NHSBSA/GAD-set actuarial factors (age, scheme section, amount). Do NOT lock a fixed percentage (GAD revises factors). Explain that the older you are when the reduction bites, broadly the larger the proportional impact, and point to the NHSBSA factor guidance.
  - H3: Is it worth it. Frame the trade-off (cash now vs reduced pension later) as individual; no blanket recommendation.
- **H2: How to make a Scheme Pays election (the practical steps)**
  - Get the pension savings statement / input amount from NHSBSA; calculate the charge (or have it calculated); declare the charge on the Self Assessment return; submit the Scheme Pays election form to NHS Pensions by the deadline. Keep evidence.
  - H3: The Self Assessment interaction. The charge still goes on your tax return even if the scheme pays it; the return reports the charge and the Scheme Pays amount.
- **H2: Revising or amending an election**
  - An election may be amended but may not be revoked (FA 2004 s.237B). Explain when a member revises (for example after a corrected input amount), and the practical route via NHS Pensions.
- **H2: Scheme Pays and McCloud / tapered years**
  - Short bridge: a McCloud re-calculation can change historic input amounts and therefore historic charges, which may be settled via Scheme Pays within the extended window; link A1. Tapered-only charges are voluntary-only; link the taper calculator and the minimise page.
- **H2: Common mistakes doctors make with Scheme Pays**
  - Missing the 31 July deadline; assuming mandatory applies when the charge is taper-only; leaving the election until after taking benefits; forgetting it still goes on the tax return; not modelling the permanent benefit reduction.

## 5. Exact HP figures, dates and statutory anchors to cite (all verified 2026-06-03)

Quote these exactly:
- Mandatory Scheme Pays test: charge **exceeds £2,000 AND** NHS scheme pension input amount **exceeds the standard £60,000** annual allowance. (Both required.)
- A charge driven only by the taper (input below £60k) is **voluntary-only**.
- Election deadline: **31 July in the year following the year in which the tax year ends**. Worked example: a **2025/26 charge must be elected by 31 July 2027**.
- Extended limb: where the scheme notifies a revised input amount late, the deadline extends to the **earlier of three months from notification or six years from the end of the original tax year** (s.237BA).
- An election **may be amended but may not be revoked**; no election once **actually entitled to all benefits**.
- Reduction applied via **NHSBSA/GAD-set actuarial factors** (do NOT state a fixed percentage).
- Supporting AA figures (link out, do not re-teach at length): standard AA **£60,000** (2025/26); taper where threshold income **> £200,000 AND** adjusted income **> £260,000**, £1 for every £2, down to a **£10,000** floor; MPAA **£10,000**.
