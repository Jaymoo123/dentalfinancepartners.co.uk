## Required structure

Open with a BLUF answer box (40 to 60 words, citable): every CQC-registered service must have a registered manager in post (or actively recruiting one) to carry on a regulated activity lawfully; for the provider this is a mandatory senior payroll cost and the person accountable for key financial and compliance paperwork, so it must be budgeted as a fixed overhead from day one (HP 21).

H2 skeleton (each H2 opens with its own 40 to 60 word citable answer):
1. Why the registered manager is a business cost, not just a hire: registration is mandatory before regulated activity and the service needs a registered manager as part of that (HP 21). Frame the role as an unavoidable fixed cost that lands BEFORE trading income.
2. Registered manager vs nominated individual vs provider: who is who in the registration structure and which of them the accountant models as payroll. Keep to the business-structure framing; do not stray into clinical or care-practice duties.
3. The loaded cost of a registered manager: model the salary as an employer cost with employer NIC 15% above £5,000 (HP 11), the Employment Allowance up to £10,500 (HP 12, which for a small operator can absorb much of the NIC on a single senior salary), pension and holiday on-cost. Do NOT state a market salary figure as fact; if a salary is used in a worked example, present it as an illustrative input the operator sets, not a claimed rate.
4. The financial paperwork the registered manager owns or signs: link to the wave-1 FVS walkthrough (do not re-explain the FVS), notifications, and the recurring compliance evidence that has a cost attached. Keep it to the money/paperwork slice.
5. The cost of NOT having one: operating without a registered manager puts registration and lawful trading at risk (HP 21, trading without meeting registration conditions). Frame as a business-continuity and revenue risk, not a clinical one.
6. Worked example: the registered manager as a line in a small-service budget, illustrative salary input, employer NIC at 15% above £5,000, Employment Allowance offset, shown as a monthly fixed cost against pre-trading and early-trading cash. Every rate labelled to its HP.
7. What we do / next step: route to /services/care-payroll (loaded-cost payroll modelling) and /for/care-startups (registration stage).

## House positions touched

- HP 21: registration mandatory before regulated activity; a registered manager is part of meeting registration; trading without it is a criminal offence. https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/registration
- HP 11: employer NIC 15% above £5,000 secondary threshold. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2025-to-2026
- HP 12: Employment Allowance up to £10,500. https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance
- HP 22 (link only, do not re-explain): FVS on CQC's template. https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/supporting-documents-provider/document/financial-viability-template
