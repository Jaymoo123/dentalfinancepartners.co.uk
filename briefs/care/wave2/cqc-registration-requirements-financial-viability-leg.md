## Required structure

Open with a BLUF answer box (40 to 60 words, citable): to register with CQC a provider must show it will meet the regulations for its regulated activity, which includes a fit-and-proper registered manager, suitable premises and, critically, evidence of financial viability, a statement on CQC's template normally prepared by an accountant, proving the service can run safely and sustainably from day one (HP 21, HP 22).

H2 skeleton (each H2 opens with its own 40 to 60 word citable answer):

List the requirement categories at a high level (regulated activity scope, statement of purpose, registered manager, premises, and the financial-viability evidence). Keep clinical/quality requirements one-line and non-clinical.
2. The financial-viability requirement (the leg this page owns): CQC requires evidence that the proposed service is financially viable, via a financial viability statement on CQC's template, normally accountant-prepared (HP 22). Explain WHAT the requirement is proving (resources to run safely from day one) and link the wave-1 FVS walkthrough for the how-to. Do NOT re-explain the template here.
3. The numbers behind "viable": what makes a financial case stand up, opening capital and funding, cash runway to break-even, a cost base modelled on current NLW £12.71 from 1 April 2026 (HP 7), employer NIC 15% above £5,000 (HP 11), Employment Allowance up to £10,500 (HP 12), and travel-time/sleep-in NMW where domiciliary (HP 5, HP 6). Cross-link the true-cost and staffing-margin calculators.
