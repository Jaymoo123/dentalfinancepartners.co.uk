## Required structure

Open with a BLUF answer box (40 to 60 words, citable): CQC registration is mandatory before a provider can lawfully carry on a regulated activity in England, and it carries real cost, the work of reaching registration (including an accountant-prepared financial viability statement), a recurring CQC fee banded by the scale of the regulated activity, and cash burned before the first fee can be billed (HP 21, HP 22).

H2 skeleton (each money H2 opens with its own 40 to 60 word citable answer):
1. What "getting CQC-registered" actually costs, the three buckets: one-off cost of reaching registration, the recurring CQC fee (a banded annual overhead, figure on CQC's fee scheme, see danger zone), and pre-trading cash burn. Frame registration as mandatory pre-trading and trading before it as a criminal offence under the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (HP 21).
2. The one-off cost of reaching registration: the financial viability statement (accountant-prepared on CQC's template, HP 22, link the wave-1 walkthrough not a re-explanation), the business plan, projections, DBS and manager-in-post costs described qualitatively. No pound figures where no HP covers them.
3. The recurring CQC fee, how it is structured (not what it is): explain that CQC charges a periodic registration fee set by a published fee scheme, banded by the scale of the regulated activity (registered places for a care home, service-user capacity band for a domiciliary agency), and that it is a permanent annual overhead not a one-off. Link CQC's fee-scheme page and the CQC fee calculator. Do NOT state a fee amount (danger zone).
4. Cash burn before you can trade: model the runway, rent/lease, fit-out, recruitment, the manager's salary and pre-opening payroll, all landing BEFORE the first invoice. Tie the wage floor to NLW £12.71 from 1 April 2026 (HP 7), employer NIC 15% above £5,000 (HP 11), Employment Allowance up to £10,500 (HP 12). Cross-link the true-cost and staffing-margin calculators.
5. Worked example: a small domiciliary start-up cost timeline, month 0 to first-invoice, showing the one-off registration work, the pre-trading payroll and overhead burn, and where the recurring CQC fee first bites, with every rate labelled to its HP and the CQC fee shown as "per CQC fee scheme" not a number.
6. Capital allowances soften the fit-out: AIA up to £1m absorbs qualifying plant/equipment (HP 15); FA 2026 sequencing AIA then 40% FYA then 14% WDA (HP 16); SBA 3% on construction (HP 17). Link the capital-allowances wave-1 blog.
7. Registration is not the finish line: the recurring CQC fee, market-oversight for large providers (HP 23), and ongoing compliance are permanent lines in the budget.
8. What we do / next step: route to /for/care-startups, /services/cqc-financial-viability-statement, and the CQC fee calculator.

## House positions touched

- HP 21: registration mandatory before regulated activity; financial leg; pre-registration trading is a criminal offence. https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-providers/registration
- HP 22: FVS on CQC's template, normally accountant-prepared. https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/supporting-documents-provider/document/financial-viability-template
- HP 7: NLW £12.71 (21+) from 1 April 2026. https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates
- HP 11: employer NIC 15% above £5,000. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2025-to-2026
- HP 12: Employment Allowance up to £10,500. https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance
- HP 15: AIA £1,000,000. https://www.gov.uk/capital-allowances/annual-investment-allowance
- HP 16: FA 2026 WDA 14% + 40% FYA sequencing. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/11/section/28 ; https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/11/section/29
- HP 17: SBA 3%. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claiming-capital-allowances-for-structures-and-buildings

## Hallucination danger zones

- **CQC registration fee amounts are the primary danger zone.** No HP and no ledger entry contains any CQC fee figure (annual fee, application fee, band thresholds, per-bed or per-capacity amounts). NEVER state one in prose. Describe the fee as a real banded recurring cost, link CQC's fee-scheme page, and point the reader to the CQC fee calculator for the current figure. If the site later wants a stated figure, it must be added as a numbered HP first.
- Link (do not invent) the CQC fee-scheme URL. Stage-2 must confirm the exact live fee-scheme URL to cite; the calculator brief names the same source.
- No DBS fee, no manager qualification cost, no consultant/accountant price (pricing is config-driven and faceless).
- Model every wage/NIC/allowance figure only at its HP rate.
- England only: CQC does not register providers in Scotland (Care Inspectorate), Wales (CIW) or NI (RQIA). Do not give devolved figures.
