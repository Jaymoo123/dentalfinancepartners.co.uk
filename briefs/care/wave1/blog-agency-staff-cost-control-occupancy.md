## Required structure

BLUF rule: every money/guide H2 opens with a citable 40-60 word answer first.

H2 skeleton:
1. Why agency staff cost so much more than your own (BLUF: agency cover carries the agency's margin on top of the worker's pay, so the loaded hourly cost sits well above a substantive worker whose true cost is pay plus employer NIC at 15% above £5,000 plus holiday pay; the gap is the case for reducing reliance) [HP11, HP7]
2. The true cost of a care hour, built up (worked build: base pay at the April 2026 NLW of £12.71, plus employer NIC at 15%, plus 12.07% holiday accrual, plus travel/mileage where relevant, versus the agency invoice rate) [HP7, HP11, HP10, HP8]
3. Controlling agency spend without breaching NMW or misclassifying (BLUF: substituting agency cover with bank/zero-hours staff is a common lever, but bank staff still trigger NMW travel/sleep-in rules and holiday-pay accrual, and cannot be labelled self-employed to dodge them) [HP5, HP6, HP9, HP10]
4. The Employment Allowance offset on your substantive wage bill (BLUF: most care operators can claim up to £10,500 per year against employer NIC, improving the substantive-vs-agency comparison further for smaller operators) [HP12]
5. Occupancy voids: the second margin lever (BLUF: an empty bed earns nothing but still carries most of its fixed cost, so void days fall almost entirely to the bottom line; account for occupancy on an available-bed-day basis and track the void cost explicitly) [operations-finance, no rate HP]
6. Break-even occupancy, worked (a simple model: fixed cost base / contribution per occupied bed-week -> the occupancy level below which the home loses money; tie back to the fee-mix income streams)
7. Reading agency spend and occupancy together (a small dashboard: agency-spend ratio, occupancy percentage, contribution per bed-week; what "good" looks like directionally, no invented benchmark)
8. Getting the numbers under control (route to /for/care-homes + the staffing-margin and true-cost-per-hour calculators) and next-step CTA
