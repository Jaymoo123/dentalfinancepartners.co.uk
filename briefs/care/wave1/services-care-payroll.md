### Concrete TS-field candidates

- **stats[3]** (HP-verifiable only): (a) sleep-in shifts pay NMW only for time awake and working, not the sleeping period, after Royal Mencap Society v Tomlinson-Blake (HP5); (b) NLW is £12.71 for workers 21 and over from 1 April 2026, the floor for the dominant care cost line (HP7); (c) employer NIC is 15% above the £5,000 secondary threshold, with Employment Allowance up to £10,500 offsetting it for eligible operators (HP11, HP12).
- **challenges[4]**: (1) paying only face-to-face time and nothing for inter-call travel creates unlawful NMW shortfalls (HP6); (2) holiday-pay reference periods that omit average overtime and travel payments (HP10); (3) labelling rota carers self-employed when they are workers in substance, risking back-dated PAYE/NIC/NMW (HP9); (4) underestimating sponsored-worker cost per head (licence, immigration skills charge) in fee models (HP14).
- **howWeHelp[3]**: (1) run payroll that treats sleep-ins, travel time and irregular-hours holiday accrual correctly; (2) model employer NIC and Employment Allowance per head so budgets are accurate, not blanket-percentage guesses; (3) build sponsored-staff and misclassification risk into your cost base before HMRC does.
