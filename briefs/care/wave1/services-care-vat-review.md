### Concrete TS-field candidates

- **stats[3]** (HP-verifiable only): (a) welfare services by a CQC-registered provider are VAT-exempt under Group 7 of Schedule 9 VATA 1994, so input VAT on purchases is irrecoverable, a permanent overhead (HP1); (b) VAT registration is compulsory only once taxable turnover exceeds £90,000, and exempt care fees do not count toward it (HP3); (c) partial-exemption de minimis allows full input-VAT recovery only if exempt input tax averages no more than £625/month and is under half of total input tax (HP4).
- **challenges[4]**: (1) treating VAT exemption as a benefit when it is a locked-in cost (HP1); (2) mixed providers not running partial exemption and losing recoverable VAT (HP4); (3) relying on a pre-2025 VAT-grouping scheme now under active HMRC challenge and removal (HP2); (4) assuming a multi-million-pound turnover triggers VAT registration when taxable turnover is nil (HP3).
- **howWeHelp[3]**: (1) map your supplies to the welfare exemption and quantify the irrecoverable-VAT cost honestly; (2) run partial-exemption calculations for mixed activity to recover what is legitimately recoverable; (3) stress-test any VAT-grouping structure against RCB 2/2025 before HMRC does.
