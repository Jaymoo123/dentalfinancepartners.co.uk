### Concrete TS-field candidates

- **stats[3]** (HP-verifiable only): (a) Annual Investment Allowance gives a 100% deduction on up to £1,000,000 of qualifying plant and machinery (hoists, beds, fit-out) per year (HP15); (b) from April 2026 the main-rate writing-down allowance is 14% and a new 40% first-year allowance applies to qualifying new main-pool additions (HP16); (c) Structures and Buildings Allowance gives 3% per year straight-line on qualifying construction, worth £120,000 a year on a £4m build (HP17).
- **challenges[4]**: (1) buying at a fee mix that looks fine until you separate LA, self-funder and FNC income (HP24); (2) missing the capital-allowances value embedded in the property and fit-out (HP15-17); (3) propco/opco structures triggering associated-company rules that hit the 25% CT rate sooner (HP18); (4) treating VAT exemption as neutral when it is a permanent irrecoverable cost on the acquired operation (HP1).
- **howWeHelp[3]**: (1) run financial due diligence that stress-tests the target's real fee mix, occupancy and staffing cost; (2) quantify the capital-allowances position (AIA, then 40% FYA, then 14% WDA, plus SBA) so the price reflects the tax value; (3) advise on acquisition structure (asset vs share, propco/opco) with the associated-company and CT consequences modelled.
