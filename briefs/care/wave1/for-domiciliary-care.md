## Search-intent class + play

Sub-segment lead hub (HIRE) with the strongest OPERATOR-PROBLEM assist layer on the site, because this is where the dedicated tier is thinnest (only Marele and CareAxis are truly domiciliary-native) and the finance is most distinctive. ## House positions touched

- HP 5: sleep-in NMW, only time awake for work counts (Mencap; sleeping-facilities condition). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/calculating-the-minimum-wage/working-hours-for-which-the-minimum-wage-must-be-paid ; https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/national-minimum-wage-manual/nmwm08180
- HP 6: inter-call travel is working time; commuting to first/from last call excluded. same gov.uk NMW hours URL
- HP 7: NLW £12.71 (21+), £10.85 (18-20), £8.00 (under-18/apprentice) from 1 April 2026. https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates
- HP 8: AMAP 55p/mile first 10,000 miles from 6 April 2026. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2026-to-2027
- HP 9: self-employed carers usually employees/workers in substance (PAYE/NMW audit risk). https://www.gov.uk/employment-status/employee
- HP 10: irregular-hours holiday accrues on hours worked; 12.07% shorthand of 5.6-week entitlement. https://www.gov.uk/holiday-entitlement-rights
- HP 11, HP 12: employer NIC 15% above £5,000 secondary threshold; Employment Allowance up to £10,500. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2025-to-2026 ; https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance

Consistency rules: never frame sleep-ins as fully NMW-paid throughout (HP 5); never reduce pay to "per visit" and ignore travel time (HP 6); NLW and AMAP are year-sensitive, re-verify at write time.
