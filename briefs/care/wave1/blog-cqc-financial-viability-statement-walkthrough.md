## Required structure

Open with a BLUF answer box (40 to 60 words, citable): a financial viability statement is a document new care providers must submit as part of CQC registration, prepared on CQC's own template and normally prepared or signed by an accountant, demonstrating the proposed service has the financial resources to run safely and sustainably from day one (HP 22).

H2 skeleton (each money H2 opens with its own 40 to 60 word citable answer):
1. What the FVS is and why CQC asks for it: registration is mandatory before providing a regulated activity and the application has a financial leg (HP 21); the FVS is the accountant-prepared part of that leg on CQC's template (HP 22). Link the live CQC template URL.
2. Who must submit one and when: new-provider registration timing; trading before registration is a criminal offence under the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (HP 21). Frame the FVS as a pre-launch gate, not a post-launch task.
3. What CQC's template actually contains: walk the sections at the level the template supports. FLAG: do not invent template line-items or a CQC-specified format beyond what the live template shows; anything not visible on the template is a Stage-2 verify against the live document.
4. The numbers a viable statement has to stand up: start-up capital and funding sources; cash-flow runway; break-even occupancy (care homes) or break-even hours (domiciliary); the cost base modelled on CURRENT rules, NLW £12.71 from 1 April 2026 (HP 7), employer NIC 15% above £5,000 (HP 11), Employment Allowance up to £10,500 (HP 12), travel-time and sleep-in NMW where domiciliary (HP 5, HP 6). Cross-link the true-cost and staffing-margin calculators (/calculators/true-cost-care-hour-calculator, /calculators/care-staffing-cost-margin-calculator) as the tools that produce these numbers.
5. Worked example: a small domiciliary start-up FVS skeleton, showing opening capital, month-by-month cash runway to break-even hours, and how the wage/NIC floor drives the required fee rate. Use HP-backed figures only; label every rate to its HP.
6. Why it is normally accountant-prepared and what a weak FVS looks like: common reasons a financial submission fails to convince (unfunded runway, wage costs modelled below the legal floor, no break-even). Positions the service without pricing claims.
7. FVS versus the wider registration pack: the FVS is the financial document; the business plan and other registration evidence sit alongside it. Note the "cqc registration business plan" neighbour.
8. What we do / next step: route to /services/cqc-financial-viability-statement and /for/care-startups.
