## House positions touched

- HP 1: welfare exemption is a cost, input VAT unrecoverable. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/welfare-services-and-goods-notice-7012
- HP 3, HP 4: £90,000 threshold on taxable turnover only; partial-exemption de minimis £625/month average for mixed providers. https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration/when-to-register ; https://www.gov.uk/guidance/partial-exemption-vat-notice-706
- HP 24: FNC £267.68 standard / £368.24 higher weekly from 1 April 2026 (England). https://www.gov.uk/government/news/better-community-care-thanks-to-nursing-funding-boost
- HP 25: CHC funds the full package; supply is to the NHS, changes VAT and fee analysis; do not conflate with FNC. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-framework-for-nhs-continuing-healthcare-and-nhs-funded-nursing-care
- HP 26: fair-cost-of-care / market-sustainability framework as fee-negotiation tool. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/care-act-statutory-guidance/care-and-support-statutory-guidance
- HP 15, HP 16, HP 17: AIA £1m; 14% WDA + 40% FYA from April 2026; SBA 3%. legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/11/section/28 and /section/29 ; https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claiming-capital-allowances-for-structures-and-buildings
- HP 18: CT 19%/25% with marginal relief; associated-company effect in propco/opco. https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates
- HP 20: business rates; SBRR below £15,000 RV (most homes are above). https://www.gov.uk/apply-for-business-rate-relief/small-business-rate-relief

Consistency rules: FNC/CHC never conflated; VAT exemption always a cost; FNC rate re-verified at write time; England default with other nations flagged.
