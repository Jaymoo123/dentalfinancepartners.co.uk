### Concrete TS-field candidates

- **stats[3]** (HP-verifiable only): (a) CQC registration is mandatory before providing regulated activities, and new providers must submit a financial viability statement on CQC's template (HP21, HP22); (b) welfare supplies are VAT-exempt so input VAT is irrecoverable from day one, and registration only bites once taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 (HP1, HP3); (c) the NLW of £12.71 (21 and over) from 1 April 2026 is the dominant cost line, before employer NIC at 15% above £5,000 and travel-time pay (HP7, HP11, HP6).
- **challenges[4]**: (1) projections that ignore inter-call travel time as paid working time and understate the wage bill (HP6); (2) leaving the FVS to the last minute and stalling registration (HP22); (3) assuming VAT registration or recovery when supplies are exempt (HP1, HP3); (4) an owner-manager pay structure not modelled for dividend tax and the £500 allowance (HP28).
- **howWeHelp[3]**: (1) set up the right structure and opening accounts, MTD-IT ready if you trade personally; (2) build projections and the CQC financial viability statement together so registration and funding align; (3) stand up payroll, VAT and the sponsored-staff cost base correctly before you take on your first client.
