## House positions touched

- HP 1: welfare services from a CQC-registered provider are VAT-exempt; exemption is a cost, not a perk (input VAT unrecoverable). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/welfare-services-and-goods-notice-7012
- HP 5, HP 6: sleep-in NMW (only time awake for work counts, per Mencap) and travel-time NMW (inter-call travel is working time). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/calculating-the-minimum-wage/working-hours-for-which-the-minimum-wage-must-be-paid
- HP 22: new providers must submit a CQC financial viability statement, normally prepared or signed by an accountant. https://www.cqc.org.uk/guidance-regulation/providers/registration/supporting-documents-provider/document/financial-viability-template
- HP 24: NHS-funded nursing care £267.68 standard / £368.24 higher weekly from 1 April 2026 (England). https://www.gov.uk/government/news/better-community-care-thanks-to-nursing-funding-boost
- HP 26: local authority fair-cost-of-care framework as the fee-negotiation tool. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/care-act-statutory-guidance/care-and-support-statutory-guidance

Consistency rules: England default; Scotland/Wales/NI flagged, never silently mixed. VAT exemption framed as a cost, never a tax advantage. Never present pre-2025 VAT-grouping schemes as established planning (HP 2 / RCB 2/2025).
