## Required structure

Open with a BLUF answer box (40 to 60 words, citable): a care home fit-out attracts capital allowances in a set order; claim the Annual Investment Allowance first (up to £1m at 100%, HP 15), then the new 40% first-year allowance on remaining new main-pool plant (HP 16), then the 14% writing-down allowance on the residual pool from April 2026 (HP 16), with structures relieved separately via SBA at 3% (HP 17).

H2 skeleton (each money H2 opens with its own 40 to 60 word citable answer):
1. What qualifies as plant and machinery in a care home: hoists, profiling beds, nurse-call systems, electrical and specialist fit-out are qualifying plant (HP 15). Distinguish plant (allowances pool) from the building structure (SBA) and from non-qualifying land.
2. Step one, the Annual Investment Allowance: £1m at 100% since 1 January 2019 (HP 15); for a new or refurbishing home AIA often absorbs the whole year-one plant spend.
3. Step two, the new 40% first-year allowance (FA 2026): s.29 introduces a 40% FYA on new, unused main-pool plant (HP 16); it applies to additions beyond the AIA cap and only to new, unused kit.
4. Step three, the writing-down allowance now 14%: FA 2026 s.28 cuts the main-rate WDA from 18% to 14% from 1 April 2026 (CT) / 6 April 2026 (IT), with a blended rate for straddling periods (HP 16); special-rate pool stays at 6% (HP 16, and integral features/thermal insulation route here).
5. The correct sequence, and why order matters: AIA, then FYA on residual new main-pool additions, then 14% WDA on the remaining pool (HP 16 states this sequencing explicitly). Worked example below.
6. Structures and Buildings Allowance for the building itself: 3% straight-line on qualifying construction of new builds and extensions from the 2020 rate (HP 17); worked figure, a £4m build gives £120,000 a year (HP 17). Keep SBA distinct from the plant pools.
7. Worked example: a £2m care-home refurbishment split into qualifying plant vs structure, showing AIA £1m absorbed, 40% FYA on the next slice of new main-pool plant, 14% WDA on the residual, 6% on special-rate items, and SBA 3% on the construction element. Every rate labelled to its HP.
8. Where allowances interact with structure and buy/sell: propco/opco affects who claims; corporation tax 19% up to £50k / 25% above £250k with marginal relief (HP 18) sets the value of the deduction. Cross-link /services/buying-a-care-home.

## House positions touched

- HP 15: AIA £1m at 100% since 1 January 2019; care equipment/beds/electrical fit-out qualify. https://www.gov.uk/capital-allowances/annual-investment-allowance
- HP 16: FA 2026 s.28 main WDA 18%→14% (dates, blended straddle); s.29 new 40% FYA on new unused main-pool plant; special rate 6%; claim sequence AIA then FYA then 14% WDA. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/11/section/28 and https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2026/11/section/29
- HP 17: SBA 3% straight-line on qualifying construction (from 2020), 33 and one-third years; £4m build = £120,000/yr. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claiming-capital-allowances-for-structures-and-buildings
- HP 18: CT 19% ≤£50k / 25% ≥£250k + marginal relief; propco/opco associated-company effect. https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates

## Hallucination danger zones

- Rates are locked to HP 16: main WDA is 14% (NOT 18%), special rate 6%, new FYA 40%, from the April 2026 dates. Do not carry forward the old 18% figure except when explaining the change and the straddling blend.
- Do not state a special-rate FYA or extend the 40% FYA to special-rate/second-hand plant; HP 16 confines the 40% FYA to new, unused main-pool plant.
- AIA is £1m (HP 15); do not state a different cap or a temporary super-deduction (expired, not in HP).
- SBA is 3% (HP 17); do not use the pre-2020 2% rate as current.
