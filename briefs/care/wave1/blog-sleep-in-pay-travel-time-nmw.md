## Required structure

BLUF rule: every money/guide H2 opens with a citable 40-60 word answer first.

H2 skeleton:
1. Do you pay minimum wage for a sleep-in shift? (BLUF: after Royal Mencap Society v Tomlinson-Blake [2021] UKSC 8, only time actually awake for the purposes of working counts for NMW; sleeping time where facilities are provided is excluded, but this flips if no sleeping facilities are provided or the worker must stay active) [HP5]
2. The sleep-in edge cases that change the answer (no sleeping facilities provided; worker required to remain active throughout; only brief naps permitted) and how HMRC's salaried-hours treatment works [HP5, NMWM08180]
3. Is travel between care calls paid? (BLUF: inter-call travel is working time and must be paid at or above NMW; commuting from home to the first call and from the last call home is excluded; paying only contact time is the sector's most common NMW breach) [HP6]
4. The NMW averaging trap: how contact-only pay drops an worker below the floor once travel is counted (worked example using the April 2026 NLW of £12.71) [HP6, HP7]
5. The rates that set the floor from 1 April 2026 (BLUF table: NLW £12.71 for 21+, £10.85 for 18 to 20, £8.00 under-18 and apprentice; this is the floor before employer NIC and holiday pay) [HP7]
6. Two hidden add-ons that compound the exposure: mileage reimbursement at AMAP (55p first 10,000 miles from 6 April 2026) and holiday pay for irregular-hours staff (accrual on hours worked; reference-period must include travel payments) [HP8, HP10]
7. The self-employed carer myth (BLUF: rota carers who cannot substitute and are directed on when/where to work are almost always employees or workers; the label does not decide it; back-dated PAYE/NIC/NMW can reach years back) [HP9]
8. How to make your rota NMW-safe (route to /services/care-payroll + the sleep-in NMW calculator) and next-step CTA
