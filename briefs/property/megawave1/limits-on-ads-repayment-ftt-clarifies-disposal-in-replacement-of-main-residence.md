# Limits on ADS Repayment: How the Scottish Tribunal Has Clarified the Disposal-in-Replacement-of-Main-Residence Test under LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 2A

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: **Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013, Schedule 2A, paragraph 8** ("Repayment of additional amount in certain cases"), verified verbatim at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/2A` on 2026-05-26. Para 8(1)(a) requires disposal of a dwelling "within the period of 36 months beginning with or ending with the effective date of the transaction". Para 8(1)(b) requires that the disposed dwelling was the buyer's "only or main residence at any time during the period of 36 months ending with the effective date" of the new purchase. Para 8(2)-(4) sets the procedural mechanics (claim made within 12 months of disposal; reduction calculated as if ADS had never applied).
- Supporting: **LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 2A para 8A** ("Repayment of additional amount: spouses, civil partners and cohabitants"), inserted in 2017, extending the para 8 framework to joint buyers in qualifying relationships where one buyer disposes and the other does not (Stage 2 must verify the exact statutory route into Sch 2A para 8A — likely the Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Relief from Additional Amount) (Scotland) Act 2018 amendments — at write time). **LBTT(S)A 2013 s.65** (Revenue Scotland review and appeal mechanism); **Tax Collection and Management (Scotland) Act 2014 ss.233-243** (FTT (Tax Chamber) Scotland route from review). **Sch 2A para 2** (definition of buyer "owning" a major interest); **Sch 2A para 6** (sole owner versus joint buyer mechanics).
- House position reference: **§23.5** (Scottish ADS mechanics, current 8% rate from 5 Dec 2024, 36-month repayment window post-Coronavirus (Scotland) (No.2) Act 2020 extension, joint-buyer aggregation under Sch 2A para 5(2), 5-year amendment / overpayment limit). **§23.5 F-B7-1 correction** (the joint-buyer aggregation is para 5(2), not para 4, which is the rate provision). **§23.8** (cross-jurisdictional 36-month alignment across SDLT / LTT / LBTT). **§23.11** (do-not-write list: ADS repayment window is 36 months, not the legacy 18-month figure).

The distinguishing angle is **the gap between buyer expectation and FTT interpretation**: where the buyer assumes any sale, gift, or transfer of legal title within 36 months will trigger the refund, the FTT has clarified that the test bites on (a) the actual passing of beneficial ownership rather than legal title alone (e.g. transfers to a connected party at undervalue, transfers to a bare trust the buyer retains, sales to a SPV the buyer controls), (b) the timing of "disposal" (effective date for LBTT-style transactions vs date of contract for other disposals, with the disposal date for repayment-trigger purposes potentially differing from the date treated as disposal for CGT), (c) the requirement that the disposed dwelling was the buyer's only or main residence "at any time during the period of 36 months ending with the effective date" (testing the position at the right end of the window, not a presumption that any sale of a former home qualifies), and (d) the para 8A spouse / civil partner / cohabitant extension when only one joint buyer disposes.
