# Government Ends Per-Room Council Tax on HMO Rooms in England: How SI 2023/1175 Reformed the Council Tax (Chargeable Dwellings) Order 1992, the Single-Dwelling Rule from 1 December 2023, and What HMO Landlords and Tenants Need to Do About Pre-Reform Banding

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the reform): the **Council Tax (Chargeable Dwellings and Liability for Owners) (Amendment) (England) Regulations 2023 (SI 2023/1175)**, in force from **1 December 2023** (reg 1(2)). The instrument amends two 1992 instruments — (a) the **Council Tax (Chargeable Dwellings) Order 1992 (SI 1992/549)** to insert a definition of HMO + new article 3C; (b) the **Council Tax (Liability for Owners) Regulations 1992 (SI 1992/551)** to update the owner-liability hookup for HMOs. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2023/1175/contents/made + https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2023/1175/regulation/1/made + https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2023/1175/regulation/2/made on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (the operative rule — article 3C SI 1992/549 as inserted by reg 2(3) SI 2023/1175): **"An HMO must be treated as a single dwelling"** for council-tax purposes. Coupled with the amendment to art 4(1) "Subject to article 3C..." this overrides the prior VOA practice of disaggregating HMOs into multiple separately-banded "dwellings" where each let-room had some self-contained features (e.g. ensuite + kitchenette). From 1 December 2023, the HMO is a single dwelling regardless of internal layout.
- Primary (definition of HMO — reg 2(2) SI 2023/1175 inserted definition into SI 1992/549): an HMO has the meaning given by **section 254 Housing Act 2004**, with certain subsections excluded (Stage 2 verifies the precise exclusions — typically the s.254(8) self-contained-flat carve-out is the operative exclusion). Under HA 2004 s.254, an HMO is a building (or self-contained flat) occupied by 3 or more persons forming 2 or more households AND sharing one or more basic amenities OR satisfying the building-type test / converted-blocks-of-flats test / s.55 prescribed-description test. Note the council-tax HMO definition is HA 2004 s.254 — the broader test — NOT the narrower s.61 mandatory-licensing 5-person test. **A 3-person 2-household shared-house qualifies as an HMO for council-tax single-dwelling treatment even if it is below the mandatory-licensing threshold.**
- Primary (the liability rule — SI 1992/551 reg 2 amended by reg 3 SI 2023/1175): **the owner**, not the occupiers, is liable for council tax on an HMO. This was the pre-reform position too — HMOs were already in Class C of SI 1992/551 — but SI 2023/1175 tightened the alignment so that the single-dwelling banding under art 3C + owner-liability under SI 1992/551 Class C operate as a coherent regime. Verify at Stage 2.
- Primary (the legacy that was reformed): pre-1 December 2023, the VOA had increasingly applied a per-room banding approach to HMOs where individual rooms had ensuite + cooking facilities + lockable doors, treating each "room" as a separately-banded dwelling under SI 1992/549 art 3 (the disaggregation rule). This produced multiple Band A bills per HMO instead of one combined band; tenants in some cases became liable in their own name; the practice provoked widespread landlord + tenant protest and the 2023 reform package. The reform was a Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities (DLUHC, now Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government — MHCLG) consultation outcome, announced in the Autumn Statement 2023 documents and laid as SI 2023/1175 on 6 November 2023.
- Supporting (background — LGFA 1992 council-tax framework): LGFA 1992 s.3 defines "dwelling" by reference to the General Rate Act 1967 hereditament test. SI 1992/549 (the Chargeable Dwellings Order) makes provision under LGFA 1992 s.3(5) for the Secretary of State to modify the s.3 definition. Art 2 + art 3 of SI 1992/549 deal with the aggregation / disaggregation framework. Art 3C (inserted by SI 2023/1175) overrides the general art 3 disaggregation rule for HMOs.
- Supporting (Scotland + Wales): the reform is **England-only**. Scotland's council-tax HMO position is governed by the Council Tax (Discounts) (Scotland) Order 1992 + the Council Tax (Dwellings) (Scotland) Regulations 1997 framework — different statutory architecture; Stage 2 confirms current Scottish position. Wales has its own Council Tax (Chargeable Dwellings) (Wales) Regulations 1992 + Welsh-Government-specific HMO licensing under Rent Smart Wales. **Page must explicitly note the England-only scope of SI 2023/1175.**
- Supporting (planning-side HMO definition vs council-tax HMO definition): the HA 2004 s.254 test is the COUNCIL-TAX trigger for single-dwelling treatment. The PLANNING-USE-CLASS HMO test under the Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987 (Class C4: small HMOs with 3-6 occupants; sui generis: large HMOs with 7+) is a different statutory test for planning purposes. The HOUSING-LICENSING HMO test under HA 2004 s.55 + SI 2018/221 (mandatory licensing) is yet another test. Three different HMO definitions across three different statutory regimes — competitor pages routinely confuse them. The council-tax test is HA 2004 s.254.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What did the government do about council tax on HMO rooms? (Operative answer: SI 2023/1175 in force from 1 December 2023 inserted art 3C into SI 1992/549, requiring HMOs to be treated as a single dwelling for council tax. The VOA practice of per-room banding ended at that date for new bandings; pre-existing per-room bandings can be reviewed retrospectively under the SI 1992/549 art 6 process.)
2. When did the change come into force? (1 December 2023, by reg 1(2) of SI 2023/1175. The SI was laid on 6 November 2023 and applies prospectively from 1 December 2023; the pre-reform per-room bandings remain in the valuation list until reviewed but are reviewable.)
3. What is an HMO for council-tax purposes? (HA 2004 s.254 definition: building or self-contained flat occupied by 3+ persons forming 2+ households AND sharing one or more basic amenities. Note this is the BROADER s.254 test, NOT the narrower s.61 mandatory-licensing 5-person test and NOT the planning C4 use-class test. A 3-person 2-household shared house with shared kitchen / bathroom qualifies as an HMO for council-tax purposes.)
4. What does the new "single dwelling" rule mean? (Art 3C SI 1992/549: the HMO building is a single dwelling for council tax — the VOA bands the building as a whole, one band, one bill. The owner is liable under SI 1992/551 reg 2 Class C — not the individual occupiers. Tenants do not have separate council-tax bills attaching to their rooms.)
5. Who is liable to pay the council tax on an HMO? (SI 1992/551 reg 2 Class C — the owner. Pre-reform, where rooms were per-banded, individual tenants were liable in some cases. Post-reform, the owner pays the single-dwelling bill and typically recovers via inclusive-rent or service-charge pass-through.)
6. If my HMO was per-room-banded before 1 December 2023, can I get the banding reviewed? (Yes. Apply to the VOA under the SI 1992/549 art 6 process challenging inclusion in the valuation list. The VOA is processing a backlog of these reviews. Stage 2 confirms the current VOA practice-note on retroactive review applications and any deadline for application.)
7. Does the SI 2023/1175 reform apply to Scotland and Wales? (No — England-only. Scotland operates under the Council Tax (Discounts) (Scotland) Order 1992 + the Council Tax (Dwellings) (Scotland) Regulations 1997 framework; Wales operates under Welsh-Government regulations. Stage 2 confirms current devolved-administration positions on HMO council-tax aggregation.)
8. Does the reform apply to converted flats (multiple self-contained units within one building)? (Generally NO. Converted flats with self-contained kitchens + bathrooms within each unit are typically banded as separate dwellings under the standard SI 1992/549 art 3 disaggregation rule. The HA 2004 s.254 HMO test typically excludes self-contained flats under s.254(8) — the building of self-contained flats does NOT qualify as a single HMO if each unit has its own kitchen + bathroom. The line between an "HMO with high-spec ensuite rooms" and "a building of self-contained flats" is the critical test post-reform. Stage 2 confirms current VOA guidance on the line.)
9. How does the reform interact with HMO mandatory licensing under HA 2004 s.61? (The s.254 HMO definition for council tax is broader than the s.61 mandatory-licensing definition (which requires 5+ persons in 2+ households). A 3-4 person small HMO is within the s.254 council-tax single-dwelling rule but may NOT be within s.61 mandatory licensing. Licensing and council-tax classification are separate questions with overlapping but non-identical definitions.)
10. What should an HMO landlord do practically? (Three steps: (i) confirm the property is within HA 2004 s.254 (3+ persons, 2+ households, shared amenity) — if yes, the single-dwelling rule applies post-1-December-2023; (ii) if pre-1-December-2023 the property was per-room-banded, apply to the VOA for retrospective review of the list entry; (iii) update tenancy / inclusive-rent pricing to reflect that the single-dwelling council tax goes to the landlord and is recovered via rent or service-charge — the per-tenant separate-bill architecture is no longer the operative position. Cross-link to existing HMO landlord-accounting pages for the rent / service-charge mechanics.)
11. Does the reform change the council-tax discount position for HMOs? (Not directly — but the single-dwelling rule reverts HMO council tax to the standard regime, so the standard discount rules apply (e.g. all-occupants-students exemption under SI 1992/558 Class N; severely-mentally-impaired discount; student-occupied HMOs may now qualify for the full Class N exemption where all occupants are full-time students — verify under SI 1992/558.)
12. Could the per-room banding regime return? (Possible but politically unlikely — the reform was a deliberate response to widespread protest from landlords + tenants over VOA practice. The SI 1992/549 framework empowers the Secretary of State to amend the disaggregation rules at any time, so a future government could revisit. As of the page write date, the single-dwelling rule under art 3C is the operative law and there is no announced reversal.)
