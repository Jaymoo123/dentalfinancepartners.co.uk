# A Complete Guide to Stamp Duty Refunds: Every Route, the Right Deadline, and How to Avoid SDLT-Claims-Firm Traps

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (procedural): FA 2003 s.80 + Schedule 11A (Land Transaction Return amendment and assessment). Sch 11A para 6 — 12-month amendment window from the filing date (the standard in-time correction route). Stage 2 verify verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/11A on write.
- Primary (out-of-window relief): FA 2003 Schedule 10 paragraph 34 (Overpayment relief) — 4-year window from the end of the relevant tax period (note: SDLT tax periods are per-transaction not per-year, so the operative trigger is the effective date of the transaction). Verify Sch 10 para 34 verbatim — Stage 2 must confirm paragraph number and exact 4-year text at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/10 on write. **Stage 1b drift watchpoint: HMRC SDLT overpayment relief sometimes referenced as Sch 10 para 34A or similar — verify the operative paragraph at write; do not guess.**
- Substantive refund-route anchors (cross-link to dedicated pages for mechanics):
  - FA 2003 Schedule 4ZA para 3(7) — replacement-of-main-residence 3-year refund (5% additional dwellings surcharge route — primary cross-link to A4 sister-pick and Wave 9 dedicated page)
  - FA 2003 Schedule 9A para 11A (non-UK resident surcharge — 1-year UK-residence requalification refund — verify paragraph number at write)
  - FA 2003 Schedule 6ZA (first-time buyer relief — Stage 2 verify schedule reference; historically first-time buyer relief was in Sch 6ZA inserted by F(No.2)A 2017 s.16 — confirm at write)
  - FA 2003 s.116(1)(a) (residential property definition — load-bearing for Bewley uninhabitable refund route via overpayment relief)
  - FA 2003 s.116(7) (6+-dwellings statutory deeming — refund route for portfolio buyers wrongly assessed on residential basis)
  - FA 2003 Schedule 6B (MDR historical — refund route for transitional-cohort claims; cross-link to A3)
- Penalty exposure: FA 2007 Schedule 24 (incorrect refund claims — claimant-side and adviser-side liability; particularly relevant given the SDLT-claims-firm landscape).
- House position reference: §1 main text (SDLT rates + surcharges + abolished MDR + surviving alternatives — the rate-architecture context); §1.C Wave 7 lock (Bewley uninhabitable refund route narrow post-Hyman / Mudan / MHB / Brown); §1.D Wave 7 lock (Sch 3 para 3 spouse/civil-partner — relevant for Sch 4ZA aggregation refund scenarios); §1.H Wave 9 lock (MDR abolition transitional refund context). NEW HP-lock candidate: §1.N "SDLT refund-route taxonomy" — Stage 1b consideration.

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **whole-landscape umbrella** for SDLT refunds. The site already carries (a) `sdlt-5-percent-surcharge-refund-claim-process` (Wave 9 — dedicated 3-year replacement route mechanics); (b) `sdlt-additional-property-surcharge-refund-routes-3-year-replacement-claim` (earlier wave — refund routes within Sch 4ZA); and the MegaWave 1 A4 sister-pick (`a-complete-guide-to-5-sdlt-surcharge-refund-claims`) covers the **six routes within the additional-dwellings-surcharge family**. This A5 page sits at a **higher level of abstraction**: it covers **every SDLT refund family**, not just the surcharge refunds, and provides the **route-classification taxonomy** that helps a reader identify which family their situation belongs to before they drill into a dedicated page.

The taxonomy / route families:
1. **Additional dwellings surcharge refunds** (Sch 4ZA) — replacement of main residence, dwelling-count, mixed-use, 6+-dwellings, rate-transition. Cross-link to A4 + Wave 9 pages.
2. **Non-UK resident surcharge refunds** (Sch 9A) — 1-year UK-residence requalification under para 11A. Largely a separate population to additional-dwellings surcharge refunds.
3. **First-time buyer relief retrospective claims** (Sch 6ZA) — buyer eligible at effective date but did not claim on the original return; 12-month amendment or 4-year overpayment relief depending on timing.
4. **MDR transitional retrospective claims** (Sch 6B — pre-1-June-2024 effective dates only) — substantial-performance evidence pack; 12-month amendment dominant.
5. **Bewley uninhabitable refunds** (s.116(1)(a) + Sch 10 para 34) — residential mis-classification; narrow post-Hyman / Mudan / MHB / Brown.
6. **Mixed-use re-characterisation** (s.116(1)(a) + Sch 10 para 34) — narrow post-Hyman.
7. **Calculation-error amendments** (rate-band misapplications, surcharge-applicability errors, linked-transaction aggregation errors).
8. **Lease premium / chargeable consideration errors** (Sch 4 — chargeable consideration definition; Sch 5 — leases; valuation disputes).

This page provides the **family-level decision tree**, the **deadline table** (12-month amendment vs 4-year overpayment relief vs route-specific windows), and the **SDLT-claims-firm risk warning** as a load-bearing differentiator from competitor coverage. The claims-firm warning is critical: the post-Stamp Duty Holiday surge in cold-call refund-claims firms has produced a wave of HMRC enquiries against speculative claims, with claimant-side penalty exposure under FA 2007 Sch 24 even where the claims firm filed on the buyer's behalf. The page should surface the typical red flags (no-win-no-fee with 30%+ contingency; cold approach naming a specific property; promise of refund without diligence on facts; refund routes asserted without statutory anchors).

Distinguish from A4 by **breadth** (every refund family, not just surcharge refunds), from existing dedicated pages by **decision-tree-first orientation** (taxonomy first, drill-down second), and from typical SDLT-claims-firm content by **honest expected-success-rate framing** (most retrofit refund claims fail; high-success routes are narrow and procedurally specific). Target length 3,000-3,600 words to fairly cover eight families plus the deadline table plus the claims-firm warning.
