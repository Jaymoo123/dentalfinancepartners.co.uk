# "Free Rental Valuation" — What It Actually Is, When It Is Tax-Defensible, and the Eight Use-Cases Where a RICS Red Book Valuation Is Required Instead

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (rental valuation as the standard professional product): **RICS Valuation — Global Standards (the "Red Book") + UK National Supplement** — the RICS-issued professional valuation standards binding on chartered surveyors. The Red Book sets the methodology for "Market Rent" valuations and the reporting framework (RICS VPS 1-6 + VPGAs). Estate-agent "free rental valuations" are NOT Red Book valuations — they are instructional sales appraisals issued for marketing purposes, not regulated as valuation work product. Cross-reference at RICS website (rics.org/uk/upholding-professional-standards/sector-standards/valuation/red-book/); Stage 2 sources the 2024 Red Book consolidated text + UK National Supplement chapters.
- Primary (rental income — tax base): **ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (Property income — ss.260-364)** — UK property business income is taxed on the cash + accruals basis depending on entity type; the valuation question relevant for income tax is the "market rent" comparable when the actual rent is below market (e.g. let to family member at below-market rent: ITTOIA 2005 s.272 + the "wholly and exclusively" test under s.34 applies to the deduction side, NOT the income side; below-market rents are taxed at the actual amount received with no deemed-market-rent uplift on the income side, with the differential treated as a gift / loss-of-allowable-expenses depending on facts). Stage 2 verifies the precise current treatment + relevant HMRC PIM (Property Income Manual) sections.
- Primary (SDLT chargeable consideration where rent is the consideration): **FA 2003 Sch 17A — Leases.** The chargeable consideration for SDLT on a lease grant includes (a) any "premium" (capital sum) AND (b) the rent payable over the lease term (the Net Present Value (NPV) calculation under Sch 17A para 3 + Annex). Rent at undervalue may be challenged if HMRC argues the consideration includes deemed market rent. Stage 2 verifies current FA 2003 Sch 17A text.
- Primary (LBTT chargeable consideration where rent is the consideration): **LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 19** — equivalent rent-NPV chargeable consideration framework for Scottish LBTT on lease grants.
- Primary (Section 24 finance-cost-restriction): **ITTOIA 2005 s.272A-272B** (inserted by FA 2015) — the 20% basic-rate-tax-credit restriction on residential property finance costs. Modelling Section 24 impact requires rental income projection — accurate rental valuations are foundational to the modelling. Cross-reference HP §4.
- Primary (MEES rent-cap): **The Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) (England and Wales) Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/962)** — prohibits letting a sub-EPC-E residential property without an exemption; the rent-cap exemption applies where the cost of improvement exceeds the £3,500 "Capped Threshold" — and the "Capped Threshold" calculation may turn on rental valuation evidence. Stage 2 verifies current SI 2015/962 text + any post-2025 amendments to the MEES regime (proposed but not yet enacted: EPC-C from 2030 — explicitly NOT in force per HP §26.3 do-not-write).
- Primary (rent-review under landlord-tenant law): **Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 Part II (ss.23-46)** + the open-market rent review machinery embedded in commercial leases. A rent review at a periodic break or 5-yearly review point requires a Red Book valuation of "market rent"; the free estate-agent appraisal is NOT acceptable evidence.
- Primary (IHT — gift / PET valuation): **IHTA 1984 s.4 (charge on death)** + **s.3A (PETs)** + **s.49 (interest in possession)** — IHT valuations are at "open market value" at the relevant date; for rental property gifts where the value of the property is a function of capitalised rental income, a Red Book valuation (or equivalent HMRC-DV-acceptable valuation) is required. HMRC's District Valuer (DV) may challenge.
- Primary (CGT — base cost / market value on connected-person transactions): **TCGA 1992 s.17** — disposals not at arm's length are deemed at market value. Where rental property is gifted / transferred to a connected person, the deemed market value drives the CGT base cost + chargeable gain. Red Book valuation evidence supports the position; an estate-agent free appraisal does NOT. Cross-reference HP §5.
- Primary (ATED — valuation date rules): **Finance Act 2013 Part 3 (ss.94-174) + Sch 33** — ATED applies to companies holding UK residential property > £500k; the chargeable amount is band-based and the band is determined by the property's value at the relevant valuation date (5-yearly revaluations). Stage 2 sources current FA 2013 Sch 33 + current ATED valuation date guidance.
- Supporting (lender refinancing + insurance reinstatement): not statutory but commercially relevant — most lenders require a Red Book valuation for refinancing; insurance reinstatement values typically use a separate methodology (sum-insured + buildings-cost-information rebuild) not Market Rent.
- House position reference: cross-reference HP §1 (SDLT framework) + HP §4 (Section 24) + HP §5 (CGT) + HP §18 (ATED). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED for this page; the page is a primer that references existing HP locks across the §1 / §4 / §5 / §18 cluster.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is a "free rental valuation" and what is it actually for? (An estate-agent / letting-agent marketing appraisal of expected rental income — typically delivered as a verbal estimate or short letter. It is offered free as a customer-acquisition tool (the agent hopes to win the instruction to find a tenant). It is useful for benchmarking but is NOT a defensible valuation for tax, lender, court, tribunal, or HMRC purposes. Industry standard distinguishes "marketing appraisal" (free) from "RICS Red Book valuation" (chargeable, professional, regulated).)
2. What is the RICS Red Book valuation? (The RICS Valuation — Global Standards + UK National Supplement — the professional standards binding on chartered surveyors. Defines methodology (VPS 1-6), reporting framework (signed valuation report), and quality bar (MRICS / FRICS qualified surveyor signing off). Typical residential rental Red Book valuation cost: £450-1,200. Commercial rental Red Book valuation: £1,500-5,000+. The Red Book valuation is the standard accepted by HMRC, lenders, courts, tribunals, and the District Valuer.)
3. When do I need a Red Book rental valuation instead of a free appraisal? (Eight principal use-cases: (1) SDLT / LBTT on a lease grant where rent is chargeable consideration; (2) rent review under a commercial lease (typically 5-yearly review or break points); (3) MEES Capped Threshold exemption application; (4) lender refinancing or remortgage application; (5) connected-person property transfer for CGT base-cost determination; (6) IHT / PET valuation for gift planning; (7) ATED 5-yearly revaluation; (8) court / tribunal evidence in a landlord-tenant dispute. The free appraisal is fine for marketing-the-property; it is NOT fine for any of these eight use-cases.)
4. How does the SDLT / LBTT rent-as-chargeable-consideration mechanic work? (FA 2003 Sch 17A para 3 + Annex (England & NI SDLT) and LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 19 (Scotland) — the chargeable consideration for a lease grant includes the rent payable over the lease term computed as Net Present Value (NPV) using HMRC's prescribed discount rate (currently 3.5%). For a 25-year commercial lease at £100k pa, NPV ≈ £1.6m (rounded). SDLT / LBTT applies to the NPV plus any premium. An undervalue rent does not avoid SDLT / LBTT but HMRC / Revenue Scotland may challenge if the rent is materially below market and the lease is between connected parties.)
5. What about a below-market rent to a family member — does it trigger a market-rent tax charge? (No. ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 taxes actual rental income received, not market rent. Below-market letting to a family member is not "deemed at market rent" for income tax. But: (a) "wholly and exclusively" test under ITTOIA 2005 s.34 may restrict allowable expense deduction where the letting is non-commercial — typical HMRC practice: deductible expenses are restricted to the actual rental income (no loss); (b) the gift element (below-market differential) is a PET for IHT purposes; (c) CGT base cost is unaffected (no disposal event in a continuing letting). The page sets out this analysis in detail.)
6. How does the MEES rent-cap exemption work? (SI 2015/962 + the "Capped Threshold" exemption: a landlord can register an exemption from the MEES standard if the cost of improving the property to EPC E exceeds £3,500. The £3,500 cap relates to the COST of works, not rental income. A separate "high-cost exemption" may rely on a 7-year payback test for energy-efficiency improvements, where rental valuation evidence may be relevant. Stage 2 confirms current MEES exemption mechanic — proposed EPC-C-from-2030 reform is NOT in force per HP §26.3 do-not-write.)
7. How do I obtain a Red Book valuation? (Engage an MRICS / FRICS qualified chartered surveyor (search via rics.org "Find a Surveyor"). Specify the purpose — "market rent valuation for [SDLT chargeable consideration / connected-person transfer / lender refinance / IHT gift] under RICS Red Book Global Standards UK National Supplement." Cost: typically £450-1,200 for residential; £1,500-5,000+ for commercial / mixed-use. Turnaround: 5-15 working days. The signed valuation report is the deliverable.)
8. Can my accountant provide a rental valuation? (No. Accountants are not regulated to provide RICS Red Book valuations. The firm's role is upstream + downstream of the valuation: upstream — identifying when a valuation is required for tax planning; downstream — incorporating the valuation into the tax position. The valuation itself is provided by a chartered surveyor.)
9. What about online "automated valuation models" (AVMs) — are they Red Book? (No. AVMs (Zoopla / Rightmove / OnTheMarket estimates) are statistical models, not RICS Red Book valuations. They are useful for benchmarking but not defensible for tax, lender, or court purposes. HMRC's District Valuer will dismiss an AVM number in any meaningful challenge.)
10. What if the letting agent's free appraisal differs materially from the Red Book valuation? (The Red Book valuation prevails for tax + compliance purposes. The free appraisal may be commercially optimistic (the agent hopes to win the instruction) or commercially cautious (the agent hopes to set realistic tenant expectations). The Red Book valuation is signed by a chartered surveyor under professional indemnity insurance and follows the prescribed methodology.)
