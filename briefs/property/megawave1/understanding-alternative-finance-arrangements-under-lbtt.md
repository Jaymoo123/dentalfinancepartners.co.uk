# Alternative Finance Arrangements under LBTT: Schedule 7 Relief for Sharia-Compliant and Other Islamic-Finance Property Purchases in Scotland

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: **Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013, Schedule 7 ("Alternative property finance relief")**, verified verbatim at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/7` on 2026-05-26. The schedule covers three head categories of alternative finance arrangement, all designed to eliminate the double LBTT charge that would otherwise arise where a financial institution acts as intermediary on Sharia-compliant home purchase plans:
  - **Sch 7 Part 1 (paras 2-6) — "Land sold to financial institution and leased to person":** the Ijara / lease-back structure. The institution purchases the land, grants a lease (or sub-lease) to the person, and the arrangement allows the person to require transfer of the property at the end of the lease term. Relief applies to the second transaction (the transfer back to the person).
  - **Sch 7 Part 2 (paras 7-12) — "Land sold to financial institution and person in common":** the Diminishing Musharaka / co-ownership structure. The institution and person jointly purchase the property as common owners, with arrangements giving the person exclusive occupation and providing for the person to acquire the institution's share progressively. Relief applies to the periodic share-buyouts.
  - **Sch 7 Part 3 (paras 13-15) — "Land sold to financial institution and re-sold to person":** the Murabaha / sale-and-immediate-resale structure. The institution purchases the land and immediately resells it to the person, who then grants a standard security (the Scots law equivalent of a mortgage) back to the institution. Relief applies to the first transaction (the institution's acquisition) so that LBTT is charged only on the resale to the person.
  - **Sch 7 Part 4 — exempt-interest treatment for the financial institution** (where it holds the property between the buyer's effective acquisition and the formal transfer). **Sch 7 Part 5 — interpretation** (definition of "financial institution", which mirrors the SDLT definition at FA 2003 s.73BA and includes specifically authorised institutions under FSMA 2000 and equivalent regulatory regimes).
- Supporting: **LBTT(S)A 2013 s.27** (reliefs — general); **LBTT(S)A 2013 s.30(1)** (claim must be made in the LBTT return per Revenue Scotland procedural rules). **TCMA 2014 ss.83-86** (amendment and review mechanics if relief was omitted from the original return). **LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 2A para 8 and para 8A** (ADS — and the interaction question with alternative finance: does ADS bite on the institution's interim acquisition, the buyer's onward acquisition, or both — Stage 2 must verify the specific Sch 2A treatment).
- Cross-jurisdictional comparators (cited but distinguished, not relied on as Scottish authority): **FA 2003 ss.71A-73** (SDLT alternative property finance — the parallel SDLT framework, with three matching head categories. The SDLT framework was introduced in 2003 with subsequent extensions to cover all major Islamic-finance home-purchase-plan structures). **LTTA 2017 Sch 10** (Welsh LTT alternative property finance relief — equivalent Welsh framework with the same three head categories). The Scottish, English-and-NI, and Welsh frameworks are deliberately aligned to avoid jurisdictional arbitrage on Sharia-compliant home finance.
- House position reference: **§23.4-23.6** (Scottish LBTT framework — main residential rates, ADS at 8% from 5 Dec 2024, six-dwellings non-residential treatment under s.59(8), MDR under Sch 5). **§23.5 do-not-write** (ADS at 8% on entire price, not 6%). **§23.10** Sch 7 is now confirmed as a new citation to add — Stage 1b conductor may consider extending the §23.10 citations list to surface Sch 7 alongside the existing Sch 2A / Sch 5 / Sch 10 / Sch 13 / Sch 17 entries. Cross-references: §1 SDLT (FA 2003 ss.71A-73 parallel framework for England + NI); §23.10 cross-jurisdictional alignment.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is alternative property finance and why does it need a special LBTT relief? (Sharia-compliant home purchase plans avoid the payment of interest — riba — by using ownership-based structures: the institution acquires the property and transfers it to the buyer over time. Without a relief, each transfer (institution-acquires-from-vendor, buyer-acquires-from-institution) would attract LBTT, producing a double charge. Sch 7 eliminates the double charge while leaving the buyer paying LBTT on the substantive acquisition.)
2. What is Ijara (Sch 7 Part 1, paras 2-6) and how does Sch 7 relief apply? (Institution buys; institution leases to person with right to acquire; relief applies to the buyer's eventual transfer-out so that only one LBTT charge arises — on the institution's initial purchase. The buyer's lease and option-to-acquire is not a charge to LBTT under the relief.)
3. What is Diminishing Musharaka (Sch 7 Part 2, paras 7-12) and how does Sch 7 relief apply? (Institution and person jointly acquire as common owners; person buys out the institution's share progressively over the financing term; each share-buyout would in principle be an acquisition by the buyer subject to LBTT, but Sch 7 relieves the periodic share-buyouts so that LBTT is charged only on the buyer's substantive co-ownership share at the outset.)
4. What is Murabaha (Sch 7 Part 3, paras 13-15) and how does Sch 7 relief apply? (Institution buys from vendor; immediately resells to buyer at a marked-up price; buyer grants a standard security to the institution. Sch 7 relief applies to the institution's first acquisition — exempt as long as the resale to the buyer happens immediately — so LBTT is charged only on the buyer's acquisition from the institution.)
5. What qualifies as a "financial institution" for Sch 7 purposes? (Sch 7 Part 5 — institutions authorised under FSMA 2000 to accept deposits, building societies, certain other prescribed institutions; broadly aligned with the SDLT definition at FA 2003 s.73BA. A private individual cannot fill the institution role; private "alternative finance" arrangements between family members do not qualify.)
6. How does Sch 7 interact with ADS (Sch 2A)? (The institution's interim acquisition: the institution is not buying for use as a residential dwelling, so on a strict reading ADS should not engage on the interim step. But Stage 2 must verify the specific Sch 2A treatment of alternative finance institutions and confirm Revenue Scotland's published position. For the buyer's substantive acquisition: ADS engages on the buyer's onward acquisition exactly as it would on a conventional purchase — if the buyer (or a joint buyer) already owns a dwelling at the effective date of the buyer's acquisition, ADS at 8% applies.)
7. How does Sch 7 relief interact with the joint-buyer rules where the buyer is married or in a civil partnership? (Diminishing Musharaka structures often involve the institution and one named buyer; the spouse / civil partner may or may not be a party to the arrangement. The page must walk the LBTT return treatment where the spouse is not a party but is occupying as principal home, where the spouse is a joint party, and where the spouse owns another dwelling separately.)
8. What are the procedural mechanics for claiming Sch 7 relief in the LBTT return? (Claim made in the original return at the effective date per Revenue Scotland procedure; amendment route under TCMA 2014 s.83 within 12 months if relief was omitted; overpayment-claim route under longer-stop limits if amendment window has closed.)
9. What evidence does Revenue Scotland expect to support a Sch 7 relief claim? (The alternative finance arrangement documents (Ijara lease, Musharaka co-ownership deed, Murabaha sale-and-resale contracts); the FSMA 2000 authorisation reference for the institution; the timeline showing the institution's acquisition and the buyer's acquisition were part of a single qualifying arrangement.)
10. Where can a buyer or institution find the equivalent reliefs in other UK jurisdictions if the property portfolio spans Scotland, England, and Wales? (FA 2003 ss.71A-73 for SDLT — England + NI; LTTA 2017 Sch 10 for Welsh LTT. The three frameworks are deliberately aligned to avoid arbitrage; cross-border portfolios using a single financing arrangement need to file in each jurisdiction the property sits.)
