# Abolition of Furnished Holiday Lettings: What Individual Owners Need to Know

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Finance Act 2025 Schedule 5 ("Furnished holiday lettings"), verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/8/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26. Five Parts. Part 1 (income tax) and Part 4 (chargeable gains) carry the individual-owner load. Part 5 paragraph 12 sets commencement: tax year 2025-26 for income tax (effective 6 April 2025 for individuals); CGT amendments commence for disposals on or after 6 April 2025.
- Supporting: ITTOIA 2005 ss.322-328A (the now-omitted FHL definition + qualifying-day mechanics); TCGA 1992 s.241 (the now-omitted "FHL trade-treated-as-trade" CGT lever underpinning historic rollover and BADR availability); ITA 2007 s.127 (sideways trading-loss relief — historically available to FHL operators on certain losses, withdrawn at abolition); FA 2004 s.189 ("relevant UK earnings" for pension contributions — FHL profit historically counted, ceases to count from 2025/26); ITTOIA 2005 s.311A (replacement of domestic items relief — the post-abolition relief route for new furniture spend, on a revenue basis); ITA 2007 s.272A (Section 24 finance cost restriction — now bites former-FHL mortgage interest in full).
- House position reference: §6 "FHL — abolition transition" (locked mechanics including the 6 April 2025 commencement, Section 24 ingress, BADR cut-off, joint-ownership 50/50 default + Form 17 requirement); §1 mainline (5% additional dwellings surcharge for SDLT on FHL acquisitions from 31 October 2024); §25.7 "FHL transitional under FA 2025 Sch 5" (grandfathered capital allowance pool mechanics — cross-referenced because individual owners with material pre-2025 pools need to know they continue writing down).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. The FHL regime ended on 6 April 2025. As an individual owner, what is the single most important thing I need to change on my 2025/26 SA100? (Stop deducting mortgage interest as an expense; the Section 24 tax-credit mechanic replaces it from this tax year.)
2. I held my FHL jointly with my spouse and split the income unequally to manage tax bands. Can I still do that? (No — without a Form 17 election the 50/50 default applies from 6 April 2025; Form 17 must be filed if you want a beneficial-ownership-aligned split. Practical sequencing of declaration-of-trust drafting plus Form 17 filing within 60 days.)
3. I have brought-forward FHL losses. Are they lost? (No — they survive but as property-business losses going forward, ring-fenced to the UK or overseas property business as appropriate. Loss of sideways relief against general income.)
4. I have a capital allowances pool from pre-2025 FHL plant spend. What happens to it? (Grandfathered under FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 5 para 18; continues writing down at 18% main pool / 6% special-rate pool; carve-out from s.35 dwelling-house restriction preserved. Cross-link the operational page.)
5. I was using FHL profit to pay relief-eligible personal pension contributions. Does that still work? (No from 2025/26 — FHL profit no longer counts as "relevant UK earnings" under FA 2004 s.189. Practical impact: the £3,600 floor still applies regardless of earnings, anything above requires other relevant earnings. Cross-link to B14.)
6. I am planning to sell my former FHL in 2026/27. What CGT rate applies? (Standard residential CGT: 18%/24%, no BADR. Anti-forestalling for between-announcement-and-abolition disposals already crystallised — not relevant for post-April-2025 disposals.)
7. I am thinking about putting my former FHL into a limited company. Is that more attractive post-abolition? (Maybe — Section 24 doesn't bite companies, but s.162 incorporation relief, SDLT-on-incorporation, and ATED need to be modelled. Cross-link to `transferring-fhl-portfolio-to-limited-company`.)
8. Do I need to register for VAT now that the FHL regime is gone? (Unchanged — holiday-accommodation supplies have always been standard-rated under VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 1 Item 1(a) carve-out; the £90k turnover threshold continues to apply. Cross-link to B16.)
9. Does abolition affect my SDLT position when I buy another holiday let? (Yes operationally — the 5% additional dwellings surcharge from 31 October 2024 catches FHL acquisitions; FHL purchasers no longer have any SDLT carve-out post-abolition. Cross-link to `sdlt-furnished-holiday-let-2025-abolition`.)
10. What is the timing of the upcoming property-income surcharge and how does it interact with my former FHL? (2% surcharge from 6 April 2027 announced in Autumn Budget 2024, draft FA 2026 — produces 22%/42%/47% effective rates on UK property income including former-FHL income; plan against the scheduled-but-not-yet-enacted dates.)
