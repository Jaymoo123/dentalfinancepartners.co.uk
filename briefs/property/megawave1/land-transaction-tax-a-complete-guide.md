# Land Transaction Tax: A Complete Guide to Welsh LTT for Buyers, Investors, and Their Advisers

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Land Transaction Tax and Anti-avoidance of Devolved Taxes (Wales) Act 2017 anaw 1 ("LTTA 2017"), s.2 "Land transaction tax" - verified verbatim "Land transaction tax is charged on chargeable transactions relating to land in Wales" at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/anaw/2017/1/contents on 2026-05-26. LTTA 2017 is structured in 8 Parts: Part 1 Overview (s.1); Part 2 The Tax and Key Concepts (ss.2-23); Part 3 Calculation of Tax and Reliefs (ss.24-31); Part 4 Leases (s.32); Part 5 Application to Certain Persons and Bodies (ss.33-43); Part 6 Returns and Payments (ss.44-65); Part 7 General Anti-avoidance Rule (s.66); Part 8 Interpretation and Final Provisions (ss.67-82).
- Supporting: LTTA 2017 s.10 (effective date - substantial-performance test parallel to FA 2003 s.44 and LBTT(S)A 2013 s.10); s.24 (residential rate-setting power); s.25 (non-residential rate-setting power); ss.30-31 (reliefs framework); Sch 5 (higher residential rates - per §23.2 and the M1-B-B2 sibling brief); Sch 7 (partnership relief - mirrors FA 2003 Sch 15); Sch 16 (group relief - mirrors FA 2003 Sch 7); Sch 19 (charities relief); Sch 22 (cross-border / cross-jurisdictional apportionment for transactions involving land in two jurisdictions). LTTA 2017 returns provisions in Part 6 - the 30-day return window for LTT is materially different from SDLT's 14-day window (FA 2003 s.76) and aligns with Scottish LBTT (LBTT(S)A 2013 s.29).
- House position reference: §23.1 (main residential rates 2026/27 unchanged from 10 October 2022: 0% to £225k, 6% to £400k, 7.5% to £750k, 10% to £1.5m, 12% above - the nil-rate band is £225k, materially higher than SDLT's £125k and LBTT's £145k); §23.2 (higher residential rates 2026/27, post-11-December-2024 uplift, 5% to £180k stepping to 17% above £1.5m); §23.3 (Welsh MDR retained-but-modified: 7 February 2025 subsidiary-dwelling carve-out, 13 February 2026 minimum-rate 3% floor; non-residential bands 0%/1%/5%/6%; no Welsh equivalent to FA 2003 s.116(7) six-dwellings rule); §23.8 (cross-jurisdictional comparison table - the four-nation contrast); §23.9 (Property Tax Partners angles for cross-border portfolios and main-residence-replacement timing); §23.10 (citation block: gov.wales source pages, LTTA 2017 statutory framework); §23.11 (do-not-write list: no Welsh non-resident surcharge, no Welsh FTB regime, MDR did not abolish across the UK on 1 June 2024).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is Land Transaction Tax and when did it start? (LTT is the Welsh property-transfer tax that replaced SDLT in Wales on 1 April 2018 under the Wales Act 2014 devolution. Administered by the Welsh Revenue Authority. Statutory basis: LTTA 2017. Operates in parallel to SDLT (England and NI) and LBTT (Scotland) - the four-nation property-transfer-tax landscape.)
2. What are the LTT main residential rates for 2026/27? (Table: 0% to £225k, 6% to £400k, 7.5% to £750k, 10% to £1.5m, 12% above. Unchanged since 10 October 2022. Apply where the buyer (and any joint buyer) does not own another dwelling at the effective date and not eligible for higher rates.)
3. What are the higher residential rates and when do they apply? (Higher rates apply where the buyer or any joint buyer owns another dwelling anywhere in the world at the effective date and that other dwelling has a market value of £40,000 or more. Current bands (post-11-December-2024 uplift): 5% / 8.5% / 10% / 12.5% / 15% / 17%. NOTE: this is a separate band structure, NOT a flat surcharge added on top of main rates. Cross-link to the higher-rates page.)
4. Is MDR still available in Wales? (YES, with modifications. Welsh MDR was modified by the Land Transaction Tax (Modification of Multiple Dwellings Relief) (Wales) Regulations 2025 (effective 7 February 2025) to exclude subsidiary-dwelling acquisitions by individual main-residence buyers, and by the Land Transaction Tax (Modification of Relief for Acquisitions Involving Multiple Dwellings) (Wales) Regulations 2026 (effective 13 February 2026) to raise the minimum rate from 1% to 3%. Cross-link to the MDR-survives page.)
5. What are LTT non-residential rates? (Bands: 0% to £225k, 1% to £250k, 5% to £1m, 6% above. Apply to commercial purchases, mixed-use purchases, and certain edge cases where residential property is treated as non-residential. Wales has NO equivalent to FA 2003 s.116(7) six-dwellings automatic non-residential rule - critical trap for portfolio buyers.)
6. Does Wales have a non-resident surcharge? (NO. Unlike England (FA 2003 Sch 9A - 2% surcharge from 1 April 2021), Wales has not introduced a non-resident surcharge. Non-UK-resident buyers in Wales pay standard or higher rates only, with no additional 2%.)
7. Does Wales have first-time buyer relief? (NO. The £225,000 nil-rate band already covers most starter purchases, so Wales has not introduced a separate FTB regime. Contrast England (£300,000 FTB nil band, withdrawn above £500,000) and Scotland (£175,000 FTB nil-band uplift, no upper cap, £600 maximum saving).)
8. What reliefs are available? (Headline reliefs: group relief (Sch 16); partnership relief (Sch 7); charities relief (Sch 19); sub-sale relief (Sch 2 Part 4); derelict-property refund (cross-link to derelict-property page). MDR (Sch 5 / regulations) covered above.)
9. When are returns due? (LTT return due to the Welsh Revenue Authority within 30 days of the effective date - materially longer than SDLT's 14-day window (FA 2003 s.76) and aligned with Scottish LBTT (LBTT(S)A 2013 s.29). Solicitor usually files via WRA online portal.)
10. What happens if I am buying land that straddles Wales and England? (Cross-border transactions: separate returns to HMRC (SDLT) and Welsh Revenue Authority (LTT), each on the consideration apportioned on a just-and-reasonable basis. The apportionment rules are in LTTA 2017 Sch 22, FA 2003 s.48A, and LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 14 for the three jurisdictions.)
11. What is the General Anti-avoidance Rule? (Part 7 LTTA 2017 introduces a Welsh-specific GAAR (s.66) covering all devolved taxes; sits alongside the SDLT-specific anti-avoidance regime in FA 2003. Stage 2 verifies whether GAAR has been engaged in Welsh Tax Tribunal cases to date.)
12. What if I have already paid LTT and find I overpaid? (Repayment routes: amend the LTT return within 12 months of original return; beyond that, the Tax Collection and Management (Wales) Act 2016 statutory overpayment-relief framework applies. Welsh-LTT-specific time limits run separately from SDLT.)
