# Higher Rates of Land Transaction Tax: A Complete Guide to Welsh LTT Higher Residential Rates for Additional-Property Purchases

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Land Transaction Tax and Anti-avoidance of Devolved Taxes (Wales) Act 2017 anaw 1 ("LTTA 2017"), Schedule 5 "Higher rates residential property transactions" - verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/anaw/2017/1/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26. Sch 5 para 3(2)(c) sets the £40,000 minor-interest threshold ("the chargeable consideration for the transaction is £40,000 or more", verbatim); para 8 contains the replacement-of-main-residence exception for single-dwelling transactions; para 8(2A)(a) sets "the period of 3 years ending with the effective date of the transaction concerned" (verbatim) as the permitted period; para 13 governs multiple-dwelling cases; para 17 contains the replacement exception for multiple-dwelling cases. Schedule introduced by LTTA 2017 s.24(10).
- Supporting: Land Transaction Tax (Tax Bands and Tax Rates) (Wales) (Amendment) Regulations 2024 - 1 percentage point uplift across all higher-rate bands in force from 11 December 2024 (verified via Welsh Government written statement at https://www.gov.wales/written-statement-changes-land-transaction-tax-higher-residential-rates announced 10 December 2024). LTTA 2017 s.24 (residential rate-setting power, used by Welsh Ministers via SI); LTTA 2017 s.10 (effective date - substantial-performance test); LTTA 2017 s.41 (return + 30-day filing window). LTTA 2017 Sch 7 (partnership relief, mirrors FA 2003 Sch 15); LTTA 2017 Sch 16 (group relief).
- House position reference: §23.2 main text (higher residential rates table 2026/27 in force from 11 December 2024: 5% / 8.5% / 10% / 12.5% / 15% / 17% across the six bands; £40,000 minor-interest threshold; replacement-of-main-residence 3-year window; joint-buyer rule that any joint buyer satisfying the conditions triggers higher rates on the whole transaction; NO corporate flat-rate equivalent to FA 2003 Sch 4A); §23.1 (main residential bands for contrast); §23.3 (MDR retained-but-modified in Wales after 7 February 2025 subsidiary-dwelling carve-out and the 3% minimum-rate floor from 13 February 2026 - critical for portfolio buyers planning a Welsh acquisition); §23.8 cross-jurisdictional table (England 5% flat surcharge vs Wales band-structure higher rates vs Scotland 8% ADS-on-entire-price - the three regimes look superficially similar but have materially different mechanics); §23.11 do-not-write list (specifically the "Welsh higher rates are a 4% flat surcharge" misconception that competitor content frequently propagates).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What are higher rates of Land Transaction Tax in Wales? (One-paragraph definition: a separate band-structure that replaces the main residential bands where the buyer or any joint buyer owns another dwelling anywhere in the world at the effective date of the transaction. NOT a flat surcharge added on top - this is the most common misconception competitor content propagates.)
2. What are the current higher-rate bands? (Table showing 5% / 8.5% / 10% / 12.5% / 15% / 17% across the £0-£180k, £180k-£250k, £250k-£400k, £400k-£750k, £750k-£1.5m, above-£1.5m bands - the post-11-December-2024 structure verified via §23.2.)
3. When did the current bands come into force? (11 December 2024 via the Land Transaction Tax (Tax Bands and Tax Rates) (Wales) (Amendment) Regulations 2024 - a 1 percentage point uplift across all higher-rate bands made by the made-affirmative procedure after the Welsh Government draft Budget announcement of 10 December 2024. Critical date: transactions with effective date BEFORE 11 December 2024 used the prior rate structure.)
4. When do higher rates apply? (Sch 5 para 3 conditions: the buyer (or any joint buyer) owns a major interest in another dwelling anywhere in the world at the effective date; that other dwelling has a market value of £40,000 or more; the new purchase is residential and the chargeable consideration is £40,000 or more. The £40k minor-interest threshold mirrors SDLT Sch 4ZA - the Welsh rule is functionally equivalent on the trigger side.)
5. What if I am replacing my main residence? (Sch 5 para 8 replacement exception: a buyer disposing of their previous main residence within the 3-year permitted period before the effective date pays main rates, not higher rates. Where the sale of the old main residence happens AFTER the new purchase effective date, higher rates apply initially with a Welsh Revenue Authority repayment available on later sale within the 3-year window.)
6. What if I own a property abroad? (Worldwide test - any major interest in any dwelling anywhere in the world triggers higher rates. Inherited interests count; bare-trust beneficial interests count; corporate-shareholder indirect interests do NOT count (corporate ownership runs through the corporate flat-rate analysis for SDLT Sch 4A in England, which has NO Welsh equivalent).)
7. What if my partner already owns a flat and I do not? (Joint-buyer aggregation: any one buyer satisfying the conditions triggers higher rates on the whole transaction per Sch 5 para 3(3). The trap is identical to SDLT Sch 4ZA and Scottish LBTT Sch 2A para 5(2).)
8. Does the Welsh higher-rates regime replicate England's SDLT non-resident 2% surcharge? (NO - Wales has not introduced a non-resident surcharge. A non-UK-resident buyer in Wales pays the same higher rates as a Welsh-resident buyer, with no additional 2%. The cross-border planning angle is real but secondary to the buyer's main-residence test.)
9. What rate do corporate buyers pay in Wales? (No flat 15% equivalent to FA 2003 Sch 4A. Welsh higher rates apply to corporate buyers (via Sch 5 para 5 - corporate-purchaser deeming), but the rates are the standard higher-rate band table above. A company buying a £600,000 Welsh dwelling pays the higher-rate bands on the full price, not a 15% flat charge. Stage 2 must verify Sch 5 para 5 mechanics for corporate-deemed-trigger cases.)
10. How does the higher-rate calculation interact with MDR? (Where the buyer acquires 2+ dwellings, MDR may be claimed (subject to the 2025 subsidiary-dwelling carve-out and the 13 February 2026 minimum 3% rate floor per §23.3); MDR averages the consideration across the dwellings, and the higher-rate bands apply to the averaged figure. Cross-link to the MDR-survives page.)
11. What if I forgot to claim main-rates relief on replacement? (Repayment claim within 3 years of the sale of the previous main residence (where the sale was post-effective-date), via the Welsh Revenue Authority repayment-of-overpaid-tax mechanism. Time-limit precision: 3-year window from sale, not from original return.)
