# Land and Buildings Transaction Tax Multiple Dwellings Relief: The Scottish Relief That Survives the SDLT Abolition (LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 5 — Eligibility, Computation, and ADS Interaction)

## Statutory anchor

- Primary: Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013 asp 11, Schedule 5 ("Multiple dwellings relief") — verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26. The schedule contains paragraphs 1-7 in the consolidated text (some paragraphs subdivided by amendment): para 1 overview; para 2 the rule (claim mechanic); paras 4 and 5 the in-scope transaction tests (single transaction with two-or-more dwellings; or single-dwelling transaction linked to other dwelling transactions); para 6 excluded transactions; para 7 attribution of consideration between dwellings and remaining property. The rate-computation mechanic uses average-consideration banding (Para 13 in the consolidated text, per legislation.gov.uk verification on 2026-05-26: total dwelling consideration divided by total dwelling count, with the per-dwelling figure rated through the LBTT residential bands and then multiplied by the dwelling count to give the tax attributable to the dwellings). Schedule 5 contains a minimum-prescribed-amount floor set by Scottish Ministers (paras 11-12 of the consolidated text — Stage 2 to verify the current floor figure / fraction and to surface the relevant Scottish Statutory Instrument). The schedule shows no outstanding effects or amendments as of 2026-05-26 verification — i.e., LBTT MDR remains in force in Scotland.
- Primary (counter-anchor / cross-border framing): Finance (No.2) Act 2024 c. 12 s.7 ("Abolition of multiple dwellings relief for SDLT") — repealed FA 2003 Schedule 6B (SDLT MDR) for transactions with an effective date on or after 1 June 2024. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2024/12/section/7 on 2026-05-26. This page's load-bearing differentiator is that **Scotland did NOT follow the UK abolition** — LBTT MDR survives under separate Scottish statute (LBTT is a devolved tax administered by Revenue Scotland). Cross-link to A1 / A2 / A3 / A20 for the SDLT-side abolition architecture.
- Supporting: LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 2A (Additional Dwelling Supplement — currently 8% from 5 December 2024 per Scottish Budget — Stage 2 to verify rate at write time against legislation.gov.uk + Revenue Scotland LBTT3015 guidance). ADS interacts with MDR: ADS is computed on the dwelling consideration and is added to (not netted from) the MDR-reduced LBTT, so the Scottish portfolio acquirer must model both the average-consideration MDR computation AND the ADS uplift.
- Supporting: LBTT(S)A 2013 s.30 (effective date — earlier of completion or substantial performance, mirroring FA 2003 s.119 in the SDLT regime); s.10-13 LBTT calculation architecture; Revenue Scotland guidance LBTT3015 (online MDR guidance — Stage 2 to verify current URL and quote the operational mechanic).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. Does Multiple Dwellings Relief still exist in Scotland? (Yes. The Scottish LBTT MDR under LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 5 remains in force and is unaffected by the FA(No.2) 2024 s.7 abolition of SDLT MDR. LBTT is a devolved tax under Scottish-Parliament statute and the UK Government's abolition of SDLT MDR has no automatic effect on LBTT. Stage 2 verifies no Scottish-Budget abolition consultation has been opened at write time.)
2. What is the LBTT MDR? (Sch 5 LBTT(S)A 2013 — relief that allows the LBTT on certain transactions involving two or more dwellings to be computed by reference to the average consideration per dwelling rather than the total consideration, often producing material savings on portfolio acquisitions by moving each dwelling into a lower LBTT residential band than would apply at the aggregate.)
3. Which transactions qualify? (Sch 5 paras 4 and 5 — para 4 covers a single transaction whose main subject-matter is two or more dwellings (with or without other property); para 5 covers a single-dwelling transaction that is linked to at least one other transaction whose main subject-matter includes dwellings. Para 6 sets excluded transactions. The "dwelling" definition is statutory; Revenue Scotland LBTT3015 expands on the operational interpretation — Stage 2 to verify URL and quote.)
4. How is the relief computed? (Average-consideration banding under para 13 of the consolidated Sch 5 text: total dwelling consideration divided by total dwelling count gives the per-dwelling figure; that figure is rated through the LBTT residential bands; the resulting per-dwelling tax is multiplied by the dwelling count to give the tax attributable to the dwellings. The remaining-property consideration (if any) is rated separately under para 7 attribution rules. Worked example with a Scottish £1.6m / 4-dwelling acquisition required at Stage 2 — verify LBTT residential bands at write time against the current rate-table on legislation.gov.uk and Revenue Scotland published rates.)
5. What is the minimum-rate floor? (LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 5 paras 11-12 — a minimum-prescribed-amount floor set by Scottish Ministers via Scottish Statutory Instrument. Stage 2 to identify the current SSI and quote the floor — the floor prevents extreme reductions on very-low-average-consideration computations. Note this differs from the historical pre-abolition SDLT MDR floor at 1% — the Scottish floor is set by Scottish Ministers and may differ.)
6. How does ADS interact with LBTT MDR? (Additional Dwelling Supplement under LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 2A applies in addition to LBTT MDR — ADS is computed on the dwelling consideration at the prevailing rate (8% from 5 December 2024 per the Scottish Budget — Stage 2 verifies) and is added to the MDR-reduced LBTT. The Scottish portfolio acquirer must model both the MDR-reduced base LBTT AND the ADS uplift to arrive at the total acquisition tax. Cross-link to the Scottish ADS page if one exists or flag the gap for follow-up.)
7. What is the cross-border position for an investor with both English and Scottish targets? (Asymmetric: SDLT MDR is abolished for English / Northern Irish acquisitions with an effective date on or after 1 June 2024; LBTT MDR remains available for Scottish acquisitions. Portfolio investors with mixed-jurisdiction targets may now find Scottish acquisitions materially more tax-efficient at the acquisition stage on a per-dwelling basis. Note the ADS rate-differential — Scottish ADS 8% versus English additional-dwellings 5% — must also be modelled.)
8. Are there any anti-avoidance rules I need to be aware of? (Standard LBTT anti-avoidance under LBTT(S)A 2013 Part 6 (anti-avoidance + GAAR-equivalent); plus the para 6 excluded-transactions carve-outs in Sch 5. Revenue Scotland enquiries on MDR claims focus on the dwelling-count test, the "dwelling" definition (annexes, sub-divisions), and the attribution between dwellings and remaining-property consideration — mirroring the HMRC enquiry patterns on the pre-abolition SDLT MDR.)
9. What is the operational claim mechanic? (Sch 5 para 2 the rule — claim by election in the LBTT return; Revenue Scotland LBTT online filing system supports MDR election. Effective date determined under LBTT(S)A 2013 s.30 — earlier of completion or substantial performance, mirroring the SDLT s.44 / s.119 mechanic.)
10. What is the current state of Scottish Government reform-watch on LBTT MDR? (Stage 2 verifies the current Scottish Budget announcements, Programme for Government text, and any open consultations from Revenue Scotland or the Scottish Government on LBTT reform. The page must NOT assert "LBTT MDR is permanent" — it must reflect the current statutory state with a reform-watch caveat. If a consultation has been opened, the page references it explicitly and dates it.)

1. **Does Multiple Dwellings Relief still exist in Scotland?**
   Yes. The Scottish Land and Buildings Transaction Tax Multiple Dwellings Relief under Schedule 5 of LBTT(S)A 2013 remains in force and operative. The Finance (No.2) Act 2024 s.7 abolition of SDLT MDR (effective 1 June 2024) applied to SDLT only, which is the property tax for England and Northern Ireland. LBTT is a devolved tax administered by Revenue Scotland under separate Scottish-Parliament statute; the Scottish Parliament has not legislated an LBTT MDR repeal as of the time of writing.

2. **What is LBTT Multiple Dwellings Relief and how does it work mechanically?**
   LBTT MDR allows the LBTT on transactions involving two or more dwellings to be computed by reference to the average consideration per dwelling rather than the total consideration. The mechanic sits at paragraph 13 of LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 5: total dwelling consideration divided by dwelling count gives the per-dwelling figure; that figure is rated through the LBTT residential bands; the resulting per-dwelling tax is multiplied back by the dwelling count to give the tax attributable to the dwellings. Where there is also remaining-property consideration (e.g. a commercial element), that consideration is rated separately under the para 7 attribution rules.

3. **Which Scottish transactions qualify for LBTT MDR?**
   Schedule 5 paragraphs 4 and 5 set the in-scope tests. Para 4 covers a single transaction whose main subject-matter is two or more dwellings (with or without other property); para 5 covers a single-dwelling transaction that is linked to at least one other transaction involving dwellings (so a portfolio assembled across multiple linked contracts can still aggregate for MDR). Para 6 sets excluded transactions, including those qualifying for group relief (Sch 10), reconstruction or acquisition relief (Sch 11), charities relief (Sch 13), or crofting community right to buy (Sch 9). The "dwelling" definition is statutory; Revenue Scotland's operational guidance interprets borderline cases (annexes, sub-divisions).

4. **How does the Additional Dwelling Supplement interact with LBTT MDR?**
   ADS under LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 2A applies in addition to LBTT and is not reduced by MDR. The current ADS rate is 8% of the relevant consideration under Schedule 2A paragraph 4(2), commenced 5 December 2024 via SSI 2024/367. A Scottish portfolio acquirer modelling the LBTT outcome must compute both the MDR-reduced base LBTT under Schedule 5 average banding plus the ADS uplift at 8% of the dwelling consideration. ADS is calculated on the full dwelling consideration, not on the per-dwelling average.

5. **Is there a minimum-rate floor for LBTT MDR claims?**
   Yes. Paragraphs 11-12 of LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 5 establish a minimum-prescribed-amount floor set by Scottish Ministers via Scottish Statutory Instrument. The floor prevents extreme reductions on very-low-average-consideration computations. Verify the current SSI and floor figure at write time against legislation.gov.uk and Revenue Scotland published guidance. The Scottish floor architecture is separate from the pre-abolition SDLT MDR 1% floor and may differ.

6. **My UK property portfolio includes both English and Scottish targets. What is the cross-border position?**
   The post-1-June-2024 architecture is asymmetric. SDLT MDR is abolished, so an English acquisition of multiple dwellings now pays SDLT on the full residential bands plus the 5% additional-dwellings surcharge with no MDR averaging. The same acquisition in Scotland still benefits from LBTT MDR averaging plus an 8% ADS stack. Depending on deal size and dwelling count, the LBTT outcome can be materially lower than the equivalent SDLT outcome on average-consideration computations. For very large 6+-dwelling portfolios the English side can pivot to FA 2003 s.116(7) automatic non-residential deeming, which may deliver a lower aggregate tax than residential plus 5% surcharge but cannot use MDR averaging.

7. **What is the operational claim mechanic for LBTT MDR?**
   The relief is claimed by election in the LBTT return at filing. LBTT(S)A 2013 s.30 sets the effective date (the earlier of completion or substantial performance, mirroring the SDLT s.44 / s.119 architecture). Revenue Scotland's online LBTT filing system supports the MDR election. Evidence pack required: transaction documents confirming the dwelling count plus the average-consideration calculation plus (where applicable) the linked-transaction architecture under para 5; valuation evidence supporting any consideration attribution under para 7; ADS computation supporting the 8% uplift.

8. **What is the position on linked transactions for LBTT MDR purposes?**
   LBTT(S)A 2013 carries its own linked-transactions rules (paralleling FA 2003 s.108 for SDLT). Where a Scottish acquisition is structured across several contracts that are linked, the aggregate consideration and aggregate dwelling count are used for the MDR computation. The substance test focuses on whether the contracts are part of a single bargain (common parties, contemporaneous timing, related subject matter). Misclassification of separate transactions as unlinked (to attempt independent MDR claims) is a known Revenue Scotland enquiry focus.

9. **What anti-avoidance posture should I expect from Revenue Scotland?**
   LBTT(S)A 2013 Part 6 contains the general LBTT anti-avoidance architecture (GAAR-equivalent plus targeted rules). The pre-abolition SDLT MDR jurisprudence on the "dwelling" definition (Fiander, Brewer, Doe v HMRC) is analytically informative but not binding on Scottish tribunals. Revenue Scotland enquiries on LBTT MDR claims focus on the dwelling-count test, annexe and sub-division treatment, and attribution between dwellings and remaining-property consideration. Documentation discipline: contemporaneous valuations, transaction architecture documented at the contract stage, MDR election supported by an evidence pack at filing.

10. **Is the Scottish Government planning to abolish LBTT MDR in line with the UK?**
    No abolition proposal has been announced as of the time of writing. The Scottish Government's Budget statements and Programme for Government documents do not contain an LBTT MDR abolition consultation. The policy could change; verify the current position at write time against the most recent Scottish Budget statement and any Revenue Scotland or Scottish-Government consultation documents. Pages writing about LBTT MDR should not assert "permanent" but should reflect "in force as of [date] with no abolition proposal announced".

11. **Does LBTT MDR apply to mixed-use acquisitions involving dwellings and commercial property?**
    Yes, in part. Where a single Schedule 5 para 4 transaction includes both dwellings and other property (e.g. a residential portfolio block with a commercial unit at ground level), MDR applies to the dwelling-attributable consideration computed under the para 7 attribution rules; the remaining-property consideration is rated separately under standard LBTT bands (non-residential where the remaining property is commercial). Pure commercial acquisitions with no dwellings do not benefit from MDR.

12. **How does the Welsh LTT MDR compare with LBTT MDR for a UK-wide investor?**
    Both Wales and Scotland retained MDR-equivalent reliefs after the SDLT abolition. Welsh LTT MDR sits at LTTA 2017 Schedule 13 (in force; minimum-floor at 3% via paragraph 6(2) substitution effective 13 February 2026; subsidiary-dwelling clarification at new paragraph 7A from 7 February 2025 via SI 2025/119). Mechanics are broadly similar (average-consideration banding) but the LTT residential bands, the LTT higher-rate surcharge for additional dwellings, and the Welsh Revenue Authority's operational guidance differ from the Scottish architecture. UK-wide investors should treat each jurisdiction's MDR as a separate computation and cross-link to the Welsh-survives sister page for the LTT mechanics.

#### Worked example 1: standalone Scottish acquisition, MDR + ADS stack

A buy-to-let landlord ("Acquirer S1") agrees to acquire 4 flats in Edinburgh from a single vendor in a single transaction. Total consideration £1.6m; average £400,000 per dwelling. Acquirer S1 already owns one main residence and one existing buy-to-let in Glasgow.

- **Test:** LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 5 para 4: single transaction, 4 dwellings, in scope for MDR. Sch 5 para 13 average-banding mechanic available. ADS under Sch 2A applies because Acquirer S1 already owns residential property.
- **MDR computation (illustrative; verify LBTT residential bands at write per §16.35):** £400,000 per-dwelling figure rated through LBTT residential bands. Approximate per-dwelling LBTT at £400k ≈ £13,350. Multiplied by 4 dwellings: total LBTT under MDR averaging ≈ £53,400. Minimum-prescribed-amount floor check (Sch 5 paras 11-12): verify the current SSI-set floor at write; on a £1.6m / 4-dwelling computation the floor is unlikely to bite.
- **ADS computation:** 8% × £1,600,000 = £128,000 (Sch 2A para 4(2) verbatim).
- **Total acquisition tax:** approximate £53,400 (LBTT MDR) + £128,000 (ADS) = **£181,400**.
- **Comparator without MDR (full-consideration banding):** £1.6m rated through full LBTT residential bands ≈ £147,950 LBTT + £128,000 ADS = £275,950. **MDR saving ≈ £94,500** on this acquisition.
- **Operational point:** ADS is the dominant single component (£128k) and is unaffected by MDR. MDR averaging delivers a material saving but the post-acquisition tax is still substantially higher than pre-ADS-uplift hypotheticals. Documentation: vendor sale particulars showing 4 dwellings; per-dwelling valuation; ADS-qualifying-additional-property declaration.

#### Worked example 2: cross-border portfolio split, asymmetric outcome

A property investor ("Acquirer S2") is comparing two acquisitions of identical commercial substance: 4 flats in Manchester at £1.6m vs 4 flats in Edinburgh at £1.6m. Acquirer S2 already owns residential property in each jurisdiction.

- **English (SDLT) outcome:** SDLT MDR unavailable (abolished 1 June 2024 per F(No.2)A 2024 s.7). Full £1.6m rated through SDLT residential bands plus 5% additional-dwellings surcharge under Sch 4ZA per HP §1.I (FA 2025 s.51, 31 October 2024 commencement). Approximate combined-rate SDLT (verify against gov.uk SDLT calculator at write): residential bands + 5% surcharge on £1.6m ≈ £193,500.
- **Scottish (LBTT) outcome:** LBTT MDR available; ADS at 8% per Sch 2A para 4(2). Per Worked example 1: ≈ £181,400.
- **Net asymmetry:** the Scottish acquisition pays ≈ £12,000 less acquisition tax than the English equivalent at this consideration + dwelling count.
- **Operational point:** the absolute differential depends on dwelling count, consideration, and surcharge interaction. At higher dwelling counts (6+), the English side may pivot to FA 2003 s.116(7) automatic non-residential deeming (workhorse surviving SDLT alternative per HP §1.H Wave 9 lock), which can deliver a lower aggregate-tax outcome than residential + 5% but cannot use MDR averaging. Cross-jurisdictional modelling at the offer / due-diligence stage is the planning step that captures this; Acquirer S2 should not assume the SDLT-side outcome is always higher.

#### Worked example 3: linked-transaction architecture, Sch 5 para 5

A portfolio investor ("Acquirer S3") agrees, in a coordinated chain of three contracts, to acquire (a) a 2-flat block in Aberdeen, (b) a single flat in Stirling, and (c) a single flat in Inverness, from three vendors who are commonly advised and whose contracts complete on the same date with substantively identical terms. Total dwellings: 4. Total consideration: £950,000.

- **Test:** LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 5 para 5: single-dwelling transactions linked to at least one other dwelling transaction. LBTT linked-transactions test (paralleling FA 2003 s.108) requires substance evaluation: common buyer + contemporaneous timing + related subject matter + coordinated transactional architecture, so the three contracts are linked.
- **Result:** the three contracts aggregate for MDR purposes. Aggregate consideration £950,000 / 4 dwellings = £237,500 per-dwelling average. Rated through LBTT residential bands (verify at write) and multiplied back by 4. Worked figure illustrative pending band verification.
- **Anti-avoidance note:** structuring three separate contracts to attempt three independent low-band LBTT computations (rather than the aggregate Sch 5 para 5 computation) attracts Revenue Scotland enquiry attention under Part 6 LBTT anti-avoidance. The substance test determines linkage, not the surface count of contracts. Acquirer S3's contemporaneous documentation should affirmatively support the linked-transaction characterisation.
- **Operational point:** Sch 5 para 5 is the route by which a portfolio assembled across several legally distinct contracts can still aggregate for MDR. Documentation discipline: transaction architecture documented at the contract stage; common-buyer-and-vendor-counsel correspondence; coordinated completion calendar.
