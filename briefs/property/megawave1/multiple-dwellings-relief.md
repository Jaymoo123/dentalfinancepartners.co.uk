# Multiple Dwellings Relief: The Three-Jurisdiction Position After the 1 June 2024 SDLT Abolition (England and Northern Ireland Repealed; Scottish LBTT MDR and Welsh LTT MDR Still Live)

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (SDLT — repealed): Finance (No.2) Act 2024 c. 12, s.7 ("Abolition of multiple dwellings relief for SDLT"). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2024/12/section/7 on 2026-05-26. s.7(1) repeals FA 2003 Schedule 6B; s.7(3) commences the repeal for land transactions with an effective date on or after 1 June 2024; s.7(4) preserves two transitional carve-outs (transactions effected in pursuance of a contract entered into and substantially performed before 1 June 2024; transactions effected in pursuance of a contract entered into on or before 6 March 2024 that is not excluded); s.7(5) defines "excluded" to include post-6-March-2024 variations, post-6-March-2024 option exercises and certain subsequent assignments; s.7(6)-(8) handle linked-transaction architecture so that pre-commencement / post-commencement linked transactions are de-linked when an MDR claim is made on the pre-commencement leg.
- Primary (SDLT — pre-abolition historical statute): FA 2003 Schedule 6B (MDR), in force 19 July 2011 (introduced by FA 2011 Sch 22) to 31 May 2024 inclusive — Stage 2 verifies the pre-abolition snapshot at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/6B/2024-05-31 — para 2 claim mechanic, para 3 "dwelling" definition, paras 4-5 average-consideration banding, para 5A later-event withdrawal, paras 6-7 supplementary.
- Primary (Scotland — live): Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013 asp 11, Schedule 5 ("Multiple dwellings relief"). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26 — paragraphs 1-7 in the consolidated text. Schedule 5 shows no outstanding effects or amendments as of 2026-05-26; LBTT MDR remains in force and unaffected by the SDLT abolition. Para 13 average-consideration banding; paras 11-12 minimum-prescribed-amount floor (set by Scottish Ministers via SSI — Stage 2 to verify the current floor).
- Primary (Wales — live): Land Transaction Tax and Anti-avoidance of Devolved Taxes (Wales) Act 2017 anaw 1, Schedule 13 ("Relief for transactions involving multiple dwellings"). Stage 2 verifies the current consolidated text at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/anaw/2017/1/schedule/13 — the Welsh LTT MDR remains in force; Welsh Revenue Authority guidance LTTA/7088 (or equivalent — Stage 2 to verify the current URL) is operative for current Welsh acquisitions.
- Supporting (cross-jurisdiction architecture): FA 2003 s.119 (SDLT effective date); FA 2003 s.44 (SDLT substantial performance); FA 2003 s.108 (SDLT linked transactions); LBTT(S)A 2013 s.30 (LBTT effective date); LTTA 2017 ss.13-14 (LTT effective date and substantial performance — Stage 2 to verify section numbers); FA 2003 s.116(7) (SDLT 6+-dwellings automatic non-residential deeming — surviving SDLT alternative for portfolio acquirers); FA 2003 Schedule 15 (SDLT partnership route); FA 2003 s.45 (SDLT sub-sale).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. Does Multiple Dwellings Relief still exist? (Yes for Scotland and Wales — LBTT MDR under Sch 5 LBTT(S)A 2013 and LTT MDR under Sch 13 LTTA 2017 both remain in force. No for England and Northern Ireland — SDLT MDR under FA 2003 Sch 6B was repealed by F(No.2)A 2024 s.7 with effect from 1 June 2024. The answer depends entirely on the location of the property.)
2. Which jurisdiction's MDR applies to my acquisition? (Diagnostic test: location of the property determines the tax regime. England and Northern Ireland = SDLT regime (relief abolished). Scotland = LBTT regime (relief live). Wales = LTT regime (relief live). Effective date determines whether transitional carve-outs apply for SDLT transactions.)
3. What was the SDLT MDR and why was it abolished? (FA 2003 Sch 6B introduced by FA 2011 Sch 22 commencement 19 July 2011 — relief computed SDLT on the average consideration per dwelling rather than total consideration. Abolished by F(No.2)A 2024 s.7 after HMRC Nov 2023 consultation found majority of claims were abusive or attempted on transactions outside the policy intent. Abolition is forward-looking: effective dates on or after 1 June 2024.)
4. Does the SDLT abolition affect my transaction if it completes after 1 June 2024 but the contract pre-dates that? (Two transitional carve-outs under s.7(4): (a) contracts entered into and substantially performed before 1 June 2024 (FA 2003 s.44 substantial performance test); (b) contracts entered into on or before 6 March 2024 that are not "excluded" under s.7(5). "Excluded" under s.7(5) includes post-6-March-2024 variations of the contract, post-6-March-2024 option exercises, and certain subsequent assignments. Plus s.7(6)-(8) handle linked-transaction anti-forestalling — pre-commencement and post-commencement linked transactions are de-linked when an MDR claim is made on the pre-commencement leg.)
5. What is the LBTT MDR (Scotland) and is it the same as the old SDLT MDR? (LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 5 — relief that allows LBTT on transactions involving two or more dwellings to be computed by reference to the average consideration per dwelling. Mechanically similar to the pre-abolition SDLT MDR but operates under separate Scottish-Parliament statute administered by Revenue Scotland. Has not been abolished. The "dwelling" definition, the claim mechanic, and the average-consideration banding are LBTT-specific and may diverge from the pre-abolition SDLT mechanic in operational detail — Stage 2 to highlight the operative differences. Cross-link to A19 for the full mechanic.)
6. What is the LTT MDR (Wales) and is it under threat? (LTTA 2017 Sch 13 — Welsh equivalent administered by the Welsh Revenue Authority. Has not been abolished. The Welsh Government has not announced an MDR abolition consultation mirroring the UK SDLT abolition at write date — Stage 2 verifies. Cross-link to the existing Welsh-survives page for the full mechanic.)
7. What replaces SDLT MDR for English / Northern Irish portfolio acquirers? (Three surviving SDLT alternatives — FA 2003 s.116(7) 6+-dwellings automatic non-residential deeming (the workhorse); FA 2003 Schedule 15 partnership / Sum of Lower Proportions route (for genuine pre-existing partnerships); FA 2003 s.45 sub-sale (narrow onward-sale arrangements). Cross-link to Wave 9 operational page for the architecture.)
8. What is the cross-border position for an investor with mixed-jurisdiction targets? (Asymmetric: a portfolio with English and Scottish targets has SDLT abolition on the English leg but LBTT MDR available on the Scottish leg. This affects the order of acquisition, the structure (single SPV per jurisdiction may be more efficient than a cross-border holding structure that aggregates targets), and the modelling at the offer / due-diligence stage. Welsh leg likewise unaffected. Note the additional-dwellings supplements differ between jurisdictions — SDLT 5% from 31 October 2024; Scottish ADS 8% from 5 December 2024; Welsh LTT higher-rates separately rated — and must be modelled alongside the MDR comparator.)
9. What is the HMRC enquiry posture on pre-abolition MDR claims? (Pre-abolition SDLT MDR claims remain open to enquiry under the standard SDLT enquiry windows; Schedule 24 FA 2007 penalty exposure applies for careless or deliberate inaccuracies. Standard HMRC attack vectors: dwelling-count inflation, "annexe is not a dwelling" challenges per Fiander / Brewer / Doe v HMRC, partial-occupancy / mixed-use re-characterisation. Cross-link to A3 for the historical eligibility architecture and enquiry-defence sections.)
10. What should a current portfolio acquirer do? (Three-step diagnostic: (i) identify the property location → determines regime → determines relief availability; (ii) for SDLT acquisitions, run the effective-date + transitional-cohort screen; (iii) for LBTT / LTT acquisitions, run the in-jurisdiction MDR computation. Cross-link to the appropriate operational page for each jurisdiction. For mixed-jurisdiction portfolios, model the SDLT-abolition outcome alongside the LBTT / LTT MDR outcomes to identify cross-border restructuring opportunities.)

### Draft FAQ entries (Stage 2 populated 2026-05-26; RUN session adapts wording, ensures no duplication of body H2/H3 phrasing)

Target 12-15. RUN session adapts + expands for FAQPage schema cleanliness.

1. **Does Multiple Dwellings Relief still exist?**
   It depends on the jurisdiction of the property. For England and Northern Ireland the SDLT MDR was abolished by Finance (No.2) Act 2024 s.7 with effect from 1 June 2024. For Scotland the LBTT MDR under LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 5 remains in force. For Wales the LTT MDR under LTTA 2017 Schedule 13 remains in force. The relief you can claim depends entirely on where the property is located and, for SDLT, on the effective date of the transaction.

2. **Which jurisdiction's MDR applies to my acquisition?**
   The diagnostic test is location of the property. Property in England or Northern Ireland sits in the SDLT regime (relief now abolished). Property in Scotland sits in the LBTT regime (relief live). Property in Wales sits in the LTT regime (relief live). For SDLT transactions, the effective date determines whether the F(No.2)A 2024 s.7(4) transitional carve-outs preserve eligibility.

3. **What was SDLT MDR and why was it abolished?**
   FA 2003 Schedule 6B (introduced by FA 2011 Schedule 22, commencement 19 July 2011) allowed SDLT on transactions involving two or more dwellings to be computed on the average consideration per dwelling rather than the total. Abolished by F(No.2)A 2024 s.7 after the November 2023 HMRC consultation found a majority of MDR claims were abusive or attempted on transactions outside the policy intent. Abolition is forward-looking: it applies to land transactions with an effective date on or after 1 June 2024.

4. **My SDLT transaction completes after 1 June 2024 but the contract pre-dates that. Can I still claim MDR?**
   Possibly, under the two transitional carve-outs in s.7(4): (a) the transaction is effected in pursuance of a contract entered into and substantially performed before 1 June 2024 (the FA 2003 s.44 substantial performance test); or (b) the transaction is effected in pursuance of a contract entered into on or before 6 March 2024 and is not "excluded". "Excluded" under s.7(5) covers post-6-March-2024 variations, post-6-March-2024 option exercises, and certain subsequent assignments. The s.7(6)-(8) linked-transaction architecture de-links pre-commencement and post-commencement linked transactions where an MDR claim is made on the pre-commencement leg.

5. **What counts as "substantial performance" for the s.7(4)(a) carve-out?**
   FA 2003 s.44 defines substantial performance: the purchaser (or someone on their behalf) takes possession of the whole or substantially the whole of the subject matter of the contract, OR pays a substantial amount of the consideration (HMRC interprets "substantial" broadly as ~90% of total consideration). For most landlord acquisitions, possession on practical completion is the trigger.

6. **What is LBTT MDR (Scotland) and how does it differ from the pre-abolition SDLT MDR?**
   LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 5 is the Scottish equivalent: relief allowing LBTT on transactions involving two or more dwellings to be computed on the average consideration per dwelling rather than the total. Mechanically similar to the pre-abolition SDLT MDR (average-consideration banding at para 13) but operates under separate Scottish-Parliament statute administered by Revenue Scotland. Has not been abolished. The Scottish ADS at 8% (Schedule 2A paragraph 4(2), commenced 5 December 2024 via SSI 2024/367) stacks on top of the MDR-reduced LBTT. Cross-link to A19 for the full Scottish mechanic.

7. **What is LTT MDR (Wales) and is it under threat?**
   LTTA 2017 Schedule 13 is the Welsh equivalent administered by the Welsh Revenue Authority. Has not been abolished. Two recent statutory changes update the operative architecture: a new paragraph 7A (subsidiary dwellings) inserted 7 February 2025 by SI 2025/119, providing that a qualifying dwelling and a purchased dwelling that is subsidiary to it are treated as if they were an interest in a single dwelling for MDR purposes; and a substitution of the minimum-prescribed-amount floor at paragraph 6(2) to 3% effective 13 February 2026 by Welsh Regulations. No abolition consultation has been announced. Cross-link to the Welsh-survives sister page for the full mechanic.

8. **What replaces SDLT MDR for English and Northern Irish portfolio acquirers?**
   Three SDLT alternatives survive the abolition: (a) FA 2003 s.116(7) automatic non-residential deeming for purchases of 6 or more dwellings in a single transaction (statutory deeming, no claim required); (b) FA 2003 Schedule 15 paragraph 10 partnership Sum of the Lower Proportions route (requires a genuine pre-existing partnership with substance; full mechanics in our Schedule 15 SDLT page); (c) FA 2003 s.45 sub-sale relief for narrow pre-completion onward-sale arrangements. None replaces MDR in scope; 4-dwelling acquisitions typically face higher SDLT post-abolition. Cross-link to the Wave 9 operational page for the architecture.

9. **What is the cross-border position for a portfolio with mixed English and Scottish targets?**
   Asymmetric. The English leg pays SDLT under the post-abolition architecture: residential bands plus the 5% additional-dwellings surcharge under Schedule 4ZA (FA 2025 s.51 commencement 31 October 2024 per HP §1.I). The Scottish leg pays LBTT under Schedule 5 average-banding plus the 8% ADS uplift under Schedule 2A. For very large portfolios on the English leg the s.116(7) 6+-dwellings route may apply (statutory deeming to non-residential rates). Acquisition ordering, single-SPV-per-jurisdiction structuring, and offer-stage modelling all merit revisiting in light of the asymmetry.

10. **What is the cross-border position for a portfolio with mixed English and Welsh targets?**
    Similarly asymmetric. The English leg has no MDR; the Welsh leg has LTT MDR under Schedule 13 average-banding plus Welsh LTT higher-rates under Schedule 5 of LTTA 2017. The Welsh minimum-floor (Schedule 13 paragraph 6(2)) was substituted to 3% effective 13 February 2026, materially limiting how low the per-dwelling average-banding can drive the Welsh tax. The Welsh subsidiary-dwelling treatment (new paragraph 7A from 7 February 2025) means a qualifying dwelling and its subsidiary dwellings count as a single dwelling for MDR — a refinement that affects how a Welsh portfolio is computed.

11. **What is the HMRC enquiry posture on pre-abolition SDLT MDR claims?**
    Pre-1-June-2024 effective-date SDLT MDR claims remain open to enquiry under the standard SDLT enquiry windows. Schedule 24 FA 2007 penalty exposure applies for careless or deliberate inaccuracies. Standard HMRC attack vectors: dwelling-count inflation (annexes mischaracterised as separate dwellings, contradicted by Fiander, Brewer, Doe v HMRC line); partial-occupancy or mixed-use re-characterisation; insufficient evidence of the "dwelling" definition being met at the effective date. Cross-link to A3 for the historical eligibility architecture.

12. **What should a current portfolio acquirer do?**
    Three-step diagnostic. Step (i): identify the property location (determines tax regime, determines relief availability). Step (ii): for SDLT acquisitions, run the s.7(4) transitional-cohort screen against the effective-date analysis. Step (iii): for LBTT or LTT acquisitions, run the in-jurisdiction MDR computation against the current Schedule 5 (Scotland) or Schedule 13 (Wales) architecture. For mixed-jurisdiction portfolios model SDLT-abolition + LBTT-MDR + LTT-MDR outcomes side-by-side at the offer stage.

13. **Can I structure my acquisition through an SPV or partnership to bypass the SDLT abolition?**
    No. The abolition is transaction-level; the buyer's identity (individual, company, SPV, partnership) does not unlock MDR for a post-1-June-2024 effective-date SDLT transaction. The three SDLT surviving alternatives operate on their own statutory criteria (s.116(7) requires actually buying 6+ dwellings; Sch 15 SLP requires a genuine pre-existing partnership; s.45 sub-sale requires genuine onward-sale arrangements). Wrapping the buyer in an SPV does not engage any of them automatically.

14. **What is the practical SDLT cost increase for a typical 4-dwelling portfolio acquisition post-abolition?**
    Materially higher. As illustration: a £1.4m four-dwelling portfolio (average £350k per dwelling) under pre-abolition MDR was computed on £350k per dwelling × 4 with surcharge stack — typically £42k-£70k SDLT depending on band interaction. The same transaction post-abolition is computed on the full £1.4m at residential bands plus 5% surcharge, typically £120k-£170k SDLT (verify against gov.uk SDLT calculator at write per §16.35). The 6+-dwellings s.116(7) route requires actually buying 6+ dwellings; a 4-dwelling buyer cannot reach for it.

15. **Are the Welsh and Scottish reliefs vulnerable to future abolition?**
    No abolition has been announced as of the time of writing. The Welsh Government's recent activity on LTT Schedule 13 has been refinement-focused (the February 2025 subsidiary-dwellings clarification and February 2026 minimum-floor substitution to 3%), suggesting an ongoing operative regime rather than an abolition trajectory. The Scottish Government has not announced an LBTT MDR consultation mirroring the UK SDLT abolition. Both positions could change; verify the current state of any consultation, Welsh Budget statement, or Scottish Budget statement at write time. Pages writing about either relief should not assert "permanent" — reflect "in force as of [date] with no abolition proposal announced".

#### Worked example 1: three-jurisdiction comparator, 4 dwellings at £2m

A property investor ("Acquirer M1") is modelling acquisitions of 4 flats at total consideration £2m (average £500,000 per dwelling) in three hypothetical jurisdictions: England, Scotland, Wales. Acquirer M1 already owns residential property in each jurisdiction (so additional-dwellings surcharges apply in each case).

- **England (SDLT, MDR abolished):** Full £2m rated through residential bands plus 5% additional-dwellings surcharge under Sch 4ZA Table A (combined-rate bands 5% / 7% / 10% / 15% / 17% per HP §1.I FA 2025 s.51). Approximate SDLT (verify against gov.uk SDLT calculator at write): ≈ £253,500.
- **Scotland (LBTT, MDR live):** £500,000 per-dwelling figure rated through LBTT residential bands. Approximate per-dwelling LBTT at £500k ≈ £23,350 (verify at write). Multiplied by 4 dwellings ≈ £93,400. Plus ADS at 8% × £2m = £160,000. **Total ≈ £253,400** — broadly equivalent to English under this dwelling-count + consideration mix; the LBTT base rate is lower but the ADS uplift is higher.
- **Wales (LTT, MDR live):** £500,000 per-dwelling figure rated through LTT residential bands; minimum-floor check at 3% of total consideration under Schedule 13 para 6(2) substituted 13 February 2026. Plus Welsh LTT higher-rate surcharge for additional dwellings under LTTA 2017 Schedule 5. Approximate figures pending band verification at write.
- **Operational point:** the three jurisdictions can deliver materially different acquisition-tax outcomes at the same headline consideration. The English-vs-Scottish comparison above is illustratively close at 4 dwellings × £500k; the differential expands or contracts at different dwelling counts and consideration tiers. RUN session verifies all rate-by-reference figures at write per §16.35.

#### Worked example 2: transitional cohort, SDLT MDR survives on post-abolition completion

A buy-to-let landlord ("Acquirer M2") contracted to buy a block of 4 flats in England on 14 February 2024. Contract included a long-stop completion in October 2025 to allow vendor refurbishment. Acquirer M2 paid the deposit at exchange and took possession of one flat on 20 May 2024 for refurbishment supervision (substantial performance under FA 2003 s.44).

- **Test:** effective date for SDLT under FA 2003 s.119 is the earlier of completion (October 2025) or substantial performance (20 May 2024). Substantial performance on 20 May 2024 precedes 1 June 2024.
- **Result:** SDLT MDR remains claimable. F(No.2)A 2024 s.7(4)(a) carve-out applies (contract entered into and substantially performed before 1 June 2024). Anti-forestalling under s.7(5) does not bite.
- **Operational point:** Acquirer M2's SDLT return reflects the 20 May 2024 effective date. MDR is computed on the average-consideration-per-dwelling basis under the (now-repealed-for-non-transitional-cases) FA 2003 Schedule 6B. Evidence pack required: contract dated 14 February 2024; deposit receipt; possession evidence (key handover, refurbishment supervision authorisation, contemporaneous correspondence).

#### Worked example 3: post-abolition surviving SDLT alternative, s.116(7) 6+-dwellings route

A portfolio landlord ("Acquirer M3") agrees to acquire 7 buy-to-let dwellings from a single vendor in England in a single transaction. Contract dated October 2025; completion January 2026. Total consideration £2.45m (average £350k per dwelling). No partnership; not connected with vendor.

- **Test:** transaction involves the acquisition of 6 or more dwellings in a single transaction per FA 2003 s.116(7) (the workhorse surviving SDLT alternative per HP §1.H). Automatic statutory deeming to non-residential rates; no claim required.
- **Calculation:** non-residential SDLT bands under FA 2003 s.55 Table B (verify current rates at write per §16.35): 0% to £150k = £0; 2% £150k-£250k = £2,000; 5% above £250k on £2.2m = £110,000. **Total non-residential SDLT ≈ £112,000**.
- **Comparison:** residential rates plus 5% surcharge on £2.45m would be markedly higher (indicative £230k-£270k depending on band interaction; verify against gov.uk SDLT calculator at write).
- **Operational point:** s.116(7) is the workhorse post-abolition route for 6+-dwellings portfolio acquisitions. Material saving vs residential-rate treatment. Evidence pack: vendor sale particulars showing 7 dwellings; valuation per dwelling; transaction documents confirming single-transaction acquisition. Linked-transaction analysis under FA 2003 s.108 may aggregate separate but related contracts; session writing notes the linked-transactions discipline per HP §1.B.
