# Abolishment of Multiple Dwellings Relief: What Changed on 1 June 2024 and What Buyers Need to Know

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Finance (No.2) Act 2024 c. 12, s.7 ("Abolition of multiple dwellings relief for SDLT") — verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2024/12/section/7 on 2026-05-26. Repeals FA 2003 Sch 6B for land transactions with an **effective date on or after 1 June 2024**. s.7(3) sets the commencement; s.7(4) preserves substantially-performed-before-1-June-2024 transitional cohort and certain pre-6-March-2024 contracts; s.7(5)-(6) provide anti-forestalling against post-Budget variations, assignments, option exercises and sub-sales used to retrofit MDR onto post-abolition completions.
- Supporting: FA 2003 s.119 (effective date = earlier of completion or substantial performance); FA 2003 s.44 (substantial performance definition); FA 2003 s.116(7) (6+-dwellings automatic non-residential deeming — surviving alternative, verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/section/116 on 2026-05-26).
- House position reference: §1 main text (MDR abolition headline + surviving alternatives summary, 1 April 2025 rates table, 5% additional dwellings surcharge from 31 October 2024); §1.H Wave 9 Stage 1b lock (full MDR abolition + transitional + anti-forestalling + 3 surviving alternatives architecture); §1.A Wave 7 lock (Sch 15 SLP partnership-route surviving alternative); §1.B Wave 7 lock (s.108 linked-transactions discipline for s.116(7) aggregation).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is Multiple Dwellings Relief and why was it abolished? (One-paragraph history: introduced FA 2011 Sch 22, abolished F(No.2)A 2024 s.7 after HMRC Nov 2023 consultation found majority of claims were abusive or attempted on transactions outside the policy intent.)
2. When did the abolition take effect? (1 June 2024 effective date per s.7(3); effective date defined by FA 2003 s.119.)
3. My contract was signed before 1 June 2024. Can I still claim MDR? (Only if substantial performance under s.44 occurred pre-1-June-2024, OR contract dated pre-6-March-2024 with no post-Budget variations / option exercises / assignment activity. Plain-English decision tree.)
4. I am buying multiple dwellings now. What replaces MDR? (s.116(7) 6+-dwellings automatic non-residential deeming is the workhorse; Sch 15 partnership route for genuine pre-existing partnerships; s.45 sub-sale for narrow onward-sale arrangements. Cross-link to the Wave 9 operational page for mechanics.)
5. Does this affect Welsh or Scottish purchases? (No. SDLT only. Welsh LTT MDR-equivalent survives under LTTA 2017 Sch 13; Scotland never had an MDR equivalent — LBTT has its own Additional Dwelling Supplement regime. Cross-link to the Welsh-survives page.)
6. What is anti-forestalling? (Plain-language: HMRC blocks attempts to retrofit MDR onto post-1-June-2024 completions via pre-existing contracts that were materially varied after the Budget, assignments to new buyers, options exercised, or sub-sale chains. The Wave 9 page covers the operational architecture; this page surfaces the concept and the three patterns blocked.)
7. I already claimed MDR on a pre-abolition transaction. Can HMRC withdraw it now? (No — abolition is not retrospective. Pre-1-June-2024 effective-date transactions stand on the eligibility criteria in force at that date.)
8. What if I claim MDR by mistake on a post-1-June-2024 transaction? (Disallowed on enquiry; penalty exposure under FA 2007 Sch 24 if careless or deliberate.)
