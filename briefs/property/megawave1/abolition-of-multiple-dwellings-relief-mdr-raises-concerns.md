# Abolition of Multiple Dwellings Relief: The Concerns That Were Raised and What Actually Happened

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Finance (No.2) Act 2024 c. 12, s.7 ("Abolition of multiple dwellings relief for SDLT") — verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2024/12/section/7 on 2026-05-26. s.7(3) commencement 1 June 2024; s.7(4) transitional carve-outs (substantially-performed pre-1-June-2024 contracts; pre-6-March-2024 contracts absent post-Budget variations); s.7(5)-(6) anti-forestalling; s.7(8) Treasury regulation-making power for further exempted transactions (used? verify at write time).
- Supporting: FA 2003 s.116(7) (6+-dwellings automatic non-residential deeming — survives as the principal route for genuine bulk acquirers, verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/section/116 on 2026-05-26); FA 2003 Sch 15 paras 10-13 (partnership SLP route surviving alternative); HMRC consultation Nov 2023 (Multiple Dwellings Relief: a call for evidence) — the policy-debate primary source.
- House position reference: §1 main text (MDR abolition headline + surviving alternatives + 5% surcharge from 31 October 2024); §1.H Wave 9 Stage 1b lock (MDR abolition operational architecture); §1.A Wave 7 lock (Sch 15 SLP genuine-partnership defence). ## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **sector-impact and policy-debate angle** on MDR abolition. The site's existing canonical MDR-abolition page (`multiple-dwellings-relief-abolition-fa-2024-transitional-rules-landlords`, Wave 9, 3,386 words) covers the operational architecture: statutory mechanics, transitional cohort identification, anti-forestalling patterns, the three surviving alternatives. The A1 sister-pick (`abolishment-of-multiple-dwelling-relief`) covers the plain-language taxpayer on-ramp. This A2 page sits in a **third lane**: it is structured around the **stakeholder concerns** raised during the HMRC consultation (Nov 2023) and in the parliamentary debate, then tested against the **post-abolition reality** (the first 24 months of operation since 1 June 2024). Audience: a PRS operator, a build-to-rent investor, a housing-association property-tax adviser, a PBSA developer, or a policy-aware landlord representative who needs to understand both **what the sector worried about** and **which concerns were well-founded versus which were mitigated by surviving s.116(7) / Sch 15 routes**. The page is **policy-informed not operational**; for operational mechanics it cross-links the Wave 9 page.

The "raises concerns" framing in the slug carries the original commentary register and is preserved for query-match. Body should be structured around the four concern-clusters that actually surfaced: (1) **bulk-acquisition viability** (PRS / BTR / PBSA economics post-abolition); (2) **transitional unfairness** (contracts mid-flight on Budget day); (3) **collateral damage to non-target acquirers** (genuine multi-property buyers who had nothing to do with the abuse-pattern HMRC consulted on); (4) **policy direction** (does abolition signal a wider crackdown on SDLT reliefs? what about Sch 15 SLP next?).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What concerns did industry bodies and tax professionals raise during the Nov 2023 HMRC consultation on MDR? (ICAEW, CIOT, BPF, NRLA, RICS positions on transitional fairness, bulk-acquisition viability, and collateral damage to non-target acquirers.)
2. Which of those concerns proved well-founded after 1 June 2024? (Empirical: 24 months of post-abolition operation. Which carve-outs softened the blow? Which did not?)
3. How did the s.116(7) 6+-dwellings rule mitigate the concerns for genuine bulk acquirers? (Statutory deeming survives; non-residential bands apply automatically; no claim machinery. The "workhorse" surviving route per §1.H.)
4. What about acquirers who fall below 6 dwellings? (The most acute pressure: 2-5 dwelling acquirers lose MDR with no automatic deeming; partnership SLP route (Sch 15) is conditional on genuine pre-existing partnership; sub-sale (s.45) is narrow. The empirical SDLT-cost increase for a 4-dwelling portfolio acquisition post-abolition is markedly higher.)
5. Did the transitional carve-outs in s.7(4) work in practice? (Substantial-performance and pre-6-March-2024-contract routes; what HMRC enquiry posture has emerged in the first 24 months?)
6. What was the policy rationale for abolition and did it land? (HMRC's consultation cited misuse of MDR — particularly mixed-use claims dressed as MDR, and reliance on the "annexe" concept to inflate dwelling counts. Did abolition simply reroute those claims into s.116(7) and Bewley-style claims, or did it shrink the relief landscape genuinely?)
7. What is the post-abolition signal for other SDLT reliefs? (FA 2025 brought further SDLT changes — non-resident surcharge clarifications, first-time buyer threshold changes. Is Sch 15 SLP next? Is sub-sale relief at risk? Where is the policy direction headed for FA 2026?)
8. What are professional bodies saying now (2026)? (Updated positions post-abolition — any calls for partial restoration? Any successful judicial review or political challenge? Empirical: none to date, verify at write time.)
9. Welsh and Scottish position: did devolved administrations follow? (No — Welsh LTT MDR equivalent survives under LTTA 2017 Sch 13; Scotland never had MDR. The devolved divergence is itself a policy point worth surfacing for cross-border investors.)
