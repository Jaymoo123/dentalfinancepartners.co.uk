# Single-Person Council Tax Discounts — A Complete Guide to LGFA 1992 s.11(1)(a), the Eleven Schedule 1 Disregards, the "Sole or Main Residence" Doctrine, and Every Interaction with the SI 1992/558 Exemption Classes

## Statutory anchor

- Primary: **LGFA 1992 s.11 in full.** Five operative subsections:
  - **s.11(1)(a)** — the 25% single-occupant discount where only one non-disregarded resident lives in the dwelling.
  - **s.11(1)(b)** — the 25% discount where the chargeable dwelling has no resident at all (subject to LA discretion via SI 2003/3011 + s.11A).
  - **s.11(2)** — the 50% discount where no resident OR all residents are disregarded (subject to LA discretion).
  - **s.11(3)** — "appropriate percentage" = 25% (i.e. the discount is statutorily fixed; LAs cannot vary the discount rate, only the underlying empty-property treatment).
  - **s.11(4)** — definition of "the appropriate day" (the day the discount applies — day-by-day basis).
  - **s.11(5)** — definition of "resident": "an individual is resident in a dwelling on a particular day if it is his sole or main residence on that day."
  Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/14/section/11 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary: **LGFA 1992 Schedule 1 — the disregards.** Eleven principal disregards covering specific categories of adult resident who are "disregarded for the purposes of discount" — i.e. their presence in the dwelling does NOT count when determining the number of "residents" for the s.11(1)(a) test. The paragraph numbering and current text must be re-verified by Stage 2; the headline categories are:
  - **Paragraph 1 — Persons in detention** (prison, immigration detention, mental-health detention under MHA 1983 Part III).
  - **Paragraph 2 — Severely mentally impaired persons** (with medical certification + entitlement to one of the qualifying state benefits per SI 1992/552).
  - **Paragraph 3 — Persons in respect of whom child benefit is payable** (the 18 + 19 year-old child-benefit-recipient route).
  - **Paragraph 4 — Students** (full-time students at a UK or EEA institution, foreign-language assistants, student nurses — definition via SI 1992/558 + SI 2005/2865).
  - **Paragraph 5 — Apprentices and youth-training trainees** (specific apprenticeship and training scheme definitions — Stage 2 verifies current paragraph wording and the qualifying scheme list).
  - **Paragraph 6 — Patients in hospitals** (NHS hospital or equivalent residential inpatient unit; the patient's previous home retains the patient's status as resident there until the hospital admission becomes "sole or main residence").
  - **Paragraph 7 — Persons in care homes / nursing homes** (the resident's previous home retains the resident's residence status if the move is for treatment and the previous home remains available).
  - **Paragraph 8 — Religious community members** (members of religious orders / communities where no income is received personally; specific definitions).
  - **Paragraph 8A — Persons resident in hostels or night-shelters.**
  - **Paragraph 9 + Paragraph 10 — Persons providing care** (carer of a non-spouse / non-partner / non-child providing 35+ hours per week of care; both paragraphs operate but with different conditions on the carer-cared-for relationship).
  - **Paragraph 11 + Paragraph 12 — Members of visiting forces + diplomats** (UK / EU / qualifying-treaty diplomatic and forces personnel).
  Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/14/schedule/1 on 2026-05-26 (Stage 2 re-verifies current consolidated text and any post-2026 amendments).
- Primary: **SI 1992/558 — exemption classes that interact with the single-person discount.** Critical interactions:
  - **Class N (all-student dwelling)** — full exemption where ALL residents are full-time students. The single-person discount does NOT apply to a Class N dwelling because Class N removes the council tax entirely. But the boundary case is important: ONE non-student + multiple students = single-person discount (not Class N); ALL students = Class N; ALL students except one + one non-student "guest" who is not a "resident" by sole-or-main-residence test = still Class N.
  - **Class U (all-severely-mentally-impaired dwelling)** — full exemption where ALL residents are severely mentally impaired (with medical certification).
  - **Class S (all-under-18 dwelling)** — full exemption where ALL residents are under 18.
  - **Class W (annex occupied by dependent relative)** — annex separately banded but fully exempt where occupied by a dependent relative aged 65+ / severely disabled / substantially mentally impaired.
  - **Class M (HMO occupied solely by Sch 1-disregarded persons)** — historically applied; interaction with the SI 2023/1175 single-dwelling-HMO rule means the discount / exemption analysis is now applied at the HMO single-dwelling level (cross-reference C3 brief).
  Stage 2 re-verifies SI 1992/558 current consolidated contents.
- Supporting: **LGFA 1992 s.16 + SI 1992/290 — the proposal / banding-challenge mechanism.** Not directly a single-person discount route but the route by which a "wrong band" appeal may be combined with a discount challenge if the LA contests the discount claim.
- Supporting: **LGFA 1992 Sch 4 — the collection limitation.** Typically 6 years for council-tax recovery, governing how far back a wrongly-paid bill (or wrongly-claimed discount) can be recovered.
- Supporting: **LGFA 1992 s.66(2)(d) — the criminal-offence framework** for the discipline of timely notification under s.16(6) (the 21-day reporting window) (Stage 2 verifies the relevant sub-section reference for the post-2025 consolidated text).

Northern Ireland operates a separate rating system (no council tax + no s.11 discount; the NI district rates system has its own occupier reliefs).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the complete statutory framework for the single-person discount? (LGFA 1992 s.11(1)(a) — 25% reduction where only one non-disregarded resident. "Appropriate percentage" fixed at 25% by s.11(3). Day-by-day operation per s.11(4). "Resident" defined by s.11(5) as the dwelling being "sole or main residence." Disregards listed in LGFA 1992 Sch 1. Discount is non-means-tested and statutory; an LA cannot vary the rate.)
2. What are the eleven Sch 1 disregards and how does each one work? (Paragraph 1 detention; para 2 severely mentally impaired; para 3 child-benefit-payable 18/19 year-olds; para 4 students; para 5 apprentices; para 6 hospital patients; para 7 care-home residents; para 8 religious-community members; para 8A hostel / night-shelter residents; paras 9-10 carers; paras 11-12 visiting forces / diplomats. Each disregard has operative criteria — Stage 2 deep-dives each.)
3. What is the "sole or main residence" test? (Question of fact developed across Williams v Horsham DC [2004] EWCA Civ 39, Bennett v Copeland BC, and numerous VTE / FTT authorities. Factors include: where the person sleeps most nights; where personal possessions are kept; where the person's family / partner is; voter registration; mail receipt; work location; intentions; presence over time. No single factor determinative; holistic assessment. A person can have only ONE sole-or-main residence at a time.)
4. How does the carer disregard work? (Sch 1 paras 9-10. Two operative scenarios: (i) carer of a co-resident severely mentally impaired / disabled person where the carer provides 35+ hours per week of care AND the cared-for is in receipt of a qualifying benefit (e.g. Attendance Allowance higher rate, DLA / PIP enhanced daily living, Constant Attendance Allowance); (ii) carer of a non-resident-but-related person where the carer is providing care to a partner / spouse / cohabitee in a specific medical context. Both paragraphs require evidence of the qualifying benefit + the 35-hours-per-week threshold.)
5. How does the student disregard work? (Sch 1 para 4 + SI 1992/558 definitions. "Full-time student" = enrolled on a course of at least 1 academic year + at least 21 hours per week of study + at a prescribed institution (UK or EEA university or further-education college). Student nurses + apprentices have separate definitions. The student certificate from the institution is the standard evidence. The disregard runs for the duration of the course (including vacation periods if remaining "enrolled"). A household of two students + one working adult = single-person discount (one non-disregarded). A household of three students = Class N exemption (zero council tax).)
6. How does the 18 + 19 year-old child-benefit route work? (Sch 1 para 3. An 18 or 19 year old in respect of whom child benefit is payable is disregarded. Child benefit continues to be payable for 18 + 19 year olds in continuing non-advanced education (e.g. A-level, BTEC, vocational training below degree). Once child benefit stops (e.g. on completion of A-levels at end of school year + not continuing to further education), the disregard ceases. A household of one working parent + one 18 year old A-level student in receipt of child benefit = single-person discount applies.)
7. How does the all-disregarded-residents outcome work? (LGFA 1992 s.11(2) — 50% discount where ALL residents are disregarded OR where no resident at all. BUT s.11(2) is then OFTEN overridden by an SI 1992/558 Class exemption that applies to the specific disregard mix: ALL students = Class N (full exemption, not 50%); ALL severely mentally impaired = Class U (full exemption); ALL under-18 = Class S (full exemption). So the practical outcome depends on the precise disregard mix: ALL students → £0 (Class N); ALL severely mentally impaired → £0 (Class U); ALL under-18 → £0 (Class S); mixed-disregard household (e.g. one student + one carer + one severely mentally impaired) → 50% discount under s.11(2) (no all-of-one-class exemption applies). The LA's discount-determination team should apply the most-beneficial outcome by default but the applicant should know the rules to push back if the LA gets it wrong.)
8. What is the cross-jurisdictional position — does the single-person discount apply across the UK? (LGFA 1992 applies in England + Wales + Scotland for the s.11 architecture. Scotland: same 25% single-person discount via LGFA 1992 (Scottish provisions); Scottish council tax has the same Class N + U + S exemption mechanism. Wales: same architecture; Welsh CTR scheme operates separately and is largely similar but distinct LA-by-LA from English scheme. Northern Ireland: NO council tax — NI operates a separate rating system under the Rates (NI) Order 1977; the NI district rates + regional rates have their own occupier reliefs (Lone Pensioner Allowance + Disabled Persons Allowance) but no exact equivalent of the LGFA 1992 s.11 single-person discount.)
9. How does the historical evolution work — when was the 25% rate set? (1993. The Community Charge (Poll Tax) operated 1989-1993 under the LGFA 1988 — individual-based, no dwelling-based discount mechanism. The 1992 reform — LGFA 1992 — repealed the Community Charge and instituted the council tax with the dwelling-based banding (Bands A-H) + s.11 discount architecture. The 25% rate was a political settlement at enactment, balancing fiscal yield with the policy goal of recognising lower household consumption in single-occupant dwellings. The rate has not changed since 1993; Stage 2 confirms no rate-altering amendment has been passed.)
10. How does the overpayment-recovery framework work? (LGFA 1992 Sch 4 — typically 6 years recovery limitation. An LA reviewing a long-standing discount claim and deciding the "sole or main residence" test or the disregard claim was not met can recover up to 6 years of misclaimed discount. Defence: timeline + evidence + the s.16(6) 21-day notification record. Appeal route: VTE under LGFA 1992 s.16(1). The page sets out the procedural pathway including the FTT Tax Chamber for VTE appeals on points of law and the High Court for judicial review.)
11. How does the discount interact with the Council Tax Reduction Scheme (CTR)? (Separately. Discount applies first; CTR applies to the residual bill. Pensioner CTR under SI 2012/2885 is centrally-prescribed and broadly equivalent to the abolished Council Tax Benefit. Working-age CTR is locally-determined under LGFA 1992 Sch 1A (England) / Sch 1B (Wales). A pensioner widow alone in a Band C dwelling on Pension Credit: single-person discount 25% → CTR pensioner-prescribed scheme can reduce the residual to £0 or near-zero depending on income.)
12. What are the policy critiques and likely reform pressures? (Three principal critique strands: (i) the 25% rate is widely argued to be too low (single-person consumption of LA services may be more than 75% of two-person consumption — argument for 15-20% only); (ii) the disregard categories may be too restrictive (e.g. carers who are spouses / civil partners are EXCLUDED from the carer disregard); (iii) the LURA 2023 empty-homes + second-homes premium framework has put discount-versus-premium calibration back on the political table. No reform legislated as of the page write date; Stage 2 verifies any 2026 / 2027 Finance Bill or LGFA amendment.)
