# First-Time Buyer Relief: Benefits and Eligibility Requirements (Sch 6ZA Deep-Dive on the "First-Time Buyer" Definition, Joint-Purchase Mechanics, Shared Ownership, and 2024 Bare-Trust Amendment)

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: FA 2003 s.57B "First-time buyers" — "(1) Schedule 6ZA provides relief for first-time buyers." Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/section/57B on 2026-05-26 (up to date with all changes in force on or before 26 May 2026).
- Primary (operative): FA 2003 Schedule 6ZA "Relief for first-time buyers" — inserted by Finance Act 2018 c. 3 s.41(3), commenced per s.41(8). Para 1(4) sets the conditions: "the purchaser, or (if more than one) each of the purchasers, is a first-time buyer who intends to occupy" the dwelling as their only or main residence; the transaction must be a "major interest in a single dwelling"; consideration must not exceed £500,000. Para 4 Table A sets the 0% / 5% rate split on the first £300,000 / £300,000-£500,000. Para 6 defines "first-time buyer". Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/6ZA on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (2024 amendment): F(No.2)A 2024 c. 12 s.8 inserted Sch 6ZA para 6(3) addressing bare trust leases (the bare-trust mechanism by which a trustee holding a lease for the benefit of the actual occupier was previously argued to disqualify the occupier from FTB status; the 2024 amendment closes specific abuse routes and clarifies treatment). Verified via the same Sch 6ZA page; verify the exact text of para 6(3) at Stage 2 write.
- Supporting: FA 2003 s.55 Table A (standard residential rates that apply when relief is unavailable); FA 2003 Sch 4ZA (additional dwellings surcharge — Sch 6ZA relief disapplies where the surcharge applies, per the joint-purchase trap); HMRC SDLT Manual SDLTM29800-29900 (FTB relief mechanics, Stage 2 verifies current manual version).
- House position reference: §1 main text (FTB relief summary line: 0% to £300k + 5% £300k-£500k + full withdrawal above £500k); §1 main text line 1588 (the joint-purchase-with-BTL-owner trap explicitly flagged); §1.I Wave 9 lock (5% additional dwellings surcharge — interacts with FTB relief). **Same NEW HP-LOCK CANDIDATE as A7: §1.K "First-Time Buyers' Relief — FA 2003 s.57B + Sch 6ZA — current £300k/£500k thresholds + 1 April 2025 reversion + joint-purchase + bare-trust mechanics"** — if Stage 1b conductor creates this lock, A7 + A10 + A11 + A12 all anchor on it.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the legal definition of a "first-time buyer" for SDLT relief? (FA 2003 Sch 6ZA para 6: an individual who has not previously been a purchaser in respect of a major interest in a dwelling situated anywhere in the world AND has not previously held an equivalent interest under specific trust arrangements. Worldwide test is the load-bearing constraint — overseas property ownership disqualifies. Stage 2 verifies the precise para 6 wording at write.)
2. What are the eligibility benefits in cash terms? (Maximum saving £5,000 on a £500,000 purchase by a sole first-time buyer with no surcharge interaction. Saving on a typical £350,000 purchase = £2,500. Worked-example table comparing FTB-relief SDLT vs standard-rate SDLT at £200k / £300k / £400k / £500k / £600k. Stage 2 verifies all figures per §16.35.)
3. What is the joint-purchase rule? (Every joint buyer must qualify as a first-time buyer per Sch 6ZA para 1(4). If one buyer has previously owned a dwelling anywhere in the world, the whole transaction loses FTB relief. The most common failure pattern: spouse / partner / family member who has owned property before joins the purchase. The relief cannot be claimed on the FTB-qualifying-buyer's share — it is an all-or-nothing transaction-level test.)
4. What is the intention-to-occupy requirement? (Sch 6ZA para 1(4) requires the dwelling to be intended as the buyer's only or main residence. Investors, second-home buyers, and pure-letting acquirers cannot claim FTB relief. The intention is tested at the effective date of the transaction; subsequent change of intention does not retrospectively withdraw relief.)
5. What is the "major interest in a single dwelling" requirement? (The transaction must be a major interest — freehold or leasehold of more than 21 years (per FA 2003 s.117). Sub-21-year leases do not engage FTB relief. The "single dwelling" limitation excludes portfolios and any multi-dwelling acquisitions.)
6. How does Sch 6ZA interact with the additional dwellings surcharge? (The surcharge under Sch 4ZA is residential-only and applies to "additional dwellings". FTB relief is unavailable where the surcharge applies — Sch 4ZA disapplies FTB. The trap: buyer A is a genuine first-time buyer; buyer B is a partner who owns a BTL property; joint purchase = no FTB relief AND 5% surcharge on the whole consideration. Cross-link to A7's worked example of this trap.)
7. What is the shared-ownership election? (Sch 6ZA paras 6A-6D: shared-ownership lease purchasers can elect to pay SDLT on the initial share only (capped at the consideration paid for that share, treating the rest as if not acquired for SDLT purposes) OR on a market-value basis treating the full market value as the chargeable consideration. Election interacts with FTB relief because the relevant-consideration test for FTB eligibility differs between the two bases. Worked example.)
8. What does the F(No.2)A 2024 s.8 bare-trust amendment do? (Closes a structuring route where a bare trust held a lease for the benefit of the occupying claimant — formerly used to argue that the trustee, not the claimant, was "the purchaser" for FTB purposes. Para 6(3) inserted by F(No.2)A 2024 s.8 clarifies that the beneficiary's status is what matters. Stage 2 verifies the exact mechanism at write.)
9. What if I had a partial interest from inheritance or gift? (Worldwide-test interaction: inheritance of any major interest in a dwelling, or gift, generally disqualifies the recipient from FTB status if the inheritance / gift was of a major interest. Stage 2 verifies the precise interaction with Sch 6ZA para 6 — the wording is "previously been a purchaser" which on its face excludes inherited / gifted interests, but the broader anti-avoidance architecture may catch certain patterns.)
10. What is HMRC's enquiry posture? (Low-to-moderate. FTB claims are typically supported by transparent paperwork (statutory declaration, conveyancer's confirmation); enquiry focus tends to be on overseas-ownership disclosure for buyers with cross-border family / asset history, and on the joint-purchase eligibility where a partner's prior ownership is ambiguous.)
11. Can I claim FTB relief if I am buying through a company or LLP? (No. Sch 6ZA para 1(4) requires the purchaser to be an individual (or each joint purchaser to be an individual). Corporate buyers, LLPs holding for company members, and trustee structures other than bare trusts within the post-2024 amendment do not qualify.)
