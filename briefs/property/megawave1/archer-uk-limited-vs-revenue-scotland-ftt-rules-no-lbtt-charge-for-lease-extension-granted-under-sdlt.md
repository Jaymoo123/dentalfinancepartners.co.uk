# Archer UK Limited v Revenue Scotland: When a Lease Extension Granted Under SDLT Falls Outside LBTT (FTT Cross-Jurisdictional Boundary Case)

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (jurisdictional cutover): Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013 c. 11 — commencement of LBTT replacing SDLT in Scotland from **1 April 2015**. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/contents on 2026-05-26. After 1 April 2015 Scottish land transactions sit within LBTT; pre-1 April 2015 Scottish transactions were within SDLT (FA 2003).
- Primary (lease variation architecture under SDLT): FA 2003 Schedule 17A para 9 (variation of leases) + Sch 17A para 1 (treatment of leases generally) — verify at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/17A at Stage 2 write. The general SDLT rule is that a variation increasing the term or rent is treated as a surrender-and-regrant for SDLT — a new chargeable transaction. The Archer point turns on whether this surrender-and-regrant treatment carries over for transitional purposes when the variation occurs post-1-April-2015 in Scotland but the underlying lease was a pre-2015 SDLT lease.
- Primary (LBTT lease variation equivalent): LBTT(S)A 2013 Schedule 19 (leases) paras 13-14 (variation of lease term + variation of rent). Verify at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/19 at Stage 2 write.
- Primary (case citation): Archer UK Limited v Revenue Scotland — Scottish First-tier Tribunal Tax Chamber. **Stage 2 sub-agent verifies the precise citation at write time** (likely [2023] FTSTC or [2024] FTSTC reference; the case has been reported in tax-press summaries but the exact tribunal citation must be confirmed against the Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service publication). If citation cannot be confirmed, flag for Stage 1b drift review and write to the statutory framework with case discussed as "Archer UK Limited (Scottish FTT, [date to be confirmed])".
- Supporting: LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 19 para 30 (transitional — leases granted before 1 April 2015 remaining subject to SDLT for the unexpired term unless varied or regranted within LBTT scope); SDLT lease-extension mechanics on the SDLT side under FA 2003 Sch 17A para 9 + s.45 (sub-sale relief, narrowly).
- House position reference: §1 main text (general SDLT rates and cluster); §1.A Wave 7 lock (Sch 15 partnership SLP — narrow relevance, partnership-held leases); existing site coverage `sdlt-leasehold-extension-vs-fresh-purchase` (general lease-extension SDLT mechanics for England/NI). **NO existing §1 sub-section addresses the SDLT-to-LBTT transitional boundary** — **NEW HP-LOCK CANDIDATE: §1.L "SDLT-to-LBTT transitional boundary for Scottish leases granted pre-1-April-2015 — Archer UK Limited principle"** for Stage 1b conductor decision. ## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is a **single-case-analysis page** on the Scottish FTT decision in Archer UK Limited v Revenue Scotland. The site has a general lease-extension page (`sdlt-leasehold-extension-vs-fresh-purchase`, English-law-centric) and Welsh + Scottish LBTT pages that cover Scottish LBTT generally; it does NOT yet have a transitional-jurisdiction-boundary page nor any single-case-analysis for the SDLT-to-LBTT cutover. The Archer case turns on a narrow but recurring fact pattern: a commercial lease granted pre-1 April 2015 in Scotland (so the original grant was within SDLT and SDLT was paid at that time); the lease is varied or extended after 1 April 2015 in such a way that the standard surrender-and-regrant analysis would, on the LBTT statutory regime, generate a fresh chargeable LBTT transaction; the taxpayer argues that the variation falls within the transitional preservation of the SDLT character of the original lease and is therefore outside LBTT charge. The FTT ruled in the taxpayer's favour on the specific facts before it.

This page covers four things competitor and tax-press coverage routinely under-serves: (a) the **statutory architecture of the SDLT-to-LBTT cutover** (LBTT(S)A 2013 commencement + Sch 19 para 30 transitional preservation for unexpired pre-2015 SDLT lease terms); (b) the **lease-variation surrender-and-regrant doctrine** as it operates under SDLT (FA 2003 Sch 17A para 9) versus LBTT (LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 19); (c) **how Archer applies the transitional in practice** — what fact-pattern features tipped the FTT toward no-LBTT-charge (likely: limited variation, no rent uplift, term extension within original lease's contemplation, no fresh consideration); and (d) the **planning point for portfolio landlords with Scottish leases predating 1 April 2015** (the SDLT-to-LBTT transitional is narrow but live; any post-2015 variation should be tested against the Archer-style analysis before assuming LBTT applies).

Distinguish from the existing `sdlt-leasehold-extension-vs-fresh-purchase` page by **jurisdictional scope** (Scotland-only, transitional fact pattern) and by **case-analysis primacy** (Archer is a single FTT decision; the page leads with the ratio rather than the general statutory framework). ## Key questions this page must answer

1. What was Archer UK Limited v Revenue Scotland about? (Single-paragraph case summary: a commercial lease originally granted pre-1 April 2015 — within SDLT — was later varied post-LBTT-commencement; the question was whether the variation triggered LBTT under the surrender-and-regrant doctrine, or whether the transitional preservation of the original SDLT lease excluded the variation from LBTT charge. The FTT ruled no LBTT charge.)
2. When does the SDLT/LBTT transitional even apply? (LBTT(S)A 2013 commencement on 1 April 2015. Scottish leases granted before that date sat within SDLT; the unexpired term continued to be SDLT-character under the transitional in Sch 19 para 30 — verify exact paragraph at Stage 2.)
3. What is the SDLT surrender-and-regrant doctrine for lease variations? (FA 2003 Sch 17A para 9: variation increasing the term or rent treated as surrender of the existing lease and grant of a new lease, generating a fresh chargeable SDLT transaction on the new lease.)
4. What is the equivalent LBTT doctrine? (LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 19 paras 13-14: similar surrender-and-regrant treatment under LBTT, but with the threshold and rate architecture of LBTT.)
5. Why did Archer fall outside LBTT? (FTT analysis: the variation was made under and consistent with the original pre-2015 SDLT lease; the transitional preserves the SDLT character of the lease; absent a fresh consideration or term-extension exceeding the transitional preservation, no LBTT transaction arose. The case turns on the precise statutory wording of the transitional and the fact-pattern features that took the variation outside LBTT scope.)
6. Does Archer mean any pre-2015 Scottish lease variation is outside LBTT? (No. Archer is fact-specific. Variations involving fresh consideration, term extensions beyond the original transitional preservation, or substantive recharacterisation are all likely within LBTT. 7. What is the planning point for portfolio landlords? (Any Scottish commercial or residential lease originally granted pre-1 April 2015 sits within SDLT for its unexpired term; post-2015 variations should be tested under Archer-style analysis BEFORE filing LBTT returns; in marginal cases consider an advance ruling application to Revenue Scotland.)
8. What does this mean for buyers of Scottish portfolios with mixed-vintage leases? (Due diligence should distinguish pre-2015 from post-2015 leases; the SDLT-versus-LBTT line affects both ongoing liabilities and future variation events.)
9. Does the Archer principle have any read-across to Welsh LTT? (Wales LTT commenced 1 April 2018; analogous transitional preservation under LTTA 2017.
