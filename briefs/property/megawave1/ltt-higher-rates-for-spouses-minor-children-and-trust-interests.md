# Welsh LTT Higher Rates: How Spouse, Minor Child and Trust Interests Count Against You

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: **Land Transaction Tax and Anti-avoidance of Devolved Taxes (Wales) Act 2017, Schedule 5 (higher rates residential property transactions)** at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/anaw/2017/1/schedule/5`. Key attribution and aggregation paragraphs verified verbatim against legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-26:
  - **Sch 5 para 5(4)(b)** — joint entitlement as tenants in common, spouse / civil partner aggregation: where the buyer and the buyer's spouse or civil partner are "living together" and "taken together…are entitled as tenants in common", they are treated as one buyer.
  - **Sch 5 para 5(6)** — joint tenants, spouse / civil partner aggregation: spouses / civil partners "living together" who are beneficially entitled as joint tenants are "treated as one joint tenant".
  - **Sch 5 para 25(3)** — interpretation of "living together" (cohabitation and not separated under a court order or in circumstances making the separation likely to be permanent).
  - **Sch 5 paras 7-8 and 16-17** — main-residence exceptions (the buyer's only or main residence carve-out for replacement-of-main-residence scenarios, single-dwelling and joint-buyer variants).
  - **Sch 5 paras 19-22** — non-individual buyer (company, trustee of certain settlements) special rules: where the buyer is not an individual, the higher rates apply automatically subject to the local-authority and other narrow exceptions in paras 20A / 21A.
  - **Sch 5 para 24** — STAGE 2 MUST VERIFY: confirm whether LTTA 2017 Sch 5 contains a minor-children attribution provision equivalent to **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 12** ("the spouse or civil partner of P, and any child under the age of 18 of P or of P's spouse or civil partner"). The visible Sch 5 text does not surface a free-standing minor-children-of-buyer attribution paragraph at write time. Stage 2 must read the full Sch 5 (paras 1-29 and any post-enactment amendments) and resolve whether minor-children dwellings are attributed by direct statutory rule, by the settlements / bare trust paragraphs, or are not attributed at all (a material divergence from SDLT, if so).
- Supporting: **LTTA 2017 s.24** ("Regulations specifying tax bands and tax rates"), verified verbatim on 2026-05-26. **LTTA 2017 s.72** (meaning of residential / non-residential property — the residential character of the property is the precondition for Sch 5 engagement). **Land Transaction Tax (Tax Bands and Tax Rates) (Wales) (Amendment) Regulations 2024** — higher-rate uplift across all six bands by 1 percentage point with effect from 11 December 2024 (5% / 8.5% / 10% / 12.5% / 15% / 17% standalone band structure per §23.2). **TCMA 2016 ss.41 + 63 + 78** (12-month return amendment and 4-year overpayment-claim repayment routes where higher rates were paid in error or replacement-of-main-residence subsequently triggers refund per Sch 5 para 23).
- House position reference: **§23.2** (Welsh LTT higher residential rates, standalone band structure starting at 5% from £1 rather than a flat surcharge on standard bands, 1 percentage point uplift from 11 December 2024); **§23.5** comparison-side (Scottish ADS joint-buyer aggregation under LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 2A para 5(2)); **§23.8** cross-jurisdictional traps; **§24** Form 17 + joint-ownership mechanics (cross-references the LTTA 2017 Sch 5 para 5 aggregation rules from the income-tax angle). **§1.A / §1.B** Wave 7 SDLT cluster locks (linked-transaction discipline carries over to LTT under LTTA 2017 s.65 jurisdictional definition per §1.B cross-border note).

This B8 page sits **alongside but distinct from** that page: it leads on the **attribution patterns** that catch buyers out — a spouse who already owns a property in their sole name, a parent buying for a teenage child, a bare trust the buyer is the beneficiary of, a discretionary settlement with the buyer as a named beneficiary. Where the Wave 5 mechanics page treats spousal aggregation as one item in a list of rules, this page is built around it.

The distinguishing angle is **the four patterns where attribution is decided by statute rather than common sense**: (a) the spouse-or-civil-partner-aggregation under Sch 5 para 5(4)(b) and 5(6), which catches buyers who genuinely thought they were buying in their sole name and that their spouse's separately-owned BTL would not aggregate; (b) the minor-children pattern — Stage 2 must resolve whether Welsh LTT directly attributes a minor child's dwelling to the parent buyer (FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 12 does so for SDLT; LTTA 2017 Sch 5 may or may not have an equivalent) or whether attribution operates only via bare trust / settlement routes; (c) the bare trust scenario, where a buyer is the beneficiary of a bare trust over a property already held — the dwelling is attributable to the beneficial owner under the general settlements / bare-trust framework; (d) the discretionary settlement where the buyer is a beneficiary among a class — generally non-attributed unless the buyer has a present interest in possession or is a defined sub-class.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What does "higher rates residential property transaction" mean under LTTA 2017 Sch 5 paras 1-3 in the family-and-trust context, and how does the test bite where the buyer has multiple relevant interests counting against them at the effective date?
2. When does a buyer's spouse's or civil partner's separately-owned dwelling aggregate against them under Sch 5 para 5(4)(b) (tenants in common) and 5(6) (joint tenants)? What does "living together" mean under para 25(3) and what scenarios remove a spouse from the aggregation rule (court-ordered separation, separation in circumstances making it likely to be permanent)?
3. **Stage 2 critical question:** Does Welsh LTT attribute a minor child's dwelling to the parent buyer in the way FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 12 does for SDLT? Verify the LTTA 2017 Sch 5 text and either cite the equivalent paragraph or explain the absence (and how the parent's beneficial-ownership position may still bring a child-held dwelling into account under the bare-trust / settlement rules).
4. What is the position where the buyer is a beneficiary of a bare trust over a property? The bare-trust beneficial-owner principle (Sch 5 para 8 [Stage 2 verifies the exact paragraph] and the general TCMA / LTTA interaction with the law of trusts) treats the beneficiary as the owner of the dwelling for the purposes of the higher-rates test.
5. What is the position where the buyer is a beneficiary of a discretionary settlement that includes a dwelling? Standard line: no attribution unless the buyer has a present interest in possession (life interest, settled interest in possession by appointment, or otherwise vested interest in the dwelling). The page must distinguish between the discretionary class beneficiary (not attributed) and the interest-in-possession beneficiary (attributed).
6. Where a parent buys a dwelling intended for occupation by an adult child — gifting or selling the property to the child after purchase, or holding the property in the parent's name with the child paying market rent — what is the higher-rates consequence at the purchase date? (Standard: higher rates apply if the parent already owns another dwelling; the post-purchase arrangement does not retroactively remove the surcharge.)
7. How does the replacement-of-main-residence rule (Sch 5 para 8(2)(b) / para 17(2)(b) — the 36-month "permitted period") interact with spousal aggregation? Where a married couple sells one home and buys another, only the disposed dwelling needs to satisfy the main-residence test; spousal-aggregation does not require both spouses to have lived in the same prior home simultaneously.
8. What is the position for joint buyers under Sch 5 para 5 where one is single and one is married (and the spouse owns a dwelling)? The married buyer's spousal-aggregation applies; the single buyer's interest counts on its face. The page must walk this combination explicitly.
9. What is the position where the buyer's spouse holds a dwelling in trust for a third party (e.g. the spouse is the legal trustee of a bare trust for a parent or sibling)? The legal-title-as-trustee position does not give the spouse beneficial ownership, so spousal aggregation should not bite — but the FTT line is sparse and Stage 2 should confirm WRA's published interpretation.
10. What are the procedural mechanics where higher rates were paid in error on a family-and-trust pattern: amendment under TCMA 2016 s.41 within 12 months of filing, overpayment claim under s.63 within 4 years of filing (s.78 time limit), Welsh Revenue Authority review, and Welsh tribunal appeal route?
