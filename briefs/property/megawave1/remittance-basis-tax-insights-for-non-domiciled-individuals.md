# Remittance Basis Tax Insights for Non-Domiciled Individuals: What Ended on 6 April 2025, What Replaced It, and the FIG / TRF / Rebasing Architecture That Now Governs UK Property Investors with Foreign Income

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (abolition): **FA 2025 s.40 + Schedule 9** — the remittance basis is unavailable for tax year 2025-26 and subsequent tax years. Section heading: "Remittance basis not available after tax year 2024-25." Sch 9 para 1 effects the disapplication; later paragraphs of Sch 9 strip out the historic deemed-dom architecture (ITA 2007 Part 14 Chapter A1) and remove the s.809B / s.809C / s.809D claim machinery from prospective use. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/8/section/40 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (replacement — FIG regime): **ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J** (inserted by FA 2025 s.37 + Sch 8) — 4-tax-year exemption for foreign income and gains for qualifying new residents (10-tax-year prior-non-residence gateway under s.845B(1)(b); per-year claim under s.845A; loss of personal allowance + dividend allowance + CGT AEA in any tax year the claim is made; claim deadline 12 months from 31 January following end of relevant tax year). Cross-referenced to HP §17.8.
- Primary (replacement — TRF): **FA 2025 s.41 + Schedule 10** — Temporary Repatriation Facility allowing the pre-2025-26 remittance-basis cohort to designate qualifying overseas capital at fixed rates (2025-26: 12%; 2026-27: 12%; 2027-28: 15%) — three designation scenarios under Sch 10 para 2(2) (pre-window unremitted accumulation), para 2(5) (in-window remittance), and para 2(8) (residual pre-6-April-2025 offshore capital). Designation deadline 12 months from 31 January after end of relevant tax year. Window closes 5 April 2028. Cross-referenced to HP §17.9.
- Primary (replacement — CGT rebasing): **FA 2025 s.42 + Schedule 11** — narrow election to rebase non-UK assets to 5 April 2017 market value, available only to individuals who claimed remittance basis at any time in 2017-18 to 2024-25 and were neither UK-domiciled nor deemed-domiciled on 5 April 2017. Election made on disposal-by-disposal basis. Most departing UK landlords will NOT qualify (HP §17.10 do-not-write). Cross-referenced to HP §17.10.
- Primary (replacement — IHT residence-based regime): **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C + ss.267ZC-267ZF** (inserted by FA 2025 architecture; LTR / Long-Term Resident test = 10 of the 20 preceding tax years for individuals aged 20+, with s.6B young-person variation). Cross-referenced to HP §22.X and §15.6.
- Supporting (Statutory Residence Test): **FA 2013 Schedule 45** — the statutory residence test continues to govern UK residence (which is the operative concept post-2025; domicile no longer governs income / CGT). Cross-referenced to HP §17.1 + §17.2 (split-year).
- Supporting (historic): **ITA 2007 ss.809A-809Z10** (the historic remittance-basis architecture) — closed to new claims from 2025-26 per FA 2025 s.40; the sections remain on the statute book for pre-6-April-2025 source-tracing purposes (relevant when designating under TRF Scenario A or B).
- House position reference: **HP §17.6** (headline framing — what ended, what replaced it). **HP §17.8** (FIG operational depth — ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J). **HP §17.9** (TRF Sch 10 mechanics). **HP §17.10** (CGT rebasing narrow eligibility). **HP §22.X** (IHT LTR test). **HP §15.6** (IHT non-resident domicile and residence test). The page is fully HP-anchored; no NEW HP LOCK NEEDED.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What was the remittance basis and when did it end? (A 200+ year UK income-tax concession: foreign-domiciled UK residents could elect under ITA 2007 s.809B / s.809D to be taxed in the UK only on foreign income / gains actually remitted to the UK, leaving foreign-source unremitted amounts outside UK tax. Closed to new claims from 6 April 2025 by FA 2025 s.40 + Sch 9. The pre-2025-26 legacy claims continue to operate for source-tracing under the TRF designation mechanism.)
2. Why was it abolished? (Policy: residence-based fairness alignment with most OECD jurisdictions; revenue: targeted post-Brexit fiscal recalibration; political: Spring Budget 2024 announcement followed by Autumn 2024 transitional extension (TRF rate ramp), with operative legislation in FA 2025. The reform was first-substantively-announced 6 March 2024 and became law on 20 March 2025.)
3. What replaced the remittance basis for new arrivals? (The Foreign Income and Gains (FIG) regime under ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J — 4-year full exemption for qualifying new residents (10-year prior-non-residence gateway), but the claim is per-year and forfeits personal allowance + dividend allowance + CGT AEA in any year claimed. See deep-dive at `/blog/non-dom-reform-april-2025-fig-regime-property-investors` and `/blog/fig-year-5-cliff-post-fig-arising-basis-planning-non-doms-landlords`.)
4. What replaced it for the legacy non-dom cohort with pre-2025 unremitted amounts? (The Temporary Repatriation Facility (TRF) under FA 2025 s.41 + Sch 10 — 3-year window with rates 12% / 12% / 15% closing 5 April 2028; three designation scenarios under Sch 10 para 2 cover pre-window unremitted accumulation, in-window remittance, and the residual pre-6-April-2025 offshore-capital scenario. See deep-dive at `/blog/trf-qualifying-overseas-capital-what-can-be-designated-non-doms`.)
5. Does the abolition affect UK rental income for non-doms? (No — UK-source rental income has ALWAYS been UK-arising-basis taxable regardless of domicile or remittance basis. The remittance basis was a foreign-income concession. Non-dom landlords with UK property paid UK income tax on UK rental income pre-2025 and continue to pay UK income tax on UK rental income post-2025. The abolition affects FOREIGN-source rental income (e.g. rental income from overseas property held personally by a UK-resident non-dom) — that income is now arising-basis taxable from year 5 onwards (or from year 1 if FIG not claimed / not eligible).)
6. What about CGT on overseas property? (Historic remittance-basis claimants could keep overseas gains outside UK tax until remitted. From 6 April 2025: gains are arising-basis taxable from year 1 of UK residence unless FIG-eligible (max 4 years of full exemption). Disposals on or after 6 April 2025 by individuals who historically claimed remittance basis MAY qualify for the FA 2025 Sch 11 rebasing election to 5 April 2017 value — but eligibility is narrow (must have claimed remittance basis at some point 2017-18 to 2024-25 AND must not have been UK-domiciled or deemed-domiciled on 5 April 2017). See `/blog/cgt-rebasing-election-fa-2025-schedule-11-narrow-eligibility-non-doms`.)
7. What about IHT — does domicile still matter? (For deaths on or after 6 April 2025: the historic domicile-based IHT regime is replaced by a residence-based "Long-Term Resident" (LTR) test under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C — non-UK-situs assets are inside UK IHT if the individual has been UK-resident for 10 of the preceding 20 tax years (with s.6B young-person variation for under-20s). Worldwide-IHT footprint is therefore now driven by residence pattern, not by historic domicile or deemed-dom 15-of-20 status. See `/blog/uk-iht-non-residents-10-year-rule-long-term-resident-architecture` if extant; cross-reference to HP §22.X.)
8. What about trust structures created under the historic non-dom regime? (Excluded-property trust treatment continues for trusts created BEFORE the individual settlor became a long-term resident under IHTA 1984 s.48ZA (inserted by FA 2025). Trusts created after LTR-status onset get full UK IHT exposure on settlor-attribution. This is the most complex transitional area; specialist trust review is essential for any non-dom settlor approaching 10-year UK residence.)
9. Can the remittance basis still be relevant in any way after 6 April 2025? (Yes — for the LEGACY cohort, the historic remittance-basis architecture remains source-traceable. TRF Scenario A requires the amount to be unremitted at the date of designation (i.e. still un-remitted under the historic remittance-basis tracking); TRF Scenario B requires the amount to be remitted during the 2025-26 to 2027-28 window; Scenario C captures non-income / non-gain offshore capital held immediately before 6 April 2025. Source-tracing back to historic remittance-basis claim years is therefore still operational for TRF designation through to 5 April 2028.)
10. What should a non-dom property investor do RIGHT NOW? (Three immediate decisions: (a) if a new arrival 2025-26 onwards — quantify foreign income / gains expected over 4 years vs personal-allowance + CGT-AEA value, then decide year-by-year whether to claim FIG; (b) if legacy non-dom with pre-2025 unremitted balance — model the TRF Scenario A/B/C choice against later-life remittance scenarios; the 12% (2025-26 / 2026-27) and 15% (2027-28) rates compare favourably with future remittance under normal rates; (c) if approaching the 10-year LTR threshold — review trust structures, asset situs, and worldwide-asset disclosure planning. Cross-link to firm's expat / non-dom advisory landing page.)
