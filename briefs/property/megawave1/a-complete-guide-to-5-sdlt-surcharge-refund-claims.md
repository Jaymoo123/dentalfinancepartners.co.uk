# A Complete Guide to 5% SDLT Surcharge Refund Claims: Every Route, Every Deadline, Every Failure Mode

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: FA 2003 Schedule 4ZA (Higher rates for additional dwellings) — full architecture: para 1 (charge); para 3 (single-dwelling buyer conditions); para 3(7) replacement-of-main-residence refund condition (3-year window); paras 4-6 (joint purchaser rules); paras 7-9 (trustee / settlement rules); paras 10-15 (married couple / civil partner aggregation); para 16 (inherited interest ≤50% share / ≤£40k value exclusion); paras 17-18 (anti-avoidance). Stage 2 must verify para citations at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/4ZA on write.
- Primary (rate change): Finance (No.2) Act 2024 c.12 raised the additional dwellings surcharge from 3% to 5% with effect from 31 October 2024 (Autumn Budget 2024). House position §1 confirms; verified.
- Procedural anchors: FA 2003 Sch 11A para 6 (12-month amendment of return window — the standard route for the replacement-of-main-residence refund); FA 2003 Sch 10 para 34 (overpayment relief — 4-year window — the catch-all for Bewley / dwelling-misclassification / mixed-use re-characterisation refunds). Stage 2 verify exact paras and windows at write.
- Supporting: FA 2003 s.116(1)(a) (residential property definition — load-bearing for Bewley refund route); FA 2003 s.116(7) (6+-dwellings auto non-residential — relevant for portfolio buyers who paid surcharge in error); FA 2003 s.44 (substantial performance — load-bearing for the "from what date" question on the 3-year window); HMRC SDLTM09807 (exceptional circumstances framework for 3-year window extension); HMRC SDLTM09730+ (Schedule 4ZA general).
- House position reference: §1 main text (5% surcharge effective 31 October 2024; surviving alternative routes to mitigate); §1.C Wave 7 lock (Bewley uninhabitable test as a refund route — narrow post-Bewley line via Hyman / Mudan / MHB / Brown); §1.D Wave 7 lock (Sch 3 para 3 spouse/civil-partner transfers on divorce — relevant for Sch 4ZA aggregation refund scenarios). ## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **comprehensive refund-route decision tree** for the 5% additional dwellings surcharge. The site already carries two adjacent refund pages: `sdlt-5-percent-surcharge-refund-claim-process` (Wave 9, focused on the **3-year replacement-of-main-residence claim process** — the dominant refund route, walks through SDLTM09807 exceptional circumstances) and `sdlt-additional-property-surcharge-refund-routes-3-year-replacement-claim` (focused on **replacement-claim route plumbing**). This A4 page sits in a **third lane**: it is the **decision-tree umbrella** that asks the reader the four-question diagnostic to identify **which** of the available refund routes applies, then routes them to the specific route page for the detailed mechanics. The page covers six routes that fragmented coverage misses: (1) **replacement of main residence** under Sch 4ZA para 3(7) — the dominant route, but cross-link to the existing dedicated page for mechanics; (2) **Bewley uninhabitable misclassification** under s.116(1)(a) — refund via Sch 10 para 34 overpayment relief, post-Bewley narrow application per §1.C; (3) **dwelling-count dispute** — annexes / granny flats wrongly counted as separate dwellings inflating Sch 4ZA exposure, refund via overpayment relief; (4) **mixed-use re-characterisation** — surcharge paid on residential basis but transaction is actually mixed-use (e.g. with commercial element), refund via overpayment relief; (5) **6+-dwellings auto-deeming missed** under s.116(7) — portfolio buyer wrongly paid residential rates plus surcharge on a 6+-dwelling acquisition, statutory deeming entitles refund; (6) **surcharge-rate-transition straddle** — transactions with effective date crossing the 31 October 2024 boundary may have paid wrong-rate surcharge (3% vs 5%), refund via amendment.

The page is **diagnostic-led not process-led**: the existing site pages cover the dominant claim process well; this page's job is to **stop a reader pursuing the wrong route**. The diagnostic flow asks: (a) Did you sell your previous main residence within 3 years of the new purchase? → replacement route. (b) Was the property substantially structurally dangerous, contaminated, or requiring complete reconstruction at the effective date? → Bewley overpayment relief. (c) Did you count an annexe or granny flat as a separate dwelling for surcharge purposes? → dwelling-count overpayment relief. (d) Did the transaction have a material non-residential component? → mixed-use overpayment relief. (e) Were you buying 6+ dwellings in a single transaction? → s.116(7) statutory deeming. (f) Did your effective date straddle 31 October 2024? → rate-transition amendment.

Distinguish from the Wave 9 page by **scope** (this is a multi-route umbrella, not a single-route deep-dive) and from the Wave 7 / earlier refund-routes page by **diagnostic orientation** (decision-tree-first not route-first). Target length 2,800-3,400 words to give each of the six routes a fair section.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the 5% additional dwellings surcharge and when did the rate change from 3%? (FA 2003 Sch 4ZA + Autumn Budget 2024 transition on 31 October 2024 — verified per §1.)
2. What are the six routes to a refund of the 5% surcharge? (Diagnostic decision-tree summary at the top of the page.)
3. Route 1 — replacement of main residence (Sch 4ZA para 3(7)): when does it apply, what is the 3-year window, what is the SDLTM09807 exceptional-circumstances extension? (Brief here; cross-link to dedicated page for mechanics.)
4. Route 2 — Bewley uninhabitable misclassification (s.116(1)(a) + Sch 10 para 34): when does it apply post-Hyman / Mudan / MHB / Brown narrowing? Evidential threshold (surveyor reports at effective date; structural dangerousness; contamination).
5. Route 3 — dwelling-count overpayment relief: annexes / granny flats wrongly counted, with the historical MDR annexe-as-dwelling jurisprudence (Fiander, Brewer, Doe) providing context — under Sch 4ZA the analogous question is whether the annexe is a "separate dwelling" within Sch 4ZA para 2 reading with s.116(1)(a). Cross-link to A3 historical MDR page for the annexe-definition jurisprudence.
6. Route 4 — mixed-use re-characterisation (overpayment relief, narrow post-Hyman): post-Hyman v HMRC [2022] EWCA Civ 185 the test for mixed-use is significantly tightened; most retrofit mixed-use claims fail. When does this route still work?
7. Route 5 — 6+-dwellings s.116(7) statutory deeming missed: portfolio buyer paid residential plus surcharge in error on a 6+-dwelling single transaction; statutory entitlement to non-residential treatment; refund via amendment or overpayment relief depending on timing.
8. Route 6 — surcharge-rate-transition straddle (31 October 2024): if effective date crossed the transition, was the right rate applied? Substantial-performance analysis under s.44 may move the effective date and thus the applicable rate.
9. Which deadline applies to which route? (12-month amendment window under Sch 11A para 6 for in-time corrections; 4-year overpayment relief under Sch 10 para 34 for out-of-amendment-window claims; the 3-year replacement-of-main-residence window has its own statutory deadline.)
10. What is HMRC's enquiry posture on each route? (Replacement route — mostly process-led, low enquiry risk if conditions clearly met; Bewley — high enquiry risk, evidential burden on claimant; dwelling-count — moderate enquiry risk; mixed-use — high enquiry risk post-Hyman; s.116(7) — low enquiry risk if dwelling-count clearly 6+ and single-transaction; rate-transition — moderate enquiry risk, evidential burden on effective-date analysis.)
11. What happens if you pursue the wrong route? (Wasted-claim risk; missed-deadline risk if the right route had a shorter window; how to convert a wrong-route claim to a right-route claim without losing the deadline.)
