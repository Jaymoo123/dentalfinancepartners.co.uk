# Impact of FHL Tax Abolition on Pension Contributions: Key Insights and Strategies

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Finance Act 2025 Schedule 5 Part 1 ("Amendments relating to income tax"), Part 5 paragraph 12 commencement (tax year 2025-26 onwards), verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/8/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26. The mechanic of pension-contribution impact runs through the omission of FHL trading-equivalence at ITTOIA 2005 ss.322-328A (now omitted by FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 1) and the consequential effect on Finance Act 2004 s.189(2)(ba) "relevant UK earnings" (which historically included FHL profit by virtue of the ITTOIA trade-treatment override).
- Supporting: FA 2004 s.189(2) definition of "relevant UK earnings" for pension-input purposes (employment income, trading income, FHL profit pre-abolition, certain patent royalties); FA 2004 s.190 "earnings limit" mechanic (the lower of relevant UK earnings or the annual allowance gates tax-relieved input); FA 2004 s.188 "active member" + "relevant UK individual" definitions; FA 2004 s.227 annual allowance (£60,000 from 6 April 2023); FA 2004 s.228ZA tapered annual allowance mechanism (taper begins at adjusted income >£260,000 + threshold income >£200,000; floor £10,000); FA 2004 s.230 carry-forward (three-year carry-forward of unused annual allowance, subject to relevant-UK-earnings limit in current year).
- House position reference: §6 "FHL — abolition transition" (the "pension earnings" element of the locked do-not-write list — pension earnings include FHL profits "only until 5 April 2025"); §8 "Lifetime Allowance — abolished, replaced framework" (LSA £268,275 / LSDBA £1,073,100 / annual allowance £60,000 — current pension architecture against which abolition impact must be modelled).

From 6 April 2025 that disappears, and the body of the page works through the practical strategies that restore pension headroom: (1) the £3,600 floor that survives regardless of earnings, (2) other-source relevant UK earnings (employment, self-employment, residual genuine trading activity), (3) salary-from-a-limited-company route (incorporate, take salary above the secondary threshold, contribute via company), (4) employer pension contributions from a limited company holding former FHL (the company-side route, not subject to relevant-UK-earnings limit but constrained by the wholly-and-exclusively gateway and the company's annual allowance), (5) SIPP commercial-property purchase (using accumulated pension wealth to buy commercial property occupied by an unconnected trading tenant — completely distinct from the holiday-let activity but a frequent next-step for former FHL owners with pension pots). ## Key questions this page must answer

1. Why did FHL profit count as "relevant UK earnings" for pension purposes before abolition, and what specifically changed at 6 April 2025? (Pre-abolition: ITTOIA 2005 ss.322-328A treated qualifying FHL as a separate property business with trade-equivalence overrides — including the FA 2004 s.189(2) "relevant UK earnings" reach. FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 1 omits the ITTOIA overrides; the trade-equivalence drops away; FHL profit now sits in the ordinary UK or overseas property business and is NOT relevant UK earnings.)
2. What is the practical impact for a landlord whose only earned income was their FHL? (The tax-relieved contribution limit collapses to £3,600 gross per year — the FA 2004 floor available regardless of earnings. Anything above that is unrelieved on the way in. For higher-rate-taxpayer former FHL owners who were paying £40,000+ per year in tax-relieved contributions, this is a £16,000+ annual tax-relief loss.)
3. The £3,600 gross floor — what does it actually give me? (Personal contribution of £2,880 net; provider reclaims £720 basic rate at source; further higher-rate relief through SA100. Available to any "relevant UK individual" — UK resident, under 75, regardless of earnings. Survives FHL abolition entirely.)
4. What else counts as relevant UK earnings now? (Employment income from PAYE; self-employment trading income from a genuine trade — not ordinary property letting; residual FHL profit only for periods of account ending before 6 April 2025; certain patent royalties under FA 2004 s.189(2)(c). Property rental income from former FHL does NOT count.)
5. If I incorporate my former FHL, does pension headroom come back? (Yes — two routes. (a) Personal contributions from salary taken from the company create relevant UK earnings up to the salary amount. (b) Employer contributions from the company are not subject to the relevant-UK-earnings limit but must satisfy wholly-and-exclusively under CTA 2009 s.54 and the annual allowance still binds at the individual level under FA 2004 s.227.)
6. SIPP commercial-property purchase — is this an answer to losing FHL pension relief? (Partial answer. Once a SIPP holds commercial property let to an unconnected trading tenant, the rents flow into the pension fund tax-free and accelerate fund growth. But: cannot purchase residential property; cannot purchase from a connected party at undervalue; cannot let to self or family. So a former FHL holiday cottage cannot be SIPP-bought. A SIPP commercial-property purchase is a separate post-abolition strategy, not a substitute mechanism for the same asset.)
7. What about the carry-forward of unused annual allowance? (FA 2004 s.230 three-year carry-forward survives — but it is still gated by current-year relevant-UK-earnings under s.190. Carrying forward £180,000 of unused annual allowance is useless if the current year's relevant UK earnings are nil.)
8. Anti-forestalling — did anyone successfully accelerate contributions into 2024/25 before abolition bit? (The contribution timing is irrelevant to abolition; the question is whether profit arose in a period of account ending before 6 April 2025 and was therefore last-year FHL. Operators who advanced their accounting period end to capture maximum profit in 2024/25 would have retained the relevant-UK-earnings treatment for that profit, but for 2025/26 forward the regime change is unconditional.)
9. Tapered annual allowance — does FHL abolition help anyone? (Marginally yes for some operators: removing FHL profit from "threshold income" and "adjusted income" computations under FA 2004 s.228ZA can pull a landlord out of the £200,000/£260,000 taper window. The mechanic is fiddly — Stage 2 worked example.)
10. What should an individual former-FHL owner actually do in 2025/26? (Decision tree: continue £3,600 floor; check whether employment or other-source earnings give additional headroom; model the incorporation route if portfolio is leveraged + higher-rate; consider SIPP commercial-property purchase as a separate strategy.)
