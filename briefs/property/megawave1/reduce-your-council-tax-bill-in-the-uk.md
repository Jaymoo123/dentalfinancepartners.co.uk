# How to Legitimately Reduce Your Council Tax Bill in the UK: Discounts, Exemptions, Banding Challenges, Disability-Band Reductions, and the Council Tax Reduction Scheme — the Complete LGFA 1992 + SI Framework Walkthrough

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the discounts — LGFA 1992 s.11): the principal statutory discounts under LGFA 1992 s.11:
  - **s.11(1)(a) — single-occupant 25% discount.** Where there is only one resident in the dwelling who does not fall to be disregarded under Sch 1, the council tax is reduced by 25%. The "appropriate percentage" is fixed at 25% by s.11(3). Applies regardless of band, income, or wealth — it is a flat statutory discount.
  - **s.11(2) — no-resident 50% discount (subject to LA discretion).** Where no resident or all residents are disregarded, the council tax is reduced by 50% (twice the appropriate percentage). Note: SI 2003/3011 Class A + Class C and the LGFA 2012 + LGFA 2003 amendments give billing authorities discretion to vary the 50% — many LAs reduce the empty-property discount to 0% or set it short (e.g. one month).
  - **Sch 1 disregards** — Schedule 1 lists persons disregarded for the purposes of discount: full-time students, student nurses, apprentices, persons receiving care, persons providing care, persons in detention, persons resident in hospitals + care homes, persons severely mentally impaired (s.4 + Sch 1), 18 + 19 year olds in respect of whom child benefit is payable, members of religious communities, foreign-language assistants, members of visiting forces, diplomats. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/14/section/11 on 2026-05-26 (note: amendments effective 1 April 2026 may add "in England" qualifiers — Stage 2 verifies).
- Primary (the exemptions — SI 1992/558 Council Tax (Exempt Dwellings) Order 1992): list of CLASSES of exempt dwellings — entirely exempt from council tax for the period the class applies. Headline classes (Stage 2 verifies current list — many classes have been modified or substantially abolished by later SIs):
  - **Class A (uninhabitable / under major repair)** — substantially abolished / made discretionary in England by 2013 reforms; check current state.
  - **Class B (unoccupied dwellings owned by a charity)** — up to 6 months.
  - **Class C (empty + unfurnished)** — substantially abolished / made discretionary; check current state.
  - **Class D (left unoccupied by person in detention)**.
  - **Class E (left unoccupied by person residing in a hospital / care home)**.
  - **Class F (deceased owner — pending probate / grant of letters)** — up to 6 months after grant.
  - **Class G (occupation prohibited by law)**.
  - **Class H (held for a minister of religion)**.
  - **Class I (left unoccupied by person receiving care elsewhere)**.
  - **Class J (left unoccupied by person providing care elsewhere)**.
  - **Class K (left unoccupied by a student)**.
  - **Class L (mortgagee in possession)**.
  - **Class M (HMO occupied solely by certain persons disregarded under Sch 1)** — note interaction with SI 2023/1175 HMO single-dwelling rule.
  - **Class N (occupied solely by full-time students)** — the principal "student exemption."
  - **Class O (armed forces accommodation)**.
  - **Class P (visiting forces accommodation)**.
  - **Class Q (left unoccupied by a bankrupt or trustee in bankruptcy)**.
  - **Class R (unoccupied pitches / moorings)**.
  - **Class S (occupied solely by persons under 18)**.
  - **Class T (annex unable to be let separately due to planning condition)**.
  - **Class U (occupied solely by persons severely mentally impaired)**.
  - **Class V (occupied by diplomat exempt)**.
  - **Class W (annex occupied by dependant relative of main-dwelling occupier)**.
  Stage 2 verifies current SI 1992/558 contents and any class abolitions or modifications.
- Primary (disabled-band reduction — SI 1992/554 Council Tax (Reductions for Disabilities) Regulations 1992): a separately-banded reduction where a substantially and permanently disabled person resides in the dwelling AND the dwelling has either (a) a room (other than bathroom / kitchen / toilet) predominantly used by and required by the disabled person; OR (b) an additional bathroom or kitchen required for meeting the needs of the disabled person; OR (c) sufficient floor space for use of a wheelchair indoors. Effect: council tax assessed at one band lower than the actual band (Band A receives a reduction calculated as 5/9ths of the Band B charge). Verify at Stage 2 against current SI 1992/554.
- Primary (Council Tax Reduction Scheme — CTR): LGFA 1992 Sch 1A (England) + Sch 1B (Wales) — inserted by the Local Government Finance Act 2012. CTR replaced the centrally-administered Council Tax Benefit in 2013. Pensioner CTR is centrally-prescribed under the Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Prescribed Requirements) (England) Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/2885). Working-age CTR is locally-determined — each LA designs its own scheme. The CTR scheme is means-tested and can reduce council tax by up to 100% for low-income households. Verify at Stage 2.
- Primary (banding-challenge route — LGFA 1992 s.16 + SI 1992/290): banding challenges to the VOA where the dwelling's band may have been wrongly entered. Time-limited: a "proposal" can be made within 6 months of becoming the taxpayer for a property OR where one of the s.24 grounds applies (material reduction in value; commencement of business use; physical change in locality). VOA review process; appeal to the Valuation Tribunal under s.16(1) of LGFA 1992.
- Supporting (excluded routes): premium reductions under LGFA 1992 s.11A + s.11B + s.11C are LA-controlled INCREASES not reductions; not in scope for this page except as the empty-homes premium / second-homes premium context that pushes some readers to seek discounts.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What discounts can I get on my council tax? (Two principal statutory discounts under LGFA 1992 s.11: (i) 25% single-occupant discount if you live alone (or only with disregarded persons under Sch 1); (ii) 50% no-resident discount where the dwelling is unoccupied (subject to LA discretion to reduce or eliminate the empty-property discount). Plus disregards under Sch 1 which can shift a household into the single-occupant discount.)
2. What is the single-person discount and who counts? (LGFA 1992 s.11(1)(a) — 25% off the council-tax bill where one resident only. Counts "non-disregarded" residents. Disregards under Sch 1: full-time students, student nurses, apprentices, severely-mentally-impaired persons, persons in detention, persons in hospital, persons in care homes, persons aged 18-19 with child benefit, members of religious communities, etc. A two-adult household where one adult is a full-time student qualifies for the single-person discount.)
3. What is the difference between a discount and an exemption? (Discount reduces by a percentage; exemption eliminates council tax entirely for the period the exemption applies. Discounts are under LGFA 1992 s.11; exemptions are under SI 1992/558 Council Tax (Exempt Dwellings) Order 1992. Different statutory routes, different application processes, different evidential thresholds. Read the LA's council-tax page for both.)
4. What are the principal exempt classes? (SI 1992/558 lists Classes A-W with specific conditions. Most-used in landlord / tenant context: Class N (all occupants full-time students); Class W (annex occupied by dependant relative); Class M (HMO occupied solely by Sch 1 disregarded persons — note interaction with SI 2023/1175 single-dwelling rule); Class F (deceased owner pending probate, up to 6 months after grant). Stage 2 lists current operative classes and the conditions for each.)
5. Can I get a reduction because someone in the household has a disability? (Yes — SI 1992/554 Council Tax (Reductions for Disabilities) Regulations 1992. The dwelling is assessed at one band lower than its actual band where (a) a room is required and used by the disabled person (other than kitchen / bathroom / toilet), OR (b) an additional bathroom or kitchen is required for the disabled person, OR (c) sufficient floor space for wheelchair use indoors. Apply to the LA with medical evidence.)
6. Can I challenge the band my property is in? (Yes — LGFA 1992 s.16 + the proposal process under SI 1992/290. The challenge is to the VOA, not the LA. Strict 6-month window from when you first became the taxpayer for the property; or from the date one of the s.24 grounds arose (material reduction in value; physical change in locality). The VOA reviews; refusal can be appealed to the Valuation Tribunal. The challenge often takes 6-18 months.)
7. What is the Council Tax Reduction Scheme (CTR) and who qualifies? (LGFA 1992 Sch 1A + Sch 1B. Means-tested reduction operated by each LA. Pensioner CTR is centrally-prescribed (SI 2012/2885) and broadly equivalent to the abolished Council Tax Benefit. Working-age CTR is locally-determined and varies significantly LA-by-LA. Apply via the LA's website. Can reduce council tax by up to 100% depending on income, capital, household composition. Cross-references to Universal Credit + Pension Credit + JSA + ESA receipt.)
8. What about empty / unoccupied properties — can I get a discount? (Historically up to 50% under LGFA 1992 s.11(2). Now substantially restricted: SI 2003/3011 + LA discretion typically eliminates the empty-property discount entirely or limits it to a short period (e.g. one month). And after 12 months of vacancy, LGFA 1992 s.11B kicks in (post-LURA 2023 commencement 1 April 2024) with a premium UP TO 100% — so the empty-property treatment has flipped from discount to surcharge. Plan accordingly: do not rely on legacy empty-property discount; check the LA's current policy.)
9. What about second homes — can I get a discount? (Pre-LURA 2023, some LAs offered a 10% discount on second homes; this has been largely abolished. Post-LURA 2023 s.80 (in force 26 October 2023, with adoption typically from 1 April 2025), LAs in England can charge a premium of up to 100% on second homes. The second-home treatment has flipped from discount to surcharge.)
10. Are there scams I should watch out for? (Yes — multiple firms offer "council tax reduction services" for a fee. Legitimate reduction routes are FREE and direct: apply to the LA for discount / exemption / CTR; apply to the VOA for banding challenge. No third-party fee is needed. Citizens Advice has a published warning on the "council tax challenge" cold-call industry. The page legitimately flags this and recommends direct application.)
11. How do I apply for a discount or exemption? (LA's council-tax department — typically online application via the LA website + supporting evidence (proof of single occupancy; student certificate from the institution; medical evidence for disability reduction; death certificate + probate-pending evidence for Class F; etc.). Application typically within 28 days for cooperative cases.)
12. How far can a discount be backdated? (Statutory entitlements (s.11 discount + SI 1992/558 exemption + SI 1992/554 disability reduction) can typically be backdated to the date the entitlement arose, subject to the LA's evidential satisfaction and the council-tax-collection limitation period (typically 6 years under LGFA 1992 Sch 4). CTR scheme may have shorter backdate windows depending on the LA scheme. Apply promptly to avoid losing entitlement to time-bar.)
