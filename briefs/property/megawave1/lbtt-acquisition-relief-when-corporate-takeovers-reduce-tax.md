# LBTT Acquisition Relief: When Corporate Takeovers Reduce the Scottish Property Tax Bill

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013 asp 11 ("LBTT(S)A 2013"), Schedule 11 Part 3 "Acquisition relief" - verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/11 on 2026-05-26. Sch 11 is titled "Reconstruction relief and acquisition relief" and is in two parts: Part 2 reconstruction relief (full LBTT exemption); Part 3 acquisition relief (LBTT reduced to "the prescribed proportion of the tax that would otherwise be chargeable" per para 6(2), where the proportion is set by Scottish Ministers by order under para 6(3)). The five qualifying conditions for acquisition relief are in para 7: (1) acquiring company acquires an undertaking from a target company; (2) consideration includes "non-redeemable shares in the acquiring company" issued to the target or its shareholders; (3) acquiring company not associated with another party arranging share transfers (anti-fragmentation); (4) acquired undertaking's main activity is not "dealing in chargeable interests" (anti-property-trading-arbitrage); (5) acquisition is "effected for bona fide commercial reasons" and not mainly for tax avoidance.
- Supporting: LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 10 (group relief - parallel relief for intra-group transfers, different qualifying tests; mirror of FA 2003 Sch 7); Sch 10A (sub-sale development relief); Sch 18 Part 3 (bare-trust look-through, relevant where the acquiring company holds via a bare-trust nominee); Sch 2A (ADS framework - acquisition relief does NOT shelter ADS independently; ADS interaction must be analysed separately); s.59(8) (six-or-more-dwellings non-residential treatment - relevant where the acquired undertaking includes a residential portfolio). Anti-avoidance interaction: Sch 11 para 7(4) and the broader LBTT(S)A 2013 anti-avoidance regime tested against tax-driven structures where the "non-redeemable shares" condition is satisfied formally but the commercial substance is absent.
- House position reference: §23.4 main LBTT residential rates and §23.6 non-residential bands (the headline rates the prescribed-proportion is applied to); §23.6 reliefs subsection (acquisition relief listed as a corporate-restructure relief, mirrors FA 2003 Sch 7 Part 2); §23.5 ADS interaction (ADS 8% applies to corporate buyers buying residential dwellings except via tightly-defined reliefs - sessions must verify which Sch 2A exclusions extend to acquisition-relief transactions); §23.10 citations block. Cross-cluster: §15 (IHT - corporate restructure may engage IHT BPR considerations on target shares); §22 (estate-planning - acquisition relief commonly part of pre-sale restructure for a family-property business).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is LBTT acquisition relief? (Plain-English: Sch 11 Part 3 reduces the LBTT charge on a corporate acquisition of an undertaking from "the tax that would otherwise be chargeable" to "the prescribed proportion of" that tax. The relief is a reduction, not an exemption - reconstruction relief (Sch 11 Part 2) is the full-exemption relief, with stricter conditions.)
2. What rate of LBTT applies after the relief? (The prescribed proportion under para 6(3) is set by Scottish Ministers by order. Sessions must verify the current Scottish Statutory Instrument at write time per §16.35 - historically aligned with the English SDLT acquisition relief rate of 0.5% but the Scottish figure can diverge and Stage 2 must confirm the operative SSI.)
3. What are the five qualifying conditions? (Para 7: (1) the acquiring company acquires an undertaking from a target company; (2) consideration includes non-redeemable shares in the acquiring company issued to the target company or its shareholders; (3) the acquiring company is not associated with another party that arranges share transfers in connection with the acquisition; (4) the acquired undertaking's main activity is not "dealing in chargeable interests"; (5) the acquisition is effected for bona fide commercial reasons and not mainly for tax-avoidance purposes. All five must be met.)
4. What counts as an "undertaking"? (Statutory term not exhaustively defined - operationally, a going-concern business with assets, liabilities, customers, employees, and goodwill, not just a discrete bundle of property assets. The HMRC interpretation of "undertaking" for SDLT Sch 7 Part 2 acquisition relief is the reference point Revenue Scotland looks at; Stage 2 must verify whether Revenue Scotland applies the HMRC SDLTM line directly or has its own gloss.)
5. Why does the "non-redeemable shares" requirement matter? (The relief is designed for genuine share-for-undertaking exchanges in a corporate restructure. Cash consideration is allowed in addition, but the share component must be non-redeemable - rights-stripped or redeemable preference shares fail the test. The relief targets continuity-of-economic-interest structures, not asset-stripping transactions disguised as restructures.)
6. What does the "main activity is not dealing in chargeable interests" exclusion do? (Excludes property-trading businesses from the relief - a property-investment business is OK, a property-dealer is not. The line is blurred for buy-to-let portfolios that trade some units; Stage 2 must verify the current Revenue Scotland and HMRC line on the "investment vs trading" distinction for portfolio-property businesses.)
7. What is the bona-fide-commercial-reasons test? (Mainly-for-tax-avoidance carve-out: a relief-only acquisition designed to dump a stamp-duty bill is disallowed. The test mirrors the SDLT Sch 7 Part 2 "main purpose" anti-avoidance gate. HMRC's published practice on TIIN clearances is illustrative for what passes and fails; Revenue Scotland may grant pre-transaction clearance on Sch 11 - verify clearance availability at Stage 2.)
8. Does acquisition relief shelter ADS? (Probably not directly - ADS sits in Sch 2A and the acquisition-relief mechanic in Sch 11 Part 3 reduces the standard LBTT, not ADS. Where the acquired undertaking includes residential dwellings (>£40k each) and the acquiring company would otherwise face ADS at 8%, the relief does NOT eliminate the ADS exposure. Stage 2 must verify whether any specific Sch 2A exclusion extends to acquisition-relief transactions; this is a high-value lead-gen handoff if the position is unclear in published guidance.)
9. How does acquisition relief interact with the six-or-more-dwellings rule (s.59(8))? (Where the acquired undertaking includes 6+ residential dwellings as a portfolio, the s.59(8) automatic non-residential treatment applies and the non-residential LBTT bands (§23.6: 0%/1%/5%) replace the residential bands. Acquisition relief then reduces this lower non-residential charge to the prescribed proportion. The combined relief stack is the principal LBTT planning route for Scottish portfolio acquisitions inside a corporate restructure.)
10. What is the difference between acquisition relief and reconstruction relief? (Sch 11 Part 2 reconstruction relief is FULL EXEMPTION but requires (a) the acquiring company to have issued the same number of shares as the target's shareholders held in the target; (b) no other consideration beyond the share-for-share; and (c) the same five anti-avoidance gates. Sch 11 Part 3 acquisition relief is REDUCTION TO PRESCRIBED PROPORTION but allows cash consideration alongside the non-redeemable share issue, providing flexibility at the cost of full exemption.)
11. How does LBTT acquisition relief compare to SDLT acquisition relief (FA 2003 Sch 7 Part 2)? (Structurally parallel - both reduce, not exempt, with the rate set at 0.5% for SDLT (Sch 7 para 9(2)) and the prescribed proportion for LBTT (Sch 11 para 6(3)). Five-condition structure matches. The cross-jurisdictional planning point: a UK-wide property-business acquisition crossing the border may engage both reliefs concurrently, with separate clearance routes (HMRC for SDLT, Revenue Scotland for LBTT).)
