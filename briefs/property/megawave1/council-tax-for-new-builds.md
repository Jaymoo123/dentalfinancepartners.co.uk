# Council Tax for New Builds: When Liability Begins, the LGFA 1992 s.17 Completion-Notice Machinery, the Substantially-Completed Test, and the Valuation Tribunal Appeal Route

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (council-tax framework): Local Government Finance Act 1992 (LGFA 1992) — the consolidating Act for the council-tax regime in England, Scotland and Wales. Part I (council tax: England and Wales) is the operative framework for England + Wales. **s.1** establishes the charge; **s.2** defines a chargeable dwelling; **s.3** defines "dwelling" by reference to the General Rate Act 1967 hereditament test + exclusion of non-domestic-rating-listed property; **s.6** sets out the council-tax liability hierarchy (resident freeholder > resident leaseholder > resident tenant > resident licensee > resident > owner); **s.16** governs appeals; **s.17** is the completion-notice gateway for new buildings. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/14/section/3 + https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/14/section/17 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (completion notice machinery — the operative mechanism for new builds): **LGFA 1992 s.17** applies Schedule 4A of the **Local Government Finance Act 1988** (LGFA 1988 Sch 4A) to Part I of LGFA 1992, with the exception of paragraph 6 of Sch 4A. References to "valuation officer" in Sch 4A are read as "listing officer" for council-tax purposes. **s.17(1)** provides: where a completion notice is served under Sch 4A, "any dwelling in which the building or any part of it will be comprised shall be deemed for the purposes of this Part to have come into existence on that day" (the completion day). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/14/section/17 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (Sch 4A mechanic): **LGFA 1988 Sch 4A** — Determination of Completion Day for New Buildings. **Para 1**: the billing authority serves a completion notice where the work remaining can reasonably be expected to be completed within 3 months OR where the building appears to be completed. **Para 2**: completion-day determination by (a) agreement between owner and billing authority, (b) the date stated in the notice if no appeal, or (c) the date determined by the Valuation Tribunal on appeal. **Para 4**: the proposed completion day must be not later than 3 months from and including the day the notice is served (where the building is incomplete on service date), or the service date itself (where the building is already substantially complete). **Para 9**: "completed" means "substantially completed," excluding customary post-completion work (finishing trades, kitchen / bathroom installation final fittings, minor cosmetic work). **Para 4(2)**: appeal to the Valuation Tribunal on grounds that the building has not been or cannot reasonably be expected to be completed by the proposed day. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1988/41/schedule/4A on 2026-05-26.
- Supporting (band entry mechanic): once the completion day is determined under s.17 + Sch 4A, the listing officer (under LGFA 1992 s.22) enters the dwelling in the council-tax valuation list with a band (A-H in England, A-I in Wales). The band is determined by reference to the dwelling's value on 1 April 1991 (England) or 1 April 2003 (Wales). The 1991 / 2003 valuation reference dates remain operative as of 2026-05-26 — England has not implemented the long-discussed council-tax revaluation; Wales completed its 2003 revaluation and has not since revised. **Stage 1b drift watchpoint:** any pending revaluation should be flagged at Stage 2 against the Treasury / MHCLG / Welsh Government policy position on the page write date.
- Supporting (first-occupation timing): completion-notice date and first-occupation date often differ. A new build is BANDED from the completion day (per s.17(1)); council-tax LIABILITY runs from the completion day (or earlier if the building is occupied before the completion notice). Empty-dwelling discounts / exemptions may apply between the completion day and first occupation (under the Council Tax (Prescribed Classes of Dwellings) (England) Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/3011) for England; equivalents in Wales / Scotland). Class A (uninhabitable) and Class C (empty unfurnished) discounts have been substantially withdrawn or made discretionary — many LAs charge full council tax from the completion day with no empty-dwelling discount. **The completion-day-to-first-occupation gap is the principal cost-trap for self-builders.**
- Supporting (council-tax-premium overlay for new-build delays): LGFA 1992 **s.11B** (long-term empty-homes premium, inserted by Local Government Finance Act 2012; reduced to 1-year threshold by Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023 (LURA 2023) s.79 in force in England from 1 April 2024) — where a new-build dwelling stands empty for 12 months from the completion day, a council-tax premium of up to 100% (rising to 200% after 5 years and 300% after 10 years) may be charged. Verify the current rate banding at Stage 2 against the LA charging policy. **Stage 1b drift watchpoint:** LURA 2023 s.79 commenced 1 April 2024 reducing the empty-homes premium trigger from 2 years to 1 year — this is the operative-current position; competitor pages predating April 2024 still cite the 2-year trigger and are stale.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. When does council tax start on a new build? (Operative answer: from the completion day determined under LGFA 1988 Sch 4A applied by LGFA 1992 s.17. Earliest practical trigger: when the building is "substantially completed" within the meaning of Sch 4A para 9 — kitchen + bathroom roughed-in, walls and ceilings finished, services connected. The completion day is NOT the building-regs completion-certificate date; the two operate on different statutory tests.)
2. What does "substantially completed" mean? (Sch 4A para 9: the building has reached the stage of customary completion. Customary post-completion work — final paint coat, kitchen-fitting carcasses, sanitary-ware connection, soft-furnishing-grade snagging — does not prevent substantial completion. Major outstanding work — no roof, no internal walls, no services — does prevent it. The line is fact-specific and is the most-litigated point at the Valuation Tribunal.)
3. Who serves the completion notice and on whom? (The billing authority (the LA collecting council tax — usually the district / borough / unitary authority) serves the notice on the owner of the building. The valuation officer (the VOA listing officer) can direct that no notice is served in particular cases — rare in practice.)
4. What date can the billing authority propose? (Sch 4A para 4: (a) where the building is incomplete at notice service, a date up to 3 months from the notice service date; (b) where the building is already substantially completed, the proposed date can be the service date itself. The "up to 3 months" is the maximum forward window; many LAs propose 1-2 months for incomplete builds.)
5. How do I challenge a completion notice? (Sch 4A para 4(2): appeal to the Valuation Tribunal within 28 days of notice service (verify Valuation Tribunal Rules currency at Stage 2). Grounds: the building has not been or cannot reasonably be expected to be completed by the proposed day. The appeal is on the completion day, not the band — the band is a separate appeal route via the listing officer.)
6. What happens during the appeal? (The proposed completion day applies for billing pending determination. If the Tribunal sets a later date, the billed amount is adjusted (refund typically; LA discretion on interest). If the Tribunal upholds the proposed date or sets an earlier date, no adjustment.)
7. Can I avoid council tax by leaving the dwelling empty? (No — the completion day triggers banding and liability regardless of occupation. Empty-dwelling discounts in England under SI 2003/3011 are now largely discretionary and many LAs charge full council tax from the completion day. The empty-homes premium under s.11B doubles council tax after 12 months of vacancy (post-LURA 2023 s.79 in force 1 April 2024) — the "empty new build" strategy is materially worse than buying a tenanted property from a council-tax-cost perspective.)
8. How is the band determined on a new build? (LGFA 1992 s.22 + s.21: the listing officer determines the band by reference to the value the dwelling would have had on 1 April 1991 (England) or 1 April 2003 (Wales). For a 2026 new build, this is a hypothetical 1991 / 2003 valuation — typically lower than current market value due to inflation but constrained by the dwelling's size, fitting, and location characteristics. The bands are A (under £40,000 in England) to H (£320,000 + in England). High-spec new builds in prime locations can be Band G or H.)
9. What if the new build is not first-banded until months after completion? (Banding occurs after the completion notice + listing officer's banding work. Council-tax liability is backdated to the completion day once banding is recorded — the bill will catch up. The completion-day determination is the operative date; banding-record-date is administrative.)
10. How does the empty-homes premium interact with a new-build held empty by the developer for marketing? (LGFA 1992 s.11B — empty-homes premium of up to 100% kicks in after 12 months of vacancy (post-LURA 2023 commencement 1 April 2024). For developer-held new-build stock awaiting sale, the LA has discretion to designate exceptions (a "developers' empty-property exemption" of up to 12 months is sometimes available under SI 2003/3011 Class C — verify at Stage 2 against the current SI). Stage 2 must confirm the LA-by-LA position on developer-held new-build vacancy.)
11. Does s.11B premium apply to second homes too? (No — s.11B is the empty-homes premium. s.11C is the SECOND-HOMES premium, inserted by LURA 2023 s.80. Both can apply to a new-build that is empty AND a second home, in which case the LA's policy on stacking governs; LURA 2023 typically applies one premium not both per dwelling-period.)
12. Does the completion-notice route apply to conversions and refurbishments? (Conversion of an existing building into a new dwelling (e.g. office-to-residential PD conversion under Class O / MA) triggers a fresh completion notice for the new dwelling. A like-for-like refurbishment of an existing residential dwelling does NOT trigger a completion notice — the dwelling continues to exist throughout. The line between "conversion creating a new dwelling" and "refurbishment of an existing dwelling" is fact-specific.)
