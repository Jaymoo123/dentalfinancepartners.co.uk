# Applicable SDLT Rates for First-Time Buyers: Current Thresholds, 1 April 2025 Reversion, and How the Relief Maps Onto the Standard Rate Table

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: FA 2003 s.57B "First-time buyers" — "(1) Schedule 6ZA provides relief for first-time buyers." Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/section/57B on 2026-05-26 (page shows "up to date with all changes known to be in force on or before 26 May 2026").
- Primary (operative): FA 2003 Schedule 6ZA "Relief for first-time buyers" — inserted by Finance Act 2018 (c. 3) s.41(3), commenced per s.41(8). Para 1(3) imposes the £500,000 absolute cap on chargeable consideration (relief unavailable above that figure); para 4 Table A sets the 0% nil band on the first £300,000 and 5% on the £300,001-£500,000 portion. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/6ZA on 2026-05-26.
- Supporting: F(No.2)A 2024 c. 12 s.8 (inserted Sch 6ZA para 6(3) addressing bare trust leases — 2024 amendment, not affecting the rate-band architecture); FA 2003 s.55 + Table A (standard residential rates, post-1 April 2025: 0% to £125k, 2% £125k-£250k, 5% £250k-£925k, 10% £925k-£1.5m, 12% above £1.5m); FA 2003 Sch 4ZA (additional dwellings surcharge — interacts with FTB relief: FTB relief disapplies where the surcharge applies, so a first-time buyer who jointly purchases with a non-FTB partner who already owns property loses FTB relief AND attracts the 5% surcharge).
- House position reference: §1 main text (residential SDLT bands from 1 April 2025; the FTB-relief line at §1 paragraph 4 confirms 0% to £300k + 5% £300k-£500k + full withdrawal above £500k as the HP-locked rate position); §1.I (Wave 9 lock — 5% additional dwellings surcharge from 31 October 2024, FA 2025 s.51); §1.H (Wave 9 lock — MDR abolition + s.116(7) 6+-dwellings deeming — narrow relevance, FTB relief is single-dwelling only). **NEW HP-LOCK CANDIDATE: §1.K "First-Time Buyers' Relief — FA 2003 s.57B + Sch 6ZA — current £300k/£500k thresholds + 1 April 2025 reversion + joint-purchase + bare-trust mechanics"** — Stage 1b conductor decision; no existing §1 sub-section currently captures FTB relief mechanics directly (only summary line in §1 main text). ## Key questions this page must answer

1. What are the current SDLT rates for first-time buyers in England and Northern Ireland? (Band-by-band table: 0% on first £300,000; 5% on the portion £300,001-£500,000; relief unavailable above £500,000 — meaning standard residential rates apply on the full consideration, not just the portion above £500,000.)
2. What changed on 1 April 2025? (Thresholds reverted from temporary £425,000 nil band + £625,000 maximum chargeable consideration cap, in force 23 September 2022 to 31 March 2025, back to the permanent £300,000 / £500,000 thresholds. Many competitor pages still cite the £425k/£625k figures.)
3. How does the £500,000 cap actually work? (Binary cliff edge, not tapered withdrawal. A £499,999 purchase by a first-time buyer claiming the relief computes SDLT as £9,999.95 — 5% on £199,999. A £500,001 purchase computes SDLT as £10,000 on the standard residential table — 0% on £125k, 2% on £125k-£250k, 5% on £250k-£500k, plus 5% × £1 on the £1 over £500k — total around £12,500.05. The £2 increase in consideration produces a £2,500+ jump in SDLT. Worked example with both sides of the cliff.)
4. How does FTB relief interact with the standard rate table for a £400,000 purchase? (Standard rates on £400k = £7,500. FTB relief on £400k = £5,000 — 0% on £300k + 5% on £100k. The relief saves £2,500 on a £400k purchase by a single first-time buyer with no surcharge.)
5. What happens with the additional dwellings surcharge? (Sch 4ZA disapplies FTB relief where the buyer or any joint buyer owns or is acquiring another dwelling. Joint purchase with a partner who owns a BTL portfolio means: NO FTB relief AND the 5% additional dwellings surcharge applies — a £400k joint purchase computes SDLT at £27,500, materially worse than the sole-first-time-buyer £5,000 figure.)
6. Does this apply to Wales or Scotland? (No. Wales has no FTB-specific relief; the £225,000 nil band on Welsh LTT serves the function for starter purchases. Scotland has its own LBTT FTB relief under LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 4A — different £175,000 nil-band uplift architecture; importantly, no upper property-value ceiling. Cross-links to both jurisdictions' pages.)
7. What is the maximum tax saving from the relief? (A £500,000 purchase by a sole first-time buyer claiming relief: SDLT = £10,000 (5% on £200k). Standard rates on £500k = £12,500 (0%+2%×£125k+5%×£250k = £0+£2,500+£12,500 = £15,000 wait recalculate — 0% on £125k + 2% × £125k = £2,500 + 5% × £250k = £12,500 → total £15,000). Saving = £15,000 - £10,000 = £5,000. The maximum saving caps at £5,000; the marketing of FTB relief sometimes overstates the figure.) **Stage 2 verify rate-by-reference calculation at write time per §16.27 / §16.35.**
8. What if I am a first-time buyer in Northern Ireland? (Identical to England — SDLT applies in NI; LBTT and LTT are devolved to Scotland and Wales only. Per §23 + §1 HP main text line 1419.)
9. How does the relief interact with shared ownership purchases? (Para 6 of Sch 6ZA has specific shared-ownership mechanics; the buyer can claim FTB relief on a market-value election OR on the initial-share-only basis. Stage 2 expands.)
