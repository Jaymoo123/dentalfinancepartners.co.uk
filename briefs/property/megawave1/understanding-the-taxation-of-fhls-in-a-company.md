# Understanding the Taxation of FHLs in a Company

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Finance Act 2025 Schedule 5 Part 2 ("Amendments relating to corporation tax"), Part 5 paragraph 12 commencement (accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2025), verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/8/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26. Removes the company-side ITTOIA-equivalent FHL overrides (CTA 2009 ss.264-269A, the company-side mirror of ITTOIA ss.322-328A).
- Supporting: CTA 2009 Part 4 (property income for companies — the residual ordinary residential property business framework former-FHL stock falls into); CTA 2010 s.18N et seq (CT residential property gains rules); FA 2013 Part 3 (ATED — Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings; £500k SDLT-value gate post-1-April-2016, with the reliefs at FA 2013 ss.132-152 still load-bearing for company-held property let on commercial terms to unconnected parties); FA 2003 Sch 4A (15% SDLT super-charge on company-held single-dwelling acquisitions over £500k); CAA 2001 s.15 (omission of FHL as a separate qualifying activity from 1 April 2025 for CT, mirroring the IT-side change); FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 5 paragraph 18 (the grandfathered capital allowances pool carve-out, applying equally to company-held former FHL); ITA 2007 Part 13 Chapter 3 + ITAct 1988 s.971 (Non-Resident Landlord scheme — for non-UK-parent groups; companies do not withhold but the scheme still binds the agent-tenant chain). Section 24 (ITA 2007 s.272A) does NOT apply to UK companies — full mortgage interest deductibility against company rental profit at CTA 2009 s.272 stands.
- House position reference: §6 "FHL — abolition transition" (the macro position); §25.7 "FHL transitional under FA 2025 Sch 5" (capital allowances carve-out, equally applicable to company-held FHL); §11 "Companies House reforms / ECCTA / Register of Overseas Entities" (RoE annual update for any overseas-parent company holding UK FHL stock — separate compliance gate that does not reset post-abolition); §3 "Limited-company landlord — Section 24 carve-out" (the company-side advantage that abolition does NOT remove); §21.5 (FIC-related capital allowance availability, cross-applicable).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What changed for a UK limited company holding FHL stock at 1 April 2025? (FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 2 omits the CTA 2009 FHL overrides; the company's qualifying-FHL business ceases to be a separate property business and folds into the ordinary UK or overseas property business; capital allowances grandfathered pool continues writing down under FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 5 para 18; CT-side CGT lever loses BADR equivalence but companies never had BADR — the rate-change impact for companies runs through the residential property gain CT mechanic at CTA 2010 s.18N+, not through BADR.)
2. Why is a limited company more attractive for former FHL stock now than it was pre-abolition? (Two-step answer. Pre-abolition: personal-side FHL operators had the full mortgage interest deduction AND BADR-on-disposal AND pension-relevant-UK-earnings — so the personal-side regime was already tax-efficient. Post-abolition: those personal advantages collapse, but the company-side full mortgage interest deductibility — which Section 24 does NOT bite — remains. The relative attractiveness flips. The decision is now driven by leverage gearing + extraction strategy + ATED-band exposure.)
3. What is the corporation tax rate on former-FHL rental profit? (Standard CT: 19% on profit up to £50,000; 25% on profit from £250,000; marginal rate of 26.5% on profit between the two bounds — the "associated companies" rule under CTA 2010 s.18D-18M divides those thresholds across a group, important for landlord SPV structures.)
4. Are mortgage interest costs fully deductible in the company? (Yes. Section 24 — ITA 2007 s.272A — applies only to individuals, partnerships of individuals, and trusts. Companies deduct interest in full under CTA 2009 s.272 + s.298 (subject to the corporate interest restriction at TIOPA 2010 Part 10, which only bites groups with net interest >£2 million per year, not single-SPV landlord companies).)
5. Does ATED apply? (Yes for any single dwelling held by a UK or non-UK company at a value over £500,000 at the 2022 revaluation date (or acquisition date if later). The 2026/27 ATED bands run from £500k-£1m at £4,500 charge through to £20m+ at £294,250. Relief available under FA 2013 ss.132-152 for genuine commercial-let property to unconnected parties — the relevant relief for former FHL stock let on arm's-length holiday-let terms.)
6. What about capital allowances on plant inside the property? (Grandfathered pool from pre-1-April-2025 plant claims continues writing down at 18% main pool / 6% special-rate pool — explicit carve-out from the s.35 dwelling-house restriction under FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 5 para 18. New post-commencement spend is barred from P&M allowances by s.35 in the same way as personal-owner-side. Replacement of domestic items relief at CTA 2009 s.250A is the company-side mirror of ITTOIA s.311A.)
7. CGT on disposal of a former-FHL property held in a company? (Treated as a chargeable gain within CT at the residential-property CGT-equivalent rate; no BADR or Investors' Relief for company disposals (those are personal-side reliefs); rollover relief at TCGA 1992 s.152 may still be available on reinvestment into other qualifying business assets — but a former-FHL holding company is a property investment company, not a trading company, so rollover availability is narrow.)
8. Should a non-UK parent company hold UK FHL stock through a UK or non-UK subsidiary? (Tax-driven choice. UK sub: full UK CT exposure but no NRL withholding and no s.971 income-tax 20% mark-up. Non-UK sub: from 6 April 2020 non-UK companies holding UK property income are inside the UK CT regime under FA 2019 Sch 2 — the historic ITA 2007 Part 13 NRL withholding via the tenant/agent chain still applies in parallel until corporate-side ATED, RoE, and CT registration are aligned. Stage 2 walks the post-2020 architecture; this page surfaces the question.)
9. Do former-FHL anti-forestalling provisions matter for a company that already held FHL stock at commencement? (No — the FA(No.2) 2024 anti-forestalling provisions targeted pre-abolition disposals between announcement (6 March 2024) and abolition (5 April 2025) where the seller wanted to lock in BADR. Companies were never eligible for BADR, so the anti-forestalling pattern had no direct company-side application. The provisions affected personal owners trying to crystallise gains before abolition.)
10. Can the company change accounting reference date to optimise commencement? (Technically yes via Companies House CH AP01; but the FA 2025 Sch 5 commencement is "accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2025" — a company with a 31 December year-end has 2025 calendar year as the last pre-commencement period for some adjustments, but para 12(2)(b) deems the period ending immediately before 1 April 2025 to be the last FHL-trading-equivalent period. ARD-shifting cannot avoid commencement.)
