# Making a Disclosure Using the Worldwide Disclosure Facility: The Step-by-Step Operational Process — Notify, Disclose, Pay — Plus the Disclosure-Pack Contents, the Year-by-Year Computation Format, and the Post-Disclosure HMRC Review Stage

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (operational framework — non-statutory but HMRC-operated): the Worldwide Disclosure Facility (WDF) is an HMRC-operated voluntary-disclosure route open from 5 September 2016 and ongoing. It is **not itself a statutory regime** — it operates within the FA 2007 Sch 24 + FA 2008 Sch 41 + FA 2017 Sch 18 + FA 2019 s.80 statutory framework, and HMRC publishes the operational mechanics in WDF guidance on gov.uk. The page therefore anchors the statutory framework but the disclosure mechanic is procedural / HMRC-administrative.
- Primary (operative penalty framework): **FA 2007 Schedule 24** — penalties for errors in returns + documents. Para 4 sets the headline penalty bands by behaviour AND offshore-category:
  - Careless: 30% (Cat 1) / 45% (Cat 2) / 60% (Cat 3) of potential lost revenue (PLR);
  - Deliberate-not-concealed: 70% / 105% / 140%;
  - Deliberate-concealed: 100% / 150% / 200%.
  Para 4A defines the category territories (HMRC publishes the territory categorisation in updated form via the Finance Act (Schedule 24 to FA 2007) (Offshore Income etc) (Designation of Territories) Order 2011 SI 2011/976 and subsequent amending orders — Stage 2 verifies the current list). Para 4A(4B) "offshore transfers" mechanism brings asset-movement structures into Cat 2/3 uplift treatment. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2007/11/schedule/24 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (failure-to-notify penalties): **FA 2008 Schedule 41** — penalties for failure to notify. Same Cat 1/2/3 architecture with offshore uplifts. Para 6 sets the headline rates with parallel structure to Sch 24. Material where the offshore matter was an unreported income source from which no return notification was made.
- Primary (FtC overlay): **FA 2017 Schedule 18** — Requirement to Correct certain offshore tax non-compliance. RTC period 6 April 2017 to 30 September 2018. Para 14: base penalty post-deadline = **200% of offshore PLR**. Para 16: penalty reducible on disclosure but with a 100% floor. Para 28: asset-based penalty layer under FA 2016 Sch 22 for high-asset-value matters. Para 30: naming-and-shaming for penalties > £25,000 or 5+ penalties. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2017/32/schedule/18 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (offshore discovery window): **TMA 1970 s.36A** inserted by FA 2019 s.80 — 12-year discovery window from end of tax year for offshore income tax and CGT, regardless of behaviour (extends the ordinary 4-year s.34 window). s.36(1) and s.36(1A) 6-year (careless) and 20-year (deliberate) windows remain operative for the relevant behaviour bands. WDF disclosure runs back across these statutory windows.
- Supporting (asset-move uplift): **FA 2015 Sch 21** — 50% asset-move penalty layer where offshore assets are moved from a specified (Cat 2 / 3) territory to a non-specified territory with the main purpose of preventing or delaying HMRC discovery. Stacks on top of the Sch 24 / Sch 41 penalty in scope cases.
- House position reference: **§27.6 Voluntary disclosure routes — LPC + WDF + general voluntary disclosure** (existing Wave 7 lock — already in house_positions.md). The WDF appears at §27.6 with the 90-day timeline and the offshore-Category 2/3 uplift mechanic; the FtC overlay appears at §27.6 with the 200% post-deadline floor; the LPC versus WDF versus CoP9 decision tree appears at §27.6 + §27.5. **This page reinforces existing §27.6 lock; no new HP lock needed.** §27.5 (LPC) is sister-lock for UK-only disclosure routes; §27.6 (WDF) is the in-scope lock for this page.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the Worldwide Disclosure Facility in one sentence and how is this page different from the existing WDF + FtC overview page? (One sentence: WDF is HMRC's voluntary-disclosure route for any UK tax position connected to offshore income, gains or assets. This page is the operational how-to; the existing site WDF page covers the regime overview + FtC overlay + territory-category penalty mechanics.)
2. Step 1 — Notify HMRC: how does the notification work? (Submit a Notify via gov.uk WDF route. The Notify identifies the taxpayer + flags intention to disclose + gives a high-level indication of the matter (offshore rental income; offshore CGT; offshore investment income; offshore trust distribution; etc.). HMRC acknowledges typically within 1-2 weeks. The 90-day disclosure timeline starts from HMRC's acknowledgment date — not from the Notify submission.)
3. Step 2 — Disclose within 90 days (extendable to 180): what goes in the disclosure pack? (Five components: (i) narrative description of the matter — facts, when arose, source documents identified, behaviour categorisation; (ii) year-by-year tax / interest / penalty computation in HMRC's expected format; (iii) supporting documentary evidence — foreign bank statements, foreign tax certificates if any, foreign property records, foreign rental records, foreign trust documentation; (iv) penalty-band proposal with reasoning (unprompted Cat 1 careless = 0-30%; etc.); (v) settlement-payment commitment with date.)
4. How is the 90-day window calculated and when can it be extended to 180 days? (Calendar days from HMRC's acknowledgment of the Notify. Extension to 180 days available on written application setting out (a) the complexity of the matter (e.g. multi-jurisdictional, 12-year computation, trust structure); (b) the steps being taken to source the records. HMRC typically grants extension where the request is reasoned and the taxpayer demonstrates active progress.)
5. What is the year-by-year computation format? (Per-tax-year, per-tax-type (income tax / CGT / IHT separately), per-source (each foreign property / each foreign investment as a separate column or row). Example layout for offshore rental: tax year | gross rental | allowable expenses | net taxable | UK tax band rate | tax due | interest from original due date to projected pay date | penalty range proposal. Stage 2 includes a worked-example layout in the page body.)
6. What evidence is required? (HMRC expects primary-source documentary evidence: foreign bank statements (typically the most-asked-for item); foreign property purchase + disposal contracts; foreign rental agreements + receipts; foreign tax returns / certificates if filed in the foreign jurisdiction (for double-tax relief); foreign trust deeds + distributions; foreign company corporate filings. Secondary evidence (taxpayer reconstruction of records from memory + indirect sources) is acceptable but increases the penalty band typically by 1-2 percentage points.)
7. How is interest calculated and how material is it? (TMA 1970 s.86 interest at the official rate (Bank of England base rate + 2.5% as of the page write date — verify at Stage 2) from the original due date to the date of payment. On a 12-year offshore catch-up, interest cumulative compound effect can equal or exceed the principal tax — particularly during the 2022-2024 high-rate period. The page works a typical example showing interest as a discrete number alongside tax.)
8. How are penalties calculated and what is the band-negotiation discipline? (FA 2007 Sch 24 + FA 2008 Sch 41 banded penalties with offshore Cat 1/2/3 uplifts. Banding determined by behaviour (careless / deliberate not concealed / deliberate concealed) and category (territory + tax type). HMRC has discretion within the band based on disclosure quality. Unprompted full unprompted disclosure typically secures the lower bound; prompted or partial disclosure secures the higher bound. FtC overlay where applicable imposes 100% floor minimum. Asset-move uplift under FA 2015 Sch 21 stacks where applicable.)
9. Step 3 — Pay on disclosure: how does the payment mechanic work? (Single payment to HMRC for the aggregate tax + interest + penalty figure proposed in the disclosure. Payment options: BACS / CHAPS / Faster Payments to the HMRC Worldwide Disclosure account; cheque also accepted. Time-to-pay arrangements available on application where the principal-plus-interest-plus-penalty figure exceeds the taxpayer's liquidity. The settlement payment is contingent on HMRC's acceptance of the disclosure-pack figures.)
10. What happens after the disclosure pack is filed and the settlement payment made? (HMRC reviews the disclosure pack — typical timeline 3-12 months. HMRC may issue questions / request further evidence / propose adjustments. Where HMRC accepts the disclosure, a settlement letter closes the matter. Where HMRC disagrees with figures or penalty bands, negotiation continues; if not resolved, formal assessment under TMA 1970 s.29 / s.36 / s.36A and the ordinary appeal route opens. The disclosure-and-settlement is not final until HMRC's confirmation letter.)
11. What if the taxpayer is at criminal-prosecution risk for the matter? (Stop. WDF does not provide criminal-prosecution immunity. Where the matter involves deliberate fraud with potential criminal exposure, the appropriate route is CoP9 / Contractual Disclosure Facility (CDF), which provides immunity for matters disclosed. Cross-link to the CoP9 page. The decision is fact-specific and typically requires specialist tax-disputes advice before commencing WDF — once WDF is in motion, the criminal-prosecution risk has not been foreclosed.)
12. How does WDF interact with foreign-jurisdiction tax positions? (Double-tax-relief mechanics under UK DTAs (typically credit-method) apply on the disclosure — foreign tax paid offsets UK tax due. The disclosure must include foreign-jurisdiction tax filings + payments as evidence. Where the foreign jurisdiction has not yet been notified by the taxpayer (offshore non-compliance in BOTH jurisdictions), a parallel foreign-jurisdiction disclosure may be required — the UK WDF does not foreclose the foreign-jurisdiction obligation.)
