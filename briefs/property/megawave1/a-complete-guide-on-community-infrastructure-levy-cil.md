# A Complete Guide to the Community Infrastructure Levy (CIL): How It Differs from SDLT and Council Tax, How It Is Calculated, Who Pays, and the Self-Build / Annex / Charitable / Social-Housing Reliefs That Cap Real-World Exposure

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (enabling power): Planning Act 2008 Part 11, ss.205-225 — the Secretary of State's regulation-making power for the Community Infrastructure Levy. s.205 empowers the regulations and provides the high-level statutory purpose (ensuring development costs are funded in a way that does not render development economically unviable); s.206 designates local planning authorities (LPAs) + the Mayor of London + the Broads Authority + the Council of the Isles of Scilly as charging authorities; s.208 establishes the liability framework triggered on commencement of development; s.209 defines "development" for CIL purposes and requires the regulations to specify the commencement-date and planning-permission take-effect mechanics; s.213 governs the charging-schedule examination and approval process. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2008/29/part/11 on 2026-05-26. **Stage 1b drift watchpoint:** the Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023 (c. 55) (LURA 2023) Part 4 amended the CIL Part 11 framework substantially (street-vote development consent overlay; charging-schedule procedure changes); some LURA 2023 CIL amendments remain subject to commencement order at the time of seeding (verify against the latest commencement-order regime at Stage 2 against gov.uk and legislation.gov.uk).
- Primary (operational regulations): the Community Infrastructure Levy Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/948), as amended by SI 2011/987, SI 2012/2975, SI 2013/982, SI 2014/385, SI 2019/1103, SI 2024/579, and pending SI 2026/55 (interpretation amendments — Stage 2 confirms in-force date). The regulations operationalise the Part 11 framework. Headline parts:
  - **Part 2 (regs 4-11):** charging authorities and core duties.
  - **Part 3 (regs 12-28):** **charging schedules** — LPA must adopt a charging schedule before it can charge CIL; the schedule sets rates per square metre of net additional internal floorspace by development type and zone.
  - **Part 4 (regs 29-39):** **liability** — who pays. Default: the landowner at the time of commencement. The "Assumption of Liability" mechanism (Form 1 or online equivalent) allows another party (typically the developer) to assume liability before commencement.
  - **Part 5 (regs 40-50):** **chargeable amount calculation** — rate (£ per m²) × net additional internal floorspace, with the BCIS All-in Tender Price Index applied for inflation indexation between the charging-schedule adoption date and the planning-permission grant date.
  - **Part 6 (regs 42-58):** **exemptions and reliefs** — minor development exemption (regs 42-42B: gross internal floorspace under 100m² + not creating a new dwelling, unless self-build); charitable relief (regs 43-48); social housing relief (regs 49-50, including discretionary relief under s.49A); self-build exemption for whole houses (reg 54A inserted by SI 2014/385); self-build exemption for residential annexes and extensions (regs 42A-42C inserted by SI 2014/385).
  - **Part 8 (regs 65-75):** **payment and instalments** — commencement notice triggers liability; standard payment within 60 days; instalment policy at LPA discretion; surcharges for non-compliance (reg 83 + reg 85).
  - **Part 9 (regs 76-103):** **enforcement** — stop notices, CIL warning notices, late-payment interest and surcharges, land-charge entry. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2010/948/contents on 2026-05-26.
- Supporting (s.106 planning obligation overlay): Town and Country Planning Act 1990 s.106 — the negotiated, site-specific planning obligation. s.106 operates independently of CIL; there is **NO statutory cross-reference between s.106 TCPA 1990 and the CIL framework**, but the CIL Regs Reg 122 governs the policy test for s.106 contributions (an obligation may constitute a reason for granting planning permission only if it is necessary, directly related to the development, and fairly and reasonably related in scale and kind). Pre-CIL, infrastructure funding was overwhelmingly via s.106; post-CIL (in CIL-charging LPAs), s.106 is reserved for site-specific mitigation and CIL handles the wider strategic-infrastructure pot. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1990/8/section/106 on 2026-05-26.
- Supporting (CIL is NOT a tax — SDLT distinction): CIL is a statutory **charge** under Planning Act 2008 Part 11 — administered by the LPA on commencement of development under planning permission. SDLT under FA 2003 Part 4 is a **tax** — administered by HMRC on a chargeable land transaction. The two regimes are entirely separate: the same transaction can trigger SDLT (on the land purchase) and later CIL (on commencement of development of the same land); the SDLT computation does not deduct CIL and the CIL calculation does not deduct SDLT. The page must keep the two regimes distinct for reader-clarity (this is the most common SERP-level conflation).
- Supporting (CIL is NOT council tax): council tax is an annual occupation-and-banding charge under the Local Government Finance Act 1992 (LGFA 1992) administered by the billing authority. CIL is a one-off development charge under PA 2008 Part 11. The page touches the distinction briefly to anchor the reader — separate sister-pages cover the council-tax topics within this same MegaWave bucket (C2-C4, C6, C8, C9).

This page covers eight things competitor coverage routinely under-serves or gets wrong: (a) the **statutory location of CIL** — PA 2008 Part 11 + SI 2010/948, NOT a tax under FA 2003 or LGFA 1992; competitor SEO pages routinely conflate CIL with "stamp duty for developers" or "council tax for new builds," which is misinformation; (b) the **liability default and the Assumption-of-Liability mechanic** — the legal default is that the landowner-at-commencement pays unless someone formally assumes liability via the Form 1 / online assumption process before commencement; competitor pages routinely state "the developer pays" without explaining the actual statutory default and the contractual-allocation discipline that follows from it; (c) the **calculation mechanic** — the BCIS All-in Tender Price Index indexation between the charging-schedule adoption date and the planning-permission grant date is non-obvious and changes the £-per-m² rate at the grant date; (d) the **net additional internal floorspace** computation — demolitions of pre-existing in-use buildings within the previous three years are deducted (reg 40(7) "in lawful use" test, six-month aggregate occupation within the three-year window) before applying the rate; competitor pages routinely state "rate times new floorspace" without the demolition-deduction mechanic; (e) the **self-build whole-house exemption (reg 54A)** — applies to a primary residence built or commissioned by the owner / occupier for own occupation, with a three-year clawback if the owner ceases to occupy as their main residence within three years of the building-completion compliance notice; (f) the **self-build annex / extension exemption (regs 42A-42C)** — the annex must be wholly within the curtilage of the main dwelling and be ancillary to it; the extension test is more permissive but still tied to the principal-dwelling occupation; (g) the **commencement notice / payment / surcharge discipline** — Form 6 commencement notice MUST be submitted to the LPA before development starts; failure to submit triggers surcharges under reg 83 (late commencement notice) + reg 84 (failure to commence after deemed commencement) + reg 85 (late payment); aggregate surcharges + interest can exceed the principal CIL liability; (h) the **CIL versus s.106 division of labour post-Reg 122 reform** — Regulation 122 + the post-April-2015 pooling restriction (no more than five s.106 contributions toward any single infrastructure project) changed the operating practice; CIL handles strategic infrastructure; s.106 handles site-specific mitigation.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the Community Infrastructure Levy and what is its statutory location? (A planning charge under Planning Act 2008 Part 11 + the Community Infrastructure Levy Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/948); paid to the LPA acting as charging authority; triggered on commencement of development under planning permission; calculated by rate-per-square-metre × net additional internal floorspace, with BCIS index uplift.)
2. How is CIL different from SDLT and from council tax? (SDLT under FA 2003 Part 4 = transaction tax on land purchase, paid to HMRC. Council tax under LGFA 1992 = annual occupation charge on dwellings, paid to billing authority. CIL = one-off development charge under PA 2008 Part 11, paid to charging authority. Separate statutory regimes, separate liability events, no offset between them. Same property can trigger all three at different stages.)
3. Which LPAs charge CIL? (Stage 2 verifies — approximately 50% of English LPAs have adopted a CIL charging schedule as of late 2025; the rest rely solely on s.106. Wales has its own equivalent regime. Scotland and Northern Ireland do not have CIL. The page lists the principal cities / regions with active CIL: London (Mayoral CIL + LPA-level CIL stacked), most of the South East, much of the Midlands, parts of the North.)
4. Who is liable to pay CIL? (Default: the landowner at the time of commencement of development. Assumption of Liability (Form 1 or online equivalent) before commencement transfers liability to the named party — typically the developer or the buyer in a serviced-plot arrangement. Liability runs with the land if not assumed; a buyer acquiring land mid-development inherits the CIL liability if no assumption is in place.)
5. How is the chargeable amount calculated? (Reg 40 formula: rate (£/m² as set in the LPA charging schedule for the relevant zone and development type) × net additional internal floorspace (m²), adjusted by the BCIS All-in Tender Price Index ratio between the charging-schedule's adoption date and the planning-permission grant date. Net additional internal floorspace = new gross internal floorspace minus existing-building floorspace where the existing building has been in "lawful use" for at least 6 months in aggregate in the 3 years before the development begins (reg 40(7)).)
6. What counts as "commencement of development" for CIL purposes? (Statutory trigger under s.209 PA 2008 + reg 7 + reg 8 SI 2010/948: a "material operation" within the meaning of s.56(4) TCPA 1990 — any work of construction, demolition, marker-out, or other operation that materially commences the permitted development. Site clearance and ground investigation are NOT material operations; first concrete pour or foundation excavation IS. The Form 6 Commencement Notice must be submitted to the LPA before any material operation.)
7. What are the principal reliefs and exemptions? (Minor development under reg 42: less than 100m² gross internal floorspace AND no new dwelling created — exempt. Self-build whole house under reg 54A: exempt subject to three-year clawback. Self-build annex / extension under regs 42A-42C: exempt subject to ancillary-to-main-dwelling tests. Charitable relief under regs 43-48: charity is the owner of the chargeable interest AND will use the building wholly or mainly for charitable purposes. Social housing relief under regs 49-50: developments delivering qualifying social housing units. Discretionary relief under s.49A for exceptional viability cases. Each relief is application-based — failure to apply on time forfeits the exemption.)
8. What happens if I do not file the Commencement Notice or fail to pay on time? (Surcharges under regs 83-85 + interest under reg 87 stack rapidly. Reg 83: failure to submit commencement notice before commencement = 20% surcharge or £2,500, whichever lower. Reg 84: failure to submit assumption of liability before commencement = 50% surcharge or £2,500, whichever lower. Reg 85: late payment = 5% surcharge after 30 days past due, additional 5% after 6 months, additional 5% after 12 months. Interest accrues at Bank of England base rate plus 2.5%. Aggregate surcharges + interest on a £100k CIL liability missed in standard payment can exceed £20k within 12 months. The LPA may also issue a CIL stop notice halting development. The CIL becomes a local land charge — registered against the land and discoverable on any subsequent disposal.)
9. How does CIL interact with s.106 planning obligations? (Regulation 122 of SI 2010/948 sets the s.106 policy test: a planning obligation may constitute a reason for granting permission only if it is necessary, directly related to the development, and fairly and reasonably related in scale and kind. The pre-April-2015 pooling restriction (no more than five s.106 contributions toward any single infrastructure project) was relaxed by SI 2019/1103 — see Stage 2 for current Reg 123 list / Infrastructure Funding Statement framework that replaced it. Practical division of labour: CIL handles strategic infrastructure (transport, schools, leisure across the area); s.106 handles site-specific mitigation (the access road for THIS development, the affordable-housing units within THIS scheme).)
10. What is the BCIS index uplift mechanic and why does it matter? (Reg 40(5)(c): the rate as set in the charging schedule on the day it was adopted is indexed by the ratio of the BCIS All-in Tender Price Index figure for the year in which planning permission was granted to the figure for the year in which the charging schedule was adopted. For LPAs that adopted their charging schedule in 2014-2015 and have not revised it, a 2026 planning-permission grant typically applies an uplift of 50%+ on the original rates. The uplift can move a borderline-viable development across the viability line and should be modelled at the planning-application stage, not on commencement.)
