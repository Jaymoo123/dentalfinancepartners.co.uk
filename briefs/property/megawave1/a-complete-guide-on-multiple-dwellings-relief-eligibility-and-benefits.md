# A Complete Guide on Multiple Dwellings Relief: Eligibility and Benefits (Historical / Transitional Reference, Pre-Abolition Architecture)

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (historical, now repealed for post-1-June-2024 effective dates): FA 2003 Schedule 6B (Multiple Dwellings Relief) — the full architecture: para 2 (claim mechanic and headline relief); para 3 (interpretation — "dwelling" definition); paras 4-5 (rate computation — average consideration per dwelling × number of dwellings); para 5A (later events triggering withdrawal); paras 6-7 (consequential and supplementary). Stage 2 must verify each para's verbatim text at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/6B/2024-05-31 (the pre-abolition version captured immediately before commencement of F(No.2)A 2024 s.7 on 1 June 2024).
- Primary (abolition): F(No.2)A 2024 c.12 s.7 — repeals Sch 6B for effective dates on or after 1 June 2024; preserves transitional cohort under s.7(4); anti-forestalling under s.7(5)-(6). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2024/12/section/7 on 2026-05-26.
- Supporting: FA 2003 s.119 (effective date); FA 2003 s.44 (substantial performance); FA 2003 s.108 (linked transactions — load-bearing for whether multiple property contracts aggregate into one MDR-eligible transaction); FA 2007 Sch 24 (penalty exposure for incorrect MDR claims on HMRC enquiry).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What was Multiple Dwellings Relief? (Historical statutory relief under FA 2003 Sch 6B, available 2011-2024; computed SDLT on average consideration per dwelling rather than total consideration, often producing material savings on portfolio acquisitions.)
2. When was MDR available? (For effective-date transactions from 19 July 2011 — FA 2011 Sch 22 commencement — to 31 May 2024 inclusive. Abolished for effective dates on or after 1 June 2024.)
3. What were the Sch 6B para 2 eligibility conditions? (Claim election; transaction(s) of two or more dwellings; "dwelling" definition under Sch 6B para 3; linked-transaction interaction under FA 2003 s.108; aggregation across linked transactions.)
4. How was the relief computed? (Sch 6B paras 4-5: SDLT computed as (consideration ÷ number of dwellings) × number of dwellings, rated under residential bands at the per-dwelling average. Minimum 1% rate floor. Worked example showing a £2m / 4-dwelling acquisition yielding pre-abolition MDR SDLT vs without-MDR SDLT — material saving for transitional cohort historical reference.)
5. What counted as a "dwelling" under Sch 6B para 3? (Statutory definition + interpretive case-law: Fiander v HMRC [2021] UKFTT (annexes); Brewer v HMRC; Doe v HMRC; HMRC SDLTM29905 historical guidance. The annexe / granny-flat question was the most litigated boundary.)
6. What were the para 5A later-event withdrawal rules? (Within 3 years of the relief claim, if dwelling-count fell below the eligibility threshold via conversion or amalgamation, relief was withdrawn — anti-forestalling at the post-completion stage.)
7. What is the position now for someone with a transitional claim? (Substantial-performance test under FA 2003 s.44 must be evidenced for any post-1-June-2024 completion of a pre-1-June-2024 contract; pre-6-March-2024 contracts have a separate carve-out under s.7(4) absent post-Budget variations; the burden is on the buyer to evidence the cohort.)
8. What is the HMRC enquiry posture on pre-abolition MDR claims? (Continues post-abolition; standard enquiry attack vectors are dwelling-count inflation, annexe-not-dwelling challenges, partial-occupancy / mixed-use re-characterisation. Penalty exposure under FA 2007 Sch 24 if claim was careless or deliberate.)
9. What replaces MDR for post-abolition acquirers? (Cross-link to A1 / A2 / Wave 9 page for the surviving-alternatives architecture — s.116(7) 6+-dwellings; Sch 15 SLP partnership; s.45 sub-sale.)
