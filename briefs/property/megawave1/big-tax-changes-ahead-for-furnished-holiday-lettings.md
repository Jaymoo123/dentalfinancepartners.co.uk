# Big Tax Changes Ahead for Furnished Holiday Lettings

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Finance Act 2025 Schedule 5 ("Furnished holiday lettings"), verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/8/schedule/5 on 2026-05-26 — five-Part architecture with commencement under Part 5 para 12 (tax year 2025-26 for IT; accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2025 for CT; CGT amendments commencing for disposals on or after 6 April 2025). The single largest change in the window.
- Supporting: F(No.2)A 2023 + FA 2024 + FA(No.2) 2024 + F(No.3)A 2024 (the SDLT-side and Budget-side reforms running parallel to FHL abolition); FA 2003 Sch 4ZA / Sch 6B (SDLT additional dwellings surcharge architecture — 5% from 31 October 2024 per F(No.2)A 2024); ITA 2007 s.272A (Section 24 — ingressing on former-FHL mortgage interest from 6 April 2025); FA 2007 Sch 24 (penalty regime — relevant for operators carrying mis-categorised pre-abolition positions into the post-abolition tax year); proposed Finance Act 2026 (the property-income surcharge announced for 6 April 2027 — 2% on UK property income, producing 22%/42%/47% effective rates); FA 2024 Sch 8 (the digital platforms reporting regime, DPI — phased commencement from 1 January 2024 reporting period); Making Tax Digital for ITSA staging at SI 2017/1018 + later orders (MTD-ITSA threshold £50,000 from 6 April 2026, £30,000 from 6 April 2027, £20,000 from 6 April 2028 — verified against current SI / HMRC guidance at write time per §16.35).
- House position reference: §6 "FHL — abolition transition" (the locked centrepiece change); §1 mainline (5% SDLT surcharge from 31 October 2024); §3 "Limited-company landlord — Section 24 carve-out" (the company-side advantage interaction); §7 "April 2027 property income tax surcharge — house framing" (the scheduled 2%/22%/42%/47%); §8 "Lifetime Allowance — abolished, replaced framework" (the pension-side regime — relevant cross-link via B14's interaction); §9 "IHT — landlord-relevant positions" (pensions-in-IHT 6 April 2027 — affecting former-FHL operators whose succession plan relies on pension wealth); §25.7 "FHL transitional under FA 2025 Sch 5" (capital allowances detail); §11 "Companies House reforms / ECCTA / Register of Overseas Entities" (cross-cutting compliance shifts within the window).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the single largest change to hit FHL operators in the 2024-2028 window? (FA 2025 Sch 5 — the regime abolition itself. Section 24 ingress; loss of BADR; loss of capital allowances on new spend; loss of pension-eligible "relevant UK earnings"; loss of full mortgage interest deduction for individual owners; loss of trading-treatment for sideways loss relief.)
2. What changed on 31 October 2024 and how does it interact with FHL operators buying additional stock? (The additional dwellings SDLT surcharge rose from 3% to 5% under F(No.2)A 2024 — applies to FHL acquisitions by any individual or company that already owns another residential property at completion. For a £400,000 FHL purchase, the increase is £8,000 of additional SDLT versus pre-31-October-2024.)
3. What changed on 30 October 2024 for FHL CGT disposals? (Residential CGT rates rose to 18%/24% from the previous 18%/28% — narrower spread between basic-rate and higher-rate. For pre-abolition FHL disposals where BADR was still available (10% rising to 14% from 6 April 2025), the rate change interacted with BADR planning; for post-abolition disposals the residential rate stands.)
4. The 6 March 2024 announcement — what locked in then? (Anti-forestalling for FHL disposals: any disposal of FHL property between announcement (6 March 2024) and abolition (5 April 2025) was tested for whether it was a "genuine" pre-abolition disposal versus an accelerated forestalling transaction; HMRC retained the right to apply post-abolition CGT rates to artificially-accelerated disposals. The pattern affected operators who sold before 5 April 2025 hoping to crystallise BADR at 10%.)
5. 1 April 2025 / 6 April 2025 — the abolition commencement dates. (FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 5 para 12: CT amendments from accounting periods beginning on or after 1 April 2025; IT and CGT amendments from tax year 2025-26 (6 April 2025 for IT, 6 April 2025 for CGT disposals). Capital allowances grandfathered pool carve-out under para 18.)
6. 6 April 2026 — what hits former-FHL operators? (Two changes. (a) MTD-ITSA staging: from 6 April 2026 individual former-FHL operators with combined trading + property-business gross qualifying income above £50,000 must use MTD-compatible software, file quarterly updates, and submit a year-end finalisation — many former-FHL operators sit above this threshold. (b) IHT BPR/APR combined £1 million cap commencement — affects landed estates and trading-business succession but not standard BTL or former FHL directly (which never qualified for BPR post-abolition per §9 HP).)
7. 6 April 2027 — the property-income surcharge. (Announced Autumn Budget 2024, scheduled in draft FA 2026; 2% surcharge on UK property income on top of standard income tax bands produces 22%/42%/47% effective rates. Former FHL income is UK property income for this purpose. Plan against the scheduled rates; do not assert as confirmed law until Royal Assent.)
8. 6 April 2027 — pensions-in-IHT. (Per §9 HP — unused defined-contribution pension funds become IHT-relevant from this date. Affects former-FHL operators whose retirement plan was "save into the pension via FHL relevant-UK-earnings, draw down in retirement" — that plan was already broken at 5 April 2025 (B14 cross-link) and the IHT side compounds the problem.)
9. 6 April 2028 — MTD-ITSA threshold drops to £20,000. (Catches lower-turnover former-FHL operators previously sitting below the staged thresholds. A single former-FHL with £25,000 gross rent + £12,000 pension drawdown would not be inside MTD-ITSA at 6 April 2026 (under £50k) but is inside it at 6 April 2027 (over £30k) and stays inside it at 6 April 2028 (over £20k).)
10. What is the cumulative effect on a typical higher-rate-taxpayer former-FHL owner across the window? (Worked example — Stage 2: takes a £75,000 gross rent, £25,000 interest, 2 properties, higher-rate-taxpayer profile through 2024/25 → 2025/26 → 2026/27 → 2027/28 — shows the net-of-tax change at each date and the cumulative effect.)
