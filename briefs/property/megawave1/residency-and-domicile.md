# UK Tax Residency and Domicile for Property Investors: The Statutory Residence Test, the End of the Domicile Concept for Income / CGT, the New Long-Term Resident Test for IHT, and How Each Drives Your UK Property Tax Position

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (residence): **Finance Act 2013 Schedule 45 — Statutory Residence Test (SRT)** — the determining mechanism for whether an individual is UK-resident in a given tax year. Three-stage cascade:
  - **Part 1 — Automatic non-residence tests:** if any one of these is met, the individual is automatically non-resident for the tax year (e.g. fewer than 16 days in UK if UK-resident in any of the 3 preceding tax years; fewer than 46 days if not UK-resident in any of the 3 preceding tax years; working full-time abroad with limited UK presence).
  - **Part 2 — Automatic UK residence tests:** if any one is met, the individual is automatically UK-resident (e.g. 183+ days in UK; UK home test; full-time work in UK).
  - **Part 3 — Sufficient ties test:** if neither automatic test is met, the individual's residence depends on the combination of (a) day-count in the UK + (b) number of UK ties (family tie, accommodation tie, work tie, 90-day tie, country tie — the last only for "leavers"). The day-band × ties matrix determines residence.
  Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2013/29/schedule/45 on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (split-year): **FA 2013 Schedule 45 Part 3 — Split-year treatment.** Eight cases (Cases 1-8) under which the tax year is split into a UK-resident part and a non-resident part — the individual is UK-resident for the year as a whole under SRT but the year is split for income / CGT purposes. Cases 1-3 cover departure scenarios; Cases 4-8 cover arrival scenarios. Each case has detailed conditions.
- Primary (IHT residence — the LTR test): **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C + ss.267ZC-267ZF** (inserted by FA 2025 architecture). The Long-Term Resident (LTR) test: an individual is treated as long-term UK resident for IHT purposes if UK-resident for 10 of the preceding 20 tax years (with s.6B young-person variation for under-20s, and a tail-period for departure). LTR triggers full UK IHT on worldwide-situs assets. Cross-referenced to HP §22.X.
- Primary (FIG regime for new arrivals): **ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J** (inserted by FA 2025 s.37) — 4-year foreign-income-and-gains exemption for qualifying new residents with 10 tax years of prior non-residence. Cross-referenced to HP §17.8.
- Primary (remittance basis abolition): **FA 2025 s.40 + Sch 9** — the historic remittance basis (ITA 2007 ss.809A-809Z10) is unavailable from tax year 2025-26 onwards. Cross-referenced to HP §17.6 and the existing C7 brief.
- Supporting (TRF + CGT rebasing): **FA 2025 s.41 + Sch 10** (TRF) + **FA 2025 s.42 + Sch 11** (CGT rebasing election). Cross-referenced to HP §17.9 + §17.10.
- Supporting (NRL scheme): **Income Tax Act 2007 Part 14 Chapter 5 (ss.971-973) + SI 1995/2902** — the Non-Resident Landlord scheme. Operative ONLY for landlords whose "usual place of abode" is outside the UK — the test is similar to but not identical to the SRT residence test (the NRL scheme uses a "usual place of abode" test with its own HMRC guidance, not the SRT directly). Cross-referenced to HP §17.5.
- Supporting (NRCGT): **TCGA 1992 Schedule 1A + Sch 4ZZB** — non-resident CGT on disposals of UK land. Applies to non-UK-residents regardless of domicile. 60-day reporting via NRCGT return. Cross-referenced to HP §17.4.
- Supporting (temporary non-residence): **TCGA 1992 s.10A** — anti-avoidance against short-term emigration to crystallise foreign gains; gains realised during a "temporary period of non-residence" (typically 5 years) are taxed in the year of return.
- Supporting (the historic domicile concept — residual operation): the common-law concept of domicile (domicile of origin / dependence / choice) is NOT defined in tax statute but in private international law (codified at e.g. Domicile and Matrimonial Proceedings Act 1973 s.4 for domicile of dependence). Domicile continues to operate for:
  - **Trust-attribution mechanics** under certain pre-FA-2025 architectures (excluded property trust treatment per IHTA 1984 s.48 / new s.48ZA inserted by FA 2025).
  - **Private international law** contexts (cross-border succession, family law, conflict of laws).
  - **DTAs (Double Taxation Agreements)** with countries that retain a domicile-based concept in the residence article.
  Domicile is NO LONGER operative for UK income tax + CGT (post-FA 2025) and substantially replaced for IHT by the LTR test. Stage 2 verifies the residual operation against current legislation + HMRC RDR / RDRM guidance.
- House position reference: **HP §17.1** (SRT). **HP §17.2** (split-year). **HP §17.3** (temporary non-residence). **HP §17.4** (NRCGT). **HP §17.5** (NRL scheme). **HP §17.6** (domicile reform headline framing). **HP §17.7** (expat compliance points). **HP §17.8** (FIG). **HP §17.9** (TRF). **HP §17.10** (CGT rebasing). **HP §22.X** (IHT LTR). **HP §15.6** (IHT non-resident domicile / residence test). naive 183-day-counting misses Cases 1-3 / 4-8 split-year treatment and the work-tie / family-tie analysis; (b) the **domicile concept ENDED for income tax + CGT on 6 April 2025** — competitor pages routinely still discuss "non-dom status" as if it confers UK tax benefits; from 2025-26 onwards, only RESIDENCE matters for income / CGT; (c) the **LTR test is the new IHT gatekeeper, not domicile** — the historic "deemed dom 15-of-20" test is gone; the new test is "UK-resident for 10 of the preceding 20 tax years" (HP §22.X); (e) the **NRCGT applies regardless of domicile** — overseas-resident owners of UK property pay UK CGT on disposal regardless of domicile, via the NRCGT return within 60 days of completion (HP §17.4); (g) the **split-year treatment is conditional, not automatic** — an arrival or departure in the middle of a tax year does NOT automatically split the year; one of the eight Cases (Cases 1-3 for departure; Cases 4-8 for arrival) must be met (HP §17.2); (h) the **residual operation of domicile** — domicile continues to operate for trust-attribution mechanics (the s.48ZA excluded-property trust transitional architecture), for private-international-law contexts, and for DTA application where a treaty retains domicile language; competitor pages routinely state "domicile no longer matters" which is wrong in nuance.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is UK tax residence and how is it determined? (UK tax residence is determined by the Statutory Residence Test (SRT) at Finance Act 2013 Sch 45. Three-stage cascade: (1) automatic non-residence tests — if any one is met, you are non-resident; (2) automatic UK residence tests — if any one is met, you are UK-resident; (3) sufficient ties test — combination of day-count + UK ties (family / accommodation / work / 90-day / country tie). Residence is determined tax-year-by-tax-year. Naive 183-day-counting misses the cascade structure and the "ties" analysis.)
2. What are the SRT automatic non-residence tests? (Three: (1) fewer than 16 days in UK if UK-resident in any of the 3 preceding tax years; (2) fewer than 46 days if not UK-resident in any of the 3 preceding tax years; (3) full-time work overseas with limited UK presence (FA 2013 Sch 45 para 14). The first test is the strict "leaver" test; the second is the strict "non-leaver" test; the third covers the FT-employed overseas case.)
3. What are the SRT automatic UK residence tests? (Three: (1) 183+ days in UK in the tax year; (2) UK home test (only home is in UK, or all homes are UK + you are present for 91+ days in tax year); (3) full-time work in UK over a 365-day period. The first test is the simplest.)
4. What is the sufficient ties test? (FA 2013 Sch 45 paras 17-20. Four ties for arrivers (family, accommodation, work, 90-day); five ties for leavers (the four arriver ties plus a "country tie" — more days in UK than in any other single country). The number of ties combined with day-count determines residence per the FA 2013 Sch 45 table — typically 16-45 days = "all 4 ties needed for residence"; 46-90 days = "3 ties"; 91-120 days = "2 ties"; 121-182 days = "1 tie" (for arrivers). The table for leavers is harsher.)
5. What is split-year treatment and when does it apply? (Cases 1-8 under FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 3. Each case has specific conditions. Departure cases (1-3) typically require leaving the UK to take up full-time overseas work or to live with a partner abroad. Arrival cases (4-8) typically require coming to the UK to take up full-time UK work, to live with a partner in the UK, or to acquire a UK home. The case must be claimed in the self-assessment return; it does NOT apply automatically.)
6. What was the historic domicile concept and what is its current operation? (Domicile is a common-law concept from private international law: domicile of origin (acquired at birth from your father's domicile or, if illegitimate, your mother's); domicile of dependence (for under-16s, follows the parent); domicile of choice (acquired by adult by long-term residence in a country + intention to remain indefinitely). Pre-6-April-2025, UK domicile or deemed-dom status drove the UK tax position on foreign income / gains (remittance basis vs arising basis) + IHT. Post-6-April-2025, domicile has substantially ENDED its operation for income tax + CGT (FA 2025 Sch 9) and substantially ENDED for IHT (replaced by the LTR test under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C). Residual operation: trust-attribution under pre-FA-2025 architectures (s.48ZA transitional); private international law; certain DTAs.)
7. What is the Long-Term Resident (LTR) test for IHT? (IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C inserted by FA 2025. An individual is long-term UK resident for IHT purposes if UK-resident for 10 of the preceding 20 tax years (with s.6B young-person variation for under-20s + a tail-period for departure). LTR triggers UK IHT on WORLDWIDE-situs assets (not just UK-situs). The 10-of-20 test replaces the historic deemed-dom 15-of-20 income tax + IHT test. See HP §22.X for operational depth.)
8. How does residency interact with UK property? (UK-source rental income is taxed regardless of residence — UK landlords pay UK income tax on UK rental income whether or not they are UK-resident. Non-UK-resident landlords use the NRL scheme (HP §17.5). UK property capital gains are taxed regardless of residence under NRCGT (TCGA 1992 Sch 4ZZB) — 60-day reporting required. ATED applies to companies regardless of company residence (HP §18). The residence question matters MOST for the worldwide-asset position: a UK-resident landlord with overseas-property income / gains is taxed on the worldwide picture (subject to FIG if eligible); a non-resident UK-property owner is taxed only on UK-source income / gains.)
9. What happens if I leave the UK — what are the residency cliffs? (Three operative tests on departure: (1) SRT day-count + ties test for the current tax year — split-year claim possible under Cases 1-3 if conditions met; (2) NRL scheme "usual place of abode" trigger — operative ahead of SRT in some scenarios (HP §17.5); (3) TCGA 1992 s.10A "temporary period of non-residence" anti-avoidance — gains realised during a temporary period (typically 5 years away from UK) are taxed in the year of return. The cliff is "are you really leaving?" — if you return within 5 years, s.10A may catch gains realised during the absence (HP §17.3).)
10. What happens if I arrive in the UK — what are the planning windows? (Three operative windows: (1) split-year claim under SRT Cases 4-8 to limit UK tax to the post-arrival period; (2) FIG regime claim under ITTOIA 2005 s.845A — 4 years of full foreign-income / gains exemption if the 10-year-prior-non-residence gateway is met (HP §17.8) — but the claim forfeits personal allowance + CGT AEA; (3) IHT LTR clock — the 10-of-20 test starts ticking from the first UK-resident year; planning around the 10-year horizon is critical for high-net-worth arrivals.)
11. How do residency and domicile interact in 2026? (Headline: residence drives income tax + CGT (post-FA 2025); residence (via LTR test) drives IHT (post-FA 2025); domicile drives only the residual trust-attribution mechanics + private-international-law contexts. The interaction is therefore SIMPLE: residence is the primary concept; domicile is the legacy / niche concept. The complexity is in the TRANSITIONAL architecture for the legacy non-dom cohort (HP §17.9 TRF + §17.10 CGT rebasing) and the trust-side transitional rules (HP §22.16 + §22.17).)
12. What should a UK-property investor do? (Three-step assessment: (a) determine current residence under SRT for the relevant tax year; (b) determine LTR status under the IHT 10-of-20 test; (c) identify any pre-2025 legacy non-dom claims that may activate the TRF window or the CGT rebasing election (deep-dive at the existing site pages). For landlords with overseas income / gains: model the FIG / arising-basis choice year-by-year. For landlords approaching the 10-year LTR threshold: review trust structures and worldwide-asset positions. Cross-link to firm's expat / non-dom advisory landing page.)
