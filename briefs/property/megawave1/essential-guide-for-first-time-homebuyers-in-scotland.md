# Essential Guide for First-Time Homebuyers in Scotland: LBTT, First-Time Buyer Relief, and What to Plan For in 2026/27

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013 asp 11 ("LBTT(S)A 2013"), Schedule 4A "First-time buyer relief" - verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2013/11/schedule/4A on 2026-05-26. Schedule 4A relieves the tax "in respect of the first £175,000 of consideration payable in respect of any chargeable transaction" (verbatim) where the buyer (a) has not previously acquired a major interest in a dwelling, (b) has not previously acquired an equivalent interest in a dwelling outside Scotland, and (c) intends to occupy the dwelling as their only or main residence.
- Supporting: LBTT(S)A 2013 s.10 (effective date - substantial-performance test, verified mirror of FA 2003 s.44); s.24 (rate-setting power, used annually by Scottish Statutory Instrument); s.29 (return + 30-day filing window); s.59(8) (six-or-more-dwellings non-residential treatment, parallels FA 2003 s.116(7) for completeness even though FTBs almost never trigger it); Schedule 2A (Additional Dwelling Supplement framework, relevant for buyer-with-existing-property-overseas edge cases).
- House position reference: §23.4 main text (main residential LBTT rates 2026/27: £145k nil, 2% to £250k, 5% to £325k, 10% to £750k, 12% above; rates frozen for 2026/27 per Scottish Budget); §23.4 Scottish FTB-relief subsection (£175k nil-band uplift, £600 maximum saving, no upper property-value ceiling, worldwide previous-ownership test); §23.5 (ADS at 8% from 5 December 2024, applies to whole purchase price not marginal, includes interaction with FTB cases where any joint buyer fails the FTB test); §23.8 cross-jurisdictional table (the Scotland vs England + NI vs Wales contrast every FTB page must surface); §23.10 citations block (revenue.scot LBTT3048 source page verified at https://revenue.scot/taxes/land-buildings-transaction-tax/lbtt-legislation-guidance/lbtt3001-exemptions-reliefs/lbtt3010-tax-reliefs/lbtt3048-first-time-buyer-relief on 2026-05-26 confirms £175,000 nil-band uplift + £600 maximum saving + worldwide previous-ownership test + intent-to-occupy-as-only-or-main-residence requirement).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is LBTT and why is it different from SDLT? (One-paragraph history: LBTT replaced SDLT in Scotland on 1 April 2015 under the Scotland Act 2012 devolution of property-transfer tax; administered by Revenue Scotland rather than HMRC; its own bands, surcharge regime (ADS), and reliefs.)
2. What rate of LBTT will I pay on my first home? (The £145,000 standard nil band, plus the £175,000 FTB uplift; worked examples at £150k, £200k, £275k, £350k showing exact LBTT due before and after FTB relief; reference Scottish Budget 2026/27 LBTT-rates-frozen position.)
3. Am I a first-time buyer for LBTT relief purposes? (Plain-English decision-tree from Sch 4A: never owned a dwelling anywhere in the world; intend to occupy as only or main residence; transaction not linked to ADS; joint-buyer rule that all buyers must be FTBs or relief is lost.)
4. How does the £600 maximum saving work? (LBTT on the £145k-£175k slice = 2% × £30,000 = £600. The relief is a nil-band uplift, NOT a flat £600 credit; for a £150k purchase the saving is only £100; for a £180k purchase the saving is the full £600; for a £500k purchase the saving is still only £600.)
5. Does first-time buyer relief have an upper property-value cap? (NO. Unlike England's SDLT FTB relief which is fully withdrawn above £500,000, Scotland's relief applies regardless of purchase price. A FTB buying a £900,000 home still gets the £600 saving.)
6. Do I have to file a return and how long do I have? (LBTT return due to Revenue Scotland within 30 days of effective date per LBTT(S)A 2013 s.29; contrast England's 14-day window. Solicitor usually files. Payment due before registration of title.)
7. What if I am buying with my partner / parent / sibling? (Joint-buyer rule: every buyer must satisfy the FTB conditions or the transaction loses FTB relief entirely. A buyer with a partner who already owns a flat anywhere in the world loses relief on the whole transaction.)
8. What if I already own a property abroad? (Worldwide test: any prior dwelling interest anywhere in the world disqualifies the buyer from FTB relief. Inherited interests count; bare-trust beneficial interests count; co-ownership interests count. Even a small interest in a foreign property defeats the relief.)
9. Could I be hit by the Additional Dwelling Supplement (ADS)? (Scenario: buyer is technically buying their first Scottish home but already owns a flat in London. ADS at 8% on the entire Scottish purchase price applies because they own another dwelling at the effective date. FTB relief is unavailable because they have previously owned a dwelling. The combination is brutal: full LBTT + 8% ADS, no FTB nil-band uplift. Cross-link to the ADS page.)
10. What happens between offer and completion (mortgage offer, missives, conclusion of missives, settlement)? (Effective date for LBTT purposes is usually settlement / substantial performance; the £175k FTB nil band is tested at that date. Mortgage offer and missives stage do not crystallise the LBTT position.)
11. Can I get the relief back if I forgot to claim at the time? (Yes - LBTT return can be amended within 12 months of original return; beyond that a repayment claim within the statutory time limits applies. Cross-link to the LBTT review / amendments page.)
