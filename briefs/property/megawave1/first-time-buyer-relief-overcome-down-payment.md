# First-Time Buyer Relief and the Deposit Challenge: How SDLT Savings Free Up Down-Payment Cash, and Where They Stop

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: FA 2003 s.57B "First-time buyers" — "(1) Schedule 6ZA provides relief for first-time buyers." Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/section/57B on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (operative): FA 2003 Schedule 6ZA "Relief for first-time buyers" — para 1(3) cap at £500,000; para 4 Table A 0% / 5% bands. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/14/schedule/6ZA on 2026-05-26.
- Supporting (deposit-side government schemes): Lifetime ISA — Savings (Government Contributions) Act 2017 c. 16 (LISA, 25% government bonus on up to £4,000 annual contribution, usable for first home purchase up to £450,000 from age 18 to age 39 opening, full conditions at https://www.gov.uk/lifetime-isa). Help to Buy ISA — closed to new accounts from 30 November 2019; existing holders can continue saving until 30 November 2029 and claim the 25% bonus on first home purchase up to a property value of £250,000 outside London / £450,000 in London. Verify all parameters at Stage 2 write per §16.35.
- Supporting (mortgage-side): not statutorily anchored on Sch 6ZA but practically intertwined — LTV bands, FCA MCOB rules, mortgage guarantee schemes; Stage 2 sub-agent references current LTV practice in passing (high-LTV 95% products + 100% products that have re-emerged) without giving regulated mortgage advice.
- House position reference: §1 main text (FTB relief summary line + 5% additional dwellings surcharge interaction); §1.I Wave 9 lock (surcharge mechanics). **Same NEW HP-LOCK CANDIDATE: §1.K "First-Time Buyers' Relief"** anchors A12 alongside A7 + A10 + A11.

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **practical-financial-planning angle** in the FTB cluster. Where A7 is the rate-table reference, A10 the legal-eligibility reference, and A11 the calculator, A12 is the **conversion-optimised page** that addresses the long-tail SERP query verbatim ("first-time buyer relief overcome down payment") and explains how the SDLT saving translates into deposit availability. The query is recognisably one a buyer types when they are mid-affordability-calculation, asking: does this relief help me get to a deposit? The honest answer is: it helps modestly (up to £5,000 in SDLT saving redirects to deposit, equivalent to ~2% of a £250k purchase or ~1% of a £500k purchase); it is not transformative in isolation; it works in combination with Lifetime ISA, Help to Buy ISA, family help, mortgage-product choice, and the wider affordability picture.

This page covers six things competitor SERP coverage routinely under-serves: (a) the **cash-flow translation** — how a £2,500 or £5,000 SDLT saving actually appears in the buyer's transaction (not as cash in hand but as a smaller line on the conveyancer's completion statement, freeing up cash that would otherwise have come from the buyer's deposit account); (b) the **interaction with Lifetime ISA mechanics** — LISA pays a 25% government bonus on contributions up to £4,000 per year, useful for first home purchase up to £450,000 property value (lower than the £500k FTB-relief cap, so a £450k-£500k purchase qualifies for FTB SDLT relief but NOT for LISA bonus on completion); (c) the **interaction with Help to Buy ISA** for holders who opened pre-30 November 2019, with a £250k London / £450k outside-London cap; (d) the **trap framing for buyers planning at the edge of affordability** — the £500k FTB-relief cliff edge is fact-relevant for buyers stretching toward the £500k purchase price boundary; a £500,001 purchase costs £2,500+ more in SDLT than a £499,999 purchase, and that £2,500+ comes off the deposit; (e) the **honest expectation-setting** — competitor pages frequently overstate the relief's deposit-availability effect; the page calibrates the buyer's expectation realistically; (f) the **joint-purchase consideration** — if the buyer's partner has previously owned property, the joint-purchase trap eliminates the SDLT saving AND adds the 5% additional dwellings surcharge, materially worsening the deposit position not improving it.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. How does FTB relief help me with my deposit? (Honest framing: the relief reduces SDLT payable at completion by up to £5,000; that £5,000 reduction means £5,000 less cash needed at completion; that cash either stays in the deposit account (freeing it for other uses) or reduces the total deposit-plus-completion-costs the buyer needs to assemble. It is not a deposit grant; it is a cost reduction. Worked example: £400k purchase, FTB relief saves £5,000 (was £10,000, becomes £5,000); buyer who was £5,000 short of the assembled-deposit-plus-completion target now reaches it.)
2. How much does FTB relief actually save me? (£0 to £5,000 depending on purchase price. Below £300k: £0 if standard residential SDLT would be £0 anyway. £300k-£500k: increasing saving up to £5,000 at the £500k cap. Above £500k: £0 because relief is unavailable. Cross-link to A11 for the worked-examples table.)
3. Can I combine FTB relief with a Lifetime ISA? (Yes, if both schemes' eligibility tests are met independently. LISA requires the property to be £450k or below to claim the 25% government bonus at completion; FTB-relief allows up to £500k consideration. The two schemes coexist; LISA is a deposit-side scheme, FTB-relief is a transaction-cost scheme.)
4. What about Help to Buy ISA? (Only available to holders who opened the account before 30 November 2019. Closed to new accounts. Existing holders can claim the 25% bonus on first home purchase up to £250k property value (£450k in London) until 30 November 2029. Verifiable at gov.uk/help-to-buy-isa.)
5. Does FTB relief work for joint purchases with my partner? (Only if both partners are first-time buyers. If your partner has previously owned property anywhere in the world, FTB relief is unavailable on the whole transaction AND if the partner still owns property the 5% additional dwellings surcharge applies — materially worse than the no-relief baseline. Cross-link to A10 for the joint-purchase trap walkthrough.)
6. What if I am buying at the edge of £500k? (The cliff edge at £500,000 is binary. £499,999 = full FTB relief. £500,001 = no FTB relief. The £2 increase produces a £2,500+ SDLT increase that comes off your deposit / completion cash. For buyers near the cap, negotiating the purchase price below £500k is often more valuable than ostensibly small price differences higher up the band.)
7. Are there any traps where the relief actually costs me more than expected? (Two main ones: the joint-purchase trap (partner's prior ownership eliminates relief and adds surcharge); the worldwide-ownership disqualification (overseas property inherited or owned previously disqualifies even where the buyer has never owned UK property). Stage 2 walks both.)
8. How does this compare to Wales and Scotland? (Wales: no separate FTB relief; £225k nil band on Welsh LTT serves the function. Scotland: separate LBTT FTB relief under LBTT(S)A 2013 Sch 4A with different parameters (no upper property-value ceiling, narrower nil-band uplift). Cross-link to the Scottish + Welsh FTB pages.)
9. What is the bottom-line deposit-availability impact of FTB relief? (Modest. Useful in combination with LISA, family help, and good mortgage shopping. Not transformative in isolation. Honest expectation-setting beats over-promise from competitor SERP.)
10. What should I do next? (Specific next steps: verify FTB eligibility on the worldwide test; check joint-purchase position with partner; check LISA / Help to Buy ISA balance; obtain an Agreement-in-Principle from a mortgage lender; instruct a conveyancer who will handle the FTB-relief claim on the SDLT return. Generic but well-targeted next-step list for the at-decision-point reader.)
