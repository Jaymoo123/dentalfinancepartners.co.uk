# Welsh LTT Calculator: How to Work Out Your Land Transaction Tax Bill in Wales for 2026/27

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: **Land Transaction Tax and Anti-avoidance of Devolved Taxes (Wales) Act 2017, sections 24 (residential rates) and 25 (non-residential rates)** at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/anaw/2017/1/contents`, with the operative rate-setting in the **Land Transaction Tax (Tax Bands and Tax Rates) (Wales) Regulations** as periodically amended. Current operative regulations include the Land Transaction Tax (Tax Bands and Tax Rates) (Wales) (Amendment) Regulations 2024 (higher residential rates uplift +1pp across all six bands, in force 11 December 2024). All citations verified against legislation.gov.uk and gov.wales on 2026-05-26 per §16.35. Rates table at Stage 2 must be re-verified at write time as Welsh Government may amend by regulations between seed and publication.
- Supporting: **LTTA 2017 s.72** (residential / non-residential property definition — feeds into which rate table applies). **LTTA 2017 Sch 5** (higher rates for additional dwellings — standalone band structure starting at 5% from £1, not a flat surcharge per §23.2). **LTTA 2017 s.10** (effective date — controls which rate table is in force). **LTTA 2017 s.41** (return filing within 30 days of the effective date). **LTTA 2017 Sch 11-19** (various reliefs that reduce the headline calculation — group, charities, partnership, sub-sale, MDR retained per §23.3, alternative finance per Sch 10).
- House position reference: **§23.1** (main residential rates 2026/27 — bands and percentages); **§23.2** (higher residential rates 2026/27 — six-band standalone structure, 5%-17%); **§23.3** (MDR retained with modifications, non-residential bands, other reliefs); **§23.8** (cross-jurisdictional 4-nation comparison table); **§23.11** do-not-write (Welsh higher rates are NOT a flat surcharge stacked on standard bands — they are a standalone band structure; the calculator walkthrough must use the standalone structure for higher-rates buyers, NOT the surcharge-on-top pattern that applies to SDLT under FA 2003 Sch 4ZA).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is Welsh Land Transaction Tax and when does the calculation engage? (LTTA 2017 s.10 effective date = substantial performance or completion; LTT applies to acquisitions of "land in Wales" — the location of the property determines the jurisdiction, not the buyer's residence.)
2. Which rate table applies — main residential, higher residential, non-residential? (LTTA 2017 s.72 residential / non-residential test; LTTA 2017 Sch 5 higher-rates test for buyers already owning a dwelling.)
3. What are the 2026/27 main residential rates and bands? (Walk the table: 0% to £225,000; 6% £225,001 to £400,000; 7.5% £400,001 to £750,000; 10% £750,001 to £1,500,000; 12% above. Verify against gov.wales at write time per §16.35.)
4. What are the 2026/27 higher residential rates and bands (additional dwellings)? (Walk the table: 5% to £180,000; 8.5% £180,001 to £250,000; 10% £250,001 to £400,000; 12.5% £400,001 to £750,000; 15% £750,001 to £1,500,000; 17% above. The higher-rates table is a standalone band structure, NOT a flat surcharge on top of the main rates per §23.2.)
5. What are the 2026/27 non-residential rates? (0% to £225,000; 1% £225,001 to £250,000; 5% £250,001 to £1,000,000; 6% above. Same lower-band threshold as residential but materially lower above £400,000.)
6. How is the calculation done band by band on a £350,000 main-residence purchase? (Worked example showing the £225,000 at 0% + £125,000 at 6% = £7,500 calculation.)
7. How is the calculation done band by band on a £350,000 higher-rates purchase (buyer already owns a dwelling)? (Worked example showing the £180,000 at 5% + £70,000 at 8.5% + £100,000 at 10% = £9,000 + £5,950 + £10,000 = £24,950 calculation. Note: the standalone-band-structure means the £225,000 nil-band advantage of the main rates does NOT apply to higher-rates buyers — a common mistake.)
8. What is the position for first-time buyers in Wales? (No separate FTB relief in Wales; the £225,000 nil band is materially higher than SDLT's £125,000 and serves the same buyer-protection function. A first-time buyer purchasing under £225,000 pays no LTT; one purchasing at £350,000 pays £7,500 per the worked example, materially less than the SDLT bill on the same price.)
9. How does the calculation change if the buyer is replacing their main residence? (Higher rates may apply at the effective date if the buyer has not yet sold the previous main residence; refund route under LTTA 2017 Sch 5 para 23 within 36 months of disposal if the sale completes within the permitted period. The calculator walkthrough should show both the at-purchase calculation and the post-refund calculation.)
10. How does the calculation interact with MDR (multiple dwellings relief) for a 2+ dwellings transaction? (MDR retained in Wales but modified by the 2025 and 2026 Regulations — minimum effective rate, subsidiary-dwelling carve-out for main-residence-with-annexe purchases. Where MDR applies, the calculation averages the consideration across the dwellings and applies the rate table to the per-dwelling figure, subject to the minimum rate floor.)
11. What happens when the property straddles the Wales/England border? (LTTA 2017 Sch 22 cross-border apportionment — separate returns to HMRC and the WRA each on the apportioned consideration; the higher-rates / 5% surcharge test bites on each jurisdiction's share separately.)
