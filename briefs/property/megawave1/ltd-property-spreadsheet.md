# Limited Company Property Spreadsheet: Template for SPV Landlord Accounting and Corporation Tax Preparation

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: **Corporation Tax Act 2010 (CTA 2010) Parts 4-5** — corporation tax computation for property businesses (the company-side property income computation rules); **CTA 2010 ss.439-455** — close company rules including director's loan account (s.455 charge); **CTA 2009 Part 4** — property income computation for companies (interaction with the company tax return CT600). All citations to be verified verbatim at Stage 2 against legislation.gov.uk per §16.35.
- Supporting: **Companies Act 2006** ss.394-414 (statutory accounts requirements for small companies; FRS 102 Section 1A applies to most property SPVs); **Companies Act 2006 ss.853A-853L** (confirmation statement annual filing); **FA 2003 Sch 4A** (15% SDLT flat rate on residential dwellings above £500,000 acquired by non-natural persons — ATED-related charge, cross-reference per §1 main text); **FA 2013 Part 3** (ATED — annual tax on enveloped dwellings, for any property held by the company with a value above £500,000 at the relevant valuation date); **CTA 2010 s.1158-1175** (REITs — generally not engaged for typical small property SPVs but cited for completeness); **CTA 2009 s.250** (loan relationships — intra-group / director loan interest treatment).
- House position reference: **§21** Wave 4 LtdCo + FIC extension (the working-detail house position for limited-company property structures, covering corporation tax, director's loan account mechanics, dividend extraction, alphabet shares, SPV ringfencing); **§21.2** (Arctic Systems carve-out for alphabet share structures); **§21.5** (FIC mechanics — generic family investment company); **§22** (IHT estate planning interaction); **§4** (Section 24 finance cost restriction — NOT applicable to companies; the page must explicitly close this question down because it is the most-asked sole-trader-versus-Ltd-Co question); **§19** (MTD ITSA — also NOT applicable to companies on rental income; companies file CT600, not MTD ITSA). **§24** (Form 17 + joint-ownership — NOT directly applicable to companies, but cross-link relevant for spouses holding shares).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **limited-company property spreadsheet template page** for buy-to-let SPVs. The site already carries `landlord-accounting-spreadsheet-template-free-excel-guide` (the existing sole-trader landlord spreadsheet page, written for individual landlords managing rental property in their own name — covers income tracking, expense categories, allowable deductions for the self-assessment return, MTD ITSA mentions). This B12 page is **distinct from** that page on the basic structural axis: limited-company property accounting has fundamentally different requirements that a sole-trader spreadsheet does not address.

The distinguishing angle is **the SPV-specific columns and tabs that a sole-trader spreadsheet does not have**: (a) corporation tax computation columns (not income tax — the rate is currently 19% small profits / 25% main rate / marginal relief between £50k and £250k profits, with associated-company rules per CTA 2010 s.18N-18P reducing the bands); (b) director's loan account tracking (advances to and from the director, s.455 charge if outstanding 9 months after year end, BIK on cheap-loan benefit, interest at HMRC official rate); (c) dividend planning columns (gross dividend declared, tax-credit treatment for the shareholder, alphabet-share allocations if multiple classes); (d) share class tracking (A / B / C shares with differential dividend rights, designed for income-splitting between spouses or for adult-child gifting without altering control — per §21.2); (e) annual filing prompts (CT600 corporation tax return within 12 months of year end, statutory accounts within 9 months for small companies, confirmation statement annually); (f) ATED prompt columns (any property held with value above £500k at the most recent revaluation date triggers ATED filing — currently 1 April 2022 for the 2023-2028 period); (g) related-party rent tracking (where the company occupies a property let by a director, or rents to a director's family — connected-party rules and market-rent requirements); (h) section-24-NOT-applicable confirmation (Ltd Co property is not subject to FA 2015 finance cost restriction — companies deduct mortgage interest fully against rental profits per CTA 2009 Part 5 loan relationships, unlike sole-trader landlords who suffer the basic-rate-tax-credit-only treatment).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is a limited-company property spreadsheet and why does it need to be different from a sole-trader landlord spreadsheet? (Different tax engine — corporation tax not income tax; different filing obligations — CT600 + statutory accounts + confirmation statement; different deduction rules — full mortgage interest deduction vs Section 24 restriction; different shareholder-extraction mechanics — dividends + salary vs sole-trader profit drawing.)
2. What tabs and columns should the spreadsheet contain at minimum? (Property-level income and expense ledger per property; consolidated profit-and-loss view; director's loan account ledger; dividend declaration log; share-class-allocation tracker if alphabet shares in use; year-end checklist; ATED prompt; corporation tax computation tab linking to the P&L.)
3. How does the spreadsheet feed into the corporation tax return (CT600)? (Schedule of property income per CTA 2009 Part 4; deductible expenses per the property-business computation rules; loan-relationship interest deduction; capital allowances on commercial fixtures only — residential FHL phase-out per §FHL B13-B18 picks in this MW; CT600 box-by-box mapping summary on a dedicated tab.)
4. How does the spreadsheet handle director's loan account and the s.455 charge? (Advances to director recorded; balance at year end checked against 9-month rule; s.455 charge calculated where outstanding; interest on overdrawn director's loan at HMRC official rate to avoid BIK; repayment-and-bed-and-breakfast anti-avoidance per CTA 2010 s.464A — 30-day window.)
5. How does the spreadsheet handle dividends and shareholder-extraction planning? (Dividend declarations recorded by class; shareholder tax credit and dividend tax band consequences modelled at the shareholder level for income-extraction planning; interaction with PAYE salary for the director.)
6. What is the ATED prompt and when does it engage? (FA 2013 Part 3 — annual filing required where the company holds any residential dwelling worth more than £500,000 at the relevant valuation date. Most small SPVs with sub-£500k properties have no ATED; the prompt protects against the threshold being crossed by acquisition or revaluation. Cross-reference Wave 7+ ATED cluster on the site.)
7. What about Section 24? (Section 24 ITTOIA 2005 / FA 2015 finance cost restriction applies ONLY to sole-trader landlords — NOT to limited-company property businesses. Companies deduct mortgage interest fully against rental profits under CTA 2009 Part 5 (loan relationships). This is one of the principal reasons landlords incorporate; the spreadsheet must not include a Section 24 column.)
8. What about MTD ITSA? (Making Tax Digital for ITSA applies to sole-trader landlords with rental income above the threshold; companies do NOT file MTD ITSA on rental income — they file the corporation tax return CT600 annually. The spreadsheet does not need MTD ITSA quarterly-update columns.)
9. How does the spreadsheet support the year-end statutory accounts under FRS 102 Section 1A? (Asset register feeding the balance sheet; investment-property fair-value adjustment if FRS 102 fair-value model used; rental income and operating costs feeding the P&L; small-company exemptions taken under Companies Act 2006 s.477 / s.480.)
10. What is the recommended tab structure for an SPV spreadsheet versus a multi-SPV / group structure? (Single-SPV: per-property ledger + consolidated P&L + DLA + dividend log + year-end checklist. Multi-SPV / group: add inter-company loan ledger, group structure tab, associated-company-count tracker for the marginal-relief CT band per CTA 2010 s.18.)
11. What are the most common mistakes the spreadsheet should prevent? (Section 24 deduction wrongly applied; mortgage interest treated as a sole-trader expense; rental income mis-mapped to the wrong tax engine; director's loan repaid then re-drawn within 30 days breaching s.464A bed-and-breakfast; ATED threshold crossed and not filed; dividend declared without sufficient distributable profits; salary set below NIC primary threshold without claiming employment allowance.)
