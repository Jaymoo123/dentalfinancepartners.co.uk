- **Framing differentiator (Stage 1 seed, 2026-05-25 — Stage 2 to expand to 6-8 lettered components):**

> The NON-RESIDENT LANDLORD CORPORATE ENTITY operational walkthrough for the Register of Overseas Entities (RoE) ANNUAL UPDATE STATEMENT — distinct from the (largely written-about) initial RoE registration. Page anchors on **ECTEA 2022 c. 10 Part 1** (verbatim sections to be verified by Stage 2 — at minimum s.7 registration / s.8 annual updating duty / s.9 delivery of updating information / s.36 offences for failure to comply with updating duty) plus the post-1-March-2024 amendments made by **ECCTA 2023 Sch 12**. (a) **The 14-day window after the update period anniversary.** Each overseas entity has an "update period" with an anniversary tied to its initial RoE registration date; the annual update statement is due within 14 days of that anniversary. Missing the window starts the s.36 daily-default penalty clock. (b) **Verification BEFORE filing.** The beneficial-owner information must be verified by a UK-regulated agent (an "authorised UK-regulated agent" or "relevant person" under the AML regime) BEFORE the annual update is submitted — the same verification discipline as initial registration. Stale beneficial-owner data without fresh verification = non-compliant filing. (c) **The "non-compliant" status trigger and what it locks.** An overseas entity that has not delivered its current update statement becomes "non-compliant" (verify exact statutory hook with Stage 2 — likely ECTEA 2022 s.8/9 + Sch 4A LRA 2002 cross-references). Practical operational consequence: HM Land Registry will refuse to register subsequent dispositions (sale, lease grant of 7+ years, legal mortgage) by the overseas entity until compliance is restored. (d) **Penalty quantum (verify at write time — set by regs).** Financial penalty up to £2,500 plus £500/day continuing default (per existing §11 house position; Stage 2 re-verifies against the operative penalty regulations; §16.27 rate-by-reference). (e) **Where RoE sits alongside other UK obligations for an overseas corporate landlord.** Annual update is ONE of (ATED return + NRL scheme compliance + NRCGT 60-day return on disposals + corporation-tax filing for the company itself if non-resident landlord company within scope). The page maps these for an overseas BVI / Jersey / Guernsey / UAE corporate holder. (f) **What the page is NOT:** not the initial-registration walkthrough (well covered by competitor inventory and gov.uk itself); not a UK-LtdCo-with-overseas-shareholder page (different regime — that's PSC disclosure under CA 2006, B3 territory). Cross-link to existing `non-resident-landlord-scheme-uk-complete-guide` (top cannib at 0.22) as the income-tax-side sibling; B2 covers the entity-disclosure-side obligation.
