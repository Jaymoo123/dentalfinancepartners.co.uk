## Statutory anchors + house-position locks

- **§27.2 Sch 24 FA 2007 — penalty bands (verbatim from HP):**
  | Behaviour | Max | Unprompted floor | Prompted floor |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Careless | 30% | **0% (no time qualifier — F-5)** | 15% |
  | Deliberate not concealed | 70% | 20% | 35% |
  | Deliberate and concealed | 100% | 30% | 50% |

- **§27.2 F-5 correction (CRITICAL — locked 2026-05-24):** Schedule 24 FA 2007 para 10 imposes **NO "within 12 months" qualifier** on the careless-unprompted 0% floor. The 12-month cliff exists in **Schedule 41 FA 2008 para 13** (failure-to-notify), NOT Sch 24. Sessions writing on inaccuracy penalties must not import the Sch 41 12-month qualifier into Sch 24 commentary. **This page is the natural home to correct the most common penalty-mitigation misconception in the landlord market.**
- **§27.2 telling/helping/giving reduction at Sch 24 para 9:** the mechanism by which a disclosure earns a position somewhere between the maximum and the floor. 30% telling / 40% helping / 30% giving allocation per HMRC operational practice (CH82460+).
- **§27.2 Sch 24 para 14 suspension:** careless penalties may be suspended for **up to 2 years** subject to suspension conditions imposed by HMRC. Deliberate penalties cannot be suspended. Suspension is HMRC discretion (**Anderson v HMRC** is the working authority); refusal is appealable to FTT.
- **§27.2 offshore uplift (Sch 24 para 4A):** Cat 1 = standard bands; Cat 2 = ×1.5 (max 45/105/150); Cat 3 = ×2 (max 60/140/200). Mitigation floors uplift in parallel.
- **§27.2 Sch 21 FA 2015 asset-move penalty:** stacks on top of Sch 24 where assets are moved from Cat 2/3 territory to defeat HMRC. SEPARATE from the offshore uplift — do not conflate.
- **§27.9 do-not-write list (critical for this page):**
  - "Sch 24 careless penalty is 30% flat" — false; max 30% with mitigation to 0% unprompted or 15% prompted; use the matrix.
  - "Offshore deliberate-concealed Sch 24 penalty is capped at 100%" — false; Cat 3 offshore uplift takes max to 200%.
  - "Reasonable excuse is HMRC officer discretion" — false; statutory defence under Sch 24 para 14 + appealable to FTT.
  - **DO NOT write "Sch 24 careless-unprompted 0% only available within 12 months"** — that is the F-5 trap. The 12-month qualifier is Sch 41 only.
- **Verification mandate at write time per §16.35:** WebFetch (a) https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2007/11/schedule/24/paragraph/10 for verbatim Sch 24 para 10 (confirms no 12-month qualifier on careless-unprompted floor); (b) https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2007/11/schedule/24/paragraph/9 for the para 9 reduction mechanic; (c) https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2007/11/schedule/24/paragraph/14 for suspension; (d) HMRC CH82460+ for telling/helping/giving operational allocation.

## Mitigation depth — the operational core (Stage 2 develops)

### Layer 1 — behaviour-band selection

- **Careless vs deliberate framing.** The behaviour determination drives everything downstream. **HMRC v Tooth [2021] UKSC 17** is the current Supreme Court authority on the deliberate-behaviour test — the test is whether the taxpayer (or person acting for them) **knew the statement to be false at the time it was made**. Post-Tooth, the deliberate band is materially narrower than pre-Tooth HMRC practice. Sessions writing must reference Tooth for any deliberate-behaviour analysis.
- **Reasonable care floor for careless.** The "reasonable care" test under Sch 24 para 3(1) is fact-and-circumstances. Failure to instruct an accountant in complex affairs is rarely reasonable care; reliance on a competent professional with full disclosure of facts IS reasonable care (subject to Hanson + David Collis case-law on agent-error attribution).

### Layer 2 — quality-of-disclosure reduction (para 9)

**Telling / helping / giving reduction matrix:**

| Limb | What it requires | % of maximum reduction window |
|---|---|---|
| **Telling** | Disclosing inaccuracy (clear admission, scope, periods, amounts) | 30% |
| **Helping** | Assisting HMRC to quantify (calculations, schedules, supporting docs) | 40% |
| **Giving** | Giving HMRC access to records (rental schedules, bank statements, property contracts) | 30% |

The **maximum reduction window** = (statutory max) − (statutory floor). Full marks on all three limbs → floor figure; zero marks → max figure. Stage 2 develops 4-6 worked landlord scenarios showing how each limb maps onto a typical LPC / nudge-letter / enquiry response. **Worked example anchor:** £40k careless prompted disclosure of 3 years' rental income — max 30%, prompted floor 15%, reduction window 15 percentage points. Full quality → 15% (£6k). Only telling, no helping/giving → 15% + 70%-weighted residual = approximately 25.5% (~£10.2k). The £4.2k delta is the operational lever this page surfaces.

### Layer 3 — suspension under para 14

- **Careless only** — deliberate penalties cannot be suspended.
- **Up to 2 years suspension period.**
- **Conditions imposed by HMRC** — typically "complete and file all SA returns on time", "maintain digital records compliant with §19.16", "engage an agent for X tax years". Breach during suspension period triggers the penalty.
- **Anderson appealability** — HMRC refusal to suspend can be appealed to FTT. Anderson v HMRC is the working authority for the suspension framework (verify exact citation at write time).
- **Strategic point:** suspension is heavily under-used in landlord penalty cases. Sessions writing should flag it as the "no-cost" mitigation lever where careless behaviour is admitted and the landlord is willing to take operational improvements.

### Layer 4 — reasonable excuse defence

- **Sch 24 para 18 (cross-ref Sch 41 para 20 + Perrin four-stage test):** statutory defence; appealable to FTT; not HMRC officer discretion.
- **Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156** four-stage test:
  1. Was there an excuse?
  2. Was the excuse reasonable in the circumstances?
  3. Did the excuse cause the failure?
  4. Was the failure remedied without unreasonable delay once the excuse ended?
- **Common landlord scenarios qualifying:** serious illness during return period; agent error with reasonable supervision (Hanson); bereavement; HMRC system outage.
- **Common scenarios NOT qualifying:** "I didn't know I had to declare rental income" (Perrin test fails at limb 2); "my accountant didn't tell me" (depends on supervision — Hanson test).

### Layer 5 — appeal route

- **30-day window under TMA 1970 s.31A** for penalty assessment.
- **FTT Tax Chamber under SI 2009/273** (cross-ref existing W7 B4 page `tribunal-appeal-process-landlords-first-tier-tribunal-tax-chamber`).
- **Martland v HMRC [2018] UKUT 178** three-stage late-appeal framework (cross-ref existing W7 B9 page).
- **ADR option** — alongside FTT route; does not pause appeal time limits.

## Worked penalty calculations (Stage 2 develops 6-8 across behaviour bands)

Stage 2 must walk through:

1. **Careless unprompted, full quality, residential rental, on shore** — £30k tax loss → 0% penalty (just tax + interest).
2. **Careless unprompted, partial quality (telling only), 4 years late** — same scenario as 1 but with reduced quality → 21% effective penalty (~£6.3k).
3. **Careless prompted (HMRC nudge letter triggered disclosure), full quality** — £30k tax loss → 15% (£4.5k).
4. **Deliberate not concealed, unprompted, full quality** — £50k tax loss → 20% (£10k).
5. **Deliberate concealed, prompted, full quality** — £100k tax loss → 50% (£50k).
6. **Cat 2 offshore careless unprompted, full quality** — £40k tax loss → 0% floor (Cat 2 floor uplifts in parallel; both max and floor multiply ×1.5 — verify at write time the floor multiplier interpretation).
7. **Cat 3 offshore deliberate concealed, prompted, no quality** — £200k tax loss → 200% (£400k) + potential Sch 21 asset-move stacking.
8. **Careless suspended** — £30k tax loss careless prompted → 15% (£4.5k) → suspended for 2 years subject to digital-records conditions → £0 if conditions met.

**F-5 trap callout:** scenario 2 (4 years late) — sessions tempted to write "no 0% floor available because outside 12-month window" — that is the Sch 41 trap. Sch 24 careless-unprompted floor has no time qualifier; the 0% is reachable on full quality whenever the disclosure happens.
