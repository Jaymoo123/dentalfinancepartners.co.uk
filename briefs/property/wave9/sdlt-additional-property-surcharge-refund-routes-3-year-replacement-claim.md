## Statutory anchors (Stage 1 seed — verify at §16.35 per-write)

**Primary statutory hooks:**
- **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA** — Higher rates for additional dwellings (the "additional-dwellings surcharge" architecture as a whole). Verify current consolidated text at legislation.gov.uk per §16.35.
- **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 3** — Condition C (the "previous main residence" replacement test). Specifically **para 3(7)** (3-year replacement window from new acquisition effective date — verify at Stage 2 §16.35; the window was extended from 18 to 36 months at one point, Stage 2 must verify current consolidated form).
- **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 8** — refund claim mechanics (the refund-route statute when old PPR is later disposed of within the replacement window).
- **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 9** — joint-purchaser + spouse / civil-partner aggregation (load-bearing for disposal-side aggregation edge cases).
- **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA paras 10-11** — partner/spouse single-dwelling treatment + acquisition-by-spouse cases.

## Key questions for Stage 2 to address (Stage 1 seed — Stage 2 expands)

1. **3-year clock mechanics** — exactly when does the 3-year window start (acquisition effective date per FA 2003 s.119, NOT completion or SDLT filing date)? When does it end (statutory expiry)? Worked example with dates.
2. **What counts as disposal of previous main residence** for the refund — sale + completion (yes); transfer to spouse on divorce (specific Sch 4ZA treatment — verify); gift to family member (yes if effective transfer); marriage-of-titles (sub-question — Stage 2 to surface); repossession (yes — disposal occurs); abandonment / dereliction (no — must be actual transfer).
3. **Sale-delay scenarios architecture** — chain-break (most common); probate-delayed sale of inherited PPR pre-acquisition of replacement (separate Sch 4ZA mechanics — sub-question); divorce-driven sale where leaving spouse retains residual interest pending decree (Sch 3 para 3 + Sch 4ZA interaction); job-relocation buy-first sell-later scenarios.
4. **Spousal-aggregation on disposal side** — both spouses must dispose of their interests in the old main residence? What if one spouse retains a small residual interest? Sch 4ZA para 9 + para 10 + para 11 interaction — Stage 2 to walk through.
5. **Refund claim window vs FA 2003 s.80 amendment window** — refund claim must be made within 12 months from disposal of old main residence OR 12 months from filing date of original return, whichever is LATER. Worked example. What happens if both windows close — discovery assessment territory (cross-link C3 if launching same wave).
6. **Repossession + receivership scenarios** — does repossession of the old main residence count as a disposal triggering the refund? When does the disposal effective date occur?
7. **Probate-delayed disposal** — buyer inherits an old PPR (probate-delayed by months/years), then acquires a new property and old PPR is later sold within 3 years of new acquisition — does the refund apply? Statutory test under Sch 4ZA Condition C.
