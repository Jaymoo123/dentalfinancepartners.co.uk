### Framing differentiator (Stage 1 seed)

> A landlord-facing walk-through of the SDLT Multiple Dwellings Relief (MDR) abolition under Finance (No.2) Act 2024 (effective for transactions with an effective date on or after 1 June 2024) — focused on three things competitor coverage tends to under-serve: (a) the **transitional cohort** — which pre-1-June-2024 contracts still qualify (substantial performance, completion timing, and the exact statutory cut-off mechanics), and how to evidence transitional eligibility on enquiry; (b) the **anti-forestalling rules** baked into FA(No.2)2024 that block retrofit MDR claims via sub-sale arrangements, options, or assignment of contracts dated pre-1-June-2024 with completion structured post-abolition; (c) the **surviving alternatives for portfolio buyers** post-abolition — the FA 2003 s.116(7) six-or-more-dwellings automatic non-residential treatment (statutory deeming, no claim required), the FA 2003 Sch 15 partnership SLP route (genuine pre-existing partnership only — Wave 7 §1.A discipline), and the FA 2003 s.45 sub-sale route (narrow, genuine pre-completion onward-sale only). Page is NOT an MDR explainer (legacy relief, do not write as live); it IS an abolition + transitional + alternatives architecture page for landlords who were planning portfolio acquisitions on the assumption MDR was available.

### Pool-thinness disclosure (Stage 2 to confirm at competitor fetch)
Most competitor pages still treat MDR as live or only briefly note abolition without surfacing the anti-forestalling architecture or the s.116(7) / Sch 15 SLP alternatives. The defensible point is the **abolition + transitional + alternatives** architecture combined with the anti-forestalling enquiry-attack pattern.

## Statutory anchors (Stage 1 seed — verify at §16.35 per-write)

**Primary statutory hooks:**
- **FA 2003 Sch 6B** — the (now-repealed) MDR statutory provision. Define what was relieved, mechanics of the relief (relief = (total consideration / number of dwellings) banded × number of dwellings, vs full consideration banded conventionally).
- **Finance (No.2) Act 2024** — abolition provisions. **Stage 2 must verify the exact section reference for MDR abolition** (likely s.X of F(No.2)A 2024 inserting effective-date cut-off of 1 June 2024) and the **anti-forestalling commencement provisions** (typically structured to look through contracts entered into pre-Budget-Day-2024 but completing post-1-June-2024 unless substantial performance occurred pre-Budget-Day).
- **FA 2003 s.119** — "effective date" definition (completion OR substantial performance, whichever is earlier). Load-bearing for transitional cohort identification.
- **FA 2003 s.45** — sub-sale relief (surviving alternative for narrow pre-completion onward-sale cases).
- **FA 2003 s.116(7)** — six-or-more-dwellings automatic non-residential treatment (surviving alternative; statutory deeming per Wave 1 correction logged 2026-05-22 against the wrong Sch 6B para 7 cite).
- **FA 2003 Sch 15 para 10** — partnership SLP route (surviving alternative; full mechanics in HP §1.A — Wave 7 lock).

**Case-law / commentary anchors:**

2. **Anti-forestalling architecture** — which scenarios are blocked: sub-sale into MDR-claiming acquirer post-1-June-2024 with pre-existing contract; option exercise post-1-June-2024 where option granted pre-1-June-2024; assignment of pre-1-June-2024 contract to acquirer who completes post-abolition. Worked examples for each.
3. **Surviving alternatives architecture** — when is s.116(7) (6+ dwellings) the right route vs Sch 15 SLP partnership vs s.45 sub-sale? Decision matrix.
4. **Welsh + Scottish divergence** — LTT MDR survives (Wales); LBTT does not have an MDR equivalent (Scotland) — Stage 2 to verify and surface as a cross-border planning point.
5. **Refund + amendment scenarios** — what if a buyer claimed MDR pre-abolition and HMRC enquires post-abolition (no retroactive abolition; relief stands for pre-1-June-2024 effective dates). What if a buyer post-abolition incorrectly claims MDR — penalty exposure under Sch 24 FA 2007 (cross-link C2 if launching same wave).
6. **Practical landlord scenarios** — typical portfolio acquisition scenarios that DID rely on MDR pre-abolition (5-property buy-to-let portfolio purchases; 6+-property acquisitions where s.116(7) is now the safe route; mixed houses-and-flats portfolios; HMO-portfolio purchases).
