### Worked operational scenario (Stage 2 addition — write the page around this anchored example)

**Anonymised persona — Tom and Priya, 3-SPV BTL portfolio in West Yorkshire.** Tom and Priya hold 6 BTL properties via three SPVs:
- **Trent Holdings Ltd** (2 Leeds flats; Tom + Priya both directors; 60% / 40% ownership so both are PSCs above the 25% threshold).
- **Calder Properties Ltd** (3 Bradford terraces; Tom is sole director + sole PSC at 100%).
- **Aire Investments Ltd** (1 Halifax HMO; Tom + Priya directors; Tom 50% / Priya 50% — both PSCs).

**Verification headcount:** TWO natural persons need to verify identity, not five-or-six. Tom does ONE GOV.UK One Login verification, receives ONE personal code, and that personal code covers him in his director and PSC roles across all three companies. Priya does the same.

**Verification route choice:** Both have biometric UK passports + UK driving licences. The One Login app route is fastest (5 minutes per person). The accountant (an ACSP since November 2025 because the firm is HMRC-AML-supervised) is offered as an option but Tom and Priya self-verify because they already have GOV.UK One Login accounts from their personal tax filings.

**Personal code provisioning sequence:**
- Trent Holdings Ltd — next confirmation statement is due 12 August 2026. Tom and Priya each provide their personal codes when the confirmation statement is filed. **Deadline:** confirmation-statement filing window (14 days from the confirmation date).
- Calder Properties Ltd — confirmation statement due 4 June 2026. Tom provides his personal code at that filing. **Deadline:** ~25 days from today (2026-05-25), so this is the most urgent.
- Aire Investments Ltd — confirmation statement due 22 November 2026. Filing falls just AFTER the 12-month transition window ends; Tom and Priya should still provide personal codes at the next routine filing, which keeps them in the safe-harbour pattern.

**What would have triggered a hard deadline earlier:** if Tom appointed a new director to any of the three companies during the transition, that NEW director's appointment would have been a "new director after 18 November 2025" event → ID verification gate applies at appointment filing, not next confirmation statement.

**ACSP route alternative path:** if Tom were a non-tech-savvy first-time landlord, the accountant-ACSP would handle verification by (a) collecting Tom's passport + driving licence + proof of address per their AML KYC file, (b) checking documents against the operative CH verification standard, (c) submitting verification to Companies House on Tom's behalf, (d) Tom receives a personal code via the ACSP's submission. ### Draft FAQ entries (Stage 2 expansion, 10 entries — session refines at write time)

**Q1. Who has to verify their identity at Companies House?**
A. Every director and every PSC (person with significant control) of every UK LtdCo. PSCs include anyone who holds more than 25% of shares OR more than 25% of voting rights OR has the right to appoint or remove a majority of directors. For a landlord-LtdCo with multiple SPVs, that typically means each natural-person director and each 25%-plus shareholder must verify identity ONCE and provide their personal code per company role at the next confirmation statement or appointment filing.

**Q2. When does this become mandatory for me?**
A. Identity verification became a legal requirement on 18 November 2025 for newly appointed directors and PSCs. For existing directors and PSCs in place before 18 November 2025, there is a 12-month transition window ending around November 2026 — verification must be completed before the company's next confirmation statement filed after the deadline. Today (verification timestamp on page) we are mid-transition: new appointments are gated, existing roles are running down their transition window. Check the operative date at write time on the Companies House campaign page.

**Q3. How does this affect landlord LtdCos specifically?**
A. Landlord LtdCos with multiple property SPVs (a common BTL structure pattern) typically have the same natural persons holding director + PSC roles across several companies. The good news: ONE identity verification event per person covers them across ALL their company roles, because Companies House issues a single personal code per person that gets linked to each role. The operational pain is per-company filing: the personal code must be provided at the next confirmation statement (or sooner if appointing a new director) for each SPV individually.

**Q4. What is the GOV.UK One Login route?**
A. The primary self-service route. You create or sign in to your GOV.UK One Login account, use the One Login app (or answer security questions, or visit a Post Office) to verify your identity using a passport, UK driving licence, or biometric residence permit. Once verified, Companies House issues a unique personal code that you then quote when filing Companies House documents that require ID verification. Free of charge from Companies House (Post Office in-person route has a separate Post Office fee).

**Q5. What is an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP) and should my accountant become one?**
A. An ACSP is a UK firm supervised for AML purposes (under the MLR 2017) that has registered with Companies House to perform identity verification on behalf of clients and to file on clients' behalf. Most established accountants and company-secretarial firms are AML-supervised already so the ACSP application is procedural. For multi-SPV landlord LtdCos where the accountant already holds the directors' KYC documents from money-laundering checks, the ACSP route can be quicker than self-verification.

**Q6. I am director of 5 SPVs. Do I need to verify 5 times?**
A. No. You verify ONCE, receive one personal code, and that code covers you across all 5 director roles (and any PSC roles if you also hold 25%+ in any of them). The per-company step is providing the personal code when each SPV files its next confirmation statement or appointment-related filing — that is filing-side admin, not re-verification.

**Q7. What documents are accepted as proof of identity?**
A. Via GOV.UK One Login: biometric passport (any country, must be in date), full or provisional UK photo driving licence (UK only; expired driving licences within 90 days of expiry are accepted), UK biometric residence permit / card, or UK Frontier Worker permit. The Post Office route accepts a similar list with in-person staff verification of the documents.

**Q8. What happens if I do not verify in time?**
A. Companies House confirms it is an offence to continue acting in a CA 2006 role without verification once the regime applies to you, and the consequences include a financial penalty plus inability to file documents that require ID verification (which can block the company's confirmation statement and create a striking-off risk under CA 2006 s.1000 for missed annual filings). The exact statutory wording of the offence sits in ECCTA 2023 ss.67-69 — the page should quote the operative wording verbatim from legislation.gov.uk at write time.

**Q9. How does identity verification interact with PSC disclosure on the confirmation statement?**
A. The confirmation statement is the operational moment at which PSC information is re-asserted to Companies House each year. From the 18 November 2025 commencement, PSCs in scope of identity verification must have completed verification AND their personal code must be quoted in the PSC section of the confirmation statement. So a landlord-LtdCo's annual filing cadence now bundles three steps: confirm PSC info still accurate, confirm each PSC has verified (provide personal code), file the confirmation statement.

**Q10. Does becoming a director of a new SPV trigger re-verification?**
A. No — your existing personal code carries forward. What changes is that the new SPV's appointment filing will require your personal code to be quoted when you are recorded as a new director. If you have not previously verified (i.e. this is your first CA 2006 role since 18 November 2025), the appointment cannot be completed until your verification is done and your personal code is in hand.

**Q11. Can a corporate director or corporate PSC verify identity?**
A. The current regime targets natural persons. The Companies House campaign page flags that corporate directors and corporate members of LLPs / officers of corporate PSCs come into scope at a later phase (no earlier than November 2026 per the published rollout). In practice, most landlord LtdCo SPV structures use natural-person directors so this is rarely operationally live for the audience — but FIC + family-trust structures that interpose corporate directors should monitor the second-phase commencement.

**Q12. Where do I check the current operative state?**
A. The Companies House campaign page (`changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk`) is the canonical commencement-state tracker. The Companies House blog (`companieshouse.blog.gov.uk`) publishes operational announcements as each phase commences. Both are authoritative. Statutory text sits at `legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/56/contents`. Avoid second-hand commentary on commencement dates because the rollout phases have shifted during the 2024-2026 period.
