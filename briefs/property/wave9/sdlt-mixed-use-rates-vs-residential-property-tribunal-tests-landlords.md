### Framing differentiator (Stage 1 seed)

> A property-investor-facing walk-through of the SDLT residential-vs-mixed-use rate line specifically as decided by the post-2019 tribunal trilogy — Hyman v HMRC ([2019] UKFTT 469, [2021] UKUT 68, [2022] EWCA Civ 185), Suterwalla v HMRC ([2024] UKFTT), and Hortons Hall (and similar 2024-25 FTT cases). The page is NOT a general mixed-use SDLT explainer (existing `sdlt-mixed-use-property-classification` covers the classification basics); it IS a case-law walk-through for property investors buying houses with **grounds, paddocks, barns, outbuildings, equestrian use, agricultural overlay, commercial-on-the-ground-floor, partial-let-commercial elements** — the specific factual matrices that the trilogy adjudicated. Page focuses on three things competitor coverage tends to flatten: (a) the **"grounds and gardens" test under FA 2003 s.116(1)(b)** as interpreted by the Hyman line — paddock or barn that is part of the dwelling's grounds is RESIDENTIAL even when fenced off or untended; the test is not "is the land used for residential purposes" but "does the land form part of the grounds of the dwelling at the effective date"; (b) the **separation tests that work** — Suterwalla's separate-grazing-licence-to-third-party was sufficient to displace grounds-classification; the discipline is genuine third-party commercial use of the land contemporaneous with the acquisition, with evidence (licence agreement, payment record, third-party operating activity); (c) the **HMRC enquiry attack pattern** — most buyer mixed-use claims fail; HMRC challenges on Hyman authority and the burden of proof is on the buyer to evidence non-residential character at the effective date. NOT covered: the Bewley uninhabitable-property test (separate doctrine; HP §1.C territory; cross-link); the general mixed-use SDLT classification mechanics (existing `sdlt-mixed-use-property-classification` page; cross-link).

## Statutory anchors (Stage 1 seed — verify at §16.35 per-write)

**Primary statutory hooks:**
- **FA 2003 s.116(1)** — definition of "residential property". Specifically:
  - **s.116(1)(a)** — "a building that is used or suitable for use as a single dwelling, or is in the process of being constructed or adapted for such use" (the Bewley territory — separate doctrine, HP §1.C).
  - **s.116(1)(b)** — "land that is or forms part of the garden or grounds of a building within paragraph (a) (including any building or structure on such land)" (the Hyman / Suterwalla / Hortons Hall territory — THIS page's primary anchor).
  - **s.116(1)(c)** — "an interest in or right over land that subsists for the benefit of a building within paragraph (a) or of land within paragraph (b)" (auxiliary).
- **FA 2003 s.55(1)(b)** — mixed-use rates trigger (transaction "consists of OR includes" non-residential property — the rate-application limb that follows from a successful mixed-use classification).
- **FA 2003 s.55 Table B** — non-residential SDLT rates (0% to £150k, 2% £150k–£250k, 5% above £250k — the rate set that applies on a successful mixed-use claim, sparing the 5% additional-dwellings surcharge).
- **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 18** — the additional-dwellings surcharge does NOT apply where the transaction is mixed-use (rate-side benefit; verify exact paragraph reference at Stage 2 §16.35).

**Case-law anchors (the trilogy + supporting):**
- **Hyman v HMRC [2019] UKFTT 469 (TC)** — FTT held the meadow / paddock / barn formed part of the grounds of the dwelling; residential rates applied.
- **Hyman v HMRC [2021] UKUT 68 (TCC)** — Upper Tribunal upheld the FTT; the "grounds" test is not about active use but about whether the land forms part of the dwelling's curtilage / grounds at the effective date.
- **Hyman v HMRC [2022] EWCA Civ 185** — Court of Appeal upheld the UT. Binding authority on the "grounds" test for s.116(1)(b) purposes.
- **Suterwalla v HMRC [2024] UKFTT** — TAXPAYER WIN. Paddock was let to a neighbouring farmer under a genuine separate grazing licence at the effective date; the FTT held the paddock did NOT form part of the dwelling's grounds because it was under genuine third-party commercial use contemporaneous with the acquisition. (Stage 2 verify FTT citation + decision date.)
- **Hortons Hall** — TAXPAYER LOSS. Property with extensive grounds claimed mixed-use; FTT held grounds-classification applied; residential rates owed. (Stage 2 verify FTT citation + decision details.)
- **Withers v HMRC** + **The How Development v HMRC** + **A & K Bewley line** — supporting FTT cases on the broader mixed-use line (Stage 2 to surface 1-2 additional for citation balance).

**HMRC manual anchors:**
- **SDLTM00400+** — residential property definition + grounds-and-gardens guidance.
- **SDLTM30200+** — specific scenarios + post-Hyman HMRC operational stance.
- **SDLTM29800+** — non-residential / mixed-use rates application.

**HP cross-references:**
- HP §1.C (Wave 7 lock): Bewley uninhabitable-property test — adjacent doctrine, separate Wave 7 page. Stage 2 must cross-link and not conflate (Bewley = s.116(1)(a) suitable-for-use; this page = s.116(1)(b) grounds-and-gardens).

## Key questions for Stage 2 to address (Stage 1 seed — Stage 2 expands)

1. **The Hyman test — exact ratio** — what specifically did the Court of Appeal hold makes land "form part of the grounds" of a dwelling for s.116(1)(b)? Not active use; not maintenance; what IS the test? (FTT, UT, CA reasoning compared.)
2. **Suterwalla — what worked** — the separate grazing licence — what specific factual elements made it succeed (third-party identity; consideration; contemporaneous-with-acquisition; documented; commercial purpose). Worked example.
3. **Hortons Hall + similar TAXPAYER LOSS cases** — what specifically failed (no separate licence; grounds maintained by the dwelling-owner; aesthetic-only fencing; no third-party use)?
4. **The s.55 Sch 4ZA para 18 surcharge-side benefit** — how much does a successful mixed-use claim save on a typical second-home buy where the surcharge would otherwise apply? Worked example showing the rate-set difference (residential + 5% surcharge vs non-residential rates).
5. **HMRC enquiry-pattern reality** — published failure-rate data (if available; otherwise the professional-body consensus that the majority of buyer mixed-use claims post-Hyman are challenged successfully by HMRC). The realistic risk profile for a buyer relying on mixed-use.
6. **Evidence requirements at effective date** — what documentation a buyer needs at the effective date to defend a mixed-use claim on enquiry: third-party licence agreement; commercial-use evidence (licence payments, third-party VAT registration, business-rate evidence); contemporaneous photographs; surveyor / land-agent reports.
7. **Equestrian / agricultural / partial-commercial scenarios** — the most common landlord-investor scenarios (large house with paddock; cottage with smallholding; mews-house with stables let to a neighbour; town-centre property with commercial ground-floor).
8. **MDR-interaction historical note** — pre-1-June-2024, some buyers combined mixed-use + MDR claims; post-MDR-abolition (A1), this is no longer available. Worth a sentence; not the main topic.
