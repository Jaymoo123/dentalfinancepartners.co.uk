## Gap-mode diagnosis (Stage 1 reasoning, refined with Stage 2 data)

**Primary 1 — STALE_FACTS / WRONG-LAW.** Four discrete defects, in order of consequence:

1. **The decisive second-hand fact is entirely MISSING.** The page never tells the reader that **full expensing (CAA 2001 s.45S) and the new 40% FYA (FA 2026 s.29) both require "new and unused" plant and therefore exclude a used van.** This is the single most important sentence the page could contain and it is absent. A reader who lands on a "second hand van" page and is not told this has been failed. This is the load-bearing fix and the reframing spine of the rewrite: *second-hand is the pivotal fact because it knocks out the two headline 100%/40% first-year routes, leaving AIA (still 100%) and WDA (14%).*

2. **Section 24 conflation (WRONG, irrelevant).** §1, §6 ("Restrictions for Sole Traders Under Section 24") and §12 lean on Section 24 (mortgage-interest finance-cost relief) as if it bears on van capital allowances. It does not. Per house_positions §38 boundary discipline, "§4 (Section 24 finance-cost relief) is interest relief; §38 is capital allowances on plant/fixtures." Strip every Section 24 reference; the only legitimate cross-mention is a one-line "do not confuse these two reliefs" clarifier.

3. **Connected-party mechanic MIS-STATED.** The page (FAQ #4 + §8) says buying from a relative is "treated as a disposal at market value", limiting the buyer's claim. That conflates two different rules. The buyer-side restriction on a used purchase from a connected person is **CAA 2001 s.217** (no AIA and no first-year allowance where plant is acquired from a connected person under the ss.214-218 anti-avoidance regime — verified in force, legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-30); the consequence is the buyer falls back to WDA in the pool, not "claim limited to market value on purchase". The market-value disposal rule (CAA 2001 s.61, item 2) is a SELLER-side rule that bites on a later SALE of the van to a connected person. Reframe both correctly and keep them separate.

4. **Cash-basis bar MISSING.** The sibling page covers it; THIS page must too. Under the cash basis a landlord/sole trader does **not** claim capital allowances on plant generally (CAA 2001 s.1A disapplies allowances), with a **car-only exception** (s.1A(4)): cars stay in the capital-allowances regime, while a van's cost is instead relieved as a cash-basis deduction (ITTOIA 2005 s.33A allows non-car P&M; s.33A(4)(c) carves cars out). Net effect for a used van bought on the cash basis: no capital-allowances claim at all (the cost is simply deducted in the cash computation). State this precisely — it is easy to garble, and the sibling's phrasing ("can only claim on cars") is correct only because cars are the one thing that stays in the allowances regime under cash basis.
