## Gap-mode diagnosis (Stage 1 reasoning, refined with Stage 2 data)

**Primary: STALE_FACTS at high reader-harm.** Three wrong framings, all against §38 (FA 2026 c.11, ENACTED 18 March 2026) and §25:

1. **Full-expensing sunset (WRONG).** Body H2 "Full Expensing for Companies" + FAQ #2 + FAQ #4 all assert a 31 March 2026 end with "the government may extend or replace the scheme". Per §38 + §25.8, full expensing (CAA 2001 s.45S) was made **permanent** at Autumn Statement 2023, confirmed Autumn Budget 2024; the section as enacted has **no sunset clause**. The entire "after March 2026 the rules may change" framing is deleted.
2. **40% FYA mis-described (WRONG).** Body H2 "First-Year Allowances After 2026" says it applies "between 1 January 2026 and 31 March 2026 (the end of the current full expensing window)" and "is available for companies and unincorporated businesses". Per §38 s.29: the 40% FYA has **no 31 March 2026 end** and exists **precisely because full expensing is companies-only** - it is the standing unincorporated/leasing route, not a window inside full expensing. FAQ #4 wrongly nests it in the full-expensing window.
3. **WDA worked examples ignore the straddling hybrid (PARTIALLY WRONG).** The "14% (it was 18% before then)" patch is correct in headline, but the worked examples compute a flat 14% with no flag that a chargeable period **straddling 1 April 2026 (CT) / 6 April 2026 (IT)** uses a **hybrid, time-apportioned rate** between 18% and 14% (FA 2026 **s.28(2)-(6)**).
