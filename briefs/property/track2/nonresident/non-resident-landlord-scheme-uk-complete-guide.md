## Cluster form-taxonomy note (READ before drafting — cross-cluster consistency)

The cluster currently uses **two different form taxonomies**, which is a latent correctness risk for the hub. The HMRC-correct taxonomy (confirmed across `changes-nrl-companies` FAQ + the agent-mechanics spoke + house position §17.5) is:

- **NRL1** — application to receive rent gross, **individual** landlord.
- **NRL2** — application to receive rent gross, **company** landlord. (NOT the quarterly return.)
- **NRL3** — application to receive rent gross, **trustee** landlord.
- **NRL6** — annual **certificate of tax deducted** that the agent/tenant gives to the landlord.
- **NRLQ** — agent's **quarterly return** of rent and tax withheld.
- **NRLY** — agent's **annual report** to HMRC.

**Drift flag (F-NRL-1, for `track2_site_wide_flags.md`):** `nrl-scheme-letting-agents-quarterly-returns-mechanics.md` uses "**NRL2**" for the agent quarterly return throughout, which collides with NRL2 = company gross-payment application. The `nrl-withholding-tax` spoke correctly uses NRLQ (quarterly) / NRLY (annual) / NRL1-2-3 (individual/company/trustee). **The hub rewrite must use the HMRC-correct taxonomy above** and the manager should raise a cluster-wide reconciliation flag so the agent-mechanics spoke is back-patched (manager-direct STALE sweep, not this brief's scope). The hub is the canonical explainer, so it must be the one that is unambiguously correct.

**Do NOT introduce "NRL8" as a primary refund channel.** If naming a repayment form at all, verify the current HMRC position at write time; the safe correct framing is "reclaim over-withheld tax via your UK Self Assessment return".

Frame the agent-side quarterly machinery in 2-3 sentences and route to the agent-mechanics spoke (NRLQ/NRLY/NRL6, reg 8, reg 10). Approval does not reduce the tax due, it changes timing/cash flow. Route to the NRL1 application-mechanics spoke for the step-by-step.

6. **Refunds and over-withholding (reclaim via Self Assessment)** (~250 words). Because 20% is withheld on rent (net of agent reg-8 deductions) rather than on the landlord's actual net profit after ALL allowable expenses, landlords frequently over-pay and reclaim. **Reclaim route = the UK Self Assessment return** (claiming mortgage-interest Section 24 credit, management fees, repairs, insurance, etc.). REMOVE the "£10,000 threshold / NRL8 for smaller amounts" framing entirely. Route to the self-assessment-filing spoke (SA100/SA105/SA109).

7. **Ongoing UK tax: Self Assessment and Making Tax Digital** (~300 words). Non-resident landlords with UK property income generally must notify and file SA (house position §17.7). MTD-for-ITSA: qualifying income > **£50,000 from 6 April 2026**, **> £30,000 from 6 April 2027**, **> £20,000 from 6 April 2028** (house position §3). NRL withholding operates ALONGSIDE MTD, not instead of it (house position §3 / the MTD-NRL interaction note). Route to the MTD landlords page and the self-assessment spoke.

8. **April 2027 property income rates (enacted)** (~250 words). 9. **Non-resident companies: the 2020 CT transition** (~250 words). Frame only: FA 2019 Sch 5 moved non-resident **company** landlords from income tax to **corporation tax** on 6 April 2020; NRL withholding continues (now credits against CT, not IT); CIR / hybrid-mismatch / Part 7ZA loss rules now apply. Non-residents pay UK CGT on UK land disposals and must file the **60-day** NRCGT return for EVERY disposal regardless of tax due (house position §17.4). Rates 18%/24% residential. This is SEPARATE from the annual SA and from the NRL income scheme. Route to the NRCGT page and the CGT pillar. Foreign tax credit is CLAIMED, not automatic (house position §16.6 do-not-write list). Route to the treaty framework guide.
