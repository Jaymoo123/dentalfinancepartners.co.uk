2. **Why the UK still taxes your UK rental income (~250w).** Source-state taxing rights: UK taxes UK property income regardless of residence (ITTOIA 2005 Part 3, §16.2). DTAs allocate, they do not exempt (§16.6 do-not-write: "DTAs eliminate UK tax" is false). Sets up the whole page.
3. **Are you actually a non-resident landlord? SRT + usual place of abode (~300w).** Persona-level SRT trigger logic (FA 2013 Sch 45, §17.1): automatic overseas tests, the day-count headlines, split-year on departure (§17.2 Cases 1-3). The NRL scheme's own "usual place of abode outside the UK > 6 months" test is distinct from SRT residence, call this out (a landlord can be in the NRL scheme on the abode test even before SRT non-residence settles). Forward-link → SRT decision tree sibling.
4. **The Non-Resident Landlord scheme: what changes about getting paid (~300w).** Statutory withholding (FA 1995 Sch 23; SI 1995/2902, §16.6): agent/tenant withholds 20% unless gross approval. Statutory, NOT treaty-based (§16.6 do-not-write). Forward-link → NRL pillar + withholding sibling. Keep shallow, siblings own depth.
5. **Comparison table + gross-vs-withheld worked example (~280w incl. table).** Snippet-bait table: 20%-withheld vs NRL1-gross across cash-flow / who operates / approval test / annual reconciliation / when each suits. Worked example: £18,000 annual rent, agent withholds £3,600 vs NRL1 gross with the same final liability reconciled at self-assessment, the difference is timing/cash-flow, not total tax. Forward-link → NRL1 gross-approval sibling. (NRL3 tenant route only where rent > £100/week, §17.5, one-line mention.)
6. **What you actually pay: rates, the personal allowance, and allowable expenses (~320w).** 2026/27 rates 20/40/45 on rental profit (§7). **Personal allowance reworded per §704** (see conflict flag, UK + EEA nationals RETAIN it under domestic law; others by treaty). Allowable expenses incl. travel for genuine property-management visits; Section 24 reducer applies equally to non-residents (forward-link → Section 24 guide).
7. **April 2027: separate property income rates (enacted) (~220w).** State as ENACTED law per §7 / §247-253: FA 2026 (Royal Assent 18 March 2026), ss.6-7, from 6 April 2027, England + NI only (Scotland/Wales set own, FA 2026 s.8/Sch 2). 22/42/47. **Critical correction:** the s.24 reducer ALSO rises to 22% (FA 2026 Sch 1, amending ITTOIA ss.274AA/274C + ITA 2007 s.399B), so NO new basic-rate wedge opens (§253 consequence). Do NOT hedge as pending (§255 do-not-write).
8. **Three worked DTA scenarios (~360w).** (a) Australia-resident: UK source tax first, Australia taxes worldwide, FTC CLAIMED in Australia for UK tax paid (not automatic, §16.6). (b) US-citizen abroad: saving clause (§16.5) means US taxes its citizens worldwide regardless; UK source tax + US FTC. (c) UAE-resident: no UAE income tax, so FTC is asymmetric and UK tax is effectively the floor (§16.5 UAE note). Each links → foreign-tax-credit sibling. Frame: DTAs allocate + relieve double tax, they do not zero UK tax (§16.6).
9. **Capital gains tax when you sell (~250w).** NRCGT: every non-resident UK land disposal is reportable within 60 days regardless of tax due (§17.4 / §5). Rates 18%/24% residential 2026/27, £3,000 AEA where the individual would qualify if resident (§5/§17.4). Rebasing 5 Apr 2015 residential / 5 Apr 2019 non-residential (§17.4). Temporary-non-residence trap: returning within 5 years (s.10A, NOT 4, §17.3) can recapture gains. Forward-link → both NRCGT siblings.
10. **Holding via a limited company (~230w).** Corporation tax **corrected per §House-position conflict**: 25% main rate, 19% small profits rate (≤£50k), marginal relief £50k-£250k (effective 26.5%) per §21.4 / §21.A, NOT a loose "19-25%". 13. **(Optional) Common mistakes (~140w).** Tightened from current page: NRL non-registration; missing 60-day NRCGT clock; assuming DTA zeroes UK tax; not claiming FTC (it must be claimed); temp-non-residence 5-year recapture surprise.
