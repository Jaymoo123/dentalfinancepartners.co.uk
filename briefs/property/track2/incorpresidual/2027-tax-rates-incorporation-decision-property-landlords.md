Post-FA-2026 it must be CLAIMED (new TCGA 1992 s.162(1)(b)); the old s.162A disapplication election was repealed (FA 2026 s.39). The claim deadline runs off the TRANSFER tax year (first anniversary of the 31 January following), not off April 2027. Business test per Ramsay v HMRC [2013] — active portfolio management, not one or two passive lets. Forward-link the s.162 depth page and the "incorporate without CGT" page.

For a basic-rate landlord living on the rent, the extraction cost cancels the corporate advantage whether they act early or late, so there is nothing to rush for. Include the **timing-decision comparison table** (below). Forward-link the sibling for the full extraction math; do NOT reproduce the full rate-by-rate comparison here.

**H2 6 — The no-transfer path: buy future purchases through the company (~240 words).** The cleanest timing answer of all: a landlord uncertain about incorporating an existing portfolio can buy the NEXT property through a company from day one.
