## Gap-mode diagnosis (Stage 1 reasoning, refined with Stage 2 data)

**Primary: STALE_FACTS (multiple, all load-bearing).** This is the dominant gap mode and the rewrite's first job. Six discrete errors, each independently reader-misleading:

1. **MTD quarterly deadlines wrong (the headline error).** Source says quarters are due **5 Aug / 5 Nov / 5 Feb / 5 May** (FAQ #2, FAQ #1, body lines 36 + 44 + "by the 5th of each submission month" line 161). **CORRECT is 7 Aug 2026 / 7 Nov 2026 / 7 Feb 2027 / 7 May 2027**, the 7th day of the second month after each quarter-end, per house_positions §19.6 and the already-corrected sibling `mtd-quarterly-deadlines-2026-2027-landlords` (rewritten 2026-05-21). The quarter PERIOD ends (5 Jul / 5 Oct / 5 Jan / 5 Apr) are correct; only the SUBMISSION deadlines are wrong. This is the load-bearing fix, the entire calendar's MTD rows are off by two days, and the page contradicts its own sibling.

2. **MTD late-submission penalties wrongly framed as escalating fixed amounts.** Source says "£200 for the first failure, rising to £400 and £800 for subsequent defaults within 12 months" (FAQ #4, body line 45). **WRONG.** Per house_positions §3 / §19.7 the regime is **points-based**: 1 point per missed quarterly update; a **£200 fixed penalty applies only at the 4-point threshold** (within a 24-month rolling window), then per further late submission while at threshold; points reset after 24 months of compliant filing. There is no £400/£800 escalator. Also **missing**: the confirmed 2026/27 first-year soft-landing (lighter-touch enforcement, the £200 financial penalty deferred where failures are not deliberate in year one). The soft-landing does NOT cover the Final Declaration or late payment, state that limit explicitly (per the corrected MTD sibling FAQ "Is there a soft-landing period for penalties?").

3. **31 July 2026 conflated with the SA filing deadline.** Source line 41-42 puts "2025/26 Self Assessment Deadline and Payments" on 31 July 2026 and implies the 2025/26 return is filed/late-penalised then. **WRONG.** **31 July 2026 is the SECOND payment on account** (for 2025/26). The 2025/26 SA online filing deadline AND balancing payment is **31 January 2027** (TMA 1970 s.8 / s.59B). The page must separate "payment-on-account dates" from "filing deadlines" cleanly. (The "£100 late filing penalty" sentence wrongly attached to the 31 July block must move to the 31 January filing context.)

4. **April 2027 property rates framed loosely and missing the §7 reducer correction.** Source (FAQ #3 + body lines 112-119) asserts 22%/42%/47% "apply regardless of your other income" and frames it as a future change without anchoring it as enacted law. Per house_positions §7 [LOCKED]: the rates were **announced at the Autumn Budget 2025 (26 November 2025)** and are **ENACTED by Finance Act 2026, ss.6-7, Royal Assent 18 March 2026**, effective 6 April 2027, for **England and Northern Ireland only** (Scotland and Wales set their own property income rates, FA 2026 s.8 / Sch 2). State as enacted law, not a proposal. **Critically missing:** the §7 consequence that the **Section 24 finance-cost reducer RISES to 22%** (FA 2026 Sch 1 amends ITTOIA 2005 ss.274AA/274C and ITA 2007 s.399B), so **no new basic-rate wedge opens**, a basic-rate landlord is NOT newly worse off because the reducer tracks the 22% rate. The page must NOT imply basic-rate landlords face a fresh hit. (Note: the existing FAQ phrase "regardless of your other income" is acceptable as a description of separate property rates, but must be rewritten so it does not imply a new wedge.)

5. **Pinned interest rate + missing MTD late-payment regime.** Source says "Late payment interest currently 7.75% per year" (FAQ #4, body lines 131-132). **WRONG to pin**, HMRC late-payment interest is variable and reset by reference to the Bank of England base rate; do not assert a single fixed percentage. Separately, the page describes only the **legacy SA late-payment penalties** and never describes the **MTD-ITSA late-PAYMENT regime** that applies from 6 April 2026 per §19.7: **3% of unpaid tax from day 15, a further 3% from day 30, then 10% per annum from day 31** (Spring Statement 2025 acceleration; do NOT use the legacy 31/46/91 day-triggers at 2%/2%/4%, which now apply only to non-MTD income tax and VAT). State interest as "variable, set by reference to the Bank of England base rate" with a link to the gov.uk rates page, not a hard number.

6. **60-day CGT stated without the "where tax is due" qualifier.** Source line 106-107 says report and pay within 60 days of completion, full stop. Per house_positions §5 / §6: for **UK residents** a 60-day return is required **only where CGT is actually due**, no 60-day filing where the gain is fully covered by PRR, losses, or the AEA. **Non-UK residents** must file for **every** UK land disposal regardless of tax due (§17.4). The hub must carry the qualifier and forward-link to the CGT-payment-deadlines sibling for the full mechanics.
