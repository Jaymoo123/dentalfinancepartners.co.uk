## Gap-mode diagnosis (Stage 1 reasoning, refined with Stage 2 data)

**Primary: STALE_FACTS / WRONG-ADVICE (the load-bearing fix).** FAQ #4 (frontmatter line 22-23) states landlords face "penalties ranging from £200 for late quarterly updates to £300+ for inadequate records. 1. **MTD ITSA late-submission is a POINTS-based regime, not an immediate £200 per update.** §19.7 / §19.9 / §19.19: 1 point per missed quarterly update; the **£200 fixed penalty only bites at the 4-point threshold** (for quarterly filers), within the points cycle. §19.9 explicitly lists "Late submission produces an immediate £200 penalty" as a DO-NOT-WRITE. The current FAQ is the canonical wrong framing.
2. **The page omits the 12-month soft-landing.** April-2026 joiners get a grace period with no late-submission penalty points for late quarterly updates in the first year (gov.uk / NRLA-verified per diagnosis, **verify exact scope + duration at write time against gov.uk** as this is an HMRC easement, not primary statute). The hub omitting this is a material gap.
3. **"£300+ for inadequate records" is invented/understated.** Inadequate-records exposure is **up to £3,000 per year of failure under TMA 1970 s.12B(5)** (house position §27.7 + §19.16; the Wave 4 sibling FAQ #13 and the Landlord-Tax-Essentials page both already use the correct £3,000). Bigger record-failure exposure flows via FA 2007 Sch 24 (inaccuracy) / FA 2008 Sch 41 (failure-to-notify) on the consequential tax loss, NOT via a "£300" figure.

The rewrite MUST replace FAQ #4 + any body penalty mention with: points-system + £200-at-4-point-threshold + 12-month grace (verify) + £3,000 inadequate-records (s.12B(5)), citing gov.uk + TMA 1970 s.12B(5).
