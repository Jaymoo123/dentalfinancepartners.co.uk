Each row's final column forward-links to the owning page. **This table is the snippet-bait and the reason the page exists.**
3. **What "market value" actually means (~250 w):** the open-market willing-buyer/willing-seller test; that the same statutory test (s.272 CGT, s.160 IHT) underlies most events but is APPLIED at different moments; comparable sales / income capitalisation / replacement cost methods (borrow RICS Red Book methodology framing); why an online estimate is not enough for HMRC. Forward-link to `free-rental-valuation`.
4. **CGT: base cost on disposal (~280 w + Worked Example 1):** gain = proceeds − allowable cost (purchase price + capital improvements + acquisition/disposal costs). 18%/24% rates + £3,000 AEA (§5). Worked Example 1: a 3-property portfolio sold over two tax years, AEA per year, band-stacking, anonymised, no fees. Forward-link to CGT pillar + calculation guide.
5. **CGT: connected-party and undervalue transfers (~250 w + Worked Example 2):** s.17 deems MV regardless of actual consideration; transfer to a relative / into a company is taxed on MV not on the price paid; spouse transfers are the no-gain/no-loss exception (s.58, §5). Worked Example 2: transfer of one flat to an adult child at "£0" still triggers CGT on the full MV gain. Forward-link to `selling-house-below-market-value`.
6. **FRS 102 company accounts: fair value (~350 w):** Section 16, investment property initially at cost, subsequently at fair value at each balance-sheet date, changes through profit or loss, no depreciation; fair-value gains are not distributable until realised. **Deferred tax under Section 29**, the timing difference between accounting profit (which recognises unrealised fair-value gains) and taxable profit (which does not tax them until disposal); deferred tax measured at the rates expected on disposal. Cite Section 16 + Section 29; re-verify periodic-review (1 Jan 2026) wording at write (§16.35).
7. **FRS 105 micro-entity alternative + LLP contrast (~250 w):** FRS 105 (small company meeting micro-entity thresholds), investment property at HISTORIC COST, no fair-value model, **deferred tax prohibited**; simpler but no balance-sheet uplift. LLP, FRS 102 Section 16 commonly elected but **no LLP-level deferred tax** (tax-transparent) vs LtdCo Section 29 overhead (§11.C.Z.9). This is the differentiator competitors miss. Forward-link to `limited-companies-and-btl-properties` / `llp-accounts`.
8. **IHT: date-of-death value (~250 w + Worked Example 3):** IHT MV at date of death (s.160); NRB £325,000 (§15.1); gifts within 7 years valued at MV at date of gift (§15.2); the CGT base-cost UPLIFT on death (s.62, heirs acquire at date-of-death MV, accrued gains wiped) as the key CGT/IHT interaction. Worked Example 3: inherited portfolio, executors value at date of death, heirs' CGT base cost resets. Forward-link to `inheritance-tax-rental-property-uk-guide`.
9. **IHT: does BPR help BTL landlords? (~220 w):** Pawson, pure BTL is "mainly investment" (s.105(3)), fails BPR (§22.1); SA with substantial services MAY qualify; **from 6 April 2026, even where APR/BPR applies, a combined £2.5m rolling-7-year 100% allowance (IHTA 1984 s.124D, FA 2026 c.11), 50% above** (§15.4). Cite s.124D direct, NOT the stale GOV.UK summary. Re-verify FA 2026 enacted status/figure at write (§16.35).
10. **Incorporation: market value at the date of transfer (~250 w + Worked Example 4):** transferring a portfolio into a company is a connected-party transfer at MV (s.17); incorporation relief (s.162) can defer the CGT where the whole business is transferred for shares; SDLT and the company's opening fair-value base all key off the transfer-date valuation. Worked Example 4: incorporation MV pins the CGT gain, the company's FRS 102 opening fair value, and the share base cost. Forward-link to `incorporating-property-portfolio-uk-2026` / `section-162-incorporation-relief`.
11. **How to get the valuation HMRC will accept (~200 w):** professional valuation evidence (RICS surveyor / experienced local agent); date-specific valuations for incorporation, inheritance, sale; HMRC can challenge unsupported estimates; valuation cost is usually tax-deductible (NO fee figure). Inline CTA: discovery-call.
12. **Common valuation mistakes (~200 w):** purchase price ≠ current value; ignoring portfolio discount when sold as a lot (allocate the lot price to each property by relative MV for per-property gains); leasehold/short-lease drag; overlooking Section 29 deferred tax on company fair-value gains (affects distributable reserves).
13. **When to seek professional help + Final thoughts (~180 w):** complex/mixed portfolios; incorporation; inheritance. Inline CTA.
