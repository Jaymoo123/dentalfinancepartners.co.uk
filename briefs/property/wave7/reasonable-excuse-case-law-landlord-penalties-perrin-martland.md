- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-24):**

> Reasonable excuse is the working statutory defence in penalty appeals — but most competitor content treats it as an HMRC-officer-discretion concept, which is wrong (§27.9). It is a statutory defence under Sch 41 para 20 + Sch 24 para 14, appealable to the First-tier Tribunal, with the controlling Upper Tribunal authority Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 setting the four-stage test. The page is the case-law deep-dive that B5 + B6 + B7 forward-cite for mitigation argument. Three readers in scope: (i) landlord with FtC / WDF / LPC penalty exposure considering reasonable-excuse defence; (ii) landlord with late-appeal application after missing 30-day Notice-of-Appeal window; (iii) accountant preparing FTT submission citing reasonable-excuse case-law. Page walks four sections: (1) **statutory framework** — Sch 41 para 20 (reasonable excuse defence to failure-to-notify) + Sch 24 para 14 (careless penalty suspension on reasonable-excuse grounds; deliberate cannot be suspended); these are distinct mechanics, do not collapse; (2) **Perrin four-stage test (UKUT 2018)** — (a) what was the excuse (taxpayer must identify); (b) was it objectively reasonable (the "reasonable person facing those circumstances" test); (c) did the excuse cause the failure (causation, not pretext); (d) was the failure remedied without unreasonable delay once the excuse ceased to apply (the timing-of-cure requirement most reasonable-excuse arguments fail on); (3) **Martland three-stage late-appeal framework (UKUT 2018, drawing on Denton CA)** — (a) length of delay; (b) reason for delay; (c) all the circumstances of the case (balancing exercise); the relief sought is permission for late Notice of Appeal under TMA s.49 / rule 20 of SI 2009/273; (4) **practical patterns from landlord case-law** — illness, bereavement, accountant failure, software failure (rarely successful), reliance on professional advice (qualified — Hok-derived authorities). Worked scenario: landlord with Sch 41 failure-to-notify penalty argues reasonable excuse on bereavement grounds — applies Perrin four stages: (a) excuse identified — death of spouse who handled finances; (b) objectively reasonable for grieving spouse; (c) caused failure (spouse handled the rental income previously); (d) remedied within 6 months of grief subsiding (within reasonable timeframe); FTT likely to accept defence. Critical framing per §27.9: reasonable excuse is **statutory + appealable**, not officer discretion.
