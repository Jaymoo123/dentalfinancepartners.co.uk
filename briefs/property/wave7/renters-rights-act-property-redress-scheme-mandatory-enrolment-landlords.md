> Mandatory enrolment for residential landlords under RRA 2025 ss.64-74 (2025 c. 26). **CRITICAL framing per §26.5 + §26.1 (HP-lock 2026-05-24): the regime is PLURAL APPROVED SCHEMES — s.64 empowers the Secretary of State to approve one OR MORE redress schemes. Do NOT write "single statutory ombudsman" or "single landlord ombudsman" — those framings appear widely in competitor / press coverage but are policy commentary, not statutory text.** (a) Scope — which landlords must enrol (residential landlords subject to RRA 2025 Pt 2; agent-side regime is the pre-existing Redress Schemes for Lettings Agency Work and Property Management Work Order 2014, SI 2014/2359 in force since 1 October 2014 — distinguish, do not conflate); (b) s.64 approval mechanism — Secretary of State approval criteria; multiple approved schemes can operate in parallel; (c) s.65 scheme criteria — complaints handling, redress remedies, independence; (d) s.66 penalty matrix for non-membership — verify verbatim from s.66 as enacted (HP-lock has £7k breach / £40k offence; confirm at write time); (e) **what is NOT on the face of the Act**: the widely-cited "£25,000 compensation cap" is policy commentary only, not statutory — write as "no statutory compensation cap on the face of the Act; government policy commentary has referenced [policy figure] but this is not legislated" per §26.5 + §26.8 do-not-write list; (f) commencement under SI 2025/1354 or SI 2026/421 (verify at write time which provisions of ss.64-74 are in force on what date); (g) operational enrolment process when first approved schemes go live; (h) tax-side: subscription fees deductible against rental income; penalties NOT deductible per s.54 ITEPA principle. NOT writing the agent-side regime (pre-existing, separate).
