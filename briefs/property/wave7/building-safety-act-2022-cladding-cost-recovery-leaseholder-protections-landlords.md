> Building Safety Act 2022 (2022 c. 30) Schedule 8 leaseholder protections — depth on what is protected, what is not, and the commencement chain. (a) **Qualifying-lease definition under s.119** — pre-14-February-2022 grant; lessee on 14 February 2022; only-or-principal-home OR ≤3 UK residential properties (the "three properties or fewer" test that excludes most portfolio landlords); (b) **Cladding remediation fully protected (Sch 8 para 8)** — qualifying leases cannot be charged for cladding remediation regardless of cost; this is the strongest protection in the Act; (c) **Non-cladding low-value carve-out (Sch 8 para 4)** — service charges below specific thresholds carved out from the protection; £175k / £325k Greater London thresholds (verify exact figures from Sch 8 para 4 at write time); (d) **Graduated cap for non-cladding (Sch 8 para 6)** — £10k / £15k / £50k / £100k thresholds depending on property value and lease characteristics (verify); (e) **HRB definition under s.65** — **18m OR 7 storeys + 2 residential units** (NOT 11m / 5 storeys — that is the qualifying-lease cohort, a different threshold per §26.8 do-not-write list and HP-lock catch); (f) **Commencement chain** — England chain via SI 2022/561 + subsequent SIs; Welsh commencement via SI 2025/1368 (Welsh commencement, NOT England — common drift catch); LFRA 2024 amendments commenced 31 October 2024 via SI 2024/1018; (g) **LFRA 2024 (Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024)** — Sch 8 amendments effective 31 Oct 2024; expansion of protected building scope and clarifications; (h) **Capital-vs-revenue split on landlord-borne remediation spend** per §26.7 — cladding replacement = capital; minor fire-safety equipment replacement = generally revenue; major reconstruction = capital. NOT writing the Building Safety Levy (developer-funded; separate framework); NOT writing the VAT-side cladding remediation relief (Wave 5 already covers via `vat-cladding-remediation-relief-residential-building-safety-act-section-30a-mechanics`).
