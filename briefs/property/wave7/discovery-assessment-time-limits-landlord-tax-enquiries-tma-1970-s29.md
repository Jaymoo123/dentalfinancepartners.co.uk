- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-24):**

> The single most-misunderstood mechanic in landlord enquiry response is which discovery time limit applies. Competitor content collapses it into "HMRC has 6 years" (false in general — see §27.9) or "12-year offshore for careless" (false — s.36A applies even WITHOUT careless behaviour). The page sets out four independent tracks: (1) ordinary 4-year limit under TMA 1970 s.34 — no behaviour element required, applies from end of year of assessment; (2) careless 6-year under s.36(1) — Sch 24 careless behaviour by taxpayer or person acting on their behalf; (3) deliberate 20-year under s.36(1A) — Sch 24 deliberate or deliberate-concealed conduct; (4) **offshore innocent-error 12-year under s.36A FA 2019** — the 2019 reform giving HMRC a 12-year window where loss of tax is attributable to offshore income / gains / assets EVEN where the taxpayer behaved entirely innocently. The page then walks the s.29 discovery framework: the s.29(1) "discovered" threshold (low bar — "view that there is an insufficiency"); the two unlock conditions for assessment outside the enquiry window — s.29(4) careless / deliberate behaviour OR s.29(5) competent-officer test (officer could not have been reasonably expected, on information then available, to be aware of the under-assessment). Veltema and Sanderson are the FTT/UT authorities on s.29(5); **HMRC v Tooth [2021] UKSC 17** is the current Supreme Court authority on the deliberate-behaviour test (and the most important recent shift in the discovery landscape). Two worked scenarios: (i) landlord with three years of undeclared UK rental income returned via Let Property Campaign — disclosure within 12 months unprompted → 0% penalty under Sch 24 + 4-year time limit applies; (ii) landlord with five years of undeclared Spanish holiday-let income — Spain is Cat 1 (CRS information exchange) but s.36A 12-year offshore limit applies (independent of behaviour); HMRC can assess years 1-5 if discovered within 12 years of relevant year-end. The page is the **foundational discovery-framework reference** that B5 / B6 / B7 / B10 forward-cite.

**Stage 1 manager note:** **B1 → B7 sequencing locked.** B7 (WDF + FtC overlay) cites s.36A FA 2019 as the operative offshore time-limit. B1 must ship before B7. B1 → B6 + B5 + B10 also forward-cite for time-limit architecture.

**Pool-thinness disclosure:** Most landlord-facing competitor pages collapse the four-bracket framework into one or two. Specialist tax-disputes firms cover it well but rarely landlord-flavoured.
