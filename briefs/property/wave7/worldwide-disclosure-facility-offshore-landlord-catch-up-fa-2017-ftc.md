- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-24):**

> WDF is the working voluntary-disclosure route for landlords with undisclosed UK tax liability connected to offshore income, gains or assets — typically European or US holiday-lets, foreign rental property held by UK residents, foreign-currency mortgage income or losses, and overseas-held SPV income. The page covers WDF mechanics AND the critical FtC FA 2017 Sch 18 overlay that determines the penalty regime for post-2018 offshore disclosures. Three readers in scope: (i) UK-resident landlord with European holiday-let income never declared; (ii) UK-resident with offshore-trust-held UK property (post-IHT 2017 deemed-domicile rules); (iii) non-resident landlord historic catch-up. Page walks five layers: (1) **WDF eligibility** — any person with undisclosed UK tax liability connected to offshore income, gains or assets; campaign opened 5 September 2016, ongoing; (2) **90-day disclosure timeline** from notification (extendable to 180 days for complex cases); penalty banding via Sch 24 / Sch 41 with offshore Cat 2/3 uplifts (see B8 — Cat 1 same-as-onshore for full-info-exchange territories like CRS members; Cat 2 ×1.5 for partial-info-exchange; Cat 3 ×2 for no-info-exchange — published HMRC Order classifies territories, verify at write time); (3) **NO criminal-prosecution immunity** — HMRC retains discretion to prosecute. WDF disclosures may unlock prosecution-deferral by HMRC operational discretion but not statutory immunity. This is the §27.9 do-not-write-item: "WDF gives criminal-prosecution immunity" is FALSE; only CoP9 / CDF (B3) gives immunity; (4) **the FtC FA 2017 Sch 18 overlay — operationally critical** — standalone offshore-correction obligation required taxpayers with undeclared offshore tax matters to disclose **by 30 September 2018**. Post-deadline failures attract minimum 200% penalty (Sch 18 para 4) reducible to 100% on full unprompted disclosure + Sch 18 para 7 asset-based penalty + naming-and-shaming. **The deadline was NOT extended** per §27.9. Post-2018 disclosures via WDF do NOT avoid FtC penalty — the WDF route operates within the FtC framework where the underlying liability is pre-October 2018; (5) **s.36A FA 2019 12-year offshore time limit** (B1) means HMRC can assess offshore years back to 12 years from end of year of assessment, EVEN where the taxpayer was innocent — the 12-year window is the no-behaviour offshore extension. So a 2014/15 offshore undeclared liability is in-time for HMRC assessment until end of 2026/27. Two worked scenarios: (i) UK-resident with 6 years of undeclared French holiday-let income (Cat 1 — France full-info-exchange), pre-2018 liability triggers FtC overlay → 100% penalty floor (post-deadline unprompted disclosure); (ii) UK-resident with offshore-trust rental income from BVI source (Cat 3 — no-info-exchange historically; CRS landscape may have shifted — verify), 4 years of liability, prompted offshore disclosure deliberate-not-concealed → 140% × tax loss + Sch 21 FA 2015 asset-move penalty if assets moved to defeat HMRC.
