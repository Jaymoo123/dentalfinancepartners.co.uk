## Manager pre-decisions

- **Suggested slug:** `record-retention-discipline-voluntary-disclosure-failure-to-notify-landlords`

- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-24):**

> Two of the most-misstated facts in landlord-facing content (per §27.9) are (a) the record-retention floor is "6 years" — wrong for income tax (5 years after 31 January following tax year per TMA 1970 s.12B); wrong for corporation tax in the other direction (6 years from end of accounting period per CA 2006 s.388 + CTA 2009 s.386); and (b) the "7-year statutory floor" — wrong (7 years is firm-practice recommendation only, not statutory). The page corrects both and combines record-retention discipline with the disclosure decision-tree (the operational question landlords ask when they realise records reveal an under-declaration). Three readers in scope: (i) landlord wondering how long to keep rental-related records; (ii) landlord with company-held BTL trying to align s.12B / s.388 / s.386 retention floors; (iii) landlord with newly-surfaced records revealing prior-year under-declaration and needing to choose between LPC / WDF / DDS / CoP9 routes. Page walks five sections: (1) **TMA 1970 s.12B income-tax retention** — 5 years after 31 January following the tax year (2026/27 records to 31 January 2033); rental is "business" for s.12B per Salisbury House Estate principle (so the longer business floor applies, not the 22-month non-business floor); records retention extends if enquiry open; (2) **CA 2006 s.388 + CTA 2009 s.386 company-landlord retention** — 6 years from end of accounting period for private companies; Companies House offences attach for failure to keep adequate accounting records (independent of HMRC penalty exposure); (3) **s.12B(5) £3,000 per year penalty** for record-keeping-only failures — rarely the operative penalty (Sch 24 / Sch 41 dominate on consequential tax loss); (4) **MTD ITSA digital-records evidence discipline (§19.16)** — runs alongside s.12B; digital-link requirement constrains form (digital-not-paper-only) for landlords within MTD scope; does NOT extend retention period; (5) **disclosure decision-tree** — when records reveal historical under-declaration, choose between LPC (residential rental — B6), WDF + FtC (offshore — B7), DDS (other UK matters), CoP9 (serious fraud with criminal-prosecution exposure — B3); decision criteria walked through with brief worked scenarios. **Firm's 7-year practical recommendation** clearly framed as practice-not-statute per §27.9 — one year above s.12B floor as risk-buffer for late-discovered errors and a defensible audit position. Two worked scenarios: (i) sole-trader landlord with 2018/19-2024/25 rental records; statutory floor for 2024/25 is 31 January 2031; firm recommends 31 January 2032; the 6-year-from-period-end framing some competitors give (false floor 5 April 2031) is wrong; (ii) LtdCo landlord with year-end 31 March 2025 accounts; CA 2006 s.388 floor is 31 March 2031; s.12B does not apply to the company.
