# Seed brief: Starting a Property Business: Sole Trader vs Limited Company vs Partnership

## Working title

Starting a Property Business: Personal Ownership vs Limited Company vs Partnership (Choosing Your Structure Before You Buy)

## Statutory anchor

- **s.24 finance-cost restriction and the 2027 22% reducer** — house_positions §7 (LOCKED). Key facts for this page:
  - 2026/27: Section 24 reducer = 20% basic-rate credit; property income at 20/40/45%
  - 2027/28 onwards (England, Wales, NI): property income at 22/42/47%; Section 24 reducer rises to 22% (FA 2026 Sch 1 amending ITTOIA 2005 ss.274AA/274C and ITA 2007 s.399B)
  - s.24 does NOT apply to companies (CT, not IT) — this is the central planning tension
  - Do not write: "reducer stays at 20% in 2027/28" or "FA 2026 is a proposal"
- **Property income tax (personal ownership, sole trader framing):** ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (property income); §7 rates lock above
- **Limited company structure:** CTA 2009/CTA 2010; Corporation Tax currently 25% main rate (19% small profits rate to £50k, marginal 26.5% £50k-£250k) — house_positions §21 for FIC/LtdCo framing (verify CT rates are locked there; if not, rate-by-reference per §16.27)
- **Partnership / LLP:** Partnership Act 1890 s.1 (general partnership); LLPA 2000 (LLP); ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 ss.846-863 (partnership income); house_positions §11.C (entity distinction, partnership taxonomy); s.24 applies to partners as individuals (NOT disapplied by partnership structure)
- **SDLT on later restructuring:** FA 2003 — transfer into company attracts full SDLT (market value consideration for connected parties, s.53 FA 2003); transfer into partnership may use Sch 15 SLP route if genuine pre-existing partnership (house_positions §1.A). This page must NOT re-cover Sch 15 SLP mechanics in full — one paragraph flag + cross-link to the dedicated Sch 15 page
- **CGT on later restructuring:** TCGA 1992 s.162 (incorporation relief — business assets rolled into company); s.165 (gift holdover, restricted for investment property). Stage 2 must verify s.162 qualification conditions for a property letting business (HMRC's position on whether a letting business qualifies as a "business" for s.162 is contested territory — flag in brief)
- **No HP lock exists for the three-way entity-choice framing for new property investors** — this brief does not require a new HP section beyond citing §7 (s.24 reducer), §1 (SDLT on restructure), and §11.C (entity taxonomy). Stage 2 may proceed on a per-write-verify basis for CT rates (rate-by-reference) and CGT restructuring provisions.

## Key questions for Stage 2

1. For a new investor buying ONE BTL property in personal name (sole trader / jointly with partner): at what rental income level does the s.24 wedge start to hurt? Provide a worked maths example at 2026/27 rates and 2027/28 rates showing the finance-cost wedge for a higher-rate taxpayer.
2. For a limited company structure: what are the practical friction costs (accountancy, filing, mortgage rate premium, CT compliance) that eat into the CT rate advantage? At what profit level does the company structure break even vs personal ownership?
3. For a partnership / LLP: does a general partnership between spouses avoid s.24? (No — s.24 applies to each partner as an individual on their share. But income splitting between a lower-rate spouse and a higher-rate spouse may still reduce the effective wedge. Verify via §11.C.4 PIM1030 reference.)
4. What is the SDLT cost of incorporating a personal-name portfolio AFTER purchase? (Market value consideration, s.53 FA 2003 — worked example for a £400k property with a £300k mortgage.) Cross-link to existing restructuring page; do not re-cover in full.
5. Does s.162 TCGA 1992 (incorporation relief) apply to a rental property portfolio? HMRC contests whether a letting business is a "qualifying business" for s.162 — what is the current position and what does Stage 2 find from legislation.gov.uk + HMRC CG manual?
6. For a new investor planning to scale to 5+ properties: is there a threshold below which personal ownership is clearly cheaper, and above which company structure becomes clearly better?
7. What are the practical non-tax differences (mortgage accessibility, lender appetite for Ltd Co BTL vs personal, SPV structuring) that belong in a decision framework even though they are not tax?
8. LLP vs general partnership for property: what are the liability and governance differences, and does the LLP have any tax advantage for property income vs a general partnership? (Per §11.C — both are transparent for income tax; salaried-member rules for LLPs are relevant if one partner takes a fixed fee.)
9. What is the interaction with the 2027 22% property income rate for BASIC-rate investors? (Per §7: no new wedge at basic rate because reducer tracks to 22%; this may reduce the urgency for basic-rate landlords to incorporate purely for s.24 reasons — state correctly.)
