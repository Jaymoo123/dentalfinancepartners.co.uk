- **TCGA 1992 s.169H** — BADR definition of "qualifying business disposal"

## Key questions (for Stage 2 research + writer)

1. What is the "trading company" test for BADR? How is "mainly" (s.165A / TCGA 1992 s.169S "trading company" definition) applied to a property company? (Property investment = investment activity; pure BTL companies fail.)
2. Why does plain buy-to-let investment property fail the trading test? (Passive rent collection is investment, not trade — cite HMRC CG64090 / case law; distinguish from a genuine property development / trading company.)
3. FHL: BADR was available on FHL disposals until 5 April 2025. What exactly was the qualifying condition (use as FHL business, 2-year qualifying period, s.169I)? Anti-forestalling: what is now prevented?
4. Serviced accommodation / short-let operations post-FHL abolition: can these ever qualify as a "trade" for BADR purposes? What HMRC criteria apply? (This is the edge case that readers will search for — requires careful "it depends on level of services" answer with clear caveats.)
5. Property development and trading companies: what structures genuinely qualify for BADR (buying-to-develop-and-sell is a trade; buying-to-rent is not)? What are the indicators HMRC uses?
6. What is the 5% material disposal test for shares in a property company that IS a trading company? (2-year ownership of 5%+ of shares in a trading company.)
7. BADR rate timeline: 10% (historical) → 14% (6 April 2025 to 5 April 2026) → 18% (from 6 April 2026). Is the current rate 18%? Stage 2 must WebFetch HMRC BADR guidance to confirm current published rate matches house_positions §5.
8. What is the £1 million lifetime limit and how does it interact with multiple disposals across years?
9. Mixed-use property company (some commercial letting, some residential investment): does the commercial side tip the balance to "trading"? (Unlikely — HMRC uses proportion test; purely commercial property investment also fails trading test.)
10. What is the position on incorporation relief (s.162 TCGA) + subsequent BADR claim on shares: does incorporating a letting portfolio into a company then selling shares achieve BADR? (No — investment company shares; BADR requires trading company.)
