# Seed brief: Property Income Allowance: The £1,000 Exemption vs Actual Expenses

## Working title

The £1,000 Property Income Allowance: When to Use It and When to Claim Actual Expenses Instead

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary:** ITTOIA 2005 Part 6A ss.783B-783BQ (property allowance: full relief, partial relief election, interaction with other reliefs, blocked situations) — NEW HP LOCK NEEDED (§29 and other §-refs do not cover the property allowance; s.24 HP covers finance-cost restriction only; see F-32 below)
- **Key sub-provisions to verify at Stage 2:**
  - s.783B: definition and scope of the property allowance
  - s.783BC: full relief (income at or below £1,000 = no Self Assessment liability on that income)
  - s.783BE / s.783BF: partial relief election (income above £1,000: taxable profit = gross income minus £1,000, rather than actual expenses)
  - s.783BJ / s.783BK: blocked cases (employer / own company paying rent — allowance unavailable)
  - s.783BL / s.783BM: interaction with rent-a-room relief (mutually exclusive)
  - Joint ownership: allowance is per-person (each co-owner gets their own £1,000)
- **Cross-reference:** s.24 finance-cost restriction (ITTOIA 2005 / ITA 2007) — house_positions §4 covers s.24; this page must not re-cover s.24 mechanics, but must note that the partial-relief route still allows finance costs as an actual expense (so partial relief can matter for higher-rate landlords with large mortgages)
- **Cross-reference:** MTD property income compliance — `making-tax-digital-property-income-2026-complete-guide` owns MTD; this page must not duplicate MTD content

## Key questions for Stage 2

1. What is the exact wording of the "blocked" conditions in ss.783BJ-783BK? Does the block apply when the EMPLOYER pays rent, or only when the payer is the taxpayer's own company? What about a partnership where the individual is also a partner?
2. Can a landlord elect partial relief in year 1 and switch to actual expenses in year 2, or is there a multi-year commitment? What is the election mechanism and deadline?
3. What is the interaction with rent-a-room relief in practice? If a landlord has both a lodger (rent-a-room) and a BTL property, can they claim rent-a-room on the lodger income and the property allowance on the BTL income separately?
4. Joint ownership: if a couple own a rental property 50/50, does each get £1,000 allowance, giving a combined £2,000 exemption? Does this apply to unmarried couples as well as spouses?
5. What counts as "property income" for the allowance? Does it include furnished-holiday letting income? Ground rent? Garage / parking space income?
6. If a landlord's gross property income is £950 (below £1,000), do they still need to register for Self Assessment and file a return, or does the full relief remove that obligation entirely?
7. What is the worked-maths break-even point? (Income level at which actual expenses start beating partial relief — essentially when actual deductible expenses + finance costs > £1,000.)
8. Does the allowance interact with s.24? Specifically, under partial relief a landlord cannot deduct finance costs as "expenses" (they deduct £1,000 instead), but can they still claim the s.24 basic-rate credit? Or does partial relief effectively exclude the s.24 credit too?
9. What is HMRC's published guidance (helpsheet / manual reference) on the property allowance and the election mechanism?
