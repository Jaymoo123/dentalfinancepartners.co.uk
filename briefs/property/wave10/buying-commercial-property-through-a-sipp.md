# Working title
Buying Commercial Property Through a SIPP or SSAS: The Complete Tax Guide

## Statutory anchor

- **FA 2004 Schedule 29A** — taxable property held by investment-regulated pension schemes (inserted by FA 2006, effective 6 April 2006; multiple amendments to 2013+); para 6+ (taxable property definition: residential + tangible moveable property); para 7 (commercial property EXCLUDED); paras 1-2 (investment-regulated scheme definition — SIPPs + SSASs only, not standard occupational DB/DC)
- **FA 2004 s.208** — member's unauthorised payment charge (40% income tax)
- **FA 2004 s.209** — scheme sanction charge (15% on scheme administrator)
- **FA 2004 ss.188-189** — pension contribution tax relief (annual allowance limit)
- **FA 2024 s.XX** — lump-sum allowances replacing lifetime allowance (LTA abolished; use FA 2024 architecture, NOT pre-2024 LTA framework)
- **TCGA 1992 s.271(1)** — CGT exemption for registered pension scheme disposals
- **FA 2004 Sch 30 + SI 2006/206 Reg 6** — SSAS employer loan conditions (50% asset cap / 5-year repayment / 1st-charge security)

**House position references:** §22.21 (SIPP/SSAS commercial property — full lock); §19.12 (pension scheme MTD exclusion); §15.5 (pensions in IHT from 6 April 2027 — cross-reference for estate planning context)

**CRITICAL:** Do not cite pre-2024 lifetime allowance framework. FA 2024 abolished LTA and replaced it with lump-sum allowance (LSA) and lump-sum-and-death-benefit allowance (LSDBA). ## Key questions (for Stage 2 research + writer)

1. What is an "investment-regulated pension scheme" and which scheme types are covered? (SIPP / SSAS yes; typical workplace DC / DB schemes no — Sch 29A paras 1-2.)
2. What exactly is "taxable property" and why does it make residential property unviable inside a SIPP/SSAS? (Sch 29A para 6: residential property definition mirrors SDLT s.116(1)(a); combined effective rate 55-70% via s.208 40% + 15% surcharge + s.209 15% sanction charge.)
3. Why is commercial property specifically excluded from the taxable property rules? (Sch 29A para 7: business premises, offices, warehouses, retail, factories, agricultural land and forestry are NOT taxable property.) What about mixed-use properties?
4. What is the borrowing limit for a SIPP/SSAS purchasing commercial property? (50% of net scheme assets — statutory limit; verify exact statutory anchor at Stage 2 via HMRC PTM manual.)
5. How does the pension scheme purchase work in practice? (Scheme trustees contract as buyer; standard commercial conveyancing; SDLT payable on purchase; VAT/OTT interaction if seller opted to tax.)
6. In-specie contribution of a commercially-held property: what are the CGT, income tax, and annual allowance mechanics? (TCGA 1992 s.271 CGT exemption inside scheme; income tax relief on contribution value subject to annual allowance; FA 2024 lump-sum allowances replace pre-2024 LTA framework — flag F-22.)
7. Can the member's business rent premises back from the SIPP/SSAS? (Yes, at arm's-length market rent; rent payments are tax-deductible for the business; rental income is tax-free inside the pension wrapper — §4 S24 does not apply. The arm's-length condition is critical — HMRC scrutinises below-market rent as an indirect benefit.)
8. SSAS employer loans: what are the conditions for a SSAS to lend to its sponsoring employer? (Sch 30: 50% of scheme assets cap; 5-year repayment term; 1st-charge security; commercial interest rate. Breach = unauthorised payment charge at s.208/209.)
9. IHT angle: commercial property inside a SIPP/SSAS is outside the member's estate for IHT (pension funds generally excluded pre-April 2027 per §15.5). What changes from 6 April 2027 when unspent pension funds are brought into IHT scope? Does commercial property inside a pension become an IHT problem?
10. ATED: is a commercial property held inside a SIPP subject to ATED? (ATED applies to residential dwellings worth over £500k held by non-natural persons — §2. Commercial property is outside ATED scope. Cross-reference §2 briefly.)
