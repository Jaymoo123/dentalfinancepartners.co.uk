# Working title: Gifting Property and Deed of Gift: CGT, IHT, SDLT and the Tax Traps

## Statutory anchor

**Primary:**
- TCGA 1992 s.17 — deemed disposal at market value on gift to a connected person (children, siblings, parents, spouses excluded from CGT no-gain-no-loss treatment unless spousal transfer under s.58); CGT crystallises at market value regardless of actual consideration.
- IHTA 1984 s.3A — a gift to an individual is a Potentially Exempt Transfer (PET); no IHT at the time of gift; fully exempt if donor survives 7 years (§15.2 house position).

**Supporting:**
- TCGA 1992 s.58 — spousal / civil partner transfers: no-gain-no-loss; donee takes donor's historic base cost. Applies only to married couples / civil partners in the same tax year.
- TCGA 1992 s.286 — connected persons definition (gifts to children, siblings, parents are connected-person transactions, triggering s.17 market value rule).
- TCGA 1992 s.165 — hold-over relief on gifts of BUSINESS assets. Does NOT apply to investment residential BTL property (§22.6 house position explicitly: "no holdover for non-business BTL"). Applies to gifts into trust under s.260 if the trust is a CLT (not settlor-interested).
- IHTA 1984 s.102 FA 1986 (Gift with Reservation of Benefit / GROB) — if the donor retains any benefit in the gifted property (e.g. living there rent-free), the gift does NOT reduce the estate; no 7-year clock starts (§15.3 house position).
- IHTA 1984 s.7(4) — taper relief where donor dies between 3 and 7 years after the PET (§15.2: taper reduces TAX not value of the gift; 20%/40%/60%/80% reductions in the IHT charge at years 3/4/5/6 respectively).
- FA 2003 s.43 + Sch 4 para 8 — SDLT on chargeable consideration; if a mortgaged property is gifted and the donee assumes the outstanding mortgage, the mortgage balance IS chargeable consideration for SDLT at standard residential rates (not a gift for SDLT purposes). A pure gift with NO outstanding mortgage: no SDLT (zero consideration).
- ITTOIA 2005 s.620 / s.624 — settlement provisions; outright gift of rental property to a minor child causes income to be attributed back to the parent-donor (§22.9 house position).

**House position reference:** §15.2 (PETs + 7-year clock, taper relief). §15.3 (GROB s.102 FA 1986, POAT, routes around GROB). §22 (s.17 deemed MV disposal; s.165 no holdover for investment BTL; s.286 connected persons). §22.6 (comparison: direct property gift vs FIC share gift). §22.9 (settlements ITTOIA s.624 for gifts to minor children). §1 (SDLT rates for chargeable consideration).
