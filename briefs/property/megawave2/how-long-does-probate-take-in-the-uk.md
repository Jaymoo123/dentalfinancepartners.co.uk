# How Long Does Probate Take in the UK? Realistic Timeline for Property-Owning Estates

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (probate as a legal process): **Senior Courts Act 1981 ss.108-127** (grants of representation regime) + **Non-Contentious Probate Rules 1987 (SI 1987/2024)** as amended. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1981/54/part/V on 2026-05-26. SCA 1981 s.114 + s.116 cover the grant categories (probate where there is a will, letters of administration where there is none); the NCPR govern application procedure, evidence requirements, and contentious-non-contentious routing.
- Primary (the IHT side — the inheritance-tax filing that gates the grant):
  - **IHTA 1984 s.4** — chargeable transfer on death; the IHT account triggers.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.216 + s.226** — duties on personal representatives to deliver an IHT account; payment-of-tax-before-grant rule.
  - **IHT400 + IHT421 framework:** for **non-excepted estates**, the personal representative submits IHT400 (full inheritance tax account) and any payable IHT before the grant. HMRC then issues IHT421 (probate summary / payment receipt) which the personal representative submits to HMCTS Probate Service as evidence the IHT side is settled. **HMRC self-declared 12-week target for IHT421 issuance from receipt of IHT400** per HMRC published service standards. Re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk publication — the 12-week target has been periodically extended in practice.
  - **Excepted estates regime (the IHT205/IHT207 successor):** per the **Inheritance Tax (Delivery of Accounts) (Excepted Estates) (Amendment) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1167)** in force from 1 January 2022, most low-value or wholly-spouse-exempt estates fall outside the IHT400 obligation. Personal representatives self-certify the estate as excepted in the probate application (no separate IHT account filed; HMRC has 60 days after grant to require additional information per IHTA 1984 s.256(1A)).
- Primary (the probate side — the grant application itself):
  - **HMCTS Probate Service** (the post-2019 single online portal). **Published service standard: 16 weeks from receipt of valid application to grant**, per HMCTS Probate Service published service-level targets. Re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk publication.
  - **Stop notices + requisitions:** the Probate Service can pause an application if the documentation is incomplete or inconsistent, adding 4-16 weeks. Common requisition triggers: original will not provided; executor identity inconsistencies; missing IHT421 for non-excepted estate; ambiguity over domicile or jurisdiction.
- Primary (the estate-administration income-tax + CGT regime — the tax compliance during probate):
  - **ITA 2007 s.466** — personal representatives' income tax position; **TCGA 1992 s.62** — uplifted base cost at death (acquisition cost = market value at date of death); **TCGA 1992 s.3** — personal representatives' annual exempt amount available for the tax year of death and the following two tax years (currently £3,000 per HMRC published figures from 6 April 2024; re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk publication).
  - **CTA 2010 ss.34-35** — administration period income tax for personal representatives at the basic rate (20%) on rental income, with no personal allowance available (PRs are not individuals for personal allowance purposes per HMRC TSEM7320).
- Primary (the rental property during administration — the income-tax + CGT mechanics):
  - **Property income from a let asset during administration:** continues to be taxable, with PRs accountable. Rents collected from death until vesting are taxed at PR-level (20% basic rate, no personal allowance) per HMRC TSEM7000+.
  - **Disposal during administration:** uplifted base cost at death under TCGA 1992 s.62; PR's £3,000 annual exempt amount available; PR rate 24% (residential) / 20% (non-residential) per FA 2024 measures from 6 April 2024.
  - **Vesting:** when the property vests in beneficiaries, the beneficiaries acquire at the PR's deemed acquisition price (the probate value), which itself is the death market value under TCGA 1992 s.62.
- Primary (the dispute / contentious-probate routing — adds substantial time):
  - **Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Act 1975** — six-month claim window from grant. The IPFDA 1975 claim window means PRs commonly hold off final distribution until 6 months past grant + reasonable additional period for late-arriving claims. This is the single biggest "non-statutory" delay built into responsible PR practice.
  - **Caveats** under **NCPR 1987 r.44** — any person can lodge a caveat that prevents a grant being issued; valid for 6 months and renewable. Removes the grant from the timeline entirely until resolved.
- Primary (the property-context complications that lengthen probate):
  - **DST (Deeds of Variation) under IHTA 1984 s.142** — post-death variation within 2 years of death. Doesn't lengthen probate per se but commonly motivates PRs to delay final distribution while beneficiaries decide whether to vary.
  - **Section 36 LPA 1925 trustee retirement / appointment** where the will appoints trustees who decline or have predeceased.
  - **Section 27 Trustee Act 1925 statutory notices** to creditors and claimants (2 months minimum; gives PRs statutory protection against late-arriving claims; recommended best practice before final distribution).
- House position references: **§15.1** (NRB, RNRB, transferable allowances — the IHT400 boxes that determine excepted-estate routing); **§15.4** (BPR + APR £1m cap effective 6 April 2026 — affects IHT400 completion for estates with qualifying business or agricultural property dying on or after that date); **§22.2** (deed of variation s.142 IHTA 1984); **§22.17** (IPDI rental-property depth, Wave 7 lock — affects probate sequencing for IPDI-trust-tied rental property); **§15.5** (pensions in IHT from 6 April 2027 — affects estate valuation timing for deaths on or after 6 April 2027). No dedicated probate-timeline HP section; this seed treats the timeline as operational/HMCTS-evidence-led territory.
