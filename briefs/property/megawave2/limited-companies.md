# Limited Companies for UK Property: The Complete Pillar Guide for Landlords (2026/27)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (the entity itself):** Companies Act 2006 (CA 2006) — the primary statute creating UK limited companies. Key sections: **s.1** defines "company" as a company formed and registered under CA 2006 (or under predecessor Acts); **s.3** sets out the three forms of limited company (limited by shares — operative for property; limited by guarantee — uncommon for property; unlimited — exceptionally rare for property); **s.7** + **s.8** + **s.9** govern incorporation procedure (model articles, memorandum requirements, registration); **s.15** confirms the company is a body corporate from the date of incorporation. Cross-reference A1 (M2-A-B1) `a-complete-guide-on-incorporating-a-company-in-uk` for incorporation mechanics depth.
- **Primary (limited liability — the operative attraction):** CA 2006 s.74 (private company limited by shares — member liability limited to amount unpaid on shares). For a typical fully-paid £1-share company, member liability beyond the £1 paid-up share capital is NIL. **Operative consequence for property:** the LtdCo's debts (mortgage; trade creditors; tenant deposit obligations; landlord liability under Housing Act 2004; ATED returns; CT liability) do NOT attach to the shareholder personally except where a personal guarantee has been given (standard lender practice for BTL mortgages) or where statutory provisions pierce the corporate veil (insolvency / fraud / Companies Act enforcement). Cross-reference §11 / §11.A — Companies House compliance + ECCTA framework.
- **Primary (corporation tax framework):** CTA 2010 s.3 (charge to corporation tax); s.18 (small profits rate); s.18A-J (associated companies marginal relief — per A5 M2-A-B1) — 25% main rate FY24+; 19% small profits rate (profits ≤£50k single-company; reduced threshold per associated-company count); 26.5% marginal relief effective rate on profits £50k-£250k. Cross-reference A4 M2-A-B1 pillar lever-map + A5 M2-A-B1 marginal relief depth + A8 this batch group relief.
- **Primary (UK property income inside a company):** CTA 2009 Part 4 (UK property businesses) ss.202-291 — corporation-tax treatment of UK property income. Full interest deductibility under CTA 2009 Part 5 (loan relationships) — **not within scope of ITTOIA 2005 s.272A Section 24** (which applies to individuals only). This is the primary driver of LtdCo adoption for leveraged property portfolios since the 2017-2020 Section 24 phase-in. Forward link to existing `section-24-vs-incorporation-which-saves-more-tax` + this batch's A9 `incorporating-an-hmo-into-a-limited-company-pros-and-cons`.
- **Primary (the dividend extraction route):** ITTOIA 2005 Part 4 Ch 3 + ITA 2007 s.8 — dividend tax rates: £500 dividend allowance + 10.75% basic / 35.75% higher / 39.35% additional from 6 April 2026 per FA 2026 s.4(1)(b) substituting ITA 2007 s.8(2). **Operative effective combined CT + dividend rate for higher-rate individual:** 25% CT + 35.75% dividend on net 75% = 51.8%. Cross-reference §21.4 + A4 + the existing `salary-vs-dividends-property-spv-2026-27-marginal-rate-analysis`. F-20 lock per §21.4.
- **Primary (director duties):** CA 2006 ss.170-177 — general duties of directors (act within powers, promote success of the company, exercise independent judgment, exercise reasonable care/skill/diligence, avoid conflicts of interest, not accept benefits from third parties, declare interest in proposed transactions). Operative for landlords appointing themselves + family members as directors. Cross-reference A1 (M2-A-B1) for incorporation depth.
- **Primary (Companies House compliance — ECCTA framework):** ECCTA 2023 Part 1 + ECTEA 2022 — companies-side reforms covered in depth by §11.A Wave 9 mini-lock. Includes: registered email address (CA 2006 s.88A+ post-ECCTA), appropriate registered office address (CA 2006 ss.86-88), confirmation statement (CA 2006 ss.853A-853L), PSC register (CA 2006 Part 21A), and ID verification for directors + PSCs (in force from 18 November 2025 per §11.A lock). Companies House operational rollout via the campaign page (per §11.A F-12 — primary tracker at `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/`). Cross-reference §11 + §11.A + the existing `eccta-2023-id-verification-mandatory-companies-house-2025-2026-landlord-ltdcos` operational sibling.
- **Primary (statutory accounts):** CA 2006 Part 15 (ss.380-474) — accounts + reports framework. Small companies (most property LtdCos) file abridged or filleted accounts under FRS 105 (micro-entities) or FRS 102 (small companies regime); CA 2006 s.382 + s.383 + s.477 size-test thresholds determine regime + audit exemption. **Operative significance for property LtdCos:** most single-SPV property LtdCos qualify as micro-entities (turnover < £632k, balance sheet total < £316k, ≤ 10 employees — Stage 2 verifies current thresholds at write time per §16.27 + §16.42).
- **Primary (PSC + people-with-significant-control):** CA 2006 Part 21A (ss.790A-790Y) + Sch 1A — register of people with significant control. PSC defined by reference to five conditions: ≥25% shares; ≥25% voting rights; right to appoint/remove majority of board; right to exercise significant influence/control; or significant influence/control over a trust/firm holding the foregoing. Operative for family property LtdCos with multiple shareholders.
- **Primary (the income-vs-trading line for property LtdCos):** CTA 2010 ss.1124-1126 + ITTOIA 2005 s.5 + extensive case-law (Marson v Morton, *Salt v Chamberlain*, the *Iswera* test) — distinguishing property investment (taxed as UK property business per CTA 2009 Part 4) from property trading (taxed as CTA 2009 Part 3 trading profits, with implications for losses, group relief consortium eligibility per A8 this batch, badges of trade analysis). **Operative for landlord LtdCos:** most rental-portfolio LtdCos are investment; mixed retain + develop / flip-vehicle LtdCos may be trading. The line is fact-intensive; Stage 2 cross-links to existing trading-vs-investment site coverage.
- **Primary (FIC sub-category):** Family Investment Company — NOT a separate statutory category; it is a private limited company under CA 2006 used for family-wealth structuring with growth-share + share-class architecture. Covered in depth by §21.5 + §22.6 + sibling MW2 pick B5 `a-complete-guide-to-family-investment-companies-fics`. Cross-reference here as one branch of the LtdCo use-case decision tree.
- **Supporting (the partnership / LLP alternatives):** Limited Partnerships Act 1907 (LP — cross-reference A3 M2-A-B1); Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2000 (LLP — cross-reference A11 + A10 this batch); Partnership Act 1890 (general partnership — cross-reference A7 this batch). The LtdCo decision is a fork in a wider entity-choice decision tree.
- **Supporting (CGT inside a LtdCo):** TCGA 1992 ss.1-2 (corporation tax on chargeable gains) — companies do NOT receive the CGT annual exempt amount (£3k FY26 individual); chargeable gains are added to CT-rated profits. Indexation allowance withdrawn from disposals on or after 1 January 2018 per F(No.2)A 2017 s.26 — frozen at December 2017 RPI for assets held before that date. Cross-reference existing site coverage on CGT inside LtdCo.

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — Quick-compare table (the pillar's headline insight)

A landlord with £50,000 of net rental profit (after deductible operating costs), £40,000 of mortgage interest, and otherwise higher-rate band on personal income:

- **Personal ownership (HRT):** taxable rental income calculated WITHOUT full interest deduction (per ITTOIA 2005 s.272A). £50k taxable income × 40% = £20k. Less 20% basic-rate credit on £40k interest = £8k. Effective tax ≈ **£12k/year**.
- **LtdCo, profits retained (no extraction):** £50k profit minus full £40k interest deductible per CTA 2009 Part 5 = £10k taxable profit. CT at 19% small-profits rate (single company, ≤£50k profit) = **£1.9k/year**. Saving £10.1k vs personal.
- **LtdCo, profits fully extracted at HRT:** £50k − £40k = £10k taxable. CT £1.9k. Distributable £8.1k. Dividend tax on £7.6k (after £500 allowance) at 35.75% = £2.7k. Combined ≈ **£4.6k/year**. Saving £7.4k vs personal.
- **LtdCo, profits fully extracted at ART:** same £10k CT base. Dividend tax at 39.35% on £7.6k = £3.0k. Combined £4.9k. Saving £7.1k vs personal.
- **Operative insight:** retention saves most; extraction at higher / additional rate still saves but by less; the decision turns on retention vs extraction intent. RUN session frames this honestly.

### Example 2 — The BTL LtdCo decision: when retention wins

- **Mrs Patel** owns a 6-property BTL portfolio personally. £90k gross rental; £45k mortgage interest; £20k operating costs. She is a higher-rate individual planning to retain profits for portfolio expansion over the next 10–15 years.
- **Personal ownership:** taxable = £90k − £20k = £70k. Tax at 40% = £28k. Less 20% basic-rate credit on £45k interest = £9k. Effective tax ≈ £19k/year.
- **LtdCo retention scenario:** profit = £90k − £20k − £45k = £25k. CT 19% (small profits) = £4.75k. Retention benefit per year = £14.25k.
- **Payback on incorporation:** SDLT post-MDR-abolition on £900k portfolio (6 × £150k) ≈ £43k + setup ≈ £10k = **£53k one-off cost**. Annual saving £14.25k. Payback ≈ 3.7 years. **LtdCo wins decisively for retention horizon > 4 years.**

### Example 3 — Multi-SPV group structure: when scale demands group operation

- **Singh Properties Group:** HoldCo + 5 SPVs (each holding 2-3 properties). Aggregate portfolio £4m; aggregate profits £200k/year; aggregate interest £100k/year.
- **CT operation:** each SPV computes CT separately. £50k associated-company gating (per CTA 2010 ss.18D–J + A5 M2-A-B1 lock) divides the small-profits / marginal-relief thresholds across the 5 associated companies → each SPV gets £10k small-profits threshold + £50k upper-marginal-relief threshold. Most SPVs land in the marginal-relief band (26.5% effective on profits above £10k per SPV).
- **Group relief flow (per A8 this batch + CTA 2010 Part 5):** SPV-A has loss-year (refurb capex year, £40k loss); SPV-B has profit £30k; SPV-C has profit £20k. SPV-A surrenders £30k loss to SPV-B (eliminates SPV-B's £30k profit) + £10k loss to SPV-C (reduces SPV-C profit to £10k). Net effect: group CT bill reduced by £40k × 26.5% ≈ £10.6k.
- **Dividend conduit (per CTA 2009 Part 9A s.931A onwards):** SPV-D distributes £50k to HoldCo as intra-group dividend (exempt from CT at HoldCo per s.931A class exemption). HoldCo distributes £50k upward to founder as ordinary dividend at the individual's marginal dividend rate.
- **Operative insight:** group operation unlocks CT group relief + intra-group dividend exemption — material savings vs single-LtdCo holding all 5 properties. Forward-link to A8 this batch + existing group-relief + group-extraction pages.

### Example 4 — HMO LtdCo: when HMO-specific advantage compounds

- **Mrs Kapoor**'s 4-HMO portfolio: £60k gross rental; £30k mortgage interest; £18k operating costs; substantial common-parts plant (≈ £80k qualifying for CAA 2001 P&M allowances per A9 this batch).
- **LtdCo extraction:** £60k − £18k − £30k = £12k profit pre-allowances. Capital allowances £20k (year 1 AIA on plant) → loss £8k carried forward. CT bill £0 in year 1; carry-forward loss reduces year-2 CT.
- **Personal ownership comparison:** capital allowances also available but s.272A interest restriction still bites; effective tax saving on capital allowances ≈ £8k year 1.
- **HMO LtdCo wins on capital-allowance arithmetic** in addition to the Section 24 advantage. Forward-link to A9 this batch decision page + existing HMO mechanics + capital-allowance pages.

### Example 5 — When personal ownership wins (the honest counterexample)

- **Mr Verma** is a basic-rate taxpayer with one single-let BTL flat. £8k gross rental; £2k mortgage interest; £1.5k operating costs.
- **Personal ownership:** taxable = £8k − £1.5k = £6.5k. Tax at 20% basic rate = £1.3k. Less 20% credit on £2k interest = £400. Effective tax ≈ £900/year.
- **LtdCo full extraction:** £8k − £1.5k − £2k = £4.5k profit. CT 19% = £855. Dividend on £3.6k (after £500) at 10.75% = £387. Combined £1,242. **Worse than personal by £342/year**.
- **PLUS SDLT cost ≈ £4k (post-MDR-abolition single dwelling) + setup £5k = £9k one-off** that never amortises.
- **Operational conclusion:** for single-property basic-rate-taxpayer landlords, LtdCo route loses. The pillar must hold this honestly; not every landlord benefits from incorporation.

### Example 6 — FIC sub-category (succession-planning use)

- **Patel Family:** Mr + Mrs Patel hold 8-property portfolio in a Family Investment Company (FIC) structure. Voting shares (Class A) held 50/50 by Mr + Mrs Patel; growth shares (Class B + C) held by adult children. Annual dividends paid only on Class A.
- **CT side:** FIC pays CT on rental profits at 25% (above small-profits rate due to portfolio size).
- **IHT side (per §22.6 lock):** future growth in property value accrues primarily to the children's growth shares (Class B + C), removing future-growth value from Mr + Mrs Patel's IHT estates. The initial gift of Class B + C shares to the children is a PET (potentially exempt transfer) that becomes wholly exempt after 7 years.
- **CGT consideration:** s.165 holdover NOT available for transfers of investment-FIC shares per §21.5 (TCGA 1992 s.165 + Sch 7 limit holdover to trading-company shares); CGT crystallises at market value on the initial growth-share gift. Minority-discount valuation typically reduces the CGT base.
- **Operative reader insight:** FIC is the succession-planning sub-category of LtdCo; not a separate statutory entity. Forward-link to B5 sibling FIC pillar + §21.5 + §22.6 locks.

### Example 7 — Extraction routes mapped (the operative LtdCo-day-to-day question)

- **Singh Property Ltd** has £80k retained earnings + £40k annual profit + Mr Singh is a higher-rate-band individual.
- **Route 1 (Salary up to PA + ST):** £12.6k salary (covering Primary Threshold + Secondary Threshold + some Personal Allowance use). CT-deductible at LtdCo level. No employee NIC (under PT); employer NIC starts above ST. Cost to LtdCo: £12.6k salary + minimal employer NIC. Cost to Mr Singh personally: £0 income tax (within PA), £0 NIC (under PT). **First lever — cheap.**
- **Route 2 (Dividend at HRT):** £30k dividend distribution. CT already paid on the profit at 25% (or 19% if SP); dividend tax on £29.5k (after £500 allowance) at 35.75% = £10.5k. Plus the original CT cost. **Standard medium-cost lever.**
- **Route 3 (Employer pension contributions):** £20k employer pension contribution. CT-deductible at LtdCo (subject to wholly-and-exclusively test per CTA 2009 s.54). No personal tax (within annual allowance £60k / tapered for high earners). **Lever for long-horizon extraction with tax-deferral.**
- **Route 4 (DLA repayment, where credit balance exists from s.162 incorporation):** £15k DLA repayment per A19 M2-A-B1 + §21.1. Tax-free return of capital up to credit-balance exhaustion. **Cheapest lever while credit balance lasts.**
- **Route 5 (Rent to director for property used by LtdCo):** modest where applicable. Director rents home-office space to the LtdCo; CT-deductible at LtdCo; director declares rental income via ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (property business). Modest practical lever.
- **Operative insight:** extraction discipline = use the cheapest unfilled lever first. Forward-link to existing extraction-pillar + A4 M2-A-B1 corporate-tax-planning pillar + A19 M2-A-B1 DLA pillar + `salary-vs-dividends-property-spv-2026-27-marginal-rate-analysis`.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target — pillar coverage)

1. **Q: What is a UK limited company?**
   A: Companies Act 2006 s.1 defines "company" as a company formed and registered under CA 2006 (or its predecessor Acts). s.3 sets out three forms: limited by shares (operative for property), limited by guarantee (uncommon for property), and unlimited (exceptionally rare). s.7 + s.8 + s.9 govern incorporation procedure. From the date of incorporation per s.15 the company is a separate legal person — a body corporate distinct from its shareholders, capable of owning property, entering into contracts, suing and being sued in its own name. The separate-legal-personality principle dates from *Salomon v A Salomon & Co Ltd* [1897] AC 22 and is the foundational concept beneath all of UK company law.

2. **Q: What is limited liability, and why does it matter for landlords?**
   A: CA 2006 s.74 limits a member's liability to the amount unpaid on their shares. For a typical fully-paid £1-share company, member liability beyond the £1 paid-up share capital is NIL. The LtdCo's debts (mortgages; trade creditors; tenant deposit obligations; landlord liability under Housing Act 2004 + Renters' Rights Act 2025; ATED; CT) do NOT attach to the shareholder personally except (i) where a personal guarantee has been given (standard lender practice for BTL mortgages narrows the limited-liability shield in practice) or (ii) where statutory provisions pierce the corporate veil (insolvency / fraud / Companies Act enforcement). The principle is robustly defended in *Adams v Cape Industries plc* — courts will not lightly disregard separate corporate personality.

3. **Q: Why have landlords moved to limited companies since 2017?**
   A: ITTOIA 2005 s.272A (introduced by F(No.2)A 2015, phased in 2017–2020) restricted mortgage-interest deduction for individuals carrying on a property business, replacing full deduction with a 20% basic-rate tax credit. Higher-rate and additional-rate landlords pay tax on rental income calculated WITHOUT interest deduction. Limited companies are NOT within s.272A scope; they deduct interest in full under CTA 2009 Part 5 loan relationships. The result is a structural advantage for leveraged property portfolios held in companies vs personally — typically £5k–£15k+ saving per year on a mid-size BTL portfolio with substantial mortgage interest.

4. **Q: What is the corporation tax framework for property LtdCos?**
   A: CTA 2010 s.3 charges CT; s.18 sets the small-profits rate (19% on profits ≤ £50k single company); ss.18A–J introduce marginal relief on profits between £50k and £250k (effective rate ≈ 26.5% in the marginal band); profits above £250k are taxed at the 25% main rate. Associated-company gating per ss.18D–J divides the £50k/£250k thresholds by the number of associated companies. UK property business profits are taxed under CTA 2009 Part 4 (UK property businesses) at CT rate. Forward-link to A5 M2-A-B1 marginal-relief depth page.

5. **Q: What are the use-case forks for property limited companies?**
   A: Eight typical configurations: (i) **BTL LtdCo** — single-let residential portfolio; (ii) **HMO LtdCo** — multi-tenant licensed HMO (A9 this batch); (iii) **FHL LtdCo** — furnished holiday let (operative through transitional period to FHL abolition per FA 2025); (iv) **Development LtdCo** — property trading / flip / new-build (trading-vs-investment line per CTA 2010 ss.1124–1126); (v) **Commercial LtdCo** — non-residential or mixed-use portfolio; (vi) **Multi-SPV group** — HoldCo + multiple SPVs for asset protection + group relief (A8 this batch); (vii) **FIC** — Family Investment Company for succession-planning (B5 + §21.5 + §22.6); (viii) **Hybrid LLP corporate member** (A10 this batch — note mixed-membership anti-avoidance trap). The choice depends on portfolio size, profile, retention vs extraction intent, succession horizon, and reader configuration.

6. **Q: What is the Companies House compliance overlay post-ECCTA?**
   A: ECCTA 2023 Part 1 (per §11 + §11.A Wave 9 mini-lock) introduces several operational obligations for ALL UK companies: appropriate registered office (CA 2006 ss.86–88 as amended; no longer permissible to use PO Box-only addresses); registered email address (new CA 2006 s.88A+ post-ECCTA); confirmation statement (CA 2006 ss.853A–853L); PSC register (CA 2006 Part 21A); and **mandatory ID verification for directors + PSCs (in force from 18 November 2025 per §11.A lock; new appointments first, existing officeholders via 12-month transition window). RUN session re-verifies rollout state at the campaign page (`https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/`) at write time. ECCTA Part 2 LP reforms covered separately per F-1.

7. **Q: What are statutory accounts obligations for property LtdCos?**
   A: CA 2006 Part 15 (ss.380–474) governs accounts + reports. Small companies (most property LtdCos) file abridged or filleted accounts under FRS 105 (micro-entities) or FRS 102 (small companies regime). CA 2006 s.382 + s.383 + s.477 size-test thresholds determine regime + audit exemption. Most single-SPV property LtdCos qualify as micro-entities (turnover < £632k, balance sheet total < £316k, ≤ 10 employees; RUN session re-verifies thresholds at write time per §16.27 + §16.42 rate-by-reference discipline). Filing window: 9 months from end of accounting reference period per s.441; first accounts within 21 months from incorporation.

8. **Q: What director duties apply to landlord-directors?**
   A: CA 2006 ss.170–177 — general duties of directors: (i) act within powers (s.171); (ii) promote success of the company (s.172); (iii) exercise independent judgment (s.173); (iv) exercise reasonable care, skill and diligence (s.174); (v) avoid conflicts of interest (s.175); (vi) not accept benefits from third parties (s.176); (vii) declare interest in proposed transactions (s.177). For landlord audiences these matter most when self-appointing as sole director and when appointing family members. The s.172 duty includes consideration of long-term consequences, employees, supplier relationships, the impact of the company's operations on the community and environment, and reputation — frequently overlooked for small property LtdCos but the statutory duty applies regardless of company size.

9. **Q: What are the extraction routes from a property LtdCo, and how do they compare?**
   A: Five typical routes: (i) **Salary** up to Personal Allowance + Primary Threshold (cheap; CT-deductible; no employee NIC under PT); (ii) **Dividend** at 10.75%/35.75%/39.35% from 6 April 2026 per F-20 §21.4 (standard medium-cost lever); (iii) **DLA repayment** where a credit balance exists (tax-free return of capital up to credit-balance exhaustion per §21.1 + A19 M2-A-B1); (iv) **Employer pension contributions** (CT-deductible at LtdCo; tax-deferred at director level subject to annual allowance + tapering); (v) **Rent to director** for property used by the LtdCo (CT-deductible; director declares rental income). The extraction-discipline rule: use the cheapest unfilled lever first. Forward-link to existing extraction-pillar + A4 M2-A-B1 corporate-tax-planning lever map + A19 M2-A-B1 DLA pillar.

10. **Q: What are the cost-side downsides of LtdCo route?**
    A: Ten operative considerations. (i) **SDLT on incorporation** at residential rate stack with 3% HRAD surcharge per FA 2003 Sch 4ZA — materially worsened by F(No.2)A 2024 s.7 MDR abolition effective 1 June 2024 per §1.M. (ii) **CGT on transfer** unless TCGA 1992 s.162 incorporation relief or FA 2003 Sch 15 partnership-incorporation relief applies. (iii) **Double-layer tax on extraction** (CT + dividend at marginal rate). (iv) **Mortgage product narrowing** in LtdCo space + typical 50–75bps rate premium. (v) **Loss of CGT annual exempt amount** inside LtdCo (corporate gains taxed without the £3k AEA). (vi) **ATED admin** for dwellings > £500k (annual return even where relief applies). (vii) **Annual filing overhead** (CT600, statutory accounts, confirmation statement, PSC register, ID verification per ECCTA). (viii) **Loss of PRA / lettings relief** on disposal. (ix) **Refinancing cost** + lender consent process. (x) **Personal guarantees** on BTL mortgages typically required by lenders, narrowing the limited-liability shield. The pillar must hold these honestly.

11. **Q: When is the LtdCo route the right answer?**
    A: Typical break-even pattern: high LTV (≥ 50%) + higher-rate or additional-rate individual + retention intent + portfolio size 3+ properties + retention horizon 5+ years → favours LtdCo. The Section 24 advantage scales with interest cost; the CT-rate advantage scales with retention horizon; the incorporation costs (SDLT post-MDR-abolition; CGT subject to s.162; setup costs) amortise over the retention period. For typical mid-size landlord configurations the payback is 3–10 years. For long-horizon family wealth structures with succession-planning intent (FIC overlay), payback is essentially never relevant — the structure is justified on IHT-planning grounds independent of income-tax math.

12. **Q: When is the LtdCo route the WRONG answer?**
    A: Several typical configurations where personal ownership wins. (i) Single low-yield property + low LTV (≤ 30%) + basic-rate individual + extraction intent — payback may never be reached. (ii) Imminent disposal intent (selling within 2–3 years) — CGT on transfer + CGT on disposal of company shares + loss of PRA / lettings relief makes the structure expensive. (iii) Highly leveraged but high-income individual where ANY net rental profit pushes them deep into additional-rate territory + extraction at 39.35% dividend rate ≈ same effective rate as personal — neutral, with incorporation costs as the swing. (iv) Reader configuration where the partnership / LLP / general-partnership alternatives suit better (forward-link to A7 + A10 + A11 this batch). The pillar holds the honest decision framework; not every landlord benefits from incorporation.

13. **Q: What are the alternatives to LtdCo?**
    A: Four operative alternatives within the entity-choice decision tree. (i) **Sole trader** (no separate entity; default for single landlords; ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 property business + Section 24 restriction; simplest compliance). (ii) **General partnership** under PA 1890 (per A7 this batch — partnership taxonomy + four-tests definition); tax-transparent per ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 + TCGA 1992 s.59; unlocks FA 2003 Sch 15 SDLT incorporation relief. (iii) **Limited Liability Partnership** under LLPA 2000 (per A11 + A10 this batch); limited liability + tax-transparent default + salaried-member regime exception + mixed-membership regime for hybrid structures. (iv) **Limited Partnership** under LPA 1907 (per A3 M2-A-B1 — post-ECCTA Part 2 compliance reforms). Plus **trust** structures (covered elsewhere on the site). The entity-choice question is upstream of the LtdCo decision; this pillar surfaces the alternatives but does not duplicate the depth of the partnership / LLP siblings.

14. **Q: How does this pillar relate to the existing BTL-complete-guide on the site?**
    A: This pillar is the **broadest LtdCo entry point** — covering all property-LtdCo use cases (BTL + HMO + FHL + development + commercial + mixed-use + multi-SPV group + FIC). The existing `buy-to-let-limited-company-complete-guide-uk` page is the **BTL-specific specialist** — one use-case among the many this pillar covers. Reading sequence: this pillar to settle the use-case fork question + entity-choice question; then the specialist page for the chosen fork's depth. The pages cross-link, with the pillar's primary architecture being the cross-link grid to all of the specialists.
