# Essential Guidelines for Running Payroll Effectively: A Property-Business Operational Guide

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (PAYE framework — the rule-set that operational guidelines sit on top of): **Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 (ITEPA 2003) ss.682-712** + **The Income Tax (PAYE) Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/2682)**. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/1/part/11 and https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/contents on 2026-05-26. ITEPA 2003 s.682 is the PAYE income definition; SI 2003/2682 is the operational rule-set governing FPS, EPS, NIC calculation, tax-code application, statutory payment offsets, and student-loan-deduction mechanics.
- Primary (RTI — Real Time Information, the operational filing cadence that defines the payroll calendar): **SI 2003/2682 reg 67B (Real Time Information)**, in force since 6 April 2013. Verbatim core obligation: an FPS (Full Payment Submission) must be sent **on or before the day each payment is made to an employee**; an EPS (Employer Payment Summary) is sent monthly to claim statutory recoveries, the Employment Allowance, CIS suffered deductions, or to declare a "no payment to employees" month.
- Primary (NIC framework — Class 1 primary and secondary contributions): **Social Security Contributions and Benefits Act 1992 (SSCBA 1992) ss.6-15** + **The Social Security (Contributions) Regulations 2001 (SI 2001/1004)**. Class 1 secondary (employer) NIC threshold reduced and rate increased per Autumn Budget 2024 measures — secondary threshold cut from £9,100 to £5,000 from 6 April 2025; rate raised from 13.8% to 15% from same date. Re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk rates page.
- Primary (Employment Allowance — the small-employer relief most property businesses use): **National Insurance Contributions Act 2014 s.1** + **NICA 2014 s.2 connected-companies rule**. £10,500 from 6 April 2025 (raised from £5,000). The £100,000 prior-year secondary-NIC cap was abolished from 6 April 2025; Employment Allowance now available to all employers of any size, with the de-minimis state aid and connected-companies single-claim rules still applying. **Connected companies rule (NICA 2014 s.2):** only one claim across a group of connected companies — most property LtdCo groups with multiple SPVs must elect a single claiming entity. HP §3 (NIC reform) headline; re-verify at Stage 2.
- Primary (statutory payments — the in-payroll statutory absorption mechanism):
  - **SSCBA 1992 Part XII (SSP)** — Statutory Sick Pay. Note: small-employer SSP recovery scheme was abolished from 6 April 2014 by the Statutory Sick Pay (General) Regulations 2014; SSP is now an employer-bearing cost with no HMRC recovery, except where an employee's SSP is treated as a Coronavirus-Covid-19-period claim (historic, now closed).
  - **SSCBA 1992 ss.164-171** + Statutory Maternity Pay (General) Regulations 1986 — Statutory Maternity Pay. SMP recovery via the Compensation rate (92% of SMP for small employers under the Small Employers' Relief threshold; 103% — i.e. 100% plus 3% compensation — for those qualifying). Threshold: total Class 1 NICs in the qualifying period £45,000 or less. SI 2003/2682 reg 75-79 covers the recovery mechanic.
  - Equivalent statutes for Statutory Paternity Pay (SPP), Statutory Adoption Pay (SAP), Statutory Shared Parental Pay (ShPP), and Statutory Parental Bereavement Pay (SPBP) — all with similar small-employer recovery routes.
- Primary (pension auto-enrolment — the payroll function's parallel duty):
  - **Pensions Act 2008 Part 1** + **Automatic Enrolment Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/772)**. Auto-enrolment is the employer's separate legal duty, but operationally embedded in payroll. Re-declaration of compliance due every 3 years from the duty-start date. Minimum total contribution 8% (3% employer + 5% employee, of which 1% is tax relief at source), on qualifying earnings between £6,240 and £50,270 (2024/25 figures; re-verify Stage 2).
- Primary (record-keeping for payroll): **TMA 1970 s.12B** + **SI 2003/2682 reg 97** + **VATA 1994 Sch 11 para 6** (where VAT-registered). Payroll records must be retained for **3 years after the end of the tax year they relate to** under SI 2003/2682 reg 97 (NOT 6 — common error in commentary; verify at Stage 2 against current HMRC PAYE Manual PAYE5000+).
- Primary (the off-payroll working rules — the IR35 overlay that catches property-LtdCo contractor arrangements): **ITEPA 2003 Chapter 10 Part 2 ss.61M-61X** (IR35 / off-payroll working, post-April-2021 reform). Where a property business engages an intermediary (PSC) for a worker who would otherwise be treated as an employee, the engager (if a medium/large business) is responsible for determining status and operating PAYE on the deemed employment payment. Most landlord LtdCos are small (s.382 Companies Act 2006 test) and therefore the OLD intermediaries rules apply (worker's PSC determines status), not the engager-side post-April-2021 rules. Stage 2 should re-anchor this on the small-business carve-out for the lettings-agency vs single-landlord-PSC fact pattern.
- House position references: **§35 (Payroll mechanics for property-business employers — MW2 extension, locked 2026-05-27)** — this is the primary anchor. §35 covers PAYE / NIC / Apprenticeship Levy / auto-enrolment as employer duties, the Employment Allowance single-director bar (LtdCo BTL with sole director-shareholder doesn't qualify), the Apprenticeship Levy connected-employer aggregation under FA 2016 s.101, BIK touchpoints (live-in accommodation per ITEPA 2003 ss.97-113), the employment-vs-self-employment Ready Mixed Concrete line for on-call refurb labour, and the corporation-tax interaction (salary + employer NIC + AL deductible per CTA 2009 Part 3). §3 (MTD / NIC reform headline); §19 (full Wave 3 MTD operational lock); §28 (off-payroll working / IR35 cluster, Wave 7 partial); §29 (VAT for property — residential lettings exempt). F-105 candidate flag from Stage 1 seed CLOSED by addition of §35.

Examples of guidelines (Stage 2 finalises the list):
1. **Lock the payroll calendar to your pay date, working backwards from the FPS deadline.** (The FPS-on-or-before rule under SI 2003/2682 reg 67B is the operational discipline anchor; the page walks through how a 28-day-pay-cycle business plans backwards from the 28th.)
2. **Validate tax codes monthly, not just at year-end.** (P9X notice + dynamic tax code change FPS submissions are the operational discipline; pages on this typically miss the mid-year code-change reconciliation step.)
3. **Process statutory payments through payroll, not as separate ex-gratia.** (SSP/SMP/SPP must be in the FPS; out-of-payroll informal payments create both a NIC issue and a recovery-claim issue.)
4. **Reconcile pension contributions to the payroll FPS pension figures monthly.** (Auto-enrolment provider statements vs payroll FPS commonly drift by £5-15 per employee per month; the reconciliation discipline catches it before the 3-yearly re-declaration.)
5. **Run a "FPS-vs-bank-payment" reconciliation alongside every pay-run.** (The reconciliation that catches a £100 typo before HMRC sees the FPS but the bank-CSV shows the higher figure.)
6. **Document a director-only payroll year-plan once and re-use it.** (Most property-LtdCo directors pay themselves to the £12,570 personal allowance threshold for NIC efficiency; the annual figure is known on day 1 of the tax year; locking the year-plan reduces month-on-month reconciliation work.)
7. **Separate the connected-company Employment Allowance election decision before April 6.** (The £10,500 allowance is a per-group claim; multi-SPV property landlords with several LtdCos must elect a single claiming entity by the start of the year — the operational discipline page makes this an annual-review item.)
8. **Calendar the Class 1A NIC + P11D deadlines explicitly in the payroll system.** (P11D 6 July; Class 1A 19 July (postal) or 22 July (electronic); the small-employer pattern is to forget these because they sit outside the monthly FPS rhythm.)
9. **Keep a paper-or-PDF trail for every status decision on a contractor engagement.** (IR35/off-payroll-working status determinations need documented evidence; the operational discipline is to have a status-decision template that runs at engagement start, with the CEST output stored.)
10. **Build a year-end-procedures runbook and exercise it before March 31.** (Final FPS with year-end indicator + P60 issue by 31 May + tax-code uplift for new tax year + Employment Allowance re-election + pension contribution rate review.)

The anti-templating axis vs competitor pages: most "essential payroll tips" content is HR-generic — separate bank account, automate everything, use cloud software. The Property Tax Partners angle is property-specific operational discipline — director-only payroll for NIC efficiency, the connected-company Employment Allowance election, IR35 status-decision discipline for property managers and lettings agents (the highest-frequency PSC engagement pattern in the property orbit), and the Class 1A NIC discipline for any company car or fuel benefit (because the property-LtdCo using a company-funded vehicle for portfolio inspections is the classic missed Class 1A pattern).

**Worked example: connected-company Employment Allowance for a multi-SPV property landlord — the Holloway-family case**

Persona: Holloway-family-property-group. Six BTL SPVs (PropCo 1-6) under common HoldCo control; each SPV holds 4-8 BTL flats. Three SPVs employ one part-time portfolio manager each (paid £22,000/year salary); two SPVs employ a cleaner each (£12,000/year, below secondary threshold so no employer NIC); one SPV is dormant (no employees, no payroll).

EA election analysis (operative 2026/27):
- The six SPVs are "connected" within NICA 2014 s.2 (common control via HoldCo). Per s.2, the £10,500 EA can only be claimed by ONE entity across the group per tax year.
- **§35.2(a) single-director bar:** SPV-1, SPV-2 and SPV-3 each have a single director (Mr Holloway). Their secondary NIC arises only on the £22,000 portfolio-manager salary, NOT on director-only payroll. Where the SPV's only employee IS the sole director-shareholder, EA is barred (NICA 2014 Sch 1 para 1(2)). **Where the SPV employs a non-director (portfolio manager), the single-director bar does NOT apply** — EA is available for that SPV.
- Optimum claim: elect a SPV with the highest secondary NIC liability. Portfolio-manager salary £22,000; secondary NIC liability = (£22,000 - £5,000) × 15% = £2,550 per SPV. With three such SPVs, group total secondary NIC = £7,650.
- The £10,500 EA fully absorbs the group's secondary NIC. Operationally, allocate the £10,500 claim to one nominated SPV. The other two SPVs pay the full secondary NIC in cash terms; the receiving SPV deducts the £10,500 from its own NIC liability (because EA isn't an inter-company refund; it offsets the claimant SPV's own NIC bill only).
- Group-wide effect: group has £7,650 NIC liability; claimant SPV uses £2,550 of its £10,500 allowance (its own NIC bill) — the unused £7,950 of the allowance is wasted because EA is not transferable between connected companies.

**Operational discipline takeaway:** for connected-company groups, the EA election decision is not "pick the best SPV"; it is "pick the SPV with the highest secondary NIC bill and accept that the rest of the group bears their own secondary NIC in cash". The fix is to consolidate payroll into a single employing SPV (group-services company) where commercially sensible; this single SPV employs all staff, pays the full group secondary NIC, and claims the £10,500 against that one consolidated bill.

**Worked example: director-only payroll discipline (single-director-shareholder LtdCo BTL)**

Persona: Holloway-family-single-SPV. Sole-director LtdCo BTL; one director (Mr Holloway), no other employees.

Director-only payroll 2026/27 setup:

## FAQ draft (10-12 entries, Stage 2 populated; RUN sub-agent polishes prose)

1. **What is the FPS-on-or-before-payment rule, and how does it shape the payroll calendar?**
   Under SI 2003/2682 reg 67B, the Full Payment Submission (FPS) must be sent to HMRC on or before the day each payment is made to an employee. This drives the operational discipline: work backwards from your pay-date. If your business pays monthly on the 28th, the FPS must clear HMRC's gateway by close of business on the 28th. The practical rule of thumb: lock your payroll-run two working days ahead of pay-date so an HMRC gateway issue (rare but possible) doesn't tip you into late-FPS territory.

2. **Why does my single-director property LtdCo not qualify for the Employment Allowance?**
   National Insurance Contributions Act 2014 Schedule 1 paragraph 1(2) excludes companies whose only employed earner is the sole director (the "single-director bar"). The Employment Allowance was tightened with this bar in 2016 specifically to stop one-person LtdCos using EA. The carve-out: where your SPV employs a non-director (a portfolio manager, a cleaner, anyone who is not the sole director-shareholder), the single-director bar lifts and you can claim. Multi-SPV property groups must also navigate the NICA 2014 s.2 connected-companies single-claim rule.

3. **How does the £10,500 Employment Allowance work for connected-company property groups?**
   NICA 2014 s.2 restricts the EA to one entity across a connected-company group per tax year. "Connected" follows the Corporation Tax Act 2010 s.450+ control test. For a multi-SPV portfolio under common HoldCo control, only one SPV can claim the £10,500. The election is made on the EPS for the chosen SPV (no separate form). The operational discipline: each March, the group's accountant or director should explicitly review and allocate the EA to the SPV with the highest projected secondary NIC liability. The £100,000 prior-year secondary-NIC cap was abolished from 6 April 2025; EA is now available to employers of any size, but the connected-company single-claim rule still applies.

4. **What is the director-only payroll "sweet spot" for a property LtdCo with no other employees?**
   The pattern is to pay a £12,570 annual salary (matching the personal allowance — no income tax) with the salary funded as a corporation-tax-deductible expense against rental profits. The salary is above the Lower Earnings Limit (£6,396) so a state-pension qualifying year accrues. Employee NIC kicks in at the primary threshold £12,570, so an exactly-aligned salary triggers no employee NIC. Employer NIC applies above the secondary threshold £5,000 at 15% — payable in cash (no EA available for single-director SPVs). Salary plus dividends remains the standard director-extraction pattern; the £12,570 salary is the floor.

5. **Why are statutory payments (SSP, SMP, SPP, SAP) routed through payroll rather than as separate ex-gratia?**
   Statutory payments must be processed through the FPS under SI 2003/2682. Routing them outside the FPS as informal ex-gratia payments creates two problems: (i) the payments are still earnings under ITEPA 2003 — they attract income tax and NIC, which the ex-gratia route fails to deduct; (ii) the employer cannot claim the statutory recovery (the 92% / 103% SMP / SAP / SPP / ShPP / SPBP recovery for small employers via the EPS) without processing through payroll. The operational discipline: every statutory absence triggers a payroll-side calculation, even if the absence is short.

6. **What are the auto-enrolment compliance touchpoints — and what does the 3-yearly re-declaration mean?**
   Under Pensions Act 2008 + SI 2010/772, every employer must (i) auto-enrol qualifying jobholders (aged 22-State Pension age, earning above £10,000 per year); (ii) deduct minimum 8% of qualifying earnings (£6,240-£50,270 band) — 3% employer + 5% employee — and remit to the pension scheme; (iii) submit a "Declaration of Compliance" to The Pensions Regulator at first staging-date; (iv) **re-declare compliance every 3 years** including automatic re-enrolment of employees who previously opted out. Missing the re-declaration is the most common auto-enrolment penalty trigger for small employers.

7. **What payroll records must I retain, and for how long?**
   SI 2003/2682 reg 97 requires PAYE records to be kept for **3 years** after the end of the tax year they relate to (verified verbatim against legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-27 — NOT 6 years, which is a common-commentary error). Some HMRC-investigation outcomes extend retention to 6 years from investigation close; certain VAT-side records run for 6 years; but the standard PAYE retention is 3 years. For property employers also subject to MTD for ITSA (where the director is a sole-trader landlord on the personal side), the personal-side records run for 5 years under TMA 1970 s.12B.

8. **How does IR35 / off-payroll working apply to property businesses engaging contractors?**
   ITEPA 2003 Chapter 10 Part 2 ss.61M-61X is the post-April-2021 reform that shifted status-determination obligations from the worker's PSC to the engager — but only where the engager is a medium or large business. Most landlord LtdCos fall within the Companies Act 2006 s.382 "small company" definition (turnover ≤ £10.2m, balance sheet ≤ £5.1m, ≤ 50 employees in two of three tests). For small-business engagers, the OLD pre-April-2021 intermediaries rules apply — the worker's PSC determines status. The operational discipline regardless: document the status decision at engagement start, store the CEST output or equivalent, and revisit at contract renewal.

9. **What are the Class 1A NIC + P11D deadlines I keep forgetting?**
   The P11D return (for taxable benefits-in-kind paid to employees during the tax year) is due to HMRC by 6 July following tax year end. Class 1A employer NIC on those benefits (currently 15% from 6 April 2025, aligned with secondary rate) is payable by 19 July (postal) or 22 July (electronic). For property businesses: the most common missed Class 1A items are (i) company-funded vehicles used for portfolio inspections, (ii) employer-paid private medical insurance, (iii) employer-provided accommodation for on-site staff (ITEPA 2003 ss.97-113, with the four customary statutory exemptions for caretakers / agricultural workers / security personnel — but residential property managers do NOT generally fit those exemptions unless their job genuinely requires occupation for proper performance).

10. **What's the year-end procedures runbook I should follow?**
    By 5 April: final FPS for the tax year with the year-end indicator marked. By 31 May: P60 issued to every employee employed at year-end. By 6 July: P11D submitted for all benefit-in-kind recipients. By 19 July (or 22 July electronic): Class 1A NIC paid. Also at year-start (6 April onwards): apply P9X tax-code uplift for the new tax year; re-elect Employment Allowance via EPS; review pension contribution rates for any threshold changes; refresh employee tax-code notices (P6 / P9 received during March / early April). The discipline: maintain a payroll runbook checklist and exercise it before the 31 March cut-off.

11. **What's the difference between this guide and your other payroll pages?**
    Three angles: (i) Our `a-complete-guide-to-uk-payroll-taxes-and-deductions-for-employers` walks WHAT taxes and deductions apply — the statute-led explainer. (ii) Our `ensuring-compliance-with-uk-payroll-reporting-regulations` walks WHAT you must file and by when — the regulator-led compliance angle. (iii) This page walks HOW to run payroll well in practice — the operational discipline. Most property-business operators need all three angles; read the cluster sequentially for the full picture.

12. **Does running my own payroll save money, or should I outsource to a bureau?**
    For property-LtdCos with a single director and no other staff, in-house payroll software (FreeAgent, Xero, Sage, basic-RTI tools) at £10-30/month is generally cheaper than a bureau. For multi-SPV property groups with 5+ employees including statutory-payment recipients and IR35-determined PSCs, a payroll bureau at £8-15/employee/month often pays for itself in error-reduction and HMRC-correspondence handling. The operational economics flip somewhere around 8-12 employees; below that, in-house is feasible if the operator has time and discipline.
