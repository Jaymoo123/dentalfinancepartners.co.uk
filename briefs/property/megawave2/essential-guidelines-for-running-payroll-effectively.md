# Essential Guidelines for Running Payroll Effectively: A Property-Business Operational Guide

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (PAYE framework — the rule-set that operational guidelines sit on top of): **Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 (ITEPA 2003) ss.682-712** + **The Income Tax (PAYE) Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/2682)**. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/1/part/11 and https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/contents on 2026-05-26. ITEPA 2003 s.682 is the PAYE income definition; SI 2003/2682 is the operational rule-set governing FPS, EPS, NIC calculation, tax-code application, statutory payment offsets, and student-loan-deduction mechanics.
- Primary (RTI — Real Time Information, the operational filing cadence that defines the payroll calendar): **SI 2003/2682 reg 67B (Real Time Information)**, in force since 6 April 2013. Verbatim core obligation: an FPS (Full Payment Submission) must be sent **on or before the day each payment is made to an employee**; an EPS (Employer Payment Summary) is sent monthly to claim statutory recoveries, the Employment Allowance, CIS suffered deductions, or to declare a "no payment to employees" month.
- Primary (NIC framework — Class 1 primary and secondary contributions): **Social Security Contributions and Benefits Act 1992 (SSCBA 1992) ss.6-15** + **The Social Security (Contributions) Regulations 2001 (SI 2001/1004)**. Class 1 secondary (employer) NIC threshold reduced and rate increased per Autumn Budget 2024 measures — secondary threshold cut from £9,100 to £5,000 from 6 April 2025; rate raised from 13.8% to 15% from same date. Re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk rates page.
- Primary (Employment Allowance — the small-employer relief most property businesses use): **National Insurance Contributions Act 2014 s.1** + **NICA 2014 s.2 connected-companies rule**. £10,500 from 6 April 2025 (raised from £5,000). The £100,000 prior-year secondary-NIC cap was abolished from 6 April 2025; Employment Allowance now available to all employers of any size, with the de-minimis state aid and connected-companies single-claim rules still applying. **Connected companies rule (NICA 2014 s.2):** only one claim across a group of connected companies — most property LtdCo groups with multiple SPVs must elect a single claiming entity. HP §3 (NIC reform) headline; re-verify at Stage 2.
- Primary (statutory payments — the in-payroll statutory absorption mechanism):
  - **SSCBA 1992 Part XII (SSP)** — Statutory Sick Pay. Note: small-employer SSP recovery scheme was abolished from 6 April 2014 by the Statutory Sick Pay (General) Regulations 2014; SSP is now an employer-bearing cost with no HMRC recovery, except where an employee's SSP is treated as a Coronavirus-Covid-19-period claim (historic, now closed).
  - **SSCBA 1992 ss.164-171** + Statutory Maternity Pay (General) Regulations 1986 — Statutory Maternity Pay. SMP recovery via the Compensation rate (92% of SMP for small employers under the Small Employers' Relief threshold; 103% — i.e. 100% plus 3% compensation — for those qualifying). Threshold: total Class 1 NICs in the qualifying period £45,000 or less. SI 2003/2682 reg 75-79 covers the recovery mechanic.
  - Equivalent statutes for Statutory Paternity Pay (SPP), Statutory Adoption Pay (SAP), Statutory Shared Parental Pay (ShPP), and Statutory Parental Bereavement Pay (SPBP) — all with similar small-employer recovery routes.
- Primary (pension auto-enrolment — the payroll function's parallel duty):
  - **Pensions Act 2008 Part 1** + **Automatic Enrolment Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/772)**. Auto-enrolment is the employer's separate legal duty, but operationally embedded in payroll. Re-declaration of compliance due every 3 years from the duty-start date. Minimum total contribution 8% (3% employer + 5% employee, of which 1% is tax relief at source), on qualifying earnings between £6,240 and £50,270 (2024/25 figures; re-verify Stage 2).
- Primary (record-keeping for payroll): **TMA 1970 s.12B** + **SI 2003/2682 reg 97** + **VATA 1994 Sch 11 para 6** (where VAT-registered). Payroll records must be retained for **3 years after the end of the tax year they relate to** under SI 2003/2682 reg 97 (NOT 6 — common error in commentary; verify at Stage 2 against current HMRC PAYE Manual PAYE5000+).
- Primary (the off-payroll working rules — the IR35 overlay that catches property-LtdCo contractor arrangements): **ITEPA 2003 Chapter 10 Part 2 ss.61M-61X** (IR35 / off-payroll working, post-April-2021 reform). Where a property business engages an intermediary (PSC) for a worker who would otherwise be treated as an employee, the engager (if a medium/large business) is responsible for determining status and operating PAYE on the deemed employment payment. Most landlord LtdCos are small (s.382 Companies Act 2006 test) and therefore the OLD intermediaries rules apply (worker's PSC determines status), not the engager-side post-April-2021 rules. Stage 2 should re-anchor this on the small-business carve-out for the lettings-agency vs single-landlord-PSC fact pattern.
- House position references: §3 (NIC + Employment Allowance reform — 2026-05-23 lock with the £5,000 secondary threshold and 15% rate); §19 (MTD for ITSA — interaction with payroll-via-LtdCo vs sole-trader bookkeeping; HP §19 + §19.7 day-trigger lock 15/30/31 days for late-payment); §28 (off-payroll working / IR35 cluster — partially locked via Wave 7); §29 (VAT for property — note that payroll services for a landlord do NOT count toward VAT registration threshold for the landlord because residential lettings are exempt supplies). No dedicated payroll HP section exists; this seed proposes a candidate § for Stage 1b consideration where the M2-C-B1 + B2 payroll trio surfaces sufficient new architecture (provisional flag F-105 — see work log).

The anti-templating axis vs the M2-C-B1 trio: this page is **process-led** not statute-led. It presents 8-10 operational guidelines (the payroll-effectiveness checklist), each backed by the statutory hook from above but framed in operational terms. Examples of guidelines (Stage 2 finalises the list):
1. **Lock the payroll calendar to your pay date, working backwards from the FPS deadline.** (The FPS-on-or-before rule under SI 2003/2682 reg 67B is the operational discipline anchor; the page walks through how a 28-day-pay-cycle business plans backwards from the 28th.)
2. **Validate tax codes monthly, not just at year-end.** (P9X notice + dynamic tax code change FPS submissions are the operational discipline; pages on this typically miss the mid-year code-change reconciliation step.)
3. **Process statutory payments through payroll, not as separate ex-gratia.** (SSP/SMP/SPP must be in the FPS; out-of-payroll informal payments create both a NIC issue and a recovery-claim issue.)
4. **Reconcile pension contributions to the payroll FPS pension figures monthly.** (Auto-enrolment provider statements vs payroll FPS commonly drift by £5-15 per employee per month; the reconciliation discipline catches it before the 3-yearly re-declaration.)
5. **Run a "FPS-vs-bank-payment" reconciliation alongside every pay-run.** (The reconciliation that catches a £100 typo before HMRC sees the FPS but the bank-CSV shows the higher figure.)
6. **Document a director-only payroll year-plan once and re-use it.** (Most property-LtdCo directors pay themselves to the £12,570 personal allowance threshold for NIC efficiency; the annual figure is known on day 1 of the tax year; locking the year-plan reduces month-on-month reconciliation work.)
7. **Separate the connected-company Employment Allowance election decision before April 6.** (The £10,500 allowance is a per-group claim; multi-SPV property landlords with several LtdCos must elect a single claiming entity by the start of the year — the operational discipline page makes this an annual-review item.)
8. **Calendar the Class 1A NIC + P11D deadlines explicitly in the payroll system.** (P11D 6 July; Class 1A 19 July (postal) or 22 July (electronic); the small-employer pattern is to forget these because they sit outside the monthly FPS rhythm.)
9. **Keep a paper-or-PDF trail for every status decision on a contractor engagement.** (IR35/off-payroll-working status determinations need documented evidence; the operational discipline is to have a status-decision template that runs at engagement start, with the CEST output stored.)
10. **Build a year-end-procedures runbook and exercise it before March 31.** (Final FPS with year-end indicator + P60 issue by 31 May + tax-code uplift for new tax year + Employment Allowance re-election + pension contribution rate review.)

The anti-templating axis vs competitor pages: most "essential payroll tips" content is HR-generic — separate bank account, automate everything, use cloud software. The Property Tax Partners angle is property-specific operational discipline — director-only payroll for NIC efficiency, the connected-company Employment Allowance election, IR35 status-decision discipline for property managers and lettings agents (the highest-frequency PSC engagement pattern in the property orbit), and the Class 1A NIC discipline for any company car or fuel benefit (because the property-LtdCo using a company-funded vehicle for portfolio inspections is the classic missed Class 1A pattern).
