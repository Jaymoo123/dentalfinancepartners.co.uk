# Ensuring Compliance with UK Payroll Reporting Regulations for Property-Business Employers

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the RTI reporting regime): **The Income Tax (Pay As You Earn) Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/2682), Part 4 regulations 67B-97M.** Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/contents on 2026-05-26.
  - **Reg 67B "Real time returns of information about relevant payments"** — the operative Full Payment Submission (FPS) requirement. Each FPS must reach HMRC **on or before** the time the employer makes the relevant payment to the employee.
  - **Reg 67D** — alternative quarterly return mechanism for very-small-employer cases (limited scope; most landlord LtdCos do NOT qualify).
  - **Reg 67E "Inaccuracies in returns"** — verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/regulation/67E on 2026-05-26: "Where an employer becomes aware of an inaccuracy in a return submitted under regulation 67B or 67D, the employer must provide the correct information in the next return for the tax year in question". Exception: if not corrected before **20 April** following the tax-year end, the employer must make a separate amendment return under reg 67E.
  - **Reg 67F** — additional information returns where required.
  - **Reg 67G "Payments to and recoveries from HMRC: payments"** — payment-cycle requirement: 22nd of the following month for electronic payment, 19th for cheque.
  - **Reg 67H + 67I** — Employer Payment Summary (EPS) mechanic, used to report nil-payment months and to recover statutory payments (statutory sick pay, statutory maternity pay) and the Employment Allowance offset.
  - **Reg 73** — annual return obligation (legacy P35 / P14 framework, mostly subsumed by RTI but the regulations retain historical residual references for non-RTI corner-cases).
  - **Reg 90** — record-keeping requirements (3 years from the end of the tax year, per HMRC standard).
- Primary (the late-filing penalty stack): **Finance Act 2009 Schedule 55** — penalties for failure to make returns; applied to the RTI regime via **Income Tax (PAYE) (Amendment No. 2) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/521)** and subsequent commencement orders. Penalty bands:
  - **Late FPS penalty:** monthly £100-£400 depending on employer size (1-9 employees: £100; 10-49: £200; 50-249: £300; 250+: £400). HMRC typically allows one "free" late filing per tax year without penalty as concession; second-onwards attract penalty.
  - **Late payment penalty (Sch 56):** 1% / 2% / 3% / 4% / 5% bands depending on the number of late months in the tax year.
  - **Daily penalties** apply after 3 months of continuing failure.
- Primary (the on-or-before exception): **HMRC guidance under reg 67B(3)** — small-employer (under-9-employees) end-of-month-tolerance has been formally withdrawn, but the practical "reasonable excuse" defence under Sch 55 para 23 remains available for genuinely impossible-to-meet-on-day cases (system failure, unforeseen absence of the payroll-runner).
- Primary (the National Insurance regulations parallel): **The Social Security (Contributions) Regulations 2001 (SI 2001/1004)** — parallel regulations for the NIC side of RTI reporting; FPS includes both PAYE income tax and NIC data.
- Primary (corporation-tax interaction for landlord LtdCos): **CTA 2009 Part 3** — salary + employer NIC + Apprenticeship Levy are deductible CT-expenses against company profits in the accounting period in which they are incurred / paid (subject to the 9-month-after-year-end paid-by deadline under CTA 2009 s.1289 for accruals).
- Supporting (auto-enrolment compliance overlay):
  - **Pensions Act 2008 + Occupational and Personal Pension Schemes (Automatic Enrolment) Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/772)** — employer auto-enrolment duties triggered on every payday for jobholder assessment.
  - **Pensions Regulator (TPR)** — enforcement body; FPS data feeds TPR's compliance monitoring.
- Supporting (Construction Industry Scheme overlay where landlord LtdCo engages construction sub-contractors for refurb work — RIGHT-EDGE of payroll reporting):
  - **CIS regulations under FA 2004 ss.57-77 + SI 2005/2045** — separate monthly CIS300 return alongside the PAYE FPS where construction labour engaged. Cross-reference to MW2 B4 `beginners-guide-to-cis-verification-in-the-uk`.
- House position references: same as C1 — payroll is NOT in scope of current locked HPs. F-100 (raised by C1) proposes a new §33 "Payroll mechanics for property-business employers" lock that would cover this page's statutes too. Stage 1b conductor decision pending.

**Worked example: 3-employee landlord LtdCo monthly cycle**

Persona: Singh-LtdCo. Three employees — director (Mr Singh, monthly salary at Personal Allowance), in-house bookkeeper (£30k pa, paid on 28th of month), part-time property manager (£18k pa, paid on 28th of month).

Monthly cycle for tax month 6 May - 5 June 2026:
- 28 May 2026: payroll runs. FPS submitted same day, on or before payday per reg 67B.
- 5 June 2026: tax month closes.
- 19 June 2026 (if any nil-pay flag, statutory payment recovery, or EA offset): EPS submitted.
- 22 June 2026: payment to HMRC due (electronic). Combined PAYE + employee NIC + employer NIC + any apprenticeship levy.

Failure scenarios:
- FPS late by 1 day (29 May): penalty £100 (1-9 employee band) — but HMRC concession waives the first late filing of the tax year, so effective penalty £0 if first slip.
- FPS late by 31 days continuous (no submission until 28 June): £100 monthly penalty × 1 = £100 (or £0 if first slip + concession).
- FPS late for full quarter (no submission 28 May, 28 June, 28 July): £100 × 3 + start of daily penalties after 3 months = material exposure.
- Payment late by 1 day (23 June electronic): potential Sch 56 1% penalty on £4-5k monthly liability ≈ £40-50.

**EPS Employment Allowance offset mechanic:**

For LtdCos qualifying for the £10,500 EA, the offset is claimed monthly via EPS. Singh-LtdCo monthly employer NIC liability ≈ £400-500 (approx 15% on £25k Singh+£13k bookkeeper+£13k manager excess over secondary threshold, divided by 12). Each monthly EPS claims the EA offset, drawing down from the £10,500 annual allowance until exhausted (typically around month 8-9 of the tax year).

## FAQ draft (10-12 entries, Stage 2 populated; RUN sub-agent polishes prose)

1. **What is the FPS on-or-before-payday rule?**
   SI 2003/2682 reg 67B requires that the Full Payment Submission reaches HMRC on or before the time the employer makes the relevant payment to the employee. The rule is verbatim: "on or before making a relevant payment to an employee, a Real Time Information employer must deliver to HMRC the information specified in Schedule A1". A payment made at 5pm on the 28th must have its FPS lodged by 5pm on the 28th, not the next morning.

2. **How does HMRC define "the time of payment" for FPS purposes?**
   The time of payment is when the funds are made available to the employee. For BACS transfers, this is typically the day the BACS file is submitted with a value date — the FPS must reach HMRC before the value date, not the BACS submission date. For cheque payments, it's the date the cheque is handed over or posted. For cash, it's the date of physical handover. Modern payroll software automates the FPS submission to coincide with the BACS file generation, but the discipline is on the employer.

3. **What is the late-FPS penalty stack and the one-free-per-year concession?**
   FA 2009 Sch 55 (extended to RTI via SI 2013/521) imposes monthly penalties of £100 / £200 / £300 / £400 depending on employer size (1-9 / 10-49 / 50-249 / 250+ employees). HMRC's published concession waives the first late FPS of each tax year — so a landlord LtdCo that ships one late FPS in May 2026 pays £0; the second late FPS in October would attract the £100 monthly penalty. Daily penalties kick in after 3 months of continuing failure.

4. **What is the EPS (Employer Payment Summary) and when do I file it?**
   EPS is the supplementary monthly return used to claim Employment Allowance offsets, report nil-payment months (no FPS would otherwise tell HMRC the employer is still active), and recover statutory payments (SSP, SMP, SPP, SAP, ShPP, NPL). The deadline is the 19th of the month following the tax month — so the EPS for tax month 6 May to 5 June is due by 19 June.

5. **What is the reg 67E correction window — and what's the 20 April cliff?**
   Where you discover an inaccuracy in a previously-filed FPS, reg 67E requires you to provide the correct information in the next FPS for the tax year in question. The verbatim text: "if the information has not been corrected before 20th April following the end of the tax year in question, the employer must make a return under this paragraph". So the in-year correction route works while you're still inside the tax year and up to 20 April. After 20 April, you must file a separate amendment return — a more involved process.

6. **What's the corporation-tax interaction for landlord LtdCos running payroll?**
   Salary, employer NIC and Apprenticeship Levy are deductible against company profits as wholly-and-exclusively-incurred expenses under CTA 2009 Part 3. The 9-month rule under CTA 2009 s.1289 catches accrued-but-unpaid earnings: a bonus accrued at year-end 31 March 2027 but not actually paid within 9 months (so by 31 December 2027) loses the CT deduction for the 2026/27 accounting period and is deductible only in the period of actual payment. Property-LtdCos with cross-period bonus arrangements should diary the 9-month threshold.

7. **Does CIS reporting (CIS300) coexist with PAYE FPS?**
   Yes. Landlord LtdCos that engage construction sub-contractors for refurb work operate the Construction Industry Scheme under FA 2004 ss.57-77 + SI 2005/2045. CIS300 monthly return is separate from FPS, due by 19th of the month following the tax month, with its own penalty regime (£100 initial + escalating). A LtdCo can be both an employer (FPS for the in-house bookkeeper) and a contractor (CIS300 for the refurb foreman) simultaneously. Our B4 brief on CIS verification covers this in more depth.

8. **What constitutes a "reasonable excuse" defence under FA 2009 Sch 55?**
   FA 2009 Sch 55 para 23 provides a reasonable-excuse defence where the failure was not due to the taxpayer's fault. *Christine Perrin v HMRC* [2018] UKUT 156 (TCC) is the leading authority — the test is a four-stage process: identify the cause of failure, determine whether that cause amounts to a reasonable excuse, determine when the reasonable excuse ceased, and determine whether the failure was remedied without unreasonable delay thereafter. HMRC typically accepts genuine system failure, sudden illness, unforeseen events; HMRC typically rejects "I forgot", "my software was expensive", "my agent was unreliable" (the agent's failure is the employer's failure unless the agent's failure was itself unforeseeable).

9. **What's the year-end reconciliation routine?**
   Six steps: (i) mark the final FPS or EPS as "Final submission for the year"; (ii) issue P60 to each employee employed at 5 April by 31 May; (iii) prepare and submit P11D forms by 6 July for any employee with benefits-in-kind (BIK); (iv) prepare P11D(b) employer declaration covering all P11Ds plus the Class 1A NIC liability by 6 July; (v) pay Class 1A NIC by 22 July (electronic) or 19 July (cheque); (vi) update payroll software with the new tax year's parameters (rates, thresholds, tax codes via P9 or P9X notifications from HMRC).

10. **What's the auto-enrolment compliance overlay?**
    Pensions Act 2008 + SI 2010/772 require every employer with at least one qualifying worker to auto-enrol them and contribute. FPS data feeds The Pensions Regulator's monitoring system. Three operational obligations: (i) auto-enrol each qualifying worker per payday; (ii) submit the Declaration of Compliance to TPR within 5 months of the duties start date; (iii) re-declare every 3 years. Failure to comply triggers TPR's escalating notice + penalty regime, which is separate from the HMRC penalty stack for PAYE.

11. **How does this page differ from your overview payroll guide?**
    Our companion page (`a-complete-guide-to-uk-payroll-taxes-and-deductions-for-employers`) is the framework + rate-stack overview — what PAYE is, the gross-to-net mechanics, the Employment Allowance + Apprenticeship Levy positions, the first-hire checklist. This page (C5) is the reporting-calendar compliance deep — when the FPS is due, when the EPS is due, when the payment is due, what the penalty stack looks like, how to fix a mistake. Read the overview first; come back here for the reporting discipline.

12. **What records must I keep, and for how long?**
    SI 2003/2682 reg 90 requires payroll records to be kept for 3 years from the end of the tax year (HMRC standard). The records cover payments, deductions, employee identification (NI numbers, addresses, P45/starter checklist info), benefits-in-kind, statutory payments. VAT-registered employers must retain VAT-related records for 6 years under VATA 1994 Sch 11 para 6. Practical recommendation: 6 years across the board for portfolio operators with VAT-engaging commercial property — simpler than tracking separate retention regimes.
