# Ensuring Compliance with UK Payroll Reporting Regulations for Property-Business Employers

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the RTI reporting regime): **The Income Tax (Pay As You Earn) Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/2682), Part 4 regulations 67B-97M.** Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/contents on 2026-05-26.
  - **Reg 67B "Real time returns of information about relevant payments"** — the operative Full Payment Submission (FPS) requirement. Each FPS must reach HMRC **on or before** the time the employer makes the relevant payment to the employee.
  - **Reg 67D** — alternative quarterly return mechanism for very-small-employer cases (limited scope; most landlord LtdCos do NOT qualify).
  - **Reg 67E "Inaccuracies in returns"** — verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/regulation/67E on 2026-05-26: "Where an employer becomes aware of an inaccuracy in a return submitted under regulation 67B or 67D, the employer must provide the correct information in the next return for the tax year in question". Exception: if not corrected before **20 April** following the tax-year end, the employer must make a separate amendment return under reg 67E.
  - **Reg 67F** — additional information returns where required.
  - **Reg 67G "Payments to and recoveries from HMRC: payments"** — payment-cycle requirement: 22nd of the following month for electronic payment, 19th for cheque.
  - **Reg 67H + 67I** — Employer Payment Summary (EPS) mechanic, used to report nil-payment months and to recover statutory payments (statutory sick pay, statutory maternity pay) and the Employment Allowance offset.
  - **Reg 73** — annual return obligation (legacy P35 / P14 framework, mostly subsumed by RTI but the regulations retain historical residual references for non-RTI corner-cases).
  - **Reg 90** — record-keeping requirements (3 years from the end of the tax year, per HMRC standard).
- Primary (the late-filing penalty stack): **Finance Act 2009 Schedule 55** — penalties for failure to make returns; applied to the RTI regime via **Income Tax (PAYE) (Amendment No. 2) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/521)** and subsequent commencement orders. Penalty bands:
  - **Late FPS penalty:** monthly £100-£400 depending on employer size (1-9 employees: £100; 10-49: £200; 50-249: £300; 250+: £400). HMRC typically allows one "free" late filing per tax year without penalty as concession; second-onwards attract penalty.
  - **Late payment penalty (Sch 56):** 1% / 2% / 3% / 4% / 5% bands depending on the number of late months in the tax year.
  - **Daily penalties** apply after 3 months of continuing failure.
- Primary (the on-or-before exception): **HMRC guidance under reg 67B(3)** — small-employer (under-9-employees) end-of-month-tolerance has been formally withdrawn, but the practical "reasonable excuse" defence under Sch 55 para 23 remains available for genuinely impossible-to-meet-on-day cases (system failure, unforeseen absence of the payroll-runner).
- Primary (the National Insurance regulations parallel): **The Social Security (Contributions) Regulations 2001 (SI 2001/1004)** — parallel regulations for the NIC side of RTI reporting; FPS includes both PAYE income tax and NIC data.
- Primary (corporation-tax interaction for landlord LtdCos): **CTA 2009 Part 3** — salary + employer NIC + Apprenticeship Levy are deductible CT-expenses against company profits in the accounting period in which they are incurred / paid (subject to the 9-month-after-year-end paid-by deadline under CTA 2009 s.1289 for accruals).
- Supporting (auto-enrolment compliance overlay):
  - **Pensions Act 2008 + Occupational and Personal Pension Schemes (Automatic Enrolment) Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/772)** — employer auto-enrolment duties triggered on every payday for jobholder assessment.
  - **Pensions Regulator (TPR)** — enforcement body; FPS data feeds TPR's compliance monitoring.
- Supporting (Construction Industry Scheme overlay where landlord LtdCo engages construction sub-contractors for refurb work — RIGHT-EDGE of payroll reporting):
  - **CIS regulations under FA 2004 ss.57-77 + SI 2005/2045** — separate monthly CIS300 return alongside the PAYE FPS where construction labour engaged. Cross-reference to MW2 B4 `beginners-guide-to-cis-verification-in-the-uk`.
