  - **s.65** — "Procedure etc for verifying identity" — generic procedure provision applying to BOTH director + PSC verification.
  - **s.66** — "Authorisation of corporate service providers" — ACSP authorisation regime (CA 2006 inserted-section for AML-supervised firm registration).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is identity verification in the UK in one paragraph?** (A statutory requirement under ECCTA 2023 ss.40-45 + s.64 + s.65 that natural persons holding director or PSC roles at UK companies prove their identity to Companies House or to an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP, ECCTA s.66). Verification produces a Companies House personal code under s.68. The verified-identity data is largely withheld from public register inspection under s.69. Identity verification is per natural person, not per company — one verification covers all of that person's directorships and PSC interests.)
2. **Who has to verify?** (Every UK company director — including non-UK residents — every PSC (broadly anyone with >25% shares / voting / appointment rights / significant influence), eventually filers + corporate-officer scenarios under a later commencement phase. Landlord BTL portfolios with multiple SPVs: every individual director + every PSC. Verification headcount = number of natural persons, not number of companies × roles.)
3. **When did the regime become mandatory?** (Phased: voluntary from 8 April 2025; LEGAL REQUIREMENT for newly appointed directors and PSCs from **18 November 2025**; 12-month transition window for EXISTING directors and PSCs ending around November 2026 (each company's deadline is its next confirmation statement filed within / after the window). Later phase no earlier than November 2026 for filers + corporate-officer scenarios. The state moves; verify against the Companies House campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` at write time per §11.A — earlier URL `https://www.gov.uk/government/news/changes-to-uk-company-law` returns HTTP 404 per F-12 Wave 9 catch.)
4. **How do I actually verify — what are the two routes?** (Route 1: GOV.UK One Login — self-service via the One Login app or website, biometric document checks, no fee. Route 2: through an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP) under ECCTA s.66 — your accountant or company-secretarial agent, if AML-supervised under MLR 2017 and separately registered with Companies House as an ACSP, can verify on your behalf. The ACSP route is preferred where the accountant already holds AML-verified ID documents. The B2 page sketches the route choice; the operational page deep-dives.)
5. **What documents count?** (Biometric passport (any country, in date) is the cleanest route. Other accepted documents include UK photo driving licence (full or provisional), UK biometric residence permit, UK Frontier Worker permit. Companies House publishes the canonical accepted-documents list; verify at write time.)
6. **What is a Companies House personal code?** (The unique identifier issued to each verified individual under ECCTA s.68. Quoted in subsequent Companies House filings (confirmation statement, appointment notification, change-of-particulars filings) to evidence verification. One person = one code = covers all their directorships and PSC interests across every UK company they are associated with.)
7. **What happens if I do not verify?** (Acting as a director or PSC of a UK company without being verified by the applicable deadline is a criminal offence — unverified persons cannot lawfully be named on Companies House filings. Sanctions include fines for the company and the individual; refusal to register filings; eventual disqualification exposure. The operational page covers sanctions in detail; this B2 page surfaces the headline.)
8. **What about US-based or other overseas directors of UK companies?** (Same regime applies — directorship of a UK company triggers the verification requirement regardless of where the director is resident. Overseas directors typically verify via the GOV.UK One Login app using a biometric passport, no UK visit required. Sibling MW2 B-page `how-us-based-directors-of-uk-companies-can-navigate-ecctas-new-identity-verification-rules` covers the overseas-director angle in depth.)
9. **What about Authorised Corporate Service Providers — should my accountant become one?** (ACSPs are firms supervised for AML under MLR 2017 that register with Companies House to provide verification services. Most established UK accountants and company-secretarial firms are AML-supervised already (HMRC, ICAEW, ACCA, ICAS, CIOT), so the ACSP application is procedural. For landlord LtdCo portfolios where the accountant already holds AML-compliant KYC, the ACSP route is often faster than self-verification. Sibling MW2 B-page on PSCs covers the ACSP framework in depth.)
10. **Is verified identity data made public?** (Mostly NO, per ECCTA s.69 "Identity verification: material unavailable for public inspection". The fact of verification is recorded on the public register; the underlying identity documents are not. This is a deliberate design: the verification regime hardens the integrity of the register without expanding the public-disclosure perimeter.)
11. **What about the existing director / PSC obligations — what else changed under ECCTA?** (ECCTA introduced multiple parallel reforms: registered email address (ss.28-30; not published publicly per §11.A), lawful purposes statement on confirmation statement (ss.59-63), abolition of local PSC registers (ss.51-52, consolidated at CH central), Registrar's objectives + new powers (s.1). Each is covered in sibling Companies House pages; this page surfaces the ID-verification element and forward-links.)
