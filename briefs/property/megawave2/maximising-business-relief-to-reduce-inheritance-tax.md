# Maximising Business Relief to Reduce Inheritance Tax

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the BPR framework):
  - **IHTA 1984 ss.103-114** — Business Property Relief. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1984/51 on 2026-05-26.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.104** — relief mechanic. Two rates of relief (post-FA-2025/2026-cap-architecture — see §15.4):
    - **100% relief** below the £1m combined APR+BPR cap (from 6 April 2026) — applies to (a) a business or interest in a business, (b) unquoted securities giving control, (c) unquoted shares.
    - **50% relief** for (a) quoted shares giving control, (b) land/buildings/plant used by a business / settled property in which the deceased had a life interest, (c) for AIM-traded shares (post 6 April 2026 — moved from 100% to 50% per FA 2025/2026 reforms).
    - **Above the £1m cap:** rate drops to 50% on the excess (HP §15.4 lock).
  - **IHTA 1984 s.105** — categories of "relevant business property":
    - s.105(1)(a) — a business or an interest in a business (sole-trader/partnership share).
    - s.105(1)(b) — securities of a company (under s.269 controlled) that gave control to the transferor.
    - s.105(1)(bb) — any unquoted shares in a company.
    - s.105(1)(c) — quoted shares giving control to the transferor.
    - s.105(1)(d) — land/buildings/machinery/plant used wholly or mainly for the purposes of a business carried on by a company controlled by the transferor or by a partnership of which the transferor was a partner.
    - s.105(1)(e) — land/buildings/machinery/plant settled in a trust of which the transferor was entitled to an interest in possession, where used for business purposes.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.105(3)** — the **investment exclusion**. Verified verbatim: "A business or interest in a business … shall not be relevant business property if the business … consists wholly or mainly of one or more of the following, that is to say, dealing in securities, stocks or shares, land or buildings or **making or holding investments**." This is the operative provision that excludes pure BTL portfolios from BPR — the *Pawson v HMRC* line (HP §22.1).
  - **IHTA 1984 s.106** — **minimum 2-year holding period**. Verified verbatim: "Property is not relevant business property in relation to a transfer of value unless it was owned by the transferor throughout the two years immediately preceding the transfer." The 2-year clock is the operational gatekeeper for new acquisitions; a property bought as a trading-business asset 18 months before death does NOT qualify regardless of merits.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.107** — **replacement-property reset rule** (the key planning lever). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1984/51/section/107 on 2026-05-26. Where qualifying business property is replaced by other qualifying business property within the prior 3 years, the original 2-year clock is preserved across the replacement — the new property doesn't need its own 2-year clock provided continuous qualification. **Critical for property-business reorganisations:** swapping one trading-business asset for another within 3 years does NOT reset the 2-year clock. Swapping a qualifying asset for a non-qualifying asset (e.g. selling a trading FHL and buying a pure BTL) does break continuity.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.108** — successive transfers. Where business property is transferred between spouses (spouse exemption applies, no chargeable transfer), the receiving spouse inherits the 2-year clock — the clock does NOT reset on inter-spouse transfer (this is a key planning point most amateur content misses).
  - **IHTA 1984 s.109** — successive transfers in 2-year period. Where transferor acquired property by an earlier transfer that itself qualified for BPR, the 2-year clock is preserved.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.110** — value of business — the value of a business means net value (assets minus liabilities). BPR applies to that net value, not gross asset value.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.111** — value of "excepted assets" — value of assets within the business not used for business purposes (cash surplus to business needs, investments held in trading company) is EXCLUDED from BPR. This is the **mixed-purpose company carve-out** — a trading company with substantial surplus cash + investment property loses BPR on the value attributable to the non-trade assets.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.112** — value of excepted assets continued. Detailed application of the carve-out.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.113 + s.113A + s.113B** — **binding contract for sale gateway**. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1984/51/section/113 on 2026-05-26. Where the transferor had entered into a binding contract to sell the business / shares before the transfer (typically before death), BPR is denied. This catches the "I'm selling the business; I'll die before completion; my estate inherits the sale proceeds with BPR" plan — closed.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.114** — replacement of business property by property of a different kind.
- Primary (the cross-tax CGT holdover interaction):
  - **TCGA 1992 s.165** — gift relief for business assets. Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/section/165. Available on gifts of qualifying business assets (Sch 7); the donor and donee jointly elect; CGT crystallises on the donee at the donee's later disposal at donor's base cost. **Property-context limitation:** s.165 is for trading business assets (Sch 7 para 2). Pure BTL is investment, not trading — s.165 unavailable. Trading property businesses (e.g. property developers' WIP, qualifying FHL pre-Apr-2025-cessation if relevant) may qualify.
  - **TCGA 1992 s.260** — holdover relief on IHT-chargeable transfers (CLTs into trust). Available on gifts into discretionary trust (a chargeable lifetime transfer for IHT) provided the trust is non-settlor-interested (ss.169B-G). **Strategic lever:** transfer BPR-qualifying property into a non-settlor-interested discretionary trust pre-death — 100% BPR available so no immediate IHT, AND s.260 CGT holdover applies (because the transfer is an IHT-chargeable transfer in form even though tax due is nil under BPR). Result: asset moves into trust without CGT, IHT-free under BPR, future appreciation outside the donor's estate. This is the **classic BPR-into-trust planning route** and is the principal lever for maximising BPR benefit.
- Primary (the £1m cap from 6 April 2026 — HP §15.4 lock):
  - **From 6 April 2026: £1m combined APR + BPR cap.** Below the cap: 100% relief; above the cap: 50% relief (effective 20% IHT on excess).
  - **Transferable between spouses:** but only £1m each — no doubling between spouses; the cap is per-deceased not per-married-couple. (This is the key change vs the pre-2026 architecture where allowances stacked.)
  - **AIM-shares 50% sub-tier:** AIM-traded shares now relieved at 50% AND do NOT consume the £1m allowance. Operationally a separate sub-tier.
  - **Anti-forestalling:** new rules apply to lifetime transfers on or after **30 October 2024** (Autumn Budget 2024 announcement) if donor dies on or after 6 April 2026 and within 7 years. Pre-announcement gifts are NOT caught.
  - **Trust anti-fragmentation:** multi-trust same-settlor cohort post-30-October-2024 share a single £1m allowance (Finance Bill 2025-26 published 21 July 2025; FA 2026 enactment expected with Autumn Budget 2025 cycle).
- Primary (the Pawson investment line — the property-investor's anti-claim discipline):
  - ***Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC)** — the leading authority on the s.105(3) investment exclusion for property businesses. Verified at https://www.bailii.org on 2026-05-26. Verified anchor at HP §22.1. Standard BTL is "wholly or mainly" investment; BPR denied. Serviced accommodation may qualify IF services are substantial — the Pawson-distinguishing factual matrix is high.
  - ***Graham v HMRC* [2018] UKFTT 306** + ***McCall v HMRC* [2009] NICA 12** — narrower SA-qualifying-for-BPR fact patterns.
  - ***Hardcastle v HMRC* [2024] UKFTT** — recent SA-qualifying case, narrowly decided in taxpayer's favour on managed-kitchen + daily-cleaning + breakfast facts.
  - ***Zetland v HMRC***, ***Henderson v HMRC***, ***Pawson*** at FTT (pre-UT appeal) — the SA-not-qualifying line.
- Primary (the FIC + BPR boundary — important non-overlap):
  - **FIC equity is NOT BPR-qualifying** typically — because most FICs are pure investment vehicles holding BTL property (Pawson). The "I'll wrap my BTL portfolio in a FIC and get BPR on the shares" plan FAILS — FIC shares are unquoted (s.105(1)(bb)), but the underlying business is investment (s.105(3) exclusion); BPR denied. HP §22.6 + §21.5.
- House position references: §15.4 (BPR/APR April 2026 cap — primary lock); §22.1 (Pawson investment line); §22.2 (deed of variation to maximise BPR post-death); §22.5 (spouse exemption + transferable allowances); §22.6 (FIC value-freeze + BPR boundary); §22.13 (trust-vs-FIC decision); §15.1 (NRB/RNRB stack). The page threads §15.4 + §22.1 + §22.13 + §15.4-anti-forestalling at the trust-route depth.

Pre-empts the most common amateur misconceptions:
- "BTL portfolios get BPR" — false; Pawson investment exclusion.
- "Wrap the BTL in a limited company and the shares get BPR" — false; unquoted-shares mechanic (s.105(1)(bb)) doesn't override the underlying investment activity (s.105(3)).
- "Wrap the BTL in a FIC and the FIC shares get BPR" — false; same Pawson reasoning.
- "BPR is unlimited at 100%" — false from 6 April 2026; £1m combined APR+BPR cap.
- "Each spouse gets their own £1m allowance, total £2m for the couple" — partial truth; £1m EACH but NOT doubled by spouse exemption (spouse exemption transfers value, not the cap).
- "I can move BPR assets between trusts to multiply the allowance" — false from 30 October 2024 (anti-fragmentation rules for same-settlor cohort).
- "AIM shares are unaffected by the cap" — partial truth; AIM rate drops to 50% AND outside the £1m cap (separate sub-tier).
- "I'll just sign the sale contract now and die before completion" — false; s.113 binding-contract-for-sale denies BPR.
- "2-year clock resets on transfer between spouses" — false; s.108 preserves the clock.
- "Replacement property always resets the 2-year clock" — partial truth; s.107 preserves where replacement is within 3 years AND remains qualifying property.
