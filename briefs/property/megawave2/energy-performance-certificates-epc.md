# Energy Performance Certificates (EPCs): Complete UK Landlord Guide — What They Are, How to Get One, MEES Compliance, and the Tax Side

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (EPC architecture): The Energy Performance of Buildings (England and Wales) Regulations 2012 ("EPB Regulations 2012", SI 2012/3118) — implementing EPC obligations for sales + lettings + construction + display in public buildings. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2012/3118/contents on 2026-05-27 (sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35). Operative anchors:
  - **EPB Regulations 2012 reg.6** — EPC required on sale / letting (the trigger duty).
  - **EPB Regulations 2012 reg.7** — Information to be provided to prospective buyer / tenant (must produce EPC before marketing begins).
  - **EPB Regulations 2012 reg.11** — Validity period (an EPC is valid for **10 years** from the date of issue).
  - **EPB Regulations 2012 reg.35-37** — Penalty regime (TSO penalty notice; up to £200 per default for residential; up to £5,000 for non-domestic depending on rateable value).
  - **EPB Regulations 2012 reg.34** — accredited assessor requirement (EPC must be produced by an accredited Domestic Energy Assessor (DEA) for residential or Non-Domestic Energy Assessor (NDEA) for commercial).
- Primary (MEES — the landlord-letting consequence stack): The Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) (England and Wales) Regulations 2015 ("MEES Regulations 2015", **SI 2015/962**). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2015/962/contents on 2026-05-27. Operative anchors:
  - **MEES Regulations 2015 reg.23-25** — Prohibition on letting "sub-standard" property (EPC F or G) without an exemption; reg.25(2) £3,500 (incl. VAT) **landlord cost cap**.
  - **MEES Regulations 2015 reg.36-37** — Enforcement by local weights and measures authority; civil penalties up to £5,000 for breach of letting prohibition; up to £5,000 false-information penalty; published-information penalty.
  - **Commencement chain (verified per §26.3 lock):** EPC E floor applies to NEW domestic tenancies since **1 April 2018**; to ALL CONTINUING domestic lets since **1 April 2020**; to continuing non-domestic lets since **1 April 2023** (reg.27).
- Primary (Scotland — distinct regime): EPCs in Scotland operate under separate Scottish Building Standards regulations + Energy Performance of Buildings (Scotland) Regulations 2008. Sessions writing pan-UK landlord content must distinguish. (For Property Tax Partners, the operative jurisdiction is England + Wales unless explicitly cross-border.)
- Supporting (proposed reforms — NOT enacted): "EPC C by 2030 for all PRS / 2028 for new tenancies / £10,000 cap" framing is **government policy aspiration only** as of 2026-05-27 per §26.3 Wave 7 lock + the existing site mythbuster `epc-c-2030-minimum-energy-efficiency-landlord-spending-cap`. **No amending Statutory Instrument has been laid** to give the EPC C trajectory statutory force.
- Supporting (grant schemes — funding for upgrades): Energy Company Obligation 4 (ECO4 — Apr 2022 to Mar 2026, successor scheme TBC); Great British Insulation Scheme (GBIS, running alongside ECO4); Boiler Upgrade Scheme (BUS — £7,500 grant for ASHP / GSHP / biomass replacements); Home Upgrade Grant 2 (HUG2 — off-gas-grid homes, verify replacement scheme status at write per §26.3).
- Supporting (Property Tax Partners angle): ITTOIA 2005 s.272 (general allowable expenses test for rental businesses + capital-vs-revenue boundary); TCGA 1992 s.38(1)(b) (capital improvements added to CGT base cost); HMRC PIM2030+ (capital vs revenue in rental businesses); HMRC BIM35400+ (general capital vs revenue line). EPC assessment fee = revenue (regulatory compliance cost). MEES upgrade spend = capital improvement (CGT base-cost addition) where it improves building specification; like-for-like repairs are revenue. Grant receipts reduce CGT base cost.
- House position reference: **§26.3** (MEES regime current state + EPC C 2030 IS NOT ENACTED critical do-not-write — the operative lock for B15); **§26.7** (tax-side hooks across §26 — MEES energy improvement spend capital/revenue line); **§26.8** (§26 cluster do-not-write — "EPC C 2030 / 2028 is now law" false; "landlord cap is now £10,000" false). **§4** (Section 24 — not engaged by EPC compliance costs; MEES is operating cost not finance cost). **§13** main do-not-write applies.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is an Energy Performance Certificate in one paragraph?** (A statutory document issued under EPB Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/3118) that rates a building's modelled energy efficiency on a scale from A to G + provides recommended upgrades. Required by law on every UK residential sale or letting (with limited exceptions such as listed buildings + certain holiday accommodation + buildings due for demolition); produced by an accredited Domestic Energy Assessor; valid for 10 years from the date of issue; registered on the central Energy Performance of Buildings Register at https://www.gov.uk/find-energy-certificate; cost typically £60-£120 for residential. The EPC band (and the SAP/RdSAP numerical rating behind it) drives the MEES letting-eligibility test for landlords + informs prospective buyers/tenants on running cost expectations.)
2. **What is the SAP / RdSAP rating and how does it map to A-G bands?** (Standard Assessment Procedure (SAP) for new dwellings; Reduced data Standard Assessment Procedure (RdSAP) for existing dwellings. Produces a numerical rating from 1 to 100 (theoretically 1 to 100+; very efficient dwellings can score above 92). Mapping: A = 92-100; B = 81-91; C = 69-80; D = 55-68; E = 39-54; F = 21-38; G = 1-20. The numerical rating is the precise driver — landlords with properties scoring exactly 39 sit at the very bottom of band E and are MEES-compliant; properties at 38 are band F and MEES-prohibited without an exemption.)
3. **How is an EPC obtained — what does the process actually look like?** (Five steps: (i) select an accredited Domestic Energy Assessor (DEA) for a residential EPC OR Non-Domestic Energy Assessor (NDEA) for commercial — find via the central register at gov.uk; (ii) DEA visits the property typically for 30-90 minutes, takes measurements, photographs heating system + insulation + fenestration + boiler + thermostat controls, gathers serial numbers + EPC-relevant data; (iii) DEA inputs data into RdSAP-approved software, generates draft EPC; (iv) EPC lodged on central register at gov.uk; (v) landlord/seller receives PDF + reference number. Typical cost £60-£120 for residential; £150-£500 for commercial depending on size + complexity. Turnaround 1-5 days typically. EPC carries 10-year validity from date of issue.)
4. **When do you need an EPC — sales, lettings, new builds, and the limited exemptions?** (Required: (a) on every residential sale (must be commissioned before marketing begins per reg.6 EPB 2012); (b) on every residential letting (new tenancy / renewal / change-of-tenant; existing tenants in pre-2008 leases without an EPC need one when the property is next let); (c) on every commercial sale or letting; (d) on completion of a new build (Part L Building Regulations requirement). Limited exemptions: listed buildings where compliance would unacceptably alter character (HOWEVER not a blanket exemption — landlords MUST seek and verify expert advice on listed status; HMRC + DLUHC published clarification confirms most listed buildings still require EPC unless specific energy-performance improvements would impair character); standalone buildings under 50 sq m floor area; holiday accommodation rented out for under 4 months per year + occupied less than 4 months / 1 year by holiday-let tenants (the FHL boundary post-FHL-income-tax-abolition — verify against current EPB 2012 reg.5 + government guidance at write); temporary buildings (under 2-year planning permission); workshops + agricultural buildings with low energy demand. Sessions writing landlord content must NOT assert "listed buildings don't need an EPC" — the exemption is conditional + the EPC is generally still produced and lodged.)
5. **How long is an EPC valid for, and when does a new one need to be produced?** (Valid for 10 years from the date of issue per reg.11 EPB 2012. A new EPC is NOT required mid-validity merely on a change of tenant or new sale — the existing EPC carries over. New EPC IS required where (a) the existing EPC has expired beyond 10 years; (b) building works trigger a Part L Building Regulations completion EPC (extensions, major refurbishments, change of use); (c) the property is voluntarily re-assessed after upgrade works to update the band on the register. Landlords with portfolios approaching 10-year mass-expiry should run scheduled re-assessment as a portfolio operation rather than ad-hoc per-property.)
6. **What is the Minimum Energy Efficiency Standard (MEES) — and what is the landlord floor?** (MEES Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/962) prohibit letting "sub-standard" private rented property — domestic EPC F or G — without an exemption registered on the PRS Exemptions Register at gov.uk. **The current enacted floor is EPC E** with effect from 1 April 2018 (new tenancies) + 1 April 2020 (all continuing lets) + 1 April 2023 (continuing non-domestic). Landlords with sub-standard properties must either upgrade to EPC E OR register an exemption. The **landlord cost cap is £3,500 (including VAT)** under reg.25(2) — landlords are not obliged to spend more than £3,500 (incl. VAT) of their own money on energy improvements; where £3,500 of spend is insufficient to bring the property to E, a high-cost exemption can be registered. Cross-link to §26.3 lock + existing site mythbuster page for the £10,000-cap policy-aspiration framing.)
7. **What are the exemptions on the PRS Exemptions Register?** (Six categories per MEES 2015 regs 24-25 + government guidance: (i) **high-cost exemption** — improvements would cost more than £3,500 incl. VAT; (ii) **devaluation exemption** — independent surveyor confirms recommended upgrades would reduce property market value by more than 5%; (iii) **consent exemption** — third-party consent required (tenant, freeholder, planning authority, lender) refused or conditional in a way that prevents the upgrade; (iv) **wall-insulation exemption** — independent installer confirms cavity-wall or external/internal-wall insulation would negatively affect building fabric or structure; (v) **all relevant improvements made** — all cost-effective improvements available within £3,500 cap have been made but property still below E; (vi) **new landlord temporary exemption** (6-month window where landlord became landlord very recently — limited to 6 months from acquisition or comparable trigger). Exemption registration on PRS Exemptions Register at gov.uk; typically 5-year duration; renewal mechanic on expiry. Without a registered exemption, letting a sub-standard property is unlawful.)
8. **What are the penalties for letting a sub-standard property?** (Civil penalty regime under reg.36-37 MEES 2015 — up to **£5,000 per non-compliant tenancy** + **£5,000 false-information penalty** + **£5,000 published-information penalty** (capped at £5,000 per breach). Enforcement by local weights and measures authority. Additional published-on-PRS-Exemptions-Register-as-defaulter consequence. Stages: penalty notice → 28-day window for representations → final penalty notice → FTT appeal route. Penalty IS NOT tax-deductible (general BIM38500+ principle that penalties for breach of law are non-deductible). Letting an unregistered sub-standard property also creates a CONTRACT-INSPECTION-NOTICE-BLOCK risk if a future MEES inspection follows. The reputational + counterparty-due-diligence consequence is increasingly material — lenders, JV partners, professional advisers check MEES status as part of due diligence on landlord portfolios.)
9. **The EPC C by 2030 trajectory — is it actually law?** (Short answer: NO. The "EPC C by 2030 for all PRS / 2028 for new tenancies / £10,000 spending cap" framing is **government policy aspiration only** as of 2026-05-27. **No amending Statutory Instrument has been laid** to give the EPC C trajectory statutory force. The 2020 government consultation + the 2026 consultation outcome + various ministerial statements all stop short of legislating a new minimum band. Sessions must NOT write "the cap rises to £10,000" or "EPC C is required from 2028" as live statute. The £3,500 cap + EPC E floor under SI 2015/962 are the enacted current state — period. Forward-link to existing mythbuster `epc-c-2030-minimum-energy-efficiency-landlord-spending-cap` for the full enacted-vs-policy walkthrough. §26.3 + §26.8 lock — critical do-not-write.)
10. **What grant schemes are available to fund upgrades?** (Four principal routes — overview only, forward-link to grants deep-dive for mechanics: (i) **ECO4** (Energy Company Obligation 4, Apr 2022 - Mar 2026, successor scheme TBC) — energy-supplier-funded; LA Flex route allows landlord access in some council areas; means-tested + property-band-tied; (ii) **GBIS** (Great British Insulation Scheme, formerly ECO+) — running alongside ECO4; general-eligibility route open to landlords in some areas; focus on insulation measures; (iii) **BUS** (Boiler Upgrade Scheme) — £7,500 grant for ASHP / GSHP / biomass heat-pump replacement of fossil-fuel heating; landlord-eligible without means-test; key route for off-gas-grid + high-EPC-impact upgrades; (iv) **HUG2** (Home Upgrade Grant 2) — off-gas-grid homes; runs to March 2025 (verify replacement scheme status at write); means-tested; landlord access partial. Grant receipts reduce CGT base cost under HMRC general principle. Forward-link to `epc-improvement-grant-schemes-landlords-eco4-bus-gbis` for the full grants walkthrough.)
11. **The tax side — are EPC + upgrade costs deductible against rental income?** (Three-line answer per §26.7 lock: (a) **EPC assessment fee + MEES compliance regulatory costs** — revenue / deductible against rental income under ITTOIA 2005 s.272 wholly-and-exclusively rule (applied generously to compulsory regulatory costs); (b) **MEES upgrade spend that materially improves the building specification** — capital improvement under TCGA 1992 s.38(1)(b) (insulation, double-glazing, heat-pump installation, high-efficiency boiler replacing low-efficiency); CGT base-cost addition; NOT deductible against rental income; (c) **MEES upgrade spend that is like-for-like repair** — revenue / deductible under s.272; example, replacing a failed boiler with a comparable-specification equivalent on a 1:1 basis. Grant receipts reduce the CGT base cost (HMRC matches grants against the expenditure they fund). Section 24 finance-cost restriction NOT engaged by EPC compliance costs (these are operating costs not finance costs). Worked-example expected — Stage 2 sub-agent ground in a typical £4,000 boiler + insulation upgrade illustrative example with clear capital/revenue split.)
12. **What is the operational EPC compliance checklist for a landlord — what should I be doing now?** (Six-step operational checklist: (i) check current EPC validity for every property in portfolio at gov.uk EPB Register — note expiry date + band; (ii) for any property below EPC E without registered exemption — STOP marketing immediately, register exemption OR upgrade to E; (iii) for any property at exact-band-E (39 SAP) with marginal risk of slipping — plan upgrade or buffer-band-D move-up; (iv) plan for portfolio-level 10-year-mass-expiry of EPCs — schedule rolling re-assessment; (v) track government consultation on EPC C 2030 + £10,000 cap trajectory + future SI laying — plan upgrades on the policy expectation but DO NOT commit to schedule until SI is laid; (vi) document MEES upgrade spend with capital/revenue tagging at point of spend for tax-side treatment + future CGT base-cost evidence. The MEES regime is operationally low-cost where the property is already at E or better; the cost spikes on the small percentage of portfolio sub-E + the future policy-aspiration EPC C requirement.)
