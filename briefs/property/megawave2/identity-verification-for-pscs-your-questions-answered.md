# Identity Verification for Persons With Significant Control (PSCs): Your Questions Answered

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (the ECCTA ID-verification architecture — PSC-side): Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 c. 56 ("ECCTA 2023"). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/56/contents on 2026-05-27 (Stage 2 sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35). Operative anchors per §11.A Wave 9 mini-lock + F-14 Wave 9 RUN catch corrections 2026-05-25:
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.64** — "**Identity verification of persons with significant control**" — **the PRIMARY PSC-side anchor for this entire page**. Section 64 is the operative provision driving the PSC-specific verification requirement.
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.65** — "Procedure etc for verifying identity" — generic procedure provision applying to BOTH director + PSC verification (so PSCs share the same procedural backbone as directors).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.66** — "Authorisation of corporate service providers" — ACSP framework available to PSCs as well as directors.
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.67** — "Exemption from identity verification: national security grounds" — narrow exemption applies to PSCs as well; rarely operatively relevant.
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.68** — "Allocation of unique identifiers" — personal code allocation applies per natural person (so a PSC who is ALSO a director of the same or another UK company has ONE code that covers BOTH roles + all UK companies).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.69** — "Identity verification: material unavailable for public inspection" — publication restriction protects residential address + full DOB day + identifying-document number + biometric data of verified PSCs (this is the operational protection that allows PSCs to comply without exposing identity-theft-attractive data on the public register).
- Primary (the PSC architecture itself — what makes a natural person a PSC in the first place): Companies Act 2006 ss.790A-790ZG + Schedule 1A. The five PSC tests at CA 2006 Sch 1A (Conditions 1-5):
  - **Condition 1:** directly or indirectly holds more than 25% of the shares in the company.
  - **Condition 2:** directly or indirectly holds more than 25% of the voting rights in the company.
  - **Condition 3:** directly or indirectly holds the right to appoint or remove a majority of the directors of the company.
  - **Condition 4:** has the right to exercise, or actually exercises, significant influence or control over the company.
  - **Condition 5:** has the right to exercise, or actually exercises, significant influence or control over the activities of a trust or firm that itself meets Conditions 1-4 (the trust / firm look-through condition — the operative anchor for trustee-PSC + beneficiary-PSC analysis).
  - **CA 2006 Sch 1A para 9 + paras 23-26** — the indirect-holding + look-through mechanics that determine when a beneficiary (rather than trustee) is the PSC, or when a higher-tier company's shareholder is the PSC of a lower-tier company. (Stage 2 sub-agent verifies precise paragraph numbers at write per §16.35; the look-through mechanic is operationally critical for FIC + trust structures.)
- Primary (the commencement chain — when PSCs must verify): per §11.A Wave 9 hardening 2026-05-25:
  - **8 April 2025:** verification opens VOLUNTARILY for directors + PSCs.
  - **18 November 2025:** verification becomes a LEGAL REQUIREMENT for newly notified PSCs (i.e. anyone first becoming an in-scope PSC after this date must verify BEFORE the PSC01 / PSC02 / PSC03 / etc. notification can be filed).
  - **18 November 2025 → ~November 2026 (12-month transition window):** EXISTING PSCs (notified to Companies House before 18 November 2025) must verify by their company's next confirmation statement filed within / after the transition window.
  - **No earlier than November 2026:** corporate-PSC officer-verification phase (where a PSC is itself a corporate entity, designated officers of that corporate entity must verify) — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies current commencement state at write per §11.A F-12 against `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/`.
- Primary (the substantive filings where the PSC personal code is then quoted): CA 2006 PSC regime — ss.790A-790ZG + the family of PSC filings (PSC01 first notification + PSC02 / PSC03 / PSC04 / PSC05 / PSC06 / PSC07 / PSC08 / PSC09 changes / ceasings / corrections); CA 2006 s.853A confirmation statement (annual quote of personal code for every in-scope PSC linked to the company).
- Primary (the trust-look-through PSC tests for FIC + family-trust structures): CA 2006 Sch 1A Conditions 4 + 5 + the indirect-holding paragraphs. **Trustee-vs-beneficiary PSC analysis is operationally complex** — in most discretionary trust structures, the TRUSTEES are the PSCs because they hold legal title + exercise day-to-day control. But where a beneficiary has fixed-interest rights AND those rights meet one of the PSC tests, the beneficiary can ALSO or INSTEAD be the PSC. Each FIC + family-trust structure must be analysed individually. Stage 2 sub-agent grounds against CA 2006 PSC regulations + Companies House published PSC guidance + STEP journal commentary at write per §16.35.
- House position reference: **§11.A** Wave 9 mini-lock (ECCTA architecture + commencement chain + ACSP framework + s.68 personal code + s.69 publication restriction). **§21.5 / §22.6 / §22.10 / §22.11** (FIC + IHT-side architecture — PSCs of FIC structures often face overlapping considerations on growth-share + dividend-access analysis). **§22.4 / §22.9 / §22.12 / §22.13 / §22.16** (trust-side architecture — beneficiary-PSC + trustee-PSC analysis under CA 2006 Sch 1A intersects with trust governance). **§13** main do-not-write applies. **§11.A do-not-write list** applies — "Each SPV needs separate ID verification" (false), "Registered email is published on the public register" (false), "ID verification is voluntary" (false now), "Schedule 12 of ECCTA 2023 amends ECTEA 2022" (false).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is a "person with significant control" (PSC) and how do I know if I am one?** (A natural person who meets any one of the five PSC tests under Companies Act 2006 Schedule 1A: (i) more than 25% of the shares; (ii) more than 25% of the voting rights; (iii) right to appoint or remove a majority of directors; (iv) right to exercise or actual exercise of significant influence or control over the company; (v) right to exercise or actual exercise of significant influence or control over the activities of a trust or firm that itself meets Conditions 1-4 (the trust / firm look-through). Practical reality: most natural-person PSCs cross the line via Condition 1 (25% shareholding) or Condition 2 (25% voting rights). The "significant influence or control" tests (Conditions 4-5) are more nuanced and typically rely on the natural person's role in the trust or firm's decision-making. If your accountant or company adviser has told you that you are a PSC, you are; the PSC analysis was done at the point of incorporation or share-issue and was filed at Companies House via the PSC01 first-notification form (or PSC02 / PSC03 if your status changed thereafter).)
2. **Do I have to verify identity if I'm a PSC but not a director?** (YES. ECCTA 2023 s.64 ("Identity verification of persons with significant control") applies the verification requirement to natural-person PSCs as well as to natural-person directors. The two cohorts are treated equivalently in the verification regime. You verify ONCE as a natural person + receive ONE personal code that covers your PSC interest regardless of whether you are also a director.)
3. **I'm a PSC of multiple UK companies — do I verify multiple times?** (NO. ONE NATURAL-PERSON VERIFICATION → ONE PERSONAL CODE → COVERS EVERY UK DIRECTORSHIP + EVERY UK PSC INTEREST YOU WILL EVER HOLD. Per ECCTA s.68 (allocation of unique identifiers). The code is permanent + persists across all your UK roles. The PER-COMPANY step is providing the code on each company's next confirmation statement (CS01) or appointment / PSC change filing — that is filing-side admin, not re-verification. A PSC of five UK companies verifies once + then has the personal code recorded on each of the five companies' next confirmation statements.)
4. **I'm a beneficiary of a family discretionary trust that owns 25%+ of a UK company — am I the PSC, or are the trustees?** (Operationally complex — the analysis turns on CA 2006 Sch 1A look-through mechanics. The starting point: in most discretionary trust structures, the TRUSTEES are the PSCs because they hold legal title to the shares + exercise the voting rights + make discretionary decisions on income + capital distributions. The trustees verify identity per the PSC regime. BUT — Condition 4 (significant influence or control) + Condition 5 (significant influence or control over the activities of a trust or firm that itself meets Conditions 1-4) can flip the analysis to a BENEFICIARY in specific circumstances: (a) where the beneficiary is also a trustee (a "beneficiary-trustee"); (b) where the beneficiary has a fixed entitlement to income or capital that meets one of Conditions 1-3; (c) where the beneficiary has a reserved power under the trust deed to direct trustee decisions; (d) where the beneficiary, as a matter of substance over form, exercises significant influence over how the trustees act. The analysis is fact-specific + must be done at the point of trust creation + revisited if the trust's structure changes. Where you have been notified by the company that you are a PSC via the trust look-through, you verify identity per the PSC regime. Where the trustees are the notified PSCs (and you are not), the trustees verify + you do not. Cross-link to existing site Trust-side pages + Family Investment Company pillar for the wider trust governance framework.)
5. **I have growth shares in a Family Investment Company — am I a PSC?** (Depends on the SUBSTANCE of the growth share rights, not just the label. Standard PSC analysis applies — does the natural person hold > 25% of the shares (Condition 1), > 25% of the voting rights (Condition 2), the right to appoint or remove a majority of directors (Condition 3), or exercise significant influence or control (Conditions 4-5)? Growth shares typically (i) carry LIMITED voting rights (often non-voting or limited-voting), (ii) carry FUTURE economic rights (entitlement to value above a hurdle), and (iii) are issued in volumes that may or may not exceed 25% by number. A common pattern in FICs: founder-shares (full voting + dividend access) held by founder-parents; growth-shares (limited voting + value-above-hurdle) issued to adult-children. The adult-child growth-shareholder may or may not be a PSC depending on whether the growth-share class crosses 25% by number of shares + whether the voting rights are calculated by reference to share-class or by aggregate. Each FIC's PSC analysis must be done at incorporation + on each share-issue. If your FIC's company secretary or accountant has notified you on PSC01 that you are a PSC, you are; if they have not, you are not.)
6. **My spouse and I jointly own 30% of a UK company — are we both PSCs?** (Typically YES — joint holders of shares are each treated as holding the same percentage for PSC threshold purposes per CA 2006 Sch 1A interpretation. A 30% joint shareholding crosses the 25% threshold for EACH joint holder. Both spouses verify identity individually + each receives their own personal code. Civil partners are treated equivalently. Co-investors (non-spouse / non-civil-partner joint holders) are also typically each treated as PSCs in the same way. Stage 2 sub-agent verifies the precise CA 2006 Sch 1A interpretation at write per §16.35 against Companies House PSC guidance + the CA 2006 PSC regulations.)
7. **What does the public register show about me + what does it NOT show?** (CRITICAL data-protection answer. Per ECCTA s.69 publication restriction + Companies House published display rules: PUBLICLY VISIBLE on the searchable PSC record at Companies House for each natural-person PSC — full legal name + month + year of date of birth (full day SUPPRESSED — the public sees "June 1975" not "12 June 1975") + nationality + country of residence + service address (registered office of the company OR separately designated service address for the natural person) + nature of control (which PSC test you meet) + date you became a PSC + verified-status flag (yes / no). NOT PUBLICLY VISIBLE per ECCTA s.69 — residential address (where it differs from service address); identifying-document number; biometric photograph; full date of birth day; signed-declaration record; the route used (One Login vs ACSP); any data captured during identity-proofing that is not part of the existing PSC public-register profile. The publication restriction is the OPERATIVE protection that allows you to comply without exposing identity-theft-attractive data to the public.)
8. **What if I don't want my residential address on a public register at all?** (You do not have to — your RESIDENTIAL address is NOT on the public register (it has not been since the Small Business Enterprise and Employment Act 2015 removed residential addresses from the public PSC + director registers). You provide a SERVICE address (typically the registered office of the company you are a PSC of, OR a separately designated service address — a chartered accountant's office, a PO-Box-equivalent service address provider, your solicitor's office) — and the SERVICE address is what is publicly visible. The residential address is privately recorded by Companies House for statutory-correspondence purposes but not published. If you have particular safety concerns (your role brings risk of harm — e.g. you are a domestic-violence survivor, a journalist, or otherwise at risk), additional protection mechanisms are available — Companies House operates a protected-information regime under CA 2006 ss.243-244 + ss.790ZF-790ZG for PSCs at risk. Apply for protection via Companies House published procedure.)
9. **What happens to my personal code if my circumstances change (marriage, name change, address move)?** (The personal code itself is PERMANENT — it does not change when your name changes, when you move house, when you marry or divorce. The UNDERLYING DATA recorded by Companies House is updated separately: a name change is notified via the standard change-of-particulars filing for each company you are a PSC of (PSC04 + related); an address change is notified the same way; a change of marital status is not typically Companies House-relevant. The personal code persists as your unique identifier per ECCTA s.68. If you change your legal name (deed poll, marriage, civil partnership, transgender transition), the verification record at Companies House should be updated to surface the former name + new name to maintain cross-linking with historical records. Stage 2 sub-agent verifies the precise change-of-particulars mechanic at write per §16.35 against Companies House published guidance.)
10. **What are the consequences if I don't verify?** (Three-layer consequence stack per §11.A: (i) **BLOCKED COMPANIES HOUSE FILINGS** — the company you are a PSC of cannot complete its next confirmation statement filing without your personal code recorded against your PSC entry; the company falls into default on its CA 2006 s.853A duty + accrues civil penalty under Sch 1B CA 2006 (up to £5,000 per failure). (ii) **CRIMINAL OFFENCE FOR CONTINUING TO ACT AS A PSC UNVERIFIED** — once the transition window closes (approximately November 2026 for existing PSCs; immediately for new PSC notifications from 18 November 2025), continuing to hold the PSC status without a personal code creates an offence exposure (specific section attributions Stage 2 sub-agent verifies at write per §16.35 — PSC-side offences live in CA 2006 amendments inserted by ECCTA + the new CA 2006 Part 11A). (iii) **PRACTICAL CONSEQUENCES FOR THE COMPANY YOU ARE A PSC OF** — confirmation-statement defaults + striking-off exposure (CA 2006 ss.1000-1011) + reputational damage on the public register. For a PSC of a Family Investment Company or a property SPV, this affects the company + your fellow PSCs + the family / joint-venture structure as a whole. Strong practical recommendation: verify within the transition window; do not let the deadline drift.)
11. **What about Family Investment Companies with multiple share classes — does verification scope shift across share issues?** (Depends on whether the new share-class issue takes you (or any other natural person) across the 25% PSC threshold or removes you from it. Common FIC operational patterns: (a) gift of growth shares to adult-children at FIC inception — the adult-children become PSCs at the point their growth-share class exceeds 25% by number (or other PSC test); (b) subsequent share-issue to a new family member — that family member becomes a PSC if their issue takes them above 25%; (c) share-class reorganisation (e.g. reclassifying growth shares as dividend-access shares for IHT-side restructuring per FA 1986 s.102B / §22.11) — may shift PSC status across classes. Each share-issue or reorganisation requires fresh PSC analysis + PSC02 (change-of-particulars) or PSC03 / PSC04 (ceasing-to-be-PSC + becoming-PSC) filings. If you become a NEW PSC after 18 November 2025 (e.g. you are issued growth shares for the first time that cross the 25% threshold), you must verify identity BEFORE the PSC notification can be filed. If you cease to be a PSC, your existing verification status persists but you simply no longer appear as a PSC of that company on the public register.)
12. **What about corporate-PSCs and the later phase — does my UK SPV with a corporate PSC need to wait?** (NO — natural-person PSCs verify NOW (per the 18 November 2025 + 12-month transition regime); CORPORATE-PSC officer verification comes into scope at a LATER phase scheduled no earlier than November 2026 per the Companies House campaign page. So if your UK SPV has BOTH natural-person PSCs (founder-parents, adult-child growth-shareholders) AND a corporate PSC (a Jersey holding company, a Delaware LLC, an English Limited Partnership, a corporate trustee), the natural-person PSCs verify now + the corporate-PSC officer verification is later-phase. Don't wait. Don't conflate the two scopes. For complex multi-tier structures (UK SPV with a corporate-PSC at one tier + natural-person ultimate-beneficial-owners further up), the natural-person verification for the ultimate-beneficial-owners may already be triggered via the Condition 4 / 5 trust-look-through if those natural persons are PSCs through the look-through chain.)
13. **What about Register of Overseas Entities (RoE) — does my overseas-entity-owned UK property mean separate PSC verification?** (DIFFERENT REGISTERS — DIFFERENT VERIFICATION REGIMES. ECCTA PSC verification covers UK COMPANY PSCs (this page). ### Example 3 — Beneficiary-vs-trustee PSC analysis (the trust-look-through complexity)

Mr Patel (UK-resident; beneficiary of a discretionary family trust ("the Patel Family Trust") established by his late father in 2018; trust deed gives the trustees (Patel's mother + uncle + a professional trust company) broad discretion to distribute income + capital among Patel + his two siblings) faces the question: is Patel a PSC of the UK property-SPV company in which the Patel Family Trust holds 60% of the shares? Analysis: starting point — the TRUSTEES of the Patel Family Trust hold legal title to the 60% shareholding + exercise the voting rights + make discretionary distribution decisions. Under standard analysis, the THREE trustees are the PSCs of the property-SPV company through their indirect-holding under CA 2006 Sch 1A; each verifies identity per s.64. Patel as a beneficiary is NOT a PSC under Condition 1 (he holds no shares); NOT under Condition 2 (he has no voting rights); NOT under Condition 3 (he cannot appoint / remove directors). The remaining question is Condition 4 (significant influence) and Condition 5 (look-through control over the trust that is itself a PSC). Under the trust deed, Patel has no reserved power to direct trustee decisions + no fixed entitlement to income or capital + does not act as a trustee himself. Conclusion: the TRUSTEES are the PSCs (three individuals — Patel's mother, uncle, and a professional trust corporation — each verifying identity individually); Patel is NOT a PSC + does not need to verify. By contrast, if the trust deed had given Patel a fixed life-interest in 50% of trust income + a reserved power to compel trustees to vote in a particular direction on FIC resolutions, Condition 5 would bite + Patel WOULD need to verify alongside the trustees. 1. **Q: What is a "person with significant control" and how do I know if I am one?**
   A: A natural person who meets any one of the five PSC tests under Companies Act 2006 Schedule 1A: (i) more than 25% of the shares; (ii) more than 25% of the voting rights; (iii) right to appoint or remove a majority of directors; (iv) right to exercise or actual exercise of significant influence or control over the company; (v) right to exercise or actual exercise of significant influence or control over the activities of a trust or firm that itself meets Conditions 1-4 (the look-through condition). Most natural-person PSCs cross the line via Condition 1 (25% shareholding) or Condition 2 (25% voting rights). If your accountant or company-secretarial agent has told you that you are a PSC, you are; the PSC analysis was done at the point of incorporation or share-issue and filed at Companies House via the PSC01 first-notification form (or PSC02 / PSC03 if your status changed).

2. **Q: Do I have to verify identity if I'm a PSC but not a director?**
   A: Yes. ECCTA 2023 s.64 applies the verification requirement to natural-person PSCs as well as to natural-person directors. The two cohorts are treated equivalently. You verify ONCE as a natural person + receive ONE personal code that covers your PSC interest regardless of whether you are also a director.

3. **Q: I'm a PSC of multiple UK companies — do I verify multiple times?**
   A: No. ONE NATURAL-PERSON VERIFICATION → ONE PERSONAL CODE → COVERS EVERY UK DIRECTORSHIP + EVERY UK PSC INTEREST YOU WILL EVER HOLD per ECCTA s.68. The code is permanent + persists across all your UK roles. The per-company step is providing the code on each company's next confirmation statement (CS01) or appointment / PSC change filing.

4. **Q: I'm a beneficiary of a discretionary family trust that owns 25%+ of a UK company — am I the PSC, or are the trustees?**
   A: Usually the TRUSTEES are the PSCs because they hold legal title + exercise voting rights + make discretionary distribution decisions. But CA 2006 Sch 1A look-through mechanics (Conditions 4-5) can flip the analysis to a BENEFICIARY where: (a) the beneficiary is also a trustee; (b) the beneficiary has a fixed entitlement to income or capital that meets one of Conditions 1-3; (c) the beneficiary has a reserved power under the trust deed to direct trustee decisions; (d) the beneficiary, as a matter of substance over form, exercises significant influence over how the trustees act. Fact-specific analysis; do it at trust creation + revisit if the structure changes. Cross-link to existing trust + FIC pillar pages.

5. **Q: I have growth shares in a Family Investment Company — am I a PSC?**
   A: Depends on the SUBSTANCE of the growth-share rights, not the label. Standard PSC analysis applies. Growth shares typically carry limited voting + future economic rights only + are issued in volumes that may or may not cross 25%. Each FIC's PSC analysis must be done at incorporation + on each share-issue. If your FIC's company secretary or accountant has filed a PSC01 notifying you as a PSC, you are; if they have not, you are not.

6. **Q: My spouse and I jointly own 30% of a UK company — are we both PSCs?**
   A: Yes. Joint holders of shares are each treated as holding the same percentage for PSC threshold purposes per CA 2006 Sch 1A interpretation. A 30% joint shareholding crosses 25% for EACH joint holder. Both spouses verify identity individually + each receives their own personal code. Civil partners are treated equivalently; co-investor joint holders typically also.

7. **Q: What does the public register show about me + what does it NOT show?**
   A: Per ECCTA s.69 publication restriction + Companies House published display rules — PUBLICLY VISIBLE: full legal name; month + year of birth (full day SUPPRESSED — the public sees "June 1975" not "12 June 1975"); nationality; country of residence; service address; nature of control (which PSC test you meet); date you became a PSC; verified-status flag. NOT PUBLICLY VISIBLE: residential address (where it differs from service address); full date-of-birth day; identifying-document number; biometric photograph; signed-declaration record; the route used (One Login vs ACSP); any verification-proofing data not part of the existing PSC public-register profile. This is the operational protection that allows you to comply without exposing identity-theft-attractive data on the public register.

8. **Q: What if I don't want my residential address on a public register at all?**
   A: Your RESIDENTIAL address is NOT on the public register and has not been since the Small Business Enterprise and Employment Act 2015 removed residential addresses from the public PSC + director registers. You provide a SERVICE address (typically the registered office of the company you are a PSC of OR a separately designated service address such as a chartered accountant's office, a service-address provider, or your solicitor's office) — and the service address is what is publicly visible. The residential address is privately recorded for statutory-correspondence purposes but not published. If you have particular safety concerns (domestic-violence survivor, journalist, otherwise at risk), additional protection is available under CA 2006 ss.243-244 + ss.790ZF-790ZG — apply via Companies House published procedure.

9. **Q: What happens to my personal code if my circumstances change (marriage, name change, address move)?**
   A: The personal code itself is PERMANENT — it does not change when your name changes, when you move house, when you marry or divorce. The underlying data recorded by Companies House is updated separately via PSC04 + related change-of-particulars filings for each company you are a PSC of. The personal code persists as your unique identifier per ECCTA s.68. If you change your legal name (deed poll, marriage, civil partnership, transgender transition), the verification record should be updated to surface the former name + new name to maintain cross-linking with historical records.

10. **Q: What are the consequences if I don't verify?**
    A: Three-layer consequence stack per §11.A. (i) BLOCKED COMPANIES HOUSE FILINGS — the company you are a PSC of cannot complete its next confirmation statement filing without your personal code recorded; the company falls into default on its CA 2006 s.853A duty + accrues civil penalty under Sch 1B CA 2006 (up to £5,000 per failure — verify quantum at write per §16.27). (ii) CRIMINAL OFFENCE FOR CONTINUING TO ACT AS A PSC UNVERIFIED — once the transition window closes (approximately November 2026 for existing PSCs; immediately for new PSC notifications from 18 November 2025), continuing to hold the PSC status without a personal code creates an offence (verify precise section attributions at write per §16.35 — PSC-side offences live in CA 2006 amendments inserted by ECCTA + the new CA 2006 Part 11A). (iii) PRACTICAL CONSEQUENCES FOR THE COMPANY YOU ARE A PSC OF — confirmation-statement defaults + striking-off exposure (CA 2006 ss.1000-1011) + reputational damage on the public register. Strong recommendation: verify within the transition window; do not let the deadline drift.

11. **Q: What if I'm a PSC of a Family Investment Company with multiple share classes — does verification scope shift across share issues?**
    A: Depends on whether the new share-class issue takes you (or any other natural person) across the 25% PSC threshold or removes you from it. Common FIC patterns: (a) gift of growth shares to adult-children at FIC inception — the adult-children become PSCs at the point their growth-share holding crosses 25% by an applicable PSC test; (b) subsequent share-issue to a new family member — that family member becomes a PSC if their issue takes them above 25%; (c) share-class reorganisation (e.g. reclassifying growth shares as dividend-access shares for IHT-side restructuring per FA 1986 s.102B) — may shift PSC status across classes. Each share-issue / reorganisation requires fresh PSC analysis + PSC02 / PSC03 / PSC04 filings. If you become a NEW PSC after 18 November 2025, you must verify identity BEFORE the PSC notification can be filed.

12. **Q: What about corporate-PSCs and the later phase — does my UK SPV with a corporate PSC need to wait?**
    A: No. Natural-person PSCs verify NOW (under the 18 November 2025 + 12-month transition regime); CORPORATE-PSC officer verification comes into scope at a LATER PHASE scheduled no earlier than November 2026 per the Companies House campaign page. So if your UK SPV has BOTH natural-person PSCs (founder-parents, adult-child growth-shareholders) AND a corporate PSC (a Jersey holding company, a Delaware LLC, an English Limited Partnership, a corporate trustee), the natural-person PSCs verify now + the corporate-PSC officer verification is later-phase. Don't conflate the two scopes.

13. **Q: What about Register of Overseas Entities (RoE) — does my overseas-entity-owned UK property mean separate PSC verification?**
    A: Different registers, different verification regimes. ECCTA PSC verification covers UK COMPANY PSCs (this page). RoE covers OVERSEAS-ENTITY BENEFICIAL OWNERS who own UK property through a non-UK legal entity (BVI, Jersey, Guernsey, Cayman, Delaware LLC), under ECTEA 2022 — covered by B13 `consequences-for-roe-non-compliance` (M2-B-B3) and existing `register-of-overseas-entities-roe-annual-update-statement-non-resident-landlords`. If you are a beneficial owner of an overseas entity that owns UK property, the RoE verification regime applies to YOU through the overseas-entity's annual update statement (separate UK-regulated agent verification + separate annual update obligation). If you are ALSO a PSC of a UK company, the ECCTA PSC verification applies separately. Each regime has its own verification mechanic.

14. **Q: I'm a US-resident PSC — does that change anything?**
    A: Same regime applies — US-resident PSCs of UK companies must verify identity per ECCTA s.64 regardless of country of residence. The practical mechanic typically favours the ACSP route (rather than GOV.UK One Login, which is optimised for UK-resident users with UK-issued identity documents). Cross-link to B18 `how-us-based-directors-of-uk-companies-can-navigate-ecctas-new-identity-verification-rules` for the full US-resident operational walkthrough including ACSP route + US identity-document acceptance + the four-way US/UK disclosure overlap (FATCA + Form 5471 + FBAR + ECCTA).

15. **Q: How does this look operationally — what does "the form" actually involve?**
    A: There is no numbered Companies House paper or PDF form. Verification is delivered electronically through (a) the GOV.UK One Login service (free self-service web + mobile flow at `https://signin.account.gov.uk/` — interactive document scan, biometric face-match, security questions; 5-15 minutes typical) OR (b) the Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP) route (your accountant or company-secretarial agent verifies your identity using its KYC platform under MLR 2017 supervision; £50-£300 one-off; 1-5 business days). Cross-link to B19 `identity-verification-form` for the form-mechanic screen-by-screen depth.

16. **Q: What is my next step — what should I actually do?**
    A: Five-step operational sequence: (i) confirm with the company secretary or accountant of every UK company you are a PSC of that you are notified as a PSC + their record of your notification status; (ii) determine your route preference — GOV.UK One Login (free, self-service, 5-15 minutes typical, requires UK passport or photo driving licence + smartphone + active One Login account) vs ACSP (£50-£300 fee, accountant-mediated, useful for less-tech-comfortable readers + non-UK-residents + multi-trust complexity); (iii) gather documents — passport / driving licence / BRP / BRC / Frontier Worker permit; address-of-residence anchor (utility bill, bank statement); (iv) complete verification — One Login OR engagement with chosen ACSP; (v) record the personal code in personal records + provide to the company secretary / accountant of every UK company you are a PSC of, who will then quote the code on the next CS01 confirmation statement for each. If you also hold UK directorships, the same personal code covers those.
