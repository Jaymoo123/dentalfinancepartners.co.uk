# LLP Accounts: The Operational Accounts and Filing Handbook for Property LLPs (2026/27)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (the LLP entity statute):** Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2000 (LLPA 2000) — establishes the LLP as a body corporate (s.1(2)); members liable only to the extent agreed in the LLP agreement / LLPA 2000 framework (s.1(5)); registered at Companies House under same operational system as companies (ss.2-3). Cross-reference future MW2 Bucket A LLP pillar (`llp-and-taxation-benefits` this batch + the existing `llp-property-investment-worth-considering`); this page does NOT restate LLP entity framework — it stands on it.
- **Primary (the load-bearing accounts SI — the operative statutory layer):** Limited Liability Partnerships (Accounts and Audit) (Application of Companies Act 2006) Regulations 2008 (SI 2008/1911) — extends CA 2006 Part 15 accounts framework to LLPs with LLP-specific modifications. **Operative architecture:**
  - **Reg 3 + Sch 1:** applies CA 2006 Part 15 (ss.380-474) accounts framework to LLPs with the specific modifications in Sch 1.
  - **Reg 4:** application of CA 2006 Part 16 audit framework with modifications.
  - **Sch 1 modifications:** terminology adapted (members instead of shareholders / directors; designated members carry director-equivalent responsibilities; LLP agreement instead of articles); accounts must be prepared in accordance with relevant accounting standards (FRS 105 / FRS 102 + SORP — see below); accounts approval + signing by designated members; small-LLP + micro-LLP size thresholds mirror small-company + micro-company thresholds with adjustments.
  - **SI 2008/1911 superseded individual provisions** — Stage 2 verifies any post-ECCTA-2023 amendments at write time (LLP-side ECCTA changes were phased separately from companies-side; verify current SI consolidated text).
- **Primary (the LLP SORP — the load-bearing GAAP overlay):** SORP "Accounting by Limited Liability Partnerships" — published by CCAB (Consultative Committee of Accountancy Bodies), most recent revision 2025 supersedes 2018 revision (Stage 2 verifies current SORP version at write time). **Operative function:** the SORP sets out LLP-specific accounting treatments that adapt FRS 102 + FRS 105 to the LLP entity-form, particularly for members' interests. **Critical SORP topics:**
  - **Members' remuneration:** the split between (a) remuneration treated as an EXPENSE of the LLP (charged before profit; deducted in arriving at profit for the year) and (b) remuneration treated as an APPROPRIATION of profit (allocated post-profit; treated as distribution).
  - **Members' capital classification:** between (a) EQUITY (members' interests classified as equity per FRS 102 Section 22 substance test — non-puttable, no contractual obligation to repay) and (b) LIABILITY (members' interests classified as financial liability where puttable on retirement or where contractual obligation to repay exists). The classification has cascade implications for the balance-sheet presentation and for whether members' remuneration becomes interest-expense (on liability-classified balances) or appropriation (on equity-classified balances).
  - **Loans and other debts due to members:** specific LLP balance-sheet line (CA 2006 Sch 1 + SI 2008/1911 modifications) — separate disclosure required.
  - **Members' agreement disclosure:** key terms of the agreement governing remuneration + capital + distribution arrangements disclosed in accounts; particularly relevant where members' interests are equity-classified (the contractual terms determine the equity-vs-liability test).
  - **Provisions, post-retirement benefits, deferred members' remuneration** — specific SORP treatments.
- **Primary (the FRS standards selection — the day-one accounting-standards decision):**
  - **FRS 105 (Micro-entities):** available to LLPs meeting micro thresholds (CA 2006 s.384A applied by SI 2008/1911 — turnover ≤ £632k, balance sheet total ≤ £316k, ≤ 10 employees; Stage 2 verifies post-FRC threshold updates). Operative for small property-investment LLPs prioritising filing simplicity; no fair-value option; investment property at historical cost less depreciation.
  - **FRS 102 (UK GAAP — Sections 1A small + full):** available to all LLPs; Section 1A "Small Entities" provides reduced disclosures for small LLPs (small thresholds per s.382-383 CA 2006 applied via SI). Section 16 (Investment Property — fair value model) is available to LLPs and is **the common election for property-investment LLPs where investor / lender / member visibility on NAV matters**.
  - **FRS 102 Section 22 (Liabilities and Equity)** is load-bearing for the equity-vs-liability classification of members' interests — sets out the substance-over-form test that the LLP SORP then operationalises with LLP-specific guidance.
  - **EU-adopted IFRS:** available to LLPs but rare; reserved for LLPs in PIE category or with specific reasons.
- **Primary (designated members + accounts responsibility):** LLPA 2000 ss.6-9 — designated members carry director-equivalent statutory accounts responsibilities. **Operative for LLP accounts:** at least 2 designated members required (LLPA 2000 s.8) — if all members are designated by default in the LLP agreement, all carry the responsibility. The accounts-approval + accounts-signing + filing obligations sit with designated members. Failure to file on time creates designated-member personal exposure under CA 2006 ss.451-453 (applied by SI 2008/1911) — including criminal liability for repeated default + civil penalty regime.
- **Primary (accounts content + format under SI 2008/1911 applying CA 2006 Part 15):**
  - **Balance sheet (LLP-modified format):** specific lines for "Loans and other debts due to members" and "Members' other interests" — distinct from a company balance sheet. Members' interests can be equity or liability per the FRS 102 Section 22 + LLP SORP test.
  - **Profit + loss account:** must distinguish profit available for division among members from members' remuneration charged as an expense (the SORP-driven split).
  - **Notes to the accounts:** disclosure of LLP agreement terms relevant to members' interests classification; disclosure of members' interests by class; disclosure of remuneration policy and members' remuneration charged as an expense + as appropriation.
  - **Statement of changes in equity (or members' interests):** reconciliation of opening to closing members' equity-classified interests with movements (new admission, retirement, profit allocation, drawings, revaluation reserve, etc).
- **Primary (filing windows + Companies House submission):** SI 2008/1911 applies CA 2006 ss.441-444 — accounts filing within 9 months of end of accounting reference period (private LLPs); first accounts within 21 months of incorporation. **Operative for small / micro LLPs:** abridged accounts (CA 2006 s.444 applied by SI) and filleted accounts (omitting P&L from public filing — s.444 + s.444A applied with modifications) available; companies-house-side filing fees waived (free filing). **Post-ECCTA operational layer:** LLP-side ECCTA implementation phased separately from companies-side per F-1 §11.B (proposed); ID verification framework + ACSP + registered email + appropriate registered office requirements extend to LLPs with LLP-specific operational rollout dates (Stage 2 verifies current LLP rollout state at write time per §11.A F-12 campaign-page reference).
- **Primary (audit thresholds — applying CA 2006 s.477 + s.479 audit exemption via SI 2008/1911):** small LLPs may take audit exemption if meeting size thresholds (turnover ≤ £10.2m, balance sheet ≤ £5.1m, ≤ 50 employees — meeting 2 of 3 in current and prior year; Stage 2 verifies current FRC-aligned thresholds). **Operative gating:** exemption denied where (a) members holding ≥10% by number or value require audit per CA 2006 s.476 (applied by SI), or (b) the LLP is part of a group where consolidation triggers audit, or (c) the LLP is in a regulated sector (financial-services LLPs, etc).
- **Primary (the property-investment LLP overlay):**
  - **Investment property accounts treatment under FRS 102 Section 16:** fair-value model commonly elected for property-investment LLPs; revaluation gains/losses recognised in P&L; **NO deferred tax provision required at LLP level** because the LLP itself is tax-transparent (members taxed individually; deferred tax considered at member level if at all). Contrast with LtdCo position (FRS 102 Section 29 deferred tax provision required on revaluation gains within the company) — this is one of the operational accounting advantages of LLP vs LtdCo for fair-value reporting.
  - **Members' remuneration vs property-income allocation:** members' shares of LLP property income are allocated per the LLP agreement; allocation is for tax-transparency purposes (each member taxed on their share per ITTOIA 2005 + Part 9 framework for LLPs taxed as partnerships per ITTOIA 2005 s.863); the accounts split between "remuneration as expense" and "remuneration as appropriation" reflects the GAAP treatment, NOT the tax treatment (which always remains transparent).
- **House position reference:** §21 (Ltd Co + FIC family — comparison anchor for LLP vs LtdCo accounts treatments); §11 + §11.A (Companies House + ECCTA — LLPs subject to phased rollout of ECCTA changes per F-1 §11.B proposed). **HP gap:** LLP-specific accounts / SORP architecture is not currently locked.
