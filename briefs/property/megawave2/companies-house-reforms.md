### Example 2 — A landlord LtdCo's quarter-by-quarter compliance roadmap

- **Q1 (Dec 2025 / Jan 2026):** inventory of every UK company in the portfolio + every natural person across directorships and PSC roles. Book One Login or ACSP verification for every natural person (typically 1-3 people across a 5-15 SPV portfolio).
- **Q1-Q2 (Jan-Mar 2026):** at each SPV, confirm (a) registered email is in place + active, (b) registered office meets the appropriate-address test (reroute any PO-box-only addresses).
- **Q2-Q3 (rolling on each SPV's confirmation statement filing date):** file confirmation statement with verified personal codes + lawful purposes statement + registered email + appropriate-address registered office. Fee £50 online per SPV (verify at write).
- **Q3-Q4 (ongoing):** monitor trigger events — new appointments (require pre-verification of incoming director), changes of registered office or email, RoE updating statements if applicable.
- **Cost:** typically £0 (One Login route) per natural person + £0-£300/year per SPV for any appropriate-address-compliant registered office reroute + £50/year per SPV confirmation statement fee. Time: 8-15 hours of director / accountant time across the year for a 5-SPV portfolio.

### Example 3 — The multi-SPV per-natural-person reveal

- **Singh portfolio:** Mr Singh is director and 100% PSC of five SPVs. One ID verification under ECCTA s.65 + s.68 = one personal code = covers all five companies. Same code quoted at each SPV's next confirmation statement.
- **Operative insight:** the per-natural-person mechanic is the single most operationally useful fact for multi-SPV BTL portfolios. Verification cost (£0 One Login / £20-£100 ACSP) is once per person, not once per company.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target — umbrella coverage)

1. **Q: What is the complete list of Companies House reforms?**
   A: Nine operative reforms in date order: 1 August 2022 RoE in force (ECTEA 2022); 1 March 2024 ECTEA amendments via ECCTA Part 3 in force; 4 March 2024 registered email + appropriate-address rule + confirmation statement fee increase to £50/£110 (ECCTA ss.28-30); 5 March 2024 lawful purposes statement on confirmation statement (ECCTA ss.59-63); 8 April 2025 voluntary ID verification opens; 18 November 2025 ID verification becomes legal requirement for new directors and PSCs + 12-month transition window opens for existing roles (ECCTA ss.40-45 + s.64 + s.65 + s.68); ~November 2026 transition window closes; no earlier than November 2026 later phase for filers + corporate-officer scenarios; phased rollout of Limited Partnership reforms under ECCTA Part 2.

2. **Q: Why have these reforms happened — what is the policy driver?**
   A: ECCTA 2023 s.1 (CA 2006 inserted-s.1081A) re-cast Companies House from a passive register to an active gatekeeper of register integrity, with four statutory objectives: properly-delivered documents, accurate information, complete register, prevention of unlawful-activity facilitation. The post-Russian-sanctions + post-Pandora-Papers economic-crime policy environment surfaced the gap between the UK's open business register (low-friction, light-touch) and the demand for register integrity (counterparty due diligence, sanctions enforcement, beneficial-ownership transparency). ECTEA 2022 addressed the overseas-entity gap; ECCTA 2023 addressed the domestic-company gap.

3. **Q: What did the 4 March 2024 commencement change?**
   A: Three operational changes in one block under ECCTA ss.28-30 + Companies (Registration etc) Regulations 2024 (RUN session verifies SI number at write). (i) Registered email address required for new companies at incorporation + for existing companies at the next confirmation statement (CA 2006 inserted-s.88A). (ii) Registered office must be an "appropriate address" capable of acknowledging service of documents (PO boxes generally do not qualify). (iii) Confirmation statement filing fee increased to £50 online / £110 paper (from a previous lower band).

4. **Q: What did the 5 March 2024 commencement add?**
   A: The lawful purposes statement on the confirmation statement under ECCTA ss.59-63 — a positive declaration that the company's intended future activities are lawful. Re-stated annually on each confirmation statement (not a one-off declaration).

5. **Q: What did the 18 November 2025 commencement bring in?**
   A: ID verification became a legal requirement for newly appointed directors and PSCs under ECCTA ss.40-45 + s.64 + s.65 + s.68. The same date opened a 12-month transition window for existing directors and PSCs to verify by their next confirmation statement filed within the window ending around November 2026. Verification is per natural person (one personal code covers every directorship + PSC interest across every UK company).

6. **Q: What is the ACSP framework and how does it sit alongside GOV.UK One Login?**
   A: Authorised Corporate Service Provider regime under ECCTA s.66 — AML-supervised firms (under MLR 2017) that have separately registered with Companies House to perform identity verification on clients' behalf. Most established UK accountants are AML-supervised already (HMRC, ICAEW, ACCA, ICAS, CIOT); the ACSP application is procedural. Direct route via GOV.UK One Login is free + self-service; ACSP route via accountant or company-secretary firm typically £20-£100 per natural person (verify at write). One-natural-person-one-personal-code rule per ECCTA s.68 applies regardless of route.

7. **Q: What happened to local PSC registers — were they abolished?**
   A: Yes. ECCTA ss.51-52 abolished the local registers companies previously had to maintain for directors, secretaries, and persons with significant control. All such information now consolidates at the Companies House central register. Companies no longer maintain separate local register copies at their registered office. Operational implication for landlord LtdCo portfolios: no more local PSC register at the registered office; rely on Companies House Find and update company information service.

8. **Q: What does the Register of Overseas Entities require — and why does it matter for landlord LtdCos?**
   A: RoE was created by ECTEA 2022 c. 10 (NOT ECCTA 2023 — common misframing). Overseas entities holding UK property must register at Companies House and disclose beneficial owners; annual updating statement due within 14 days of the end of each update period (per ECTEA 2022 s.7; NOT 14 days of registration anniversary — the operative clock is the update period). HMLR disposition-block under LRA 2002 Sch 4A (inserted by ECTEA Sch 3) blocks non-compliant overseas entities from dispositions of registered UK land. Landlord LtdCo implication: typically NOT directly applicable to UK-incorporated landlord LtdCos, BUT relevant where a landlord uses an overseas-entity vehicle (Jersey, BVI, Isle of Man holding companies of UK land), or where a UK landlord LtdCo deals with an overseas-entity counterparty.

9. **Q: What is happening with Limited Partnerships under ECCTA Part 2?**
   A: ECCTA Part 2 brings LPs registered under LPA 1907 into a CA-2006-style regime: registered office, annual confirmation, general partner identity verification via the s.66 ACSP regime, striking-off powers, information-disclosure obligations. Phased rollout — operational state moves; RUN session verifies against the campaign page at write per §11.B candidate flag. Landlord implication: applies to property-fund LPs (English LP widely used in real-estate fund finance), JV-development LPs, family-investment LPs holding tenanted property.

10. **Q: What is Schedule 12 of ECCTA — does it amend RoE?**
    A: No. Schedule 12 is "Criminal liability of bodies: economic crimes" — failure-to-prevent fraud (a Part 5 matter). It does NOT amend ECTEA 2022 or the Register of Overseas Entities. This is a common misframing — RoE primary anchor is ECTEA 2022 c. 10, amended in places by ECCTA Part 3 but not Sch 12.

11. **Q: What should a landlord LtdCo portfolio do this quarter to stay compliant?**
    A: Seven-step checklist. (a) confirm registered email address is in place + active on every SPV. (b) confirm registered office is an appropriate address on every SPV. (c) ensure the lawful purposes statement is filed at the next confirmation statement. (d) identify every director and PSC across the portfolio (per natural person) + book each one's ID verification via GOV.UK One Login or ACSP. (e) quote each verified personal code at each subsequent filing. (f) if any portfolio vehicle is an overseas entity holding UK land, confirm RoE registration is current. (g) if any portfolio vehicle is an LP under LPA 1907, monitor ECCTA Part 2 phased rollout at the campaign page.

12. **Q: How does ECCTA reach my BTL lender relationship?**
    A: Lender underwriters now read your Companies House file as part of refinance and credit-committee review. Common checkpoints: appropriate-address registered office (PO-box flags risk); registered email in place; lawful purposes statement current; verified personal codes for every director + PSC on most recent filing; confirmation statement filed on time. A clean current file reduces friction at term-sheet and credit-committee stages. Sibling page `companies-house-reforms-explained-for-business-success` covers the lender / counterparty due-diligence angle in depth.

13. **Q: How much will all of this cost me each year?**
    A: For a typical 5-SPV portfolio with 1-2 natural persons across the directorships and PSC roles: One Login verification £0 (self-service) or ACSP £20-£100 per person (verify at write); confirmation statement fee £50 online × 5 SPVs = £250/year (verify at write); registered office reroute £0 (if accountant office or director residential address) to £50-£300/year per SPV (if using a staffed virtual office provider). Time cost is the bigger variable: 8-15 hours/year of director / accountant time across the portfolio.

14. **Q: Where do I check the operative state?**
    A: Two canonical sources per §11.A. **Primary tracker:** Companies House campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` with topic sub-pages (`/identity-verification/`, `/authorised-corporate-service-providers/`, `/confirmation-statement-changes/`, `/changes-to-company-registers/`). **Secondary tracker:** Companies House blog `https://companieshouse.blog.gov.uk/` for operational announcements + phase-rollout posts.
