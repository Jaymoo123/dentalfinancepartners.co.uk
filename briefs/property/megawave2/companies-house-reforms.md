# Companies House Reforms: The Complete Taxonomy of Every ECCTA 2023 Change and What It Means for Landlord LtdCos

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 c. 56 ("ECCTA 2023"). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/56/contents on 2026-05-26 (sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35). **§11.A Wave 9 mini-lock corrects earlier framings — ECCTA Part 1 is organised as 23 sections under crossheadings (no Chapter structure), Schedule 12 is "Criminal liability of bodies: economic crimes" and does NOT amend ECTEA 2022, RoE primary anchor is ECTEA 2022 c. 10 not ECCTA.**
  - **ECCTA Part 1 — Companies** (organised by section-grouped crossheadings — verified verbatim per §11.A):
    - **s.1** — Registrar's objectives (CA 2006 amendment inserting new s.1081A statement of objectives).
    - **ss.2-7** — Company formation: identity verification at incorporation; disqualification checks.
    - **ss.8-22** — Company names: restrictions, change powers, enforcement.
    - **ss.23-27** — Business names.
    - **ss.28-30** — Registered offices + registered email addresses (CA 2006 inserted-s.88A and following).
    - **ss.31-39** — Disqualification regime updates.
    - **ss.40-45** — Directors (includes ID verification mechanics for new + existing directors).
    - **ss.46-50** — Register of members (private register reforms).
    - **ss.51-52** — Abolition of local registers for directors, secretaries, and PSCs (consolidates at Companies House central register).
    - **ss.59-63** — Confirmation statements + lawful purposes notification.
    - **ECCTA Identity-verification crossheading (s.64-69)** — ID-verification regime architecture per §11.A: s.64 PSC verification, s.65 procedure, s.66 ACSP authorisation, s.67 national-security exemption, s.68 unique identifiers (personal code), s.69 publication restriction.
  - **ECCTA Part 2 — Limited Partnerships** (LP reforms — separate from Part 1 companies-side, F-1 candidate §11.B for full lock):
    - Brings limited partnerships under LPA 1907 into a CA-2006-style regime: registered office, annual confirmation, GP ID verification routing through s.66 ACSP regime, striking-off powers, information-disclosure obligations. **Stage 2 sub-agent treats Part 2 as in-progress phased rollout — verifies at the campaign page at write per §11.B candidate F-1 + §11.A F-12.**
  - **ECCTA Part 3** — amendments to ECTEA 2022 / Register of Overseas Entities (see ECTEA 2022 c. 10 below).
  - **ECCTA Part 5** — failure-to-prevent fraud + criminal liability of bodies (Schedule 12).
- Supporting (RoE anchor): **Economic Crime (Transparency and Enforcement) Act 2022 c. 10 ("ECTEA 2022")** — primary statute for the Register of Overseas Entities; amended by ECCTA 2023 Part 3 but the 2022 Act remains the operative anchor. §11.A locks the verbatim section attributions: s.4 (application for registration), s.7 (updating duty + 14-day window from end of update period — NOT registration anniversary), s.8 (failure-to-comply criminal offence — NOT removal from register, that is s.9), s.34 (compulsory-registration-notice power for unregistered overseas entities owning UK land), s.36 (definitional — meaning of "daily default fine"), plus LRA 2002 Sch 4A (HMLR disposition-block — the actual anchor for the HMLR consequence; inserted by ECTEA 2022 Sch 3).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is the complete inventory of Companies House reforms in date order?** (Provide the date-ordered table: 1 August 2022 RoE in force; 1 March 2024 ECTEA 2022 amendments in force via ECCTA Part 3; 4 March 2024 registered email + appropriate address rule for new companies; 5 March 2024 lawful purposes statement on confirmation statement; 8 April 2025 voluntary ID verification opens; 18 November 2025 ID verification becomes legal requirement for new directors and PSCs + 12-month transition window opens for existing; ~November 2026 transition window closes; no earlier than November 2026 separate later phase for filers and corporate-officer scenarios; LP Part 2 reforms phased separately. Each row has an in-force date, the statutory anchor, and a one-line operational summary.)
2. **What did the 4 March 2024 commencement actually change?** (Three operational changes in one block: (a) registered email address required for new companies at incorporation and for existing companies at the next confirmation statement filing — CA 2006 inserted-s.88A and following; (b) registered office must be an "appropriate address" capable of acknowledging service of documents — PO boxes do not qualify; (c) the confirmation statement filing fee increased to £50 online / £110 paper. Cross-link to existing confirmation statement page for the operational walkthrough.)
3. **What did the 5 March 2024 commencement add?** (Lawful purposes statement on the confirmation statement: a positive declaration that the company's intended future activities will be lawful. Re-stated annually on each confirmation statement (not a one-off declaration). Cross-link to existing confirmation statement page.)
4. **What did the 18 November 2025 commencement bring in?** (ID verification becomes a legal requirement for newly appointed directors and PSCs; 12-month transition window opens for existing directors and PSCs to verify by their next confirmation statement filed within the window ending ~November 2026. Cross-link to B2 (definitional), B6 (emails / scam-vetting), B7 (commencement-day in-depth) and the existing operational walkthrough.)
5. **What is the ACSP framework and how does it sit alongside GOV.UK One Login?** (Authorised Corporate Service Provider regime under ECCTA s.66: AML-supervised firms (under MLR 2017) that have separately registered with Companies House to perform identity verification on clients' behalf. Most established UK accountants are AML-supervised already (HMRC, ICAEW, ACCA, ICAS, CIOT); the ACSP application is procedural. Direct route via GOV.UK One Login (free, self-service); ACSP route via accountant or company-secretary firm (typically £20-£100 fee). One-natural-person-one-personal-code per ECCTA s.68 applies regardless of route.)
6. **What happened to local PSC registers — were they abolished?** (Yes. ECCTA ss.51-52 abolished local registers for directors, secretaries, and PSCs. All such information consolidates at the Companies House central register. Companies no longer maintain separate local register copies. Operational implication for landlord LtdCo portfolios: no more local PSC register at the registered office; rely on Companies House WebCheck / Find and update company information service.)
7. **What does the Register of Overseas Entities require — and why does it matter for landlord LtdCos?** (RoE was created by ECTEA 2022 (NOT ECCTA 2023 — common misframing). Overseas entities holding UK property must register with Companies House and disclose beneficial owners; annual updating statement due within 14 days of the end of each update period (NOT 14 days of registration anniversary — the operative clock is the update period, defined separately in s.7); HMLR disposition-block under LRA 2002 Sch 4A (inserted by ECTEA Sch 3) blocks non-compliant overseas entities from dispositions of registered UK land. Landlord LtdCo implication: typically NOT directly applicable to UK-incorporated landlord LtdCos, BUT relevant where a landlord uses an overseas-entity vehicle (Jersey / BVI / Isle of Man holding companies of UK land), or where a UK landlord LtdCo deals with an overseas-entity counterparty.)
8. **What is happening with Limited Partnerships under ECCTA Part 2?** (ECCTA Part 2 brings LPs registered under LPA 1907 into a CA-2006-style regime: registered office, annual confirmation statement, GP identity verification via the s.66 ACSP regime, striking-off powers, information-disclosure obligations. Phased rollout — operational state moves; verify against the campaign page at write per §11.B candidate. Landlord implication: applies to property-fund LPs (English limited partnership widely used in real-estate fund finance), JV-development LPs, family-investment LPs holding tenanted property.)
9. **What is Schedule 12 of ECCTA — does it amend RoE?** (No. Schedule 12 is "Criminal liability of bodies: economic crimes" — failure-to-prevent fraud (a Part 5 matter). It does NOT amend ECTEA 2022 or the Register of Overseas Entities. This is a common misframing — RoE primary anchor is ECTEA 2022 c. 10, amended in places by ECCTA Part 3 but not Sch 12.)
10. **What should a landlord LtdCo portfolio do this quarter to stay compliant?** (Practical checklist: (a) confirm registered email address is in place and active (every UK company); (b) confirm registered office is an appropriate address (an accountant's office or a staffed virtual office typically qualifies; a pure PO-box mail-forwarder typically does not); (c) ensure the lawful purposes statement is filed at the next confirmation statement; (d) identify every director and PSC across the portfolio (per natural person), book each one's identity verification — direct via GOV.UK One Login OR via your accountant if an ACSP — well before the next confirmation statement deadline within the 12-month transition window; (e) quote each verified personal code at each subsequent filing; (f) if any portfolio vehicle is an overseas entity holding UK land, confirm RoE registration is current and the updating statement is filed within 14 days of the end of each update period; (g) if any portfolio vehicle is an LP under LPA 1907, monitor ECCTA Part 2 phased rollout at the campaign page. Cross-link to each per-reform deep dive.)
11. **Why have these reforms happened — what is the policy driver?** (One-paragraph framing: the post-Russian-sanctions + post-Pandora-Papers economic-crime policy environment led to ECTEA 2022 (RoE) and ECCTA 2023 (companies-side + LP-side + failure-to-prevent-fraud). The Registrar's new objectives at s.1 (CA 2006 inserted-s.1081A) re-cast Companies House from a passive register to an active gatekeeper of register integrity. ID verification, lawful purposes statement, appropriate address rule, abolition of local registers, ACSP framework, RoE — each is a brick in the gatekeeper wall. Landlord LtdCos are not the policy target, but the compliance overlay applies the same way to every UK company.)
12. **Where do I check the operative state — what is the canonical source?** (Two trackers per §11.A: primary campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` with per-topic sub-pages (/identity-verification/, /authorised-corporate-service-providers/, /confirmation-statement-changes/, /changes-to-company-registers/); secondary Companies House blog `https://companieshouse.blog.gov.uk/` for operational announcements + phase-rollout posts. DO NOT use the deprecated `gov.uk/government/news/changes-to-uk-company-law` URL — returns HTTP 404 per F-12.)
