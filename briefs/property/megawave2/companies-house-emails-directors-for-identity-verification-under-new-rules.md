## Key questions this page must answer

1. **Is this email genuine — how can I authenticate a Companies House communication?** (Legitimate CH emails will (a) come from a verified gov.uk-domain sender (typically `@companieshouse.gov.uk` or `@notifications.service.gov.uk`); (b) reference your specific company by name + company number; (c) NEVER ask for password / financial details / direct payment via emailed link; (d) direct the recipient to log in at the genuine GOV.UK / Companies House portal — NOT to a third-party look-alike URL; (e) align with what is currently being communicated on the official Companies House campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` + Companies House blog `https://companieshouse.blog.gov.uk/`. Scam-vetting checks: hover over links to reveal true URL; check sender domain in header detail; cross-reference with the campaign page; if uncertain, contact Companies House directly via the published phone number — never via a phone number in the email itself. Report suspected scams to Action Fraud (verify URL at write).)
2. **What does the email actually require — what action does it ask me to take?** (Genuine ID-verification-related CH emails typically (a) inform the recipient of the new ID-verification requirement under ECCTA 2023; (b) reference the recipient's role (director / PSC / both) at the company; (c) indicate the deadline for verification (anchored to the company's next confirmation statement within the transition window, or earlier for newly appointed roles); (d) direct the recipient to begin verification via GOV.UK One Login OR via an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP) under ECCTA s.66; (e) provide the recipient with their confirmation-statement filing date for reference. No payment is required to verify (CH does not charge for One Login route). Acting via the genuine portal is the response.)
3. **How fast do I need to act?** (Deadline depends on the recipient's role and the company's filing cycle. **Newly appointed directors / PSCs** — verification before the role can be recorded on Companies House (effectively immediate on appointment, since the appointment filing requires the personal code). **Existing directors / PSCs** — verification due by the company's next confirmation statement filed within the 12-month transition window (which began 18 November 2025 and ends around November 2026; verify against §11.A + campaign page at write). Where the confirmation statement is filed late, verification is still required by that filing date. Operationally: don't wait until the confirmation-statement deadline. The verification process takes 10-30 minutes via One Login for typical biometric-passport holders; can be slower if document checks need additional review.)
4. **What if I ignore the email?** (Continuing to act as a director or PSC of a UK company without being verified by the applicable deadline is a criminal offence under the ECCTA 2023 framework (verify precise section reference at write per §16.35). The company itself is also exposed: Companies House can refuse to accept the company's confirmation statement filing without verified personal codes for all directors and PSCs; failure to file a confirmation statement is itself an offence under CA 2006 ss.853A-G as amended by ECCTA. Individual sanctions include fines; cumulative non-compliance can lead to disqualification proceedings. The operational page covers sanctions in depth; this B6 page surfaces the headline urgency.)
5. **What is the verification process — what do I actually do?** (Process summary (full walkthrough on the operational page): (a) gather identity documents — biometric passport is cleanest, alternatives include UK photo driving licence, UK biometric residence permit, UK Frontier Worker permit; (b) choose route — GOV.UK One Login (self-service, no fee) OR Authorised Corporate Service Provider (your AML-registered accountant); (c) complete verification (10-30 minutes for typical passport-holder via One Login app); (d) receive Companies House personal code under ECCTA s.68; (e) quote the personal code in subsequent CH filings (next confirmation statement, appointment notifications, change-of-particulars filings). The personal code is per-natural-person — one verification covers all your directorships and PSC interests across every UK company.)
6. **Should I use GOV.UK One Login or an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)?** (Decision: One Login = self-service, free, suitable for tech-comfortable directors with valid biometric documents and active One Login accounts. ACSP = your accountant or company-secretarial firm does it, suitable where the accountant already holds AML-verified KYC documents, or for directors without active One Login accounts. Cost: One Login is free; ACSPs typically charge a small fee (£20-£100 range — verify at write). Sibling MW2 B-pages on ACSPs go deeper. The operational page covers the route-choice decision in depth.)
7. **What if I have multiple directorships across several SPVs (typical landlord BTL portfolio)?** (Verification is per natural person, NOT per company. One verification = one Companies House personal code = covers every directorship and PSC interest you hold across every UK company. So a landlord director of five SPVs verifies ONCE; the personal code is then quoted at each SPV's next confirmation statement. Filing-side admin per company; verification-side once per person. This is the single most useful operational fact for landlord-LtdCo portfolios. Cross-link to the operational page for the per-company filing cadence walkthrough.)
8. **What if I'm overseas and can't easily visit a UK Post Office or in-person ID check?** (Overseas directors of UK companies verify the same way via GOV.UK One Login app using a biometric passport (any country, in date) — no UK visit required. The One Login app uses phone camera for biometric document check and selfie match; works internationally. ACSP route also available if your UK accountant can verify your KYC documents in their AML compliance file remotely. Sibling MW2 B-page `how-us-based-directors-of-uk-companies-can-navigate-ecctas-new-identity-verification-rules` covers the overseas-director angle in depth.)
9. **What if the email refers to my company but the role description is wrong?** (Companies House may email based on its register snapshot at a recent date. If the role description (e.g. "you are listed as a director of ABC Ltd") is wrong — director has resigned but filing not made; PSC interest changed but not updated; etc. — this is itself a register-accuracy issue that needs correcting. Action: log in to Companies House via the genuine portal and review the current register; file the appropriate change-of-particulars or termination filing; verify (or not, depending on current state) accordingly. Cross-link to sibling Companies House pages on filing updates.)
10. **What's the broader regime — what other ECCTA reforms should I be aware of?** (Headline parallel reforms (each covered in sibling pages): (a) registered email address requirement (ss.28-30; not published publicly); (b) lawful purposes statement on confirmation statement (ss.59-63); (c) abolition of local PSC registers (ss.51-52, consolidated at CH central); (d) Registrar's new powers (s.1); (e) Authorised Corporate Service Provider regime (s.66); (f) Register of Overseas Entities under ECTEA 2022 (sibling page on RoE for overseas-entity property holdings). Forward-link to operational page + B2 definitional page + Companies House confirmation statement page for the wider regime view.)
