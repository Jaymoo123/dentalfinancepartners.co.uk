# Essential Bookkeeping Tips for Sole Traders and Property-Income Individuals

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (record-keeping for self-assessment): **TMA 1970 s.12B** — verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1970/9/section/12B on 2026-05-26. Verbatim core obligation: "Any person who may be required to make a tax return must keep all such records as may be requisite for the purpose of enabling him to make and deliver a correct and complete return." Retention period:
  - **Traders, professionals, businesses, companies:** 5 years from 31 January following the tax year (i.e. 5 years and ~10 months from the tax year end).
  - **Others (i.e. non-business self-assessment filers):** 1 year from 31 January following the tax year.
  - Extension during open HMRC enquiry; retention until enquiry closes.
- Primary (trading income — for property developers and other sole-trader-status property businesses): **ITTOIA 2005 Part 2 (ss.3-261)**.
  - **s.7** — charge to tax on trade profits.
  - **s.25** — calculation of profits in accordance with GAAP (or cash basis from s.25A).
  - **s.25A** — cash basis option for trading income (available since 6 April 2013 for unincorporated traders; default since the 2024-25 tax year per FA 2024 s.21 amendments).
  - **s.34** — "wholly and exclusively" rule for trading deductions.
- Primary (property income — for individual landlords NOT carrying on a trade): **ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (ss.260-364)**.
  - **s.264** — charge to tax on UK property business profits.
  - **s.271A (inserted by FA 2017)** — cash basis is the **default** for unincorporated property businesses unless an election out to accruals is made; threshold £150k gross rental.
  - **s.272** — limited cross-reference to Part 2 trading-income provisions for GAAP-method property businesses (verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2005/5/section/272 on 2026-05-26 — subsection (1) omitted by F(No.2)A 2017; subsection (2) lists which trading-income sections apply).
  - **s.272A-272B** — finance cost restriction (Section 24) — see HP §4.
- Primary (MTD for ITSA — the bookkeeping discipline accelerator):
  - **TMA 1970 ss.12B-12E + Sch A1** — MTD for ITSA framework introduced by FA 2017 + FA 2021 + FA 2022 amendments.
  - **The Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1076)** — operational digital-recordkeeping + quarterly-update mechanics.
  - **Commencement dates (HP §3 locked):** £50k gross-income threshold from 6 April 2026; £30k from April 2027; £20k from April 2028.
  - **HP §19 (Wave 3 MTD extension) — full operational lock** covers digital-link rules, quarterly-update cadence, end-of-period statement (EOPS), final declaration mechanics.
- Primary (VAT registration threshold — bookkeeping must surface the threshold-crossing event):
  - **VATA 1994 Sch 1 para 1** — £90,000 (taxable supplies) registration threshold from 1 April 2024 (HP §3 + §29).
  - Important for property businesses: residential lettings are EXEMPT (Sch 9 Group 1) so do not count toward the threshold; commercial lettings (where opted to tax) and serviced-accommodation are STANDARD-RATED and DO count.
- Primary (NIC for sole-trader-status individuals — Class 2 abolished, Class 4 remains): **SSCBA 1992 ss.11-15** — Class 4 NIC on self-employment profits. Class 2 (£3.45/week) was abolished from 6 April 2024 by FA 2024 s.18; self-employed earners with profits below the small-profits-threshold get a credit treated as a Class 2 payment for benefits.
- Primary (income tax administration deadlines): **TMA 1970 s.8** — self-assessment return filing deadlines (31 October paper; 31 January online); s.59A (payments on account); s.59B (balancing payment).
- Supporting (the rent-a-room scheme — the £7,500 threshold sets the bookkeeping floor for resident landlords):
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Part 7 Chapter 1 ss.784-802** — rent-a-room relief.
- House position references: §3 (MTD for ITSA headline + commencement chain); §4 (Section 24 finance cost restriction); §19 (full Wave 3 MTD operational lock); §29 (VAT for property cluster, Wave 8 mini-lock). Bookkeeping is NOT a separate locked HP cluster; this page threads §3 + §4 + §19 + §29 plus the statutory anchors above.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. Are you a sole trader (ITTOIA 2005 Part 2 trading income) or an individual landlord (ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 property income)? The disambiguation tests and the canonical edge cases (FHL operator pre-April-2025; property developer flip-vs-hold line; rent-a-room at scale; serviced-accommodation; lettings-agency owner-operator).
2. What records must you keep under TMA 1970 s.12B, and for how long (5 years from 31 January following the tax year for trading / business; 1 year for non-business)?
3. What is the cash basis default for property businesses under ITTOIA 2005 s.271A (since April 2017; threshold £150k gross rental) vs the cash basis default for trading businesses under s.25A (since 2024-25 tax year per FA 2024 s.21), and when does it pay to elect into accruals (mortgage interest, large bad-debt risk, year-end stock for developers)?
4. How does Section 24 (finance cost restriction; HP §4) shape bookkeeping for individual landlords — the need to separately track mortgage interest as a 20%-basic-rate-credit category, not as a deductible expense?
5. What is the VAT-registration threshold-watching discipline for property-orbit sole traders — and which receipts count toward the £90k threshold (commercial lettings opted to tax + serviced-accommodation YES; residential lettings NO; mixed-use businesses must split)?
6. What is the bank-account discipline (separate business account is best-practice but not mandatory under TMA 1970; the HMRC enquiry framework looks for traceable separation)?
7. What is the receipts / invoices discipline (HMRC's "ELS" — Electronic List of Sales — under MTD for ITSA from April 2026; the 7-year-retention for VAT-registered businesses under VATA 1994 Sch 11 para 6)?
8. What is the bookkeeping discipline for mixed-use property — partial private use of phone, vehicle, home-office (cross-link MW2 B5 home-office page)?
9. What is the MTD for ITSA quarterly-update cadence (HP §19) and how does it intersect with bookkeeping (digital-link rule; quarterly summaries from compatible software; end-of-period statement)?
10. What is the loss-relief disjunction — property income loss can only be carried forward against same-business future profits (ITA 2007 s.118); trading loss can be sideways-relieved under ITA 2007 s.64 against general income (with restrictions). Bookkeeping must surface the loss correctly for the right relief route.
