# Essential Bookkeeping Tips for Sole Traders and Property-Income Individuals

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (record-keeping for self-assessment): **TMA 1970 s.12B** — verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1970/9/section/12B on 2026-05-26. Verbatim core obligation: "Any person who may be required to make a tax return must keep all such records as may be requisite for the purpose of enabling him to make and deliver a correct and complete return." Retention period:
  - **Traders, professionals, businesses, companies:** 5 years from 31 January following the tax year (i.e. 5 years and ~10 months from the tax year end).
  - **Others (i.e. non-business self-assessment filers):** 1 year from 31 January following the tax year.
  - Extension during open HMRC enquiry; retention until enquiry closes.
- Primary (trading income — for property developers and other sole-trader-status property businesses): **ITTOIA 2005 Part 2 (ss.3-261)**.
  - **s.7** — charge to tax on trade profits.
  - **s.25** — calculation of profits in accordance with GAAP (or cash basis from s.25A).
  - **s.25A** — cash basis option for trading income (available since 6 April 2013 for unincorporated traders; default since the 2024-25 tax year per FA 2024 s.21 amendments).
  - **s.34** — "wholly and exclusively" rule for trading deductions.
- Primary (property income — for individual landlords NOT carrying on a trade): **ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (ss.260-364)**.
  - **s.264** — charge to tax on UK property business profits.
  - **s.271A (inserted by FA 2017)** — cash basis is the **default** for unincorporated property businesses unless an election out to accruals is made; threshold £150k gross rental.
  - **s.272** — limited cross-reference to Part 2 trading-income provisions for GAAP-method property businesses (verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2005/5/section/272 on 2026-05-26 — subsection (1) omitted by F(No.2)A 2017; subsection (2) lists which trading-income sections apply).
  - **s.272A-272B** — finance cost restriction (Section 24) — see HP §4.
- Primary (MTD for ITSA — the bookkeeping discipline accelerator):
  - **TMA 1970 ss.12B-12E + Sch A1** — MTD for ITSA framework introduced by FA 2017 + FA 2021 + FA 2022 amendments.
  - **The Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1076)** — operational digital-recordkeeping + quarterly-update mechanics.
  - **Commencement dates (HP §3 locked):** £50k gross-income threshold from 6 April 2026; £30k from April 2027; £20k from April 2028.
  - **HP §19 (Wave 3 MTD extension) — full operational lock** covers digital-link rules, quarterly-update cadence, end-of-period statement (EOPS), final declaration mechanics.
- Primary (VAT registration threshold — bookkeeping must surface the threshold-crossing event):
  - **VATA 1994 Sch 1 para 1** — £90,000 (taxable supplies) registration threshold from 1 April 2024 (HP §3 + §29).
  - Important for property businesses: residential lettings are EXEMPT (Sch 9 Group 1) so do not count toward the threshold; commercial lettings (where opted to tax) and serviced-accommodation are STANDARD-RATED and DO count.
- Primary (NIC for sole-trader-status individuals — Class 2 abolished, Class 4 remains): **SSCBA 1992 ss.11-15** — Class 4 NIC on self-employment profits. Class 2 (£3.45/week) was abolished from 6 April 2024 by FA 2024 s.18; self-employed earners with profits below the small-profits-threshold get a credit treated as a Class 2 payment for benefits.
- Primary (income tax administration deadlines): **TMA 1970 s.8** — self-assessment return filing deadlines (31 October paper; 31 January online); s.59A (payments on account); s.59B (balancing payment).
- Supporting (the rent-a-room scheme — the £7,500 threshold sets the bookkeeping floor for resident landlords):
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Part 7 Chapter 1 ss.784-802** — rent-a-room relief.
- House position references: §3 (MTD for ITSA headline + commencement chain); §4 (Section 24 finance cost restriction); §19 (full Wave 3 MTD operational lock); §29 (VAT for property cluster, Wave 8 mini-lock). Bookkeeping is NOT a separate locked HP cluster; this page threads §3 + §4 + §19 + §29 plus the statutory anchors above.

**Worked example: trading-vs-property-income disambiguation — the FHL operator**

Persona: Holloway-family. Owned 4-property portfolio for 8 years, traditionally treated as property income (ITTOIA 2005 Part 3). In 2026/27 considering converting one to a serviced-accommodation business.

Pre-2025:
- All 4 properties: standard residential BTL letting → ITTOIA Part 3 property income, cash-basis default under s.271A (below £150k gross rental), Section 24 finance cost restriction applies, residential rates of income tax.

Post-conversion (one property runs as serviced accommodation from April 2026):
- 3 properties: still ITTOIA Part 3 — unchanged treatment.
- 1 property (serviced accommodation): tests against the "trade vs investment" line. Per *Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC), FHL was historically borderline; the FHL regime is now abolished from April 2025 (HP §3 lock); serviced-accommodation businesses cross into trading territory ONLY if they pass active-business tests (housekeeping, marketing, hospitality services beyond the room). If trading, the income moves to ITTOIA Part 2 — different cash-basis rules (s.25A default with no upper threshold), Section 24 disapplied, but the property may then be VAT-standard-rated (Sch 9 Group 1 residential exemption falls away for serviced-accommodation).

Bookkeeping consequences:
- Records split: Part 2 trading income separated from Part 3 property income.
- VAT-threshold watch: serviced-accommodation receipts add toward the £90k threshold; once crossed, mandatory VAT registration.
- Loss-relief routing: Part 3 property losses can only go forward against same-business future profits (ITA 2007 s.118); Part 2 trading losses can be sideways-relieved against general income (ITA 2007 s.64) — strategic to allocate expenses correctly to the right income type.
- MTD impact: if combined trading + property income exceeds £50k from April 2026, MTD for ITSA applies. Quarterly updates required.

## FAQ draft (10-12 entries, Stage 2 populated; RUN sub-agent polishes prose)

1. **Am I a sole trader or an individual landlord — and why does it matter for bookkeeping?**
   "Sole trader" means a self-employed individual carrying on a trade under ITTOIA 2005 Part 2 (property developers, lettings-agency owner-operators, surveyors, contract farmers). "Individual landlord" means an individual receiving property income under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 — passive rental investment. The distinction matters because the legislation is different: cash-basis default rules differ (s.25A vs s.271A), loss-relief mechanics differ, Section 24 only applies to property income, and the badges-of-trade line decides which regime applies to borderline cases like serviced accommodation or property-flipping.

2. **What records do I need to keep, and for how long?**
   TMA 1970 s.12B requires "all such records as may be requisite for the purpose of enabling him to make and deliver a correct and complete return". For sole traders and other business activity, the retention period is 5 years from 31 January following the tax year. For non-business self-assessment filers, the retention period is 1 year from the same anchor date. During an open HMRC enquiry, retention extends until enquiry closure. VAT-registered businesses must retain VAT-related records for 6 years under VATA 1994 Sch 11 para 6.

3. **What is the cash basis default for property businesses, and when should I elect out?**
   Per ITTOIA 2005 s.271A (inserted by FA 2017), cash basis is the default for unincorporated property businesses with cash receipts under £150,000 per tax year. Above £150k, GAAP (accruals) is mandatory. Election-out into accruals can pay off for landlords with material mortgage interest (the interaction with Section 24 is cleaner on accruals), large bad-debt risk, or year-end timing manipulation needs. Most BTL portfolio operators under the £150k threshold stay on cash basis for simplicity.

4. **And what about the cash basis default for trading sole traders — has that changed?**
   Yes. Since the 2024/25 tax year, FA 2024 s.21 made cash basis the DEFAULT for unincorporated trading businesses (previously it was opt-in with a £150k turnover cap). There is no longer an upper threshold for the trading cash-basis default — any size of sole-trader trading business can use it, unless they elect into traditional accruals. For property developers carrying on a trade, the trading cash basis means recording revenue when cash comes in and expenses when paid; year-end work-in-progress no longer sits on a balance sheet.

5. **How does Section 24 finance cost restriction shape bookkeeping for individual landlords?**
   Per ITTOIA 2005 s.272A-272B (HP §4 lock), mortgage interest and other finance costs on residential rental property are NOT deductible expenses against rental profits. Instead they're tracked as a separate item generating a 20% basic-rate tax credit applied after calculating profits. Bookkeeping must therefore separate mortgage interest from other property expenses — most accounting software now has a dedicated category for it. Companies (LtdCo BTL holders) are unaffected — they deduct interest as a normal expense.

6. **What's the VAT-registration threshold-watching discipline for property-orbit businesses?**
   The compulsory VAT registration threshold is £90,000 taxable supplies in any rolling 12-month period (VATA 1994 Sch 1 para 1, from 1 April 2024). Residential rental income is EXEMPT under VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 1 — these receipts do NOT count toward the threshold. Commercial rental income (where the landlord has opted to tax) and serviced-accommodation income DO count. Mixed-use property businesses (some residential, some commercial-opted, some serviced-accommodation) must split receipts at the point of recording for accurate threshold monitoring.

7. **When does MTD for ITSA hit me, and what does it change?**
   Per HP §3 + §19 lock, MTD for ITSA commences 6 April 2026 for sole traders + landlords with combined trading + property income above £50,000 per tax year (qualifying income). The £30k threshold follows from April 2027 and £20k from April 2028. From the commencement date, qualifying taxpayers must: (i) keep digital records under SI 2021/1076 "digital-link" rules; (ii) submit quarterly updates from compatible software; (iii) submit an end-of-period statement (EOPS) per income source; (iv) submit a final declaration replacing the historic self-assessment return.

8. **What about Class 2 NIC — I've heard it was abolished?**
   Yes, Class 2 NIC was abolished from 6 April 2024 by FA 2024 s.18. Self-employed earners with profits BELOW the small-profits threshold previously had to opt-in to Class 2 to maintain a state-pension contribution record; post-abolition they automatically receive a Class 2-equivalent credit treated as a paid Class 2 NIC. Class 4 NIC (6% on profits between £12,570 and £50,270, 2% above) is unchanged — verify exact rates at write date as percentages have ratcheted.

9. **Do I need a separate business bank account?**
   No, not as a strict legal requirement under TMA 1970 — but yes, as a best-practice. HMRC's enquiry framework looks for traceable separation of business and personal cash flows; mixed-account bookkeeping is the most-common cause of enquiry escalation. Many high-street banks offer free or cheap business accounts for landlords + sole traders. For LtdCo BTL portfolios, a separate company account is mandatory (the company is a separate legal person).

10. **What's the rent-a-room scheme threshold and when does it cross over into trading?**
    Per ITTOIA 2005 ss.784-802, the rent-a-room scheme gives a £7,500 annual relief for income from letting furnished accommodation in your own home. Below £7,500: no tax liability and no need to report. Above £7,500: choose between (i) taxed on excess receipts above £7,500 with no expenses, or (ii) taxed on profits in the normal way with full expense deduction. For resident-landlord operations crossing into commercial-style management (multiple rooms, short stays, full housekeeping), the activity can shift from "rent-a-room property income" to "serviced-accommodation trading" — a different bookkeeping regime entirely.

11. **What's the loss-relief disjunction between trading and property income?**
    Trading losses (ITTOIA Part 2) can be sideways-relieved against general income of the same year under ITA 2007 s.64 (with restrictions on the £50k+ general income cap). Property losses (ITTOIA Part 3) can ONLY be carried forward against same-business future property profits under ITA 2007 s.118 — no sideways relief available. The disjunction means bookkeeping must correctly classify the loss source at the recording stage; misclassifying a developer-trade loss as a property loss strips the sideways-relief route.

12. **How does this page differ from your MTD-for-landlords deadline page?**
    Our existing page `making-tax-digital-landlords-april-2026-deadline` is event-driven — focused on the 6 April 2026 commencement, who's caught, what to do this month. This page (C6) is discipline-driven — the operational bookkeeping habits a sole trader or property-orbit individual needs to be in regardless of MTD timing. The bookkeeping discipline matters even for individuals under the MTD threshold (£50k 2026, £30k 2027, £20k 2028) because HMRC enquiry exposure exists whether or not MTD applies. Read the MTD page if you're caught by the April 2026 deadline; read this page for the underlying record-keeping habits.
