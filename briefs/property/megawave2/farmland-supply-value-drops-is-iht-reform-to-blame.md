# Farmland Supply Is Up, Value Is Down: Is the April 2026 IHT Reform Really to Blame?

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the reform alleged to be moving the market): **IHTA 1984 Part V Chapter II ss.115-124C as amended by Finance Act 2025 / Finance (No. 2) Bill 2025-26 / Finance Act 2026**. The structural change is the introduction of a **£1 million combined BPR + APR cap** per estate effective for transfers of value on or after **6 April 2026**.
  - **Below £1m of qualifying property:** 100% relief as historically applied under IHTA 1984 s.116 (APR) and s.104 (BPR).
  - **Above £1m:** 50% relief, producing an effective IHT rate of 20% on the above-cap qualifying value.
  - **Combined, not separate:** the cap is a single £1m envelope shared across both APR and BPR — a mixed estate cannot stack a £1m APR allowance on top of a £1m BPR allowance.
  - **Anti-forestalling (lifetime transfers):** the new rules apply to lifetime transfers made **on or after 30 October 2024** (Autumn Budget 2024 announcement date) if the donor dies **on or after 6 April 2026** and within 7 years of the gift. Pre-announcement gifts are NOT caught. HP §15.4 verbatim.
  - **Trust anti-fragmentation:** trusts settled by the same settlor **on or after 30 October 2024** share a single £1m allowance divided across them; pre-30-October-2024 trusts each retain their own £1m. HP §15.4 verbatim.
- Primary (the underlying definitions — what "agricultural property" and "agricultural value" mean, which determine what the cap actually catches): **IHTA 1984 s.115** (definition; verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1984/51/section/115 on 2026-05-26).
  - **s.115(2):** "agricultural property" = agricultural land or pasture, woodland and any building used in connection with the intensive rearing of livestock or fish where occupying adjacent agricultural land, **cottages, farm buildings and farmhouses, together with the land occupied with them, as are of a character appropriate to the property**.
  - **s.115(3):** "agricultural value" = the value the property would have if subject to a perpetual covenant prohibiting use otherwise than as agricultural property. **Hope value (development potential, equestrian uplift, residential conversion value) sits OUTSIDE the relief and falls into the chargeable estate without protection.**
- Primary (the surviving carve-outs under the cap):
  - **s.116:** 100% rate where vacant possession or post-1-September-1995 tenancy; 50% rate otherwise. The cap sits ABOVE this layer — eligible property gets to 100% below £1m and 50% above; legacy 50%-rate property gets 50% across the board (uncapped at the 50% rate; the cap only changes the rate from 100% to 50% on the >£1m slice).
  - **s.117:** minimum periods — 2 years owner-occupied; 7 years let.
  - **Sch A1 extension:** UK agricultural property look-through extended to UK agricultural land per Autumn Budget 2025 announcement; FA 2026 enactment. HP §15.4 + Wave 6 cross-reference.
- Primary (the non-reform drivers — for proportional attribution):
  - **Agriculture Act 2020 + Direct Payments to Farmers (Legislative Continuity) Act 2020** — the post-Brexit subsidy transition. Basic Payment Scheme phased reduction 2021-2027; Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) phased introduction; Countryside Stewardship enhanced offers. Net effect on viability of mid-sized arable farms is a meaningful adjacent cause to any 2024-2026 market move and must be foregrounded in the attribution analysis.
  - **Environment Act 2021 + Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG) mandatory from February 2024** — created a parallel demand-side market for "biodiversity units" from landholders, partially offsetting the BPS withdrawal headwind but not uniformly across regions.
  - **Bank of England Bank Rate path** — the August 2023 peak of 5.25%, with gradual cuts from August 2024 onwards. Higher debt-service costs on farm borrowing were a 2023-2024 cycle headwind for leveraged-buyer demand independent of any tax change.
- Case-law anchors (for the qualification-gate that determines who gets caught vs not):
  - **Brander v HMRC [2010] UKUT 300 (TCC)** — agricultural and woodland estate; holistic-activity analysis for the BPR/APR split.
  - **McKenna's Executors v HMRC [2006] SpC 565** — the farmhouse "character appropriate" test.
  - **HMRC v Atkinson [2011] UKUT 506 (TCC)** — occupation of farmhouse during sickness; s.117 occupation continuity.
  - **Pawson v HMRC [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC)** — trading-vs-investment line (cross-reference for the BPR boundary on lettings-with-services).
- House position references: **§15.4** (BPR + APR £1m cap, locked); **§22.X.4** (Sch A1 look-through extension to UK agricultural land); **§22.1** (BPR investment-line for landlord BTL context — Pawson).
