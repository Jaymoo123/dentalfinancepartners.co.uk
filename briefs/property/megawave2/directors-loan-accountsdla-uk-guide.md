# Directors' Loan Accounts (DLAs): A Complete UK Guide for Company Directors (2026/27)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (overdrawn DLA charge):** CTA 2010 s.455 — close-company loan-to-participator charge. **Rate 35.75% for loans made on or after 6 April 2026** (was 33.75% before that date) per FA 2026 s.4(1)(b) substituting ITA 2007 s.8(2) to "the dividend upper rate is 35.75%" for 2026/27 onwards. s.455 rate references the dividend upper rate at ITA 2007 s.8(2) by-reference — **§16.27 + §16.42 rate-by-reference verification pattern: sessions writing s.455 must verify s.8(2) at write time per §21.1 + F-9 lock 2026-05-24**. Charge falls on amounts unpaid 9 months and 1 day after end of accounting period in which the loan was made. **Repayable on later director repayment** — the s.455 is provisional, not permanent.
- **Primary (anti-avoidance — bed-and-breakfast):** CTA 2010 s.464C and s.464D — repayments treated as not relieved against s.455 where the loan was substantially repaid within 30 days AND the same close company made a further loan to the same participator within the 30-day window. Designed to defeat year-end-repay-and-redraw schemes. s.464D extends to arrangements over £15k where an intention to redraw exists, even outside the 30-day rule.
- **Primary (benefits to participators):** CTA 2010 s.453 — broader close-company benefits-to-participators framework; deductibility-side rules on interest paid to director on credit balances.
- **Primary (BIK on interest-free / cheap loans):** ITEPA 2003 ss.173-191 — taxable employment income where the loan exceeds £10,000 (the "qualifying loans" threshold) and the company charges less than the HMRC official rate of interest. HMRC official rate set quarterly (recent rates have varied between 2% and 3.75%; verify current at "Beneficial loan arrangements — HMRC official rates" gov.uk publication at write time).
- **Supporting (credit-balance interest):** interest paid by company to director on credit-balance DLA is taxable income on director (savings income; PSA £1,000 / £500 / nil basic / higher / additional + £5,000 starting-rate band may apply) per ITTOIA 2005 ITSA architecture. Company-side deductibility under CTA 2010 s.453 close-company rules.
- **Supporting (incorporation-credit-balance context):** TCGA 1992 s.162 incorporation relief — generates a tax-free DLA credit balance on transfer of an existing rental portfolio into a newly-formed company (§21.1 lock). Tax-free repayment route while the balance persists.
- **House position reference:** §21.1 primary (DLA mechanics — credit balances from s.162 incorporation; debit-balance s.455 mechanics at 35.75% from 6 April 2026; HMRC official rate; F-9 / Wave 6 lock 2026-05-24; **DLA exhaustion trap** worked-example discipline expected). §21.4 secondary (2026/27 rate stack confirmation; ITA 2007 s.8(2) dividend-upper-rate by-reference). §21.2 (share-class architecture — interaction with DLA repayment-order planning).
