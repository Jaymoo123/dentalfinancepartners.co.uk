# Does Your Property Business Qualify as a Partnership? Partnership Act 1890 vs Co-Ownership for Landlords

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (definition):** Partnership Act 1890 (PA 1890) s.1(1) — "Partnership is the relation which subsists between persons carrying on a business in common with a view of profit." Three cumulative tests: (1) two or more persons; (2) carrying on a business (not merely owning property); (3) in common (joint conduct, not parallel solo); (4) with a view of profit (intent to make profit, even if loss-making in fact). All four tests must be satisfied for partnership to exist.
- **Primary (rules for determining existence — the negative tests):** PA 1890 s.2 — rules for determining the existence of partnership. **s.2(1) is the load-bearing rule for property:** "Joint tenancy, tenancy in common, joint property, common property, or part ownership does not of itself create a partnership as to anything so held or owned, whether the tenants or owners do or do not share any profits made by the use thereof." **Passive co-ownership of rental property is NOT a partnership** by itself, even when the co-owners share rental profits — verbatim. s.2(2): sharing gross returns alone does not create partnership. s.2(3): receipt of share of profits is **prima facie evidence** of partnership but not conclusive — six rebuttal heads (debt repayment, salary/wages, annuity to widow/child, interest on loan, annuity from share-purchase price, vendor receiving rentcharge — these specific receipts do NOT make recipient a partner).
- **Primary (HMRC operative guidance):** HMRC Business Income Manual BIM72005-BIM72165 (Partnerships) — operative guidance on identifying when a partnership exists for income tax purposes. **BIM72015 specifically addresses property co-ownership** and confirms the s.2(1) position — joint ownership of property "does not necessarily mean that there is a partnership". HMRC pursues the substance-over-form test: whether the parties are merely co-owners receiving their share of joint income, or whether they are carrying on a business together. Stage 2 verifies BIM72005+ at write time per §16.35.
- **Primary (HMRC operative guidance — partnership tax return obligation):** TMA 1970 s.12AA — partnership statement / return obligation. SA800 partnership tax return is required where a partnership exists — penalty for non-filing accrues per partner. **Filing the SA800 is one of the strongest objective indicators** HMRC uses to test partnership existence. The decision to file vs not file SA800 is the **operative trigger** for most landlord-side partnership questions — the wrong call has both downside risks (SA800 penalty for missed filing on real partnership) and upside risks (locked-in partnership treatment with SDLT Sch 15 + s.59 transfer-of-going-concern implications when subsequently incorporating).
- **Supporting (income tax — taxation framework):** ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 (Partnerships) ss.846-863 — taxation of partnership profits as transparent. Each partner taxed individually on their share of profits per the profit-sharing agreement (PA 1890 s.24(1) default: equal shares if no contrary agreement). ITA 2007 s.852 (notional trade for each partner). Property partnerships taxed under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (property income) at partner level — not as a single entity.
- **Supporting (capital gains — taxation framework):** TCGA 1992 s.59 (partnerships) + HMRC SP D12 (Partnership statement of practice on CGT) — each partner has fractional interest in each partnership chargeable asset; disposals by partnership are notional disposals by each partner of fractional interest. SP D12 sets out HMRC's operative CGT treatment of partnership asset movements (introduction / withdrawal / share rebasing / dissolution).
- **Supporting (SDLT — partnership transfers):** FA 2003 Sch 15 (the partnership SDLT regime). **A property "partnership" for SDLT Sch 15 purposes is defined more narrowly than for income tax** — para 1 Sch 15 requires the partnership to carry on a business (substance test, not mere ownership). The Sch 15 partnership-transfer reliefs (sum-of-lower-proportions on transfer-in, sum-of-lower-proportions on transfer-out, intra-partnership transfers) are unavailable to mere co-ownership structures. This is operationally **the largest single tax-saving consequence** of partnership-vs-co-ownership classification for landlords contemplating incorporation: Sch 15 para 10 incorporation relief can dramatically reduce SDLT on transferring a partnership-held portfolio to a connected LtdCo (no SDLT on the partners' own proportions transferring into a LtdCo they own in same shares), where the same transfer from co-ownership would attract full SDLT on the market-value transfer.
- **Supporting (VAT — disaggregation):** where multiple persons co-own and co-let property under separate VAT registrations to keep below threshold, HMRC may apply disaggregation under VATA 1994 Sch 1 para 1A (artificial separation). Question of partnership existence interacts with disaggregation question — separate enquiries but factually related.
- **House position reference:** §21 (Ltd Co + FIC family — partnership-vs-LtdCo entity choice). §22 (IHT — partnership BPR question — Pawson investment line in §22.1). §3 (incorporation mechanics — partnership status is the gating test for SDLT Sch 15 reliefs on incorporation; see Ltd Co incorporation mechanics depth in §3). **New HP coverage gap:** §11 + §11.A cover ECCTA Part 1 (companies-side); §11.B is being requested by F-1 (M2-A-B1) for ECCTA Part 2 (LP reforms). ## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the legal definition of a partnership under PA 1890 s.1 (the four cumulative tests: two-plus persons + business + in common + with a view of profit)?
2. Why does s.2(1) PA 1890 matter for landlords — passive co-ownership of property, joint tenancy, or tenancy in common does NOT create a partnership, even where the co-owners share rental profits?
3. What is the substance-over-form test HMRC applies (BIM72015): joint bank account, single management agreement, joint business name, holding-out to third parties, joint borrowing — none individually decisive, all relevant.
4. What is the difference between a partnership and a joint venture or co-ownership arrangement for property purposes?
5. When does a husband-and-wife jointly-letting a property qualify as a partnership? (Answer: typically NOT — they are co-owners receiving rental income proportionate to beneficial entitlement; partnership requires active joint conduct of a business beyond mere co-ownership.)
6. When does a family operating multiple properties together qualify as a partnership? (Answer: depends on substance — separate properties owned individually do not aggregate to a partnership; jointly owned + jointly managed multi-property portfolio with joint borrowing and joint marketing increasingly looks like one.)
7. What is the operative trigger of partnership existence — the SA800 partnership tax return obligation under TMA 1970 s.12AA — and what are the penalties for non-filing where a partnership genuinely exists?
8. How does the partnership question interact with the SDLT Sch 15 partnership-transfer reliefs (huge SDLT savings on incorporation are conditional on existence of a partnership; forward link to A3 + Sch 15 mechanics page)?
9. What are the income tax (ITTOIA 2005 Part 9) and CGT (TCGA 1992 s.59 + HMRC SP D12) tax consequences of partnership classification vs co-ownership?
10. What is the difference between a general partnership (PA 1890), a limited partnership (LPA 1907 — forward link to A3), and an LLP (LLPA 2000 — forward link to A14 / A15 / A10 hybrid-LLP page)?
11. What is the difference between a partnership and a limited company (CTA 2010 / CA 2006), and when is each preferable for property purposes?
12. What is "holding-out" liability under PA 1890 s.14 (a person who represents themselves to a third party as a partner can be liable as one), and why does this matter for landlords entering into name-on-the-lease arrangements with non-co-owners?
