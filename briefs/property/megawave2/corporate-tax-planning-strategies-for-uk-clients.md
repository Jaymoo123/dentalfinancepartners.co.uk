# Corporation Tax Planning Strategies for UK Property Companies: A Lever Map for Landlord LtdCos (2026/27)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary:** CTA 2010 (charge + rates + reliefs) — s.3 (charge); s.18A (SPR charge); s.18B (marginal relief trigger); s.18D (marginal relief formula + standard fraction 3/200); s.18E (associated-companies definition + divisor); s.18L (augmented profits); s.18N (CIHC exclusion + qualifying-purpose carve-out at s.18N(2)(b) + s.18N(3) connected-tenant exclusion expansive scope per §21.A drift catch); s.450 (control definition); s.455 (overdrawn DLA charge — 35.75% on loans made on/after 6 April 2026 per FA 2026 s.4(1)(b) + ITA 2007 s.8(2) by-reference per §21.1).
- **Primary:** CTA 2009 (computation) — Parts 3-4 (trading + property income within charge to CT); s.54 (deductibility of expenses); Part 8A (NRCGT for non-resident companies on UK land disposals per TCGA 1992 s.1A cross-reference). CTA 2009 Part 16 (R&D expenditure relief — usually not relevant to BTL).
- **Primary:** TCGA 1992 — s.4 + s.1A (chargeable-gains treatment within CT); s.171 (intra-group transfers at no gain / no loss); s.179 (degrouping charge); Sch 7AC (Substantial Shareholding Exemption — SSE).
- **Supporting:** TIOPA 2010 Part 4 (transfer pricing) — s.166 SME exemption; usually keeps landlord-SPV transactions out, but exceeds at 250 staff / €50m turnover thresholds.
- **Supporting:** TIOPA 2010 Part 10 (Corporate Interest Restriction — CIR) — generally £2m group-wide net interest threshold; portfolio operators with multi-SPV finance can hit this.
- **Supporting:** CAA 2001 — Structures and Buildings Allowance (SBA); Annual Investment Allowance (AIA, £1m); First-Year Allowances (FYA); special-rate vs main-pool plant & machinery.
- **House position reference:** §21.A (FA 2021 CT three-figure framework + associated-companies divisor + augmented profits test) **primary**; §21.4 (2026/27 rate stack: CT rates, NI thresholds, dividend rates per §21.4 F-19 + F-20 corrections, EA, s.455 rate) **primary**; §21.5 (CIHC + qualifying-purpose carve-out s.18N — the most-misunderstood mechanic for portfolio operators with family-tenant property); §21.1 (DLA mechanics — extraction lever); §21.2 (share-class + s.624 settlements legislation — extraction lever); §22.6 (FIC value-freeze IHT framing — long-term planning lever).

Specifically, this page identifies seven levers and walks the property-LtdCo operator through whether each lever is relevant to their structure:
1. **CIHC avoidance via the "let commercially" carve-out (s.18N(3))** — relevant where any SPV holds family-occupied or connected-tenant property (§21.5 + §21.A).
2. **Associated-companies divisor management (s.18E)** — relevant for multi-SPV operators; deciding whether SPV fragmentation costs more SPR than it saves in risk-isolation.
3. **Extraction mix optimisation** — salary / dividend / DLA / pension; per the 2026/27 rate stack (§21.4).
4. **Group relief (CTA 2010 Part 5)** — between SPVs under common ownership, where one is loss-making and another profitable.
5. **Intra-group asset transfers (TCGA 1992 s.171)** — restructuring portfolios at no gain / no loss before disposal, with the s.179 degrouping charge as the trap.
6. **SBA + AIA + capital-allowances stacking** — commercial-property SPVs only; BTL residential = limited.
7. **CIR planning (TIOPA 2010 Part 10)** — portfolio operators with material group-wide interest above £2m.

Child pages (existing + sibling) cover each lever in mechanics depth. This page does not duplicate them; it orchestrates which to consider and in what order.
