## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What changed on 18 November 2025 in one paragraph?** (From that date, identity verification under ECCTA 2023 became a legal requirement for newly appointed directors and persons with significant control at UK companies. The same date opened a 12-month transition window for existing directors and PSCs to verify by their next confirmation statement filed within the window. Voluntary verification has been available since 8 April 2025; the 18 November 2025 date is the move from voluntary to mandatory for new appointments and the start of the existing-role transition clock.)
2. **Does the regime apply to me from today?** (Three-way split. New appointments on or after 18 November 2025: yes, verify before the appointment can be recorded. Existing directors / PSCs continuing in role: yes, verify by your next confirmation statement filed within the 12-month transition window ending around November 2026. Reader-side stale-data check: confirm against the Companies House campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` at the moment of reading per §11.A discipline — operative state moves.)
3. **What does the 12-month transition window actually mean for existing directors and PSCs?** (Each company has a confirmation statement filing cycle anchored to its incorporation anniversary (or 12 months from the previous confirmation statement). Within the 12-month window (18 November 2025 to around November 2026), the next confirmation statement filed for that company must carry verified personal codes for every in-scope director and PSC. Operationally: don't wait until the confirmation statement deadline (the verification takes 10-30 minutes via One Login for typical biometric-passport holders but can extend if document checks need additional review or if ACSP capacity is constrained near the deadline).)
4. **What about new appointments — what does "before the appointment can be recorded" mean in practice?** (The Companies House appointment filing (form AP01 / equivalent online) now requires the appointee's personal code. If the personal code is not available at the moment of filing, the appointment cannot lawfully be recorded. Practical sequence: prospective director or PSC verifies first (acquires personal code under ECCTA s.68), then the company files the appointment quoting the code. Pre-18 November 2025 appointments did not need a personal code at filing; from 18 November 2025 they do.)
5. **What if I was appointed before 18 November 2025 — do I count as "existing" or "newly appointed"?** (Existing. The "newly appointed" classification turns on the appointment date, not the verification status. Anyone appointed on or before 17 November 2025 falls under the 12-month transition window (verify by next confirmation statement within the window). Anyone appointed on or after 18 November 2025 falls under the new-appointment rule (verify before the appointment can be recorded). The two routes meet around November 2026 when the transition window closes and all directors and PSCs should be verified.)
6. **What do I need to do this week?** (Practical week-one checklist: (a) confirm whether you are a director or PSC (or both) of any UK company; (b) if you are a director or PSC of one or more companies, identify your next confirmation statement filing date for each — Companies House WebCheck (free) shows this for each company; (c) decide your verification route — GOV.UK One Login (free, self-service, 10-30 minutes for biometric-passport-holders) or ACSP (Authorised Corporate Service Provider, usually your accountant or company secretary, typically £20-£100 fee where charged — verify at write); (d) book the verification well before the next confirmation statement deadline; (e) once verified, quote the personal code at the next confirmation statement filing for each company. The personal code is per natural person — one verification covers every directorship and PSC interest you hold across every UK company.)
7. **What is the GOV.UK One Login route — what do I actually do?** (Process summary (full walkthrough on the operational page): (a) gather identity documents — biometric passport is cleanest, alternatives include UK photo driving licence, UK biometric residence permit, UK Frontier Worker permit; (b) download GOV.UK One Login app on phone, follow biometric-document-check + selfie-match flow; (c) complete verification (typically 10-30 minutes); (d) receive Companies House personal code under ECCTA s.68; (e) quote the personal code at the next confirmation statement filing or appointment filing for each company.)
8. **What is the ACSP route — should I use my accountant?** (ACSPs are firms supervised for AML under MLR 2017 that have separately registered with Companies House as Authorised Corporate Service Providers. The ACSP route is often faster for clients whose accountant already holds AML-verified ID documents on file. Cost: typically £20-£100 (verify at write — fees vary by firm). For multi-SPV BTL portfolios, the per-natural-person verification mechanic means the cost is once per person, not once per company, so the ACSP fee is amortised across every directorship + PSC interest.)
9. **What if I am a director of multiple SPVs (typical landlord BTL portfolio)?** (Verification is per natural person, NOT per company. One verification = one Companies House personal code = covers every directorship and PSC interest across every UK company. So a landlord director of five SPVs verifies ONCE; the same personal code is then quoted at each SPV's next confirmation statement filing. This is the single most useful operational fact for landlord-LtdCo portfolios. Cross-link to the operational page for the per-company filing cadence walkthrough.)
10. **What happens if I do not verify by the applicable deadline?** (Continuing to act as a director or PSC of a UK company without being verified by the applicable deadline is a criminal offence under the ECCTA 2023 framework. The company itself is exposed: Companies House can refuse the company's confirmation statement filing without verified personal codes for all in-scope directors and PSCs; failure to file a confirmation statement is itself an offence under CA 2006 ss.853A-G as amended by ECCTA. Individual sanctions include fines; cumulative non-compliance can lead to disqualification proceedings. The operational page covers sanctions in depth; this B7 page surfaces the headline.)
11. **What about overseas directors of UK companies — does the same date apply?** (Yes. The commencement date of 18 November 2025 + the 12-month transition window apply regardless of where the director or PSC is resident. Overseas directors typically verify via the GOV.UK One Login app using a biometric passport — no UK visit required. Sibling MW2 B-page `how-us-based-directors-of-uk-companies-can-navigate-ecctas-new-identity-verification-rules` covers the overseas-director angle in depth.)
12. **What about the rest of the ECCTA reforms — what is still phased in?** (Headline parallel reforms (each covered in sibling pages): (a) registered email address requirement (ss.28-30; in force 4 March 2024 for new companies, from next confirmation statement onwards for existing); (b) lawful purposes statement on confirmation statement (ss.59-63; in force 5 March 2024); (c) abolition of local PSC registers (ss.51-52, consolidated at CH central); (d) Registrar's new powers (s.1); (e) ACSP regime (s.66); (f) later phase no earlier than November 2026 for filers + corporate-officer scenarios. Forward-link to B8 / B9 / B10 umbrella pages once shipped + to the existing confirmation statement page.)
