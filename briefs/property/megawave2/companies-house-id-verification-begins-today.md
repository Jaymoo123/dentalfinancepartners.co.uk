### Example 1 — The calendar mechanic in plain English

- **18 November 2025 at 09:00:** Mr Patel accepts an appointment as director of a new SPV (`Patel BTL 7 Ltd`) that he is incorporating that morning to acquire a BTL flat. The appointment paperwork is filed at Companies House at 11:00 the same day. Companies House rejects the filing — there is no personal code for Mr Patel because he has not yet completed ID verification. Mr Patel completes GOV.UK One Login verification via the app at 14:00 (passport + selfie + biometric document check; ~18 minutes); receives his personal code at 14:25; refiles the appointment at 15:00; appointment is recorded.
- **Same day at 11:00, separate scenario:** Ms Kapoor (already a director of `Kapoor Properties 1-5 Ltd` since 2019) does NOT need to do anything different on 18 November 2025. Her existing appointments are unaffected; she has until her next confirmation statement for each company (March 2026 for `Kapoor Properties 1`; June 2026 for `Kapoor Properties 2`; etc) to complete verification within the 12-month transition window. She books One Login verification for December 2025 (well before the earliest deadline).
- **Operative insight:** the date is a hard line for NEW appointments. Existing roles get a 12-month runway. Two readers in identical seats face very different deadlines depending on appointment date.

### Example 2 — Multi-SPV portfolio: one verification covers all

- **Singh portfolio:** Mr Singh is director and 100% PSC of five SPVs (`Singh BTL 1-5 Ltd`), each holding 2-3 flats. Confirmation statement filing dates span the year (March, May, July, September, November).
- **One verification:** Mr Singh completes One Login verification on 20 November 2025 — passport + selfie + document check, 22 minutes total, receives one personal code under ECCTA s.68.
- **Five filings:** the same code is quoted on each SPV's next confirmation statement: March 2026 (`Singh BTL 1`), May 2026 (`Singh BTL 2`), July 2026 (`Singh BTL 3`), September 2026 (`Singh BTL 4`), November 2026 (`Singh BTL 5`). Five companies, one verification, one code.
- **Operative insight:** the per-natural-person mechanic at ECCTA s.68 means the verification cost (zero on One Login route; ~£20-£100 on ACSP route) is once per person, not once per company. For multi-SPV portfolios with one or two natural persons across all directorships + PSC roles, the verification footprint is trivial.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target — calendar-event coverage)

1. **Q: What changed on 18 November 2025?**
   A: From that date, identity verification at Companies House became a legal requirement for newly appointed directors and persons with significant control of UK companies under the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 (ECCTA 2023) ss.40-45 + s.64 + s.65 + s.68. Voluntary verification has been available since 8 April 2025; the 18 November 2025 date is the move from voluntary to mandatory for new appointments. The same date opened a 12-month transition window for existing directors and PSCs to verify by their next confirmation statement filed within the window ending around November 2026.

2. **Q: Does the regime apply to me from today?**
   A: Three-way split. (i) **New appointments on or after 18 November 2025:** yes, you must verify before the appointment can be recorded on the public register. (ii) **Existing directors / PSCs continuing in role:** yes, you must verify by your next confirmation statement filed within the 12-month transition window ending around November 2026. (iii) **Not a director or PSC at all:** the regime does not reach you until you accept a new role. Reader-side stale-data check: confirm against the Companies House campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` at the moment of reading.

3. **Q: What does the 12-month transition window actually mean?**
   A: Each UK company has a confirmation statement filing cycle (annual; anchored to incorporation anniversary or 12 months from previous filing). Within the 12-month window from 18 November 2025 to around November 2026, the next confirmation statement filed for that company must carry verified personal codes for every in-scope director and PSC. Operationally: book verification in the first half of the window (December 2025 / January 2026); do NOT wait until Q4 of the window when document-check queues and ACSP capacity tighten near the closing deadline.

4. **Q: How do I actually verify? What is GOV.UK One Login?**
   A: GOV.UK One Login is the government's free self-service identity verification platform. Process: download the One Login app; capture biometric passport (or alternative document — UK photo driving licence, UK biometric residence permit, UK Frontier Worker permit); take a selfie for biometric matching; complete the document-check + liveness check (typically 10-30 minutes for biometric-passport holders); receive your Companies House personal code under ECCTA s.68. The personal code is then quoted at any subsequent confirmation statement or appointment filing.

5. **Q: What is an ACSP and should I use my accountant?**
   A: Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs) under ECCTA s.66 are AML-supervised firms (under MLR 2017) that have registered with Companies House to perform identity verification on clients' behalf. Most established UK accountancy practices are AML-supervised already (HMRC, ICAEW, ACCA, ICAS, CIOT); the ACSP registration is procedural. Cost is typically £20-£100 per natural person (verify at write — fees vary by firm). The ACSP route is often more efficient where the accountant already holds AML-verified KYC documents — they can use those to complete the verification without you needing to repeat document upload. For multi-SPV BTL portfolios the per-natural-person rule means the fee is once per person, not once per company.

6. **Q: I am a director of five SPVs. Do I have to verify five times?**
   A: No. Verification is per natural person, NOT per company. One verification under ECCTA s.65 = one personal code under s.68 = covers every directorship and PSC interest you hold across every UK company. A landlord director of five SPVs verifies ONCE; the same personal code is then quoted at each SPV's next confirmation statement filing. This is the single most operationally useful fact for multi-SPV BTL portfolios.

7. **Q: I was appointed before 18 November 2025. Am I "new" or "existing"?**
   A: Existing. The classification turns on appointment date, not on verification status. Anyone appointed on or before 17 November 2025 falls under the 12-month transition window (verify by next confirmation statement within the window). Anyone appointed on or after 18 November 2025 falls under the new-appointment rule (verify before the appointment can be recorded). The two routes converge around November 2026 when the existing-roles window closes and all directors and PSCs should be verified.

8. **Q: I am an overseas director of a UK company. Does this reach me?**
   A: Yes. The regime applies to UK-company directors and PSCs regardless of residence. Verification via the GOV.UK One Login app works from anywhere in the world with a biometric passport (most modern passports qualify) — no UK visit required. 9. **Q: What happens if I do not verify by the applicable deadline?**
   A: Continuing to act as a director or PSC of a UK company without being verified is a criminal offence under the ECCTA 2023 framework — fines + cumulative non-compliance can lead to disqualification proceedings. The company itself is also exposed: Companies House refuses the confirmation statement filing if personal codes are missing for any in-scope director or PSC; failure to file a confirmation statement is itself an offence under CA 2006 ss.853A-G as amended by ECCTA. Civil financial penalty regime under Sch 1B CA 2006 allows up to £5,000 per relevant filing failure (verify at write).

    A: Five-step practical checklist. (i) confirm whether you are a director or PSC (or both) of any UK company.     A: ECCTA 2023 Part 1 has eight operative reforms beyond just ID verification: (i) Registrar's new statutory objectives at s.1 (CA 2006 inserted-s.1081A — the gatekeeper mandate); (ii) registered email address from 4 March 2024 (ECCTA ss.28-30); (iii) appropriate-address rule for registered office from 4 March 2024 (no PO-box-only); (iv) lawful purposes statement on confirmation statement from 5 March 2024 (ECCTA ss.59-63); (v) abolition of local PSC registers (ss.51-52); (vi) ACSP framework (s.66); (vii) Limited Partnerships reforms under ECCTA Part 2 (phased); (viii) Register of Overseas Entities updates (ECCTA Part 3 amending ECTEA 2022).
