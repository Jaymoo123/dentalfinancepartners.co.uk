# A Practical Guide to Shareholders in UK Property Companies: Rights, Dividends, and Tax Treatment

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary:** Companies Act 2006 Part 17 (a company's share capital) + Part 13 (resolutions and meetings) + Part 14 (control of political donations and expenditure — limited relevance) + Part 18 (acquisition by limited company of its own shares — for buy-back planning). Shareholder rights anchored at CA 2006 ss.21 (alteration of articles), ss.281-300 (resolutions), ss.392+ (year-end), ss.829+ (distributions and lawfulness test). **Stage 2 must verify precise CA 2006 section numbers + headings at write time per §16.35 (WebFetch against legislation.gov.uk currently returning JS-anti-bot 202 in env).**
- **Supporting (tax-side):** Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005 (ITTOIA 2005) — s.624 (settlements legislation, settlor-attribution risk where consideration is not full for shares); s.626 (spouse-exception carve-out from s.624 per *Jones v Garnett* [2007] UKHL 35 — §21.2 lock); Part 4 Chapter 3 (taxation of dividends from UK resident companies). Dividend tax rates 2026/27: £500 dividend allowance; **10.75% basic / 35.75% higher / 39.35% additional** (basic and higher raised by 2pp from 6 April 2026 per FA 2026; F-20 Wave 4 correction — §21.4 lock).
- **Supporting (CGT):** TCGA 1992 s.4 (CGT rates 18% / 24% on residential disposals, 10% / 20% on non-residential), s.17 (deemed disposal at market value on connected-party transfers), s.165 (holdover relief for trading-company shares; NOT available for investment-FIC shares — §21.5 lock), s.169B-169G (settlor-interested trust exclusions from holdover).
- **Supporting (IHT):** IHTA 1984 — investment-FIC shares do NOT qualify for BPR per *Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC); growth-share design transfers value out of estate as PETs (§22.6 lock).
- **House position reference:** §21.2 (share-class structures and the settlements legislation — alphabet shares, growth shares, *Jones v Garnett* spouse-exception carve-out, minor-child anti-attribution); §21.4 (dividend rates 2026/27 with F-20 correction to 10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35%); §21.5 (FIC mechanics — share-class architecture, CIHC status, share-gift CGT + IHT treatment); §22.6 (FIC as estate-planning value-freeze tool — IHT-side framing of share gifts).
