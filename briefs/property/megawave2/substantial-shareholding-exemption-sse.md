# Substantial Shareholding Exemption (SSE): The Schedule 7AC TCGA 1992 Reference for Property Groups — Family of Exemptions, FA 2017 Reforms, Para 5 Anti-Avoidance, and the s.179 Degrouping Interaction

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (the SSE regime itself):** TCGA 1992 Schedule 7AC (inserted by FA 2002 s.44 + Sch 8). The schedule operates by deeming, on disposal of qualifying shares by a company, that any chargeable gain (or allowable loss) does not arise — **automatic; no claim; no election.** **Operative for property groups:** SSE on the share-sale exit route lets a HoldCo dispose of a subsidiary's shares free of corporation tax on the gain, provided the subsidiary is (or has been within the relevant window) a TRADING COMPANY (Sch 7AC Pt 3 + para 19 trading-company test + HMRC CG53116 "substantial" = 20%+ threshold). The mirror loss-disallowance under para 1(2) is the key asymmetric trap — SSE is unambiguously good on a profitable disposal and unambiguously bad on a loss-making one. Cross-reference §28 trading-vs-investment + Pawson investment-line for the trading-test boundary.
- **Primary (the substantial shareholding test):** Sch 7AC para 7 — investing company must have held 10%+ of ordinary share capital + 10% of rights to profits available for distribution + 10% of rights to assets on a winding-up, for a continuous 12-month period within the six years before the disposal. **Operative for property groups:** the 12-month-within-6-years window means structures retrofitted in the disposal year FAIL the test; structuring decisions must be in place 12-18 months ahead of any planned exit. The three-limb test (share-capital + profits + assets) means unusual share structures (preference shares, deferred shares, non-voting shares, alphabet-share arrangements per §21.2) can BREAK one limb even where the headline 10% holding looks fine.
- **Primary (the trading-company test — para 19):** Sch 7AC para 19 (single-company subsidiary) + para 20 (subsidiary that is the holding company of a trading sub-group). The "trading company" definition imports the badges-of-trade case law (cross-reference §28.5) and the HMRC CG53116 operative position that "substantial" non-trading activities means 20%+ on any reasonable measure (income, asset values, expense allocations, employee/director time, overall purpose). **Operative for property groups:** Pawson investment-line means BTL subsidiary fails para 19 categorically; development-for-sale subsidiary passes para 19; build-to-rent subsidiary fails para 19 (end-purpose test). Cross-reference §28 + existing pages on the trading-investment line.
- **Primary (the immediately-before / immediately-after rule — FA 2011 amendments):** Sch 7AC para 19(2) (post-FA 2011 form) — trading-company test applied immediately before AND immediately after the disposal, NOT throughout the 12-month substantial-shareholding period (which was the original FA 2002 form). FA 2011 narrowing means a company that ceased trading 18 months pre-disposal can still pass the substantial-shareholding test as a trading-company-at-the-time, provided trading status was held at some 12-month point in the prior 6 years. **Operative for property groups:** the immediately-after limb is the single most common SSE planning failure — DevCo that has sold its last unit and become a cash-shell at completion FAILS the immediately-after test even if it held trading status 12 months earlier. Sequencing matters. (HMRC published examples at CG53127+ illustrate the trap.)
- **Primary (the FA 2017 reform package — removed investing-company trading requirement):** F(No.2)A 2017 s.27 + Sch 5 — three operative changes: (a) **REMOVED** the prior requirement that the INVESTING company (the seller) be itself a trading company or member of a trading group (the prior "TC investing in TC" architecture); (b) **EXTENDED** the post-disposal trading-test window for cases where the subsidiary is being wound down (Sch 7AC para 19 (2A) — the post-disposal "for as long as required" clause replaced); (c) **ADDED** the new QII exemption at Sch 7AC para 3A — qualifying-institutional-investor route (where ≥80% of the investing company's ordinary share capital is held by qualifying institutional investors like pension funds + sovereign wealth funds + life assurance + UK regulated CISs + charities — full list at HMRC CG53080P+). **Operative for property groups:** post-2017 a HoldCo that itself sits on investment subsidiaries (BTL InvestCos) can ALSO benefit from SSE on disposal of a separately-held trading subsidiary (DevCo). Pre-2017 structures where the InvestCo BTL-heavy HoldCo blocked SSE on DevCo disposal are now unblocked. The QII exemption opens SSE to PE/institutional-fund-owned property structures previously trapped by the trading-investing-co requirement.
- **Primary (the para 5 main-purpose anti-avoidance):** Sch 7AC para 5 — SSE disapplied if the disposal forms part of arrangements the main purpose (or one of the main purposes) of which is the avoidance of a charge to tax. **Operative for property groups:** the para 5 test is the principal HMRC challenge route on engineered SSE structures (e.g., parking-an-asset-into-a-trading-subsidiary-shortly-before-share-sale; using SSE to disguise what is in substance a property-asset disposal; intra-group transfer mechanics where DevCo never genuinely operated as a trading entity). The "main purpose" wording is broader than purposive-construction Ramsay — Stage 2 verifies HMRC operative position at SDLTM01510+ analogue + HMRC ACT/CT manual. No formal clearance process for Sch 7AC; non-statutory clearance available under CIRD80570+ for genuine doubt scenarios where commercial substance is clear but legal analysis uncertain.
- **Primary (the s.179 degrouping interaction):** TCGA 1992 s.179 (degrouping charge on assets transferred intra-group within 6 years of departure) interacts with SSE in two operative directions:
  - **Modified s.179 charge into SSE-protected disposal:** FA 2011 Sch 10 modified s.179 so that the degrouping charge on assets transferred intra-group within 6 years is, on subsequent share-sale departure, added to the share-sale CONSIDERATION (and then exempted under SSE) rather than imposed as a separate company-level charge. Net effect: where SSE applies, degrouping charge effectively disappears.
  - **Asset-strip-before-sale trap:** moving the underlying asset OUT of the SSE-target company before the share sale (so the share sale captures only the shell) is the anti-SSE-stripping anti-avoidance configuration; para 5 main-purpose challenge interacts. Cross-reference §21.8 multi-company group operation lock (proposed via F-3 batch M2-A-B2) for the full multi-SPV operational layer; cross-reference existing `multi-company-group-extraction-spv-holding-co-dividend-conduit-mechanics` for intra-group asset transfers.
- **Primary (the no-claim / automatic / loss-symmetry consequence):** Sch 7AC para 1(2) (gain disregarded) + para 1(3) (loss disregarded). No claim, no election, no opt-out. **Operative consequence — the critical asymmetric trap:** where SSE applies, the seller's CHOICE is removed. A group selling a development subsidiary at a £2m LOSS cannot opt to bring the loss into charge to offset other gains — para 1(3) deeming is mandatory. The asymmetric design is intentional (HMRC operative position: SSE prevents both economic-double-taxation on gains AND economic-double-relief on losses) but the operational result is that LOSS-MAKING SSE-eligible share disposals are tax-orphan losses — significant for property groups exiting underperforming development subsidiaries.
