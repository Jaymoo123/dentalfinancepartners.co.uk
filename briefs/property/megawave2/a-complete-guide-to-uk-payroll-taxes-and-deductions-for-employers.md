# A Complete Guide to UK Payroll Taxes and Deductions for Property-Business Employers

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (charge to tax on employment income): **Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 ("ITEPA 2003")** — Part 2 (charge to tax on employment income, ss.3-61L), Part 3 (earnings + benefits code, ss.62-226A), Part 4 (exemptions), Part 11 (PAYE — ss.682-712). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2003/1/contents on 2026-05-26.
- Primary (PAYE operational mechanics): **The Income Tax (Pay As You Earn) Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/2682)** — Part 2 (codes, regs 13-20), Part 3 (deduction and repayment, regs 21-67A), Part 4 (payments, returns and information including RTI, regs 67B-97M), Part 5 (employers, regs 98-104). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2682/contents on 2026-05-26.
  - **Reg 21** — duty to deduct PAYE by reference to employee code.
  - **Reg 67B** — Real Time Information (RTI) returns: the Full Payment Submission (FPS) is the operative on-or-before-payment-day return.
  - **Reg 67G** — payment cycle for RTI employers (typically by 22nd of the following month where paid electronically, 19th by cheque).
  - **Reg 73** — annual return obligation (legacy mechanic; RTI subsumes most of it but the historical P35/P14 references remain in the regulations).
- Primary (NIC): **Social Security Contributions and Benefits Act 1992 ("SSCBA 1992")** — Part 1, ss.1-19A; primary Class 1 contributions on employee earnings + secondary Class 1 contributions on employers; Class 1A on benefits in kind. Operative rates set annually in the Welfare Benefits Up-rating Order / National Insurance Contributions Regulations cycle.
- Primary (Apprenticeship Levy): **Finance Act 2016 ss.99-121** — 0.5% levy on employers' annual pay bill above £3 million, with £15,000 allowance.
- Supporting:
  - **Employment Rights Act 1996 s.8** — statutory itemised pay statement requirement.
  - **National Minimum Wage Act 1998** + **National Minimum Wage Regulations 2015 (SI 2015/621)** — minimum pay floor (annually uprated).
  - **Pensions Act 2008 + Automatic Enrolment Regulations** — auto-enrolment duties on UK employers with at least one worker.
  - **Education (Student Loans) (Repayment) Regulations 2009 (SI 2009/470)** + **2018 postgraduate loan regs** — student / postgraduate loan deductions via payroll.
  - **Attachment of Earnings Act 1971** + **Welfare Reform Act 2012 s.105** — court-ordered AEOs + DEAs (Direct Earnings Attachments).
- House position reference: payroll is NOT a locked HP cluster in current scope (§3 covers MTD for ITSA only; §11.A covers Companies House ECCTA; §21 covers Ltd Co + FIC operational tax but not payroll mechanics). **NEW HP LOCK CANDIDATE** for Stage 1b: a §33 (or similar) "Payroll mechanics for property-business employers" cluster anchored on ITEPA 2003 + SI 2003/2682 + SSCBA 1992. Raise as F-100 in flags (see below).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

The existing site has a `accountant-payroll-services.md` services page (high-level firm-services framing, not an operational walkthrough) and a `section-24-employment-income-paye-landlords.md` page (narrow s.24 angle: how PAYE income interacts with the basic-rate-restricted finance-cost credit for individual landlords with side-employment). Neither covers the operative payroll-as-an-employer framework, and neither is property-business specific.

This page takes the **landlord-becomes-an-employer** angle: the property investor whose portfolio has scaled to the point where they hire an in-house bookkeeper, an on-site property manager for an HMO, a refurb foreman for a regular pipeline of refurbs, or staff for a serviced-accommodation operation. The page is the gateway to four follow-ups already in the MW2 candidate pool: `ensuring-compliance-with-uk-payroll-reporting-regulations` (C5 — operational compliance deep), `essential-guidelines-for-running-payroll-effectively` (C7 — process discipline), and the IR35 / off-payroll handover triggered where the "employee" is actually a personal-service company supplying the same labour.

The single closest competitor framing is the generic "PAYE for small employers" gov.uk + accountancy-firm pages. The Property Tax Partners angle is twofold: (1) the property-investor cohort hits payroll later than other small businesses (typical first hire is at portfolio scale 8-15 units, not at year-1 startup), so the page assumes a sophisticated owner already running a LtdCo BTL portfolio; (2) the cross-engagement with corporation tax (employer NIC + apprenticeship levy + salary deductible against CT profits per CTA 2009 Part 3 / Part 9) is foregrounded, not bolted on.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. When does a property-business owner become an "employer" for PAYE purposes (the first-payment trigger and the registration deadline under SI 2003/2682 reg 9)?
2. What is the rate stack on a £30k salaried in-house bookkeeper for a landlord LtdCo in 2026/27 — gross-to-net for the employee, total cost-to-employer for the company?
3. Which Class of NIC applies on what — primary Class 1 employee, secondary Class 1 employer, Class 1A on benefits, Class 1B on PSAs — and what are the operative thresholds for 2026/27?
4. How does the Employment Allowance (currently £10,500 per qualifying employer per tax year) interact with secondary NIC, and which property-business structures qualify (single-director LtdCos do NOT qualify per ER allowance rules)?
5. What does the FPS submission look like for a landlord LtdCo running monthly payroll, and what are the penalty triggers for late or absent submissions under SI 2003/2682 reg 67E?
6. What are the statutory deduction priorities when an employee has student loan + court-ordered AEO + pension auto-enrolment overlapping?
7. How does the Apprenticeship Levy apply to property-business groups, and when does the £3 million pay-bill threshold engage for portfolio operators with multiple SPVs?
8. What benefits-in-kind issues do landlord-employers commonly trip on (employer-provided accommodation for live-in property managers under ITEPA 2003 ss.97-113; employer-provided vehicle for refurb foreman; private fuel)?
9. How are auto-enrolment duties triggered (jobholder-assessment thresholds), and what is the contribution stack (8% total, 3% minimum employer) once a worker is enrolled?
10. What is the operational onboarding checklist when a landlord LtdCo first hires (PAYE registration, payroll software selection, RTI gateway credentials, NEST or alternative AE provider, employment contract, NMW-compliance audit on hours and rate)?
