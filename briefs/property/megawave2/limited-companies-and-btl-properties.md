# Limited Companies and BTL Properties: The Operational Handbook for Running a BTL LtdCo (2026/27)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (the company entity):** Companies Act 2006 (CA 2006) — body-corporate framework. Cross-reference A20 (M2-A-B2) `limited-companies` pillar for entity overview; this page does NOT restate CA 2006 architecture, it stands on it.
- **Primary (corporation tax on property income):** CTA 2009 Part 4 (UK property businesses) ss.202-291 — corporation-tax treatment of UK property income. **Operative for BTL LtdCo:** property rental profits taxed as a single UK property business (s.205); loan-relationship interest fully deductible under CTA 2009 Part 5 (no Section 24 restriction — that is ITTOIA 2005 s.272A for individuals only). Cross-reference existing `section-24-vs-incorporation-which-saves-more-tax`.
- **Primary (CT rate stack):** CTA 2010 s.3 + s.18 + ss.18A-J marginal relief — 19% small profits / 25% main / 26.5% marginal effective. **Critical interaction for BTL LtdCo groups:** associated-company count under s.18D-J dilutes the £50k / £250k thresholds across the group; a 5-SPV multi-property structure has thresholds of £10k / £50k per SPV (effective £50k group / £250k group divided by associated count = 5). Cross-reference A5 M2-A-B1 marginal-relief depth.
- **Primary (statutory accounts framework — the load-bearing operational layer):** CA 2006 Part 15 (ss.380-474) — accounts + reports framework. Small companies regime (ss.382-384) + micro-entity regime (s.384A-B per SI 2013/3008 implementation) — most single-SPV BTL LtdCos qualify as micro-entities or small companies. **Accounting standards fork — the operative day-one decision:**
  - **FRS 105 (Micro-entities):** simplest; no fair-value option; investment property held at historical cost less depreciation; NO deferred tax on fair-value revaluations (because no fair-value movements). Most appropriate for single-SPV BTL LtdCos prioritising filing simplicity. FRS 105 abridged accounts; minimal disclosures.
  - **FRS 102 Section 16 (Investment Property — Fair Value Model):** investment property held at fair value through profit or loss; revaluation gains/losses recognised in P&L; **CT consequences** — fair-value revaluation gains are NOT chargeable to CT (TCGA 1992 chargeable-gains framework applies only on actual disposal); but the accounting P&L includes the revaluation movement, creating a book-vs-tax timing difference; deferred tax provision required per FRS 102 Section 29 on the gain. Suitable for BTL LtdCos where stakeholders (lenders, investors, valuers) want real-time NAV.
  - **FRS 102 Section 17 (Property, Plant and Equipment):** alternative treatment treating the property as PPE held at cost less depreciation OR at revaluation through OCI. Rare for pure BTL portfolios; more common for owner-occupied or mixed-use.
- **Primary (the accounting-standards selection decision):** the company can elect either FRS 105 or FRS 102 at first accounts; switching subsequently requires comparatives restatement and is operationally costly. **The operative decision factors:** lender requirements (some commercial BTL lenders require FRS 102 with fair-value model for portfolio valuation); investor / co-investor visibility needs; growth path (FRS 102 is more flexible if the company will exceed micro thresholds in 1-2 years; FRS 105 forces a regime change at that point).
- **Primary (legal vs beneficial title — the day-one property transfer):** Land Registration Act 2002 + Companies Act 2006 + Trustee Act 2000 — the company holds **legal title** registered at HM Land Registry under its registered name + company number; **beneficial title** also vests in the company (a true conveyance to the company, not a bare-trust nomineeship, except where the company is itself a nominee — rare for BTL). The TR1 transfer + AP1 registration application names the company as transferee with company-number annotation. Cross-reference existing `how-to-transfer-property-into-limited-company-uk` for the transfer mechanics; this page covers the post-transfer holding mechanics.
- **Primary (mortgage charge registration — the lender side):** Companies Act 2006 ss.859A-859Q (registration of charges) — any mortgage granted by the company over its property MUST be registered at Companies House within 21 days of creation (s.859A); failure means the charge is void against the liquidator + creditors (s.859H). **Operative for BTL LtdCo:** Form MR01 filed within 21 days of completion; £15 paper fee or £10 online fee per charge. **Common landlord trap:** non-bank lenders (private finance, bridging) sometimes overlook the 21-day window — the company is required to file even if the lender drops the ball. Land Registry side: Form CH1 registers the lender's charge against the title at HMLR (separate registration; both required).
- **Primary (the company-side ATED overlay):** FA 2013 ss.94-174 — Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings. Applies to companies (and partnerships with at least one corporate member) holding single dwellings valued > £500,000. **Operative reliefs for typical BTL LtdCo:** property-rental-business relief (s.133 — covers LtdCo letting to third parties at arm's length); property-developers relief (s.138); property-traders relief (s.139). ATED return required even when relief claimed (NIL return). Cross-reference existing site coverage on ATED.
- **Primary (the directors' loan / DLA treatment for intercompany funding):** CTA 2010 ss.455-464A — loans to participators. **Critical for BTL LtdCo growth path:** common pattern is for the founder-director to lend the company the deposit + working capital from personal funds; the loan to director sits as a creditor in the company accounts (NOT an s.455 trigger because money flows FROM director TO company); subsequent repayments to director from rental cashflow are NOT taxable income (return of capital). **The s.455 trap fires the other way:** company makes loan TO participator (the director) — 33.75% s.455 charge (from FA 2026 changes per F-20 §21.4; verify rate at write time) on the loan balance outstanding 9 months and 1 day after year-end. Cross-reference A19 M2-A-B1 `directors-loan-accountsdla-uk-guide` depth.
- **Primary (year-end + accounting reference date):** CA 2006 ss.391-392 — first accounting reference date (last day of month of incorporation anniversary by default); changing ARD (s.392 — shorter or longer with restrictions). **Operative for BTL LtdCo:** changing year-end can be used strategically for tax planning (defer profits into next CT period, accelerate losses, align with property-purchase timing for first-year capital allowances on integral features). Cross-reference existing `btl-limited-company-year-end-date-changing-tax-planning`.
- **Primary (intercompany / intra-group transfers):** TCGA 1992 s.171 (no-gain-no-loss intra-group transfers — 75% group test); CTA 2010 s.131+ (group relief). FA 2003 Sch 7 (SDLT group relief) — operative when re-housing a property between SPVs in a HoldCo group. Cross-reference F-3 (M2-A-B2) proposed §21.8 multi-company group operation lock + existing `multi-company-group-extraction-spv-holding-co-dividend-conduit-mechanics` + `sdlt-group-relief-schedule-7-fa-2003-claw-back-connected-party-recovery-depth`.
- **Primary (the disposal / exit framework):** TCGA 1992 ss.1-2 (CT on chargeable gains for companies — no AEA, no indexation post-2017). **Operative for BTL LtdCo exit:**
  - **Asset sale (company sells property):** chargeable gain at company level; CT at 19% / 25% / 26.5% effective; net proceeds inside company; subsequent extraction faces dividend layer (51.8% combined for HRT individual).
  - **Share sale (founder sells shares to acquirer):** CGT at individual level; BADR potentially available IF the company is a trading company (most BTL investment LtdCos are NOT trading — s.165A definition; BADR usually unavailable). Investor's relief (TCGA 1992 s.169VC+) similarly trading-only. Stage 2 verifies current BADR rates per FA 2024 s.10 increases.
  - **Voluntary Striking-Off (Companies Act 2006 ss.1003-1011):** Form DS01 if company has not traded for 3 months + meets conditions; distributions to shareholders pre-strike-off treated as capital under CTA 2010 s.1030A if ≤ £25,000 (otherwise full distribution treatment per s.1000). Limited use for BTL LtdCo — typically requires asset sale first.
  - **Members' Voluntary Liquidation (MVL):** IA 1986 Pt IV Ch III — distributions in liquidation treated as capital (s.122 IA 1986 + Sch B1 + TCGA 1992 s.165 / s.169LA BADR by analogy where eligible). Common exit route for retained-profit BTL LtdCo. **Anti-phoenix TAAR (ITA 2007 s.396B):** caught if same/similar trade resumed within 2 years; operates as anti-avoidance against MVL-then-reincorporate income-tax sidestep.
- **Primary (post-ECCTA Companies House overlay):** ECCTA 2023 Part 1 (per §11.A lock) — registered email, appropriate registered office address, confirmation statement, PSC register, ID verification (mandatory for new appointments from 18 November 2025). Cross-reference §11.A + A20 pillar §11.A coverage; this page does NOT restate ECCTA framework but cross-links.
- **House position reference:** §21 (Ltd Co + FIC family — DLA + share-class + rent-charging + salary-vs-dividends + FIC); §21.4 (rate stack with F-20 dividend rate correction); §11 + §11.A (Companies House + ECCTA); §3 (incorporation mechanics); §1.M (post-MDR-abolition SDLT). **F-3 (M2-A-B2) §21.8 multi-company group operation lock requested** — pending Stage 1b conductor decision; this page touches multi-SPV transfer mechanics if F-3 locked. **No NEW HP lock requested by THIS pick** — accounts-standards-selection mechanics (FRS 105 vs FRS 102 fair-value model) are accountancy-technical rather than HP-locked tax positions; existing locks plus operative FRS guidance suffice.
