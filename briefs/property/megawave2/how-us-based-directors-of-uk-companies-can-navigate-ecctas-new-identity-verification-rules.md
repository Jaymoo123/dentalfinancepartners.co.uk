# How US-Based Directors of UK Companies Can Navigate ECCTA's New Identity Verification Rules

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (the ECCTA ID-verification architecture — UK-side): Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 c. 56 ("ECCTA 2023"). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/56/contents on 2026-05-27 (sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35). Operative anchors (per §11.A Wave 9 mini-lock + F-14 Wave 9 RUN catch corrections 2026-05-25):
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.64** — "Identity verification of persons with significant control" (PSC-side ID verification provision).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.65** — "Procedure etc for verifying identity" (generic procedure provision applying to both director and PSC verification — the how-to of the verification process).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.66** — "Authorisation of corporate service providers" (Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs) authorisation regime — the route that US-based directors will primarily use).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.67** — "Exemption from identity verification: national security grounds" (narrow national-security carve-out — not generally relevant to US-resident directors).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.68** — "Allocation of unique identifiers" (each verified individual receives a UID / Companies House personal code used across all UK directorships + PSC interests they hold).
  - **ECCTA 2023 s.69** — "Identity verification: material unavailable for public inspection" (verified-identity data largely withheld from public inspection — protects US-resident private data on the UK public register).
  - **Director-side ID verification operative provisions** live in CA 2006 amendments inserted by ECCTA earlier in Part 1 (ss.2-7 + ss.40-45 area) plus the new CA 2006 Part 11A — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies precise section numbers at write time per §16.35.
- Primary (the commencement chain — when US-based directors must verify): per §11.A Wave 9 hardening 2026-05-25:
  - **8 April 2025:** ID verification opens VOLUNTARILY for directors + PSCs.
  - **18 November 2025:** ID verification became a LEGAL REQUIREMENT for newly appointed directors and PSCs.
  - **18 November 2025 → ~November 2026 (12-month transition window):** EXISTING directors and PSCs must verify by their next confirmation statement filed within / after the transition window. Per-company timing anchored to the confirmation-statement cycle.
- Primary (the parallel UK MLR-2017 supervision framework — for ACSPs): Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/692). ACSPs must be supervised under MLR 2017 by an AML supervisor (HMRC, FCA, professional body such as ICAEW, ACCA, ICAS, CIOT, Law Society). The ACSP's MLR-2017 supervision is the mechanism by which they can accept US identity documents on behalf of the US-resident director + complete the Companies House verification with the documents reformulated against UK-compliant criteria.
- Supporting (the US-side parallel reporting overlap — NOT a substitute for ECCTA verification): Internal Revenue Code §6038 + IRS Form 5471 (US-person owner of foreign corporation reporting obligation); FBAR (FinCEN Form 114, where the UK company has financial accounts); IRS Form 8938 (FATCA-related). Sessions writing on this page must NOT advise on US tax / regulatory matters as if Property Tax Partners has US-side expertise — the page references the US-side parallels for context and signposts US-qualified CPA / tax adviser engagement.
- Supporting (US-UK double taxation): UK/US Double Tax Treaty (1975 + 2001 protocol) — relevant where the US director receives dividends from the UK company (treaty withholding rates) or where the structure produces UK-side taxable income on US-resident individuals (Article 4 residence + Article 6 immovable property + Article 10 dividends). Stage 2 sub-agent verifies at write per §16.3. §10 main + §16.5 (Wave 2 DTAs extension) — for treaty interaction context.
- House position reference: **§11.A** Wave 9 mini-lock (the operative ECCTA architecture + commencement chain + ACSP framework + the corrected verbatim section attributions per F-14 + F-11 catches). **§11.A practical writing rules for Bucket B sessions** — B1 ID verification operational lead. **§16.5** (DTAs — UK/US specific notes). **§17** (Leaving the UK / expat — Wave 2) for cross-reference where the US-resident director is a UK expat. **§13** main do-not-write applies. **§11.A do-not-write list** applies — "ID verification is voluntary" (false now), "Registered email is published on the public register" (false), "Each SPV needs separate ID verification" (false — one natural-person verification covers all directorships + PSC interests).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **Can I verify identity from the US using GOV.UK One Login?** (Short answer: technically yes if you can complete the GOV.UK One Login flow, but in practice the GOV.UK One Login service is designed and optimised for UK-resident users with UK-issued identity documents — UK driving licence + UK passport + UK address. US-resident users may face difficulties: the GOV.UK One Login document-photo-and-face-match flow accepts a US passport, but the address-verification + UK-residence-anchoring elements of the wider GOV.UK One Login user journey may not be straightforward for a US-domiciled user. The PRACTICAL preferred route for US-based directors is the ACSP route — where a UK-authorised Authorised Corporate Service Provider accepts US identity documents under their MLR 2017 supervision and completes the Companies House verification on the US director's behalf. Sub-agent flags: the GOV.UK One Login operational user-journey is evolving — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies the current US-resident user experience at write per §16.35 against gov.uk One Login service status + Companies House published guidance.)
2. **The ACSP route — what is it, why is it the practical preferred route for US-based directors?** (Authorised Corporate Service Provider — a firm AML-supervised under MLR 2017 (typically a UK accountant, company-secretarial firm, or solicitor) that has registered with Companies House to perform identity verification on behalf of clients per ECCTA s.66. For US-based directors, the ACSP is the practical preferred route because: (a) the ACSP is geographically + culturally fluent in US-side identity documents — they accept US passport + US driver's license + US Social Security Card + US utility bills + US bank statements; (b) the ACSP completes the Companies House submission on the US director's behalf, removing the GOV.UK One Login user-journey from the US director's responsibility; (c) the ACSP fee is typically £150-£300 one-off (verify at write against current market rates) — paid once; the resulting Companies House personal code covers every UK directorship + PSC interest the US director holds + is permanent (does not need to be renewed annually); (d) the ACSP can provide ongoing service for confirmation-statement filings, ID-document-update mechanics + annual compliance as part of a broader UK-side adviser relationship. Forward-link to existing site ACSP-cluster pages for the ACSP architecture deep-dive.)
3. **Which US identity documents does the ACSP accept?** (Under MLR 2017 + Companies House published ID-document standards: (i) **PHOTO IDENTITY** — US passport (PRIMARY accepted document, used by most US-resident clients); US state-issued driver's license (acceptable if accompanied by a secondary document); US military ID (acceptable for serving members); US permanent-resident card (acceptable for green-card holders); (ii) **ADDRESS-OF-RESIDENCE** — US utility bills (electricity, gas, water, telecoms) dated within last 3 months; US bank statements dated within last 3 months; US state-issued formal address-verification letter; US property-tax bill / mortgage statement; (iii) **OTHER** — US Social Security Card may be used as supplementary identity-verification document. ACSP firms have firm-specific document policies under their MLR 2017 supervision — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies acceptable-document list at write against the chosen ACSP firm's published acceptance list. PERMANENTLY EXCLUDED: documents issued by non-recognised authorities; documents not in English (require certified translation); documents lacking machine-readable security features (some older US state IDs).)
4. **What does the ACSP fee + workflow look like?** (Typical workflow over 5-10 business days: (i) US director contacts ACSP + signs engagement letter; (ii) US director provides US identity documents via secure encrypted upload portal (most ACSPs use cloud-based KYC platforms — Onfido, Veriff, Yoti, Jumio); (iii) ACSP conducts video-verification call (typically 15-30 minutes, scheduled around US/UK time zones) where US director shows ID + live face + answers questions; (iv) ACSP performs underlying database checks (electoral-register equivalents + US background checks via approved providers + sanctions screening + PEPs screening); (v) ACSP submits ID verification request to Companies House; (vi) Companies House issues the personal code to the verified individual + ACSP receives confirmation; (vii) ACSP delivers personal code + confirmation documentation to US director. Typical fee: £150-£300 one-off (verify at write); some ACSPs bundle into broader UK-side adviser package; some charge per-director-per-directorship though most apply per-natural-person. Personal code is PERMANENT — covers all UK directorships + PSC interests the US director will ever hold.)
5. **Time-zone + remote video-verification mechanics?** (US/UK time-zone spread is 5-8 hours depending on US time zone. ACSP video-verification calls are typically scheduled to fit within the ACSP's UK working hours (8am-6pm GMT/BST) — which translates to early-morning-US for East Coast (3am-1pm EST) + middle-of-night-US for West Coast (12am-10am PST). Most ACSPs offer flexible scheduling with extended hours (some until 9pm UK time = 4pm EST / 1pm PST) for US-resident clients. Some ACSPs also accept pre-recorded video verification with separate quality-control review. Document transmission is via secure cloud KYC platforms — no postal exchange of documents required. The remote-verification standard meets MLR 2017 + Companies House requirements as long as the document-and-face-match meets KYC standards.)
6. **The 18 November 2025 commencement + 12-month transition window — what is my deadline?** (Two-track deadline regime: (i) **NEW APPOINTMENTS** — if you are being newly appointed as a director or PSC of a UK company on or after 18 November 2025, you must be verified BEFORE the appointment can be filed at Companies House. Operationally: the ID verification + personal code allocation must complete BEFORE the AP01 / PSC01 filing; (ii) **EXISTING APPOINTMENTS** — if you were a director or PSC of a UK company before 18 November 2025, you have a 12-month transition window closing approximately November 2026 to be verified by your next confirmation statement. The precise per-company deadline is anchored to the confirmation-statement cycle of each UK company you are a director/PSC of — so if you are a director of three UK companies with confirmation statements due in February 2026 + May 2026 + September 2026, you must be verified before the earliest of those filing dates (February 2026) to ensure all three filings can proceed. ONE NATURAL-PERSON VERIFICATION COVERS ALL UK DIRECTORSHIPS + PSC INTERESTS — you do not need to verify separately per company. Sub-agent flags: commencement dates pre-locked at §11.A; Stage 2 sub-agent verifies against gov.uk campaign page at write per §11.A F-12.)
7. **FATCA + Form 5471 + FBAR + ECCTA — the four-way US/UK disclosure overlap?** (FOUR PARALLEL DISCLOSURE OBLIGATIONS — NOT DUPLICATIVE OR SUBSTITUTABLE: (i) **UK ECCTA Companies House verification + public register disclosure** — UK-side; verified identity + personal code + public-register entries on directorship + PSC interest + share capital + registered office; this is the topic of this page; (ii) **US IRS Form 5471** — US-side; US persons (US citizens + US residents + green-card holders) who are officers, directors, or 10%+ shareholders of foreign (i.e. non-US) corporations must file Form 5471 annually with their US federal tax return; multiple categories (1-5) depending on level of ownership + officer status; substantial reporting requirements + significant penalties for non-filing ($10k+ per failure); (iii) **FBAR — FinCEN Form 114** — US-side; US persons with financial interest in or signature authority over foreign financial accounts with aggregate value > $10k at any point in calendar year must file FBAR annually; UK company bank accounts are reportable; (iv) **IRS Form 8938 / FATCA** — US-side; US persons with specified foreign financial assets above certain thresholds must file Form 8938 with US federal tax return. **CRITICAL: each of these is INDEPENDENT — completing the UK ECCTA verification does NOT satisfy any of the US-side reporting obligations + vice versa.** US persons must engage US-qualified CPA / tax adviser separately for US-side compliance. Property Tax Partners (UK firm) handles UK-side ECCTA + UK-side tax; US-side compliance requires US-qualified adviser. Stage 2 sub-agent: do NOT advise on US-side compliance as if PTP is US-qualified — refer + signpost.)
8. **What about UK/US Double Tax Treaty implications?** (Where the US director receives UK-source income (dividends from the UK company + management fees + rent passed to the US director directly), treaty allocation under UK/US Double Tax Treaty (1975 + 2001 protocol) applies. Article 4 (residence tiebreaker) determines treaty residence — typically clear for US-resident-only individual + UK company + US residence wins. Article 6 (immovable property) — UK property income is UK-taxed even where shareholder is US-resident — NRL Scheme applies to direct rental income; for UK-LtdCo-held rental, the company pays UK CT on rental income then the dividend to US shareholder is treaty-rate withheld (typically 15% under Article 10 unless qualifying for lower rates). Article 10 (dividends) — UK withholding tax on dividends to US-resident shareholder is treaty-rate capped (usually 15% portfolio rate; lower for substantial-shareholder cases). Article 13 (capital gains) — UK CGT on UK property still bites US-resident shareholder per UK NRCGT rules. US-side foreign tax credit available on US federal return for UK tax paid. Cross-link to existing site UK/US treaty + non-resident-landlord pages for the deeper treaty mechanics. §16.5 reference. Property Tax Partners (UK firm) handles UK side; US-qualified CPA handles US side.)
9. **What are the consequences of non-verification?** (Three-layer consequence stack per §11.A: (i) **BLOCKED COMPANIES HOUSE FILINGS** — confirmation statement filings cannot proceed without personal code from each in-scope director + PSC; the company falls into default on its CA 2006 s.853A duty + accrues civil penalty under Sch 1B CA 2006 (up to £5,000 per failure); (ii) **CRIMINAL OFFENCE FOR CONTINUING TO ACT UNVERIFIED** — once the transition window closes (approximately November 2026 for existing roles; immediately for new appointments from 18 November 2025), continuing to act as a director or PSC without a personal code is an offence (specific section attributions Stage 2 sub-agent verifies at write per §16.35 — director-side offences live in CA 2006 amendments inserted by ECCTA); (iii) **DIRECTOR DISQUALIFICATION + STRIKING-OFF EXPOSURE** — Companies House may pursue director disqualification proceedings under Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986 + strike-off proceedings against the company itself under CA 2006 ss.1000-1011 for continued non-compliance. For US-resident directors, criminal-track exposure raises practical extradition + cross-border-enforcement concerns — Companies House Enforcement has historically pursued civil penalties + strike-off rather than criminal prosecution of overseas individuals, but the regime gives them the option.)
10. **What is the practical compliance checklist for a US-based director?** (Eight-step operational checklist: (i) identify every UK directorship + PSC interest you currently hold or are about to take up; (ii) identify the confirmation-statement filing date for each UK company you are a director/PSC of (look up via Companies House at https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/); (iii) determine your earliest required-verification date — for new appointments, BEFORE the appointment can be filed; for existing roles, BEFORE the next confirmation statement after 18 November 2025 within the 12-month transition window; (iv) select a UK-authorised ACSP — typically your UK accountant or company-secretarial agent; verify the firm is registered as an ACSP at Companies House + AML-supervised under MLR 2017; (v) gather US identity documents — US passport + US driver's license + US utility bills (last 3 months) + US bank statement (last 3 months); (vi) book ACSP video-verification call + complete onboarding within 5-10 business days; (vii) receive personal code from ACSP + record in your records — the code is permanent and covers ALL UK directorships + PSC interests you will ever hold; (viii) quote personal code on each subsequent confirmation statement + appointment filing. Separately: engage US-qualified CPA / tax adviser to address Form 5471 + FBAR + Form 8938 + FATCA US-side obligations — these are independent of the UK ECCTA verification.)
11. **What about US-resident PSCs who are not directors?** (The verification regime applies to BOTH directors AND PSCs (persons with significant control — 25%+ shares / voting / appointment rights / significant influence per CA 2006 PSC tests). A US-resident family member who is a 25%+ shareholder (or otherwise qualifying PSC) of a UK FIC or UK property-SPV but who is NOT a director must STILL be ID-verified. Same routes available — ACSP preferred for US-resident PSCs. Same deadline regime applies — for existing PSCs, verification before next confirmation statement within transition window; for new PSCs (e.g. a US family member receiving share-class transfer post-incorporation), verification before the appointment can be filed. Operational reality: for FIC structures with multi-generation US-resident family-member PSCs, ALL US-resident PSCs must individually verify. Cross-link to existing FIC cluster pages for the PSC architecture context.)
12. **What about US LLCs as members / directors of UK companies?** (Where a UK company has a US LLC as a member or where US LLC is a "corporate director" of UK company: separate corporate-director regime. ECCTA 2023 reforms restrict corporate directors generally — operative provision Stage 2 sub-agent verifies at write per §16.35. For US LLC as a SHAREHOLDER of UK company (most common pattern): the LLC is a "person" but the PSC architecture looks through to the LLC's ultimate beneficial owners — the natural persons behind the US LLC may themselves be in-scope PSCs requiring individual verification. The "look-through" mechanic per CA 2006 PSC regulations. Stage 2 sub-agent verifies precise look-through mechanic + corporate-director-restriction operative provision at write per §16.35.)
13. **What if I am no longer a UK-resident — am I a UK expat with continuing UK directorships?** (US-resident UK expat is a common reader-profile: UK national who has emigrated to the US, retains UK directorships + PSC interests in UK family companies / family-investment companies / UK property structures. Same ECCTA verification regime applies — natural-person verification through ACSP preferred; UK identity documents acceptable (UK passport + UK driving licence even if no longer UK-resident). Some UK expats may have US-issued documents (green card + US driver's license) + UK-issued documents (UK passport); ACSP accepts whichever is most operationally convenient. Additional UK-side considerations for expat: §17 SRT residence test + IHT long-term-residence per §22.X may affect tax position separately; cross-link to existing expat-cluster pages for the wider expat tax framework.)
