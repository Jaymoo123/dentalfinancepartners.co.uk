## Key questions this page must answer

1. **Where do I start — what is Step 1 of the roadmap?** (Step 1 = inventory. Two lists: (a) every UK company in your portfolio (SPVs, holding companies, management companies, LP general partners — every entity with a Companies House filing obligation); (b) every natural person holding a director or PSC role at any of those companies. The lists are the input to every subsequent step. Most landlord LtdCo portfolios with 5-15 SPVs have 1-3 natural persons across the directorships and PSC roles; the inventory is short. Output artefact: a spreadsheet with company name, company number, confirmation statement filing date, registered office address, registered email status, current directors, current PSCs.)
2. **What is Step 2 — verifying every natural person's identity?** (Step 2 = book ID verification for every natural person on the list. Each natural person verifies ONCE under ECCTA s.68 and receives a personal code that covers every directorship + PSC interest across every UK company they are associated with. Route choice: GOV.UK One Login (free, self-service, 10-30 minutes for biometric-passport holders) OR ACSP (your accountant or company secretary, typically £20-£100 fee where charged — verify at write). Book this in Q1 of the 12-month transition window, NOT Q4 — the verification process has occasional document-check delays; do not let a verification queue jam your confirmation statement filing. Output artefact: a personal code per natural person, stored securely.)
3. **What is Step 3 — refreshing registered email + appropriate-address on every SPV?** (Step 3 = at each SPV, confirm (a) the registered email address is in place and active (CA 2006 inserted-s.88A) and (b) the registered office meets the "appropriate address" test (CA 2006 inserted appropriate-address rule under ss.28-30 commencement block — capable of acknowledging service of documents; PO boxes generally do not qualify). For SPVs whose registered office is a director's residential address or an accountant's office, these typically qualify. For SPVs using a pure PO-box mail-forwarding service, reroute to an appropriate-address provider before the next confirmation statement. Output artefact: updated registered email + (if needed) updated registered office for each SPV.)
4. **What is Step 4 — filing the next confirmation statement?** (Step 4 = at each SPV's next confirmation statement filing date (anchored to incorporation anniversary or 12 months from previous filing), file the confirmation statement with: (a) each director's verified personal code; (b) each PSC's verified personal code; (c) the lawful purposes statement (positive declaration that intended future activities are lawful); (d) the registered email address; (e) the appropriate-address registered office. Fee schedule (verify at write): £50 online / £110 paper. Companies House will refuse the filing if personal codes are missing for any in-scope director or PSC. Output artefact: filed confirmation statement per SPV with all five elements in place.)
5. **What is Step 5 — ongoing maintenance through the transition window?** (Step 5 = monthly / quarterly maintenance. Trigger events that require interim filings during the transition window: (a) new director or PSC appointment — appointment cannot be recorded without verified personal code for the new natural person (book verification before the appointment paperwork); (b) departure of director or PSC — termination filed via TM01 / equivalent online; (c) change of registered office or registered email — change filed promptly; (d) change of PSC controller threshold — disclosable via PSC notification; (e) RoE updating statement (if applicable to any overseas-entity vehicle in the portfolio) — due within 14 days of the end of each update period. Output artefact: an interim-filings log keyed to trigger events.)
6. **What is Step 6 — handling Limited Partnerships under ECCTA Part 2?** (Step 6 applies ONLY if the portfolio includes any LP under LPA 1907 (property-fund LPs, JV-development LPs, family-investment LPs). ECCTA Part 2 phased rollout brings LPs into a CA-2006-style regime: registered office, annual confirmation, GP identity verification via s.66 ACSP regime, striking-off powers, information-disclosure obligations. State moves — verify at the campaign page at write per §11.A + F-1 / §11.B candidate. Output artefact: per-LP compliance log keyed to ECCTA Part 2 phase rollout.)
7. **What is Step 7 — handling overseas-entity counterparties?** (Step 7 applies where the portfolio deals with any overseas entity holding UK property (overseas-entity vendor, lender with overseas-entity SPV, JV partner using overseas-entity vehicle). RoE check: counterparty must be on the Companies House Register of Overseas Entities with a current updating statement (within 14 days of the end of each update period). HMLR disposition-block under LRA 2002 Sch 4A blocks non-compliant overseas entities from dispositions. Practical check at deal stage: verify counterparty's overseas entity ID + most recent updating statement filing date via Companies House. Output artefact: counterparty RoE compliance check log.)
8. **What deadlines anchor the roadmap?** (Three deadline categories: (a) static commencement dates — 4 March 2024 registered email + appropriate address; 5 March 2024 lawful purposes statement; 18 November 2025 ID verification legal requirement for new appointments; (b) rolling transition deadline — for each SPV with existing directors and PSCs, verification due by the next confirmation statement filed within the 12-month transition window ending ~November 2026; (c) trigger-event deadlines — new appointment requires verified code before recording; termination filing within 14 days; RoE updating statement within 14 days of end of update period; etc. The roadmap key deadline for most portfolios is the SPV-specific "next confirmation statement within the transition window" — for an SPV whose confirmation statement falls in March 2026, all verification must complete by March 2026; for one in September 2026, by September 2026.)
9. **What artefacts should I keep — what is the documentation footprint?** (Practitioner artefact list: (a) portfolio inventory spreadsheet (companies + natural persons + filing dates + current state); (b) personal code register (one code per natural person, stored securely + accessible at filing time); (c) verification route + cost record per natural person (One Login vs ACSP); (d) registered email + appropriate-address confirmation per SPV; (e) lawful purposes statement record (annual basis); (f) interim filings log keyed to trigger events; (g) RoE counterparty check log (where applicable); (h) LP Part 2 compliance log (where applicable). For a small portfolio (1-3 natural persons, 5-15 SPVs), the documentation footprint is modest — one spreadsheet plus a personal-code register.)
10. **What goes wrong if I do not navigate this in sequence?** (Common failure modes: (a) confirmation statement filing refused at deadline because a director's personal code is missing — typically resolves with verification booked late but risks late-filing penalties; (b) new director appointment paperwork cannot record because verification was not pre-arranged; (c) RoE counterparty disposition blocked at HMLR because counterparty updating statement was overdue — deal stalls; (d) registered office address fails appropriate-address test in a Companies House review — company has to reroute under deadline pressure; (e) registered email goes stale (forwarding address bounces) — CH correspondence missed. Each failure mode is preventable by following the roadmap in sequence; each becomes harder to resolve under deadline pressure.)
11. **How do I budget time and cost for the roadmap?** (Time budget for a typical small landlord LtdCo portfolio (1-3 natural persons, 5-15 SPVs): Step 1 inventory — 1-2 hours total; Step 2 verification — 30-60 minutes per natural person; Step 3 email + appropriate-address refresh — 15-30 minutes per SPV; Step 4 next confirmation statement filing — already in the annual cycle, marginal time for personal-code entry; Step 5 ongoing maintenance — modest unless multiple trigger events occur; Steps 6-7 only if relevant. Cost budget: One Login route is free; ACSP route is typically £20-£100 per natural person (verify at write); registered office reroute (if needed) typically £50-£300/year per SPV (verify at write); confirmation statement fees £50 online per SPV per year. The roadmap is not expensive in absolute terms; the cost of failure mode (a) above is higher than the cost of the roadmap.)
12. **Where do I track operational state across the roadmap?** (Two canonical sources per §11.A: primary campaign page `https://changestoukcompanylaw.campaign.gov.uk/` (with per-topic sub-pages); secondary Companies House blog `https://companieshouse.blog.gov.uk/`. Both should be reviewed at the start of each quarter for state changes. DO NOT use the deprecated `gov.uk/government/news/changes-to-uk-company-law` URL — returns HTTP 404 per F-12. Cross-link to B2 / B6 / B7 / B11 / B12 + existing deep dives for the per-reform depth once a specific reform comes into your roadmap focus.)
