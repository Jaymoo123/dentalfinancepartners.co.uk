# Companies House Changes for Limited Partnerships: ECCTA 2023 Part 2 Reforms to the Limited Partnerships Act 1907

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary:** Limited Partnerships Act 1907 (LPA 1907), as substantively amended by **Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 (ECCTA 2023) Part 2** (sections 110-145 territory — covering LP reforms; **Stage 2 must verify precise ECCTA Part 2 section range + each amending section heading at write time per §16.35 — current env WebFetch returning JS-anti-bot 202 from legislation.gov.uk; Stage 2 to re-verify via browser**).

- **Supporting (existing LP framework):** LPA 1907 ss.1-17 (the original short Act — partner roles, formation, dissolution mechanics). The LP "limited partner" liability shield only applies where the limited partner does NOT participate in management; ECCTA 2023 does not alter this fundamental architecture but tightens disclosure + transparency around the LP.
- **Supporting (tax-side):** LPs are tax-transparent for income + CGT purposes (each partner taxed on their share of profits / gains per partnership taxation rules — ITTOIA 2005 + TCGA 1992 Part III); the ECCTA reforms do NOT change tax treatment but increase compliance + filing burden. This page is **NOT** primarily a tax page; it is a Companies House compliance page.
