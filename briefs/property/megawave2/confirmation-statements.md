# Confirmation Statements: What They Are, What They Contain, and What Has Changed Under ECCTA 2023 — Complete UK Guide for Landlord LtdCo Operators

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Companies Act 2006 ss.853A-853L (confirmation statement obligation — as amended by ECCTA 2023). Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2006/46/contents on 2026-05-26 (sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35). Operative anchors:
  - **CA 2006 s.853A** — Duty to deliver confirmation statements (the obligation backbone — every UK company must file at least one confirmation statement per "review period" of 12 months).
  - **CA 2006 s.853B** — Review period (defining the 12-month review cycle).
  - **CA 2006 ss.853C-G** — Substantive content of the confirmation statement (registered office, directors, PSCs, share capital and shareholders, SIC codes for principal business activities, etc.), as amended by ECCTA 2023 to add the lawful purposes statement and personal-code requirements.
  - **CA 2006 s.853L** — Offences for failure to file (criminal offence; default fines).
- Primary amendments: Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 c. 56 ("ECCTA 2023"). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/56/contents on 2026-05-26 (sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35). The four amendments relevant to the confirmation statement:
  - **ECCTA ss.28-30** — Registered email address requirement (every UK company must designate a registered email address; CA 2006 inserted-s.88A and following). Effective for new companies from 4 March 2024; for existing companies from the next confirmation statement filed.
  - **ECCTA ss.28-30 (appropriate-address rule)** — Registered office must be an "appropriate address" capable of acknowledging service of documents. Effective 4 March 2024.
  - **ECCTA ss.59-63** — Lawful purposes statement on the confirmation statement (positive declaration that the company's intended future activities are lawful). Effective 5 March 2024.
  - **ECCTA s.68 + s.64 + s.65 + ss.40-45** — Personal code requirement at filing (every in-scope director and PSC must quote their Companies House personal code at the confirmation statement filing once they are in scope of the ID verification regime). 18 November 2025 legal-requirement commencement for newly appointed roles + 12-month transition window for existing roles closing ~November 2026.
- Supporting (fee schedule): Companies Act 2006 Sch 8 + The Companies (Fees) Regulations 2024 — verify SI number at write per §16.27. **Operative fees from 1 May 2024:** £50 online / £110 paper for the annual confirmation statement (verify at write).
- Supporting (penalties): CA 2006 s.853L criminal offence for failure to file (default fine ≤ level 5 on the standard scale for E&W + daily default fine); civil financial penalty regime under Sch 1B (CA 2006 inserted by ECCTA) — maximum £5,000 per relevant filing failure (verify at write).
- House position reference: **§11 main text** + **§11.A Wave 9 mini-lock** (full statutory-section depth for ECCTA amendments; commencement chain hardened; ACSP framework; multi-SPV per-natural-person rule; do-not-write list). ## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is a confirmation statement in one paragraph?** (The annual data-refresh filing that every UK company must deliver to Companies House under CA 2006 s.853A. Each company has a "review period" of 12 months; at least one confirmation statement per review period. The filing confirms (or updates) the snapshot of the company that the public register holds: registered office, directors, PSCs, share capital and shareholders, principal business activities. Post-ECCTA 2023, it also carries the registered email address, the lawful purposes statement, and a verified personal code for every in-scope director and PSC.)
2. **What does the confirmation statement contain — the five-element walkthrough?** (Element 1: registered office address (with appropriate-address compliance post-4-March-2024). Element 2: directors (full name, service address, date of birth (last two digits redacted on public register), nationality + occupation + country of residence; with verified personal code under ECCTA s.68 once in scope of ID verification regime). Element 3: persons with significant control / PSCs (identity, nature of control — 25%+ shares / voting / appointment rights / significant influence; with verified personal code once in scope). Element 4: share capital + shareholders (statement of capital, members register snapshot for share-issuing companies). Element 5: SIC codes for principal business activities (up to four 5-digit codes from the SIC 2007 classification). Plus, since ECCTA 2023: the registered email address; the lawful purposes statement.)
3. **When is the confirmation statement due — the review period?** (Each UK company has a "review period" of 12 months that runs from the day after the previous confirmation statement filing (or, for newly incorporated companies, from the day after incorporation). The confirmation statement must be filed within 14 days of the end of the review period under CA 2006 s.853A. Companies House sends reminders ahead of the deadline but the filing obligation is on the company. Operationally: most companies file at the same calendar date each year, anchored to incorporation anniversary.)
4. **What changed under ECCTA 2023 — the four operational additions?** (Four operational additions, sequenced by commencement date: (a) **4 March 2024** — registered email address required (CA 2006 inserted-s.88A); for new companies at incorporation, for existing companies at the next confirmation statement; (b) **4 March 2024** — appropriate-address rule for registered office (must be capable of acknowledging service of documents; PO boxes generally do not qualify); (c) **5 March 2024** — lawful purposes statement (positive annual declaration that intended future activities are lawful, under ECCTA ss.59-63); (d) **18 November 2025** — verified personal code from ID verification required at filing for every newly appointed director + PSC, with a 12-month transition window for existing roles ending ~November 2026 (under ECCTA ss.64-69 + ss.40-45 + s.68). Cross-link to existing operational deep-dive page for the change-by-change walkthrough.)
5. **How much does it cost to file?** (Operative fee schedule from 1 May 2024 (verify at write per The Companies (Fees) Regulations 2024 + §16.27 rate-by-reference): £50 online / £110 paper. Online filing via the Companies House WebFiling portal or Find and update company information service; paper filing via CS01 form. The online route is overwhelmingly the operational default.)
6. **What happens if I miss the deadline?** (Failure to file the confirmation statement on time is a criminal offence under CA 2006 s.853L — default fine ≤ level 5 on the standard scale for E&W + daily default fine (rate-by-reference; verify at write per §16.27). Civil financial penalty regime under Sch 1B (CA 2006 inserted by ECCTA) — Companies House can impose up to £5,000 per relevant filing failure (verify at write). Cumulative non-compliance can lead to strike-off proceedings under CA 2006 ss.1000-1011. Even where a single late filing attracts only a small penalty, the company's credit score and underwriting profile tend to absorb a hit. For landlord LtdCos with active lender relationships, late filings show up in lender due diligence.)
7. **How does it interact with the company's other annual filings?** (The confirmation statement is one of three core annual filings for a UK company: (a) annual accounts (under CA 2006 Part 15 — separately deadlined, 9 months after the accounting reference date for private companies); (b) confirmation statement (under CA 2006 s.853A — within 14 days of the end of the review period); (c) corporation tax return (CT600, under FA 1998 Sch 18 — within 12 months of accounting period end). The three filings are independent in deadline but operationally linked: the confirmation statement keeps the register snapshot current; the accounts reflect financial state; the CT600 reflects tax computation. Landlord LtdCo operators typically calendar all three around a single annual cycle for efficiency.)
8. **Can the confirmation statement contain changes — or is it just a confirmation of existing data?** (Both. The confirmation statement can be filed either as a pure confirmation ("the data on the register is correct at this date") or with changes to most data elements (directors, PSCs, registered office, SIC codes, share capital, statement of capital, etc.). Some changes (e.g. company name change, change of registered office to a different jurisdiction within the UK) require separate filings; most ongoing data-refresh changes can be wrapped into the confirmation statement. Operationally: keep the register snapshot continuously current through interim filings (TM01 for terminating an appointment, AP01 for appointing a new director, change-of-particulars filings as needed); the confirmation statement then confirms rather than discovering.)
9. **What is the personal code requirement — what does it look like at filing?** (Post-18 November 2025 for newly appointed roles + by next confirmation statement within 12-month transition window for existing roles: every in-scope director and every in-scope PSC must quote their Companies House personal code (under ECCTA s.68) on the confirmation statement filing. The personal code is per natural person — one verification covers every directorship and PSC interest across every UK company. Filing portal will prompt for the code; missing codes block the filing. Cross-link to B2 (ID verification definitional) and existing operational walkthrough for the route choice (One Login vs ACSP) and the verification mechanic.)
10. **What is the lawful purposes statement — is it a meaningful obligation?** (Positive annual declaration that the company's intended future activities are lawful (ECCTA ss.59-63, effective 5 March 2024). Typically a tick-box on the online filing. Substantive value depends on enforcement: false statements are an offence under the existing CA 2006 + false-statement offences framework; the attestation creates a documented record. For honest companies, a tick-box; for dishonest companies, an evidentiary artefact in subsequent fraud or false-statement proceedings. Companies cannot meaningfully "qualify" the attestation — the declaration is binary (intended future activities are lawful, yes / no).)
11. **What is the registered email address requirement — and is it public?** (Every UK company must designate a registered email address (CA 2006 inserted-s.88A under ECCTA ss.28-30, effective 4 March 2024). The registered email is a Companies House-to-company correspondence channel — it is NOT published on the public register and counterparties / members of the public cannot see it. Common operational pattern for landlord LtdCo portfolios: use the accountant's monitored mailbox or a director's dedicated business email. Keep the address active and monitored — if Companies House correspondence bounces, the company is in default of its registered-email obligation, which is an offence.)
12. **What is the appropriate-address rule for the registered office?** (CA 2006 inserted appropriate-address rule under ss.28-30, effective 4 March 2024: the registered office must be (a) where service of documents can be acknowledged + (b) where someone is present to receive correspondence + (c) that meets Companies House's appropriate-address criteria. PO boxes and pure mail-forwarding services generally do not qualify. Staffed virtual offices, accountant offices, or director residential addresses typically do. Companies that fail the test can be subject to Companies House moving the registered office to a default address — which then triggers a series of compliance flags on the file.)
13. **What does the annual confirmation statement look like for a typical landlord LtdCo SPV?** (Typical small landlord LtdCo SPV: one director (the landlord), one PSC (the landlord, holding >25% of shares), one registered office (accountant's office or director's home address — appropriate-address compliant), one registered email (accountant's monitored mailbox), one share class with one issued share to the director, SIC code 68209 "Other letting and operating of own or leased real estate". Annual filing: £50 online, ~10 minutes via WebFiling once the data is settled, lawful purposes tick-box, director's verified personal code quoted, PSC's verified personal code quoted (same code where landlord is both director and PSC), confirmation statement filed. Multi-SPV portfolios with one common director / PSC: per-SPV filing × number of SPVs, one verified personal code amortised across all.)
