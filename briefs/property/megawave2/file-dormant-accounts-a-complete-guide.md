# How to File Dormant Accounts in the UK: A Complete Guide for Property Company Directors

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (definition of dormant company): **Companies Act 2006 s.1169** — "dormant" defined as a company having no "significant accounting transactions" during the relevant period. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2006/46/section/1169 on 2026-05-26.
  - **s.1169(2):** a "significant accounting transaction" means a transaction that would be required to be entered in the company's accounting records, EXCEPT for: (a) any transaction arising from the taking of shares in the company by a subscriber to the memorandum as a result of an undertaking of his in connection with the formation of the company; (b) any transaction consisting of the payment of (i) a fee to the registrar on a change of the company's name, (ii) a fee to the registrar on the re-registration of the company, (iii) a penalty for failure to deliver accounts, (iv) a fee to the registrar for the registration of an annual confirmation statement (formerly annual return).
  - **Critical operational consequence:** even a tiny non-exempt transaction (a £1 bank charge, a £5 insurance premium refund, a £20 utility deposit returned) breaks dormancy for the period. Most amateur dormancy claims fail on this micro-transaction gate, not on any conceptual misunderstanding.
- Primary (dormant accounts filing routes — Companies House side):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.448 (audit exemption for dormant subsidiary companies)** + **s.448A (notice for the exemption)** + **s.448B (conditions for the exemption)** — verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2006/46/section/448 on 2026-05-26. Dormant subsidiary audit exemption requires a parent-company guarantee on Form AA06.
  - **AA02 (Dormant company accounts) form** — the simplified filing route available to small dormant private companies that have never traded since incorporation. Replaces full Section 396 statutory accounts; comprises a balance sheet plus director's statements. Filing free of charge; must be filed within the standard 9-month-from-ARD window (s.442 CA 2006).
  - **Full small-company accounts route** — required where the company has traded at any point since incorporation, even if currently dormant. The AA02 simplified-form route is NOT available. The filing comprises a balance sheet, notes (FRS 105 micro-entity or FRS 102 1A small-company regime), and director's statements. Same 9-month-from-ARD deadline.
- Primary (the filing deadlines + the penalty regime — the operational discipline this page must foreground):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.442** — accounts filing deadline: 9 months from accounting reference date (ARD) for private companies; 6 months for public companies. First accounts after incorporation: 21 months from incorporation date for private companies.
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.453 + s.453(5) + The Companies (Late Filing Penalties) and Limited Liability Partnerships (Filing Periods and Late Filing Penalties) Regulations 2008 (SI 2008/497)** — late-filing civil penalty regime. **Verbatim 2026/27 figures** (re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk publication):
    - **Up to 1 month late:** £150 private / £750 public.
    - **More than 1 month, up to 3 months:** £375 / £1,500.
    - **More than 3 months, up to 6 months:** £750 / £3,000.
    - **More than 6 months:** £1,500 / £7,500.
    - **Doubled if late two years running.**
  - **The penalty regime applies regardless of dormancy.** Filing dormant AA02 accounts late attracts the same penalty as filing trading accounts late.
- Primary (the confirmation statement parallel duty — must be filed even in dormancy):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.853A** (confirmation statement obligation, replaced annual return from 30 June 2016 — ECCTA 2023 reforms in force from March 2024 added the registered email + lawful purpose statement to the CS01 content set; cross-reference MW1 B-batch `companies-house-confirmation-statement-changes-2024-onwards-psc-disclosure`). The CS01 must be filed annually (within 14 days of the confirmation date); £13 fee for online submission, £40 for paper.
- Primary (the HMRC corporation-tax dormant-status parallel — distinct from Companies House dormant):
  - **Corporation Tax Act 2009 (CTA 2009)** + HMRC operational practice. A company can be **Companies House dormant** (no significant accounting transactions under s.1169) but **not** **HMRC dormant** (HMRC's narrower test treats a company as dormant only if it is not within the charge to corporation tax; receiving any income within the charge, however small, makes it active for HMRC).
  - **HMRC notification:** the company tells HMRC it is dormant by writing or via the HMRC online service. HMRC will typically waive the requirement to file a CT600 for the dormancy period. If trading later resumes, the company has 3 months from the start of the new accounting period to notify HMRC under FA 2004 s.55 (Para 2 Sch 18 FA 1998 chargeability notification).
- Primary (the property-business fact patterns that commonly break dormancy):
  - **Bank interest received** — even £0.50 of interest on a dormant SPV's retained cash counts as a significant accounting transaction. The classic property-LtdCo dormancy failure is forgetting that the small bank balance from a prior sale is still earning interest, especially under post-2022 base-rate-cycle higher interest payments.
  - **Insurance premium refunds** — a pro-rata refund from a lapsed property insurance policy after disposal is a significant accounting transaction.
  - **Utility deposit returns** — a returned utility deposit (energy, water) after a sale completes is a significant accounting transaction.
  - **Bank charges** — monthly account-fee bank charges count as significant accounting transactions.
  - **Operational mitigant:** close the company's bank account before claiming dormancy. Move any retained cash to the parent-shareholder / member personal account before the start of the dormant period. Cancel all live insurance / utility / direct-debit relationships.
- Primary (the small-company audit-exemption thresholds — relevant where dormancy is being claimed alongside small-company status):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.477 (small companies)** + **s.382 (small-company qualifying conditions)** as amended by **The Companies (Accounts and Reports) (Amendment and Transitional Provision) Regulations 2024 (SI 2024/688)** — effective for financial periods commencing on or after 6 April 2025: turnover up to £15m (previously £10.2m), balance sheet total up to £7.5m (previously £5.1m), average employees up to 50 (unchanged). The threshold uplift means a far wider range of holding companies qualify for small-company audit-exemption + abbreviated filing rights.
- House position references: §3 (NIC, dormancy ties to the dormant-LtdCo group case for connected-company Employment Allowance election); §21.5 (FIC mechanics — dormant FIC SPVs are common in family-investment structures); §22.13 (trust-vs-FIC boundary — relevant for dormant FIC vs dormant trust-owned SPV decisions); §22.16 (TRS — dormant trusts still register if they meet the express-trust criteria). No dedicated dormant-companies HP section; this seed proposes nothing new — dormancy is operational, not subject to a separate policy lock.
