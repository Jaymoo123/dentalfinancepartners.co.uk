# How to File Dormant Accounts in the UK: A Complete Guide for Property Company Directors

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (definition of dormant company): **Companies Act 2006 s.1169** — "dormant" defined as a company having no "significant accounting transactions" during the relevant period. Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2006/46/section/1169 on 2026-05-26.
  - **s.1169(2):** a "significant accounting transaction" means a transaction that would be required to be entered in the company's accounting records, EXCEPT for: (a) any transaction arising from the taking of shares in the company by a subscriber to the memorandum as a result of an undertaking of his in connection with the formation of the company; (b) any transaction consisting of the payment of (i) a fee to the registrar on a change of the company's name, (ii) a fee to the registrar on the re-registration of the company, (iii) a penalty for failure to deliver accounts, (iv) a fee to the registrar for the registration of an annual confirmation statement (formerly annual return).
  - **Critical operational consequence:** even a tiny non-exempt transaction (a £1 bank charge, a £5 insurance premium refund, a £20 utility deposit returned) breaks dormancy for the period. Most amateur dormancy claims fail on this micro-transaction gate, not on any conceptual misunderstanding.
- Primary (dormant accounts filing routes — Companies House side):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.448 (audit exemption for dormant subsidiary companies)** + **s.448A (notice for the exemption)** + **s.448B (conditions for the exemption)** — verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2006/46/section/448 on 2026-05-26. Dormant subsidiary audit exemption requires a parent-company guarantee on Form AA06.
  - **AA02 (Dormant company accounts) form** — the simplified filing route available to small dormant private companies that have never traded since incorporation. Replaces full Section 396 statutory accounts; comprises a balance sheet plus director's statements. Filing free of charge; must be filed within the standard 9-month-from-ARD window (s.442 CA 2006).
  - **Full small-company accounts route** — required where the company has traded at any point since incorporation, even if currently dormant. The AA02 simplified-form route is NOT available. The filing comprises a balance sheet, notes (FRS 105 micro-entity or FRS 102 1A small-company regime), and director's statements. Same 9-month-from-ARD deadline.
- Primary (the filing deadlines + the penalty regime — the operational discipline this page must foreground):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.442** — accounts filing deadline: 9 months from accounting reference date (ARD) for private companies; 6 months for public companies. First accounts after incorporation: 21 months from incorporation date for private companies.
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.453 + s.453(5) + The Companies (Late Filing Penalties) and Limited Liability Partnerships (Filing Periods and Late Filing Penalties) Regulations 2008 (SI 2008/497)** — late-filing civil penalty regime. **Verbatim 2026/27 figures** (re-verify at Stage 2 against current gov.uk publication):
    - **Up to 1 month late:** £150 private / £750 public.
    - **More than 1 month, up to 3 months:** £375 / £1,500.
    - **More than 3 months, up to 6 months:** £750 / £3,000.
    - **More than 6 months:** £1,500 / £7,500.
    - **Doubled if late two years running.**
  - **The penalty regime applies regardless of dormancy.** Filing dormant AA02 accounts late attracts the same penalty as filing trading accounts late.
- Primary (the confirmation statement parallel duty — must be filed even in dormancy):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.853A** (confirmation statement obligation, replaced annual return from 30 June 2016 — ECCTA 2023 reforms in force from March 2024 added the registered email + lawful purpose statement to the CS01 content set; cross-reference MW1 B-batch `companies-house-confirmation-statement-changes-2024-onwards-psc-disclosure`). The CS01 must be filed annually (within 14 days of the confirmation date); £13 fee for online submission, £40 for paper.
- Primary (the HMRC corporation-tax dormant-status parallel — distinct from Companies House dormant):
  - **Corporation Tax Act 2009 (CTA 2009)** + HMRC operational practice. A company can be **Companies House dormant** (no significant accounting transactions under s.1169) but **not** **HMRC dormant** (HMRC's narrower test treats a company as dormant only if it is not within the charge to corporation tax; receiving any income within the charge, however small, makes it active for HMRC).
  - **HMRC notification:** the company tells HMRC it is dormant by writing or via the HMRC online service. HMRC will typically waive the requirement to file a CT600 for the dormancy period. If trading later resumes, the company has 3 months from the start of the new accounting period to notify HMRC under FA 2004 s.55 (Para 2 Sch 18 FA 1998 chargeability notification).
- Primary (the property-business fact patterns that commonly break dormancy):
  - **Bank interest received** — even £0.50 of interest on a dormant SPV's retained cash counts as a significant accounting transaction. The classic property-LtdCo dormancy failure is forgetting that the small bank balance from a prior sale is still earning interest, especially under post-2022 base-rate-cycle higher interest payments.
  - **Insurance premium refunds** — a pro-rata refund from a lapsed property insurance policy after disposal is a significant accounting transaction.
  - **Utility deposit returns** — a returned utility deposit (energy, water) after a sale completes is a significant accounting transaction.
  - **Bank charges** — monthly account-fee bank charges count as significant accounting transactions.
  - **Operational mitigant:** close the company's bank account before claiming dormancy. Move any retained cash to the parent-shareholder / member personal account before the start of the dormant period. Cancel all live insurance / utility / direct-debit relationships.
- Primary (the small-company audit-exemption thresholds — relevant where dormancy is being claimed alongside small-company status):
  - **Companies Act 2006 s.477 (small companies)** + **s.382 (small-company qualifying conditions)** as amended by **The Companies (Accounts and Reports) (Amendment and Transitional Provision) Regulations 2024 (SI 2024/688)** — effective for financial periods commencing on or after 6 April 2025: turnover up to £15m (previously £10.2m), balance sheet total up to £7.5m (previously £5.1m), average employees up to 50 (unchanged). The threshold uplift means a far wider range of holding companies qualify for small-company audit-exemption + abbreviated filing rights.
- House position references: §3 (NIC, dormancy ties to the dormant-LtdCo group case for connected-company Employment Allowance election); §21.5 (FIC mechanics — dormant FIC SPVs are common in family-investment structures); §22.13 (trust-vs-FIC boundary — relevant for dormant FIC vs dormant trust-owned SPV decisions); §22.16 (TRS — dormant trusts still register if they meet the express-trust criteria). No dedicated dormant-companies HP section; this seed proposes nothing new — dormancy is operational, not subject to a separate policy lock.

**Worked example: the post-disposal SPV dormancy transition — the Holloway-family case**

Persona: Holloway-family-SPV-1. Sole-director LtdCo BTL that has held a single rental flat in Cambridge since 2019. The property was sold in November 2025 with completion proceeds £180,000 (after CGT). Director intends to hold the SPV dormant for 18 months while scouting the next purchase, then re-acquire and resume trading.

Lifecycle transition steps (operational discipline):
1. **December 2025:** final post-sale receipts identified (insurance refund £45 from cancelled landlord policy; final agent commission rebate £92). Both are significant accounting transactions that prevent dormancy starting in November 2025.
2. **January 2026:** all transactions closed; final bank account closing balance £180,137. Director transfers retained cash to personal account as either a loan repayment (director's loan account credit) or a dividend (FA 2026 dividend rates apply at distribution).
3. **February 2026:** SPV bank account closed. Insurance policies cancelled. Any remaining direct-debits ceased.
4. **April 2026:** new accounting period commences with company in dormant state from start. Director writes to HMRC notifying dormancy (or via HMRC online service).
5. **September 2026:** Companies House confirmation statement due — CS01 filed online at £34. ID verification under ECCTA 2023 must be in place for the director / PSC.
6. **December 2026:** accounts for year ending September 2025 due 9 months from ARD. Because the SPV traded for part of the period (April-November 2025) before going dormant, the AA02 simplified form is NOT available — full small-company accounts must be filed.
7. **April 2027:** next accounting period starts (year ending September 2026 was first dormant-throughout period). For this period the AA02 simplified form IS available — never traded during the period.
8. **June 2027:** AA02 filed for the September 2026 dormant year-end period.

**Critical dormancy-failure mitigants (the property-specific patterns the page must surface):**
- Close the bank account or move to a fee-free no-interest account before claiming dormancy. Even £0.50 of interest breaks dormancy.
- Cancel all live direct-debits before dormancy starts. Bank charges and ongoing-fee payments are significant accounting transactions.
- Settle and refund all utility deposits before dormancy. Refunded utility deposits are significant accounting transactions.
- Cancel landlord-buildings + contents insurance after disposal completes. Pro-rata refunds are significant accounting transactions.
- Document the "dormant from" date precisely. Most amateur dormancy claims start the dormant period at the disposal-completion date when in fact small post-completion transactions continue for weeks afterwards.

## FAQ draft (10-12 entries, Stage 2 populated; RUN sub-agent polishes prose)

1. **What is a dormant company under UK law?**
   Per Companies Act 2006 s.1169, a dormant company has had no "significant accounting transactions" during the relevant period (accounting reference period). Four narrow exceptions are excluded from the "significant" test: subscriber-share transactions on formation, fees to the registrar on a name change or re-registration, late-filing penalties, and the fee for filing a confirmation statement. Any other transaction — a £1 bank charge, a £0.50 interest credit, a £45 insurance refund — breaks dormancy. The threshold is binary: it is not about size of transaction, but about whether one happened.

2. **What is the difference between Companies House dormancy and HMRC dormancy?**
   They are two parallel regimes with different tests. Companies House dormancy is the s.1169 "no significant accounting transactions" test. HMRC dormancy is the narrower "not within the charge to corporation tax" test — a company receiving any income that falls within the CT charge (CTA 2009 Part 3) is active for HMRC, even if Companies House would still consider it dormant. The practical operative rule: a company can be Companies House dormant but HMRC-active, but not the reverse. The notifications to each authority are separate steps.

3. **Which dormant-accounts filing route should I use — AA02 or full small-company accounts?**
   AA02 (the simplified dormant-company accounts form) is available ONLY if the company has never traded since incorporation. Once a company has traded at any point — even one rental month in its history — the AA02 simplified route is permanently closed for that company. Full small-company accounts must be filed instead (FRS 105 micro-entity or FRS 102 1A small-company regime). Both routes have the same 9-month-from-ARD filing deadline.

4. **What is the AA02 form content?**
   The AA02 comprises a single-page balance sheet (showing called-up share capital, profit-and-loss account, and total) plus director's statements (confirming dormancy and the appropriate audit-exemption claim). The form is free to file via Companies House WebFiling online service. No notes, no P&L, no cash-flow statement — just the balance sheet plus statements.

5. **What is the late-filing penalty for dormant accounts?**
   The same as for trading accounts. Per SI 2008/497, private companies pay £150 if filed up to 1 month late, £375 if 1-3 months late, £750 if 3-6 months late, and £1,500 if more than 6 months late. Public companies pay equivalent doubled tiers (£750 / £1,500 / £3,000 / £7,500). All amounts double if the company has filed late two consecutive years in a row. The penalty regime applies regardless of dormant status — filing a dormant AA02 one day late costs £150 just like a full set of trading accounts.

6. **Do I still need to file a confirmation statement during dormancy?**
   Yes. CA 2006 s.853A confirmation statement obligation continues regardless of dormancy. The CS01 must be filed within 14 days of the confirmation date (12 months from incorporation, then annually). Online CS01 fee is £34, paper £62 (Companies House fee schedule revised 1 May 2024 — re-verify at write time). The CS01 also confirms the director / PSC identity-verification status under ECCTA 2023, which applies to dormant LtdCos no less than active ones.

7. **What happens if I forget to notify HMRC that the company is dormant?**
   If HMRC is not notified and the company is then expected to file a CT600, failure to file by the statutory deadline triggers a £100 fixed penalty + escalating tiers. The fix is to write to HMRC explaining dormancy retrospectively; HMRC will typically accept the position and waive past CT600 obligations for genuinely-dormant periods. If trading resumes, the company has 3 months from the start of the new accounting period to notify HMRC under Sch 18 FA 1998 para 2 (chargeability notification).

8. **What property-specific transactions commonly break dormancy?**
   Four patterns:
   - Bank interest received on retained cash. Even £0.50 of interest is a significant accounting transaction.
   - Insurance premium refunds (pro-rata refunds after policy cancellation post-disposal).
   - Utility deposit returns (energy, water, council tax deposits returned post-sale).
   - Bank charges (monthly account-fee charges count as transactions).
   The operational mitigant is to close the bank account, cancel all live insurance and utility relationships, and document the "dormant from" date as the actual zero-activity start — not the disposal completion date.

9. **Can a dormant subsidiary claim audit exemption?**
   Yes, but with conditions. Under CA 2006 s.448 + s.448A + s.448B, a dormant subsidiary can claim audit exemption if (i) the parent company is established under UK law and includes the subsidiary in its consolidated accounts, (ii) all members agree to the exemption, and (iii) the parent gives a statutory guarantee on Form AA06 covering all the subsidiary's outstanding liabilities at the end of the financial year. The guarantee is a real commitment — many parents reject the route once they understand the guarantee's scope.

10. **Should I keep my SPV dormant or strike it off?**
    Five factors decide: (i) intent to re-use for a future purchase — keep dormant; (ii) preservation of historic CT losses (only valuable if you expect future profits within the same trade) — keep dormant subject to CTA 2010 Part 14 change-of-ownership tests; (iii) preservation of historic VAT registration (valuable if you opted to tax commercial property) — keep dormant; (iv) the running costs of keeping alive (~£34-50/year confirmation statement + accountant time for AA02 + bank account fees) vs the cost of strike-off-then-re-incorporate later (~£50 strike-off + ~£12 future incorporation); (v) directors' time and administrative attention. For a director who is uncertain about a future purchase within 24 months, strike-off is usually cleaner; for an actively-portfolio-rebalancing landlord with 6-12 month next-purchase horizon, dormancy is the right choice.

11. **What is the small-company threshold uplift effective from 6 April 2025?**
    Per SI 2024/688 amendments to CA 2006 s.382, small-company qualifying conditions uplifted from 6 April 2025: turnover threshold raised from £10.2m to £15m; balance-sheet total raised from £5.1m to £7.5m; employee threshold unchanged at 50. The two-of-three test continues. For dormant LtdCos this matters because the small-company audit-exemption umbrella (CA 2006 s.477) covers more companies under the new thresholds.

12. **What happens when I want to wake the SPV back up to acquire a new property?**
    Three steps: (i) notify HMRC within 3 months of the start of the new accounting period that the company is again chargeable to CT (Sch 18 FA 1998 para 2); (ii) re-open the bank account or set up a new one; (iii) the next set of accounts will be full small-company accounts (not AA02, because trading resumed during the period). Brought-forward CT losses from prior trading may be available depending on the change-of-ownership tests (CTA 2010 Part 14 s.673 / s.674 / s.676) — if the same shareholders control, losses typically survive; major change in nature of activity restricts them.
