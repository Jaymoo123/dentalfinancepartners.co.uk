# Partnership and Partnership Agreement: Roles, Types and Benefits for UK Property Partnerships (2026/27)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (the base partnership statute):** Partnership Act 1890 (PA 1890) — establishes the general partnership framework. **Critical for this page:** s.24 — "rules as to interests and duties of partners subject to special agreement". s.24 sets out the NINE statutory default rules that govern a partnership IN THE ABSENCE OF a contrary agreement; these defaults are commonly inappropriate for typical property partnership setups and are the reason a written partnership agreement is operationally essential. **The s.24 defaults are the load-bearing point of this page** — many partnerships operate without an agreement and unknowingly trigger the defaults that work against them.
- **Primary (the s.24 nine defaults — the operative content):**
  - **s.24(1):** all partners entitled to share equally in capital and profits, and must contribute equally toward losses — regardless of actual capital contributions. Operative for property partnerships where one partner contributed the deposit and another the management time — DEFAULT TREATS THEM EQUALLY despite unequal contributions.
  - **s.24(2):** partnership must indemnify partner for payments + personal liabilities incurred in ordinary and proper conduct of partnership business. Operationally sensible default; commonly retained.
  - **s.24(3):** partner who advances money beyond agreed capital is entitled to interest at 5% per annum from the date of advance. Operationally low rate; commonly varied.
  - **s.24(4):** partner is NOT entitled to interest on capital before profits are ascertained. Operationally restrictive; commonly varied — partners who contributed more capital commonly want interest on the differential capital before profit-share.
  - **s.24(5):** every partner may take part in management of the business. **Critical for property partnership:** sleeping partner setups need s.24(5) DISPLACED — otherwise the sleeping partner has statutory management rights they may not be exercising.
  - **s.24(6):** no partner entitled to remuneration for acting in the partnership business — i.e., NO SALARY for managing partner by default. Operationally typically displaced where one partner contributes significantly more time / management.
  - **s.24(7):** no new partner may be admitted without consent of ALL existing partners. Operational gating; commonly varied to majority or supermajority decision.
  - **s.24(8):** ordinary matters connected with partnership business may be decided by majority; NO CHANGE in nature of partnership business without consent of ALL partners. Operationally rigid — single dissenting partner can veto business-type changes (e.g., expanding from residential BTL into commercial property requires unanimous consent under s.24(8) by default).
  - **s.24(9):** partnership books to be kept at the principal place of business and every partner has access. Operationally standard; commonly retained.
- **Primary (partnership-formation default duration):** PA 1890 s.32 + s.33 + s.26 — partnership at will (no fixed duration) is dissolvable by any partner's notice (s.26). **Critical:** default termination on notice means a single partner can dissolve the entire partnership unilaterally — this is the single most operationally-dangerous default for property partnerships, particularly when the partnership has long-lived assets (residential portfolio with mortgages that would need refinancing on dissolution). A written agreement specifying minimum-duration / continuation-on-death / continuation-on-retirement / buyout mechanics is essential to displace s.26.
- **Primary (partnership-formation default termination on partner events):** PA 1890 s.33(1) — partnership is dissolved by death or bankruptcy of any partner (subject to contrary agreement). Operational catastrophe for property partnerships — death of a partner triggers automatic dissolution under the default; written agreement commonly provides for continuation with deceased partner's estate paid out per buyout formula.
- **Primary (the duty of good faith — load-bearing for written-agreement context):** PA 1890 ss.28-30 — duties of partners to render true accounts and full information (s.28); to account for benefit derived from any transaction concerning the partnership without consent (s.29); not to compete with the partnership (s.30). **Critical:** these duties are NOT displaceable to the extent of fundamental good faith but CAN be modified by agreement for specific conflicts (e.g., a partner who also operates a separate property portfolio outside the partnership may want a written carve-out from s.30).
- **Primary (the holding-out liability — important for partner-type architecture):** PA 1890 s.14 — a person who represents themselves (or knowingly allows themselves to be represented) as a partner is liable to the third party as if they were a partner. **Operative for sleeping partner setups:** sleeping partner whose name appears on the partnership letterhead, website, or lease as a partner is liable for partnership debts to third parties relying on the representation, EVEN IF the agreement says they have no management role. Operationally significant; written agreement should govern naming + holding-out.
- **Primary (partnership types — the entity-choice fork):**
  - **General partnership (PA 1890):** unlimited joint-and-several liability for all partners; tax-transparent; no Companies House filing; PA 1890 default applies subject to written agreement. Cross-reference A7 M2-A-B2 `does-your-business-qualify-as-a-partnership` for the definitional layer.
  - **Limited partnership (LP — LPA 1907):** at least one general partner with unlimited liability + at least one limited partner with liability limited to capital contributed; limited partner CANNOT participate in management (otherwise loses limited-liability protection per LPA 1907 s.6(1)); Companies House registration required; tax-transparent. Cross-reference A3 M2-A-B1 `companies-house-changes-limited-partnership-requirements` for the LP-specific compliance / ECCTA Part 2 reforms (per F-1).
  - **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP — LLPA 2000):** body corporate at law (s.1(2)) but transparent for income tax (ITTOIA 2005 s.863); members' liability limited to agreed amounts (LLPA 2000 s.1(5)); members can participate in management (no LP s.6(1) restriction); Companies House registration + annual accounts + designated members required. Cross-reference siblings `llp-accounts` + `llp-and-taxation-benefits` (this batch) for accounts-side and tax-side handbooks.
  - **Corporate partner:** any of general partnership / LP / LLP can have a body-corporate member (LtdCo). Operationally common in property contexts (hybrid LLP with corporate member) but anti-avoidance regimes (mixed-membership rules ITA 2007 ss.850C-E per F-5 + A10 M2-A-B2) systematically dismantle the typical income-splitting motivation. Cross-reference A10 `hybrid-limited-liability-partnership` for the hybrid-LLP depth.
- **Primary (partner roles — the operative architecture):**
  - **Managing partner / senior partner:** partner with primary day-to-day management responsibility; may receive salary (s.24(6) displaced) + weighted profit share above pari passu; commonly a designated member in an LLP.
  - **Sleeping / dormant partner:** partner who contributes capital but no management; CRITICAL — needs s.24(5) management rights displaced AND s.14 holding-out exposure managed via letterhead / website / lease-naming conventions.
  - **Salaried partner:** partner who receives a salary (or salary-equivalent) regardless of profits; in an LLP, exposure to the salaried-member rules ITA 2007 ss.863A-G (cross-reference F-4 + A11 M2-A-B2) — Conditions A/B/C analysis determines whether treated as employee for PAYE + NIC.
  - **Junior partner:** partner with fixed share or capped share; commonly entry-level partner on a path to senior partnership; commonly first to be removed under buyout mechanics.
  - **Limited partner (LP only):** capital contribution + liability limited to contributed amount; NO MANAGEMENT (LPA 1907 s.6(1)).
  - **Corporate partner / member:** body-corporate member; common for FIC-overlay setups (FIC as corporate member of family LLP) or external-investor setups.
- **Primary (partnership agreement key clauses — operational checklist):**
  - **Capital contributions** — initial and ongoing; interest on capital (displacing s.24(4)).
  - **Profit-sharing ratios** — including how profits are divided (equal / proportionate to capital / per agreement formula) (displacing s.24(1) equal default).
  - **Drawings + remuneration** — monthly drawings entitlements; partner salaries (displacing s.24(6)); year-end balancing.
  - **Loan accounts** — partner advances beyond capital and interest thereon (displacing s.24(3)).
  - **Decision-making + voting** — what requires unanimity / supermajority / simple majority (displacing s.24(7) + s.24(8)).
  - **Management roles + responsibilities** — including sleeping partner architecture (displacing s.24(5) for sleeping partners).
  - **Admission of new partners** — process + thresholds + buy-in mechanics.
  - **Retirement / death / removal of partners** — buyout formula (commonly NAV per accounts or independent valuation); continuation on partner events (displacing s.33(1) default dissolution on death/bankruptcy); notice periods; restrictive covenants post-exit.
  - **Dissolution** — circumstances triggering dissolution; mechanics; surviving-partner buyout vs sale-of-business; tax-side impact (s.59 CGT fractional disposals on dissolution + SP D12 treatment).
  - **Confidentiality + non-compete + non-solicitation** — modifying s.30 in specific bona-fide-commercial ways.
  - **Dispute resolution** — mediation / arbitration / court; commonly important for family property partnerships.
  - **Bank mandates + signing authorities** — operational delegation framework.
- **Primary (tax-transparency framework for general partnerships and LLPs):** ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 ss.846-863 — partnership tax-transparency. ITA 2007 s.852 (notional trade for each partner). LLP-specific transparency under s.863. TCGA 1992 s.59 (general partnerships) + s.59A (LLPs) — CGT transparency. SP D12 (HMRC Statement of Practice on Partnership CGT). Cross-reference F-2 (M2-A-B2) §11.C partnership architecture lock + sibling `llp-and-taxation-benefits` (this batch) for tax-side handbook.
- **Primary (the SDLT partnership relief gating — operative cross-link):** FA 2003 Sch 15 — partnership-SDLT regime applies to general partnerships, LPs, and LLPs alike (each treated under the same Sch 15 framework). Sch 15 SLP relief on partnership-to-LtdCo incorporation depends on para 1 business gate being met. Cross-reference existing `partnership-sdlt-relief-schedule-15-fa-2003-incorporation-sum-lower-proportions` + A7 M2-A-B2 for para 1 analysis.
- **House position reference:** §21 (Ltd Co + FIC family — entity-choice anchor); §11 + §11.A (Companies House + ECCTA); §3 (incorporation mechanics — partnership status is gating for Sch 15 SLP); §22 (IHT side). Under PA 1890 s.33(1) (or equivalent under LLP agreement default), partnership automatically dissolves; assets must be wound up; CGT triggered on partnership-asset disposals at member level per s.59; IHT exposure on deceased's share. Written continuation + buyout provisions would have allowed surviving members to continue the partnership and buy out the deceased's estate per agreement formula, avoiding immediate CGT crystallisation.
