# Transferring a Business Out of a UK Limited Company: The Honest Decision Frame for Property LtdCos (2026/27 — Disincorporation, MVL, Strike-Off, In-Specie Distribution, Share Sale)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary (the framing — disincorporation relief NO LONGER EXISTS as of 2026):** TCGA 1992 s.162B (introduced FA 2013 Sch 14 + repealed-via-sunset on 31 March 2018; NOT extended; NOT renewed by any subsequent Finance Act). **CRITICAL DO-NOT-WRITE:** disincorporation relief — the time-limited 2013-2018 relief that allowed a small company to transfer goodwill + land used in the business to its shareholders at lower-of-cost / market-value with deferred CGT — IS NOT AVAILABLE in 2026/27. Any property-business-out-of-company exercise must reckon with the full unrelieved CGT + SDLT + income-tax / dividend-tax cost. Cross-reference HMRC CG65700+ + CG65745+ (historic relief now inoperative). Stage 2 verifies no late-stage policy reversal at write time per §16.35.
- **Primary (the corporate-level CGT on asset disposals):** TCGA 1992 s.17 (deemed market-value disposal on connected-person transfers — load-bearing for in-specie distributions to shareholders; shareholder is connected per s.286). **Operative for property LtdCo exit:** transferring a let property out of the company to a shareholder is a DISPOSAL at MARKET VALUE for the COMPANY (regardless of consideration actually paid); corporation tax at 25% main rate (or 19% small profits rate via marginal relief — cross-reference §21.A F-31 CT three-figure framework) applies to the gain (proceeds — original base cost — indexation if pre-1998 acquired, frozen at Dec 2017 per FA 2018). The corporate-level CGT is unaffected by whether the shareholder pays the company anything — s.17 deems MV consideration. Cross-reference existing `cgt-property-transfer-limited-company-calculate` for the mirror personal-to-corporate calculation.
- **Primary (the SDLT on property transfer-out):** FA 2003 s.43 (chargeable transactions) + Sch 4 (consideration includes assumption of mortgage + non-cash consideration) + Sch 4ZA (3% / 5% additional-dwellings surcharge per §1.I MW1 lock; verify current rate at write time). **Operative for property LtdCo exit:** transferring let property out of the company to a shareholder is a LAND TRANSACTION for SDLT purposes (Sch 4 consideration = MV of property + assumption of mortgage + any cash consideration); SDLT chargeable at the surcharge rate if the shareholder ends up owning an additional dwelling (almost always for property-LtdCo exit scenarios). Cross-reference existing `sdlt-transfer-property-company-cost`. **Critical no-relief points:** (a) Sch 7 SDLT group relief is UNAVAILABLE because the transfer is to a non-corporate (the shareholder is a natural person, not a group company); (b) the partner-introducing FA 2003 Sch 15 SLP relief is UNAVAILABLE because the transfer is OUT of a corporate, not INTO a partnership; (c) no §1.D divorce-separation Sch 3 relief unless transfer is part of a divorce-proceedings settlement.
- **Primary (the corporate-level CT on in-specie distribution):** CTA 2010 Part 23 (distributions) + CTA 2009 Part 3 (charge to CT on income — distribution income to recipient; corporate-level deemed MV disposal). **Operative for property LtdCo exit via in-specie distribution:** distributing a let property out of the company to a shareholder as a non-cash dividend (in-specie distribution) is treated as: (a) a DISPOSAL by the company at MV (TCGA 1992 s.17 — CT at 25% / 19% on gain); (b) a DISTRIBUTION received by the shareholder at the MV of the property (taxable at dividend rates 10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35% per §21.4 F-20 lock for individual shareholders; intra-group dividend exemption per CTA 2009 s.931A+ for corporate shareholder cross-reference §21.8 proposed lock per F-3); (c) the shareholder steps into the property at MV as the new acquisition base cost (TCGA 1992 s.17 again at the shareholder end). Mismatch between (a) the company's CT-on-gain on the asset + (b) the shareholder's dividend-tax-on-MV-of-property + (c) the SDLT on the transfer creates the TRIPLE-CHARGE trap that makes in-specie distribution one of the most expensive exit routes.
- **Primary (the MVL route — TCGA 1992 s.122 capital treatment):** Insolvency Act 1986 Part IV (winding up) + Companies Act 2006 Part 31 + Insolvency (England and Wales) Rules 2016 (operational mechanics); TCGA 1992 s.122 (distributions in a winding-up are capital receipts in the shareholder's hands subject to CGT not income tax). **Operative for property LtdCo exit via MVL:** a solvent company appointing a licensed insolvency practitioner (IP) as liquidator routes the entire wind-up under MVL. Distributions to shareholders during the MVL are CAPITAL in the shareholder's hands per s.122 (not dividend-income), taxed at CGT residential rates 24% / 28% surcharge (verify current FA 2026 calibration) on the gain (proceeds — base cost of shares). **Critical advantage:** capital treatment vs dividend treatment is favourable for higher-rate shareholders where the share base cost is low (the difference between 24% CGT and 35.75% / 39.35% dividend rates is meaningful — a £500k extraction saves c.£60-75k under MVL vs dividend route for an additional-rate shareholder). **Critical caveats:** (a) IP fees typically £3,000-£10,000 for a simple property MVL (higher for complex multi-property; commercial reality); (b) MVL requires a solvency statement by directors with criminal penalty for false declaration; (c) targeted anti-avoidance rule (TAAR) at ITTOIA 2005 s.396B (introduced FA 2016) recharacterises MVL distribution as dividend where the same shareholder restarts a similar business within 2 years (phoenix rule — load-bearing for property founders considering MVL-then-restart). Cross-reference existing `mvl-members-voluntary-liquidation-property-company-cgt-vs-income-treatment`.
- **Primary (the strike-off route — CA 2006 s.1003 + FA 2012 s.1030A £25k capital cap):** Companies Act 2006 ss.1003-1010 (voluntary striking-off by company application — DS01 form to Companies House) + Corporation Tax Act 2010 s.1030A (inserted by FA 2012 s.34 + Sch 4) — the £25,000 capital-treatment cap on distributions in anticipation of dissolution under ESC C16 (extra-statutory concession C16 abolished as ESC; replaced by s.1030A statutory framework). **Operative for property LtdCo exit via strike-off:** for a company with NET ASSETS ≤ £25,000 at the time of dissolution, distributions to shareholders in anticipation of dissolution can be treated as CAPITAL (CGT residential rate to shareholder) rather than INCOME (dividend rate); for net assets > £25,000, the dividend-route default applies UNLESS the route is restructured via MVL (with IP fee). **Critical caveats:** (a) the £25k cap is calculated on net assets AT THE TIME OF DISTRIBUTION not at year-end (operational sequencing matters); (b) phoenix TAAR ITTOIA 2005 s.396B applies equally to strike-off as to MVL — recharacterises capital to dividend on same-business-restart within 2 years; (c) strike-off requires no trading + no name change + no application to court + no creditors-owed-amounts > £15,000 in last 3 months (CA 2006 s.1004 conditions); failure of any condition = strike-off application invalid; (d) HMRC + creditors can object to strike-off (CA 2006 s.1006); (e) STRUCK-OFF COMPANY ASSETS PASS TO THE CROWN AS BONA VACANTIA — leaving property in a company that is struck off WITHOUT proper distribution = the property passes to the Crown and recovery requires costly restoration proceedings. Cross-reference existing `extracting-cash-from-property-spv-extraction-sequence-pillar-2026-27` for the extraction-sequence layer.
- **Primary (the s.179 degrouping interaction for multi-company groups):** TCGA 1992 s.179 (degrouping charge on assets transferred intra-group within 6 years before the company holding the asset leaves the group). **Operative for property LtdCo group exit:** where a property HoldCo + SPV group is being wound down, intra-group transfers within the 6-year window before an SPV leaves the group (via sale to third party + via in-specie distribution + via MVL dissolution) trigger s.179 degrouping charges at the SPV-leaving-the-group point. FA 2011 modification routes the degrouping charge into the share-sale consideration where SSE applies (cross-reference SSE sibling pick this batch); where SSE does not apply, the degrouping charge is a separate CT charge on the leaving company at 25%. Stage 2 verifies sequencing pitfalls + cross-references §21.8 proposed multi-company group lock per F-3 batch M2-A-B2.
- **Primary (the VAT deregistration + CGS clawback):** VATA 1994 s.81(3) + Sch 4 para 5 (deemed supply of business assets at MV on deregistration if VAT was reclaimed on acquisition) + SI 1995/2518 Part XV regs 112-116 (Capital Goods Scheme — VAT clawback on property held within 10-year CGS adjustment period). **Operative for property LtdCo exit where VAT has been opted-to-tax:** company holding commercial property under option-to-tax (Sch 10 — cross-reference §29.3 VAT cluster) faces (a) deemed supply at MV on deregistration triggering VAT charge unless TOGC conditions met (rare on disincorporation), (b) CGS clawback on capital goods (property + integral fixtures + extensions) where the property is within the 10-year CGS adjustment period and is being transferred OUT of the OTT-electing entity into a non-OTT shareholder ownership. Cross-reference §29.5 CGS lock + existing VAT pages. Stage 2 verifies operative VAT mechanics — load-bearing for commercial-property-out-of-company exit scenarios.
- **Primary (the CAA disposal event on plant + machinery + integral features):** Capital Allowances Act 2001 ss.55-67 (disposal events) + s.61 disposal event 8 (transfer between connected persons) + s.196 (election to fix transfer value). **Operative for property LtdCo exit:** transferring property OUT of the company to a shareholder triggers a CAA DISPOSAL EVENT 8 on any plant + machinery + integral features that previously qualified for capital allowances (annual investment allowance / first-year allowance / writing-down allowance pools); deemed disposal at MV per s.61 connected-person; balancing charge / allowance crystallises in the company's final CT computation; shareholder steps into property without inheriting the capital allowance history (the s.198 election available on commercial-property TRADING-asset transfers does NOT apply to investment-asset transfers — cross-reference §25.11 s.198 lock per Wave 7).
- **Primary (the operational closure compliance):**
  - **CT600 final accounts:** company files CT600 to date of cessation of trade + final CT600 to date of dissolution (cessation accounting period typically does not align with the company's standard accounting reference period); CT600 must reconcile distributions to shareholders + asset disposals + s.179 degrouping (if applicable) + any clawbacks (CIS / R&D / patent box / capital allowance).
  - **VAT cancellation:** if VAT-registered, deregistration via Form VAT7 within 30 days of cessation of trade or transfer of business (whichever applies); deemed-supply VAT on stock + capital goods at MV per VATA 1994 s.81(3) + Sch 4 para 5 if VAT reclaimed.
  - **PAYE closure:** RTI PAYE scheme closure via final FPS / EPS marker; P45s for employees; PAYE settlement of any final liabilities; employer's NIC reconciliation.
  - **Companies House dissolution:** DS01 form for strike-off (CA 2006 s.1003) OR liquidator-filed L120 for MVL dissolution; confirmation statement (post-ECCTA per §11.A) + final accounts filing if year-end falls within strike-off window.
- **Primary (the comparison frame — entity-exit options):**
  - **Option A — full asset sale at company level + extraction:** company sells property assets to third party (corporate-level CGT at 25% on gains); cash proceeds extracted to shareholders via dividend (10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35% — additional-rate band) OR via MVL capital treatment (24% / 28% CGT residential rate); company then dissolved via MVL or strike-off. Suitable for: founder wanting to retire from LtdCo property holding + buyer for assets at fair MV available.
  - **Option B — share sale to third party (SSE-eligible or not):** founder sells LtdCo shares to buyer (one-step transaction; no asset-level CGT inside company; CGT on shares to founder at residential rates if property-investment company); buyer takes over company with historic liabilities + property + lease arrangements + DLA balances. SSE may apply at HoldCo level if subsidiary-of-HoldCo structure + DevCo trading test passes (cross-reference SSE sibling pick this batch). Suitable for: founder selling property business as ongoing concern; buyer wanting tax-efficient acquisition route.
  - **Option C — in-specie distribution to shareholder + dissolution:** company distributes property to shareholder at MV (triple-charge: CT on company gain + dividend income tax on shareholder MV receipt + SDLT on transfer); company then dissolved with residual cash via strike-off or MVL. Suitable for: shareholder wanting to RETAIN property in personal ownership (e.g., reverting to personal-name landlord for S24 reasons reversed; succession planning). MOST EXPENSIVE route in absolute tax cost terms — but may be cheapest after factoring in retention value if alternative is forced sale.
  - **Option D — MVL with property in-specie distribution:** company appoints IP as liquidator; property transferred to shareholder in-specie via MVL; distribution treated as CAPITAL per s.122 (shareholder pays CGT residential rate on share gain not dividend rate on property MV); SDLT still applies on the property transfer + CT on company gain at MV per s.17 (company still has the gain) + CAA disposal event still crystallises. **Critical operational note:** MVL changes the SHAREHOLDER-LEVEL tax (capital not income); does NOT change the COMPANY-LEVEL CT (still applies) nor the SDLT (still applies on property transfer). The MVL advantage is shareholder-level tax-rate arbitrage (24% / 28% CGT vs 35.75% / 39.35% dividend) — significant for higher-rate shareholders with low share base cost.
  - **Option E — strike-off with capital treatment under FA 2012 s.1030A £25k cap:** small company (net assets ≤ £25k) extracted via dividend up to £25k + strike-off via DS01 form; the £25k is treated as CAPITAL per s.1030A (CGT residential rate to shareholder). Suitable for: small property LtdCo with single low-value property + low residual cash; founder wanting fast cheap exit. Phoenix TAAR per s.396B applies (no same-business-restart within 2 years).

  - **Single £500k-property single-shareholder LtdCo modelled through 5 routes — additional-rate-band shareholder:** Property MV £500k; LtdCo base cost £200k; mortgage £150k; LtdCo retained profit £80k cash; shareholder is additional-rate taxpayer; share base cost £10k. **Option A (asset sale + extract via dividend):** company sells property £500k → corporate gain £300k → CT @ 25% = £75k. Cash to extract = £500k − £150k mortgage − £75k CT + £80k retained = £355k. Dividend to shareholder: £355k less £500 allowance × 39.35% = £139.6k. Total tax = £75k + £139.6k = £214.6k. **Option B (share sale to third-party investor):** founder sells shares for c.£385k (discount for inherited £75k CT + historic liabilities; SSE n/a for investment LtdCo).
