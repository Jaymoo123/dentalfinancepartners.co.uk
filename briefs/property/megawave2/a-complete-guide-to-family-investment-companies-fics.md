# A Complete Guide to Family Investment Companies (FICs): What UK Property Owners Need to Know

## Statutory anchor
- Primary: Companies Act 2006 c. 46 (the FIC is a private limited company under CA 2006; bespoke articles of association under s.18, with reserved-matter mechanics anchored at CA 2006 s.21 amendment procedure). Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2006/46/contents on 2026-05-26 (Stage 2 sub-agent re-verifies at write time per §16.35).
- Supporting (corporate tax): CTA 2010 s.18N ("Close investment-holding companies") — verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/4/section/18N on 2026-05-26; subsection (2)(b) permitted-purpose for commercial land lettings; subsection (3) connected-person carve-out (most BTL-let FICs escape CIHC status by virtue of unconnected-tenant lettings — §21.5 lock). Also: CTA 2010 ss.18N-Q (CIHC framework), CTA 2010 s.455 (overdrawn DLA charge at the dividend upper rate set by ITA 2007 s.8(2) — 35.75% for loans made on or after 6 April 2026 per FA 2026 s.4(1)(b); §21.1 + §21.4 + §16.27 rate-by-reference verification discipline).
- Supporting (settlement / share-class): ITTOIA 2005 s.624 (settlements legislation) + s.626 (spouse-exception) + *Jones v Garnett* [2007] UKHL 35 (Arctic Systems carve-out). TCGA 1992 s.165 + Sch 7 (holdover relief limited to trading-company shares; investment FIC share gifts NOT eligible — §21.5 lock).
- Supporting (IHT angle, headline only — deep FIC-IHT mechanics live on sibling pages): FA 1986 s.102 (gifts with reservation, GROB risk where founder retains use of FIC-held property — §22.6 lock); IHTA 1984 s.105 + *Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC) (BPR not available for investment FICs — §22.1 lock); IHTA 1984 s.6A (long-term-resident test for spouse exemption post-FA-2025 — §22.5 refresh 2026-05-25).
- House position reference: §21.5 (FIC corporate-side mechanics — CIHC qualifying-purpose carve-out, share classes, governance, CT profile, holdover unavailable for investment FICs); §22.6 (FIC IHT-side value-freeze framing — boundary with §21.5 deliberately maintained); §21.1 + §21.4 (DLA + s.455 rate-by-reference discipline); §22.1 (BPR + *Pawson* investment line); §15.3 (GROB s.102 FA 1986 family-home pattern, cross-applies to FIC where founder uses underlying property); §22.5 refresh (LTR-based spouse exemption mechanics, post-FA-2025).

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is a family investment company in one paragraph?** (A private limited company under CA 2006 that holds investment assets — typically property, shares, cash — with bespoke articles of association that separate control from economic entitlement via multiple share classes. Distinguished from a trading company by predominantly investment-income profile. "Family" is shorthand for the typical shareholder set — founder + spouse + children + sometimes grandchildren — but no statutory definition exists. The Companies Act doesn't recognise "FIC" as a category; it is a private limited company with structuring choices.)
2. **Who actually uses one?** (Multi-generation families with property + investment portfolios typically above c. £2m where (a) the founder has clear wealth-transfer objectives over a 10+ year horizon, (b) the family is governance-capable, and (c) the family wants founder control retained for life while economic growth accrues to the next generation. Single-property landlords and short-horizon owners are usually better served by simpler structures.)
3. **What does it cost to set up and run?** (Setup typically £10,000-£25,000 covering legal advice + tax planning + company formation + articles drafting. Annual maintenance £4,000-£8,000 covering CT return + statutory accounts + Companies House filings + board governance. Figures vary by adviser; verify at write time via competitor cost surveys. The cost floor is one reason the FIC route is generally uneconomic below the c. £2m portfolio mark.)
4. **What are the headline tax effects?** (Corporation tax on rental profits at 19% small profits rate up to £50k / 25% main rate from £250k / marginal band in between — §21.4 lock. No Section 24 finance-cost restriction at corporate level. Dividend extraction taxed in shareholders' hands at 10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35% with £500 dividend allowance — F-20 corrected rates per §21.4. Director loan account credit balances repayable tax-free until exhausted, then s.455 overdrawn-DLA charge at 35.75% for loans made on or after 6 April 2026 — §21.1.)
5. **Is an FIC a trust?** (No. Common confusion. An FIC is a company; a trust is a separate legal arrangement where trustees hold assets for beneficiaries. The two can be combined — a discretionary trust can hold FIC shares — but the FIC itself is a company. The sibling page `fic-vs-discretionary-trust-property-comparison` covers the trust-vs-FIC decision in depth; this page surfaces the distinction without re-walking it.)
6. **Does the FIC qualify for Business Property Relief (BPR) on the founder's death?** (No, for investment FICs. *Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC) — passive rent collection from residential lettings is mainly investment, caught by IHTA 1984 s.105(3). BPR does NOT apply. §22.1 lock + §21.5 lock. The sibling page `fic-iht-treatment-bpr-myth` covers the myth in depth.)
7. **Will I have to pay CGT when I transfer my existing properties into the FIC?** (Yes, in most cases. TCGA 1992 s.17 deemed disposal at market value on transfer to a connected company. s.162 incorporation relief MAY apply where the transferred portfolio is a genuine business — *Ramsay v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 226 (TCC) business test — but a passive few-property buy-to-let portfolio usually fails the test and CGT crystallises in full. SDLT applies separately on the property transfer at standard residential rates plus 5% additional dwellings surcharge — §1 lock. Sibling page on FIC-funding mechanics goes deeper.)
8. **Can I gift FIC shares to my children?** (Yes, and this is a core wealth-transfer mechanism. The gift is a PET; full IHT exemption after 7 years' survival. BUT s.165 TCGA 1992 holdover relief does NOT apply to investment FIC share gifts (Sch 7 limits holdover to trading-company shares), so CGT crystallises at market value on the gift. Growth-share design lowers the gift value via minority discount + restricted-rights valuation; sibling pages `fic-growth-shares-and-freezer-shares-design` + `fic-gifting-shares-children-property-7-year-iht-mechanics` go deeper.)
9. **What is the close investment-holding company (CIHC) status — is it a problem?** (Pure-investment FICs are at risk of CIHC status under CTA 2010 s.18N, which would deny the small profits rate (flat 25% CT regardless of profit level). The s.18N(2)(b) carve-out preserves the small profits rate for FICs holding commercial lettings to unconnected persons — most BTL-let FICs escape CIHC status by this route. Where the FIC holds shares / cash / connected-tenant property predominantly, CIHC status applies. §21.5 lock — Wave 1 lesson: most BTL SPVs are NOT CIHCs because the carve-out applies. Verify per-FIC at incorporation and on each material change of asset mix.)
10. **What about Companies House identity verification and the ECCTA 2023 reforms?** (Every FIC director and PSC must be ID-verified under ECCTA 2023 ss.40-45 / s.64 / s.65 — legal requirement for new directors / PSCs from 18 November 2025; existing directors / PSCs must verify within the 12-month transition window per their next confirmation statement. Multi-SPV families verify each individual ONCE — verification is per-person, not per-company. §11.A lock. Sibling pages in this MegaWave cover ID verification and ACSPs in depth.)
