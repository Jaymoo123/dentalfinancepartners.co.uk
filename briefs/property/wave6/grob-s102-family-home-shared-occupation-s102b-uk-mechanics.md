> **Family-home GROB depth** — distinct from Wave 4 C3 (let-property GROB mechanics where children pay rent) and Wave 2 A2 (broad GROB walkthrough). Spotlight on **FA 1986 s.102B "Gifts with reservation: share of interest in land"** as the operative narrow carve-out for the most common family-home gift pattern: parent gifts an undivided share in the family home to an adult child, both continue to occupy. The page walks the **three statutory exit routes** from s.102 for family-home gifts: (i) full-market-rent occupation under s.102 + s.102A general rule (parent pays the donee a commercial open-market rent in cash, regularly, documented; informal or below-market rent re-triggers GROB); (ii) parent ceases occupation entirely (s.102A subsection (4)(a) safe harbour for the donor moving out and not returning as a regular occupier); (iii) **s.102B(4) shared-occupation carve-out** — parent gifts an undivided share (e.g. 50%), both parent and donee actually occupy as their joint home, parent receives NO benefit (other than negligible) provided by or at the expense of the donee for any reason connected with the gift. Per s.102B(4) verbatim, both donor and donee must occupy the land AND no donor-benefit-from-donee. **Two worked scenarios in property context**: (a) widow-and-adult-child sharing post-bereavement — mother survives husband, gifts 50% share to adult daughter who has been living in the home, both continue to occupy, expense-sharing structured so each pays their share of council tax / utilities / repairs proportionate to ownership share; s.102B(4) carve-out satisfied; (b) parents-downsizing-into-converted-annexe — parents gift 100% to adult child, parents move into self-contained annexe with separate utilities and own front door, child occupies main house; s.102A(4)(a) ceasing-occupation route satisfied (provided "occupation" of the annexe doesn't constitute "occupation of the land" — fact-dependent, with HMRC's *Buzzoni* [2013] EWCA Civ 1684 framing relevant). **POAT backstop** under FA 2004 Sch 15: where an arrangement escapes GROB (e.g. via undervalue sale plus loan-back, or via a discretionary trust mechanism that nominally excludes the donor) the Pre-Owned Assets Tax frequently catches it instead, levying an annual income-tax-style benefit on the donor's continuing use. Sessions on family-home gift planning must thread both gates: GROB on the IHT side under FA 1986 ss.102/102A/102B, and POAT on the income-tax side under FA 2004 Sch 15. **Differentiator from Wave 2 A2:** A2 is broad GROB statute walk; B5 is family-home-specific with s.102B as the spine.
