- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2 deepened, 2026-05-23):**

> **Adult-child-cohort applied decision-tree** — partner to Wave 4 C4 (`iht-7-year-clock-property-gifting-mid-life-landlord-strategy`, which is mid-life parent applied) but with the **receiving-side angle**: parent gifts a BTL or family home to an adult child; what tax mechanics apply, what structuring options exist, what traps to avoid. Walks the **five-route decision tree**: (i) **direct gift now** (TCGA 1992 s.17 deemed-MV trigger because parent and adult child are connected persons under s.286; no s.165 holdover because BTL investment is not a business asset within s.165 — see *Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 0050 (TCC) investment-line; no s.260 holdover because direct gift to individual is a PET not a CLT; **dry-charge CGT bite at 24% residential rate** for higher-rate parent; 7-year PET clock for IHT); (ii) **gift in stages** (annual gift of percentage shares to stay within parent's CGT AEA £3,000 + IHT annual exempt amount £3,000 — slow but cost-efficient where time horizon permits); (iii) **via bare trust** (where adult child wants the trust structure for asset-protection-from-divorce reasons; same CGT/IHT outcome as direct gift because bare trust is transparent — see B6); (iv) **via FIC** (parent forms FIC, transfers property to FIC for shares, then gifts shares to adult child; CGT on transfer-in to FIC at MV but no dry-charge IHT because share-gift is a PET not a CLT; see Wave 4 A9 `fic-gifting-shares-children-property-7-year-iht-mechanics`); (v) **hold to death** (PRR if main residence; full CGT base-cost uplift to MV on death under TCGA 1992 s.62; pay IHT at 40% on excess over NRB + RNRB). **Two adult-child worked scenarios in property context**: (a) parent-helping-adult-child-onto-housing-ladder via gift of BTL (parent retains no occupation, no rent receipt; clean PET; 7-year clock starts; if parent dies within 7 years, taper relief; dry CGT bite at gift); (b) parent gifting BTL to adult child but retaining rent (**GROB trap** under FA 1986 s.102 — see B5 + B7; the gift is in the donee's name but the donor receives benefit, so the property is treated as remaining in the donor's death estate at death MV; achieves nothing for IHT). **Differentiator from Wave 4 C4** (`iht-7-year-clock-property-gifting-mid-life-landlord-strategy`): C4 is mid-life parent (45-58 cohort) angled on the 7-year-clock IHT mechanic specifically; B8 is **adult-child-receiving** angle, focused on receiver-side decisions + cross-vehicle structuring options (the five-route decision tree). Also distinct from `cgt-gifting-property-family-members-uk` (legacy site page) which is the base CGT mechanic; B8 is the multi-route decision tree.
