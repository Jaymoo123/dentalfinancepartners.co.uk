- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2 deepened, 2026-05-23):**

> Post-FA-2006 IIP architecture is widely misunderstood in practitioner content. **Pre-22-March-2006 IIPs** are grandfathered into the original IHTA 1984 s.49(1) regime (the IIP beneficiary is treated as beneficially entitled to the underlying property for IHT, so the property falls into their death estate at death market value). **Post-22-March-2006 IIPs** are within the relevant property regime (10-year periodic charge under s.64, exit charges, no death-of-life-tenant aggregation) UNLESS they qualify under one of three statutory carve-outs in IHTA 1984 s.49(1A): (a) an **Immediate Post-Death Interest (IPDI)** under s.49A (an IIP arising on the settlor's death under a will or intestacy, with conditions in s.49A(2)-(4)); (b) a **Transitional Serial Interest (TSI)** under ss.49B-49E (a narrow set of post-2006 IIPs that succeed a pre-2006 IIP within statutory limits); (c) a **Disabled Person's Interest** under s.89B. For landlord estates the IPDI route via will is the most common structural use: settlor's will leaves rental portfolio on IPDI for surviving spouse (spouse exemption applies on first death; spouse gets rental income for life; spouse's IPDI counted in their death estate at second death, but RNRB available because property passes to lineal descendants on second death). **The IPDI mechanism is the single most under-explained tool in landlord estate planning.** Walks a Patel-style £1.2m BTL portfolio with IPDI-for-spouse + remainder-to-children architecture, demonstrating: (i) spouse-exemption on first death; (ii) deemed-IIP treatment under s.49(1)+(1A) on second death; (iii) RNRB-on-second-death where conditions met; (iv) interaction with the §22.5 / §24 spouse-exemption mechanics for non-marital cohabitants (different rules). Also covers ss.71A-71G (bereaved minor trusts and 18-to-25 trusts as alternative carve-outs to IPDI for cases where the will routes through children directly).
