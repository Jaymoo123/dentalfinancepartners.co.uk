- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2 deepened, 2026-05-23):**

> A9 is the **pre-sale event** angle: landlord selling an SPV (share sale, not asset sale) needs to strip surplus cash before the buyer prices the discount. Three routes: (i) **pre-sale dividend** — income-tax expensive but clean, (ii) **pre-sale pension contribution** — caps bind (forward-link Wave 6 A5 for AA / carry-forward), (iii) **cash-left-in negotiated as separate consideration** — buyer-discount equivalent, but shifts the tax-character from CGT (share sale) to dividend / income at the personal level depending on structuring. **The trap is the ITA 2007 Pt 13 Ch 1 transactions in securities anti-avoidance regime**, verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2007/3/section/682 on 2026-05-23 ("Overview of Chapter" — counteracting income tax advantages from transactions in securities), with the substantive counteraction power at **ITA 2007 s.684 "Person liable to counteraction of income tax advantage"** (transactions in securities including "a distribution in respect of securities in a winding up" per s.684(2)(f)). The s.685 close-company-receipt-of-consideration trigger and the s.686 "fundamental change of ownership" exclusion are the operative tests. Frame as buyer-asks-this-question — the buyer's negotiation posture is "either strip cash before completion and price the discount, or leave cash and I'll price for the post-completion extraction cost". Two worked share sales: clean dividend-strip before completion / cash-left-in as separate consideration negotiation. Distinct from Wave 1 B3 (SSE wrapper for corporate-shareholder share sale) by being the cash-strip pre-sale mechanic, not the SSE-route exit.
