- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> The anti-fragmentation rule defeating the planning pattern of separating "the developer", "the seller", and "the profit-recipient" into different legal persons. CTA 2010 s.356OH / ITA 2007 s.517H attributes coordinated activities by related persons to the chargeable person for main-purpose evaluation (verified verbatim against legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-25 — operative attribution mechanic: "the profit or gain ... is to be taken to be what that profit or gain would be if R were not a distinct person from C (and, accordingly, as if everything done by or in relation to R had been done by or in relation to C)"). Page walks: (a) the verbatim s.356OH attribution mechanic — R's relevant contributions treated as if made by C (chargeable person), with R's contributions encompassing professional services, financial contributions, and risk assumptions; (b) the **two-pathway "related" test** — Connection test via CTA 2010 ss.1122-1123 (standard "connected persons" definition — control, family, group); Relatedness test via s.356OT (defined separately — covers wider business-arrangement relationships); (c) the **relevant-contribution threshold** — contributions count as relevant unless "the profit made or to be made by R in respect of the contribution is insignificant having regard to the size of the project" (verbatim verified 2026-05-25); (d) the **tax-neutrality provision** — reimbursements by R to C for CT liability arising from the attribution are protected: not taken into account in calculating profits / losses, and not regarded as a distribution; (e) the typical fragmentation pattern: developer "DevCo" performs the development; landowner "LandCo" sells the developed land; profit recipient "ProfitCo" takes the share via management contracts or option arrangements; s.356OH attributes all three sets of activities to whoever is identified as the chargeable person (typically the realising entity); (f) JV / developer-network structures — landowner + developer + funder partnership-style splits — caught by the attribution mechanic where the parties are connected or related per s.356OT; (g) cross-reference to s.356OK / s.517K (wider anti-avoidance umbrella) — distinguished from s.356OH (fragmentation attribution mechanic) as different operative scopes — s.356OK catches arrangements designed to avoid the regime generally; s.356OH attributes activities for main-purpose evaluation; (h) the six-month associated-persons window at s.356OB(8) / s.517B(8) interaction — "any time in the period beginning when the activities of the project begin and ending 6 months after the disposal" (drift catch: HP §28.4 cited s.356OB(2) for the six-month window; the time-period definition is at subsection (8), the chargeable-person rule sits at subsection (2) — both verified 2026-05-25); (i) PTP firm-positioning — landowner-developer-funder JV structures must be designed with the fragmentation rule explicitly modelled at heads-of-terms stage; retrofitting after the deal is closed is high-risk. NOT writing Conditions A/B/C/D (B1-B4); NOT writing s.356OK general anti-avoidance in depth; NOT writing indirect disposals (B5).
