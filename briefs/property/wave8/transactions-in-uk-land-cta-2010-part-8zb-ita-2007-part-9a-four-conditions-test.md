The page walks: (a) the **two-regime symmetric architecture** with side-by-side section-number table (s.356OA / s.517A overview; s.356OB / s.517B four-conditions; s.356OC / s.517C trading-profit treatment; s.356OD / s.517D indirect disposals via property-rich entities; s.356OG / s.517G chargeable person — non-resident-inclusive; s.356OH / s.517H anti-fragmentation; s.356OK / s.517K wider anti-avoidance; s.356OM / s.517N tracing); (b) the **four-conditions test verbatim from s.356OB(4)-(7) / s.517B(4)-(7)** — Condition A (acquisition main-purpose), Condition B (derived-property acquisition main-purpose), Condition C (held as trading stock — deterministic, no purpose evaluation), Condition D (development main-purpose); (c) the **disjunctive "main purpose, or one of the main purposes" wording** — multiple main purposes can coexist; investment intent alongside profit intent does NOT defeat the test where profit intent is genuine and substantial; (d) the **six-month associated-persons window** at s.356OB(2) catching disposals by associated persons within six months of the acquisition / development event; (e) the **operative consequence** — trading profit treatment at marginal income tax rates (40% / 45% for higher / additional rate individuals) plus Class 4 NIC for self-employed individuals, OR Corporation Tax (19% SPR / 26.5% marginal / 25% main rate per §21.A) for companies, INSTEAD of the post-30-October-2024 CGT rates of 18% basic / 24% higher (per §5); (f) **non-resident-scope** confirmation that both Parts apply to non-UK-resident persons — closing the pre-FA-2016 offshore-developer planning gap (cross-reference B8); (g) worked-example CGT-vs-trading comparison at a £400k gain — CGT route (24% × (£400k − £3k AEA) = £95,280) vs trading route (40% × £400k + Class 4 NIC at 6% over £50,270 = £160,000 + £21,058 = £181,058) showing the £85k+ swing.
