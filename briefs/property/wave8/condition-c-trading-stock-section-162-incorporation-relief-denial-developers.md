- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> THE CONTRARIAN angle on TCGA 1992 s.162 incorporation relief — when s.162 does NOT apply because the property is appropriated as TRADING STOCK under Condition C of the four-conditions test (CTA 2010 s.356OB(6) / ITA 2007 s.517B(6) — verbatim verified 2026-05-25: "the land is held as trading stock"). Condition C is the **deterministic** test in the four-conditions set — no main-purpose evaluation needed; if the land is held as trading stock, the regime engages. Page works the architecture defeating the s.162 incorporation framing for property developers: (a) verbatim Condition C wording — six words, no purpose test, deterministic; (b) s.162 TCGA 1992 statutory requirements (verified verbatim 2026-05-25 — s.162(1)(a) requires "transfers to a company a business as a going concern, together with the whole assets of the business, or together with the whole of those assets other than cash" wholly or partly in exchange for shares; s.162(1)(b) requires the transferor to claim by the first 31 January anniversary following the tax year of transfer); (c) why trading-stock transfers fail s.162 — TCGA 1992 s.161 treats appropriation INTO trading stock as a deemed disposal at market value (CGT event), and s.162's "business as a going concern" requirement is read as excluding trading-stock appropriations because the property has changed character from investment to stock at the appropriation point (per HP §28.6 + §28.11); (d) the ALTERNATIVE route — CTA 2010 s.157 + Part 22 (intra-group transfers of trade) for a developer SPV moving trading-stock between group companies, plus ITTOIA 2005 s.172 for appropriation FROM trading stock (rare); (e) the CIHC interaction — §21.A.2 CT three-figure framework + the EXPANSIVE s.18N(3) "let commercially" exclusion (Stage 1b correction — "let commercially" reaches connected persons + spouses/civil partners + relatives + spouses of relatives — so family-tenant property in a developer SPV is potentially CIHC, taxed at 25% main rate regardless of profit level); (f) the relationship to the existing PILLAR page `section-162-incorporation-relief-property-landlords` — complementary not cannibal: existing page covers the standard investment-business-incorporation case; B4 covers the contrarian developer / trading-stock case where s.162 fails; (g) worked example — landlord-developer incorporating £2m of trading-stock-classified development property — s.162 route DENIED, TCGA 1992 s.161 deemed disposal at £2m MV triggers CGT (or trading profit if also caught by Conditions A/D), Part 22 trade-transfer route the operative alternative for intra-group restructures. NOT writing Conditions A/D (B2/B3); NOT writing the standard s.162 mechanics (covered by existing pillar).
