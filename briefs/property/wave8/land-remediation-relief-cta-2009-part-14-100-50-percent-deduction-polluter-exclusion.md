- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> The "100% normal deduction + 50% additional deduction = 150% total" framing per CTA 2009 Part 14 (ss.1143-1175 — verified 2026-05-25 against legislation.gov.uk; Part 14 ends at s.1175, NOT s.1179 per Stage 1 drift catch instruction). Per s.1149(8) verbatim: "The amount of the additional deduction is 50% of the qualifying land remediation expenditure" (verified verbatim 2026-05-25). Plus the **polluter exclusion at s.1150 + relevant-connection definition at s.1178** (drift catch: HP §25.12.4 cites "Relevant connection (s.1150(3))" but the verified statutory reference is OUTBOUND to s.1178 from s.1150(1)(b) — s.1178 holds the relevant-connection definition; sessions citing relevant-connection must cite s.1178, not s.1150(3)). LRR is for INNOCENT remediators only — acquired-contaminated cases (vendor caused contamination) typically qualify; caused-contamination cases (claimant caused) do not. **COMPANY-ONLY relief** — individual landlords cannot claim. Page walks: (a) the additive 100% + 50% = 150% framing (per HP §25.12.9 do-not-write — the bare "150% relief" shorthand misframes the architecture and can mislead on interactions with other reliefs); (b) the **SIX qualifying conditions A-F per s.1144** (verified 2026-05-25 — not four; Condition A contaminated/derelict state via s.1145 / s.1145A; Condition B causation; Condition C activity relevance per ss.1146 / 1146A; Condition D activity categories — staffing, materials, contracted-out, qualifying connected sub-contracted; Condition E not subsidised per s.1177; Condition F not landfill tax); (c) revenue vs capital treatment — s.1147 revenue-account deduction; s.1148 capital-account election (most landlord-developer remediation is capital-account); (d) the **polluter exclusion s.1150** verbatim — "A company is not entitled to relief under this Chapter in respect of expenditure on land all or part of which is in a contaminated or derelict state if the land is in a contaminated or derelict state wholly or partly as a result of any thing done, or omitted to be done, at any time by — (a) the company, or (b) a person with a relevant connection to the company (see section 1178)" (verified verbatim 2026-05-25); (e) the **s.1178 relevant-connection three-pathway test** — (i) connected when contamination/dereliction act/omission occurred; (ii) connected when major interest in land was acquired by the company; (iii) connected at any time when relevant land remediation was undertaken — temporal nexus required at one of three points; (f) the **16% loss-conversion tax credit per s.1154** — "16% of the amount of the qualifying land remediation loss" (verified verbatim 2026-05-25); economically WORSE than carry-forward at marginal CT rate (25% main / 26.5% marginal / 19% SPR per §21.A) unless cash-flow dominates; useful for early-stage developer SPVs with no taxable profit; (g) cross-references — Wave 6 §25 (CAA 2001 capital allowances framework — LRR sits OUTSIDE the CAA regime, separate Part 14 of CTA 2009); Wave 7 §25.11 (s.198 fixtures election — purchase-side capital allowances; LRR + s.198 operate on the same brownfield-acquisition fact pattern but on different cost categories); §25.12 HP-lock (closes Wave 6 F-17 inter-wave queue item); §28 transactions-in-UK-land (trading vs investment determines whether LRR claimed under s.1147 revenue route or s.1148 capital-account election).
