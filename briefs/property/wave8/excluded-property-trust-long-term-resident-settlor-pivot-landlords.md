- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> The trust-side operational depth on the settlor-LTR pivot. Page anchors verbatim on **IHTA 1984 s.6** (post-FA-2025 LTR-based excluded property test per FA 2025 s.44(2)(4)) plus the **FA 2025 Sch 13 paras 40-46 transitional provisions** for pre-6-April-2025 trusts. A8 builds on A7's s.6A architecture — sessions cross-link A7 as the LTR-test pillar. (a) **The settlor-LTR pivot framework.** Pre-FA-2025, a trust's excluded-property status depended on the **settlor's domicile** at the time of addition to the trust (under historic s.6 read with s.48). Post-FA-2025, status depends on whether the **settlor was LTR** (per A7's s.6A test) at the time of property addition. The pivot from domicile to LTR is the defining architectural change. (b) **The three operational outcomes.** A trust contains property. The status test runs at the time of EACH addition (not at trust formation). Outcomes: (i) settlor non-LTR at time of addition AND property situated outside UK → excluded property, outside UK IHT (Relevant Property Regime exit charges still apply per IHTA 1984 ss.64-65 but on a £0 chargeable value for excluded property); (ii) settlor LTR at time of addition AND property situated outside UK → NOT excluded; within UK IHT on the worldwide value, subject to 10-year periodic charges and exit charges; (iii) UK situs property → always within UK IHT regardless of settlor LTR status (Schedule A1 enveloped-residential look-through still bites for UK residential held offshore). (c) **The transitional preservation per FA 2025 Sch 13 paras 40-46.** Trusts settled BEFORE 6 April 2025 by then-non-UK-domiciled settlors retain excluded-property status for property settled before 6 April 2025. So a pre-FA-2025 EPT does not lose status on 6 April 2025 for its existing assets. But — property ADDED to the trust on or after 6 April 2025 is tested under the new LTR settlor regime. So a now-LTR settlor adding £200k to a previously-fully-excluded EPT on 1 May 2025 creates a "mixed" trust where the pre-6-April-2025 £-base remains excluded but the new £200k addition is within UK IHT. (d) **The HMRC trust-tax tracking burden.** Trustees must track per-addition status — date of addition, settlor LTR status at that date, situs at addition. The 10-year periodic charge (s.64) and exit charges (s.65) apply at the trust level on the non-excluded slice. Sessions write this tracking obligation explicitly. (e) **Sch A1 enveloped-residential look-through UNAFFECTED by FA 2025.** UK residential property held via offshore corporate / trust structures since 6 April 2017 has been within UK IHT regardless of any other excluded-property analysis. FA 2025 does not change this — Sch A1 continues to apply. So a non-LTR settlor's offshore trust holding UK residential via a BVI company is still within UK IHT on the UK residential value under Sch A1, even though the corporate share itself might otherwise be excluded property under the LTR pivot. Cross-reference §22.X.4 + §22.X.1 + §15.6. NOT writing s.6A LTR test depth (A7 pillar covers) or spouse exemption + s.267ZC (A9 separate).

**Stage 2 manager note:** Stage 2 cross-check against IHTA 1984 s.6 on legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-25 confirmed verbatim wording: "Property situated outside the United Kingdom is excluded property if the person beneficially entitled to it is an individual who is not a long-term UK resident" (FA 2025 s.44(2)(4)). Note s.6 wording itself refers to "individual" beneficially entitled — the trust-settlor-pivot for relevant-property trusts works through separate trust-side provisions (FA 2025 s.45 changes for settled property; Sch 13 paras 40-46 transitional). Sessions writing on trust-side mechanics should cite s.45 + Sch 13 paras 40-46 for the settlor-LTR pivot, not s.6 alone. Sch A1 unaffected — confirmed by FA 2025 reading.
