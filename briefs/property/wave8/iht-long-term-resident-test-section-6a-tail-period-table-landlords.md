> The IHT long-term-resident (LTR) PILLAR page for the Wave 8 IHT cluster. A7 anchors the s.6A statutory test that A8 (excluded property trust pivot) and A9 (spouse exemption + s.267ZC election) build on. Page anchors verbatim on **IHTA 1984 s.6A** (inserted by FA 2025 Sch 13, effective 6 April 2025) with the 10-of-20 baseline test and the 8-row tail-period lookup table. Verified verbatim against legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-25. (a) **The baseline test (s.6A(1) verbatim):** "an individual is a 'long-term UK resident' at all times in a tax year if they were UK resident for at least 10 of the previous 20 tax years". Single test, applied tax-year by tax-year. (b) **"UK resident" means SRT-resident.** Cross-reference to FA 2013 Sch 45 — same residence concept as income tax and CGT. No separate IHT residence definition. (c) **The tail-period table (s.6A(3) verbatim — full 8 rows):** required consecutive non-UK tax years to lose LTR status post-departure scale from 3 (for 13 or fewer prior years) up to 10 (for 20 prior years):
>
> | Prior UK-resident tax years (within last 20) | Required consecutive non-UK tax years to lose LTR |
> |---|---|
> | 13 or fewer | 3 |
> | 14 | 4 |
> | 15 | 5 |
> | 16 | 6 |
> | 17 | 7 |
> | 18 | 8 |
> | 19 | 9 |
> | 20 (all 20) | 10 |
>
> (d) **Operational implication for landlord-emigrants.** A landlord UK-resident for 15 of the last 20 tax years (typical for established UK BTL portfolio holders) requires **5 consecutive non-UK tax years** post-departure to lose LTR status — NOT 3 years (the popular misconception from the pre-FA-2025 deemed-domicile s.267(1)(b) flat-3-year tail). During the tail period the individual remains within UK IHT on **worldwide assets** including overseas property, overseas pensions, and overseas investments. (e) **s.6B young-person variation (under-20s).** For individuals under age 20 at start of current tax year, the "20" denominator is replaced by actual whole tax years alive pre-current tax year; "10" numerator is half that (rounded up). Under-1s NOT LTR per s.6B(3). Relevant for trust-of-minor planning. (f) **s.6 excluded property test (verbatim):** "Property situated outside the United Kingdom is excluded property if the person beneficially entitled to it is an individual who is not a long-term UK resident" (FA 2025 s.44(2)(4)). So non-LTR = worldwide property outside UK IHT; LTR = worldwide property inside UK IHT. UK situs property always in UK IHT regardless of LTR status. (g) **What the historic s.267 deemed-domicile test gave you — and why it's gone.** Pre-FA-2025: deemed domicile after 15-of-20 + 3-year tail to break. Post-FA-2025: s.267 omitted; the s.6A 10-of-20 + scaling-tail table replaces. Sessions writing post-6-April-2025 advice must NOT cite s.267 (historic framework removed). Cross-reference §22.X.1 + §22.X.2 + §15.6. NOT writing trust-side mechanics (A8 separate) or spouse exemption mechanics (A9 separate) or returning-to-UK + s.10A (A10 separate). A7 is the LTR-test pillar; A8 + A9 cross-link back to A7 for the s.6A architecture.
