- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> Commercial-property TOGC depth — the option-matching trap that destroys most attempted property TOGCs at completion. Page walks: (a) **The TOGC framework** under SI 1995/1268 (Value Added Tax (Special Provisions) Order 1995) Article 5 — verified 2026-05-25. A transfer of a business as a going concern is treated as NEITHER a supply of goods NOR a supply of services for VAT purposes. No output VAT chargeable; no input-tax recovery question for the transferee on a non-supply. The primary cite is SI 1995/1268 Article 5; VATA 1994 s.49 is the registration-and-continuity provision, NOT the TOGC operative cite (common drift catch); (b) **The general conditions** — (i) the assets transferred must be used by the transferee in carrying on the SAME KIND of business as the transferor (not necessarily identical, but materially the same); (ii) the transferee must already be, or immediately become, a taxable person under VATA 1994 (or the Manx equivalent); (iii) no significant break in trading between transferor cessation and transferee commencement; (c) **The property-specific conditions** for sale of land + buildings in the TOGC: (i) **the option-matching requirement** — where the transferor has opted to tax the property under Sch 10, the TRANSFEREE must itself opt to tax AND notify HMRC of that option no later than "the relevant date" (typically the tax point — the earlier of the date of supply or any pre-payment); (ii) **the para-12 exclusion** — the TOGC treatment does NOT apply where the transferee's onward supplies of the property would be exempt under Sch 10 para 12 anti-avoidance (developers-of-exempt-land — see C7); (iii) **the written notification to the transferor** — transferee must notify the transferor in writing that the exemption-conditions (paras 5/6/12) do not apply to them; (d) **The operational walk-through** — typical sale of a fully-let commercial multi-let office building from vendor (opted) to purchaser. Steps: (1) purchaser confirms VAT-registration status; (2) purchaser opts to tax property pre-completion + notifies HMRC pre-completion via VAT 1614A; (3) purchaser issues written notification to vendor that disapplications under Sch 10 paras 5/6/12 do not apply; (4) parties contract on no-VAT basis with TOGC indemnity. Failure at ANY step = standard-rated supply with 20% VAT chargeable, recoverable inclusive of any SDLT-on-VAT-inclusive-consideration uplift (FA 2003 s.51); (e) **The cashflow + SDLT implications** of getting it WRONG — a £5m opted commercial building wrongly invoiced as TOGC carries £1m output VAT that the vendor must remit even if not collected from the purchaser, plus the SDLT on a VAT-inclusive £6m consideration costs ~£60k more in residential or ~£25k more in commercial SDLT than on a £5m base. Material risk; (f) **The bare-land + part-let scenarios** that fall outside TOGC — vendor selling vacant building that has never been let (no business to transfer); part-let building where unlet portions may not count toward "going concern" depending on lease structure. Distinct from C1 (framework pillar) and C2 (revocation depth — option-matching here PRESERVES the option, no revocation).
