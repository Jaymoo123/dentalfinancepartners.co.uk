- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> Property partnerships sitting on the trading-vs-investment line — JV developer structures (typical: landowner + developer + funder partnership) commonly fall under Condition A or Condition D and are taxed as TRADING partnerships at marginal rates (40% / 45% income tax + Class 4 NIC for individual partners; CT for corporate partners), not as INVESTMENT partnerships at CGT rates (18% / 24% post-30-October-2024 per §5). Page walks: (a) the partnership-level trading-vs-investment classification — CTA 2010 Part 8ZB / ITA 2007 Part 9A applies at the **partner level** on the partner's share of partnership profit/gain (per ITTOIA 2005 s.848 partnership taxation framework — partnership is transparent for income tax / CGT, opaque for some other purposes); each partner's share of profit on a partnership land disposal is tested against Conditions A-D; (b) the **typical landowner + developer + funder JV** structure — landowner contributes land, developer contributes services + project management, funder contributes capital + risk capital; profit-share allocations differ from capital-contribution ratios; partnership-deed waterfall typical (developer + funder priority returns, then landowner residual or vice versa); (c) the **anti-fragmentation interaction** per B6 — JV partnership structures with separated developer / landowner / profit-recipient pose s.356OH / s.517H attribution risk where parties are connected or related; deed-level fragmentation cannot defeat the attribution mechanic; (d) the **incorporation route constraint** per B4 — partnership-to-company incorporation typically denied s.162 incorporation relief if partnership holds development land as trading stock; Part 22 trade-transfer route is the operative alternative for partnership-to-company transitions; (e) the **FA 2003 Sch 15 SDLT interaction** at partnership transitions — Sch 15 Part 3 governs transfers IN to partnerships (para 10 — sum of lower proportions for connected-party transfers), partnership-interest transfers (paras 14, 17 — property-investment partnership rules), and transfers OUT (para 18); existing PTP partnership-SDLT pillar at `partnership-sdlt-relief-schedule-15-fa-2003-incorporation-sum-lower-proportions` covers SDLT-side mechanics — B10 cross-links and notes the trading-vs-investment line is SEPARATE from SDLT classification (SDLT is acquisition-side charge; transactions-in-UK-land is disposal-side trading-profit charge; no double-charge issue per HP §28.8 but architecturally distinct); (f) **practical partnership-deed drafting** — exit / sale waterfall must address Part 8ZB / Part 9A exposure for individual partners; SPA wording should address whether partner share of profit is reported as trading income (Schedule D Case I via ITTOIA 2005) or chargeable gain (TCGA 1992); enquiry-pack documentation must support the trading-vs-investment classification at partnership level (badges of trade per B7 as supporting evidence under the main-purpose evaluation); (g) corporate partner cases — LLPs with corporate members (developer SPV + funder SPV + landowner SPV) — each corporate partner's share is tested against Part 8ZB; CT-side trading profit at 19% / 26.5% / 25% per §21.A. NOT writing the SDLT Sch 15 mechanics in depth (covered by existing pillar); NOT writing Conditions A/D in depth (B2/B3).
