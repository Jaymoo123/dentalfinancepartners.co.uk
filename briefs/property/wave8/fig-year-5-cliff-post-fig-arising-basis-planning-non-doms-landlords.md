- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> The hard year-5 cliff and the pre-cliff planning playbook for landlord-new-arrivals. Page anchors on **ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(2)** verbatim — relief applies for the qualifying tax year plus "the next three tax years" — meaning **maximum four tax years** of FIG access. From tax year 5 onwards the individual is taxed on the **arising basis** as a standard UK resident on worldwide income and gains under the general charging provisions (ITTOIA 2005 for property income; TCGA 1992 s.1 for gains). (a) **No transitional taper.** Unlike the historic remittance-basis approach where a non-dom could continue claiming year-on-year indefinitely (subject to the £30k / £60k / £90k remittance basis charge under ITA 2007 s.809C), FIG has a binary cliff — year 4 is fully exempt on foreign income / gains; year 5 is fully on arising basis. No partial relief; no scaled-down exemption. (b) **Pre-cliff portfolio restructuring options.** Within the 4-year window the individual can: (i) **gift non-UK assets** to family before year 5 — capital gifts of non-UK situs assets by a non-LTR individual (per the IHT s.6 excluded property rule per §22.X.4) are outside UK IHT; CGT s.17 deemed disposal still applies at market value but FIG covers the gain itself if the gift triggers a chargeable event; (ii) **settle non-UK assets into trust** — IHT excluded-property treatment depends on settlor's LTR status at the time of addition (per §22.X.4 — pre-LTR-status additions to trust are excluded property going forward, even if settlor later becomes LTR after year 5); (iii) **accelerate foreign income receipts** into years 1-4 (declare foreign dividends, realise foreign gains, receive foreign property income) so they are FIG-claimable; defer arising-basis-eligible UK income / gains until after year 4. (c) **TRF interaction for prior remittance basis cohort (rare for new arrivals).** A new arrival in 2025-26 + claiming FIG does NOT need TRF — TRF (§17.9) is for the pre-2025-26 remittance-basis cohort with pre-2025-26 unremitted balances. A new arrival has no pre-2025-26 UK tax history to clean up. Sessions writing must NOT conflate FIG year-5 cliff (new arrivals) with TRF window-close (pre-2025-26 cohort) — different populations, different mechanisms. (d) **Departure-before-cliff as a planning option.** Individuals who depart UK before year 5 (taking themselves outside the FIG window) re-set the eligibility clock — but **only if** they then satisfy 10-tax-year prior non-residence on re-arrival per s.845B(1) (per A1 brief). Mid-window departure does not bank year 5 onwards as "future FIG", it just ends the current 4-year window. Cross-reference §17.6 + §17.8. NOT writing eligibility-gateway (A1) or election cost calculus (A2) or TRF mechanics (A4-A5) or returning-to-UK + s.10A interaction (A10).

**Stage 2 manager note:** Stage 2 cross-check confirmed s.845B(2) provides the 4-year window verbatim — "next three tax years after a qualifying tax year". HP §17.8.2 correctly forbids "year 5 is a taper year" framing. Sessions must NOT cite ITA 2007 s.809B (remittance basis claim) as a fallback after the FIG cliff — s.809B claims are abolished from 2025-26 per FA 2025 s.40; the year-5 individual has only the arising basis or departure. Pre-cliff restructuring around the s.6 excluded property regime (§22.X.4) is the highest-value planning hook and the operational complement to the FIG cliff itself.

**Pool-thinness disclosure:** Competitor coverage tends to frame the cliff as a "consideration" without working the specific pre-cliff planning steps.
