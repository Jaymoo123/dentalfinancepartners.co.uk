- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> Pre-FA-2016 planning routes for non-resident corporate developers (using offshore structures to escape UK CT scope) are CLOSED. Both Parts (CTA 2010 Part 8ZB + ITA 2007 Part 9A) catch non-UK-resident persons — the chargeable-person scope at CTA 2010 s.356OG / ITA 2007 s.517G is not territorially-restricted (verified 2026-05-25 — s.517G "P (the person realising the profit or gain)" contains no residence condition; same for s.356OG; HP §28.1 framing confirmed). Page walks: (a) the pre-2016 offshore-developer planning route — typical structure: Cayman / BVI / Channel Islands development SPV holds UK development site, executes development off-balance-sheet (via UK service-co or contracted-out arrangements), sells the developed land or the SPV shares; pre-2016 the offshore SPV claimed no UK CT trading-profit exposure (UK CT scope was UK-resident or UK-PE based); (b) why it now fails — Part 8ZB / Part 9A apply regardless of UK residence; the chargeable person at s.356OG / s.517G is "the person who realises the profit or gain" with no residence carve-out; the regime explicitly designed to close the offshore-developer planning gap noted in HMRC consultation pre-FA-2016; (c) the **NRCGT-vs-Part 8ZB priority rule** per HP §28.8 — where transaction is trading, Part 8ZB / Part 9A takes PRIORITY over NRCGT (TCGA 1992 s.1A) because Part 8ZB / Part 9A imposes trading-profit treatment (income), not chargeable-gain treatment (capital); NRCGT operates as the capital-gain backstop where the transaction is not trading; (d) the typical fact pattern walk-through — non-resident corporate developer acquires UK development site via offshore SPV; develops via UK service-co or main contractor; sells the developed land; under Part 8ZB, profit treated as trading profit at the prevailing CT rate (25% main rate; SPR not typically available because the SPV is offshore and likely doesn't meet the s.18N qualifying-purpose carve-out architecture — per §21.A); (e) interaction with double-tax treaties — UK CT charge on trading profits is generally consistent with most UK DTAs; treaty-shopping route via Mauritius / Cyprus / Luxembourg holding companies offers no Part 8ZB defence because the regime catches the realising person regardless of corporate residence; (f) the non-resident developer's compliance position — UK CT return filing required; agent / branch / fixed place of business not required to trigger Part 8ZB scope; payment on account regime applies; (g) the standalone application across all four conditions — Part 8ZB applies to non-residents on Conditions A, B, C, and D equally (per HP §28.11 — "non-resident developers escape UK tax" is forbidden framing); (h) cross-reference to existing NRCGT-side coverage and SRT decision-tree for the residence-status determination. NOT writing the four-conditions framework in depth (cite B1 PILLAR); NOT writing the indirect-disposal mechanics (B5).
