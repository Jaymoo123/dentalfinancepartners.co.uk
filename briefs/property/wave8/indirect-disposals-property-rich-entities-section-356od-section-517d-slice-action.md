- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> The "slice of the action" planning pattern that this regime exists to defeat — a developer takes profit-share via SHARE DISPOSAL in a property-rich corporate intermediary (or partnership-interest disposal) rather than direct land disposal, hoping to convert trading profit into capital gain. CTA 2010 s.356OD / ITA 2007 s.517D catches indirect disposals where property "derives at least 50% of its value from land in the United Kingdom" (verbatim verified 2026-05-25 — drift catch: HP §28.3 describes s.356OD as a "parallel main-purpose test"; the verified statute uses a **three-condition framework** for indirect disposals, not a restatement of the four-conditions test — see Stage 2 drift note below). Page walks: (a) the verbatim s.356OD operative test — three conditions: (i) disposal of property deriving at least 50% of value from UK land; (ii) person is a party to or concerned in an arrangement concerning some or all of the land; (iii) main purpose (or one of main purposes) of the arrangement is to deal in or develop the project land AND realise profit from disposal of property deriving value from that land; (b) the **50% derivation test** — interpreted in line with NRCGT property-rich tests; tracing rules at CTA 2010 s.356OM / ITA 2007 s.517N walk through multi-tier structures (offset numbering: s.356OM pairs with s.517N, not s.517M — drift catch); (c) the "slice of the action" counterfactual — pre-2016 standard planning route was to hold development land in an SPV and sell SPV shares instead of selling the land, taking the profit as capital gain at lower rates; the regime closes this route; (d) interaction with NRCGT (TCGA 1992 s.1A) — for non-resident sellers, both NRCGT and Part 8ZB / Part 9A can engage; Part 8ZB / Part 9A takes PRIORITY where engaged (trading profit treatment, not chargeable gain) per HP §28.8; NRCGT operates as the capital-gain backstop where transaction is not trading; (e) SSE interaction — substantial shareholding exemption (TCGA 1992 Sch 7AC) for corporate sellers of trading-company shares is NOT a defence to s.356OD because s.356OD operates on TRADING profit treatment (income), not chargeable gain — SSE is a chargeable-gain relief, structurally distinct; (f) the typical fact pattern — developer holds a portfolio of development sites in an SPV (or SPV-of-SPVs structure); commercial sale is structured as a share sale to the buyer (preserving tax basis for buyer's purposes and avoiding SDLT on the SPV's underlying land); s.356OD engages on the seller's profit if main-purpose evaluation is met. NOT writing direct-land Conditions A/B/C/D (B1-B4); NOT writing anti-fragmentation (B6); NOT writing non-resident scope (B8).

**Stage 1 manager note:** B5 is the indirect-disposal overlay. Cross-references B1 PILLAR for the framework and B8 for the non-resident interaction with NRCGT. **Stage 2 drift catch:** s.356OD is NOT a restatement of the four-conditions test (HP §28.3 says "parallel main-purpose test") — verified text shows a three-condition framework specific to indirect disposals: (1) 50% UK-land derivation; (2) arrangement participation; (3) main-purpose test on the arrangement. Sessions writing must use the three-condition framework, not assume Conditions A-D are re-applied to share disposals. **Recommend manager refresh HP §28.3 to reflect three-condition framework.**

**Pool-thinness disclosure:** Competitor coverage tends to conflate s.356OD with NRCGT's indirect-disposal mechanic, or to misframe the section as a restatement of the four-conditions test. Clean separation of s.356OD (trading profit treatment via 50% derivation + arrangement participation + arrangement main purpose) from NRCGT s.1A + Sch 1A (chargeable gain via 25% / 75% participation thresholds) is the defensible point.
