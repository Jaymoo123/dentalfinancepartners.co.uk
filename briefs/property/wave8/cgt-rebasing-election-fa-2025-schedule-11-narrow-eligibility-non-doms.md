> The counter-narrative page on the FA 2025 CGT rebasing election. Page anchors verbatim on **FA 2025 s.42 + Sch 11** with the FIVE cumulative eligibility conditions per Sch 11 para 1(1) that most departing UK landlords will FAIL — contrary to widespread misconception that "rebasing to 5 April 2017 value helps UK property holders". Verified verbatim against legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-25. (a) **Condition (a) — held on 5 April 2017.** Asset must have been held by the individual on the rebasing date. Disposes of post-2017-acquired assets are excluded. (b) **Condition (b) — disposal on or after 6 April 2025.** Pre-6-April-2025 disposals fall under the old regime; the operative window opened with FA 2025 commencement. (c) **Condition (c) — asset NOT situated in UK between 6 March 2024 and 5 April 2025.** The asset must have been a NON-UK asset throughout that 13-month look-back window. **A UK property held by a non-dom does NOT qualify** — UK situs throughout the window defeats condition (c). This is the killer condition for UK landlords. Sessions write this explicitly: rebasing benefits non-UK assets only. (d) **Condition (d) — individual not domiciled in UK before tax year 2025-26.** Pre-2025-26 non-dom status required. Someone who acquired UK domicile of choice mid-2024 cannot use rebasing. (e) **Condition (e) — active s.809B remittance basis claim history.** Individual must have made a claim under ITA 2007 s.809B for at least one tax year within 2017-18 to 2024-25, AND for those years neither s.809D (automatic RB for low-foreign-income) nor s.809E (small-foreign-income exception) applied. So they must have ACTIVELY elected remittance basis at least once — automatic RB or de minimis exception doesn't satisfy. (f) **Rebasing date verified verbatim: 5 April 2017.** NOT 5 April 2019 (which appeared in earlier consultation but was NOT enacted) and NOT 5 April 2025. The Sch 11 para 1(2) wording is: "it is to be assumed that P acquired the asset on 5 April 2017 for a consideration equal to its market value on that date". (g) **Per-disposal election — irrevocable (para 3 verbatim).** Default IS rebasing for qualifying assets; election under para 3 is to opt OUT for a specific disposal. "An election under this paragraph is irrevocable" per statute. (h) **UK property is NRCGT not Sch 11.** Departing landlords selling UK land use the NRCGT rebasing regime (TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A/1B/4AA) with the separate rebasing dates **5 April 2015** (residential) or **5 April 2019** (non-residential). NRCGT rebasing is the operative regime for UK situs; FA 2025 Sch 11 is for non-UK situs only. NOT writing FIG eligibility (A1) or TRF (A4-A5) or IHT LTR test (A7). Cross-reference §17.10 + §17.6 + §17.4.
