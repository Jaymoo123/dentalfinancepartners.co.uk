> The four-quadrant decision matrix for cross-border spouse-portfolio planning post-FA-2025. Page anchors verbatim on **IHTA 1984 s.18** (spouse exemption, post-FA-2025 LTR-based) and **IHTA 1984 s.267ZC** (SPOUSAL LTR election — NOT generic, refreshed per HP §22.5 + §22.X.5 on 2026-05-25). A9 builds on A7's s.6A architecture. (a) **The four-quadrant matrix.** Outcomes depend on BOTH spouses' LTR status:
>
> | Transferor LTR? | Receiving spouse LTR? | s.18 outcome |
> |---|---|---|
> | LTR | LTR | s.18(1) unlimited exemption — no IHT on transfer |
> | LTR | non-LTR | s.18(2) limited exemption — capped at s.18(2A) by-reference value (currently £325k cumulative lifetime per Sch 1 Table NRB upper limit); receiving spouse may elect under s.267ZC to access full exemption (cost: worldwide assets into UK IHT scope) |
> | non-LTR | LTR | s.18(1) full exemption — transferor's status not material; receiving spouse's LTR status is the gating criterion per s.18 wording |
> | non-LTR | non-LTR | s.18(1) full exemption — but transferor's non-LTR status means only UK situs property in UK IHT scope at all (per s.6 excluded property per §22.X.4); the exemption is structurally less material because worldwide non-UK assets are already outside UK IHT |
>
> (b) **The s.18(2A) by-reference cap.** Sessions must NOT hard-code £325k. The s.18(2A) wording (verified verbatim 2026-05-25) references "the amount shown in the second column of the first row of the Table in Schedule 1" — so the cap automatically tracks the NRB. Current value £325k for 2026-27 per Sch 1 Table (rate-by-reference per §16.27). (c) **s.267ZC SPOUSAL election (refreshed per HP §22.5 + §22.X.5 on 2026-05-25 — NOT a generic election).** Verified verbatim from legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-25 — the election requires a SPOUSAL CONNECTION; there is NO freestanding "anyone non-LTR can elect to be LTR" route. **Condition A (s.267ZC(3) verbatim):** "at any time within the period of 7 years ending with the date on which the election is made, P had a spouse or civil partner who was a long-term UK resident". **Condition B (s.267ZC(4) verbatim):** "a person ('the deceased') dies and, at any time within the period of 7 years ending with the date of their death, the deceased was — (a) a long-term UK resident, and (b) the spouse or civil partner of P". So the election is SPOUSAL-only — open to non-LTR spouses of LTR spouses (Condition A, lifetime election), or to surviving spouses (or PRs) of deceased LTR spouses (Condition B, post-death election). Sessions writing on the election must write "spousal LTR election" or "s.267ZC spousal election" — NOT "generic LTR election". (d) **s.267ZA distinction — narrow transitional only.** s.267ZA was NARROWED by FA 2025 Sch 13. The spousal connection date in s.267ZA conditions A/B must be "before 6 April 2025" within the 7-year window. s.267ZA has no automatic repeal date; it remains available indefinitely for legacy spousal-connection cases (where the spouse was UK-domiciled pre-6-April-2025). Sessions writing must determine which section applies on facts: s.267ZA for pre-6-April-2025 spousal connection + pre-6-April-2025 domicile; s.267ZC for post-6-April-2025 LTR-based connection. Both are spousal. (e) **Cost of election.** Worldwide assets into UK IHT scope for the election period. Election irrevocable considerations per s.267ZD procedural. Sessions write the worked cost-vs-benefit example — when does electing IN exceed the s.18(2) £325k cap savings? (f) **s.267ZF DTA bridging.** Where a DTA operates by reference to "deemed domicile" (older treaties), s.267ZF treats deemed domicile as engaged where the individual is LTR — bridge rule for older treaties not yet updated. Cross-reference §22.X.5 + §22.X.6 + §22.5. NOT writing s.6A test depth (A7 pillar covers) or trust-side mechanics (A8 separate).
