- **Framing differentiator (Stage 2, 2026-05-25):**

> The **nine badges of trade** per *Marson v Morton* [1986] 1 WLR 1343 — framed as supporting evidence under the post-FA-2016 statutory four-conditions test (CTA 2010 Part 8ZB / ITA 2007 Part 9A), NOT as a standalone test that overrides statute. Page walks each badge against landlord-flipping fact patterns and emphasises that no single badge is determinative — the overall picture decides. Page anchors: (a) the **nine badges, verbatim and complete** per *Marson v Morton* (drift-catch discipline: do NOT condense to fewer than nine badges per Stage 1 instruction): (1) Subject matter of the realisation (commodities = trading; investment assets = capital); (2) Length of period of ownership (short = trading; long = investment); (3) Frequency or number of similar transactions (multiple = trading); (4) Supplementary work done on the property (development = trading); (5) Circumstances of realisation (forced sale = capital; marketed sale = trading); (6) Motive of the taxpayer (profit-intent at acquisition = trading); (7) Method of financing (short-term borrowing for resale = trading); (8) Profit-seeking motive; (9) Way the asset was actually used; (b) the supporting authorities — *Iswera v IRC* [1965] 1 WLR 663 (Privy Council, single transaction can be trading if profit-intent at acquisition + active steps to enhance value); *Salt v Chamberlain* [1979] STC 750 (share-dealing investor/trader line by analogy); *Page v Lowther* [1983] STC 799 (single property flip held to be trading; intent at acquisition decisive); *Pickford v Quirke* (1927) 13 TC 251 (repeated transactions establish trade even where each individual transaction might look capital); (c) **the structural relationship to the post-FA-2016 statutory regime** — per HP §28.5, the badges remain operative as supporting evidence under the Conditions A/B/D main-purpose evaluations; BUT the statutory regime can catch a transaction that fails the badges test (statutory overrides at the four-conditions level), and the badges test can support a trading conclusion even where Part 8ZB / Part 9A applies — both work in parallel; (d) **landlord-flipping fact pattern walk-through** — work each badge against three personas: serial-flipper (multiple short-hold sales), accidental-flipper (single sale with short hold due to circumstance change), and refurbish-and-sell developer (intentional value-add then sale); (e) the long-held-land trap — length of ownership is one of nine badges, NOT determinative; trading classification can apply to long-held land if main-purpose-at-acquisition or main-purpose-of-development was profit-from-disposal (per HP §28.11); (f) the standalone-test trap — do NOT present badges of trade as a standalone test that can override statute (per HP §28.11); statutory regime can catch transactions failing the badges test; (g) HMRC BIM20000+ as the working manual reference. NOT writing the statutory Conditions A/B/C/D (B1-B4); NOT writing the four-conditions framework in depth (cite B1 PILLAR).
