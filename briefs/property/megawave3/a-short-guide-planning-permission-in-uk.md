# Planning Permission for Landlords: The Tax-Aware Short Guide

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (the foundational definition of "development"): Town and Country Planning Act 1990 s.55(1) — "development" means "the carrying out of building, engineering, mining or other operations in, on, over or under land, or the making of any material change in the use of any buildings or other land." Verified verbatim 2026-05-27 against https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1990/8/section/55. s.55(2)(f) carves out movement within the same Use Class (no planning permission required for use changes within a class). §32 lock + new anchor.
- Primary (Use Classes Order — the use-class taxonomy): Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987 (SI 1987/764), as substantially amended by SI 2020/757 (introducing the consolidated Class E from 1 September 2020) and subsequent amending SIs. Class E (Commercial, business and service); Class F.1 (Learning and non-residential institutions); Class F.2 (Local community); Class C1-C4 (Residential — hotels / dwellinghouses / residential institutions / HMOs up to 6 persons); Class B2 (General industrial); Class B8 (Storage and distribution); Class Sui Generis (uses not in any class — e.g. drinking establishments, large HMOs over 6 persons). Stage 2 verifies current consolidated text + amending SIs at write time per §16.35.
- Primary (General Permitted Development Order — automatic permissions without application): Town and Country Planning (General Permitted Development) (England) Order 2015 (SI 2015/596) — confirmed in force 2026-05-27 (legislation.gov.uk shows "no known outstanding effects" for the underlying SI; Class MA exists in Schedule 2 Part 3 per the contents page). Schedule 2 catalogues PD rights by category. Key landlord-relevant classes: **Class MA** (commercial, business and service uses to dwellinghouses — introduced by SI 2021/428 effective 1 August 2021); Class A and AA (extensions and additional storeys); Class L (HMO conversions); Class Q (agricultural building to dwelling, narrowed by SI 2024/579). Stage 2 verifies Class MA conditions + limits at write time per §16.35.
- Primary (CIL — Community Infrastructure Levy): Planning Act 2008 Part 11 (ss.205-225) — foundation Act; The CIL Regulations 2010 (SI 2010/948 as amended) — operative regulations. CIL applies on chargeable development including most material changes of use; charging schedules set per-square-metre rates by charging authority. §32.1 lock.
- Primary (s.106 planning obligations): TCPA 1990 s.106 — bespoke planning obligation route; CIL Regs reg.122 five-tests for enforceability ((a) necessary; (b) directly related; (c) fairly and reasonably related in scale and kind). Pre-LURA-2023 reg.123 5-pooling restriction REPEALED. §32.2 lock.
- Primary (LURA 2023 reforms — phased commencement watchpoint): Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023 c. 55 Part 4. Pooling restriction repealed; Development Levy planned to replace CIL in stages; pages must verify in-force position at write per §32.3 lock.
- Primary (tax consequence — capital vs revenue line on planning costs): ITTOIA 2005 s.33 / CTA 2009 s.53 (no deduction for capital expenditure). Planning-permission application fees on a new development are CAPITAL (added to base cost for CGT). Application fees on minor alterations to an existing rental property may be revenue if the underlying work is repair, not improvement (see Wave 7 capital-vs-revenue distinction).
- Primary (ATED interaction — change-of-use may bring property into / out of ATED scope): FA 2013 Part 3 ss.94-174 (ATED architecture); §18 lock. A change of use from non-dwelling to dwelling can bring a property into ATED scope; sessions on landlord change-of-use must check ATED status post-change.
- Primary (SDLT interaction — change-of-use may trigger reclassification or future recalculation): FA 2003 s.116 (residential vs non-residential / mixed-use rates). Change of use after acquisition does NOT retrospectively change the SDLT due, but a sale post-change of use will use the new classification. §1 + §1.J locks.
- Primary (VAT interaction — option to tax + reduced-rate conversion): VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 1 (exempt land default); Sch 10 (option to tax — paras 5/6 automatic / certified dwelling disapplication); Sch 7A Group 6 (5% reduced rate on qualifying residential conversions); Sch 8 Group 5 (zero-rating on first-grant-of-major-interest in new dwellings). §29 lock — option-to-tax disapplies on conversion to residential per Sch 10 para 5/6.
- Primary (capital allowances on landlord-side spend during conversion): CAA 2001 Part 2 (plant and machinery); SBA per CAA 2001 Part 2A (Structures and Buildings Allowance, currently 3% straight-line per FA 2020 s.30). §25 lock.
- Supporting (judicial review of planning decisions): TCPA 1990 s.288 (statutory route for high-court challenge to certain decisions); Civil Procedure Rules Part 54 (judicial review generally). For landlord-side coverage, note only the existence of these routes; full litigation is specialist territory.
- Supporting (devolved jurisdictions): planning in Wales / Scotland / Northern Ireland operates under distinct regimes (TCPA 1990 applies to England + Wales subject to Welsh amendments; Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997; Planning (NI) Order 1991). PTP scope is England-only; sessions hedge accordingly.
- House position reference: §32 (CIL + s.106 — fully locked MW1); §32.1 (CIL statutory framework); §32.2 (s.106 framework); §32.3 (LURA 2023 reforms); §32.4 (CIL vs s.106 division of labour); §32.7 (do-not-write list); §18 (ATED architecture); §1 + §1.J (SDLT residential / mixed-use lines); §29 (VAT architecture including option-to-tax conversion-route disapplication); §25 (capital allowances). **No new HP lock needed** — the §32 lock and the related-cluster locks already cover the planning angle. Stage 1b may want to consider a §32-extension micro-lock pinning the TCPA 1990 s.55 definition + Use Classes Order Class E consolidation + GPDO 2015 Class MA conditions as quotable verbatim lines; informational, not blocking.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **When permission is needed — the s.55 definition + the use-class carve-out** — TCPA 1990 s.55(1) defines "development" as "carrying out of building, engineering, mining or other operations" OR "the making of any material change in the use of any buildings or other land" (verified verbatim 2026-05-27). Two trigger limbs: (a) operations on the land (building works); (b) material change of use. s.55(2)(f) carves out movement WITHIN the same Use Class — moving from one use to another inside Class E (e.g. shop to office) does not need permission. Movement BETWEEN classes typically does. Movement within Sui Generis uses always needs permission. Landlord starting-point: identify the current use class, identify the proposed use class, see if movement is intra-class (no permission), GPDO-allowed (no permission), or requires application.
2. **The Use Classes Order — Class E and the post-2020 consolidation** — Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987 (SI 1987/764) as substantially amended by SI 2020/757 created the consolidated **Class E** from 1 September 2020 — bundling former Class A1 (shops), A2 (financial / professional services), A3 (restaurants / cafes), B1 (business / office / light industrial), D1(a) (medical / health services), D2(e) (gyms / indoor sport). Within Class E, movement is now permitted without planning permission (s.55(2)(f) carve-out). This consolidation was the largest reform of the use-class regime since 1987 and has direct landlord-side implications: a Class E ground-floor unit can switch between shop, office, café, gym, or clinic without permission. Residential uses (Class C) remain separate. Stage 2 verifies the consolidated Class E content + the post-2020 amending SIs at write time per §16.35.
3. **Permitted Development — the GPDO 2015 (SI 2015/596) and Class MA** — Where movement between classes is needed, the GPDO 2015 may grant permission automatically. **Class MA** (commercial, business and service uses to dwellinghouses) — introduced by SI 2021/428 effective 1 August 2021 — allows change from Class E (commercial / business / service) to Class C3 (dwellinghouses) under specified conditions: maximum 1,500 sqm floor space; building vacant for at least 3 months immediately prior; not on land of "specially-designated" status (national park, AONB, conservation area etc., subject to specific exclusions). The PD route requires **prior approval** from the local planning authority (procedural step short of full permission). Other key classes: **A / AA** (single-storey rear extension; additional storeys), **L** (HMO formation conversions), **Q** (agricultural building to dwelling, narrowed by SI 2024/579), **R** (agricultural building to flexible commercial uses). Stage 2 verifies Class MA + related conditions at write time per §16.35.
4. **CIL — when chargeable development triggers it** — Planning Act 2008 Part 11 + SI 2010/948. CIL applies to chargeable development including (a) new build over 100 sqm, (b) creation of a new dwelling, (c) certain change-of-use development. Landlord conversion of an office to a flat IS chargeable development for CIL purposes. Rates are local-authority set — CIL charging schedules published by each charging authority — from £0 (no CIL adopted) to £400+ per sqm in high-charging boroughs. Key landlord exemptions: **self-build (reg.42)** for single-dwelling owner-occupier construction; **annexe + extension (reg.42A)** for 100+ sqm extensions / annexes; **social housing (regs.49-52)**; **charitable (regs.54-58)**. All exemptions require formal notice + procedural compliance + 3-year clawback discipline. Forward-link to the MW1 C1 CIL deep-dive.
5. **s.106 obligations — the bespoke layer on top of CIL** — TCPA 1990 s.106 allows bespoke obligations negotiated per development. Common categories: affordable housing, highway works (often parallel s.278 Highways Act 1980), education contributions, open-space contributions. Five-tests for enforceability per CIL Regs reg.122 — must be (a) necessary, (b) directly related, (c) fairly and reasonably related in scale and kind. **Pre-LURA-2023 pooling restriction REPEALED** — five-obligation cap on funding the same infrastructure item is gone. CIL and s.106 coexist on the same development BUT cannot fund the same item (Planning Act 2008 s.218 + CIL reg.122). Forward-link to a future s.106 deep-dive if Stage 2 + RUN decides to seed one.
6. **The tax consequences of getting permission — capital vs revenue + base cost** — Planning-application fees, professional planning consultant fees, and the substantive build cost are CAPITAL expenditure (ITTOIA 2005 s.33 / CTA 2009 s.53). Added to base cost of the property for CGT purposes (TCGA 1992 s.38 — "enhancement expenditure"). NOT deductible against rental income. The deductible-vs-capitalisable line is decisive: a landlord redoing a roof on an existing rental property is repair (revenue-deductible); a landlord obtaining planning permission to add a storey to the same property is enhancement (capital). The line is fact-sensitive at the margin; Wave 7 capital-vs-revenue cluster covers the deeper test (forward-link). For corporate property businesses, the same architecture under CTA 2009 — capital expenditure adds to base cost; revenue spend deductible.
7. **The change-of-use ATED trap** — A change of use from non-dwelling to dwelling can bring a property into ATED scope (FA 2013 Part 3) if (a) the property is held in a corporate envelope AND (b) value exceeds £500,000 (post-1-April-2016 ATED threshold). Landlord-developer companies converting commercial to residential need to plan for ATED entry post-conversion: rates per ATED band (£500k-£1m = £4,500 per annum 2024/25; bands escalate steeply); rates indexed annually per FA 2013 s.101. §18 lock + §18 ATED cluster forward-link. Mitigation routes: ATED relief for property-development trade (FA 2013 s.137); for property-rental businesses (s.133); for charitable use (s.150). Each relief requires the property to satisfy the trade / business / use test on a daily basis.
8. **The VAT change-of-use mechanics** — Where a commercial property is converted to residential, two VAT levers engage: (a) the developer's **option to tax** under VATA 1994 Sch 10 para 2 (which may have been in place on the commercial property to allow input-tax recovery) is **automatically disapplied for the residential conversion** under Sch 10 para 5 (intended dwellings / relevant residential purpose); recipient-certification disapplication available under para 6 / VAT 1614D. (b) The **5% reduced rate** on qualifying residential conversion services under Sch 7A Group 6 applies to construction services that convert non-residential into residential, or that change the number of dwellings. The 5% rate is a developer's lever for cash-flow on the build cost. The onward sale as a first-grant-of-major-interest in a new dwelling is **zero-rated** under Sch 8 Group 5, making the 5% input tax FULLY recoverable. §29.2-29.7 lock for the architecture.
9. **The SDLT non-retrospection rule** — SDLT is charged at acquisition based on the use at the effective date of transaction (FA 2003 s.116). A change of use AFTER acquisition does NOT retrospectively recalculate the SDLT due — the original transaction is closed. But a future SALE will use the use at the date of that sale; a commercial-to-residential conversion will increase SDLT for the next buyer (residential rates plus possibly the higher rates additional dwelling surcharge). The buyer's SDLT calculation is the buyer's problem; the seller-landlord doesn't owe more SDLT on the conversion itself. §1 + §1.J locks.
10. **The planning + Part 8ZB / Part 9A interaction — when planning moves you onto the developer side** — A landlord obtaining planning permission for a substantial development, then carrying out the development with intent to sell, sits squarely in CTA 2010 Part 8ZB / ITA 2007 Part 9A territory. Condition D (development main-purpose test) catches a landlord who acquires-to-let then develops-to-sell — the long-held rental status does not insulate the disposal from trading classification. The C8 sibling page (`are-you-a-property-investor-or-developer`) is the decision-guide; this page is the early-warning that planning permission is the OBVIOUS HMRC signal that intent has shifted. Investment-side landlords planning a major extension or conversion must work through the C8 framework before committing. Forward-link to C8.
