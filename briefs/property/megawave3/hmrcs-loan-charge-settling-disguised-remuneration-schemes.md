# HMRC's Loan Charge: Settling Disguised Remuneration Schemes — A Property-Business Director Guide

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — loan charge itself:** Finance (No.2) Act 2017 **Schedule 11** ("Employment income provided through third parties: loans etc outstanding on 5 April 2019"). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2017/32/schedule/11`. Schedule 11 is the operative provision that crystallises a one-off income tax + NICs charge on the outstanding balance of disguised-remuneration loans as at 5 April 2019. **In force**, with amendments per FA 2020.
- **Primary — Morse-review amendments restructuring eligibility:** Finance Act 2020 **Schedule 2** (Part 1 — "Limiting income tax charge on loans"). Verified at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2020/14/schedule/2` (Stage 2 to re-verify the section-by-section anchors at write time). FA 2020 Sch 2 reformed the loan charge to: (i) **limit charge to loans made on or after 9 December 2010** (was 6 April 1999); (ii) **carve out loans made between 9 December 2010 and 5 April 2016 where the taxpayer disclosed and HMRC did not act**; (iii) **introduce a deferral / instalment route** for taxpayers paying via PAYE; (iv) **deny double-charging** where pre-2019 income tax had already been settled.
- **Primary — disguised remuneration head-rule:** Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 **Part 7A** ("Employment income provided through third parties") ss.554A-554Z21. Inserted by FA 2011 Sch 2 from 6 April 2011. Part 7A is the bedrock statutory framework; the FA (No.2) 2017 Sch 11 loan charge is the 2019 sweep-up bolted onto Part 7A.
- **Supporting — settlement framework:** HMRC's published "**Disguised Remuneration: Settling your tax affairs**" guidance (settlement opportunity published in successive iterations from August 2017 onwards; the November 2017 published settlement terms; the September 2018 follow-up settlement opportunity; the 30 September 2020 extended deadline after the Morse review). The "Settlement Opportunity" is **administrative HMRC practice, not a statutory regime** — sessions writing on it must not frame it as a statutory route on equal footing with the loan charge itself.
- **Supporting — voluntary-disclosure route boundary:** §27.5 (CoP9 / CDF — Wave 7 lock); §27.6 (LPC + WDF + general voluntary disclosure — Wave 7 lock). The loan-charge settlement route is **distinct from** LPC (LPC is for residential landlords with undisclosed rental income), distinct from WDF (offshore), and adjacent to but separate from CoP9 / CDF (HMRC-initiated civil fraud investigation with criminal-prosecution immunity). Sessions must NOT collapse loan-charge settlement into the LPC route.
- **House position reference:** §27 (HMRC enquiry + tax compliance mechanics — Wave 7 lock) for the general penalty / mitigation architecture; §27.5 (CoP9 / CDF) for the criminal-prosecution boundary; §27.6 (voluntary disclosure routes) for the comparison framework. **NEW HP LOCK CANDIDATE FLAGGED — §27.10 "Disguised remuneration loan charge + settlement framework" mini-lock** to anchor FA (No.2) 2017 Sch 11 + FA 2020 Sch 2 Morse amendments + ITEPA 2003 Part 7A architecture for future sessions writing in this space. ## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the statutory loan charge (FA (No.2) 2017 Sch 11) and when does it bite (5 April 2019 outstanding-loan crystallisation date)?
2. How did the Morse review (December 2019) restructure the regime, and what did FA 2020 Sch 2 enact (9 December 2010 cut-off; carve-out for disclosed-and-unactioned pre-April-2016 loans; PAYE-instalment deferral route)?
3. What is the statutory disguised-remuneration head-rule (ITEPA 2003 Part 7A, ss.554A-554Z21) and how does the loan charge sit alongside it?
4. What is HMRC's published Settlement Opportunity and what are its operational terms — what was the September 2020 deadline, and is there still a route for late entrants?
5. How does the loan-charge settlement route compare with LPC / WDF / CoP9 — when is which the right route?
6. What penalty exposure attaches to a loan-charge disclosure — is it Sch 24 inaccuracy (where prior returns were under-stated) or Sch 41 failure-to-notify (where chargeability was never declared)? Both can apply on different aspects of the same scheme.
7. What is the interaction with the **§19 MTD ITSA regime** for the post-charge tax cycle — the loan charge is a one-off historic-loan crystallisation; ongoing MTD ITSA is a separate regime, and the two do not overlap statutorily.
8. What is the **5-year time-limit interaction** under TMA 1970 s.34 + s.36 — has the discovery window for pre-2019 conduct closed for innocent-error cases, or does the 20-year deliberate-behaviour limb under s.36(1A) keep HMRC's reach open?
9. What is the **"contractor loan" vs "remuneration trust" distinction** within the disguised-remuneration family, and does the loan charge bite on both equally?
10. What is the position of a **director who repaid the loan before 5 April 2019** — does the loan charge still bite, and what happens if HMRC challenges the repayment as artificial?
