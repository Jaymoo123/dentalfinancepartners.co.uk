# Know About Let Property Campaign: The Landlord's Guide to HMRC's Voluntary Disclosure Route

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — LPC as published HMRC campaign:** Let Property Campaign published guidance at **gov.uk/guidance/let-property-campaign** — HMRC campaign open since **9 September 2013**, no announced end date. The LPC is a **published administrative disclosure route**, not a standalone statutory regime; it operates within the existing Schedule 41 FA 2008 (failure-to-notify) penalty framework and Schedule 24 FA 2007 (inaccuracy) penalty framework, unlocking unprompted-disclosure mitigation floors for taxpayers who self-correct via the LPC channel rather than waiting for HMRC discovery.
- **Primary — underlying statutory framework:**
  - **Schedule 41 FA 2008** (failure-to-notify) — the operative penalty framework when a landlord has never registered for self-assessment for their rental income. Verified at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2008/9/schedule/41` per Wave 7 §27.3 lock 2026-05-24. Sch 41 para 13 imposes the disclosure mitigation: unprompted floor 0% (non-deliberate within 12 months) / 20% (deliberate) / 30% (deliberate-concealed); prompted floor 10% / 35% / 50%. Categories 2 and 3 offshore uplifts apply where rental property is overseas.
  - **Schedule 24 FA 2007** (inaccuracy) — the operative penalty framework when the landlord HAS filed self-assessment returns but those returns are inaccurate / under-stated. Standard maxima 30% / 70% / 100%; F-5 / Wave 7 correction logged 2026-05-24 — Sch 24 para 10 imposes NO 12-month qualifier on the careless-unprompted 0% floor (the 12-month qualifier is at Sch 41 para 13 only). Sessions writing must not collapse Sch 24 + Sch 41 disclosure floors.
  - **TMA 1970 section 7** — the chargeability-notification obligation that the LPC remediates. A taxpayer with new chargeability must notify HMRC within 6 months of the end of the tax year in which liability arose; failure triggers Sch 41 exposure. The LPC sits as the remediation route for that failure.
  - **TMA 1970 section 29** — HMRC's discovery power, with the time-limit framework at section 34 (4 years) / section 36(1) (careless 6 years) / section 36(1A) (deliberate 20 years) / section 36A (offshore innocent-error 12 years per FA 2019). §27.1 Wave 7 lock.
- **Primary — three-step LPC mechanic (HMRC published process):**
  - **Step 1 — Notify:** landlord notifies intent to disclose via the gov.uk LPC notification form. No penalty consequence at this stage. HMRC issues an acknowledgement and a Disclosure Reference Number (DRN).
  - **Step 2 — Disclose:** within **90 days** of HMRC's acknowledgement, the landlord submits a full disclosure — tax + interest + proposed penalty calculation, by tax year covered, with supporting evidence (rental income figures, allowable deductions, supporting documents for cost basis on any disposals during the disclosure period).
  - **Step 3 — Pay:** the full disclosed liability (tax + interest + penalty) is paid on or before submission of the disclosure. Time-to-pay arrangements may be agreed in advance with HMRC where the liability is material; this is the operational exception, not the rule.
- **Eligibility scope:** LPC is for **residential landlords** (UK + non-UK resident) with undisclosed rental income. Excludes: **companies** (LtdCo route differs — voluntary disclosure via DDS or CT-specific channels, not LPC; cross-link to A11 `how-making-tax-digital-affects-limited-companies` for the LtdCo angle); **trusts** (TRS route under §22.16 Wave 7 lock for trust-registration obligations; rental disclosure for trust property uses the SA900 amendment or DDS route); **non-residential property** (commercial / mixed-use disclosure uses the general DDS route, not LPC). House position §27.6 Wave 7 lock confirms the eligibility scope verbatim.
- **House position reference:** §27.6 (Voluntary disclosure routes — LPC + WDF + general "voluntary disclosure" — Wave 7 lock) primary; §27.3 (Sch 41 failure-to-notify) operative; §27.2 (Sch 24 inaccuracy) operative; §27.1 (discovery time limits) operative; §27.5 (CoP9 / CDF) for the criminal-prosecution boundary. No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — the architecture is locked across §27.1-§27.6.
