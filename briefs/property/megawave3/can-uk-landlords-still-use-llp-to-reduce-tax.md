# Can a UK Landlord Still Use an LLP to Reduce Tax? The Honest Post-2024 Picture

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (LLP statutory architecture): Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2000 ss.1-19 + SI 2008/1911 (Limited Liability Partnerships (Application of Companies Act 2006) Regulations 2009 — applies CA 2006 Parts 15 + 16 with modifications). §11.C.Z pre-locked. Sessions verify against legislation.gov.uk at write per §16.35.
- Primary (partnership tax-transparency — load-bearing for the page): ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 ss.846-863; ITA 2007 s.852; **ITTOIA 2005 s.863 (LLP treated as a partnership for income tax)**. §11.C + §11.C.6 pre-locked.
- Primary (Section 24 finance cost restriction applies AT THE MEMBER LEVEL on the member's share of property rental profit): ITTOIA 2005 s.272A + s.274A. The page MUST surface this at the entry-point. §4 pre-locked. The "LLP fixes Section 24" framing in the wider market is wrong; this page must correct it.
- Primary (salaried-member regime — gates who counts as a "real" partner): ITA 2007 ss.863A-G. Three conjunctive conditions: (A) 80% disguised salary; (B) lack of significant influence over the affairs of the partnership; (C) capital contribution < 25% of disguised salary. **All three required for reclassification.** §11.C.X pre-locked. Post-*BlueCrest* [2024] UKSC 33: Condition B "significant influence over the affairs of the partnership" does NOT require whole-LLP influence; influence over a significant part can be enough. This NARROWS the FTT/UT reading — matrix-silo'd member roles may not qualify. §11.C.X.2 pre-locked.
- Primary (mixed-membership regime — dismantles the founder-LtdCo income-splitting structure): ITA 2007 ss.850C-E. Three operative steps: (i) corporate member allocated profit share; (ii) "excess" test (corporate's share > arms-length commercial entitlement); (iii) "power to enjoy" test (individual member directly or indirectly able to benefit from corporate's allocated profits). All three required for reallocation. §11.C.Y pre-locked. **CRITICAL:** mixed-membership rules systematically dismantle the income-splitting attraction for typical founder-LtdCo setups; sessions must not write "hybrid LLPs solve Section 24" (false per §11.C.Y do-not-write).
- Primary (LLP CGT treatment): TCGA 1992 s.59 (general partnership fractional-interest framework) + s.59A (LLP-specific extension treating LLP property as held by members directly for CGT). §11.C.7 pre-locked. Sessions verify s.59A wording at write per §16.35.
- Primary (LLP SDLT-on-incorporation — putting property into LLP from individual ownership): FA 2003 Sch 15 paras 9-13 — partnership SDLT regime including para 10 sum-of-lower-proportions (SLP) relief mechanics. §1.A pre-locked. SDLT charge can be NIL where the partner contributing land is the only partner with an interest (subject to anti-avoidance windows + 3-year claw-back).
- Primary (CGT-on-incorporation of partnership): TCGA 1992 s.59 SP D12 mechanics where the partnership exists before the transaction; **note s.162 (incorporation relief) applies to going-concern business transfer to a COMPANY, NOT to an LLP** — LLP is tax-transparent and the partnership-side mechanic is not the same as a company-incorporation relief. Sessions must not collapse the two.
- Primary (settlements legislation — relevant where LLP members are family members + profit shares deviate from capital ratios): ITTOIA 2005 ss.620-628 + s.629 (minor children attribution). §22.9 + §22.10 pre-locked.
- Primary (Pawson BPR investment line — LLP property investment is INVESTMENT for IHT BPR, NOT trading): *Pawson v HMRC* [2013] UKUT 050 (TCC). §22.1 pre-locked. Sessions must not write "use an LLP for BPR" — the investment-vs-trading test sits on the activity, not the entity wrapper.
- Primary (LLP accounts + SORP + designated members): SI 2008/1911 + LLP SORP 2025 + FRS 102 Section 22 (substance test). §11.C.Z pre-locked. Audit-exemption thresholds + filing windows + ID-verification post-ECCTA framework.
- Primary (LLP-specific anti-money-laundering / Companies House reform overlay): ECCTA 2023 Part 1 + Part 3; LLP-side ID verification rollout. §11.A pre-locked. Sessions verify current commencement state at Companies House publications at write per §16.27.
- Supporting (Welsh / Scottish LLP property — devolved LBTT / LTT on LLP-side partnership transactions): §23 architecture pre-locked. Welsh LTT + Scottish LBTT have parallel partnership-SDLT-equivalent mechanics; sessions cross-reference §23 if cross-jurisdictional LLP scenarios are relevant.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **The HEADLINE TRAP — LLP wrapper does NOT escape Section 24 at the member level** — ITTOIA 2005 s.272A finance-cost restriction applies to the **individual member's share of partnership rental profit**. Partnerships are tax-transparent for income tax per ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 + s.863 (LLP-specific). The LLP does not pay income tax; the members do, on their share. A higher-rate individual member of a property-investment LLP is subject to s.272A on their share of finance costs exactly as if they held the property directly. **Common misframing in landlord forums: "set up an LLP to bypass Section 24" — wrong.** This must be the page's opening line.
2. **Scenario A (LLP still works) — family income-splitting between adult family members with genuine economic substance** — Where adult family members are all GENUINE partners contributing capital + decision-making + economic exposure, and the profit-share allocation is reasonable relative to contribution, the LLP can split rental income across multiple personal allowances + lower marginal-rate bands. Section 24 still applies on each member's share, but the splitting itself can shift income from a higher-rate band to a basic-rate band (or use up multiple personal allowances). Sessions verify the GENUINE-PARTNER requirement (Sch D Case I / Marson v Morton badges + partnership s.1 PA 1890 four-cumulative-tests per §11.C.1) — paper partnerships without economic substance fail. **Settlements legislation overlay:** where the partners are family members + profit-share deviates from capital contribution + the LLP confers a benefit not commensurate with that contribution, ITTOIA 2005 ss.620-628 can attribute income back to the original owner. §22.9 + §22.10 pre-locked.
3. **Scenario B (LLP still works) — minor capital + asset-protection / partnership-flexibility benefits, not driven by tax** — Where the LLP is structured for non-tax reasons (asset protection, succession flexibility, written partnership agreement with bespoke profit-share + capital-share + dispute-resolution + retirement mechanics, member-replacement without triggering SDLT on the underlying property), the entity choice is defensible irrespective of the tax position. Sessions characterise this honestly — "an LLP can be the right structure for reasons other than tax". The LLP cost-stack (designated members + accounts + SORP + audit threshold + ID verification post-ECCTA) is the trade-off.
4. **Scenario C (LLP still works) — adding a corporate member where the corporate's commercial entitlement is REAL and there is NO power-to-enjoy link back to individual members** — The hybrid-LLP structure (individual member + corporate member) is lawful under LLPA 2000. The mixed-membership regime at ITA 2007 ss.850C-E only fires where (i) corporate's share exceeds arms-length commercial entitlement AND (ii) individual member is directly or indirectly able to benefit from corporate's allocated profits. Where the corporate member is a genuine institutional / external / unrelated party (e.g. a PE-fund corporate, a partner-firm corporate, a charity corporate) and has no individual-member family-or-control link, the mixed-membership rules do not fire. This is the residual lawful hybrid-LLP use case per §11.C.Y.6. Sessions must NOT generalise this to founder-LtdCo + founder-individual structures — those are exactly what the mixed-membership regime catches.
5. **Scenario D (LLP still works) — SDLT-on-incorporation route into LLP using FA 2003 Sch 15 para 10 (SLP relief)** — Where a landlord has held property individually (or as a general partnership) and is restructuring into LLP form, the partnership-SDLT regime applies. Sch 15 para 10 sum-of-lower-proportions relief can reduce the SDLT charge on the transfer to NIL where the partner contributing the land is the only partner with an interest in the LLP at the transfer + during the 3-year claw-back window. **Trap:** the 3-year claw-back per Sch 15 para 17A. Bringing new partners in within 3 years can trigger retrospective SDLT. §1.A.5 pre-locked. Sessions cross-link to §1.A pages rather than re-walking SLP step-by-step.
6. **Scenario X (LLP does NOT work) — using a founder-LtdCo as corporate member to capture "excess" profits at corporation-tax rates** — This is the textbook FA-2014 mixed-membership target. Individual member's allocated profit is restricted to commercial level; "excess" goes to corporate member; individual has indirect access via shareholding in corporate. **ITA 2007 ss.850C-E fires:** excess profits are reallocated back to individual at marginal rate, with double-tax credit for corporate's CT only partially neutralising the reallocation. Outcome is generally worse than the original structure intended. §11.C.Y.3 pre-locked. **Do-not-write per §11.C.Y:** "Section 24 is solved by a hybrid LLP" + "double-tax credit makes reallocation neutral".
7. **Scenario Y (LLP does NOT work) — salaried-member rules catch what looks like a "partner" but is structurally an employee** — ITA 2007 ss.863A-G. Three conjunctive conditions (A 80%+ disguised salary; B lack of significant influence; C capital < 25%). Post-*BlueCrest* [2024] UKSC 33, Condition B narrowed — influence over a significant PART of the LLP's affairs is enough. Matrix-silo'd roles may not qualify for the safe harbour the FTT had read in. Sessions characterise this as "introducing junior family / silent partner members must clear the salaried-member gate". Practical operative safe harbour: Condition C capital-contribution route (≥25% capital takes member OUT of the regime), easier to demonstrate than Condition B significant-influence. §11.C.X.4 pre-locked. **Do-not-write per §11.C.X:** "Condition C requires 25% capital" (INVERTED — <25% IN, ≥25% OUT) + "salaried-member rules don't apply to property LLPs" (FALSE — apply to all LLP business types regardless of trading/investment status).
8. **Scenario Z (LLP does NOT work) — paper partnership without economic substance** — Where the LLP is created without genuine business activity, capital contribution, or partnership decision-making, HMRC can challenge the existence of the partnership itself per PA 1890 s.1 four-cumulative-tests + §11.C.1. The fall-back is co-ownership taxed under the underlying ownership form. PA 1890 s.2(1) negative explicitly rules out "joint property / co-ownership = partnership" — §11.C.2 pre-locked. **Critical:** s.2(1) negative is one of the most common landlord misframings; "we own property together as joint owners therefore we are a partnership" is wrong unless the four-cumulative-tests are met. Sessions surface this as a foundation gate.
9. **The cost-stack the LLP carries — SI 2008/1911 modified CA 2006 accounts + LLP SORP + designated members + ID verification + audit thresholds + SA800** — Cost-side: minimum 2 designated members with director-equivalent responsibility per §11.C.Z.6 (not honorific); SA800 partnership return mandatory per TMA 1970 s.12AA + §11.C.5; LLP SORP applies; FRS 102 Section 22 equity-vs-liability substance test for members' interests; audit exemption per applied CA 2006 s.477 + s.479; filing window 9 months per applied s.441. Post-ECCTA 2023 + LLP-side ID verification rollout per §11.A + §11.C.Z.10. Sessions characterise this honestly as a real cost not a paperwork formality.
10. **The decision framework — when does an LLP actually reduce tax?** — Sessions close with a 5-point decision framework: (a) is the partnership GENUINE (PA 1890 four-cumulative-tests met)? (b) does the partnership confer real income-splitting benefit across personal allowances + lower bands net of LLP cost-stack? (c) is there a non-tax reason that survives even if the tax case is marginal (asset protection / succession / partnership-flexibility)? (d) if the LLP includes a corporate member, is the corporate's commercial entitlement REAL and is there NO power-to-enjoy link to individuals? (e) does the structure clear the salaried-member gate for all member roles? Where any of (a)-(e) fails, the LLP is unlikely to be the right answer. Sessions return the question to a per-landlord case-by-case calculation, NOT a default recommendation either way.
