# HMRC's Nudge Letters on Business Asset Disposal Relief: A Landlord Response Guide

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — BADR introduction:** TCGA 1992 **section 169H** ("Introduction"). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/section/169H`. s.169H opens Chapter 3 of Part V — "Business Asset Disposal Relief" (renamed from "Entrepreneurs' Relief" by FA 2020 s.23 from 6 April 2020). The chapter spans ss.169H-169SA and provides a reduced rate of CGT on qualifying business disposals.
- **Primary — material-disposal gateway:** TCGA 1992 **section 169I** ("Material disposal of business assets"). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/section/169I`. s.169I(2) defines material disposals across three categories: (a) disposal of the whole or part of a business; (b) disposal of business assets after cessation; (c) disposal of shares or securities of a company. s.169I(3) imposes the **2-year qualifying period** (raised from 1 year by FA 2019 s.39 from 6 April 2019). For category (c) share disposals, the "personal company" + 5% ordinary-share-capital + 5% voting-rights test under s.169S(3) applies throughout the 2-year window.
- **Primary — rate trajectory:** TCGA 1992 s.169N (the BADR rate). **Rate trajectory verified to §5 of house_positions.md (Wave 2 lock):** 10% pre-6-April-2025; **14% from 6 April 2025 to 5 April 2026**; **18% from 6 April 2026** (aligning with the standard residential-property CGT lower-band rate). FA 2024 s.7 enacted the 14% step; FA 2025 followed with the 18% step. Sessions must verify the live rate at write time per §16.27.
- **Primary — FHL transitional protection (now-closed eligibility):** Furnished Holiday Lets fell within BADR until **5 April 2025 abolition**. Finance Act 2025 Schedule 5 Part 4 preserves BADR eligibility for qualifying disposals where the trading period predates abolition — see §5 of house_positions.md ("FHL — abolition transition" Wave 2 lock) + §25.7 (Wave 6 capital allowances FHL transitional lock). FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 5 paragraph 13 sets the CGT commencement architecture distinctly from the IT/CT commencement (1 April 2025 CT / 6 April 2025 IT under para 12). The **anti-forestalling rules** between 6 March 2024 (announcement) and 5 April 2025 (abolition) prevent artificial pre-abolition disposals.
- **Primary — investment-property exclusion:** TCGA 1992 s.169I is restricted to **business** assets (trading); pure investment property — buy-to-let portfolios, residential lettings, pure-rental SPVs — is outside the gateway. Same trade/investment line as the s.165 hold-over relief (cross-ref sibling A9 page).
- **Supporting — HMRC enquiry powers triggered by nudge letter:** TMA 1970 s.9A (enquiry into self-assessment return) + s.29 (discovery assessment if the return is out of enquiry-window); s.31A (30-day appeal window if HMRC issues an assessment); §27.1 (Discovery assessment time limits — Wave 7 lock).
- **Supporting — penalty exposure if BADR claim is found invalid:** Schedule 24 FA 2007 (inaccuracy penalties — careless 0%-30%, deliberate 20%-70%, deliberate-concealed 30%-100%); §27.2 lock. As with hold-over, the nudge-letter 60-day window operationally engages unprompted-disclosure floors if the taxpayer self-corrects.
- **House position reference:** §5 (CGT on UK residential property — BADR rate trajectory + FHL abolition framing) primary; §25.7 (FA 2025 Sch 5 FHL transitional — Wave 6 lock); §27 + §27.1 + §27.2 (HMRC enquiry + Sch 24 penalty mechanics — Wave 7 lock). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — the rate trajectory + investment-exclusion + FHL transitional architecture is already locked.

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **landlord-response guide for the HMRC nudge-letter campaign on Business Asset Disposal Relief claims**. Sibling MW3-A pick **A9 (`hmrcs-nudge-letters-demand-urgent-review-of-gift-hold-over-relief-claim`)** covers the SAME campaign-mechanic (HMRC's "One Too Many" / nudge-letter framework) on the **hold-over relief** regime (TCGA 1992 s.165 + s.260). The two pages MUST be clearly differentiated at Stage 2 — same nudge-letter framing, different underlying relief mechanic, different operational failure points.

Differentiation pillars for this page (vs A9):

- **Statutory regime:** TCGA 1992 ss.169H-169SA (BADR) is a CGT-rate reduction on qualifying business disposals; s.165 + s.260 (hold-over) is a CGT-deferral mechanic on gifts. Entirely separate Chapters of Part V of TCGA 1992.
- **Property-cohort relevance:** BADR is almost never available to landlords (investment, not trading) — making the page's primary frame the **surprise-disqualification angle** for FHL operators post-5 April 2025 abolition and for LtdCo property directors who fail the "trading company" test under s.165A.
- **The campaign-mechanic backstory:** HMRC's BADR nudge-letter campaigns have targeted: (a) former-FHL operators who disposed in 2024/25 or 2025/26 and claimed BADR without confirming FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 4 transitional eligibility; (b) LtdCo property-portfolio directors who claimed BADR on share disposal despite the company being investment not trading; (c) mixed-use property disposals where the trading element was not properly apportioned; (d) the s.169I 2-year qualifying-period failure (especially for short-hold FHL operators); (e) the s.169S 5%-economic-interest-throughout test failure where shareholdings have diluted during the qualifying period.
- **The rate-trajectory dimension (10% → 14% → 18%):** A9's hold-over relief is a deferral mechanic with no rate-component; this page's BADR has a moving rate that makes the **monetary stake of a wrongly-claimed BADR larger over time**. The 18%-from-6-April-2026 step aligns BADR with the standard residential lower-band CGT rate, which means BADR is now offering no rate advantage at the residential rate — sessions must flag this rate-convergence as a planning-decision input.

The angle this page takes: a landlord, former-FHL operator, or LtdCo property director who has received an HMRC nudge letter on their BADR claim needs the **statutory framework + the operational response sequence** — the 60-day window, the FA 2025 Sch 5 Part 4 transitional check for FHL claims, the s.169I 2-year + s.169S 5%-throughout audit for LtdCo claims, the in-place amend-vs-leave-and-defend decision, and the §27.5 CoP9 boundary if HMRC's letter signals deliberate-behaviour suspicion.
