# Are you leaving the UK permanently? The landlord's checklist for a clean break vs the s.10A 5-year trap

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (residence determination):**
  - **FA 2013 Schedule 45** — Statutory Residence Test (SRT). Part 1 sets out the rules; Part 3 contains split-year Cases 1-8 (Cases 1-3 leaving; Cases 4-8 arriving). Verified against legislation.gov.uk at write time.
- **Primary (temporary non-residence — the trap):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.10A** — temporary non-resident rule. Per HP §17.3 verified against HMRC CG26540 on 2026-05-22: individual is "temporary non-resident" where their period of non-UK residence is 5 years or less, having previously been UK-resident in 4 or more of the 7 tax years before departure; gains realised during non-residence are deemed to arise in the year of return.
  - **ITA 2007 s.812** — income-tax parallel for dividend / pension lump-sum income during temporary non-residence.
- **Primary (UK property income for non-residents):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Part 3** + **CTA 2009 Part 4** — UK property income continues to be UK-taxable under domestic charge.
  - **FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902** — NRL scheme. Tenants / letting agents withhold 20% unless NRL1 / NRL2 / NRL3 approval held.
- **Primary (CGT on UK land for non-residents):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** — NRCGT (FA 2019 rewrite). 60-day return required for every UK land disposal by non-residents, including indirect disposals of property-rich entity shares, regardless of whether tax is due.
- **Primary (new arrival / FIG regime — for the inverse direction):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Chapter 5 ss.845A-845J** inserted by **FA 2025 s.37** — Foreign Income and Gains relief. Effective from tax year 2025-26 ("The amendments made by this section have effect for the tax year 2025-26 and subsequent tax years"). 4-tax-year window; 10-of-prior-10-years non-UK-residence gateway under s.845B(1); per-year claim required.
- **Primary (transitional for departing non-doms — TRF + rebasing):**
  - **FA 2025 s.41 + Sch 10** — Temporary Repatriation Facility (12% / 12% / 15% across 2025-26 / 2026-27 / 2027-28). Window closes 5 April 2028.
  - **FA 2025 s.42 + Sch 11** — CGT rebasing election to 5 April 2017 value (narrow eligibility — five conditions per Sch 11 para 1(1); does NOT cover UK situs property, only non-UK assets per condition 3).
- **Primary (IHT residence-based regime):**
  - **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C + ss.267ZC-267ZF** (FA 2025 architecture) — Long-Term Resident (LTR) test: 10-of-20 tax years. Tail-period table per HP §22.X.2 governs IHT exposure after departure.
- **House position reference:** **§17 in full (Wave 2 expat extension)**, §17.1 (SRT), §17.2 (split-year), §17.3 (s.10A 5-year temporary-non-residence trap), §17.4 (NRCGT), §17.5 (NRL operational), §17.6 (domicile reform + FIG / TRF headline), **§17.8 (FIG operational depth — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, **§17.9 (TRF operational depth — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, **§17.10 (CGT rebasing election narrow eligibility — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, §22.X (IHT LTR operational depth — Wave 8 mini-lock), §10 + §16 (DTA framing for the destination-country tie-breaker context).

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What does "leaving the UK permanently" actually mean for tax purposes? (Per §17.3: more than 5 complete tax years of non-UK-residence is the operative line for shedding the s.10A recapture risk; SRT determines residence year-by-year, no single test for "permanent intent".)
2. How does the SRT apply in the departure year? (FA 2013 Sch 45 cascade: automatic overseas tests, then automatic UK tests, then sufficient ties. UK-day count, work pattern, and ties matter.)
3. What is the split-year treatment? (FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 3 Cases 1-3 for leaving: Case 1 starting full-time overseas work; Case 2 partner of Case-1 individual; Case 3 ceasing to have any UK home. Split-year is NOT optional; statute determines application and priority.)
4. **The s.10A temporary-non-residence trap.** Why does selling a non-UK asset during non-residence not give CGT escape if return is within 5 years? (Gain deemed to arise in the year of return; ITA 2007 s.812 parallel for certain income.)
5. Does s.10A catch gains on UK property sold during non-residence? (UK land is already in NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A; s.10A primarily matters for non-UK situs assets and for any UK base-cost issues that pre-NRCGT gains carry forward.)
6. What is the NRL scheme position for a landlord keeping UK rentals after the move? (20% withholding default unless NRL1 / NRL2 / NRL3 approval; treaty residence does not displace NRL — see §16.6.)
7. What about the 60-day NRCGT return? (Required for every UK land disposal by a non-resident, including indirect disposals of property-rich entity shares, regardless of whether tax is due — see §17.4 HP language verbatim.)
8. **The new IHT long-term-residence test.** How does IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C apply post-departure? (10-of-20 tax years test; tail period per §22.X.2 — IHT exposure can persist for up to 10 years after departure depending on prior UK-residence years.)
9. What is the inverse FIG window for someone who LEFT and now RETURNS? (Per HP §17.8: 10-of-prior-10-years non-UK-residence gateway under ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1); 4-year FIG window with per-year claim; loss of personal allowance + CGT AEA in each claim year.)
10. What about the TRF for someone who claimed remittance basis pre-2025-26? (Per HP §17.9: 12% / 12% / 15% across 2025-26 / 2026-27 / 2027-28; deadline per Sch 10 para 8(1) is 31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year.)
11. What about the CGT rebasing election? (Per HP §17.10: narrow eligibility — five conditions including the asset NOT being UK situs between 6 March 2024 and 5 April 2025 (condition 3). UK property is NOT eligible for rebasing under FA 2025 Sch 11; UK land follows its own NRCGT rebasing dates per §17.4.)
12. What is the destination-country DTA position? (Cross-references to specific-DTA sibling pages: B1 UK-IoM, B2 UK-Spain, B3 UK-India; Art 4 tie-breaker may apply where dual-resident; Art 6 + Art 13 allocate UK source-state taxing rights regardless.)

### Example 1 — The permanently-vs-temporarily decision tree (the page's first concrete artefact)

Render as semantic HTML `<ol>` — this is the strategic anchor a landlord needs in the first conversation about emigration:

1. **Identify your departure year.** When does your UK residence year-by-year actually break? SRT determines this — not your intent. Use FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 1 cascade: automatic overseas tests → automatic UK tests → sufficient ties.
2. **Check the split-year cases (Cases 1-3 for leaving).** Case 1 (starting full-time overseas work); Case 2 (partner of Case-1 individual); Case 3 (ceasing to have any UK home). Split-year is NOT optional — statute determines application + priority per Sch 45 Part 3.
3. **Decide your minimum non-UK-resident period.** To shed the s.10A 5-year recapture trap, you need MORE than 5 complete tax years non-UK-resident (and you had to have been UK-resident in 4+ of the 7 preceding tax years to engage s.10A at all per §17.3). If you cannot commit to 5+ complete non-UK tax years, you are at risk of return-year recapture on gains realised during non-residence.
4. **Inventory your UK property holdings.** Each UK rental property continues to be UK-taxable on rental income (ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 / CTA 2009 Part 4 — domestic charge survives any treaty allocation). Each UK property disposal triggers NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A + 60-day return per §17.4 — regardless of whether tax is due.
5. **Apply for NRL1 / NRL2 / NRL3 approval before the move.** Without approval, your letting agent / tenant must withhold 20% per §17.5; with approval you receive rent gross + self-assess.
6. **Check your IHT LTR position per IHTA 1984 s.6A + tail-period table per §22.X.2.** If you've been UK-resident in 10 of preceding 20 tax years you are LTR. On departure you remain LTR (worldwide-asset IHT exposure) for a tail period scaling 3-10 years depending on your prior-UK-resident-year count. UK property is in UK IHT regardless of LTR status (UK situs).
7. **If you previously claimed remittance basis, consider TRF designation per §17.9 + FA 2025 Sch 10.** Rates 12% / 12% / 15% across 2025-26 / 2026-27 / 2027-28; designation deadline 31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year; window closes 5 April 2028.
8. **Identify your destination-country DTA position.** Specific-DTA cross-link: UK-IoM (B1), UK-Spain (B2), UK-India (B3), or the country-specific page if it exists. Art 4 tie-breaker may apply where dual-resident; Art 6 + Art 13 allocate UK source-state rights on UK property regardless.
9. **Engage specialist representation before the move.** Pre-departure planning + tax-year-end disposals + post-departure income/gain timing matters operationally.

### Example 2 — Mrs Carter, UK landlord moving to Portugal permanently (the most common scenario)

Mrs Carter (UK-resident throughout 2018/19 to 2025/26 — 8 of preceding 20 tax years; UK-born + UK domiciled-of-origin; sells her UK consulting business in 2025 + moves to a Portuguese coastal town in May 2026 intending to settle for life; retains a London BTL flat + a Manchester BTL flat through the move). Position:
- **SRT in 2026/27 departure year:** depending on Portugal arrival date + UK day-count + workdays + ties, likely splits — Case 3 (ceasing to have any UK home) applies if she sells her London home before moving (her primary UK home goes); otherwise Case 1 if she starts full-time overseas work in Portugal. Either way, split-year treatment for 2026/27 treats her as UK-resident only for the pre-departure part-year.
- **s.10A position:** she was UK-resident in 4+ of the 7 preceding tax years (well above the gateway). To shed s.10A she needs more than 5 complete non-UK tax years (so 2027/28 + 2028/29 + 2029/30 + 2030/31 + 2031/32 — 5 complete — would still be inside the trap; she needs 2032/33 too — 6 complete non-UK tax years — to be safe).
- **NRL on continuing UK rentals:** she applies for NRL1 before the May 2026 move; HMRC typically approves within 6 weeks. Her letting agents pay her gross from approval date; she self-assesses UK rental income each year via SA100 + SA105 with NRL6 evidence on file.
- **NRCGT on continuing UK property:** if she sells either UK BTL during non-residence, the 60-day NRCGT return is required regardless of whether tax is due. Residential rates 18% / 24% from 30 October 2024 per §17.4; AEA £3,000 (preserved for individuals retaining UK personal allowance per ITA 2007 s.56 — UK national).
- **IHT LTR per §22.X.2:** Mrs Carter has been UK-resident for 8 of preceding 20 years — **BELOW the 10-of-20 LTR threshold** per IHTA 1984 s.6A. She is NOT LTR. On departure, she is immediately outside UK IHT for worldwide assets. UK property remains in UK IHT (UK situs per §22.X.4). For someone with lower prior-UK-resident-year count, the LTR position is simpler.
- **Pre-FA-2025 contrast:** under historic deemed-domicile, Mrs Carter would have shed UK-domicile (UK-domicile-of-origin) only through acquiring a Portuguese domicile of choice — high common-law evidential bar (intent + physical move + cutting UK ties). Post-FA-2025 LTR is residence-based per §22.X — domicile irrelevant; the LTR 10-of-20 test resolves cleanly.
- **Destination DTA:** UK-Portugal Convention 1968 (verify currency at write — older treaty; check whether MLI-modified). Art 6 allocates UK source-state rights on UK property income; Art 13 on UK property gains; Art 4 tie-breaker available where dual-resident.
- **Practical clean-break checklist:** (i) NRL1 application 6 weeks pre-move; (ii) inform HMRC of leaving via P85; (iii) close UK home before / immediately on move (Case 3 split-year trigger); (iv) plan any post-departure non-UK asset disposals to AVOID falling within the 5-year s.10A window (defer until tax year 2032/33+); (v) Portuguese-side tax registration + planning for FTC mechanics on UK rental.

### Example 3 — The s.10A trap: Mr Ellis returns to UK after 4 years in Australia

Mr Ellis (UK-resident throughout 2018/19 to 2025/26; satisfies s.10A "4-of-7 preceding" gateway). Moves to Sydney in June 2026 for a 5-year corporate role intending eventually to settle in Australia. In 2028/29, while Australian-resident, he sells a portfolio of pre-departure-acquired Australian shares for a £210,000 gain. Plans changed: he and his family return to UK in May 2030 (after 4 complete non-UK tax years: 2026/27, 2027/28, 2028/29, 2029/30).
- **s.10A position per §17.3:** Mr Ellis's non-UK residence period is 4 complete tax years — within the "5 years or less" window. He had been UK-resident in 4+ of the 7 preceding tax years. He is a "temporary non-resident".
- **Effect:** the £210,000 gain on Australian shares is deemed to arise in his 2030/31 return year + chargeable to UK CGT in that year. At standard CGT rates (10% / 20% non-residential for individuals; verify post-30-October-2024 rates at write per §17.4 — the 18%/24% post-October-2024 reform was residential-only initially; non-residential rates aligned subsequently — verify current per gov.uk at write).
- **UK property gain — separate analysis:** if Mr Ellis sold a UK BTL flat during his non-residence, NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A applied at the time of disposal (60-day return + 18%/24% residential). s.10A does NOT overlay UK land (already in NRCGT). s.10A primarily catches non-UK situs assets + any pre-2015/2019 UK base-cost issues per §17.4.
- **Australian-side credit:** Australia taxed him on the £210,000 gain in 2028/29 (Australian-resident at time of disposal). UK CGT on the same gain in 2030/31 is creditable against Australian CGT already paid — but only the LOWER of the two amounts is the operative credit + Australian-side procedural mechanic for retrospective credit may be cumbersome; specialist Australian + UK advice needed.
- **Planning lesson:** to break s.10A, Mr Ellis needed MORE than 5 complete non-UK tax years. A 4-year stint with planned return crystallises the recapture risk on non-UK gains realised during non-residence. The planning rule: if you are uncertain about staying away for 5+ complete tax years, defer non-UK asset disposals until you have committed to the 6+ year minimum OR realise them BEFORE departure to claim UK CGT at exit rates (with PRR / AEA / loss-offset planning).
- **Common misframing:** "I sold the Australian shares while Australian-resident so Australia tax applies and UK has no claim." FALSE — UK has dormant claim under s.10A that crystallises on return.

### Example 5 — The TRF for departing non-doms (per §17.9)

Sra Marquez (Spanish citizen; UK-resident 2016/17 to 2025/26 — claimed remittance basis annually until 6 April 2025; accumulated £620k of unremitted Spanish rental + Spanish dividend income across that period). She moves back to Madrid permanently in October 2026 (becomes Spanish-resident from 2026/27 — split-year treatment may apply to the 2026/27 departure year). Position:
- **Pre-FA-2025 remittance basis:** the £620k unremitted foreign income / gains sat in her offshore accounts indefinitely under the historic remittance regime — taxable only on actual remittance to UK.
- **Post-FA-2025 architecture per §17.6:** remittance basis abolished from 6 April 2025; FIG regime substituted for new arrivals (not applicable to her — she's been UK-resident >10 of preceding 10 years).
- **TRF designation per §17.9:** Sra Marquez can elect to designate qualifying overseas capital (Scenarios A, B, or C per FA 2025 Sch 10 para 2) at the TRF rate. Her £620k pre-2025 unremitted accumulation falls under Scenario A. Designation rates: 12% in 2025-26, 12% in 2026-27, 15% in 2027-28.
- **Designation timing:** she designates £620k of pre-2025 unremitted capital in 2026-27 → 12% × £620k = £74,400 UK tax. Designation deadline is 31 January 2029 per Sch 10 para 8(1) (12 months after 31 January 2028 self-assessment deadline for 2026-27).
- **Effect:** once designated + paid, the £620k becomes "clean capital" for UK purposes — Sra Marquez can remit (or already-remitted post-FA-2025 amounts crystallise at the rate) without further UK tax. The £74,400 is the operative cost.
- **If she had waited until 2027-28:** rate steps up to 15% → £93,000 (the difference £18,600 is the cost of delay).
- **If she had waited until 2028-29 or later:** TRF window closes 5 April 2028 — no further designations possible; the £620k falls back to standard remittance taxation on later remittance (potentially at higher effective rates if Sra Marquez has accumulated additional income / gains by then).
- **No-residence-requirement nuance:** TRF eligibility per §17.9 is for individuals with pre-2025-26 unremitted foreign income / gains from a period when they claimed remittance basis. Departure from UK does NOT preclude eligibility — the TRF is the cleanup mechanism for ANY previous remittance-basis cohort regardless of subsequent residence. Sra Marquez's move to Madrid does not affect her TRF rights.

### Example 6 — The CGT rebasing election: why most departing UK landlords don't qualify (per §17.10)

Mr Patel (UK-domiciled-of-origin; born in Mumbai; UK-resident 2014/15-2025/26; claimed remittance basis under ITA 2007 s.809B for 4 of those tax years up to and including 2023/24; moves back to Mumbai in October 2026). He has heard about the "rebasing election to 5 April 2017" and assumes his UK BTL portfolio + Indian shares are eligible. WRONG — the eligibility is narrow.
- **FA 2025 Sch 11 para 1(1) five conditions per §17.10:**
  - **(1) Asset held on 5 April 2017** — Mr Patel's London BTL purchased 2020 → FAILS condition 1 for that asset; passes for his Indian-share portfolio if held since 2017.
  - **(2) Disposal on or after 6 April 2025** — passes (he's planning post-2026 disposals).
  - **(3) Asset NOT situated in UK between 6 March 2024 and 5 April 2025** — Mr Patel's London BTL is UK situs throughout → **FAILS condition 3 absolutely. UK property cannot be rebased under FA 2025 Sch 11.** Indian shares pass (non-UK situs).
  - **(4) Individual not domiciled in UK before tax year 2025-26** — Mr Patel was UK-domiciled-of-origin → potentially FAILS depending on whether he acquired Indian domicile-of-choice; specialist domicile analysis needed (high evidential bar pre-FA-2025).
  - **(5) Made claim under ITA 2007 s.809B at least once in tax years 2017-18 to 2024-25** — Mr Patel claimed remittance basis in 4 tax years up to 2023/24 → passes condition 5 IF the claims fell within 2017-18 to 2024-25 (verify at write).
- **Conclusion:** UK BTL CANNOT be rebased under FA 2025 Sch 11 (condition 3 absolute exclusion). Indian shares MAY be eligible if all 5 conditions met — specialist verification essential.
- **UK property rebasing per §17.4:** UK land follows its OWN NRCGT rebasing dates — 5 April 2015 default for residential acquired before that date; 5 April 2019 for non-residential. Mr Patel's BTL acquired 2020 → no rebasing benefit (acquired post-default-dates; cost is acquisition cost).
- **Election mechanics (Sch 11 para 3):** per-disposal; TMA 1970 ss.42-43 procedural rules; **election is irrevocable once made**. For qualifying assets, default IS rebasing; opt-out election available per para 3 (rarely useful — opt-out chosen where the historic base cost is HIGHER than the 5 April 2017 value).

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: I'm planning to leave the UK permanently — what is the most important single thing to understand about tax?**
   A: The "permanently" vs "temporarily" distinction matters operationally. Per TCGA 1992 s.10A and §17.3, if your period of non-UK residence is **5 years or less** AND you were UK-resident in 4 or more of the 7 tax years before departure, you are a "temporary non-resident". Any gain you realise on assets owned at departure (and disposed during your non-residence) is deemed to arise in your year of return and is chargeable to UK CGT then. To shed this trap, you need MORE than 5 complete tax years non-UK-resident. UK property gains during non-residence are caught by NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A independently regardless of return.

2. **Q: How does the Statutory Residence Test apply in my departure year?**
   A: FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 1 cascade: (i) automatic overseas tests — any one met → non-UK resident (under 16 UK days if UK-resident in any of preceding 3 years; under 46 UK days if not; full-time overseas work meeting the 35-hour test); (ii) automatic UK tests — any one met → UK-resident (183+ UK days; only home in UK for at least 91 consecutive days; full-time UK work); (iii) sufficient ties test — combine UK ties (family, accommodation, work, 90-day, country) with UK day count per HMRC RDR3 Annex A. "UK day" = present in UK at midnight (with deeming for transit and exceptional circumstances).

3. **Q: What is split-year treatment and is it optional?**
   A: Where SRT makes you UK-resident for the whole departure year but you genuinely departed mid-year, FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 3 Cases 1-3 (for leaving) may apply: Case 1 (starting full-time overseas work); Case 2 (partner of a Case-1 individual); Case 3 (ceasing to have any UK home). Split-year treatment is NOT optional — the cases either apply on the facts or they do not. Where multiple cases could apply, statute determines priority per paras 53-55.

4. **Q: I'm keeping my UK BTL flat after the move — what's my position on rental income tax and withholding?**
   A: UK rental income remains UK-taxable under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (individuals) / CTA 2009 Part 4 (companies) regardless of your residence. Apply for NRL1 (landlord-side gross-payment approval) BEFORE you move — HMRC typically approves within six weeks. Without NRL1 / NRL2 / NRL3 approval, your letting agent must withhold 20% of rent + account quarterly to HMRC under FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902. The NRL scheme is statutory; treaty residence does not displace it.

5. **Q: When I sell my UK BTL flat as a non-resident, what's the procedure?**
   A: NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A applies. 60-day return required FROM COMPLETION DATE for every UK land disposal — REGARDLESS of whether tax is due. Residential rates 18% basic / 24% higher-band from 30 October 2024. AEA £3,000 for individuals (preserved if you retain UK personal allowance under ITA 2007 s.56). Indirect disposals of shares in UK property-rich entities (75%+ UK land value; 25%+ holder stake) are also caught under Sch 1A Part 4.

6. **Q: I've been UK-resident for 15 of the previous 20 tax years — how does the new IHT residence-based regime catch me on departure?**
   A: Per IHTA 1984 s.6A inserted by FA 2025 (see §22.X), you are a Long-Term UK Resident (LTR) because you were UK-resident in at least 10 of the preceding 20 tax years. On departure, you remain LTR (worldwide-asset IHT exposure) for a tail period determined by your prior-UK-resident-year count per the §22.X.2 table — for 15 prior UK-resident years, the required consecutive non-UK tax years to lose LTR is **5**. So your worldwide assets remain in UK IHT for 5 complete non-UK tax years post-departure. UK situs property is in UK IHT regardless of LTR status per §22.X.4 Sch A1. Pre-FA-2025 "3-year tail" advice anchored on s.267(1)(b) deemed-domicile is obsolete for anyone with 14+ prior UK-resident years.

7. **Q: I left UK in 2020 + want to return now — can I claim the FIG regime as a "new arrival"?**
   A: Possibly, but the gateway is strict. Per ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1) and §17.8: you must (a) be UK-resident in the current year; (b) NOT have been UK-resident in any of the 10 preceding tax years; (c) not be disqualified; (d) be over age 10. If you left UK in 2020 and have been non-UK-resident every year since 2020/21, by 2030/31 you would have 10 complete tax years non-UK-resident and could claim FIG for 2030/31 onwards (4-year window with per-year claim). Claiming costs your UK personal allowance + dividend allowance + CGT AEA for each claim year. Claim deadline: 31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year.

8. **Q: I claimed remittance basis pre-2025 + have accumulated unremitted foreign income — should I use TRF before the window closes?**
   A: Probably yes if you have significant unremitted accumulation. Per §17.9 + FA 2025 Sch 10, TRF designation rates are 12% (2025-26), 12% (2026-27), **15% (2027-28)**; window closes 5 April 2028. Designation crystallises the amount as clean capital for UK purposes — no further UK tax on later remittance. Designation deadline per Sch 10 para 8(1) is 31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year. Designating in 2026-27 at 12% rather than 2027-28 at 15% on (say) £500k saves £15k. Specialist advice essential — TRF interacts with other transitional provisions.

9. **Q: Can I rebase my UK BTL portfolio to 5 April 2017 value under the FA 2025 election?**
   A: NO. Per §17.10, FA 2025 Sch 11 para 1(1) condition 3 EXCLUDES assets that were situated in UK between 6 March 2024 and 5 April 2025. UK property is UK situs throughout. UK land follows its OWN rebasing dates under the NRCGT regime per §17.4 (5 April 2015 for residential acquired before that date; 5 April 2019 for non-residential acquired before then). The FA 2025 Sch 11 rebasing benefits non-UK assets only, for individuals who actively claimed remittance basis under ITA 2007 s.809B in at least one tax year 2017-18 to 2024-25, AND meet conditions 1, 2, and 4. Narrow combination.

10. **Q: How does the destination country's DTA interact with my UK property?**
    A: For most modern UK DTAs in OECD form: Art 6 allocates UK source-state taxing rights on UK property income; Art 13(1) allocates UK source-state rights on UK property gains (Art 13(4) extends to indirect disposals for modern treaties — verify your specific destination treaty); Art 4 tie-breaker resolves dual-residence cases via permanent home, centre of vital interests, habitual abode, nationality, mutual agreement. Specific-DTA cross-links: UK-IoM (B1), UK-Spain (B2), UK-India (B3 — older treaty without Art 13(4) — but UK NRCGT still applies regardless via statute). The destination state typically gives FTC for UK tax paid under its credit mechanism.

11. **Q: I had a 12-month UK secondment overseas + returned in year 2 — am I within s.10A?**
    A: Probably not if you remained UK-resident throughout. s.10A bites where you BECOME non-UK-resident (typically by leaving UK + breaking SRT residence for at least one complete tax year). A short overseas secondment that does NOT break UK residence (still meets sufficient-ties test; still UK-resident) does not engage s.10A at all. If your secondment lasted long enough to break UK residence (e.g. full-time overseas work satisfying automatic-overseas-test), s.10A engages if you then return within 5 years. See B19 `do-i-have-to-pay-uk-tax` for the short-term-departure variant.

12. **Q: What is the difference between this page and the 12-month pre-departure checklist?**
    A: The 12-month pre-departure checklist (`leaving-uk-landlord-12-month-pre-departure-checklist`) is the OPERATIONAL checklist — month-by-month action items in the lead-up to the move. This page is the STRATEGIC decision-frame — permanently vs temporarily, the s.10A 5-year line, the IHT LTR tail-period mechanic, the FIG / TRF / rebasing decision-tree for non-doms, the destination DTA framing. Use them together: this page for the strategic understanding; the checklist for the operational sequence.

13. **Q: What about the residual non-tax operational steps when I leave?**
    A: Tax aside: notify HMRC via P85 form within reasonable time; consider whether your UK address is your registered office for any UK ltd-cos you control (may need to change); update PSC + director records at Companies House per ECCTA 2023 ID-verification regime (§11.A); review UK bank account residency status; pension treatment + the new IHT-on-pension-pots reform (operative from 6 April 2027 — verify currency at write). For company-owner emigrants with UK property in a ltd-co structure, the cross-link B17 `changes-nrl-companies` covers the company-side angle.
