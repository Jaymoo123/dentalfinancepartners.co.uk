# CIS Templates for Property Businesses — The Five Forms Every Contractor Needs, Anchored to the Statute That Governs Each

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (statutory framework for all CIS templates): Finance Act 2004 Part 3 ss.57-77 — operative core of the Construction Industry Scheme. §33 pre-locked at MW2 Stage 1b sign-off 2026-05-27.
- Primary (monthly contractor return — CIS300): FA 2004 s.70 (Periodic returns by contractors etc.) — the operative statutory hook delegating return + records + nil-return + subcontractor-information mechanics to regulations. **Verified verbatim at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2004/12/section/70 on 2026-05-27**: "(1) The Board of Inland Revenue may make regulations requiring persons who make payments under construction contracts (a) to make to the Board, at such times and in respect of such periods as may be prescribed, returns relating to such payments; (b) to keep such records as may be prescribed relating to such payments; (c) to provide such information as may be prescribed, at such times as may be prescribed, to persons to whom such payments are made or to such of those persons are of a prescribed description." Nil-return obligation: "(d) a return to be made where no payments have been made in the period to which the return relates." Verification declaration + employment-status declaration also flow from s.70. §33.4 pre-locked.
- Primary (CIS300 monthly return — operational implementation): SI 2005/2045 reg 4(1) — "A return must be made to the Commissioners for Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs in a document or format provided or approved by the Commissioners". Reg 4(2) sets the deadline at "not later than 14 days after the end of every tax month". Required contents include: contractor's name + UTR + Accounts Office reference + tax month + for each subcontractor (name + NINO or company registration number + payment + materials cost + tax deducted + verification reference where unregistered). **Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2005/2045/regulation/4 on 2026-05-27** — original version, no operative amendments displayed. Stage 2 sub-agent verifies current consolidated SI text at write per §16.35.
- Primary (Payment and Deduction Statement — PDS to subcontractor): **SI 2005/2045 reg 4(8)** — written statement must be given by the contractor to every registered-for-deduction or unregistered subcontractor "not later than 14 days after the end of the tax month" with the prescribed contents: contractor's name + contractor's employer's reference + tax month or payment date + subcontractor's name + subcontractor's UTR + "the total amount of contract payments made by the contractor to the sub-contractor" + the materials cost amount the contractor is satisfied represents the direct cost of materials to a person other than the contractor + total amount deducted under FA 2004 s.61 of the Act + (for unregistered subcontractors) the verification reference for higher-rate deduction. **Verified at https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2005/2045/regulation/4 on 2026-05-27** — reg 4(8) contents list confirmed. Reg 4(9) permits electronic delivery where the subcontractor has consented and the format permits paper printing. §33.4 cross-reference + new operative anchor at write-time-verified level.
- Primary (subcontractor verification — pre-payment template requirement): FA 2004 s.69 + SI 2005/2045 reg 6 — contractor obtains subcontractor identifiers from HMRC + HMRC returns one of three verification statuses (gross-payment / registered-for-deduction / unregistered) + verification reference typically valid 2 tax years for that contractor-subcontractor relationship. §33.2 pre-locked.
- Primary (deduction-base mechanic — drives the subcontractor invoice template requirement): FA 2004 s.61(2) + SI 2005/2045 reg 6 — materials portion is EXCLUDED from the deduction base. §33.3 pre-locked. **The s.61(2) materials exclusion is the operative reason a labour/materials-split subcontractor invoice template is non-negotiable** — without the split on the invoice, the contractor defaults to treating the whole payment as labour (per HMRC operational guidance), over-deducts, and creates a CT / IT reconciliation problem for the subcontractor at year-end.
- Primary (penalties — non-compliance with the template mechanics): FA 2009 Sch 55 (late filing of CIS300 + nil-returns); FA 2009 Sch 56 (late payment of deducted amounts); FA 2007 Sch 24 (inaccuracy penalties — apply to under-deduction where contractor pays without verifying or treats materials wrong). §27.2 + §27.3 pre-locked.
- Primary (employment-status template anchor — for the on-boarding checklist): *Ready Mixed Concrete (South East) Ltd v Minister of Pensions and National Insurance* [1968] 2 QB 497 — three-element test (mutuality of obligation + control + integration / consistency with employment). §35.2 pre-locked. **CIS-registered status does NOT establish self-employment for employment-status purposes** — the on-boarding template must include a status-test review step, NOT just a CIS-registration check, because reclassification exposure is the load-bearing risk for long-running engagements on a single development site.
- Primary (interaction with VAT domestic reverse charge — drives an additional invoice-template requirement for VAT-registered counterparties): SI 1995/2518 + the domestic reverse charge SI for construction services in force from 1 March 2021 (SI 2019/892 — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies current SI reference at write per §16.35). **The invoice template must indicate reverse-charge applicability where both parties are VAT-registered and the customer is not an end-user** — the customer accounts for VAT on the service; the supplier shows the net plus the reverse-charge statement. §33.5 pre-locked + §29 cross-reference.
- Primary (CIS Manual operative anchor for template practice): HMRC CIS Manual (CISR) — particularly CISR40000+ (contractor verification + monthly returns) + CISR45000+ (PDS practice + materials-line guidance) + CISR60000+ (gross-payment status). Stage 2 sub-agent verifies current CISR text at write per §16.35.
- House position reference: §33 (CIS architecture — full lock); §33.1 (in-scope categorisation — determines WHICH property businesses need the templates); §33.2 (verification process — drives the verification template); §33.3 (withholding rates + labour-only deduction base — drives the invoice template); §33.4 (returns + penalties — drives the CIS300 + PDS templates); §33.5 (VAT domestic reverse charge sibling — drives the reverse-charge-aware invoice template); §29 (VAT for property — cross-reference); §35.2 (Ready Mixed Concrete employment-vs-self-employment — drives the on-boarding status-test template); §27.2 + §27.3 (penalties framework — drives the operational compliance checklist). **No new HP lock needed** — §33 cluster fully pre-locked at MW2 Stage 1b sign-off 2026-05-27. 4. **Template 3 — Subcontractor verification checklist (FA 2004 s.69 + SI 2005/2045 reg 6)** — Sessions walk the pre-payment verification template: (a) subcontractor identifiers required (individuals — UTR + name + NINO; companies — UTR + company registration number; partnerships — partnership UTR + each partner UTR); (b) contractor logs into HMRC CIS Online or uses CIS-compliant software to submit verification; (c) HMRC system returns one of three statuses + verification reference; (d) verification reference typically valid 2 tax years for that contractor-subcontractor relationship per §33.2; (e) contractor applies the correct withholding rate at the next payment + records the verification reference on the contractor's subcontractor register + (for unregistered subcontractors) populates the verification reference field on the next CIS300 + PDS. **Critical re-verification trigger:** when the 2-tax-year window expires, re-verification is required; absent re-verification, the contractor risks under-deduction exposure under FA 2007 Sch 24 + §27.2. Sessions cross-link to MW2 B4 `beginners-guide-to-cis-verification-in-the-uk` for the verification process deep-dive.

Sessions cross-link to existing site `domestic-reverse-charge-construction-vat-landlords` for the VAT-side reverse-charge sibling regime.
6. **Template 5 — Subcontractor on-boarding compliance checklist (Ready Mixed Concrete 1968 status-test + gross-payment-status tracker + MLR ID verification overlay)** — Sessions walk the on-boarding checklist: (a) **employment-status review per *Ready Mixed Concrete* three-element test** (mutuality of obligation + control + integration / consistency with employment) — CIS-registered status does NOT establish self-employment for employment-status purposes per §35.2; long-running engagements on a single development site, working exclusively for the contractor, integrated into contractor's workflow, with significant control over working pattern = HIGH reclassification risk = consider CEST review + Status Determination Statement workflow; (b) gross-payment-status check at verification + ongoing tracker for s.66 cancellation risk; (c) MLR ID verification (Money Laundering Regulations 2017 — beneficial-ownership + sanctions screening for subcontractors above threshold); (d) ECCTA / Companies House ID verification overlay where the subcontractor is a UK company per §11.A (ID verification post-ECCTA 2023 commencement state — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies current commencement at write); (e) insurance certificate check (employers' / public liability appropriate to construction operations); (f) right-to-work check where the subcontractor is an individual. Sessions surface this as the operational floor for portfolio contractors with frequent subcontractor on-boarding — the on-boarding template is the protective layer against status, MLR, and ID-verification exposure that aggregates across a developer / REIT / large-landlord-group payroll over a year.
7. **The format question — paper / spreadsheet / CIS-compliant software / HMRC CIS Online — what does compliance require?** — Sessions walk the operational format choice: (a) **HMRC CIS Online** is the baseline route — free, web-based, manual data entry, suitable for small / occasional contractors; (b) **CIS-compliant payroll software** (Sage / Xero / IRIS / specialist CIS software) is the operational floor for portfolio contractors — handles verification + CIS300 + PDS generation + reconciliation; (c) **bespoke ERP / CMS** for very large operators (REITs / large developers) — must be CIS-compliant and tested against HMRC's CIS API. The page does NOT recommend specific software products (that is sales territory and creates affiliate / endorsement risk) — it names the operational format question and the gross-shape categories. 8. **The reconciliation discipline — PDS-to-CIS300-to-payment trail (the audit trail HMRC will follow if the contractor is selected for review)** — Sessions surface the reconciliation discipline: every payment to a subcontractor must be supported by (a) the subcontractor's invoice with labour/materials split + reverse-charge treatment where applicable; (b) the verification reference at the time of payment; (c) the PDS issued to the subcontractor within 14 days; (d) the CIS300 entry for the tax month covering the payment; (e) the deducted-amount payment to HMRC by 19th of the month following (22nd for electronic). **The trail is the audit-evidence pack HMRC will request on a CIS compliance review** — gaps create Sch 24 inaccuracy penalty exposure even where the underlying liability has been paid in full. Sessions emphasise this as operational discipline, not paperwork formalism.
9. **The penalty stack the templates protect against — Sch 55 (late filing) + Sch 56 (late payment) + Sch 24 (inaccuracy)** — Sessions surface the penalty exposure: (a) Sch 55 late-filing penalty on CIS300 — standard quanta apply per §27.2 (Stage 2 sub-agent verifies current quanta at write per §16.27); (b) Sch 56 late-payment penalty on deducted amounts not remitted to HMRC by the 19th / 22nd; (c) Sch 24 inaccuracy penalty where under-deduction has occurred — careless / deliberate / deliberate-and-concealed bands at standard percentages; (d) under-deduction recovery — the contractor remains liable for the under-deducted amount unless HMRC accepts a Regulation 9 direction (SI 2005/2045 reg 9 — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies reg 9 wording at write per §16.35) transferring the liability to the subcontractor where reasonable care has been taken. The templates are the protective layer against this stack — reg-9-direction relief depends on the contractor demonstrating reasonable care, which is evidenced by the verification template + PDS audit trail + labour/materials invoice discipline + on-boarding status-test review.
10. **The annual + cycle-end operational template add-ons (P60-equivalent + employer reconciliation + subcontractor annual statement)** — Sessions surface the cycle-end discipline: (a) annual subcontractor reconciliation (cumulative payments + deductions for the tax year — typically delivered alongside the final PDS of the tax year, although not a separate statutory document); (b) end-of-tax-year HMRC reconciliation (the contractor's CIS300 returns must reconcile to the deducted-amount payments made to HMRC across the year — gaps trigger enquiry); (c) gross-payment-status annual review per FA 2004 s.64 + s.66 — HMRC may review gross-payment status annually based on compliance + turnover; the contractor should track each gross-payment subcontractor's continuing eligibility and adjust the deduction rate immediately on any HMRC cancellation notice; (d) where the contractor is also a subcontractor in its own right (common in multi-tier development projects), the contractor receives PDSs from its own contractors and must reconcile those into its own CT / IT computation. Sessions cross-link to §33 for the deeper architecture.
