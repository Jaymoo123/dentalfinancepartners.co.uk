## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What happened in the £3m mortgage-fraud case** — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies via CPS press archive + court report database; report the year, jurisdiction, defendants' professional status, headline fraud value, and sentencing outcome at one-paragraph depth. Do NOT name individuals if the disciplinary record or court judgment cannot be independently confirmed; pivot to "in a typical mortgage-fraud prosecution" framing if verification fails.
2. **What is mortgage fraud under UK law** — Fraud Act 2006 s.2 "fraud by false representation": dishonestly making a false representation, intending to make a gain or cause loss. Mortgage fraud is the textbook case: false income declared, false employer, false deposit source, falsely stated owner-occupation when the property is in fact for buy-to-let. Maximum sentence: 10 years' imprisonment on indictment.
3. **Why are accountants and financial advisers in the cross-hairs** — they sign mortgage-reference letters, supply income certifications, prepare SA302s and tax-year overviews used as income evidence by lenders, and (for financial advisers) place the mortgage product. False representation by the adviser on any of these documents is direct Fraud Act exposure; assistance with concealment is POCA exposure.
4. **The §330 POCA "failure to disclose" reflex** — an accountant in the regulated sector who knows or suspects (or has reasonable grounds to know or suspect) that another person is engaged in money laundering must report it as a Suspicious Activity Report to the National Crime Agency through the firm's nominated officer. Standard is *Da Silva* "more than mere speculation" and *Saik* "actual suspicion" — a lower bar than belief. The reporting reflex extends to historic rental income concealment, undisclosed CGT on property disposals, and dirty deposits.
5. **The "tipping-off" prohibition (POCA s.333A)** — once a SAR has been filed, the accountant cannot tell the client a SAR exists. This is the most operationally surprising rule for clients: your accountant may continue to handle your work while a SAR is live, and may not lawfully say so. The duty is criminal-law-grade.
6. **MLR 2017 reg 8 — am I in the regulated sector?** — the SI 2017/692 reg 8(2) list explicitly catches "auditors, insolvency practitioners, external accountants and tax advisers" alongside "estate agents and letting agents." A practice does not have to be a Chartered Accountant; the test is the activity (provision of accountancy or tax-advisory services in the course of business in the UK), not the qualification. Bookkeepers offering tax advice are caught. Letting agents are caught for property-management activity ≥ €10,000 monthly rent (the €10k trigger is verified at the MLR 2017 reg 14 letting-agent extension — Stage 2 sub-agent re-verifies the threshold + currency at write time).
7. **What "customer due diligence" actually looks like on a property-finance file** — identity verification (passport or driving licence + recent utility bill or HMRC letter); source-of-funds enquiry (where did the deposit come from, can you evidence it, does the trail match the income profile we already hold); ongoing monitoring (does the post-funding pattern match the file). Enhanced due diligence triggers: high-risk third countries, PEP status, complex unusual transactions, unusual source-of-funds patterns. Stage 2 details the practical evidence package.
8. **What if I (the client) realise mid-instruction that my position is not as I represented** — this is the page's voluntary-disclosure pivot. The Let Property Campaign (for unpaid rental tax) and the Worldwide Disclosure Facility (for offshore matters) are voluntary routes that pre-empt the criminal track. **Forward-link to the LPC cluster** (`know-about-let-property-campaign`, `benefits-of-participating-in-the-let-property-campaign`) and to §27.5 CDF / Code of Practice 9 for the more serious cases. Do NOT re-walk those pages; surface the route in one paragraph and forward-link.
9. **What does conviction mean for the accountant's career** — automatic disqualification under the relevant professional body (ICAEW / ACCA / CIOT / CIMA disciplinary regulations — Stage 2 verifies the specific by-laws); separately, automatic loss of MLR 2017 supervised-firm status; civil recovery proceedings under POCA Part 5; potential s.328 arrangements liability for any partner or director who knew or suspected. Career-ending; firm-ending; the prosecution cost is borne personally because professional indemnity does not cover criminal conduct.
10. **The practical lesson for property investors choosing an accountant** — short list: (i) is the firm MLR 2017 supervised, by whom, and is the supervisor named on the firm's website; (ii) does the engagement letter set out CDD requirements; (iii) is the firm willing to walk away from instructions that smell wrong (genuine red-flag: a firm that pushes through CDD friction is the firm to avoid). The page closes on the **trust-but-verify** framing: a properly supervised accountant is your client-side defence against the criminal track, not an obstacle to it.
