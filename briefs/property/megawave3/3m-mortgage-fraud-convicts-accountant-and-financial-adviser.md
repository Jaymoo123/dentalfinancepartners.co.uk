## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What happened in the £3m mortgage-fraud case** — Stage 2 sub-agent verifies via CPS press archive + court report database; report the year, jurisdiction, defendants' professional status, headline fraud value, and sentencing outcome at one-paragraph depth. Do NOT name individuals if the disciplinary record or court judgment cannot be independently confirmed; pivot to "in a typical mortgage-fraud prosecution" framing if verification fails.
2. **What is mortgage fraud under UK law** — Fraud Act 2006 s.2 "fraud by false representation": dishonestly making a false representation, intending to make a gain or cause loss. Mortgage fraud is the textbook case: false income declared, false employer, false deposit source, falsely stated owner-occupation when the property is in fact for buy-to-let. Maximum sentence: 10 years' imprisonment on indictment.
3. **Why are accountants and financial advisers in the cross-hairs** — they sign mortgage-reference letters, supply income certifications, prepare SA302s and tax-year overviews used as income evidence by lenders, and (for financial advisers) place the mortgage product. False representation by the adviser on any of these documents is direct Fraud Act exposure; assistance with concealment is POCA exposure.
4. **The §330 POCA "failure to disclose" reflex** — an accountant in the regulated sector who knows or suspects (or has reasonable grounds to know or suspect) that another person is engaged in money laundering must report it as a Suspicious Activity Report to the National Crime Agency through the firm's nominated officer. Standard is *Da Silva* "more than mere speculation" and *Saik* "actual suspicion" — a lower bar than belief. The reporting reflex extends to historic rental income concealment, undisclosed CGT on property disposals, and dirty deposits.
5. **The "tipping-off" prohibition (POCA s.333A)** — once a SAR has been filed, the accountant cannot tell the client a SAR exists. This is the most operationally surprising rule for clients: your accountant may continue to handle your work while a SAR is live, and may not lawfully say so. The duty is criminal-law-grade.
6. **MLR 2017 reg 8 — am I in the regulated sector?** — the SI 2017/692 reg 8(2) list explicitly catches "auditors, insolvency practitioners, external accountants and tax advisers" alongside "estate agents and letting agents." A practice does not have to be a Chartered Accountant; the test is the activity (provision of accountancy or tax-advisory services in the course of business in the UK), not the qualification. Bookkeepers offering tax advice are caught. Letting agents are caught for property-management activity ≥ €10,000 monthly rent (the €10k trigger is verified at the MLR 2017 reg 14 letting-agent extension — Stage 2 sub-agent re-verifies the threshold + currency at write time).
7. **What "customer due diligence" actually looks like on a property-finance file** — identity verification (passport or driving licence + recent utility bill or HMRC letter); source-of-funds enquiry (where did the deposit come from, can you evidence it, does the trail match the income profile we already hold); ongoing monitoring (does the post-funding pattern match the file). Enhanced due diligence triggers: high-risk third countries, PEP status, complex unusual transactions, unusual source-of-funds patterns. Stage 2 details the practical evidence package.
8. **What if I (the client) realise mid-instruction that my position is not as I represented** — this is the page's voluntary-disclosure pivot. The Let Property Campaign (for unpaid rental tax) and the Worldwide Disclosure Facility (for offshore matters) are voluntary routes that pre-empt the criminal track. **Forward-link to the LPC cluster** (`know-about-let-property-campaign`, `benefits-of-participating-in-the-let-property-campaign`) and to §27.5 CDF / Code of Practice 9 for the more serious cases. Do NOT re-walk those pages; surface the route in one paragraph and forward-link.
9. **What does conviction mean for the accountant's career** — automatic disqualification under the relevant professional body (ICAEW / ACCA / CIOT / CIMA disciplinary regulations — Stage 2 verifies the specific by-laws); separately, automatic loss of MLR 2017 supervised-firm status; civil recovery proceedings under POCA Part 5; potential s.328 arrangements liability for any partner or director who knew or suspected. Career-ending; firm-ending; the prosecution cost is borne personally because professional indemnity does not cover criminal conduct.
10. **The practical lesson for property investors choosing an accountant** — short list: (i) is the firm MLR 2017 supervised, by whom, and is the supervisor named on the firm's website; (ii) does the engagement letter set out CDD requirements; (iii) is the firm willing to walk away from instructions that smell wrong (genuine red-flag: a firm that pushes through CDD friction is the firm to avoid). The page closes on the **trust-but-verify** framing: a properly supervised accountant is your client-side defence against the criminal track, not an obstacle to it.

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The §330 reflex on a routine property-finance file

A landlord client asks their accountant to prepare an SA302 + tax-year-overview pack for a remortgage application on a four-property BTL portfolio. During the routine workpaper cross-check the accountant notices the client has declared £42,000 rental income on the SA return for the latest year but the underlying bank statements show £58,000 of rent receipts across the four bank accounts. The £16,000 gap is unexplained. The accountant's *Da Silva* threshold ("more than mere speculation") is met — the gap is documentary, not speculative.

- **Step 1:** The accountant raises an internal SAR to the firm's nominated officer (no client contact yet; raising internally is NOT tipping off because the disclosure is within the firm's MLR-compliant SAR pipeline per the AMLGAS guidance).
- **Step 2:** Nominated officer reviews and (typically) files an external SAR to the National Crime Agency via the SAR Portal. NCA receives; UKFIU analyses; client is not told.
- **Step 3:** The accountant continues with the remortgage paperwork using ONLY the figures the client provided on the SA return — the file does not include the bank statements that surfaced the gap. The s.333A tipping-off prohibition prevents the accountant from saying "I noticed a discrepancy and reported it"; the prohibition does not prevent the accountant from declining to certify figures they do not believe.
- **Step 4:** If the lender requires a signed accountant's certificate restating the SA figures, the accountant declines unless the client first updates the SA return or instructs HMRC. Refusing to certify is a commercial act, not tipping off.
- **Step 5:** Client is encouraged toward the LPC (`https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/let-property-campaign-your-guide-to-making-a-disclosure`) as the voluntary route. The pivot is procedural ("here is the route to update your tax position"), not an acknowledgement that a SAR exists.

### Example 2 — MLR 2017 reg 8 scope: am I in?

- **Profile A — single-practice tax adviser:** sole trader, no audit work, no insolvency, prepares SA returns and offers tax-advisory services for ~30 BTL landlord clients. In scope under reg 8(2)(c) "tax advisers". Must register for MLR supervision with a professional body (ICAEW / ACCA / CIOT) or HMRC where no professional body covers them.
- **Profile B — bookkeeper offering "tax help" informally:** prepares ledgers for ~10 sole-trader landlords and "helps with the numbers" on their SA returns each year. Functionally a tax adviser per HMRC's wide test; in scope under reg 8(2)(c). Bookkeeping alone is NOT in scope; bookkeeping + tax advice IS.
- **Profile C — letting agent managing rents above the threshold:** manages 35 BTL properties on behalf of a portfolio landlord; total monthly rent £52,000. In scope under reg 8(2)(f) "letting agents" because monthly rent exceeds the €10,000 threshold (HMRC supervision routing per reg 14 + SI 2017/692 supervision regulations; verified at `https://www.gov.uk/guidance/money-laundering-regulations-who-needs-to-register`).
- **Profile D — letting agent handling only short-let / FHL bookings at low total rent:** monthly rent on the agent's books is well below €10,000-equivalent. NOT in scope under reg 8(2)(f) by reference to the supervision threshold. Practical caveat: scope can change month-to-month if portfolio grows; agents typically register at the first crossing.

### Example 3 — Penalty cliff if the client (not the accountant) hits the criminal track

Mortgage fraud under Fraud Act 2006 s.2 carries a **maximum 10 years' imprisonment on indictment** (s.1(3)(b) Fraud Act 2006). Where the client's mortgage application materially overstated income, deposit source, or owner-occupation intent, the live exposure includes:

- Criminal prosecution under Fraud Act 2006 s.2 (10-year max).
- Civil recovery under POCA Part 5 (asset confiscation of any property obtained via the false representation).
- Bankruptcy on enforcement of a confiscation order.
- Disqualification from acting as a company director under CDDA 1986 s.2 (criminal-conviction route).
- For the accountant (if implicated by signed reference letters or income certifications): direct Fraud Act 2006 s.2 exposure on the false representation, separate POCA s.328 arrangements exposure on knowingly entering an arrangement that facilitates retention of criminal property, and automatic loss of MLR supervised-firm status on conviction.

### Example 4 — The "how to choose an accountant" practical checklist (RUN section)

For the property-investor reader audience. RUN session renders as a short checklist (not a table) with five items:

1. **Is the firm MLR 2017 supervised, and by whom?** Ask. The supervisor is named at most firms' regulation page (ICAEW + ACCA + CIOT + ICAS + AAT + IFA are the common professional-body supervisors; HMRC supervises firms not covered by a professional body). A firm that hesitates on this question is a red flag.
2. **Does the engagement letter set out CDD requirements?** A compliant firm will need ID + address verification + source-of-funds enquiry on first engagement. Frictionless onboarding "to get you started fast" is the wrong signal.
3. **Will the firm walk away from instructions that smell wrong?** Ask in the initial meeting how they would handle a hypothetical income-overstatement scenario. The right answer is "we decline; we may also be obliged to report." The wrong answer is "we work around it."
4. **Does the firm employ named tax advisers, or sub-contract to overseas unsupervised teams?** Sub-contracted bookkeeping to overseas unsupervised providers is an MLR perimeter risk and signals weak compliance culture.
5. **What is the firm's renewal practice on PI insurance + AML training?** Annual PI renewal + annual AML training are baseline. Both should be in the firm's literature.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target)

1. **Q: What is mortgage fraud under UK law?**
   A: Fraud Act 2006 s.2 ("Fraud by false representation"): a person commits the offence by (a) dishonestly making a false representation, and (b) intending to make a gain or cause loss. A representation is false if it is untrue or misleading and the person knows it might be. Mortgage fraud is the textbook s.2 case — false income, false employer, false deposit source, falsely declared owner-occupation when the property is in fact for letting. Maximum sentence 10 years on indictment (Fraud Act 2006 s.1(3)(b)).

2. **Q: Why are accountants and financial advisers exposed?**
   A: They sign accountants' reference letters, prepare SA302s and tax-year overviews used as income evidence by mortgage lenders, certify trading-income for self-employed applicants, and (for financial advisers) place the mortgage product. A false representation by the adviser on any of these documents is direct Fraud Act 2006 s.2 exposure. Assistance with concealment, even after the lender has paid out, is separate POCA s.328 arrangements exposure.

3. **Q: What is the §330 POCA reporting reflex?**
   A: An accountant in the regulated sector who knows or suspects (or has reasonable grounds to know or suspect) that another person is engaged in money laundering must report it via a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) to the National Crime Agency through the firm's nominated officer. The threshold is *R v Da Silva* "more than mere speculation"; the bar is lower than belief. The reporting reflex covers historic rental income concealment, undisclosed CGT on property disposals, and dirty deposits funding property purchases.

4. **Q: What is the "tipping off" prohibition?**
   A: POCA 2002 s.333A makes it an offence for a person in the regulated sector to disclose anything likely to prejudice an investigation following a SAR. The prohibition applies once a SAR has been filed or is contemplated. Operational consequence for clients: your accountant may continue to handle your work while a SAR is live and is criminally prohibited from telling you. The prohibition is calibrated to the *Squirrell* boundary — declining to continue acting is not tipping off; explaining why is.

5. **Q: Is my bookkeeper covered by MLR 2017?**
   A: Bookkeeping alone is not in scope. Bookkeeping + tax advice IS in scope under reg 8(2)(c) "external accountants and tax advisers". HMRC reads "tax adviser" widely — anyone helping with SA-return numbers in the course of business is captured. A bookkeeper who walks the line should register; HMRC enforcement reports show that under-registered tax-advisory work attracts the highest civil-penalty load in the supervised population.

6. **Q: My letting agent is small — does MLR 2017 apply?**
   A: The threshold for letting-agent supervision (per `https://www.gov.uk/guidance/money-laundering-regulations-who-needs-to-register`) is property or land with rent at or above €10,000-equivalent monthly. A small letting agent managing two flats at £900 each is below the threshold. An agent managing a portfolio with combined monthly rents over €10,000 is in scope and must register with HMRC unless covered by a professional body. The threshold is per-agent, not per-landlord.

7. **Q: What is customer due diligence and what should I expect?**
   A: Identity verification (passport or driving licence + recent utility bill or HMRC letter); source-of-funds enquiry (where did your deposit come from, can you evidence it, does the trail match the income profile we already hold); ongoing monitoring (does the post-funding pattern match the file). Enhanced due diligence is triggered by high-risk third countries, politically-exposed-person status, unusual transaction patterns, or unusual source-of-funds. CCAB AMLGAS sets out the operational floor; HMRC accountancy sector guidance gives the HMRC-supervised perspective.

8. **Q: What if I realise mid-instruction that I have not declared all my rental income?**
   A: The Let Property Campaign (`https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/let-property-campaign-your-guide-to-making-a-disclosure`) is HMRC's voluntary-disclosure route for unpaid tax on residential rental income. Penalty discount against the enforced-enquiry route is meaningful — typically single-digit-percent under the LPC offer versus 30%-100% under Sch 24 / Sch 41. For more serious historic exposure including suspected fraud, the Contractual Disclosure Facility under HMRC Code of Practice 9 is the pre-prosecution route (§27.5).

9. **Q: What does conviction mean for the accountant's career?**
   A: Conviction triggers (a) automatic disqualification under the relevant professional body's bye-laws (ICAEW / ACCA / CIOT / ICAS / AAT disciplinary regulations); (b) automatic loss of MLR 2017 supervised-firm status; (c) civil recovery proceedings under POCA Part 5 on any benefit; (d) potential POCA s.328 arrangements exposure for any partner or director who knew or suspected. PI insurance does not cover criminal conduct; the prosecution and confiscation costs are personal. Career-ending, firm-ending.

10. **Q: How do I choose a property accountant who will protect me?**
    A: Five checks: ask which professional body supervises the firm under MLR 2017; read the engagement letter for explicit CDD terms; ask how the firm would handle a hypothetical income-overstatement scenario (right answer: decline and may have to report); confirm tax-advisory work is done by named in-house staff not unsupervised overseas sub-contractors; check that PI insurance and annual AML training are in place. A firm that pushes through CDD friction is the firm to avoid.

11. **Q: How does this page differ from `know-about-let-property-campaign`?**
    A: The LPC page is operational — what the LPC route is, how to use it, what the penalty discount looks like. This page is the broader professional-conduct picture: what the AML / MLR 2017 / POCA cordon looks like around your property accountant, why the §330 reporting reflex exists, and how the LPC fits as the voluntary route that pre-empts the criminal track. Read both together: this page for the framework, the LPC page for the disclosure mechanics.

12. **Q: I am the accountant — should I file a SAR every time I have a doubt?**
    A: No. The *Da Silva* "more than mere speculation" threshold means a SAR is required only where you cross from speculation into reasonable grounds to know or suspect. Documentary anomalies (unexplained income gaps, source-of-funds inconsistencies, structuring patterns) are typical triggers. Vague unease without an underlying factual basis is not the bar. The CCAB AMLGAS gives the operational treatment; document your suspicion analysis even where you conclude no SAR is needed — the file note is your evidential record.
