# ATED rates 2026/27: the band table, the FA 2013 s.99 + s.101 indexation mechanism, and the revaluation cycle

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (the rate table):**
  - **FA 2013 s.99** — "Amount of tax chargeable" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Subsection (4) sets out the original 2013-enacted annual chargeable amount table: £3,500 / £7,000 / £23,350 / £54,450 / £109,050 / £218,200 across the six taxable-value bands. These are the **statutory base figures**; current-year operative amounts derive from these via s.101 CPI indexation (see below).
  - Subsection (1)-(3) sets out the chargeable-period basis (1 April to 31 March); subsections (5)-(6) provide the pro-rata fraction calculation for chargeable persons entering / leaving the charge mid-period.
- **Primary (the CPI indexation mechanism):**
  - **FA 2013 s.101** — "Indexation of annual chargeable amounts" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). For each chargeable period beginning on or after 1 April, the s.99(4) amounts are adjusted by the September-to-September CPI rise, rounded down to the nearest £50. Treasury must issue the order specifying the new amounts before 1 April each year.
  - The 2026/27 published figures (£4,600 / £9,450 / £32,200 / £75,450 / £151,450 / £303,450 per HP §18.1 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22) are the cumulatively-indexed product of the 2013 base figures + the September-to-September CPI rises across 2013-2025, rounded to the nearest £50.
- **Primary (the 5-yearly revaluation cycle):**
  - **FA 2013 ss.102-105** — taxable-value determination + revaluation date framework. Revaluation dates: 1 April 2012, 1 April 2017, 1 April 2022, **1 April 2027**, and every five years thereafter. The chargeable value for a chargeable period is the property's open-market value at the most recent valuation date that pre-dates the start of the chargeable period.
  - **FA 2013 s.124-125** — newly-built / converted dwellings: use of acquisition value or construction-completion value until the next 5-yearly revaluation date.
- **Primary (Pre-Return Banding Check):**
  - HMRC operational concession (not legislative): owners with valuations within 10% of a band boundary can request HMRC's view in advance (no fee, no consequence on the return obligation). Useful for properties near £1m, £2m, £5m, £10m, £20m where band-boundary effects materially change the charge.
- **Primary (mixed-use apportionment):**
  - **FA 2013 s.116** — single-dwelling interest definition; mixed-use treatment is just-and-reasonable apportionment (no statutory formula). HMRC accepts floor-area or value-based evidence.
- **Primary (chargeable persons + relief catalogue, cross-reference):**
  - **FA 2013 Part 3 ss.94-174** for the full architecture; non-natural persons (UK + non-UK companies, partnerships with corporate member, CIS) per s.97; relief catalogue ss.133-150 per HP §18.3 (briefly referenced; full relief mechanics live on B5 + B11 + B12).
- **House position reference:** §2 (headline ATED bands 2025/26 + 2026/27, verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full)**, §18.1 (the verified 2025/26 + 2026/27 table — the operative source-of-truth at write time), §18.2 (chargeable persons), §18.3 (relief catalogue cross-reference), §18.4 (return mechanics + 5-yearly revaluation + PRBC + mixed-use apportionment), §18.8 (do-not-write list, specifically the "ATED only applies to overseas companies" anti-pattern and the FA 2013 Schedule 33 mis-citation).

### Example 2 — The s.99 + s.101 indexation mechanism walked through

Render as semantic HTML `<ol>`. The statutory mechanism is mechanical but most reader misunderstandings come from not seeing it:

1. **FA 2013 s.99(4) original-enacted table** sets six annual chargeable amounts: £3,500 (band 1, £500k-£1m); £7,000 (band 2, £1m-£2m); £23,350 (band 3, £2m-£5m); £54,450 (band 4, £5m-£10m); £109,050 (band 5, £10m-£20m); £218,200 (band 6, over £20m). These are the statutory floor figures from the 2013/14 chargeable period.
2. **FA 2013 s.101 indexation formula** for each subsequent chargeable period: previous year's chargeable amount multiplied by (1 + September-to-September CPI rise), rounded down to the nearest £50. The CPI reference month is September of the year before the chargeable period starts.
3. **Treasury order timing.** Treasury must issue the order specifying the new chargeable amounts before 1 April of each chargeable period. In practice the figures are announced in November (after the September CPI publication), giving owners time to plan.
4. **Cumulative effect 2013/14 to 2026/27.** Across 13 chargeable periods (2013/14 to 2025/26 indexation applied to 2026/27) the cumulative CPI uplift is roughly 31% (band 1 went from £3,500 to £4,600 = +31%). Across higher bands the cumulative percentage uplift is similar (bands track the same CPI series).
5. **The £50 round-down.** Each year's indexed figure is rounded DOWN to the nearest £50. This is conservatively in HMRC's favour at higher bands (£303,450 vs an un-rounded £303,470-ish at band 6).
6. **The November announcement.** Each November HMRC announces the next-year figures via a Treasury order + gov.uk publication. Owners with valuations within 10% of a band boundary should engage with PRBC mechanism (see Example 4) and plan their next-year filing position promptly.

### Example 3 — Mid-period acquisition pro-rata (FA 2013 s.99(5)-(6))

Trevor Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co) acquires a £3.2m London BTL flat on 1 October 2026 — 182 days remaining in the 2026/27 chargeable period (1 April 2026 to 31 March 2027). No relief applies (let to a connected family member; CIHC + ATED relief blocked per the §21.A + §21.5 expansive exclusion).

- **Band determination:** £3.2m falls in the £2m-£5m band → 2026/27 annual chargeable amount £32,200.
- **Pro-rata calculation per s.99(5)-(6):** £32,200 × (182 / 365) = **£16,055.07** (rounded per HMRC).
- **Return + payment deadline:** ATED return required within 30 days of first becoming chargeable per FA 2013 s.159 — so by 31 October 2026 (30 days after 1 October acquisition). Payment due with the return.
- **Following chargeable period (2027/28):** the operative valuation date is 1 April 2027 (next 5-yearly revaluation date). Trevor must commission a 1 April 2027 valuation; if the property has appreciated to £4.1m or beyond, the chargeable amount per s.99 + indexation applies; if depreciated below £2m, the lower band applies. Full-year return + payment for 2027/28 due 30 April 2027.
- **Operational lesson:** mid-period acquisitions have a tight 30-day return window from chargeability under s.159; the pro-rata calculation is mechanical per s.99(5)-(6); the 5-yearly revaluation reset at 1 April 2027 creates a fresh banding check.

### Example 4 — Pre-Return Banding Check around the £1m boundary

Olivetti Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co holding a London townhouse) commissioned a RICS valuation at 1 April 2022 of £1,030,000. The property is within 10% of the £1m band boundary (boundary value: £1,000,000; difference £30,000 = 3% above the boundary). Wide range of plausible valuations within 10% either direction; the band difference is material:
- If valued at £999,000 (below £1m): 2026/27 chargeable amount = £4,600 (band 1).
- If valued at £1,001,000 (just above £1m): 2026/27 chargeable amount = £9,450 (band 2).

The band-boundary effect is a £4,850 annual difference — over 5 years c. £24,250.

**PRBC submission per HMRC operational concession:**

- Owner submits PRBC to HMRC with the RICS valuation report, methodology notes, and comparator evidence.
- No fee. HMRC issues a published view (typically within 30-60 days; some longer for complex cases).
- The PRBC view is NOT statutorily binding but represents HMRC's considered position on the valuation evidence presented; submitting the PRBC view on the return is treated as good-faith disclosure for Sch 24 inaccuracy purposes.
- If HMRC's PRBC view is band 1 (under £1m), Olivetti files at band 1 with the PRBC reference; Sch 24 inaccuracy exposure on later HMRC enquiry is mitigated because the position was disclosed and the PRBC view was followed.
- If HMRC's PRBC view is band 2 (over £1m), Olivetti has the option to file at band 2 (paying the higher charge with certainty) or to commission a more rigorous valuation challenging HMRC's PRBC view + file at band 1 with a fully-documented position (higher inaccuracy-risk but lower-charge if substantively defensible).

**Operational anchor:** the PRBC is the single most valuable HMRC concession for owners with borderline valuations. Use it.

### Example 5 — Mixed-use apportionment (flat-over-shop per FA 2013 s.116)

Saffron Estates Cayman Limited (Cayman company) owns a 1,800 sq ft mixed-use building on a London high street comprising a 500 sq ft ground-floor retail unit (let to a tenant on a 10-year lease at £55k/year) and a 1,300 sq ft first-floor + second-floor residential apartment (let to a connected family member as primary residence). Whole building open-market value 1 April 2022 was £1,650,000.

- **Step 1: apportion the value between residential + non-residential elements per FA 2013 s.116 just-and-reasonable test.**
  - **Floor-area basis:** residential 1,300 / 1,800 = 72.2% of floor area → £1,650,000 × 72.2% = £1,191,700 residential value.
  - **Value-based basis (alternative):** residential value via residential-only comparable / yield analysis suggests £1,180,000 (broadly aligned with floor-area approach).
  - HMRC accepts either approach where consistent with the available evidence; documented methodology essential.
- **Step 2: determine ATED applicability of the residential element.**
  - Residential element value £1,191,700 (floor-area basis) > £500,000 ATED threshold → ATED applies.
  - Band: £1m-£2m → 2026/27 chargeable amount £9,450 (residential element charged at the band that covers the residential value, NOT a fraction of the whole-building band).
- **Step 3: CIHC + connected-occupant trap (per §21.A + §21.5).**
  - The residential element let to a connected family member defeats the s.18N(2)(b) "let commercially" carve-out → CIHC + ATED relief blocked.
- **Step 4: chargeable amount.**
  - Full residential-element annual chargeable amount: £9,450 (no relief; full year).
- **Step 5: PRBC route available if the residential-element valuation is contentious.**
  - The apportionment methodology + the residential-element value should both be PRBC-eligible if either is within 10% of a relevant boundary.

### Example 6 — Misframings the page must correct (verbatim do-not-write list)

- **Misframe 1:** "ATED only applies to overseas companies." FALSE per §18.2 + §18.8 anti-pattern — ATED applies to ANY non-natural person holding UK residential property over £500k, including UK companies, UK partnerships with corporate member, UK collective investment schemes. The overseas-company framing is a popular misconception driven by the Sch A1 enveloped-residential look-through context.
- **Misframe 2:** "ATED rates are frozen since 2013." FALSE per §18.1 — rates are CPI-indexed annually per FA 2013 s.101. The 2013 enacted figures (£3,500 / £7,000 / £23,350 / £54,450 / £109,050 / £218,200) have risen by c. 31% cumulatively to 2026/27 figures (£4,600 / £9,450 / £32,200 / £75,450 / £151,450 / £303,450).
- **Misframe 3:** "ATED was enacted by Schedule 33 FA 2013." FALSE per §18.8 anti-pattern — ATED is FA 2013 Part 3 (ss.94-174). There is no Schedule 33 to FA 2013; the original Bill numbering may have used Sch 33 but the enacted statute is Part 3.
- **Misframe 4:** "The current revaluation date is 1 April 2022 and that's it until 2027." MISLEADING — the operative valuation date for 2026/27 chargeable period IS 1 April 2022 (for dwellings held throughout), but **the NEXT revaluation date is 1 April 2027** and from the 2027/28 chargeable period onwards the chargeable value is the 1 April 2027 open-market valuation. Owners should plan ahead.
- **Misframe 5:** "PRBC is mandatory for valuations within 10% of a band boundary." FALSE — PRBC is an HMRC operational CONCESSION (no fee; voluntary submission). It is not a statutory requirement. Owners can self-assess at the boundary and accept Sch 24 inaccuracy risk on later HMRC enquiry; PRBC simply pre-empts that risk.
- **Misframe 6:** "PRBC is statutorily binding on HMRC." FALSE — PRBC is HMRC's published view at the time given; not legislatively binding. Material change of fact (new comparable evidence, valuation methodology challenge) could in principle support a different HMRC position later. In practice PRBC views are typically held to.
- **Misframe 7:** "Reliefs reduce the ATED rate." FALSE per §18.3 — reliefs REMOVE the charge for relieved periods entirely; they do not reduce the rate. A property that qualifies for s.133 property-rental-business relief throughout the chargeable period pays £0 ATED for that period. The return obligation continues (claim-only return) per §18.4.
- **Misframe 8:** "Pro-rata applies to chargeable amount per dwelling per relief-eligible part-period." MISLEADING — s.99(5)-(6) pro-rata applies based on the days the person is chargeable, NOT based on days the relief applies. A dwelling that is chargeable for 365 days but with relief for 200 days pays £0 (relief covers all days); a dwelling chargeable for 182 days (mid-period acquisition) with no relief pays the pro-rated amount; a dwelling chargeable for 365 days with relief for 100 days pays the full annual charge minus relief, calculated per s.158 mechanics (verify at write).
- **Misframe 9:** "ATED bands are reviewed every 5 years." FALSE — bands (the £500k / £1m / £2m / £5m / £10m / £20m thresholds) have been UNCHANGED since 2013; the CHARGEABLE AMOUNTS within each band are CPI-indexed annually per s.101. The 5-yearly cycle is the VALUATION DATE cycle (1 April 2017 → 2022 → 2027 → 2032), not the band-threshold cycle.
- **Misframe 10:** "ATED 2026/27 chargeable period is 1 April 2026 to 5 April 2027." FALSE — ATED chargeable period is 1 April to 31 MARCH per s.99(2). (Conflation with income-tax / CGT 5 April year-end is the common confusion.)
- **Misframe 11:** "New-build chargeable value is the 5-yearly valuation date value." FALSE per ss.124-125 — new-build dwellings use ACQUISITION or CONSTRUCTION-COMPLETION value until the next 5-yearly revaluation date. From the next 5-yearly date onwards the standard valuation regime applies.
- **Misframe 12:** "Mixed-use buildings are excluded from ATED." FALSE per FA 2013 s.116 — mixed-use is just-and-reasonable apportionment; the residential element falls in ATED if its apportioned value exceeds £500k.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: What are the ATED bands and chargeable amounts for 2026/27?**
   A: Per HP §18.1 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22, the 2026/27 chargeable amounts are: £500,001-£1m: **£4,600**; over £1m-£2m: **£9,450**; over £2m-£5m: **£32,200**; over £5m-£10m: **£75,450**; over £10m-£20m: **£151,450**; over £20m: **£303,450**. Bands themselves (the £500k / £1m / £2m / £5m / £10m / £20m thresholds) have been unchanged since 2013; the chargeable amounts are CPI-indexed annually per FA 2013 s.101.

2. **Q: How are the chargeable amounts calculated?**
   A: FA 2013 s.99(4) sets six original 2013-enacted figures (£3,500 / £7,000 / £23,350 / £54,450 / £109,050 / £218,200). FA 2013 s.101 indexes those amounts for each subsequent chargeable period: previous year's amount × (1 + September-to-September CPI rise), rounded DOWN to the nearest £50. Treasury issues the indexation order before 1 April each year; HMRC publishes the figures via gov.uk (typically announced November after the September CPI publication).

3. **Q: What is the ATED chargeable period?**
   A: 1 April to 31 March per FA 2013 s.99(2). The 2026/27 chargeable period runs 1 April 2026 to 31 March 2027. Return + payment is due 30 April 2026 per FA 2013 s.159. NOT 5 April / 6 April (those are income-tax / CGT dates; ATED is distinct).

4. **Q: What is the taxable value?**
   A: The open-market value of the single-dwelling interest at the most recent valuation date that pre-dates the start of the chargeable period (per FA 2013 ss.102-105). For 2026/27 chargeable period, the operative valuation date for most dwellings is **1 April 2022** (the 5-yearly revaluation date). New-build / converted dwellings use acquisition or construction-completion value until the next 5-yearly date per ss.124-125.

5. **Q: When is the next ATED revaluation date?**
   A: **1 April 2027**. From the 2027/28 chargeable period onwards, the chargeable value is the open-market value at 1 April 2027. Owners holding properties near band boundaries should commission a 1 April 2027 RICS valuation in early 2027 + consider the PRBC route (see FAQ 7).

6. **Q: My property valuation is just above or below a band boundary — what should I do?**
   A: Use the **Pre-Return Banding Check (PRBC)** route. HMRC operational concession (no fee, no consequence on the return obligation): owners with valuations within 10% of a band boundary can submit a PRBC request with the RICS valuation report + methodology evidence. HMRC issues a published view (typically 30-60 days). The PRBC view is not statutorily binding but is treated as good-faith disclosure if followed on the return — material protection against Sch 24 inaccuracy penalties on later HMRC enquiry.

7. **Q: How does pro-rata work for a mid-period acquisition?**
   A: Per FA 2013 s.99(5)-(6): chargeable amount × (days in chargeable period during which person is chargeable / days in chargeable period). Example: company acquires a £3m dwelling on 1 October 2026 → 182 days remaining in 2026/27 → £32,200 × (182/365) = **£16,055**. Return + payment due within 30 days of first becoming chargeable per FA 2013 s.159 (so by 31 October 2026 in this example).

8. **Q: My building is mixed-use (flat over shop), how does ATED apply?**
   A: Per FA 2013 s.116, mixed-use treatment is just-and-reasonable apportionment. HMRC accepts floor-area or value-based apportionment evidence. ATED applies ONLY to the residential portion, and only if the residential portion's apportioned value exceeds £500k. A £1.65m mixed-use building with 72% residential floor area = £1.19m residential value → in ATED band 1 (£1m-£2m), 2026/27 amount £9,450 (subject to relief eligibility). Documentation of the apportionment methodology is essential.

9. **Q: Reliefs cover my chargeable period — do I still owe anything?**
   A: NO ATED is payable for periods where relief applies (s.133 property-rental-business, s.138 developer, s.141 trader, s.144 farmhouse, etc. per HP §18.3). BUT the return obligation is unchanged: a claim-only return must still be filed per FA 2013 s.159. Failing to file the claim-only return triggers the £100/£200/£300 late-filing escalator per HP §18.4 + FA 2009 Sch 55. Relief catalogue and claim mechanics live on B5 / B11 / B12; penalty mechanics on B8 / B9.

10. **Q: Are the bands the same as 2013?**
    A: The BAND THRESHOLDS (£500k / £1m / £2m / £5m / £10m / £20m) have been unchanged since the 2015/16 chargeable period (the £500k entry band was added then; original 2013 enactment had a £2m entry threshold lowered in stages). The CHARGEABLE AMOUNTS within each band are CPI-indexed annually per s.101. Band 1 has risen from £3,500 (2015/16) to £4,600 (2026/27); band 6 from £218,200 to £303,450.

11. **Q: Does ATED apply to UK companies or only overseas companies?**
    A: BOTH. Per FA 2013 s.97 + §18.2, ATED applies to any non-natural person holding a single-dwelling interest in UK residential property worth over £500k: UK companies, non-UK companies, partnerships with a corporate member, collective investment schemes. The "ATED only applies to overseas companies" framing (HP §18.8 anti-pattern) is wrong; it confuses the FA 2003 Sch 4A 15% SDLT charge architecture (also non-natural person scope) with the ATED architecture (same scope, different tax).

12. **Q: What happens if I under-band the return?**
    A: Sch 24 inaccuracy exposure. Behaviour-tiered: careless 0-30%; deliberate not-concealed 20-70%; deliberate-concealed 30-100%; offshore Category 3 uplift up to 200%. PRBC submission pre-empts inaccuracy risk where the band is borderline; documented RICS valuation + comparable evidence supports good-faith disclosure framing. Cross-link to B9 for the full Sch 24 + Sch 55 + Sch 56 penalty architecture.

13. **Q: What is the £50 round-down doing in the indexation formula?**
    A: Per FA 2013 s.101, each year's indexed figure is rounded DOWN to the nearest £50. This is a statutory simplification (avoiding chargeable amounts to the nearest penny). The round-down is consistently in HMRC's favour at higher bands (a true uplift to £303,488 rounds down to £303,450 — £38 lost; modest at the £4,600 band — a true £4,612 rounds to £4,600).

14. **Q: When does Treasury publish the next-year figures?**
    A: November of each year (after the September CPI publication that drives the indexation calculation). The Treasury order must be issued before 1 April of the chargeable period. The HMRC gov.uk ATED rates-and-allowances publication is the operative public source for the figures; the underlying Treasury order is published on legislation.gov.uk.
