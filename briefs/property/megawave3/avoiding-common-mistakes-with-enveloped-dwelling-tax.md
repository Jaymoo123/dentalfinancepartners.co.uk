# Avoiding common mistakes with the Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings: the prevention-first orientation page for chargeable persons and their advisers

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (the chargeable-person and charge architecture):**
  - **FA 2013 Part 3** (ss.94-174) — the operative ATED legislation. Sessions writing prevention-discipline copy must anchor to the Part 3 framework rather than to gov.uk summary-page language; verbatim citations defeat the most common drift errors.
  - **FA 2013 s.94** "Charge to tax" — the basic charging provision (verified at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27 architecture map). ATED is charged on **non-natural persons** holding a UK single-dwelling interest worth more than **£500,000** on the operative valuation date.
  - **FA 2013 s.99** "Annual chargeable amounts" — the base table; HP §18.1 carries the 2025/26 and 2026/27 figures verified gov.uk 2026-05-22 (£500,001-£1m £4,450/£4,600; £1m-£2m £9,150/£9,450; £2m-£5m £31,050/£32,200; £5m-£10m £72,700/£75,450; £10m-£20m £145,950/£151,450; over £20m £292,350/£303,450).
  - **FA 2013 s.101** "Indexation of annual chargeable amounts" — CPI uplift for the year ended September; HMRC publishes the next-year table each November.
- **Primary (the valuation architecture — the most-mistaken layer):**
  - **FA 2013 s.102** "Taxable value" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Five-yearly Crown dates (1 April 2012 / 2017 / 2022 / 2027) PLUS substantial-acquisition / partnership / CIS valuation events.
  - **FA 2013 s.102(2A)** added by FA 2015 — the 5-yearly Crown date does NOT operate during the chargeable period beginning on it. The 1 April 2027 revaluation bites from 2028/29, NOT 2027/28. This is the most common ATED valuation-timing mistake propagated by competitor pages.
  - **FA 2013 s.103** "Substantial acquisitions and disposals" — £40,000 threshold (verified at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27); linked-transaction connected-party aggregation rules.
- **Primary (the relief-claim discipline — the most-mistaken procedural layer):**
  - **FA 2013 ss.133-150** — the relief catalogue (HP §18.3): s.133 property rental business, s.137 dwellings open to the public, s.138 property developer, s.141 property trader, s.144 farmhouse, s.145 employee accommodation, s.149 social housing, s.150 charitable use, ss.134-135 demolition / conversion. **All reliefs must be claimed on the return** (s.159 / Sch 33 para 1) — there is no automatic application. Claim-only returns are still returns; failure to file is a penalty event under FA 2009 Sch 55 even where no tax is due.
- **Primary (the return + amendment + penalty interaction layer):**
  - **FA 2013 Sch 33** para 1 (return content + declaration), para 3 (amendment — end-of-next-chargeable-period window, NOT 12 months from filing — verified verbatim 2026-05-27), para 4 (HMRC obvious-error correction within 9 months), para 8 (enquiry power), para 21 (discovery assessments).
  - **FA 2009 Sch 55** late-filing penalties (£100 immediate + £200 at 3 months + £300 at 6 + £300 at 12 months — HP §18.4); **FA 2009 Sch 56** late-payment penalties; **FA 2007 Sch 24** inaccuracy penalties (careless 0-30% unprompted / 15-30% prompted; deliberate 20-70% / 35-70%; deliberate-and-concealed 30-100% / 50-100%); **FA 2015 Sch 21** offshore Cat-2 / Cat-3 uplift (1.5x / 2x for offshore matters or offshore transfers — applies to most overseas-corporate ATED filers).
- **Primary (the s.136 catalogue and the s.135 clawback — the most-mistaken relief-conditions layer):**
  - **FA 2013 s.136** "Meaning of 'non-qualifying individual'" — eight-category catalogue (HP §18.3 + B11 + B12 detail): (a) individuals entitled to the interest, (b) connected persons, (c) partnership members, (d) settlors of trusts holding the interest, (e) relatives of connected persons / settlors, (f) spouses / civil partners of relatives, (g) participants in collective investment schemes, (h) persons connected to those participants.
  - **FA 2013 s.135** "Non-qualifying occupation: look-forward and look-back" (verified verbatim 2026-05-27 at B11 seed time). Look-forward up to 3 subsequent chargeable periods; look-back to earlier days held by the same "relevant person" unless a s.133(1)(a) qualifying-use day intervenes.
- **Primary (the ATED-CGT abolition + RoE parallel-regime layer):**
  - **FA 2019 (the operative repeal vehicle)** — abolished ATED-related CGT from 6 April 2019. Gains on enveloped dwellings now fall under standard NRCGT at TCGA 1992 s.1A (HP §17.4 + §18.5). Sessions writing prevention copy must walk this explicitly — competitor pages still cite the old 28% ATED-CGT rate.
  - **Economic Crime (Transparency and Enforcement) Act 2022 (ECTEA 2022)** — Register of Overseas Entities (HP §11 + §11.A). RoE runs PARALLEL to ATED, not in place of it. An overseas company holding a UK dwelling must comply with BOTH; RoE compliance does NOT discharge the ATED return obligation, and vice versa.
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full — the authoritative working detail)**, §18.1 (band table), §18.2 (chargeable persons), §18.3 (relief catalogue), §18.4 (return mechanics + 30 April deadline + £100/£200/£300 penalties + five-yearly revaluation + PRBC + mixed-use), §18.5 (ATED-CGT abolition + NRCGT interaction), §18.6 (RoE parallel regime), §18.7 (HMRC OTM letters campaign), §18.8 (do-not-write list — the operative drift catalogue this seed builds on).

The framing differentiator is the **mistake-and-prevention pattern** (not a generic "things to watch out for" list):

1. **The "ATED is only for overseas companies" myth.** UK companies are equally in scope under s.94 (HP §18.2). Many UK BTL ltd-cos with director-purchased flats at £500k+ slip into ATED without realising; the prevention pattern is to check the chargeable-person test before any UK ltd-co acquires a £500k+ residential dwelling.
2. **The "reliefs are automatic where the dwelling is let" myth.** Reliefs under ss.133-150 must be CLAIMED on the return (HP §18.3 + §18.8). Even where the relief is full and tax due is zero, the return is still required by 30 April. Failure to file is a Sch 55 penalty event regardless of whether tax is due. The prevention pattern is the diary entry "30 April ATED return — even for relief-only periods".
3. **The "ATED-CGT applies to gains over £500k" myth.** ATED-CGT was abolished 6 April 2019 (HP §18.5 + §18.8). Gains on enveloped dwellings now fall under standard NRCGT at TCGA 1992 s.1A (18%/24% from 30 October 2024 for non-resident-company disposals; CT rate via the non-resident chargeable gains route for many corporate filers). Sessions writing on enveloped-dwelling disposals must walk this explicitly.
4. **The "1 April 2027 revaluation bites 2027/28" myth.** Wrong per s.102(2A) FA 2015 on-ramp clause (verified verbatim 2026-05-27 in B14 seed). The 1 April 2027 Crown date does not operate during the chargeable period beginning on it; it operates from 2028/29. The prevention pattern is to plan against the correct on-ramp timing — and to commission a 2027 RICS valuation in 2027 for use in the 2028/29 return (filed by 30 April 2028).
5. **The "Sch 33 amendment window is 12 months from filing" SA-analogue myth.** Wrong per Sch 33 para 3(3) (verified verbatim 2026-05-27 in B13 seed). The operative deadline is **end of the next chargeable period** after the period to which the return relates, with a 3-months-from-delivery extension under sub-(4) for late filers. Practitioners reading across from TMA 1970 s.9ZA get this wrong. Prevention: diary the amendment-window expiry date against the period-boundary, not against the filing date.
6. **The "letting to family at market rent unlocks s.133 relief" myth.** Wrong per s.136 catalogue (HP §18.3 + B11 + B12). s.133 has two independent conditions — market rent AND no non-qualifying-individual occupation. The s.136 eight-category catalogue blocks relief regardless of rent level for connected occupancy. Prevention: walk the s.136 catalogue with the prospective occupant before allowing any family-related letting.
7. **The "PRBC is for overseas filers only / for expensive bands only" myth.** PRBC (HP §18.4) is available at no cost to any chargeable person whose dwelling valuation is within 10% of a band boundary. A £2.05m flat near the £2m boundary moves between £9,450 and £32,200 (2026/27) — the PRBC is the no-cost lever. Prevention: use PRBC for any borderline valuation before the 30 April return.
8. **The "RoE compliance discharges ATED obligations" myth.** Wrong per HP §11 + §18.6. RoE (overseas-entity beneficial-ownership disclosure to Companies House) and ATED (annual tax return + payment to HMRC) are parallel regimes. An overseas company holding a UK dwelling must comply with both. RoE non-compliance separately blocks Land Registry dispositions (a transactional bite) but does not pause the ATED return clock.
9. **The "demolition relief covers a long redevelopment" myth.** s.134-135 demolition / conversion relief requires demonstrable intention to demolish or convert to non-residential — a "we may redevelop someday" position fails. The relief evidence pattern requires planning applications, contractor engagements, programme dates. Prevention: don't claim ss.134-135 without contemporaneous redevelopment-intention evidence.
10. **The "mixed-use is residential below the commercial threshold" myth.** Wrong. Mixed-use ATED applies to the residential element where that element is worth >£500,000 (HP §18.4). The whole-property mixed-use SDLT rate question is separate from the ATED apportionment question. Prevention: where a single building has a >£500,000 residential element, apportion just-and-reasonably; do not assume mixed-use defeats the ATED scope.
11. **The "offshore Cat-2 / Cat-3 uplift doesn't apply if the company is UK-tax-resident" myth.** FA 2015 Sch 21 offshore-matter uplift applies to the LOCATION OF THE MATTER or LOCATION OF THE TRANSFER, not the residency of the filer. An overseas-corporate ATED filer with offshore banking arrangements faces Cat-2 / Cat-3 even on UK-source ATED matters where the underlying records or proceeds sit offshore. Sessions tax-tips copy walks the territory analysis at penalty time.
12. **The "voluntary disclosure under WDF resolves ATED" myth.** WDF (Worldwide Disclosure Facility) is for offshore income / capital / IHT non-compliance. ATED-specific historical non-compliance routes through the **ATED OTM-letter response** track (HP §18.7) or through Sch 33 para 3 amendment (if window open) / Sch 33 para 4 HMRC correction request / Sch 33 para 21 discovery response. Prevention: use the right disclosure track for ATED — not the generic WDF.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. **Who is in scope for ATED?** (Non-natural persons holding UK dwellings >£500k per s.94 + HP §18.2. UK companies + non-UK companies + partnerships with at least one corporate member + collective investment schemes. NOT individuals, NOT trustees (provided no corporate trustee structure), NOT charities holding on charitable trusts subject to relief.)
2. **Why must a return be filed even where a relief covers the whole period?** (ss.133-150 reliefs are claimed on the return per HP §18.3. There is no automatic application. Claim-only return discipline + 30 April deadline + Sch 55 penalty regime apply.)
3. **What happens on 1 April 2027 and which period does it affect?** (Crown valuation date per s.102 — but per s.102(2A) FA 2015 on-ramp, it does NOT operate during the 1 April 2027 - 31 March 2028 chargeable period. It operates from 1 April 2028 - 31 March 2029 (return filed by 30 April 2028 using the 1 April 2027 valuation). Plan RICS valuation work for 2027 calendar year.)
4. **When does a substantial acquisition create a fresh valuation date?** (Per s.103 — chargeable consideration ≥£40,000. Below threshold, no fresh valuation event. Connected-party linked-transaction rules can aggregate sub-£40k acquisitions.)
5. **What is the s.133 two-conditions architecture?** (Commercial basis + view to profit AND no non-qualifying individual occupation per s.136. Both must hold. Market rent satisfies the first; the s.136 eight-category catalogue blocks the second for connected occupancy.)
6. **Is ATED-CGT still in force?** (No — abolished 6 April 2019. Gains on enveloped dwellings now fall under standard NRCGT at TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A. HP §18.5 + §18.8.)
7. **How long does the Sch 33 para 3 amendment window run?** (End of the next chargeable period after the period to which the return relates, with 3-months-from-delivery extension under sub-(4) for returns filed on/after 1 January in the following period. NOT 12 months from filing date.)
8. **What is the Pre-Return Banding Check (PRBC) and when should I use it?** (HMRC's non-binding pre-return view of the band, no cost, for dwellings within 10% of a band boundary. Use it for borderline-value dwellings before the 30 April deadline.)
9. **Does RoE compliance discharge ATED obligations?** (No — they are parallel regimes. ATED return + payment to HMRC. RoE annual update to Companies House. Both required.)
10. **What is the offshore Cat-2 / Cat-3 penalty uplift?** (FA 2015 Sch 21 — 1.5x (Cat-2) or 2x (Cat-3) on standard Sch 24 inaccuracy percentages where the inaccuracy concerns an offshore matter or offshore transfer. Applies even to UK-tax-resident filers where records / proceeds sit offshore.)
11. **What disclosure track should I use for historical ATED non-compliance?** (ATED OTM-letter response track per HP §18.7, OR Sch 33 para 3 amendment if window open, OR Sch 33 para 4 HMRC correction request, OR direct correspondence with HMRC's ATED team. WDF (Worldwide Disclosure Facility) is for offshore income / capital / IHT — not ATED.)
12. **What documentation discipline supports a defensible ATED position?** (RICS Red Book valuation at each operative valuation date; tenancy agreements for s.133 rental relief; commercial-basis-and-view-to-profit evidence (rent at market level, voids documented, profit-making intent); s.136 catalogue evidence for any related-person query; PRBC submission for borderline bands; redevelopment-intention evidence for ss.134-135 demolition / conversion reliefs; employee-accommodation contracts for s.145 + s.146 + s.147 carve-outs.)

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The 5-question ATED pre-acquisition checklist (the page's first concrete artefact)

Render as semantic HTML `<ol>`. This is the prevention-architecture-anchor that owners run BEFORE acquiring a £500k+ UK dwelling via a non-natural person:

1. **Will the property be owned by a non-natural person on the operative valuation date?** (FA 2013 s.94 + HP §18.2: UK company / non-UK company / partnership with at least one corporate member / collective investment scheme. If YES, ATED scope test triggered.)
2. **Is the residential element worth more than £500,000 on the operative valuation date?** (FA 2013 s.94 floor. For mixed-use, just-and-reasonable apportion under s.117 + HP §18.4. If YES, ATED chargeable amount applies subject to reliefs.)
3. **Will any relief under FA 2013 ss.133-150 apply throughout the chargeable period?** (HP §18.3 catalogue: s.133 rental + s.134 transitional + s.137 open-to-public + s.138 developer + s.141 trader + s.144 farmhouse + s.145 employee + s.149 social housing + s.150 charitable. Check eligibility BEFORE acquisition; document the relief route + qualifying evidence.)
4. **Is the dwelling within 10% of a band boundary at the operative valuation date?** (HP §18.4 PRBC: if YES, plan to file a Pre-Return Banding Check before the 30 April return deadline; commission a RICS Red Book valuation contemporaneous to the operative valuation date.)
5. **Are there parallel-regime obligations?** (Overseas-incorporated owner → RoE under ECTEA 2022 (HP §11). Dwelling held in connected-person scenario → s.135 / s.136 clawback exposure (B11 / B12). Substantial-acquisition triggers → s.103 £40k valuation-event analysis (B14). Compliance audit required before completion.)

### Example 2 — The "ATED is for overseas companies only" mistake walked

Trevor Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co; sole director + shareholder Mr Trevor; UK-tax-resident; acquired a £1.4m London BTL flat in 2023 via the ltd-co for buy-to-let income). Mr Trevor's accountant filed CT600 each year but never flagged ATED — common assumption: "ATED is only for non-resident companies".

**Architecture analysis (per HP §18.2 + FA 2013 s.94):**

- Trevor Holdings Limited is a UK COMPANY → non-natural person.
- Holds a UK single-dwelling interest worth >£500k on the 1 April 2022 operative valuation date.
- Within ATED scope under s.94 — UK-tax-resident status is irrelevant; the non-natural-person test is the gateway.

**ATED tax position (assume £1.4m valuation, band 2 £1m-£2m):**

- 2023/24 chargeable amount: £8,650 (2023/24 band 2 per indexed table — Stage 2 verifies at write).
- 2024/25 chargeable amount: £9,150 (per HP §18.1 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22).
- 2025/26 chargeable amount: £9,150.
- 2026/27 chargeable amount: £9,450.

**Relief eligibility check:**

- Trevor Holdings lets the flat to Sarah Quayle (unconnected professional contact, market rent £45k/year, standard AST). Per FA 2013 s.133 two-conditions analysis (commercial-basis + no non-qualifying-individual occupation per s.136): both conditions met. **s.133 rental relief AVAILABLE.**
- If properly claimed on the return, ATED = £0 for each period.

**Mistake consequence (4 years of un-filed returns):**

- Sch 55 late-filing penalty per missed return: £100 immediate + £200 at 3 months + £300 at 6 months + £300 at 12 months = £900 per return × 4 missed returns = £3,600.
- Plus Sch 24 inaccuracy considerations for any unfiled-but-tax-due years (here, £0 tax due due to s.133 relief if properly claimed; Sch 24 unlikely to apply on £0 PLR).
- Plus interest on any tax due (here, minimal — relief eliminates the underlying charge).

**Remediation route:**

- File overdue returns under Sch 33 para 3 (within-window for recent periods) + voluntary disclosure for out-of-window periods via direct ATED-team correspondence.
- Claim s.133 relief on each return; document s.136 catalogue walk for Sarah Quayle (unconnected) for each period.
- Engage with HMRC's reasonable-excuse position (Perrin v HMRC framework) for Sch 55 penalty mitigation — "honest mistake; new-acquisition unfamiliarity with the regime; immediate correction on discovery".

**Prevention pattern:** before any UK ltd-co acquires a £500k+ UK residential dwelling, run the 5-question pre-acquisition checklist (Example 1). UK-resident company status is NOT a defence to ATED scope.

### Example 3 — The "reliefs are automatic" mistake walked

Olivetti Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co; owns a £900k Manchester BTL flat acquired 2020; let throughout to an unconnected tenant on a standard AST at market rent). Mr Olivetti's accountant assumed s.133 rental relief applied automatically because "the flat is let" and did not file any ATED returns.

**Architecture analysis:**

- Olivetti Holdings is in ATED scope (UK company, £900k > £500k floor, non-natural-person holder).
- s.133 relief is available (both conditions met) but **MUST BE CLAIMED ON THE RETURN per FA 2013 s.159 + Sch 33 para 1** (HP §18.3 + §18.8 do-not-write list).
- "Claim-only return" discipline applies: the return is required EVEN WHERE the chargeable amount after relief is £0.

**Mistake consequence (5 years of un-filed claim-only returns):**

- Sch 55 late-filing penalty per missed return: £900 (£100 + £200 + £300 + £300 ladder) × 5 = **£4,500**.
- No underlying tax due (s.133 covers fully), but Sch 55 escalator runs independently of tax position.
- HMRC OTM letter may surface this 3-5 years into the non-filing pattern.

**Remediation route:**

- File overdue claim-only returns + claim s.133 relief on each.
- Reasonable-excuse argument for Sch 55 penalty mitigation: "honest mistake based on adviser assumption that relief was automatic; immediate correction on discovery". Perrin v HMRC four-stage framework.
- Expect partial-mitigation; some Sch 55 penalty likely sticks where the "honest mistake" cannot be sustained for the full 5-year period (responsibility to confirm filing obligations is the chargeable person's, not just the adviser's).

**Prevention pattern:** the "30 April ATED return discipline" applies WHETHER OR NOT tax is due. Diary entry for each chargeable period; instruct adviser to file claim-only returns wherever relief is being claimed.

### Example 4 — The "ATED-CGT applies to gains over £500k" mistake walked

Saffron Estates Cayman Limited (Cayman-incorporated; held a £4.2m London townhouse since 2015; sold in March 2026 for £5.8m, realising a £1.6m gain). Saffron's adviser cites the old 28% ATED-CGT rate and prepares a CGT calculation on that basis.

**Architecture analysis (per HP §18.5 + §18.8 + FA 2019 abolition):**

- ATED-CGT was **abolished 6 April 2019**. The 28% ATED-CGT rate has not been operative for 7 years.
- Saffron's disposal in March 2026 falls under standard NRCGT at TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A (HP §17.4).
- For a non-UK-resident company disposing of UK residential property: NRCGT rates per TCGA 1992 s.1A — 18% / 24% for individuals, but for COMPANIES the disposal is treated as a CT chargeable gain (with the 25% main CT rate post-FY2023 if profits over £250k or CIHC).

**Correct calculation:**

- Acquisition cost: £4.2m (2015).
- Disposal proceeds: £5.8m (March 2026).
- Gain: £1.6m.
- For a CIHC non-UK-resident corporate landlord (likely CIHC given single-asset envelope structure): 25% CT × £1.6m = **£400,000 CT** (subject to incidental costs + indexation if pre-2017 base cost claim, depending on rebasing rules).
- The 28% old-ATED-CGT rate would have produced £448,000 — but it doesn't apply. The 25% CT under NRCGT is the correct rate.
- For an SPR-eligible non-resident corporate landlord (rare for ATED-relevant single-asset structures): 19% CT × £1.6m = £304,000.

**Remediation if 28% applied:**

- £48,000 overpayment (£448,000 – £400,000) recoverable via amendment + repayment claim under FA 2009 s.102 with statutory interest.
- Adviser engagement required: correct the calculation; refile if necessary; document the correct NRCGT-CT treatment.

**Prevention pattern:** sessions writing on enveloped-dwelling disposals MUST cite TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A as the operative successor regime. The 28% ATED-CGT figure should not appear in current ATED prevention copy except as a historical reference. Any adviser citing 28% ATED-CGT is at least 7 years out of date.

### Example 5 — The "1 April 2027 revaluation bites 2027/28" mistake walked

Ironwood Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co; owns a £3.4m London flat acquired May 2018). Mr Ironwood's adviser instructs Ironwood to commission a 1 April 2027 RICS Red Book valuation in early 2027 "for use in the 2027/28 ATED return filed by 30 April 2027". Mr Ironwood pays the £8,000 RICS valuation fee and the adviser files the 2027/28 return on 30 April 2027 using the 2027 valuation.

**Architecture analysis (per FA 2013 s.102(2A) FA 2015 on-ramp + HP §18.4 + B14 seed verified verbatim 2026-05-27):**

- FA 2013 s.102(2A): "The five-yearly valuation dates do not apply when determining taxable value during the chargeable period beginning on that date."
- The 1 April 2027 Crown date does NOT operate during the 2027/28 chargeable period (1 April 2027 - 31 March 2028).
- For the 2027/28 chargeable period, the operative valuation date is the PRIOR date (1 April 2022 Crown date, or the most recent intervening substantial-acquisition / partnership / CIS event).
- Ironwood's 1 April 2022 RICS valuation (assume £3.2m → band 3 £2m-£5m) governs the 2027/28 ATED charge.
- The 1 April 2027 valuation FIRST applies to the 2028/29 chargeable period (return filed by 30 April 2028).

**Mistake consequence:**

- Adviser's £8,000 RICS valuation fee was procedurally premature (still valuable for 2028/29 but a year early).
- The 2027/28 return filed using the 2027 valuation is technically wrong — the operative date should have been 1 April 2022.
- If the 2027 valuation produced a higher band (e.g. £5.2m → band 4 £5m-£10m), Ironwood OVERPAID the 2027/28 ATED. Refund recoverable via Sch 33 para 3 amendment within window.
- If the 2027 valuation produced a lower band (e.g. £2.9m → band 3 same as 2022 valuation), no immediate harm — but the adviser's understanding of the on-ramp clause is wrong and may produce errors in future periods.

**Remediation route:**

- Refile the 2027/28 return correctly using the 1 April 2022 valuation under Sch 33 para 3 amendment.
- Reclaim any overpaid tax + statutory interest under FA 2009 s.102.
- Use the 1 April 2027 RICS valuation for the 2028/29 return (filed 30 April 2028).

**Prevention pattern:** sessions writing on the 2027 revaluation timing MUST cite s.102(2A) FA 2015 verbatim. The 2027 RICS valuation is for the 2028/29 return, not the 2027/28 return. This is one of the most common practitioner-side drift errors in the ATED architecture — competitor pages, advisers, and even some HMRC training materials get it wrong.

### Example 6 — Misframings the page must correct (verbatim do-not-write list)

- **Misframe 1:** "ATED only applies to non-resident companies." FALSE per FA 2013 s.94 + HP §18.2 — ATED applies to non-natural persons (UK companies + non-UK companies + partnerships with corporate member + CIS), regardless of tax-residency. UK BTL ltd-cos with director-purchased £500k+ flats are equally in scope.
- **Misframe 2:** "Reliefs apply automatically where the dwelling meets the relief conditions." FALSE per FA 2013 s.159 + Sch 33 para 1 + HP §18.3 — reliefs under ss.133-150 must be CLAIMED on the return. Claim-only returns are still returns. Failure to file is a Sch 55 penalty event regardless of whether tax is due.
- **Misframe 3:** "ATED-related CGT applies to gains on enveloped dwellings at 28%." FALSE per FA 2019 abolition + HP §18.5 + §18.8 — ATED-related CGT was abolished 6 April 2019. Gains on enveloped dwellings now fall under standard NRCGT at TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A. For non-resident corporate landlords: CT at 19-25% applies via the non-resident chargeable gains route. The 28% rate is 7 years stale and should not appear in current ATED content.
- **Misframe 4:** "The 1 April 2027 revaluation bites the 2027/28 chargeable period." FALSE per FA 2013 s.102(2A) FA 2015 on-ramp clause — the 5-yearly Crown date does NOT operate during the chargeable period beginning on it. The 1 April 2027 valuation bites from 2028/29 onwards. The 2027/28 period uses the prior valuation date (1 April 2022 Crown date or most recent intervening event).
- **Misframe 5:** "The Sch 33 amendment window is 12 months from filing." FALSE per FA 2013 Sch 33 para 3(3) — the operative deadline is end of the next chargeable period after the period to which the return relates, with a 3-months-from-delivery extension under sub-(4) for late filers. The 12-months-from-filing analogy is borrowed from TMA 1970 s.9ZA SA practice and is wrong for ATED.
- **Misframe 6:** "Letting to family at market rent unlocks s.133 rental relief." FALSE per FA 2013 s.133 two-conditions + s.136 catalogue — s.133 has TWO independent conditions: (a) commercial basis + view to profit (market rent gateway) AND (b) no non-qualifying-individual occupation per s.136. The s.136 eight-category catalogue blocks relief regardless of rent level for connected occupancy. Both conditions must hold.
- **Misframe 7:** "The PRBC is only for overseas filers or for expensive bands." FALSE per HP §18.4 + HMRC PRBC guidance — PRBC is available at no cost to ANY chargeable person whose valuation is within 10% of a band boundary, regardless of filer residency or band level. A £2.05m flat near the £2m boundary is just as eligible as a £20m flat near the £20m boundary.
- **Misframe 8:** "RoE compliance discharges the ATED return obligation." FALSE per HP §11 + §18.6 — RoE (Register of Overseas Entities under ECTEA 2022) and ATED (annual tax return + payment to HMRC) are parallel regimes. Overseas-incorporated owners of UK dwellings must comply with both. RoE failure separately blocks Land Registry dispositions but does not pause the ATED return clock.
- **Misframe 9:** "Demolition relief (s.134) covers a long redevelopment programme." MISLEADING per FA 2013 s.134 — the relief requires demonstrable intention to demolish or convert to non-residential, with "without undue delay" discipline (delay only "justified by commercial considerations or cannot be avoided"). "We may redevelop someday" without planning applications / contractor engagements / programme dates fails the evidential test.
- **Misframe 10:** "Mixed-use property escapes ATED if the commercial element exceeds 50%." FALSE per FA 2013 s.117 + HP §18.4 — ATED applies to the RESIDENTIAL ELEMENT where that element is worth >£500,000. The commercial-vs-residential proportion does NOT determine ATED scope; the residential-element value does. Just-and-reasonable apportionment (floor-area or value-based) determines the residential-element value for s.102 valuation purposes.
- **Misframe 11:** "Offshore Cat-2 / Cat-3 uplift doesn't apply to UK-resident filers." FALSE per FA 2015 Sch 21 — the offshore uplift applies to the LOCATION OF THE MATTER or LOCATION OF THE TRANSFER, not the residency of the filer. An overseas-corporate ATED filer with offshore banking, decision-making, or records faces Cat-2 / Cat-3 even on UK-source ATED matters. The territory analysis is done at the matter level.
- **Misframe 12:** "Voluntary disclosure under the WDF resolves ATED non-compliance." FALSE — WDF (Worldwide Disclosure Facility) is for offshore income / capital / IHT non-compliance under the FA 2017 Sch 18 framework. ATED-specific historical non-compliance routes through the ATED OTM-letter response track (HP §18.7), Sch 33 para 3 amendment (within window), Sch 33 para 4 HMRC correction request, or direct correspondence with HMRC's ATED team. Use the right disclosure track.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: My UK ltd-co holds a £900k London BTL flat. Do I need to worry about ATED?**
   A: YES. Per FA 2013 s.94 + HP §18.2: ATED applies to non-natural persons (including UK companies) holding UK dwellings worth >£500k. UK-resident company status is NOT a defence. Run the 5-question pre-acquisition checklist (Example 1 above) and check whether s.133 rental relief (or any other relief) applies. Even if relief covers fully, file the 30 April claim-only return to avoid Sch 55 penalty escalator.

2. **Q: My flat is let, so the s.133 rental relief applies. I don't need to file anything, right?**
   A: WRONG. Per FA 2013 s.159 + Sch 33 para 1 + HP §18.3 + §18.8: reliefs MUST BE CLAIMED on the ATED return. There is no automatic application. Even where the relief is full and tax due is £0, the claim-only return is required by 30 April each year. Failure to file triggers Sch 55: £100 + £200 + £300 + £300 = £900 per missed return.

3. **Q: I'm selling my company's £4m London flat. Do I pay the 28% ATED-related CGT?**
   A: NO. ATED-related CGT was ABOLISHED 6 April 2019 (HP §18.5). Disposals now fall under standard NRCGT at TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A. For a non-resident corporate landlord, the disposal is taxed as a CT chargeable gain at 19-25% (depending on company size + CIHC status). The 28% figure is 7 years stale.

4. **Q: When does the 1 April 2027 revaluation start affecting my ATED bill?**
   A: From the 2028/29 chargeable period — NOT 2027/28. Per FA 2013 s.102(2A) FA 2015 on-ramp clause: the 5-yearly Crown date does NOT operate during the chargeable period beginning on it. Your 2027/28 chargeable period (1 April 2027 - 31 March 2028) continues to use the prior valuation date. Commission your 1 April 2027 RICS Red Book valuation within 2027 calendar year for use in the 2028/29 return filed by 30 April 2028.

5. **Q: How long do I have to amend an ATED return?**
   A: Until the END of the next chargeable period after the period to which the return relates, per FA 2013 Sch 33 para 3(3). So a 2025/26 return can be amended until 31 March 2027. Sub-(4) extension: if the return was filed on/after 1 January in the following chargeable period, the window extends to 3 months from delivery. NOT 12 months from filing (that's the self-assessment rule under TMA 1970 s.9ZA — a different regime).

6. **Q: I want to let our company's £1.4m flat to my daughter at full market rent. Will the s.133 relief apply?**
   A: NO, almost certainly. FA 2013 s.133 has TWO conditions: (a) commercial basis + view to profit (market rent satisfies); (b) no non-qualifying-individual occupation per s.136. Your daughter is a relative of a connected person under s.136 Category (e) = non-qualifying individual. Condition (b) fails regardless of rent level. Full ATED charge applies + CIHC consequences + s.135 clawback exposure across 4 years (B11 + B12 deep-dives).

7. **Q: My flat is valued at £2.05m — should I do a PRBC?**
   A: YES. The Pre-Return Banding Check (PRBC) is available at no cost for valuations within 10% of a band boundary (your £2.05m is 2.5% above the £2m boundary). The PRBC asks HMRC to confirm the band (£1m-£2m £9,450 vs £2m-£5m £32,200 for 2026/27 — a £22,750 difference). Submit a contemporaneous RICS Red Book valuation alongside the PRBC request well before the 30 April return deadline.

8. **Q: I'm RoE-compliant. That covers ATED, right?**
   A: NO. RoE (Register of Overseas Entities under ECTEA 2022, HP §11) and ATED (annual return + payment to HMRC under FA 2013 Part 3) are PARALLEL regimes. Both must be complied with separately. RoE failure separately blocks Land Registry dispositions; ATED failure separately triggers Sch 55 + Sch 24 + Sch 21 offshore penalty stack. Different obligations; different consequences.

9. **Q: Can I claim s.134 demolition relief if I'm thinking about redeveloping?**
   A: NO, not without contemporaneous redevelopment-intention evidence. FA 2013 s.134 requires demonstrable intention to demolish or convert to non-residential, with "without undue delay" discipline. "Thinking about it" fails. Documentary evidence required: planning applications, contractor engagements, programme dates, architects' instructions, demolition surveys. Without contemporaneous evidence, the relief claim is exposed to HMRC challenge + Sch 24 inaccuracy penalty.

10. **Q: My company is UK-tax-resident. Cat-2 / Cat-3 offshore uplift doesn't apply to me, right?**
    A: NOT NECESSARILY. FA 2015 Sch 21 offshore uplift applies to the LOCATION OF THE MATTER or LOCATION OF THE TRANSFER, not the residency of the filer. If your company has offshore bank accounts, offshore decision-makers, or records held offshore, Cat-2 / Cat-3 may apply even on UK-source ATED matters. The territory analysis is done at the matter level. Specialist tax advice essential.

11. **Q: For ATED historical non-compliance disclosure, do I use the Worldwide Disclosure Facility (WDF)?**
    A: NO. WDF is for offshore income / capital / IHT non-compliance under FA 2017 Sch 18. For ATED-specific historical non-compliance, the routes are: (a) ATED OTM-letter response track per HP §18.7; (b) Sch 33 para 3 amendment if within window; (c) Sch 33 para 4 HMRC obvious-error correction request; (d) direct correspondence with HMRC's ATED team. Use the right track.

12. **Q: What documentation do I need to defend my ATED position under HMRC enquiry?**
    A: (a) RICS Red Book valuation at each operative valuation date; (b) tenancy agreements + market-rent comparables + commercial-basis evidence for s.133; (c) s.136 catalogue walk for any related-person query; (d) PRBC submission for borderline-band cases; (e) redevelopment-intention evidence (planning applications, contractor engagements) for ss.134-135 claims; (f) employee-accommodation contracts + s.146 / s.147 controlling-interest analysis for s.145 claims. Contemporaneous documentation supports the Sch 33 para 1(3) "correct and complete to the best of knowledge" declaration.

13. **Q: How do I keep on top of the ATED 30 April deadline?**
    A: Diary entries in your tax-compliance calendar: (a) 1 February each year — confirm RICS valuation is current (operative valuation date per s.102 analysis); (b) 1 March — submit any PRBC for borderline-band cases; (c) 1 April — chargeable period begins; (d) 15 April — internal sign-off on return + relief claim documentation; (e) 30 April — file return + pay tax (or claim-only return if relief applies). Repeat annually. Set diary against the chargeable-period boundary, NOT against the prior year's filing date.

14. **Q: We're moving family into the company-owned flat for a year. What does that do to our ATED position?**
    A: Significant exposure. Family occupancy triggers s.136 non-qualifying-individual occupation → s.133 relief denied for those days → full ATED charge applies. PLUS s.135 clawback: the look-forward extends up to 3 subsequent chargeable periods unless a qualifying-use day intervenes; the look-back unwinds earlier periods unless a qualifying-use day intervenes. PLUS CIHC effect at CTA 2010 s.18N(3): the company becomes a CIHC; 25% main CT rate locked in regardless of profit level. The combined exposure typically exceeds £30-50k/year for a £1-2m flat. Consider de-envelope (B5 strategic decision) BEFORE moving family in.
