# A Complete Guide to Periodic Tenancy (After the Renters' Rights Act 2025)

## Statutory anchor
- Primary (pre-existing periodic-tenancy law): Housing Act 1988 s.5 — "Security of tenure" — converts a fixed-term assured shorthold tenancy (AST) to a **statutory periodic tenancy** on expiry of the fixed term where the tenant remains in occupation. The mechanism predates the Renters' Rights Act 2025 and continues to operate for tenancies outside the RRA reform window.
- Primary (RRA 2025 default-periodic regime): Renters' Rights Act 2025 (2025 c. 26) — Royal Assent 27 October 2025; commencement phased. The substantive tenancy-reform Part (the periodic-from-grant default) was brought into force by SI 2026/421 (Commencement No. 2 and Transitional and Saving Provisions Regulations 2026) on 1 May 2026. **All new assured tenancies are periodic from grant; existing fixed-term ASTs convert to periodic on commencement.** §20.3 lock.
- Primary (rent period and rent-rise mechanic): RRA 2025 s.6 — "Statutory procedure for increases of rent" — restricts rent increases to the Section 13 HA 1988 route, once per 12-month period, with 2 months' written notice; tribunal-only challenge route. §20.6 lock.
- Primary (advance-rent prohibitions): RRA 2025 s.8 (inserting HA 1988 s.4B — post-tenancy advance-rent terms of no effect) + RRA 2025 s.9 (amending Tenant Fees Act 2019 — pre-tenancy advance-rent prohibited payment). §20.8 lock.
- Primary (tenant notice period): RRA 2025 s.5 (amending Protection from Eviction Act 1977 to insert PEA 1977 s.5(1ZA)) — tenants can end the tenancy with 2 months' written notice. §20.2 lock.
- Primary (section 21 abolition + reformed section 8): RRA 2025 s.2 ("Abolition of assured shorthold tenancies", omits HA 1988 Pt.1 Ch.2 + s.6A) and RRA 2025 s.3 (Sch 1 reformed possession grounds). §20.2 lock.
- Primary (rent-receipt-basis tax recognition for landlords): ITTOIA 2005 s.271A (cash basis default for individual landlords under £150,000 property + trading turnover) + s.272A-272B (finance cost restriction). §4 lock.
- Supporting (deposit protection): Housing Act 2004 ss.212-215 + Tenancy Deposit Schemes Order 2007 — three authorised schemes (DPS, TDS, mydeposits); 30-day deposit-information window; failure penalty up to 3x deposit + bar on Section 21 notices (the bar is moot post-RRA-2025 commencement because s.21 is abolished, but the 3x deposit penalty survives).
- Supporting (CT base-cost mechanics on disposal): TCGA 1992 s.222 + s.223 (PPR), s.1H (CGT residential rates). §5 lock.
- House position reference: §20.3 (periodic-tenancy default + AST phase-out) is the canonical lock; §20.2 (s.21 abolition + reformed s.8); §20.6 (rent-rise mechanics + Section 13); §20.8 (advance-rent prohibitions); §20.9 (transition for existing tenancies); §20.11 (PTP tax-side hooks); §20.12 (commencement timeline locked to SI 2025/1354 + SI 2026/421); §26.1 (RRA 2025 pointer + naming discipline). **No new HP lock needed.** F-15 (HA 1988 Sch 1 paras 3D / 3E — 21+ year carve-out) and F-14 (s.5(1ZA) statutory hook for tenant notice) corrections already in §20.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What is a periodic tenancy in one paragraph** — a tenancy where the tenant has the right to occupy on a recurring rent-period basis (monthly, in practice, since RRA 2025 caps the rent period at monthly), with no fixed end date set in the contract. The tenant ends it by giving notice; the landlord ends it by serving a reformed Section 8 notice on one of the statutory grounds. Distinguished from a fixed-term tenancy (where both parties are bound to the end date) and from a tenancy at will (where either party can end immediately).
2. **The four routes by which a tenancy becomes periodic** — (a) **periodic from grant** (default under RRA 2025 s.2 omitting HA 1988 Pt.1 Ch.2 + the periodic-default mechanic; in force 1 May 2026 per SI 2026/421); (b) **statutory periodic** on expiry of a pre-RRA AST fixed term under HA 1988 s.5 (still operative for tenancies outside the RRA reform window or where the fixed term has expired before the tenant continues in occupation); (c) **contractual periodic** where the tenancy agreement itself describes a rolling tenancy with no fixed term (less common post-RRA because contractual fixed-term terms over 6 months are no longer enforceable in the assured regime); (d) **common-law periodic** where there is no written tenancy agreement and the tenant has been paying rent on a recurring basis with the landlord's acceptance (the rent acceptance creates an implied periodic tenancy by conduct).
3. **What changed on 1 May 2026** — the major SI 2026/421 commencement brought the periodic-from-grant default into force. From that date, every new assured tenancy in England is periodic from the day it is granted; every existing fixed-term AST converts to periodic; six-monthly or annual rent periods are no longer permitted (RRA 2025 s.6 + §20.3 lock). Section 21 no-fault eviction is abolished by RRA 2025 s.2; possession is by reformed Section 8 grounds only.
4. **What is the standard rent period now** — monthly. RRA 2025 caps the rent period at one month; six-monthly and annual rent contracts are unenforceable. This shifts the cashflow rhythm for landlords who previously took rent quarterly or six-monthly (typical at the higher end of the market). Rent is collectible monthly; the cash-basis rental-income recognition under ITTOIA 2005 s.271A follows the actual receipt point.
5. **Tenant notice rights** — the tenant can end the tenancy at any point with 2 months' written notice (PEA 1977 s.5(1ZA) inserted by RRA 2025 s.5). No minimum-occupation lock-in; no requirement to wait until a "natural end" of a notional fixed term. Operational consequence for landlords: shorter average tenancy length, more void-period exposure, higher re-let costs across a portfolio over time.
6. **Landlord notice rights — possession via reformed Section 8** — 17 grounds restructured (§20.2 lock). Selected operative grounds: Ground 8 (8 weeks' rent arrears) + Ground 10/11 (persistent arrears) for rent-arrears possession; Ground 12 (breach of tenancy obligation); Ground 14 (anti-social behaviour); new landlord-sale ground (with 12-month re-letting restriction under HA 1988 s.16E inserted by RRA 2025 s.15); new landlord-occupation ground (same 12-month restriction). Notice periods vary by ground (2 weeks for Ground 14; 4 weeks for landlord-sale; up to 4 months for some structural grounds). Forward-link to the Section 8 grounds deep-dive page.
7. **Rent rises on a periodic tenancy** — Section 13 procedure only. One rent increase per 12-month period; 2 months' written notice on the prescribed form; tenant can challenge at the First-Tier Tribunal (Property Chamber); tribunal cannot set rent above the landlord's proposed amount. Contractual rent-review clauses are unenforceable for rent increases (§20.6 lock). Forward-link to the Section 13 deep-dive.
8. **Advance rent and the deposit-substitute trap** — RRA 2025 s.8 + s.9 abolish the practice of demanding 6 or 12 months' rent upfront as a deposit-substitute. Pre-tenancy: any pre-lease rent payment is a prohibited payment under the Tenant Fees Act 2019 (RRA 2025 s.9 amendment). Post-tenancy: terms providing for rent due in advance of the rent period are of no effect (HA 1988 s.4B inserted by RRA 2025 s.8), with narrow carve-outs for an "initial 28-day period" and the "permitted pre-tenancy period" (the gap between contract execution and the first day of the tenancy). §20.8 lock.
9. **Deposit on a periodic tenancy** — five-week-rent maximum under the Tenant Fees Act 2019 (six weeks where annual rent exceeds £50,000); must be protected in an authorised scheme (DPS, TDS, or mydeposits) within 30 days under HA 2004 ss.212-215, with the prescribed information served on the tenant in the same window. Failure exposes the landlord to a court-ordered penalty of 1x to 3x the deposit. The post-RRA-2025 bar on Section 21 notices is no longer the operative consequence (s.21 is abolished); the 3x penalty survives as the deterrent.
10. **Tax-side recognition for landlords on a periodic tenancy** — (a) rental income recognised on the cash basis (ITTOIA 2005 s.271A) at the point of receipt; monthly rent period aligns the receipt rhythm with the tax-recognition rhythm. (b) Vacant-period costs (council tax, utilities, building insurance, mortgage interest) remain deductible during void periods between tenancies as expenses of the property business — voids do not break the property-business continuity for tax purposes. (c) Mortgage interest under the s.24 restriction (§4 lock) is unaffected by tenancy type. (d) On eventual sale, CGT base cost is unaffected by tenancy type; the let-out-while-living-elsewhere position triggers the s.223 final-9-month exemption (TCGA 1992) at the end of ownership only, not over the tenancy period. (e) Section 13 rent-rise cadence interacts with cashflow modelling: where mortgage interest rises faster than the annual rent uplift permitted, the s.24 reducer may bind earlier (the §20.11 lock cross-reference). Forward-link to the s.24 cluster and the void-period deductions cluster.
