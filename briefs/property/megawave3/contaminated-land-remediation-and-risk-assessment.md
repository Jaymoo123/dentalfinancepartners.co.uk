# Contaminated land remediation and risk assessment: the ATED relief architecture for contaminated dwellings, the LRR financial relief cross-reference, and the source-pathway-receptor risk-assessment precondition

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (the ATED-side reliefs that bite on contaminated dwellings):**
  - **FA 2013 s.134** "Property rental businesses: transitional relief" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Relief applies where the dwelling is unoccupied and one of four conditions is met: (1) "steps are being taken to secure that the interest will be sold without undue delay"; (2) "steps are being taken to demolish the dwelling" with any replacement constructed for "relievable" use; (3) "steps are being taken to convert the dwelling into a different dwelling" for relievable use; (4) "steps are being taken to secure that the dwelling will be converted into a building other than a dwelling without undue delay". Day X qualifies for s.134 relief where preceded by s.133 qualifying-rental-business days. "Without undue delay" is defined as delay only "justified by commercial considerations or cannot be avoided". No explicit overall time limit but the "without undue delay" anchor disciplines long-pause cases.
  - **FA 2013 s.138** "Property developers" — dwelling held as trading stock by a property developer carrying on a property development trade for at least 2 years (HP §18.3). Available for contaminated-land sites being prepared for development as trading stock.
  - **FA 2013 s.141** "Property traders" — dwelling held as trading stock by a property trader (buy-sell business). Available where contaminated-land remediation is incidental to a property-trading business.
  - **FA 2013 s.137** "Dwellings open to the public" — commercially run and open to public for ≥28 days/year; rarely operative for contaminated sites but Stage 2 considers.
- **Primary (the LRR financial relief on remediation expenditure):**
  - **CTA 2009 Part 14 (ss.143-218)** "Remediation of contaminated or derelict land" — the operative Land Remediation Relief (LRR) statute. **s.143** establishes "qualifying land remediation expenditure" requirements: expenditure on relevant land in a contaminated or derelict state, on relevant remediation activities, that is allowable as a deduction in computing profits. **100% deduction** for qualifying expenditure (current CT-period deduction) PLUS **50% additional deduction** under s.1149 CTA 2009 / s.1147 — total **150% relief** for qualifying LRR claimants.
  - **CTA 2009 s.146** "Polluter exclusion" — LRR is denied where the claimant or a connected person caused the contamination. Sessions writing on LRR-and-developer pages must walk this exclusion: a developer who buys contaminated land from an arms-length seller can claim; a developer who contaminated the site itself, or who is connected to the polluter, cannot.
  - **CTA 2009 s.1149** "Additional deduction for qualifying land remediation expenditure" — the 50% additional deduction mechanic.
  - **CTA 2009 s.1147** "Entitlement to relief: company" — the entitlement provisions.
  - Existing site pages on LRR detail: `land-remediation-relief-150-percent-claim-mechanics-ltdco-developer-investor` and `land-remediation-relief-cta-2009-part-14-100-50-percent-deduction-polluter-exclusion`. Stage 2 cross-links rather than duplicating.
- **Primary (the contaminated-land regulatory regime that defines the underlying "contaminated" status):**
  - **Environmental Protection Act 1990 Part 2A (ss.78A-78YC)** — the statutory contaminated-land regime. **s.78A** defines "contaminated land" as land that appears to the local authority to be in such a condition that "significant harm is being caused or there is a significant possibility of such harm being caused" OR "significant pollution of controlled waters is being caused or there is a significant possibility of such pollution being caused". The local authority is the primary contamination-determination body; HMRC's LRR / ATED scope cross-applies the EPA 1990 Part 2A definitional architecture.
  - **DEFRA Technical Guidance (CLR Report 11 + subsequent)** — the operative risk-assessment framework. The source-pathway-receptor (SPR) conceptual model: contamination is established where there is (a) a contaminant source, (b) a pathway, and (c) a receptor (human, ecological, controlled-water) — all three must be present and linked for the land to be "contaminated" under EPA 1990 Part 2A. Sessions writing on risk-assessment copy must walk the SPR model explicitly; the popular "any soil contaminant = contaminated land" simplification is wrong.
- **Primary (the SDLT-adjacent doctrine for severely-contaminated property):**
  - **PN Bewley v HMRC [2019] UKFTT 65 (TC)** — the SDLT "uninhabitable property" test (HP §1.C). Where a property is so dilapidated, contaminated, or structurally compromised that it is "not suitable for use as a dwelling" at the effective date of the SDLT transaction, the property may be charged at the **non-residential** SDLT rates rather than the higher residential / 3%-surcharge / 15%-flat-rate / FBT rates. Bewley is the leading authority. Sessions on contaminated-land + ATED must distinguish: ATED scope depends on the dwelling being a residential dwelling worth >£500k on the operative valuation date (FA 2013 s.94 + s.116 + s.117); a Bewley-qualifying severely-contaminated property may fall outside ATED scope entirely (no "dwelling" exists in the FA 2013 sense). HP §1.C carries the locked framing.
- **Primary (the NRCGT and CGT interaction at disposal):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Schs 1A/1B/4AA** (HP §17.4) — non-resident CGT on UK land disposals. Disposal of a contaminated-land site by a non-resident corporate landlord triggers NRCGT regardless of contamination status (though the contamination affects the disposal proceeds figure, not the charging mechanism).
  - **TCGA 1992 s.38** — allowable expenditure on CGT calculation: enhancement expenditure that is reflected in the state or nature of the asset at disposal. Remediation expenditure that improves the land's state (decontamination → marketable) is allowable as enhancement expenditure under s.38(1)(b). LRR-claimed expenditure cannot also be claimed as enhancement expenditure (the standard no-double-relief discipline) — Stage 2 verifies the LRR / s.38 interaction.
- **House position reference:** §1.C (Bewley uninhabitable-property test — Wave 7 SDLT extension lock 2026-05-24), §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), §17.4 (NRCGT — TCGA 1992 s.1A + Schs 1A/1B/4AA), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full)**, §18.3 (relief catalogue — ss.133-150 including s.134 transitional, s.138 developer, s.141 trader), §18.4 (return mechanics + valuation), §18.5 (ATED-CGT abolition + NRCGT interaction). ## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **cross-regime page** on contaminated-land tax architecture for envelope owners. It sits in the ATED bucket because the operative ATED relief decision is what triggers many envelope owners to engage with contaminated-land taxation; but the page must address the full cross-regime picture because contaminated-land taxation is irreducibly multi-statute. The framing differentiator:

1. **ATED is the first lens, not the only lens.** For an envelope owner contemplating a contaminated-land remediation programme, the immediate questions are: (a) does ATED apply during the works? (b) which ATED relief (if any) bites? (c) what is the ongoing return obligation? Sessions answer these first, then move to LRR / SDLT / NRCGT.
2. **s.134 transitional relief is the most-frequently-operative ATED relief on contaminated sites.** Where the dwelling moves from s.133 rental use to a contaminated-state non-use, the s.134 four-conditions architecture (sell / demolish / convert-to-different-dwelling / convert-to-non-residential — all "without undue delay") is the route. Sessions must walk the s.134 conditions carefully — the "without undue delay" anchor disciplines long-pause cases and is the most common failure mode for ATED relief on contaminated sites.
3. **s.138 / s.141 developer / trader reliefs require trading-stock characterisation.** A contaminated-land site held as INVESTMENT does NOT qualify for s.138 / s.141 — only sites held as trading stock by a developer (2-yr trade gateway) or trader. Sessions distinguish investment vs trading-stock characterisation (cross-applies the Wave 9 Bucket B Pickford / Marson / Ramsay framework from HP §22 / §28).
4. **LRR (CTA 2009 Part 14) is the financial relief on the remediation EXPENDITURE, separate from ATED.** LRR delivers 150% relief (100% standard + 50% additional) on qualifying land remediation expenditure for companies in CT scope. For non-resident corporate landlords post-6-April-2020 (B17), LRR is now in scope (was IT-regime previously). Sessions cross-link to the existing LRR pages (`land-remediation-relief-150-percent-claim-mechanics-ltdco-developer-investor` + `land-remediation-relief-cta-2009-part-14-100-50-percent-deduction-polluter-exclusion`) rather than duplicating; the differentiation is that B18 is the ATED-first orientation that walks LRR as supporting context.
5. **The polluter-exclusion at CTA 2009 s.146.** LRR is denied where the claimant or a connected person caused the contamination. Sessions walking developer-side contaminated-land strategies must walk this exclusion as the most common practitioner trap.
6. **The risk-assessment precondition: SPR model under DEFRA technical guidance.** Contamination status is established via the source-pathway-receptor (SPR) framework. Sessions must walk SPR: source (the contaminant), pathway (e.g. soil-water-soil-air-soil-plant), receptor (human / ecological / controlled-water). Where any of source / pathway / receptor is missing, the land is NOT "contaminated" under EPA 1990 Part 2A — and LRR / ATED-relief-on-contamination claims fail at the threshold. SPR is the operative gatekeeper.
7. **The Bewley distinction for severely-contaminated property (HP §1.C).** Where contamination is so severe that the property is not "suitable for use as a dwelling" at any point in the relevant period, the property may fall outside the FA 2013 "dwelling" definition entirely (ATED scope question) AND may be charged at non-residential SDLT rates at acquisition (Bewley). Sessions distinguish Bewley scenarios (no-longer-a-dwelling) from contaminated-but-still-a-dwelling scenarios (within ATED scope but eligible for s.134 / s.138 / s.141 relief).
8. **The NRCGT + s.38 enhancement-expenditure interaction at disposal.** Remediation expenditure that improves the land's state is enhancement expenditure under TCGA 1992 s.38(1)(b) at disposal. LRR-claimed expenditure cannot double-relieve as enhancement expenditure. Sessions walk the no-double-relief discipline + the ATED-CGT abolition (HP §18.5) framing.
9. **The risk-assessment documentation pattern.** Phase 1 desk study + Phase 2 intrusive investigation (soil sampling, groundwater monitoring) + Phase 3 remediation strategy + Phase 4 verification report. Each phase generates documentation that supports both EPA 1990 Part 2A determinations AND HMRC LRR / ATED-relief claims. Sessions walk the four-phase pattern explicitly.
10. **The local-authority determination layer.** EPA 1990 Part 2A determinations are made by the local authority (s.78A) — not by HMRC, not by the developer. HMRC accepts local-authority determinations as evidence of contaminated status; absence of formal determination is not necessarily fatal (the source-pathway-receptor framework can be established by the claimant via professional consultant reports) but is a meaningful evidential weakness. Sessions walk the local-authority engagement pattern.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. **Does ATED apply to a contaminated UK residential dwelling held by a non-natural person?** (Generally yes if the property remains a "dwelling" under FA 2013 s.116-117 and is worth >£500k on the operative valuation date. The contamination may reduce the taxable value (s.102 valuation moment) but does not remove the property from scope. Bewley-extreme contamination may take the property outside the dwelling definition entirely — fact-specific.)
2. **Which ATED reliefs are available for contaminated-land scenarios?** (s.134 transitional relief — for unoccupied periods where steps are being taken to sell / demolish / convert / convert-to-non-residential without undue delay; s.138 property-developer trade relief — for sites held as trading stock by a 2-yr+ developer; s.141 property-trader relief — for sites held as trading stock by a property trader. All claimed on the return per Sch 33 para 1 — no automatic application.)
3. **What is the "without undue delay" standard under s.134?** (Per s.134 definition: delay is acceptable only if "justified by commercial considerations or cannot be avoided". Sessions walk the evidential pattern — planning timelines, contractor lead times, remediation programme dates, supply-chain documentation.)
4. **What is Land Remediation Relief (LRR) and who can claim?** (CTA 2009 Part 14. 150% total relief (100% standard deduction + 50% additional deduction) on qualifying land remediation expenditure. Companies in CT scope — UK-resident and (post-6-April-2020) non-resident corporate landlords. s.146 polluter exclusion: claimant or connected person did not cause the contamination.)
5. **What is "qualifying land remediation expenditure"?** (Per s.143 CTA 2009: expenditure on relevant land in a contaminated or derelict state, on relevant remediation activities, allowable as a deduction in computing profits. Stage 2 walks the detailed conditions.)
6. **What is the source-pathway-receptor (SPR) risk-assessment model?** (DEFRA technical guidance. Contamination requires: source (contaminant), pathway (route of exposure), receptor (human / ecological / controlled-water). All three must be present and linked. Absence of any one element means the land is not "contaminated" under EPA 1990 Part 2A.)
7. **How is "contaminated land" defined under EPA 1990 Part 2A?** (Per s.78A: land that appears to the local authority to be in such a condition that significant harm or pollution is being caused or there is a significant possibility of such harm or pollution.)
8. **Who makes the contamination determination — HMRC, the local authority, or the claimant?** (Primary determination: local authority under EPA 1990 Part 2A s.78A. HMRC accepts local-authority determinations as evidence. Absence of formal determination is not fatal — claimant can establish SPR via professional consultant reports — but is an evidential weakness.)
9. **What is the Bewley test and when does it apply?** (PN Bewley v HMRC [2019] UKFTT 65 — SDLT test for property so dilapidated / contaminated / structurally compromised it is "not suitable for use as a dwelling" at the effective date. Non-residential SDLT rates apply; HP §1.C carries the locked framing. Bewley scenarios may also fall outside ATED's "dwelling" definition — sessions must distinguish from contaminated-but-still-a-dwelling cases.)
10. **What is the four-phase risk-assessment documentation pattern?** (Phase 1 desk study + Phase 2 intrusive investigation (soil sampling, groundwater monitoring) + Phase 3 remediation strategy + Phase 4 verification report. Each phase generates documentation supporting EPA 1990 Part 2A determinations and HMRC relief claims.)
11. **What is the no-double-relief discipline between LRR and TCGA s.38 enhancement expenditure?** (Remediation expenditure improving the land's state is enhancement expenditure under s.38(1)(b) at disposal. LRR-claimed expenditure cannot also be claimed as enhancement expenditure on disposal. Sessions walk the discipline.)
12. **What is the polluter-exclusion trap for developers?** (CTA 2009 s.146. Developer who caused the contamination, or who is connected to the polluter, cannot claim LRR. Common scenario: corporate group with one entity that operated a contaminating industrial use, then transfers the land to a sibling entity for redevelopment — the sibling is connected and cannot claim LRR. Sessions walk this carefully.)
