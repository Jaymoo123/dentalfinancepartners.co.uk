# UK-Spain Double Taxation Convention: what Spanish-resident landlords of UK property and emigrating UK landlords face

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (treaty):** 2013 UK-Spain Double Taxation Convention, signed 14 March 2013, in force 12 June 2014. Effective UK withholding taxes 12 June 2014; UK corporation tax 1 April 2015; UK income tax and capital gains tax 6 April 2015. Modified by the Multilateral Instrument (MLI) — UK effective dates 1 January 2023 (withheld taxes), 1 April 2023 (UK CT), 6 April 2023 (UK income tax + CGT). Published at gov.uk "Spain: tax treaties". Follows OECD Model 2017 form.
- **Primary (UK domestic — overrides treaty for UK situs property):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Part 3** (UK property income, individuals).
  - **CTA 2009 Part 4** (UK property income, companies).
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** (NRCGT — Finance Act 2019 rewrite).
  - **FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902** (Non-Resident Landlord scheme — statutory withholding).
  - **TIOPA 2010 s.18 + s.130** (foreign tax credit relief framework).
- **Supporting:** OECD Model 2017 Commentary on Articles 6 / 13 / 4 / 23; Spanish *Ley del Impuesto sobre el Patrimonio* (wealth tax — Spain-side credit framework only).
- **House position reference:** §10 (DTAs general), §16 (DTAs Wave 2 extension), §16.5 (UK-Spain treaty 2013: Art 13(4) property-rich entity rule applies; Spanish wealth tax creditable in Spain only — UK has no wealth tax to credit), §16.6 (NRL is statutory, not treaty), §17.4 (NRCGT regime), §17.5 (NRL operational mechanics).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)
This page is the **UK-Spain bilateral specific** landlord page — narrow scope, deep mechanics. It is NOT the general "what is a DTA" intro (B20 `dont-pay-twice-an-introduction-to-tax-treaties` covers that) and it is NOT the abstract OECD-model walkthrough. The whole page sits in the Spain bilateral posture: a Spanish-resident individual or a Spanish incorporated company holding UK BTL property, OR a UK-resident landlord who has emigrated to Spain and continues to let UK property. The framing differentiator is the **two-way credit asymmetry**: UK retains primary taxing rights over UK property (Art 6) and UK property gains (Art 13(1) + Art 13(4) indirect), Spain credits UK tax paid under its tax-credit method, AND Spanish wealth tax on UK-property holdings is creditable only on the Spanish side. UK landlords who think the treaty exempts them from UK SA self-assessment are wrong; Spanish landlords who think Spanish wealth tax is mirrored on the UK side are also wrong. Sibling pages B1 (UK-IoM) and B3 (UK-India) take parallel form but with state-specific notes; cross-links go between the three.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. Does the 2013 UK-Spain Convention exempt a Spanish-resident landlord from UK tax on UK rental income? (No — Art 6 allocates source-state taxing rights to the UK; UK domestic charges under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 / CTA 2009 Part 4 apply.)
2. Does the treaty displace the NRL scheme? (No — NRL is statutory under FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902. Spanish-resident landlords still apply for NRL1 gross-payment approval.)
3. How does Art 13(4) apply on disposal of shares in a UK property-rich entity by a Spanish resident? (Yes — UK retains taxing rights on shares in property-rich entities; NRCGT regime under TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A applies; 60-day return required.)
4. How does Art 4 tie-breaker apply where an individual is dual-resident under UK SRT and Spanish 183-day / *centro de intereses vitales* rule? (Cascade: permanent home, centre of vital interests, habitual abode, nationality, mutual agreement.)
5. How does Spanish wealth tax (impuesto sobre el patrimonio) interact with UK property? (Creditable against Spanish wealth tax in Spain only; UK has no symmetric wealth tax, so no UK-side credit. Stage 2 verifies current Spanish band thresholds and regional variations — e.g. Madrid 100% bonification.)
6. How does the MLI modification (effective from 2023) affect the 2013 Convention in practice? (Principal purpose test imported; treaty-abuse provisions strengthened. Stage 2 verifies the specific MLI Articles applied to UK-Spain.)
7. How does the FIG regime under ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J (FA 2025 s.37) apply to a Spanish citizen newly resident in the UK with Spanish property income? (Standard 4-year FIG window; Spanish property income is foreign income within s.845H categories — Stage 2 verifies verbatim list at write time.)
8. How does s.10A TCGA 1992 temporary non-residence apply to a UK-resident landlord who moves to Spain for 4 years then returns? (Gain recapture in return year on UK assets owned at departure — UK land already in NRCGT, so s.10A primarily catches non-UK situs gains. The 5-year-or-less window is the trigger.)
9. Are Spanish companies inside or outside UK ATED? (ATED applies to non-natural persons including non-UK companies; a Spanish *sociedad limitada* holding a UK dwelling worth more than £500,000 is in scope — cross-reference §18 + Bucket B ATED picks.)
10. How does the new long-term residence IHT test (IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C + ss.267ZC-267ZF, FA 2025 architecture) apply to a UK-domiciled-of-origin emigrant to Spain? (10-of-20 residence test; the tail period for IHT exposure can extend up to 10 years post-departure depending on the individual's prior UK-residence years; cross-reference §22.X.)
