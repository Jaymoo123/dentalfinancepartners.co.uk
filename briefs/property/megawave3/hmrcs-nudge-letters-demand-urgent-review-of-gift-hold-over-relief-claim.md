# HMRC's Nudge Letters Demand Urgent Review of Gift Hold-Over Relief Claim: A Landlord Response Guide

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — hold-over relief on business-asset gifts:** TCGA 1992 **section 165** ("Relief for gifts of business assets") + **Schedule 7** Parts 1-2 (extending the relief to agricultural property + settled property; restricting the relief for non-business-asset components). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/section/165`. The critical operative point: s.165 relief is **restricted to assets used for the purposes of a trade, profession or vocation** carried on by the transferor or by their personal company. **Investment property does NOT qualify** — buy-to-let portfolios, residential lettings, and pure-rental businesses are outside the s.165 gate per *Pawson* + the settled HMRC view (cross-ref §22.1 BPR investment-line lock).
- **Primary — hold-over relief on chargeable IHT gifts:** TCGA 1992 **section 260** ("Gifts on which inheritance tax is chargeable etc"). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/section/260`. s.260 relief applies to chargeable transfers within the meaning of IHTA 1984 — typically CLTs (chargeable lifetime transfers into trust) — and operates by deferring the donor's CGT and transferring the held-over gain to the recipient's base cost.
- **Primary — settlor-interested trust exclusion:** TCGA 1992 **sections 169B-169G** — excludes s.260 hold-over relief where the trust is settlor-interested (the settlor or settlor's spouse can benefit). Verified live 2026-05-27. §22.4 + §22.12 of house_positions.md lock the operational depth on settlor-interested-trust exclusions; sessions writing must NOT cite s.169G as the settlor definition (s.169G subsections (2)-(5) were omitted by FA 2009 — F-4 / Wave 6 Bucket B lock).
- **Primary — non-resident disposal restriction (FA 2019 amendment):** s.260 hold-over relief is restricted where the disposal involves UK land and the transferee is UK-resident (FA 2019 anti-avoidance, narrowing the route for non-resident-to-UK-resident hold-over claims).
- **Supporting — HMRC enquiry powers triggered by nudge letter:** TMA 1970 s.9A (enquiry into self-assessment return) + s.29 (discovery assessment if the return is out of enquiry-window); s.31A (30-day appeal window if HMRC issues an assessment); §27.1 (Discovery assessment time limits — Wave 7 lock) for the 4 / 6 / 12 / 20 year framework.
- **Supporting — penalty exposure if the hold-over claim is found invalid:** Schedule 24 FA 2007 (inaccuracy penalties — careless 0%-30%, deliberate 20%-70%, deliberate-concealed 30%-100%); §27.2 lock. The 60-day nudge-letter window is operationally HMRC's "give the taxpayer a chance to come forward unprompted" mechanic — unprompted-disclosure floors apply if the taxpayer self-corrects within the window.
- **House position reference:** §27 (HMRC enquiry + tax compliance mechanics — Wave 7 lock) primary; §27.1 (discovery time limits) + §27.2 (Sch 24 inaccuracy penalties) operative for the post-nudge enquiry path; §22.4 + §22.12 (settlor-interested trust ss.169B-169G framework) operative for the s.260 limb. No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — the architecture is already locked at §27 + §22 + the existing landing pages on BPR / FIC / trust comparisons.

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **landlord-response guide for the HMRC nudge-letter campaign on hold-over relief claims**. Sibling MW3-A pick **A10 (`hmrcs-nudge-letters-on-business-asset-disposal-relief`)** covers the SAME campaign-mechanic (HMRC's "One Too Many" / nudge-letter framework) but on a different underlying relief (Business Asset Disposal Relief under TCGA 1992 ss.169H-169S, formerly Entrepreneurs' Relief). The two pages MUST be clearly differentiated at Stage 2 — same nudge-letter framing, different underlying relief mechanic, different operational failure points.

Differentiation pillars for this page (vs A10):

- **Statutory regime:** TCGA 1992 s.165 + s.260 + Schedule 7 + ss.169B-169G (hold-over) vs TCGA 1992 ss.169H-169S (BADR) — entirely separate Chapters of Part V of TCGA 1992.
- **Property-cohort relevance:** s.165 fails for buy-to-let portfolios (no trade); s.260 is the live route for property gifts into discretionary trust (subject to settlor-interest trap) — both are recurring landlord-planning structures. BADR (the A10 angle) is almost never available to property landlords (investment, not trading) so A10's primary frame is the surprise-disqualification angle.
- **The campaign-mechanic backstory:** HMRC's nudge-letter campaigns on hold-over have repeatedly targeted: (a) gifts of buy-to-let property to adult children where the donor wrongly claimed s.165 on the basis that the rental was a "business" — failure point is the trade/investment line; (b) gifts of shares in property LtdCos where the company is investment not trading (§21.5 FIC investment-only lock); (c) gifts into settlor-interested trusts under the s.260 route, falling foul of ss.169B-169G; (d) gifts of agricultural property where the agricultural element was not properly bifurcated from non-agricultural per Schedule 7 Part 1.

The angle this page takes: a landlord (or recipient of property from a landlord) who has received an HMRC nudge letter asking them to "review and confirm or amend" a prior hold-over claim needs the **statutory framework + the operational response sequence** — the 60-day window, the disclosure-via-Digital-Disclosure-Service route if the claim was wrong, the in-place amend-vs-leave-and-defend decision, and the §27.5 CoP9 boundary if HMRC's letter signals deliberate-behaviour suspicion.
