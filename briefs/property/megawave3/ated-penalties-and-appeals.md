# ATED penalties and appeals: the full architecture (Sch 55, Sch 56, Sch 24) and the appeal routes for non-natural-person filers

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (ATED return + payment obligations):**
  - **FA 2013 s.159** — annual ATED return required within 30 days of first becoming chargeable (90-day extension for new dwellings / conversions under ss.124-125). **Verified at legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-27.**
  - **FA 2013 s.161** — return must include self-assessment of tax under s.99 + interim relief + market value statements. **Verified at legislation.gov.uk on 2026-05-27.**
  - **FA 2013 s.163** — adjusted chargeable amount return (where the chargeable amount changes mid-period because relief gained or lost).
- **Primary (late filing penalty regime):**
  - **FA 2009 Schedule 55** — Sch 55 Table at para 1 items 11A (ATED return) and 11B (ATED ACA return). Operative paragraphs:
    - **Para 3** — £100 fixed penalty on the day after the filing date.
    - **Para 4** — daily £10 penalties from 3 months (HMRC must give notice).
    - **Para 5** — at 6 months: 5% of tax liability OR £300 if greater.
    - **Para 6** — at 12 months: 5% of tax liability OR £300 if greater, with behaviour uplifts (deliberate-concealed 100%, deliberate not-concealed 70%, careless 30%) per Sch 55 para 6 + para 6A.
    - **Paras 17-18** — prompted / unprompted disclosure mitigation (mirrors Sch 24 architecture).
  - HP §18.4 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22 carries the standard ATED escalator as £100 immediate, £200 at 3 months, £300 at 6 months and 12 months. This is the HMRC-published practical figure set; the underlying Sch 55 paras 3-6 mechanics produce these amounts.
- **Primary (late payment penalty regime):**
  - **FA 2009 Schedule 56** — Sch 56 applies to ATED via the same items 11A / 11B route. Operative paragraphs:
    - **Para 3** — 5% of unpaid tax at 30 days after due date.
    - **Para 3 sub-para (3)** — additional 5% at 5 months.
    - **Para 3 sub-para (4)** — additional 5% at 11 months.
    - Interest under FA 2009 s.101 + FA 2009 s.102 applies in parallel.
- **Primary (inaccuracy in return):**
  - **FA 2007 Schedule 24** — inaccuracy penalty regime. Behaviour-tiered:
    - **Careless** — maximum 30%; mitigation to 0% unprompted disclosure within 12 months; 15% prompted minimum.
    - **Deliberate not-concealed** — maximum 70%; mitigation to 20% unprompted; 35% prompted minimum.
    - **Deliberate-concealed** — maximum 100%; mitigation to 30% unprompted; 50% prompted minimum.
    - **Offshore Category 2 / 3 uplifts** under Sch 24 para 4A and the Offshore Inaccuracies Order, up to 200% for Category 3 deliberate-concealed.
- **Primary (the two appeal grounds, independent):**
  - **FA 2009 Sch 55 para 23 — reasonable excuse defence** (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27): "Liability to a penalty ... does not arise ... if P satisfies HMRC or (on appeal) the First-tier Tribunal or Upper Tribunal that there is a reasonable excuse for the failure." Three statutory exclusions: insufficient funds (unless beyond control), reliance on another person (unless reasonable steps taken), failure to remedy once excuse ceased.
  - **FA 2009 Sch 55 para 16 — special reduction** (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27): "If HMRC think it right because of special circumstances, they may reduce a penalty under any paragraph of this Schedule." Excluded grounds at para 16(2): inability to pay; cross-taxpayer revenue balancing.
  - **FA 2007 Sch 24 para 14 — reasonable excuse defence** (inaccuracy parallel).
  - **FA 2007 Sch 24 para 11 — special reduction** (inaccuracy parallel).
- **Primary (appeal mechanics):**
  - **TMA 1970 s.31** — appeal right against penalties.
  - **TMA 1970 s.31A** — 30-day appeal window (non-negotiable per HP §27.9; late appeals require Martland-framework FTT application).
  - **TMA 1970 s.49** — statutory review process: appellant requests HMRC internal review (stops the 30-day FTT clock); FTT clock restarts at fresh 30-day window from review conclusion.
- **Primary (controlling case-law):**
  - **Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 (TCC)** — Upper Tribunal four-stage reasonable-excuse test (HP §27.9 lock).
  - **Martland v HMRC [2018] UKUT 178 (TCC)** — Upper Tribunal three-stage late-appeal framework.
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27, verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full)**, §18.4 (return mechanics + verified penalty schedule), §18.7 (OTM letters campaign, typical penalty-trigger context), §27 (HMRC enquiry + appeals full architecture), §27.2 (Sch 55 paras 17-18 disclosure mitigation), §27.3 (Sch 56 late payment mechanics), §27.4 (Perrin four-stage), §27.5 (Martland three-stage), §27.9 (do-not-write list, multiple specific anti-patterns).

### Example 2 — Sch 55 late-filing escalator walked (with arithmetic)

Iberic Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co holding a £2.4m London dwelling; £9,450 2026/27 ATED charge; relief CLAIMED for 2024/25 and 2025/26; 2026/27 return missed). Filing date 30 April 2026; not filed by year-end.

- **Day after filing date (1 May 2026):** Sch 55 para 3 £100 fixed penalty triggers. **Cumulative: £100.**
- **3 months (31 July 2026):** Sch 55 para 4 daily penalties potentially trigger (HMRC must give notice; in ATED practice HMRC typically issues the £200 (= 3-month escalator) instead of daily £10s — operative HP §18.4 escalator figure). **Cumulative: £300.**
- **6 months (31 October 2026):** Sch 55 para 5 triggers: 5% of tax due OR £300 if greater. 5% × £9,450 = £472.50; £300 is the floor. The HMRC-published escalator figure is £300; the para 5 mechanism could give £472.50 where tax-geared exceeds floor. **Cumulative under floor logic: £600; under tax-geared logic: £772.50.**
- **12 months (30 April 2027):** Sch 55 para 6 triggers: 5% of tax due OR £300 if greater, with behaviour uplifts per para 6A. Careless 30% upper / 0% lower floor with full unprompted disclosure within 12 months. Deliberate not-concealed 70% upper / 20% floor. Deliberate-concealed 100% upper / 30% floor. **For careless behaviour with prompted disclosure: 15% × £9,450 = £1,417.50.**
- **Total cumulative for 12-month delinquent careless prompted-disclosure case:** £100 + £200 + £300 + £1,417.50 = **£2,017.50** of late-filing penalties on a £9,450 ATED bill.
- **If deliberate-concealed (worst case) prompted-disclosure:** 50% × £9,450 = £4,725 at para 6; total £100 + £200 + £300 + £4,725 = **£5,325** of late-filing penalties (= 56% of the ATED bill in penalty alone).
- **Plus Sch 56 late-payment** (assuming tax also late): 5% at 30 days (£472.50) + 5% at 5 months (£472.50) + 5% at 11 months (£472.50) = £1,417.50 in late-payment penalties.
- **Plus interest** under FA 2009 ss.101-102 (current HMRC late-payment interest rate — verify gov.uk at write).
- **Grand total worst case (12-month delinquent + deliberate-concealed + prompted disclosure + late tax payment + interest):** £5,325 + £1,417.50 + interest = c. £7,000+ on a £9,450 ATED bill. The arithmetic justifies engaging specialist tax representation immediately on receipt of any HMRC ATED enquiry.

### Example 3 — Sch 24 inaccuracy worked (rate-band mis-declaration)

Cordova Cayman Limited (Cayman company holding a £6.2m London apartment; ATED return filed by 30 April 2025 for 2025/26 chargeable period; declared at £5m-£10m band giving £73,050 ATED at 2025/26 figures, paid in full; HMRC enquiry October 2026 establishes the dwelling was correctly valued at £10.3m at the 1 April 2022 valuation date, so the band should have been £10m-£20m at £146,650). The undeclared tax = £146,650 − £73,050 = £73,600.

- **Category determination:** Cordova used a professional RICS valuation that was internally consistent but understated. The behaviour analysis depends on the facts: if the valuation methodology was reasonable + the under-valuation was bona-fide error, **careless** (Sch 24 para 3(1)(a) — failure to take reasonable care). If the methodology was knowingly aggressive + UBO directed the valuer, potentially **deliberate not-concealed** (para 3(1)(b)). Cordova is Cayman-incorporated → **offshore-element** at play; Cayman is a Category 3 jurisdiction (no automatic information exchange) per SI 2011/975 → **Category 3 uplift** at para 4A.
- **Careless scenario (HMRC accepts bona-fide error):**
  - Sch 24 para 4 maximum: 30%. Sch 24 para 9 reduction: prompted disclosure within 12 months → 15% minimum floor. So 15-30% range.
  - **Offshore Category 3 uplift:** 1.5x multiplier per para 4A → 22.5%-45% range.
  - HMRC typically agrees mid-range for cooperative disclosure: c. 30% × £73,600 = **£22,080 inaccuracy penalty.**
- **Deliberate not-concealed scenario (HMRC challenges):**
  - Sch 24 para 4 maximum: 70%. Sch 24 para 10 reduction: prompted disclosure → 35% minimum floor.
  - **Offshore Category 3 uplift:** 1.5x multiplier per para 4A → 52.5%-105% range.
  - Worst case: 105% × £73,600 = **£77,280 inaccuracy penalty** (more than the underlying tax).
- **Deliberate-concealed scenario (extreme):**
  - Sch 24 para 4 maximum: 100%. Sch 24 para 10 reduction: prompted → 50% minimum floor.
  - **Offshore Category 3 uplift:** 2.0x multiplier (200% maximum per SI 2011/975 + para 4A) → 100%-200% range.
  - Worst case: 200% × £73,600 = **£147,200 inaccuracy penalty** (double the underlying tax).
- **Plus underlying tax** £73,600 + **interest** under FA 2009 ss.101-102 from the original due date.
- **Operational lesson:** offshore-corporate ATED filers face dramatically asymmetric penalty exposure on inaccuracy. The route to the low end of each band is unprompted-disclosure-within-12-months under Sch 24 para 10. Engage specialist tax representation BEFORE submitting an amended return; DDS (Digital Disclosure Service) timing matters; specialist tax counsel may negotiate behaviour categorisation with HMRC.

### Example 5 — The cumulative arithmetic for a 5-year multi-dwelling non-filer

Sapphire Estates Cayman Holdings Limited (Cayman company holding 4 London apartments worth £3.2m / £4.8m / £6.7m / £12.4m; deliberately non-filed ATED returns 2021/22 through 2025/26; HMRC OTM letter received March 2026 prompting voluntary disclosure).

- **Per-dwelling per-year ATED at 2025/26 figures (HMRC will recalculate per-year per the published bands):** £3.2m = £18,500 band; £4.8m = £18,500 band (just under £5m); £6.7m = £73,050; £12.4m = £146,650. Total annual ATED at 2025/26 rates: £18,500 + £18,500 + £73,050 + £146,650 = **£256,700 per year** × 5 years = **£1,283,500 of historic ATED.**
- **Sch 55 late-filing escalator per year per dwelling:** £100 + £200 + £300 + tax-geared para 6 (at deliberate-not-concealed Cat 3 with prompted disclosure: 105% of ATED). For the £12.4m dwelling: £100 + £200 + £300 + 105% × £146,650 = £154,583 per year × 5 years = c. £772,915 in late-filing penalties on that single dwelling. Across all 4 dwellings: c. £1,348,675 in Sch 55 late-filing penalties alone.
- **Sch 56 late-payment escalator per year per dwelling:** 5% + 5% + 5% = 15% of unpaid tax × £256,700 × 5 = **£192,525 in late-payment penalties.**
- **Sch 24 inaccuracy:** doesn't apply where no return was filed (Sch 41 wrongdoing might, depending on facts).
- **Interest** under FA 2009 ss.101-102 on £1.28m over 5 years at HMRC late-payment interest rates (verify rate at write — typically 7%+ annualised): c. £400k+ in interest.
- **Total historic exposure:** £1.28m ATED + £1.35m Sch 55 + £193k Sch 56 + £400k+ interest = **c. £3.2 million.**

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: What penalty regimes apply to an ATED filer?**
   A: THREE parallel regimes. (a) **Late filing** under FA 2009 Sch 55 (operative via items 11A + 11B at the Table at para 1): £100 immediate; £200 at 3 months; £300 at 6 months; £300 at 12 months plus tax-geared paras 5-6 / 6A uplifts beyond 12 months. (b) **Late payment** under FA 2009 Sch 56: 5% at 30 days, additional 5% at 5 months, additional 5% at 11 months; FA 2009 ss.101-102 interest in parallel. (c) **Inaccuracy** under FA 2007 Sch 24 (where the return contains an understatement): careless 0-30%; deliberate not-concealed 20-70%; deliberate-concealed 30-100%; offshore Category 2 1.5x; Category 3 up to 200%. A single delinquent filer can attract all three simultaneously.

2. **Q: What is the ATED late-filing penalty schedule?**
   A: Per HP §18.4 verified gov.uk 2026-05-22: £100 immediate (Sch 55 para 3 day-after-filing-date fixed penalty); £200 at 3 months (Sch 55 para 4 mechanism; HMRC ATED practice issues £200 escalator rather than daily £10s); £300 at 6 months (Sch 55 para 5: 5% of tax due OR £300 if greater); £300 at 12 months (Sch 55 para 6: 5% of tax due OR £300 if greater, with behaviour-tiered uplifts per para 6A). Beyond 12 months the tax-geared paras 5-6 / 6A uplifts take the penalty up to behaviour maxima (careless 30%; deliberate not-concealed 70%; deliberate-concealed 100% of tax due).

3. **Q: What about late-payment penalties under Sch 56?**
   A: ATED falls in FA 2009 Sch 56 via the same items 11A / 11B route. Para 3 escalator: 5% of unpaid tax at 30 days after due date; additional 5% at 5 months; additional 5% at 11 months. Cumulative cap 15% of unpaid tax. Interest under FA 2009 ss.101-102 runs in parallel from the original due date (currently c. 7-8% annualised; verify gov.uk at write). Late-payment penalties are SEPARATE from late-filing penalties; both can apply to the same delinquent return.

4. **Q: What is the inaccuracy penalty regime under Sch 24?**
   A: Behaviour-tiered. **Careless** (failure to take reasonable care, Sch 24 para 3(1)(a)): maximum 30%, mitigation to 0% unprompted disclosure within 12 months, 15% prompted minimum floor. **Deliberate not-concealed** (para 3(1)(b)): 20-70%; 35% prompted minimum. **Deliberate-concealed** (para 3(1)(c)): 30-100%; 50% prompted minimum. **Offshore Category 2 uplift** (1.5x) under para 4A; **Offshore Category 3 uplift** (2.0x → 200% maximum) for jurisdictions without automatic information exchange per SI 2011/975. Relevant for ATED filers via overseas-incorporated structures (Cayman, BVI, etc).

5. **Q: What is the offshore Category-3 200% uplift?**
   A: Per FA 2007 Sch 24 para 4A + SI 2011/975 (Offshore Inaccuracies Order), jurisdictions are classified Category 1 (full information exchange with UK), Category 2 (some exchange), Category 3 (no exchange). For ATED inaccuracies involving Category 3 jurisdictions the penalty maximum doubles to 200%. Common Category 3 jurisdictions historically include certain Caribbean and Pacific offshore centres; verify current SI 2011/975 list at write. Channel Islands + Isle of Man are typically Category 2. The uplift is non-trivial: a £100k undeclared ATED inaccuracy at deliberate-concealed Category 3 could carry a £200k penalty (vs £100k for the same behaviour at Category 1).

6. **Q: How does the disclosure-mitigation matrix work?**
   A: Sch 24 para 10 (mirrored in Sch 55 paras 17-18). Unprompted disclosure (taxpayer acts before HMRC has crystallised the inquiry) within 12 months of the failure produces the lowest mitigation floor: 0% careless / 20% deliberate not-concealed / 30% deliberate-concealed. Prompted disclosure (after HMRC has opened formal enquiry / issued OTM letter / discovery assessment): 15% careless / 35% deliberate not-concealed / 50% deliberate-concealed. Beyond 12 months the floors shift upward. The OTM-letter window (HP §18.7) is the operationally critical disclosure-mitigation moment: engagement within the window typically allows unprompted-floor framing for the substantive disclosure.

7. **Q: What is the two-routes appeal architecture for late-filing penalties?**
   A: Two independent statutory grounds. **Reasonable excuse** under FA 2009 Sch 55 para 23: appellant's defence on an objective test (Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 (TCC) four-stage framework). **Special reduction** under FA 2009 Sch 55 para 16: HMRC's discretionary judgment; FTT supervisory only (Sch 55 para 22). Routes are NOT mutually exclusive; typically pleaded simultaneously. See B8 (`ated-late-filing-penalty-failed-reasonable-excuse-or-special-circumstances-appeal`) for the operational deep-dive on the two routes.

8. **Q: What is the inaccuracy-penalty appeal architecture?**
   A: Parallel structure to late-filing. **Reasonable excuse** under FA 2007 Sch 24 para 14 (mirror of Sch 55 para 23 but specifically for inaccuracy). **Special reduction** under FA 2007 Sch 24 para 11 (mirror of Sch 55 para 16). Plus behaviour-categorisation challenge: appellant can challenge HMRC's "deliberate-concealed" / "deliberate-not-concealed" / "careless" determination on Sch 24 para 3 — moving the behaviour category down reduces the maximum and the mitigated floor.

9. **Q: What is the 30-day appeal clock?**
   A: TMA 1970 s.31A — 30 days from the date of the penalty notice. The appellant has three options within the window: (a) appeal direct to the First-tier Tribunal under s.31; (b) request HMRC statutory review under s.49 (stops the FTT clock pending HMRC's review conclusion notice, then a FRESH 30-day FTT window opens); (c) pay without prejudice and lodge a protective appeal. Late appeals (after 30 days) require formal Martland v HMRC [2018] UKUT 178 (TCC) framework application to the FTT.

10. **Q: What does the statutory review under TMA 1970 s.49 do?**
    A: Statutory review is HMRC's INTERNAL review process — an officer not previously involved with the case re-examines the penalty decision. Request the review within the 30-day appeal window via written notice. HMRC typically issues a review conclusion notice within 45 days (longer for complex cases). On receipt of the notice, a FRESH 30-day FTT appeal window opens. Review is operationally a cheaper, faster first step than direct FTT appeal; appellants often secure penalty discharge / reduction at review without litigation.

11. **Q: I received an HMRC OTM letter about possible unfiled ATED returns — what should I do?**
    A: ENGAGE WITHIN THE OTM WINDOW. Per HP §18.7, OTM letters are HMRC One-to-Many compliance letters inviting voluntary disclosure; they are NOT assessments. Acting within the OTM window typically allows your subsequent disclosure to be characterised as unprompted (lowest mitigation floor); delay typically crystallises into prompted-disclosure floors. Operational sequence: (i) engage specialist tax representation within 7 days; (ii) gather documentation across all chargeable periods; (iii) prepare DDS (Digital Disclosure Service) submission with full disclosure; (iv) submit with calculation showing unprompted-disclosure framing.

12. **Q: I didn't file an ATED return because the dwelling was relief-eligible (claim-only) — am I exposed?**
    A: YES. Per §18.4, the £100/£200/£300 Sch 55 escalator applies to ANY missed ATED return INCLUDING claim-only returns where relief covers the chargeable period. The tax-geared paras 5-6 effectively don't bite at zero tax due (the 5% defaults to £300 floor), but the £100/£200/£300 escalator does. A multi-year multi-dwelling non-filer with claim-only relief still faces material penalty exposure: 4 dwellings × 5 years × £600 = £12,000 minimum. Sch 55 para 23 reasonable-excuse + para 16 special-reduction routes remain available.

13. **Q: My company is in a Category 3 jurisdiction (Cayman / BVI / etc) — am I automatically exposed to the 200% uplift?**
    A: Only if the inaccuracy is deliberate-concealed AND involves an offshore element. The Sch 24 para 4A + SI 2011/975 uplift applies to inaccuracies where the offshore-element involvement is established. For a Cayman ATED filer where the inaccuracy is bona-fide careless (e.g. a defensible valuation methodology that turned out to under-state band), the careless maximum applies (uplifted to 60% for Category 3 careless: 30% × 2.0). The Category 3 uplift bites hardest at deliberate-concealed (200% maximum). Specialist tax representation can challenge HMRC's behaviour categorisation as part of the appeal process.

14. **Q: What is the cumulative exposure for a 5-year multi-dwelling non-filer?**
    A: Dramatic. Each chargeable period generates a separate Sch 55 escalator + Sch 56 escalator per dwelling. A 5-year non-filer for a single £2m dwelling at 2026/27 figures faces 5 × (£100+£200+£300) = £3,000 minimum Sch 55 escalator plus tax-geared paras 5-6 uplifts where tax was due plus Sch 56 5%/5%/5% per year plus interest. A 4-dwelling 5-year deliberate-not-concealed case can comfortably exceed £3 million in cumulative exposure (penalties + tax + interest). The arithmetic is harsh; OTM voluntary disclosure within the unprompted window is the standard remediation route; specialist representation essential.
