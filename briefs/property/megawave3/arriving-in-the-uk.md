# Arriving in the UK: the inbound landlord's checklist for FIG, LTR, SRT split-year and the UK-property trap that rebasing will not save

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (residence determination — inbound):**
  - **FA 2013 Schedule 45** — Statutory Residence Test (SRT), with split-year Cases 4-8 in Part 3 specifically handling arriving individuals. Case 4 (starting to have only a UK home), Case 5 (starting full-time UK work), Case 6 (ceasing full-time overseas work), Case 7 (partner of someone in Case 6), Case 8 (starting to have a UK home). Where multiple cases apply, statute determines priority per Sch 45 Part 3 paras 53-55.
- **Primary (FIG 4-year window for new arrivals):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Chapter 5 ss.845A-845J** inserted by **FA 2025 s.37 + Sch 8**. Effective tax year 2025-26.
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1)** — qualifying new resident test: four cumulative conditions per HP §17.8: (a) UK-resident for current tax year, (b) NOT UK-resident for each of the 10 tax years before that tax year, (c) not disqualified per s.845B(2), (d) individual at least 10 years old at commencement of the tax year.
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845A** — claim mechanism; relief deducted at Step 2 of the ITA 2007 s.23 income tax calculation. Claim deadline **31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year** per the s.845A regime (NOT the TMA 1970 s.43 generic 4-year amendment window).
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845H** — qualifying foreign income categories (broad scope ~22-23 distinct types; verify verbatim at write time per §16.35).
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845I** — disqualified income (anti-fragmentation; excludes UK-derived income channelled through offshore structures).
- **Primary (IHT residence-based regime — long-term-resident trigger):**
  - **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C** — Long-Term Resident (LTR) test: 10-of-20 tax years (FA 2025 architecture). LTR worldwide-IHT exposure begins on the day the 10-of-20 threshold is crossed.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.6B** — under-20 variation (HP §22.X.3 micro-lock).
  - **IHTA 1984 ss.267ZC-267ZF** — LTR election framework + tail-period (HP §22.X.5).
- **Primary (CGT for any continuing non-residence + UK-property asymmetry):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** — NRCGT continues to apply to any pre-arrival UK land disposal made while still non-resident; 60-day return required regardless of tax due.
  - **FA 2025 s.42 + Sch 11** — the CGT rebasing election. **Five conditions per Sch 11 para 1(1)** per HP §17.10; **condition 3 excludes UK situs assets** — a UK property held by an inbound non-dom is NOT rebased under Sch 11. UK land follows the NRCGT-specific rebasing dates (5 April 2015 residential, 5 April 2019 non-residential) per §17.4.
- **Primary (returning non-dom transitional — TRF):**
  - **FA 2025 s.41 + Sch 10** — Temporary Repatriation Facility. Relevant only for individuals who PREVIOUSLY claimed remittance basis pre-2025-26 (an arriver who has never been UK-resident before will not have a TRF designation pool). Rates 12% / 12% / 15% for 2025-26 / 2026-27 / 2027-28; window closes 5 April 2028.
- **Primary (continuing UK rentals during initial UK-resident year — NRL turn-off):**
  - **FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902** — NRL scheme. Once arrival makes the landlord UK-resident under SRT, the NRL withholding obligation falls away from the residence date; agents / tenants need NRL6 cessation discipline (worth flagging operationally).
- **Primary (s.10A symmetric position for returning UK national):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.10A** — temporary non-residence (verified against HMRC CG26540 per HP §17.3 sibling verification 2026-05-22). Relevant where a UK national who left, sold non-UK assets while non-resident, and now returns within 5 years finds those gains crystallise in the year of return.
- **House position reference:** **§17 in full**, §17.1 (SRT), §17.2 (split-year Cases 4-8 specifically), §17.3 (s.10A symmetric trap for returnees), §17.4 (NRCGT continuing-non-residence relevance + rebasing dates), §17.5 (NRL turn-off mechanics), §17.6 (domicile reform + FIG headline), **§17.8 (FIG operational depth — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, **§17.10 (rebasing narrow eligibility, condition-3 UK-situs exclusion — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, **§22.X (IHT LTR — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, §22.X.1 (10-of-20 test), §22.X.3 (s.6B under-20 variation), §22.X.5 (ss.267ZC-267ZF election framework), §16 (DTA framing for source-state tie-breaker context).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)
This is the **inbound mirror** of B6 (`are-you-leaving-the-uk-permanently`) and the inverse-direction page of the bucket's expat cluster. The framing differentiator is the **honest FIG-vs-rebasing-vs-LTR triangulation** that the popular "4 years tax-free for new arrivals" framing flattens. Specifically the page must:

1. **Refuse the 4-years-tax-free shorthand.** The FIG window is per-year-claim with **lost personal allowance + lost CGT AEA** in each claim year. Claiming FIG is net-positive only where foreign income / gains for that year exceed the lost allowance value, so sessions writing planning copy must hedge.
2. **Anchor the 10-of-prior-10-years gateway.** This is the operative entry test for FIG per s.845B(1), not "non-dom" status, which is no longer the door from 6 April 2025. An inbound expat who was last UK-resident in 2017-18 (i.e. 8 tax years of prior non-residence by 2025-26) does NOT qualify for FIG; they enter as a standard arising-basis resident.
3. **Walk the UK-property rebasing trap.** Condition (3) of FA 2025 Sch 11 para 1(1) excludes UK-situs assets. An inbound non-dom holding a UK BTL pre-arrival cannot use Sch 11 rebasing to step up base cost; UK land has its own NRCGT-specific rebasing dates (5 April 2015 / 2019) only. This is the most-commonly-misexplained corner of the new regime.
4. **Lay out the LTR 10-of-20 IHT trigger.** Worldwide IHT exposure begins once the 10-of-20 threshold is crossed; the s.6B under-20 variation and the ss.267ZC-267ZF election architecture matter for high-value inbound estates. Pre-arrival estate-planning has a window before the LTR clock starts.
5. **Note the symmetric s.10A trap for returning UK nationals.** A landlord who left, was non-UK-resident for ≤ 5 years, sold non-UK assets during non-residence (e.g. an offshore portfolio at gains) and now arrives back within the 5-year period: those non-residence gains are deemed to arise in the year of return per s.10A. This is the inbound mirror of B6's outbound treatment.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What does "arriving in the UK" mean for tax: when does UK residence start? (SRT determines residence year-by-year; split-year Cases 4-8 can apportion the arrival year so that pre-arrival foreign income is not UK-taxed even though the individual is UK-resident for the whole tax year. Cases are NOT optional; statute determines whether and which case applies.)
2. **What is the FIG 4-year window and who actually qualifies?** (Per s.845B(1): 4 cumulative conditions including the 10-of-prior-10-years non-residence gateway and the age-10 floor. Per-year claim required; failing to claim forfeits that year's relief. Loss of personal allowance + CGT AEA in each claim year — the trade-off must be modelled.)
3. **What income / gains does FIG cover?** (Per s.845H ~22-23 categories — verify verbatim at write time. Cover includes foreign property income, foreign trade profits, foreign pensions, foreign interest / dividends, foreign annuities. Per s.845I, anti-fragmentation excludes UK-source income routed through offshore vehicles.)
4. What is the FIG claim deadline and what happens if missed? (Per s.845A: 31 January 12 months after the end of the relevant tax year, so for 2025-26 claim, the deadline is 31 January 2028. NOT the TMA 1970 s.43 four-year amendment window. Late claims forfeit the year's relief.)
5. **The LTR IHT clock.** When does worldwide IHT exposure begin under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C? (10-of-20 tax years; once crossed, full UK IHT exposure on the worldwide estate.)
6. What is the s.6B under-20 variation? (Per HP §22.X.3: residence years before age 20 weighted differently; relevant for arriving young adults / accompanied minors.)
7. What is the **UK-property rebasing trap**? (FA 2025 Sch 11 condition (3) excludes UK-situs assets; UK BTLs and UK residential property are NOT eligible for FA 2025 rebasing. Inbound non-doms holding pre-arrival UK property cannot step up base cost via FA 2025 Sch 11; the only UK-land rebasing dates are 5 April 2015 (residential) and 5 April 2019 (non-residential) per the separate NRCGT regime at TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A.)
8. What is the NRCGT 60-day position during the pre-arrival window if an arriver disposes of UK land before becoming UK-resident? (60-day return required for every UK land disposal by a non-resident, regardless of tax due, per HP §17.4.)
9. When does TRF help an arriver, and when does it not? (Per HP §17.9: TRF is for individuals who PREVIOUSLY claimed remittance basis. A first-time arriver has no TRF pool; a returning UK non-dom who claimed remittance basis pre-2025-26 may have one and should consider designation by 31 January 2028 deadline.)
10. **The symmetric s.10A trap for returning UK nationals.** Why does selling non-UK assets while abroad not give CGT escape if return is within 5 years? (HP §17.3: temporary non-resident if non-UK-resident for 5 years or less, having been UK-resident in 4 or more of the 7 tax years before departure; gains deemed to arise in year of return.)
11. What does NRL cessation look like? (Per HP §17.5 + §17.7: from the SRT-determined UK-residence start date, the NRL 20% withholding obligation falls away; letting agents / tenants need NRL6 close-out discipline; arriving landlord then files normal SA return for UK property income.)
12. What about destination-state DTA tie-breakers during the arrival year? (Where the arriving individual is simultaneously dual-resident under SRT (UK) and source-state domestic law, Art 4 DTA tie-breaker (HP §16.4) applies. Specific UK-country DTA notes at §16.5 + sibling pages B1 / B2 / B3.)

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The inbound decision tree (the page's first concrete artefact)

Render as semantic HTML `<ol>`. This is the strategic anchor a new arrival needs in the first conversation about UK tax:

1. **Identify your residence year-by-year via SRT.** FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 1 cascade: automatic overseas tests, then automatic UK tests, then sufficient ties. Your arrival date does NOT trigger UK residence by itself; the tax year's day count and ties determine the position.
2. **Check split-year Cases 4-8 for the arrival year.** Case 4 (starting to have only a UK home), Case 5 (starting full-time UK work), Case 6 (ceasing full-time overseas work), Case 7 (partner of a Case-6 individual), Case 8 (starting to have a UK home). Split-year is NOT optional; statute determines which case applies and priority where multiple apply (paras 53-55).
3. **Inventory your prior-10-years residence position.** To claim FIG under ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1), you need to have been NON-UK-resident for each of the 10 tax years preceding the current tax year (gateway). Verify each year via your day-count records. If you were UK-resident in ANY of the previous 10 tax years, FIG is NOT available; you enter as an arising-basis resident.
4. **Decide whether to claim FIG year-by-year.** Per s.845A, claim is per-year + irrevocable for that year. Claiming costs your UK personal allowance (currently £12,570 frozen to April 2028) + dividend allowance + CGT AEA (£3,000). Claim only where your foreign income / gains for that tax year exceed the lost-allowance value. The break-even is roughly £25k-£30k of foreign income at additional rate; lower at basic / higher rate.
5. **Inventory your UK property holdings BEFORE the arrival date.** Any UK property disposal made while still non-resident is NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A + 60-day return per §17.4 (regardless of tax due). UK rental income falls under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 / CTA 2009 Part 4 regardless of your residence.
6. **Stop the NRL withholding from the residence start date.** Once your SRT analysis makes you UK-resident, FA 1995 Sch 23 NRL withholding falls away. Notify your letting agents + tenants in writing; complete NRL6 close-out paperwork; arrange normal SA self-assessment for the post-arrival rental income.
7. **Check your inbound IHT position under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C.** You become LTR (worldwide-IHT exposure) once UK-resident in 10 of the preceding 20 tax years. For a first-time arrival, the clock starts at year 1 of UK residence; LTR exposure triggers from year 10 (typically) of UK residence. Pre-arrival estate planning has a window before the clock starts.
8. **Returning UK nationals: check s.10A.** If you left UK ≤ 5 years ago, were UK-resident in 4+ of the 7 preceding tax years at that departure, and sold non-UK assets during your non-residence, those gains are deemed to arise in your year of return per TCGA 1992 s.10A. The CGT crystallises NOW (year of arrival), not when you originally sold.
9. **Returning non-doms with pre-2025 unremitted accumulations: consider TRF.** Per FA 2025 Sch 10, designation at 12% (2025-26), 12% (2026-27), 15% (2027-28); window closes 5 April 2028. A pre-2025-26 remittance-basis user who is now returning to UK can designate the pool at TRF rate to crystallise as clean capital.
10. **Inbound non-doms holding pre-arrival UK property: rebasing is NOT available.** Per FA 2025 Sch 11 para 1(1) condition 3, UK situs assets are EXCLUDED from rebasing. Your London BTL acquired in 2019 cannot be rebased to 5 April 2017 value; the only UK-land rebasing dates are 5 April 2015 (residential) / 5 April 2019 (non-residential) under the NRCGT regime per §17.4.
11. **Check source-state DTA tie-breakers for the arrival year.** If you remain dual-resident under SRT (UK) and your departure country's domestic law for part of the arrival year, Art 4 DTA tie-breaker cascade applies (permanent home, centre of vital interests, habitual abode, nationality, mutual agreement). Cross-link to B1 / B2 / B3 for country-specific notes.
12. **Engage specialist representation pre-arrival.** Pre-arrival timing of foreign-asset disposals, pension-pot positioning, share-vesting events, and trust distributions matters materially. Post-arrival the planning window narrows dramatically.

### Example 2 — Mrs Khoury, first-time arrival from Lebanon (the classic inbound non-dom)

Mrs Khoury (Lebanese citizen; never previously UK-resident; arrives London October 2026 with husband + 2 children; intends to settle long-term; brings c. £4.2m investment portfolio held with Beirut + Geneva private banks generating c. £180k/year of foreign dividend + interest + bond-coupon income; also holds a Lebanese rental property generating c. £45k/year; planning to purchase a £3.5m London family home + a £1.2m London BTL once settled). Position:

- **SRT 2026/27 arrival year:** depending on UK day count + work pattern, likely Case 4 (starting to have only a UK home — she retains the Beirut apartment so possibly Case 8 starting to have a UK home with overseas home retained) or Case 5 if her husband starts full-time UK work + she's the partner. Split-year may apportion the tax year so that pre-October 2026 foreign income is outside UK scope.
- **FIG eligibility:** Mrs Khoury has been non-UK-resident for every tax year before 2026/27 (10-of-prior-10 gateway clearly met per s.845B(1)); aged > 10; not disqualified → ELIGIBLE. She can claim FIG for each of tax years 2026/27, 2027/28, 2028/29, 2029/30 (4-year window).
- **FIG claim economics:** Mrs Khoury's annual foreign income is £180k + Lebanese rental £45k = £225k. UK personal allowance (£12,570) + dividend allowance (£500 from April 2024) + CGT AEA (£3,000) total c. £16k. Lost-allowance cost of claiming FIG at her marginal additional rate (45%) is c. £7,200 of additional tax exposure on the lost allowances. Net FIG saving: £225k × 45% = £101,250 of UK tax avoided − £7,200 lost-allowance cost = c. £94,000 net saving per year. CLAIM. Total 4-year FIG benefit c. £376,000.
- **FIG claim deadlines:** 31 January 2028 for 2026/27 claim; 31 January 2029 for 2027/28; etc. Per s.845A, late claim forfeits the year's relief.
- **UK property income (post-purchase):** Mrs Khoury's planned UK BTL purchase generates UK property income — UK-taxable under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 from purchase date regardless of FIG status. FIG covers FOREIGN income / gains only.
- **UK property purchases:** SDLT scope. The £3.5m London family home triggers SDLT at residential rates + 3% surcharge if she already owns the Lebanese property (verify additional-dwelling test); first-time-buyer relief NOT available (she's a property owner). The £1.2m BTL triggers SDLT residential rates + 5% surcharge from 31 October 2024 (additional dwelling — surcharged at the £125k+ threshold per FA 2003 + SI 2024/1015 increase).
- **Inbound IHT under LTR per §22.X:** Mrs Khoury is at year 1 of UK residence in 2026/27. LTR trigger comes at year 10 of UK residence (assuming she stays continuously UK-resident). Pre-LTR worldwide-asset IHT exposure starts only once 10-of-20 crossed; meanwhile UK situs property is in UK IHT regardless. Pre-trigger window for non-UK estate-planning (e.g. settling non-UK assets into excluded-property trusts before the 10-year clock crosses) is operationally precious.
- **Pre-arrival planning lost:** rebalancing the £4.2m portfolio to crystallise gains BEFORE arrival would have crystallised those gains outside UK CGT scope (she was non-UK-resident at the time). Post-arrival the portfolio's accrued gains crystallise under UK CGT (subject to FIG for the 4-year window only).
- **CGT rebasing UNAVAILABLE for her UK-property purchases:** even though she purchases the London home + London BTL AFTER becoming UK-resident (Sch 11 condition 1 fails — she does not hold them on 5 April 2017). Standard acquisition-cost base under TCGA 1992 ss.38-39 applies.
- **Cross-check on rebasing for her Beirut portfolio:** Beirut + Geneva-held non-UK shares acquired pre-5 April 2017 might satisfy Sch 11 conditions 1 + 3 (not UK situs) + 4 (not UK-domiciled — she is Lebanese) + 5 (claim made under s.809B at least once 2017-18 to 2024-25 — she has NEVER been UK-resident so NEVER claimed remittance basis → FAILS condition 5). Rebasing UNAVAILABLE. She enters with portfolio at original Lebanese acquisition cost.

### Example 3 — Mr Olsson, returning Swedish business owner with London BTL (4 years out, came back)

Mr Olsson (Swedish citizen; UK-resident 2015/16-2021/22 — 7 tax years; held London BTL bought 2018 + Swedish investment portfolio acquired 2010; moved back to Stockholm 2022/23; sold Swedish portfolio for £350k gain in 2024/25 while Swedish-resident; returns to UK April 2026 for new corporate role; sole shareholder + director of UK Ltd through which he holds the BTL). Position:

- **SRT 2026/27 return year:** Mr Olsson satisfies automatic UK tests if full-time UK work starts April 2026 → UK-resident 2026/27. Split-year Case 5 (starting full-time UK work) likely applies; pre-arrival part-year outside UK income tax scope.
- **FIG eligibility check:** s.845B(1)(b) requires NON-UK-resident for each of the 10 tax years before 2026/27. Mr Olsson was UK-resident 2015/16-2021/22 — within the 10-year lookback (2016/17-2025/26 inclusive) he was UK-resident for 6 years. **FAILS the 10-of-prior-10-years gateway → FIG NOT AVAILABLE.** He enters as a standard arising-basis resident.

Estimated UK CGT at higher rate 20% = £70k.
- **Sweden-side credit:** Sweden taxed Mr Olsson on the £350k gain in 2024/25 (Swedish-resident at disposal). Pragmatic options now: ensure Swedish-side credit is maximised; consider deferring further non-UK disposals to post-2031/32 to avoid more s.10A exposure on any later realisations.

### Example 4 — FA 2025 Sch 11 rebasing election: why an inbound non-dom's UK BTL CANNOT be rebased

Mr Hayek (Brazilian citizen; UK-resident 2018/19-2021/22 claimed remittance basis under ITA 2007 s.809B; left UK 2022/23; returns October 2026 intending to settle; holds a £980k Lisbon flat acquired 2014 + a London BTL acquired 2017 + a Hong Kong shares portfolio acquired 2010). 2026: Mr Hayek hears about the "FA 2025 rebasing election to 5 April 2017 value" and assumes his London BTL + Lisbon flat + Hong Kong portfolio can all be rebased.

- **Threshold note:** Mr Hayek satisfies FIG gateway? He was UK-resident 2018/19-2021/22 — within the lookback period of 10 tax years before 2026/27 (2016/17-2025/26 inclusive) → FAILS the 10-of-prior-10-years gateway → FIG NOT AVAILABLE. He enters as arising-basis resident.
- **Rebasing election per §17.10 — five conditions per Sch 11 para 1(1):**
  - **(1) Asset held on 5 April 2017** — London BTL acquired 2017 (verify exact contract / completion date at write — purchases completing 6 April 2017 onwards FAIL); Lisbon flat acquired 2014 PASSES; Hong Kong portfolio acquired 2010 PASSES.
  - **(2) Disposal on or after 6 April 2025** — PASSES for any post-2026 disposal.
  - **(3) Asset NOT situated in UK between 6 March 2024 and 5 April 2025** — London BTL is UK situs throughout → **FAILS condition 3 ABSOLUTELY for the London BTL.** Lisbon flat is Portuguese situs → PASSES. Hong Kong portfolio (assuming HK-incorporated shares + HK-bank custody) → non-UK situs → PASSES.
  - **(4) Individual not domiciled in UK before tax year 2025-26** — Mr Hayek is Brazilian-domiciled-of-origin → PASSES (assuming he has not acquired UK domicile-of-choice; high evidential bar for displacing origin domicile).
  - **(5) Claim made under ITA 2007 s.809B at least once in tax years 2017-18 to 2024-25** — Mr Hayek claimed remittance basis 2018/19-2021/22 (4 tax years within the 2017-18 to 2024-25 window) → PASSES.
- **Conclusion:**
  - **London BTL CANNOT be rebased under FA 2025 Sch 11 (condition 3 absolute exclusion).** UK property is UK situs throughout — Sch 11 rebasing is non-UK-asset-only. The misconception that "rebasing applies to all assets held by non-doms" is wrong for UK situs.
  - **UK-property-specific rebasing per §17.4:** UK land follows its OWN NRCGT rebasing dates — 5 April 2015 default for residential acquired before that date; 5 April 2019 for non-residential. Mr Hayek's London BTL acquired 2017 → no NRCGT rebasing benefit either (acquired AFTER 5 April 2015 residential default; cost is acquisition cost).
  - **Lisbon flat MAY be rebased** to 5 April 2017 value if all five conditions met (1 PASSES — held 2014; 3 PASSES — Portuguese situs; 4 + 5 PASS). Acquisition cost 2014 vs 5 April 2017 value: if the flat appreciated 2014-2017, rebasing UPSTEPS Mr Hayek's base cost → reduces UK CGT on later disposal. Election irrevocable per Sch 11 para 3 + per-disposal (not portfolio-wide).
  - **Hong Kong portfolio MAY be rebased** similarly (all five conditions met assuming HK situs holds).
  - **Default IS rebasing for qualifying assets per Sch 11 para 3;** opt-out election available where 5 April 2017 value is LOWER than original cost (rare — usually rebasing helps).
- **Operational lesson:** the inbound non-dom needs a per-asset rebasing analysis BEFORE making any post-arrival disposals. UK situs assets are out of scope absolutely; non-UK situs assets need the 5-condition test; some assets may benefit, some may not.

### Example 5 — The LTR 10-of-20 clock for HNW first-time arrivals (Sir Henderson archetype)

Sir Henderson (US citizen + UK-resident-on-arrival; arrives March 2026 (tax year 2025/26) for retirement; HNW estate: $42m total worldwide; $6m UK residential property (London townhouse purchased 2018 via UK Ltd — held to date), $28m US-based investment portfolio + $4m US business interests + $4m UK-based investment trust). Position:

- **UK arrival 2025/26:** Sir Henderson is UK-resident from arrival year. FIG gateway check: never UK-resident before → 10-of-prior-10 PASSES; aged > 10 → CLEAR FIG ELIGIBILITY. Can claim FIG for 2025/26-2028/29 (4 tax years).
- **LTR clock per IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C:** at end of 2025/26, Sir Henderson has been UK-resident 1 of preceding 20 tax years. NOT LTR. Worldwide assets outside UK IHT.
- **LTR trigger timing:** if Sir Henderson remains continuously UK-resident, LTR trigger comes at end of 2034/35 (10 of preceding 20 tax years: 2025/26-2034/35 inclusive). At that point his worldwide $42m estate enters UK IHT (assuming he survives to that date + UK NRB / RNRB unchanged + no spouse exemption planning).
- **UK situs property remains in UK IHT regardless** per §22.X.4 — his UK Ltd holding the London townhouse is subject to Sch A1 enveloped-residential look-through (effective 6 April 2017): the Ltd's London townhouse is in UK IHT regardless of LTR status. His UK investment trust (UK-situs) is in UK IHT regardless. So $6m + $4m = $10m of UK-situs property is in UK IHT scope from day 1 of arrival.
- **Pre-LTR window for non-UK estate planning:** Sir Henderson has c. 9 years of non-LTR status. Settling non-UK assets into an EXCLUDED PROPERTY TRUST per IHTA 1984 s.48 BEFORE the LTR trigger date is CRITICAL: assets settled while settlor is non-LTR are excluded property for IHT purposes; assets settled AFTER LTR trigger lose excluded-property status. Specialist private-client team essential. Note: s.48 architecture itself was reformed by FA 2025 (the old s.48(3) non-UK-domiciled-settlor test was OMITTED) — replaced by s.48ZA LTR-based test per §22.X.7 (verify exact mechanics at write — load-bearing for HNW inbound estate planning).
- **s.6B under-20 variation per §22.X.3:** Sir Henderson is > 20 years old → variation does NOT apply. (For arriving young adults or accompanied minors aged < 20 at arrival, residence years count differently per s.6B — see §22.X.3 micro-lock for detail.)
- **LTR election framework per §22.X.5 (ss.267ZC-267ZF):** Sir Henderson may have election options once LTR trigger approaches — to elect into worldwide-IHT scope EARLIER for spousal-exemption purposes (where spouse is LTR + Sir Henderson is not). Specialist verification at write.
- **TRF UNAVAILABLE:** Sir Henderson has never been UK-resident pre-2025-26 → no remittance-basis pool → no TRF designation eligibility. (TRF is for the cleanup of HISTORIC remittance-basis users only.)
- **Verdict:** Sir Henderson should claim FIG for the 4-year window (2025/26-2028/29), maximising income / gains realisations from his $28m US portfolio + $4m US business interests during the window. Post-window, arising-basis on worldwide income / gains. Pre-LTR estate planning window (years 1-9 of UK residence) is precious for non-UK asset trust settlement. UK situs property is in UK IHT regardless from day 1 — no planning option there beyond standard NRB / RNRB + spouse exemption.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: I'm arriving in UK to settle permanently — what is the single most important thing to understand about UK tax?**
   A: There are TWO equally important entry points. (a) The FIG regime per ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J (inserted by FA 2025 s.37) gives a 4-year window for new arrivals to elect foreign income / gains out of UK tax, BUT the gateway is strict (10 tax years non-UK-resident before arrival per s.845B(1)) and the claim costs your UK personal allowance + CGT AEA each year. (b) The IHT LTR test per IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C triggers worldwide-IHT exposure once you've been UK-resident in 10 of the preceding 20 tax years. For a first-time arrival, that typically means LTR trigger at year 10 of UK residence. Pre-arrival estate planning has a window before the clock starts.

2. **Q: How does the Statutory Residence Test apply in my arrival year?**
   A: FA 2013 Sch 45 Part 1 cascade: (i) automatic overseas tests (under 16 UK days if UK-resident in any of preceding 3 years; under 46 if not; full-time overseas work satisfying the 35-hour test); (ii) automatic UK tests (183+ UK days; only home in UK for 91+ consecutive days; full-time UK work); (iii) sufficient ties test combining UK ties (family, accommodation, work, 90-day, country) with UK day count per HMRC RDR3 Annex A. For arrivals, split-year Cases 4-8 in Sch 45 Part 3 can apportion the arrival year so that pre-arrival foreign income is outside UK scope even though you are UK-resident for the whole tax year. Split-year is NOT optional; statute determines whether and which case applies.

3. **Q: What are the split-year arrival Cases 4-8 and which one applies to me?**
   A: Case 4 (starting to have only a UK home — your previous overseas home ceases); Case 5 (starting full-time UK work meeting the 35-hour test); Case 6 (ceasing full-time overseas work that previously made you non-resident); Case 7 (partner of someone in Case 6); Case 8 (starting to have a UK home — UK home acquired but overseas home retained). Where multiple cases could apply, statute determines priority per Sch 45 Part 3 paras 53-55. Cases vary in their split-date determination — Case 4 + 8 around the home test; Case 5 around the work-start date; Case 6 + 7 around the overseas-work-cessation date.

4. **Q: I've been working in Hong Kong for 6 years — can I claim the FIG regime when I arrive in UK?**
   A: It depends on the gateway test. Per ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1)(b), you need to have been NON-UK-resident for each of the **10 tax years** before the year you claim. 6 years is insufficient — you need 10. If you were last UK-resident in 2019/20 and arrive 2026/27, you have been non-UK-resident for tax years 2020/21-2025/26 (6 years) → 4 years short. You enter UK as a standard arising-basis resident. Specialist pre-arrival planning is essential: timing foreign-asset disposals BEFORE arrival, pension positioning, share-vesting events.

5. **Q: I qualify for FIG — should I claim it every year for 4 years automatically?**
   A: NO — model the trade-off per year. Per ITTOIA 2005 s.845A, claiming FIG for a tax year FORFEITS your UK personal allowance (£12,570 to 5 April 2028), dividend allowance (£500), and CGT AEA (£3,000). The lost-allowance cost at additional rate (45%) is c. £7,200 of additional tax. Break-even is roughly £25k-£30k of foreign income / gains per year at additional rate (lower at basic / higher rate). If your foreign income is below the break-even, NOT claiming FIG (i.e. accepting arising-basis taxation on the foreign income) is cheaper. Per-year decision.

6. **Q: What is the FIG claim deadline?**
   A: Per ITTOIA 2005 s.845A, 31 January 12 months after the end of the relevant tax year. For tax year 2025/26, deadline is 31 January 2028. For tax year 2026/27, 31 January 2029. NOT the TMA 1970 s.43 generic 4-year amendment window. Late claim FORFEITS that year's relief — irrevocable.

7. **Q: I hold a London BTL that I bought as a non-resident before arriving — can I rebase it to 5 April 2017 value under the FA 2025 election?**
   A: NO. Per FA 2025 Sch 11 para 1(1) condition 3 (§17.10), assets situated in UK between 6 March 2024 and 5 April 2025 are EXCLUDED from rebasing. Your London BTL is UK situs throughout — condition 3 fails ABSOLUTELY. The only UK-land rebasing dates are 5 April 2015 (residential acquired before that date) / 5 April 2019 (non-residential) under the NRCGT regime per §17.4 — those don't help if you acquired post-2015 / 2019 either. Your base cost for UK CGT on later disposal is your original acquisition cost.

8. **Q: I sold a UK rental flat before arriving in UK — did I trigger any UK tax obligations?**
   A: YES. NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A applies to non-resident disposals of UK land — 60-day NRCGT return required regardless of whether tax is due. Residential rates 18% / 24% from 30 October 2024 per §17.4. Even if your gain was £0 or you sold at a loss, the 60-day return is mandatory. If you missed the deadline, Sch 55 FA 2009 penalty machinery applies; advise specialist remediation.

9. **Q: When do I become liable to UK IHT on my worldwide assets?**
   A: Per IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C (§22.X), you become a Long-Term UK Resident once you've been UK-resident in 10 of the preceding 20 tax years. For a first-time arrival with no prior UK residence in the preceding 20 tax years, LTR trigger typically comes at year 10 of continuous UK residence. UK situs property is in UK IHT REGARDLESS of LTR status (Sch A1 look-through for enveloped UK residential property effective 6 April 2017). The under-20-year-old variation per s.6B applies if you arrive aged under 20. Pre-LTR window for non-UK estate planning (excluded-property trust settlement) is operationally critical for HNW arrivals.

10. **Q: I'm a returning UK national who sold a non-UK investment portfolio while abroad — am I exposed to UK CGT on return?**
    A: POTENTIALLY YES via TCGA 1992 s.10A temporary non-residence (§17.3). If you (a) were UK-resident in 4+ of the 7 tax years before departure AND (b) your non-UK-residence period is 5 years or less, the non-UK-portfolio gain realised during non-residence is DEEMED to arise in your year of return + chargeable to UK CGT then. To shed s.10A you needed MORE than 5 complete non-UK tax years. UK property gains during non-residence are caught by NRCGT independently. Foreign-side tax credit may be available via TIOPA 2010 s.18 but only up to the lower of UK / foreign tax.

11. **Q: I claimed UK remittance basis in 2019/20 and 2020/21 before leaving — do I have a TRF pool to designate?**
    A: POSSIBLY. Per FA 2025 Sch 10 (§17.9), TRF designation is for individuals with pre-2025-26 unremitted foreign income / gains from a tax year in which they claimed remittance basis under ITA 2007 s.809B. If you have unremitted foreign income / gains accrued in your 2019/20 or 2020/21 remittance-basis years, the pool is designatable. Rates 12% (2025-26), 12% (2026-27), 15% (2027-28); designation deadline 31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year per Sch 10 para 8(1); window closes 5 April 2028. Designation crystallises the pool as clean capital. Whether to designate depends on the pool size + your post-arrival remittance plans + specialist advice.

12. **Q: How does my pre-arrival country's DTA interact with my UK position?**
    A: During the part of the arrival year where you are dual-resident under SRT (UK) + source-state domestic law, Art 4 DTA tie-breaker resolves residence allocation via permanent home → centre of vital interests → habitual abode → nationality → mutual agreement (HP §16.4). Source-state taxing rights on income arising before the UK-residence allocation persist. Specific-DTA cross-links: UK-Spain (B2), UK-India (B3), UK-IoM (B1) for country-specific notes. Where source-state taxation persists during the arrival-year split, UK FTC via TIOPA 2010 s.18 may relieve double taxation.

13. **Q: I'm bringing UK rental property with me — do I still need NRL withholding once I become UK-resident?**
    A: NO. Once SRT makes you UK-resident, the FA 1995 Sch 23 NRL withholding obligation falls away from your UK-residence start date. Notify your letting agent + tenants in writing (NRL6 close-out paperwork); the agent / tenant stops withholding 20%; you self-assess UK rental income on SA100 + SA105 going forward. Practical step: arrange the NRL cessation paperwork promptly to avoid letting-agent admin lag.

14. **Q: I'm arriving with a Cayman / Jersey / Singapore trust I settled before arrival — what happens to it?**
    A: Pre-arrival trust settlements where you (as settlor) were non-UK-resident + non-LTR at the time of settlement typically qualify as excluded property per IHTA 1984 s.48 architecture (now reframed by FA 2025 — s.48(3) original deemed-domicile test OMITTED, replaced by new s.48ZA LTR test per §22.X.7). Once you become LTR (year 10 of UK residence typically), any FURTHER settlements into the trust lose excluded-property status; the existing settled property may also be reviewed per the s.48ZA mechanics. Pre-LTR trust planning for HNW arrivals is a specialist area requiring private-client counsel + tax counsel.
