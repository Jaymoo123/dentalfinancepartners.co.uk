# Arriving in the UK: the inbound landlord's checklist for FIG, LTR, SRT split-year and the UK-property trap that rebasing will not save

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (residence determination — inbound):**
  - **FA 2013 Schedule 45** — Statutory Residence Test (SRT), with split-year Cases 4-8 in Part 3 specifically handling arriving individuals. Case 4 (starting to have only a UK home), Case 5 (starting full-time UK work), Case 6 (ceasing full-time overseas work), Case 7 (partner of someone in Case 6), Case 8 (starting to have a UK home). Where multiple cases apply, statute determines priority per Sch 45 Part 3 paras 53-55.
- **Primary (FIG 4-year window for new arrivals):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Chapter 5 ss.845A-845J** inserted by **FA 2025 s.37 + Sch 8**. Effective tax year 2025-26.
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1)** — qualifying new resident test: four cumulative conditions per HP §17.8: (a) UK-resident for current tax year, (b) NOT UK-resident for each of the 10 tax years before that tax year, (c) not disqualified per s.845B(2), (d) individual at least 10 years old at commencement of the tax year.
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845A** — claim mechanism; relief deducted at Step 2 of the ITA 2007 s.23 income tax calculation. Claim deadline **31 January 12 months after end of relevant tax year** per the s.845A regime (NOT the TMA 1970 s.43 generic 4-year amendment window).
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845H** — qualifying foreign income categories (broad scope ~22-23 distinct types; verify verbatim at write time per §16.35).
  - **ITTOIA 2005 s.845I** — disqualified income (anti-fragmentation; excludes UK-derived income channelled through offshore structures).
- **Primary (IHT residence-based regime — long-term-resident trigger):**
  - **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C** — Long-Term Resident (LTR) test: 10-of-20 tax years (FA 2025 architecture). LTR worldwide-IHT exposure begins on the day the 10-of-20 threshold is crossed.
  - **IHTA 1984 s.6B** — under-20 variation (HP §22.X.3 micro-lock).
  - **IHTA 1984 ss.267ZC-267ZF** — LTR election framework + tail-period (HP §22.X.5).
- **Primary (CGT for any continuing non-residence + UK-property asymmetry):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** — NRCGT continues to apply to any pre-arrival UK land disposal made while still non-resident; 60-day return required regardless of tax due.
  - **FA 2025 s.42 + Sch 11** — the CGT rebasing election. **Five conditions per Sch 11 para 1(1)** per HP §17.10; **condition 3 excludes UK situs assets** — a UK property held by an inbound non-dom is NOT rebased under Sch 11. UK land follows the NRCGT-specific rebasing dates (5 April 2015 residential, 5 April 2019 non-residential) per §17.4.
- **Primary (returning non-dom transitional — TRF):**
  - **FA 2025 s.41 + Sch 10** — Temporary Repatriation Facility. Relevant only for individuals who PREVIOUSLY claimed remittance basis pre-2025-26 (an arriver who has never been UK-resident before will not have a TRF designation pool). Rates 12% / 12% / 15% for 2025-26 / 2026-27 / 2027-28; window closes 5 April 2028.
- **Primary (continuing UK rentals during initial UK-resident year — NRL turn-off):**
  - **FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902** — NRL scheme. Once arrival makes the landlord UK-resident under SRT, the NRL withholding obligation falls away from the residence date; agents / tenants need NRL6 cessation discipline (worth flagging operationally).
- **Primary (s.10A symmetric position for returning UK national):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.10A** — temporary non-residence (verified against HMRC CG26540 per HP §17.3 sibling verification 2026-05-22). Relevant where a UK national who left, sold non-UK assets while non-resident, and now returns within 5 years finds those gains crystallise in the year of return.
- **House position reference:** **§17 in full**, §17.1 (SRT), §17.2 (split-year Cases 4-8 specifically), §17.3 (s.10A symmetric trap for returnees), §17.4 (NRCGT continuing-non-residence relevance + rebasing dates), §17.5 (NRL turn-off mechanics), §17.6 (domicile reform + FIG headline), **§17.8 (FIG operational depth — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, **§17.10 (rebasing narrow eligibility, condition-3 UK-situs exclusion — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, **§22.X (IHT LTR — Wave 8 mini-lock)**, §22.X.1 (10-of-20 test), §22.X.3 (s.6B under-20 variation), §22.X.5 (ss.267ZC-267ZF election framework), §16 (DTA framing for source-state tie-breaker context).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)
This is the **inbound mirror** of B6 (`are-you-leaving-the-uk-permanently`) and the inverse-direction page of the bucket's expat cluster. The framing differentiator is the **honest FIG-vs-rebasing-vs-LTR triangulation** that the popular "4 years tax-free for new arrivals" framing flattens. Specifically the page must:

1. **Refuse the 4-years-tax-free shorthand.** The FIG window is per-year-claim with **lost personal allowance + lost CGT AEA** in each claim year. Claiming FIG is net-positive only where foreign income / gains for that year exceed the lost allowance value — sessions writing planning copy must hedge.
2. **Anchor the 10-of-prior-10-years gateway.** This is the operative entry test for FIG per s.845B(1) — not "non-dom" status, which is no longer the door from 6 April 2025. An inbound expat who was last UK-resident in 2017-18 (i.e. 8 tax years of prior non-residence by 2025-26) does NOT qualify for FIG; they enter as a standard arising-basis resident.
3. **Walk the UK-property rebasing trap.** Condition (3) of FA 2025 Sch 11 para 1(1) excludes UK-situs assets. An inbound non-dom holding a UK BTL pre-arrival cannot use Sch 11 rebasing to step up base cost; UK land has its own NRCGT-specific rebasing dates (5 April 2015 / 2019) only. This is the most-commonly-misexplained corner of the new regime.
4. **Lay out the LTR 10-of-20 IHT trigger.** Worldwide IHT exposure begins once the 10-of-20 threshold is crossed; the s.6B under-20 variation and the ss.267ZC-267ZF election architecture matter for high-value inbound estates. Pre-arrival estate-planning has a window before the LTR clock starts.
5. **Note the symmetric s.10A trap for returning UK nationals.** A landlord who left, was non-UK-resident for ≤ 5 years, sold non-UK assets during non-residence (e.g. an offshore portfolio at gains) and now arrives back within the 5-year period: those non-residence gains are deemed to arise in the year of return per s.10A. This is the inbound mirror of B6's outbound treatment.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What does "arriving in the UK" mean for tax — when does UK residence start? (SRT determines residence year-by-year; split-year Cases 4-8 can apportion the arrival year so that pre-arrival foreign income is not UK-taxed even though the individual is UK-resident for the whole tax year. Cases are NOT optional; statute determines whether and which case applies.)
2. **What is the FIG 4-year window and who actually qualifies?** (Per s.845B(1): 4 cumulative conditions including the 10-of-prior-10-years non-residence gateway and the age-10 floor. Per-year claim required; failing to claim forfeits that year's relief. Loss of personal allowance + CGT AEA in each claim year — the trade-off must be modelled.)
3. **What income / gains does FIG cover?** (Per s.845H ~22-23 categories — verify verbatim at write time. Cover includes foreign property income, foreign trade profits, foreign pensions, foreign interest / dividends, foreign annuities. Per s.845I — anti-fragmentation excludes UK-source income routed through offshore vehicles.)
4. What is the FIG claim deadline and what happens if missed? (Per s.845A: 31 January 12 months after the end of the relevant tax year — for 2025-26 claim, the deadline is 31 January 2028. NOT the TMA 1970 s.43 four-year amendment window. Late claims forfeit the year's relief.)
5. **The LTR IHT clock.** When does worldwide IHT exposure begin under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C? (10-of-20 tax years; once crossed, full UK IHT exposure on the worldwide estate.)
6. What is the s.6B under-20 variation? (Per HP §22.X.3: residence years before age 20 weighted differently; relevant for arriving young adults / accompanied minors.)
7. What is the **UK-property rebasing trap**? (FA 2025 Sch 11 condition (3) excludes UK-situs assets; UK BTLs and UK residential property are NOT eligible for FA 2025 rebasing. Inbound non-doms holding pre-arrival UK property cannot step up base cost via FA 2025 Sch 11; the only UK-land rebasing dates are 5 April 2015 (residential) and 5 April 2019 (non-residential) per the separate NRCGT regime at TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A.)
8. What is the NRCGT 60-day position during the pre-arrival window if an arriver disposes of UK land before becoming UK-resident? (60-day return required for every UK land disposal by a non-resident, regardless of tax due, per HP §17.4.)
9. When does TRF help an arriver, and when does it not? (Per HP §17.9: TRF is for individuals who PREVIOUSLY claimed remittance basis. A first-time arriver has no TRF pool; a returning UK non-dom who claimed remittance basis pre-2025-26 may have one and should consider designation by 31 January 2028 deadline.)
10. **The symmetric s.10A trap for returning UK nationals.** Why does selling non-UK assets while abroad not give CGT escape if return is within 5 years? (HP §17.3: temporary non-resident if non-UK-resident for 5 years or less, having been UK-resident in 4 or more of the 7 tax years before departure; gains deemed to arise in year of return.)
11. What does NRL cessation look like? (Per HP §17.5 + §17.7: from the SRT-determined UK-residence start date, the NRL 20% withholding obligation falls away; letting agents / tenants need NRL6 close-out discipline; arriving landlord then files normal SA return for UK property income.)
12. What about destination-state DTA tie-breakers during the arrival year? (Where the arriving individual is simultaneously dual-resident under SRT (UK) and source-state domestic law, Art 4 DTA tie-breaker (HP §16.4) applies. Specific UK-country DTA notes at §16.5 + sibling pages B1 / B2 / B3.)
