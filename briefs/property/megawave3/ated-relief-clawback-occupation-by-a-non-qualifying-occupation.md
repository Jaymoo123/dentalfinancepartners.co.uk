# ATED relief clawback under FA 2013 s.135: the look-forward and look-back trap when a non-qualifying individual (s.136) occupies the dwelling

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (the relief whose conditions clawback unwinds):**
  - **FA 2013 s.133** — "Property rental businesses" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). A day qualifies for relief if the single-dwelling interest is "being exploited as a source of rents or other receipts (other than excluded rents) in the course of a qualifying property rental business". A "qualifying property rental business" is one "run on a commercial basis and with a view to profit". Relief is **DENIED** if "a non-qualifying individual is permitted to occupy the dwelling" on that day.
- **Primary (the clawback / look-forward + look-back mechanic):**
  - **FA 2013 s.135** — "Non-qualifying occupation: look-forward and look-back" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Look-forward: if a non-qualifying individual occupies a dwelling on any day, "No subsequent day in that chargeable period, or in any of the subsequent 3 chargeable periods" can be treated as relievable unless a day of qualifying use under s.133(1)(a) intervenes. Look-back: earlier days are not relievable if "a relevant person is entitled to the single-dwelling interest on that day," unless a s.133(1)(a) qualifying-use day falls between the earlier day and the non-qualifying occupation day. "Relevant person" includes (i) the person entitled on the non-qualifying occupation day, or (ii) any partnership member who exploited the interest in a property rental business.
- **Primary (definition of non-qualifying individual):**
  - **FA 2013 s.136** — "Meaning of 'non-qualifying individual'" (verified at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Subsection (1) sets out the eight-category catalogue covering: (a) individuals entitled to the interest (otherwise than as partnership member); connected persons; partnership members; settlors of trusts holding the interest; relatives of connected persons / settlors; spouses / civil partners of relatives; participants in collective investment schemes; and persons connected to those participants. Cross-applied to s.133 and s.135 — and to the developer variant at s.139.
- **Primary (developer-chain analogue):**
  - **FA 2013 s.139** — "Property developers: exchange of dwellings" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Sub-section (2): "A day is not relievable by virtue of this section if on that day a non-qualifying individual is permitted to occupy the dwelling." Developer-exchange path has the same non-qualifying-individual exclusion architecture as s.133 rental.
- **Primary (the CIHC qualifying-purpose carve-out interaction):**
  - **CTA 2010 s.18N(3)** — close investment-holding company test. Where the enveloped company's purpose is letting commercially to non-connected persons, it escapes CIHC treatment (and gets the small-profits 19% rate). Letting to connected persons OR their spouses OR relatives of connected persons OR relatives' spouses defeats the qualifying-purpose carve-out. The CIHC test mirrors but is NOT identical to the s.136 non-qualifying-individual catalogue — sessions must not conflate.
- **Primary (interim relief mechanic):**
  - **FA 2013 ss.99-100 + s.101** — interim relief allows the chargeable amount to be reduced for relief-eligible portions of the chargeable period. The interim relief claim is on the return; clawback under s.135 generates adjusted chargeable amount returns under s.163 if non-qualifying occupation supervenes mid-period.
  - **FA 2013 s.163** — "Return of adjusted chargeable amount"; required where relief gained or lost during the chargeable period.
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 — verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full)**, §18.3 (relief catalogue), §18.4 (return mechanics + relief clawback architectural note: "Relief is **clawed back** where conditions cease to be met within a period of non-qualifying occupation. The most common clawback trigger is occupation by a connected non-qualifying individual — director's family member moving in for a few months while between rentals"), §18.8 (do-not-write list), §21.A (CT three-figure framework — connection to CIHC), §21.5 (CIHC depth — qualifying-purpose carve-out and connected-occupant interaction).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)
This page is the **clawback-mechanic deep-dive** for the most punitive ATED relief structure: the s.135 look-forward / look-back rules. The popular framing — "if a family member moves in, you lose the year's relief" — is **structurally wrong on the look-forward side**: s.135 extends loss of relief into the next 3 chargeable periods unless a qualifying-use day under s.133(1)(a) intervenes. A single weekend of a director's daughter staying in the dwelling at year-end can poison the next 3 chargeable years of relief if the dwelling sits vacant or off-let pending refurbishment.

The framing differentiator is the **architecturally honest treatment of the look-forward + look-back combination**: relief is fragile, period-extension is harsh, and the only escape is interposing genuine qualifying-use days (commercial arm's-length letting to unconnected tenants). This page sits **adjacent to but distinct from** B12 (`ated-relief-for-related-persons-and-market-rent-a-complete-guide`, which covers the WHEN-can-relief-coexist-with-connected-party-let question + market-rent positioning) and B5 (the strategic-decision ATED page).

Specifically the page must:

1. **Refuse the year-loss collapse.** Look-forward is up to **3 subsequent chargeable periods** under s.135. Sessions writing "you lose the year" misstate the architecture — the answer is "you lose up to 3 subsequent years unless a s.133(1)(a) qualifying-use day intervenes."
2. **Walk the s.136 non-qualifying individual eight categories.** The catalogue is wider than most owners assume. Settlors of trusts holding the interest are caught; partnership members are caught; spouses of relatives of connected persons are caught (multiple-hop family).
3. **Anchor the look-back mechanic.** The s.135 look-back rule unwinds earlier days where the same "relevant person" was entitled. A director who acquired the dwelling in 2023 and lets it commercially throughout 2023-2025, then allows his daughter to occupy briefly in early 2026, may lose earlier relief retrospectively if no qualifying-use day under s.133(1)(a) sits between the 2023-2025 occupation period and the 2026 daughter-occupation event.
4. **Explain the qualifying-use intervention.** A s.133(1)(a) day is one on which the dwelling is being exploited as a source of rents in the course of a qualifying property rental business. A short commercial arm's-length let (e.g. to a contractor unconnected with the owner) can sever look-forward / look-back chains if genuinely qualifying.
5. **Note the s.163 adjusted-chargeable-amount return obligation.** Where relief is lost mid-period, a s.163 ACA return is required — separate from the annual s.159 return. Failure to file the ACA return triggers Sch 55 items 11A / 11B as for the annual return.
6. **Cross-reference the CIHC qualifying-purpose carve-out (CTA 2010 s.18N(3))** — the connected-occupant test under s.18N(3) is a parallel-but-different test that also poisons company-level tax treatment. Sessions must not conflate s.136 (ATED) with s.18N(3) (CT), but should note both bite together for connected occupancy.
7. **Practical mitigation patterns.** Genuine arm's-length commercial-let interposition; documentation of non-occupation (director's family member visits but does not "occupy"); short-let through a regulated agent at market rent. These work only where the documentation is contemporaneous.
8. **Penalty consequences if clawback discovered late.** Clawback-triggered ACA return omitted → Sch 55 late-filing penalties + Sch 24 inaccuracy on the original-period return (where the s.135 look-back retrospectively unwinds the original relief). Cross-link to B8 + B9.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. **What triggers ATED relief clawback?** (FA 2013 s.135: non-qualifying occupation on any day. Even a single day with a non-qualifying individual permitted to occupy the dwelling triggers loss of relief on that day AND look-forward + look-back extension.)
2. **Who is a "non-qualifying individual" under s.136?** (Eight-category catalogue: (a) individual entitled to the interest (otherwise than as partnership member), and seven additional categories covering connected persons, partnership members, settlors, relatives, spouses of relatives, CIS participants, and connected-to-CIS-participant individuals. Stage 2 must verbatim-verify the full catalogue against legislation.gov.uk.)
3. **The look-forward rule.** What is the period of subsequent disqualification? (Per s.135: the chargeable period of the non-qualifying occupation PLUS the subsequent 3 chargeable periods. Subsequent days are not relievable unless a s.133(1)(a) qualifying-use day intervenes.)
4. **The look-back rule.** How far back can s.135 unwind prior relief? (No statutory time limit on look-back; the rule operates by "earlier day where a relevant person is entitled" — back to acquisition by the relevant person, unless a s.133(1)(a) qualifying-use day falls between that earlier day and the non-qualifying occupation day.)
5. **Who is the "relevant person" for look-back?** (Per s.135: (i) the person entitled on the non-qualifying occupation day, or (ii) any partnership member who exploited the interest in a property rental business.)
6. **What is a "qualifying-use day" under s.133(1)(a)?** (A day on which the dwelling is being exploited as a source of rents or other receipts in the course of a qualifying property rental business — i.e. run on a commercial basis and with a view to profit. The intervention day must itself be qualifying; a vacant period does NOT intervene.)
7. **The s.163 adjusted-chargeable-amount return obligation.** (Where relief gained or lost mid-period, an ACA return is required separately from the annual s.159 return. Sch 55 items 11A + 11B both apply for late filing.)
8. **The CIHC interaction at CTA 2010 s.18N(3).** (Connected occupant defeats the company's qualifying-purpose carve-out from CIHC treatment. CIHC = 25% main rate, no small-profits rate, regardless of profit level. The s.18N(3) test is parallel to but NOT identical to s.136. Sessions must cite both where connected occupancy occurs.)
9. **Common scenarios that trigger clawback.** (a) Director's family member uses the dwelling between tenants. (b) Settlor occupies a dwelling held by a corporate trustee. (c) Partner in a partnership-with-corporate-member uses the dwelling personally. (d) CIS participant occupies a unit in a fund-held dwelling. (e) Spouse of a relative of a connected person — multi-hop family caught by s.136 categories.
10. **Mitigation patterns that work.** (a) Genuine arm's-length commercial let to an unconnected tenant interposing a qualifying-use day (s.133(1)(a)). (b) Contemporaneous documentation showing non-occupation (visits without overnight stay, no key issue, no possession). (c) Charging market rent and physical separation evidence — but this only avoids the connected-occupant test if the occupant is NOT within the s.136 catalogue (see B12 for the market-rent angle when occupant IS within the catalogue).
11. **Penalty exposure from late-discovered clawback.** (Sch 55 + Sch 56 + Sch 24 in parallel: late ACA return generates Sch 55 escalator; under-declared chargeable amount via Sch 24 inaccuracy on the original return; offshore Category 2 / 3 uplift if overseas-corporate filer. Unprompted disclosure mitigation under Sch 24 paras 9-10 + Sch 55 paras 17-18 if disclosed before HMRC contact. Cross-link B8 + B9.)
12. **Pre-decision check for owners.** Before allowing any connected-person occupation, the owner / director / trustee should: (a) take advice on s.136 category coverage, (b) ensure a contemporaneous qualifying-use day (s.133(1)(a)) interposes, (c) prepare the s.163 ACA return for affected periods, (d) document the start + end of any non-qualifying occupation precisely.
