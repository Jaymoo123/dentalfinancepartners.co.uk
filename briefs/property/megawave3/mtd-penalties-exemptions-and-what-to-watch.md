# MTD Penalties, Exemptions, and What to Watch: A Practitioner-Grade Catalogue for UK Landlords (April 2026 Onwards)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — points-based late submission penalty regime (FA 2021 Sch 24):** Verified at write time 2026-05-27 against the enacted Schedule 24 of Finance Act 2021. The operative provisions:
  - **Paragraph 5(9)(b)** sets the **4-point penalty threshold** for quarterly returns (Column B group; MTD ITSA quarterly updates fall within this group).
  - **Paragraph 15(2)** awards a penalty point for each failure; when the person already has the maximum number of points, paragraph 15(4) triggers a fixed **£200 penalty** for the failure-at-threshold and for each subsequent failure while at threshold.
  - **Paragraph 8(1)** sets the points-reset mechanism: all accumulated points expire simultaneously when **both** conditions are met — (a) the person makes all required returns on time for the relevant period (paragraph 8(4)(b) specifies **12 months** for quarterly submissions), AND (b) the person has submitted all returns due in the **preceding 24 months**. The reset is a **dual-condition test**, not a single 12-month or 24-month rolling window. HMRC notifies on points-expiry.
- **Primary — late payment penalty regime under FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended by Spring Statement 2025 (§19.7 lock):** §19.7 Wave 3 lock 2026-05-22 with Correction logged 2026-05-22 (F-6 in prior wave architecture). Verbatim: *"The new rates will be 3% of the tax outstanding where tax is overdue by 15 days, plus 3% where tax is overdue by 30 days, plus 10% per annum where tax is overdue by 31 days or more."* — Spring Statement 2025 HTML document. This **15 / 30 / 31 day-trigger schedule at 3% / 3% / 10% percentages** applies to **MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026**. The legacy FA 2021 Sch 26 schedule of 31 / 46 / 91 day-triggers at 2% / 2% / 4% percentages continues to apply to **VAT and non-MTD income tax** (taxpayers below MTD threshold). Sessions writing on MTD ITSA penalty content MUST use 15 / 30 / 31 with 3% / 3% / 10%; the legacy schedule is a recurring §19.9 "Do not write" item.
- **Primary — comprehensive exemption catalogue under SI 2026/336 (Income Tax (Digital Obligations) Regulations 2026) — verified at write time 2026-05-27 against legislation.gov.uk:** SI 2026/336 came into force **1 April 2026**, replacing **SI 2021/1076 (Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021)** which was revoked on that date. F-3 logged on the §19 architecture migration. The exemption regime spans:
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 18** — exemption where an exclusion notice is or has been in place in relation to a relevant person on the relevant day. Verbatim partial: *"This regulation applies where an exclusion notice is or has been in place in relation to a relevant person on a day ('the relevant day')."* The digital obligations do not apply for that day or the tax year containing that day.
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 19** — exclusion notice mechanism (definition, application, "in place" timing — Stage 2 must fetch verbatim).
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 20** — meaning of "excluded". Verbatim: *"In regulation 19, a person is 'excluded' if the person is— (a) digitally excluded, or (b) unable to meet any condition relating to the verification of identity arising under regulation 46, by reason of any circumstance beyond the person's control."* "Digitally excluded" is defined at **TMA 1970 Sch A1 paragraph 14(2)**.
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 24** — three-tax-year income-exemption exit. Verbatim: *"This Chapter also applies to a person for a digital obligation tax year ('the tax year Y') after the tax year 2028-29 if— (a) a digital obligation applied to the person in relation to the tax year Y-3, the tax year Y-2 and the tax year Y-1, and (b) the amount of the person's qualifying income for each of those three tax years was not more than the qualifying amount for that tax year."* Cumulative three-year test — all three preceding years must satisfy the income threshold for the exemption to apply in year Y. Replaces SI 2021/1076 reg 22 cited at §19.5 lock.
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 25** — qualifying income definition (the gross-self-employment-turnover-plus-gross-property-rental-aggregated test per §19.2 Wave 3 lock).
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 27** — qualifying amount for a tax year (the £50k / £30k / £20k threshold figures per §19.1 Wave 3 lock).
- **Primary — excluded categories beyond the individual-exemption regime (§19.3 lock):** §19.3 Wave 3 lock 2026-05-22 captures the categorical exclusions that operate independently of the qualifying-income test:
  - **Limited companies** are outside MTD ITSA entirely (file annual CT600s under separate digital-record rules; MTD for CT is a future cycle with no confirmed date).
  - **General partnerships** are deferred (originally April 2027, now deferred to TBC). Treat as "MTD for partnerships expected in a later phase, no confirmed date as of May 2026". Partners with separate sole-trader / landlord income outside the partnership remain in MTD ITSA via that other income.
  - **LLPs** are partnerships for MTD purposes (deferred).
  - **Trustees** are outside MTD ITSA; trust property income reported via the SA900 trust return continues unchanged.
  - **Non-UK resident individuals** with UK property income are in scope where threshold met; the NRL scheme (§17.5) operates alongside MTD, not in place of it.
- **Primary — TMA 1970 Sch A1 framework + para 14(2) digital-exclusion definition:** Sch A1 (inserted by F(No.2)A 2017 Sch 14) establishes the "relevant persons" framework; paragraph 14(2) defines "digitally excluded" cross-referenced by SI 2026/336 reg 20. Sessions writing should NOT rely on TMA 1970 Sch A1 for the qualifying-income definition itself (that is at SI 2026/336 reg 25); Sch A1 sets the framework, the operative substantive rules sit in the new SI.
- **Supporting — appeals route under FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 22 (reasonable excuse) and TMA 1970:** a person is not liable to a points penalty where they have a reasonable excuse for the failure, the excuse persists, and the failure is remedied without unreasonable delay after the excuse ends. Stage 2 + RUN must verify para 22 verbatim at write time per §16.35; the reasonable-excuse threshold mirrors the established TMA 1970 s.118(2) line developed at FTT (the *Perrin* test) for the reasonable-objective-honest-taxpayer standard.
- **House position reference:** §19.7 (primary anchor — penalty regime, Wave 3 lock with Spring Statement 2025 correction 2026-05-22), §19.3 (excluded categories), §19.5 (exit / income-drop rule, cross-link to A7 deep-dive), §19.8 (abandoned £10k threshold — historical context), §19.9 (Do not write list — pin the canonical penalty figures), §19.10 (Wave 4 — ASA / agent transition discipline for the agent-change watch-item), §19.13 (Wave 4 — letting-agent net-of-fees trap for the threshold-test boundary watch-item). NEW HP LOCK NEEDED **only at the SI-citation-migration level** (F-3 logged); the substantive penalty + exemption mechanics are fully covered.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What are the late submission penalties under MTD ITSA — under FA 2021 Sch 24 the regime is points-based: 1 point per missed quarterly update; 4-point threshold for quarterly filers (paragraph 5(9)(b)); £200 fixed penalty at threshold and for each subsequent failure while at threshold (paragraph 15(4))?
2. How does the points-reset mechanism work — paragraph 8(1) dual-condition test: (a) all required returns made on time for the relevant period (12 months for quarterly filers per paragraph 8(4)(b)) AND (b) all returns due in the preceding 24 months submitted. Both conditions must be met; HMRC notifies on points-expiry.
3. What are the late payment penalties under MTD ITSA — under FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended by Spring Statement 2025: 3% of tax outstanding at day 15, additional 3% at day 30, 10% per annum from day 31 onwards. This is the doubled accelerated schedule applying to MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026; the legacy 31/46/91 day-2%/2%/4% schedule continues for VAT and non-MTD income tax.
4. What is the digital-exclusion exemption — SI 2026/336 reg 18 + reg 20 + TMA 1970 Sch A1 para 14(2): an exclusion notice can be issued where the person is (a) digitally excluded (age / disability / religious belief / remote-location / mental-incapacity grounds per the Sch A1 definition) OR (b) unable to meet identity-verification conditions under reg 46 by reason of circumstance beyond their control. Application is made to HMRC; notice is durable until withdrawn.
5. What is the income-exemption exit route — SI 2026/336 reg 24 verbatim: applies for tax year Y after 2028-29 where (a) a digital obligation applied for Y-3, Y-2, and Y-1, AND (b) qualifying income did not exceed the qualifying amount for each of those three tax years. Cumulative three-year test. Cross-link to A7 for the deep-dive on transition mechanics and notification to HMRC.
6. What categorical exclusions apply (§19.3) — limited companies (outside MTD ITSA entirely), general partnerships (deferred phase commencement, no confirmed date), LLPs (treated as partnerships), trustees (SA900 trust return continues), pension trustees (SIPP / SSAS-held property — §19.12 Wave 4 lock).
7. What about voluntary opt-in / opt-out — taxpayers below the qualifying-amount threshold can join MTD voluntarily from 6 April 2025 (pilot) or 6 April 2026 (general). Voluntary participants are bound by the quarterly cycle and penalties. They can also elect not to be exempt (where they meet an exemption condition) — verify exact reg number under SI 2026/336 (likely reg 23 successor; Stage 2 to verify).
8. What is the appeals route — under FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 22 (reasonable excuse) and the established *Perrin* line at FTT (reasonable-objective-honest-taxpayer standard): a penalty point or £200 penalty can be appealed where the failure had a reasonable excuse, the excuse persisted through the failure period, and the failure was remedied without unreasonable delay after the excuse ended. Special-circumstances reduction available under Sch 24 paragraph 17 where appropriate.
9. What operational scenarios should a landlord actively watch for — (a) **mid-year sale of last property**: final quarterly update covers partial quarter; EoPS + final declaration cover full year to cessation; cessation notification to HMRC (§19.15 Wave 4 lock); (b) **income drop year**: single year below threshold does NOT trigger exemption — needs three consecutive years per reg 24; in-scope status persists until cumulative three-year test met; (c) **spouse-asymmetric scope** (one spouse in MTD, one not): separate ASA authorisation, separate quarterly cycles, household bookkeeping must split per share (cross-link A19); (d) **agent transition mid-year**: ASA authorisations do NOT transfer; new agent must re-request authorisation; old agent's access revoked via gov.uk authorisation portal (§19.10 Wave 4 lock); (e) **letting-agent net-of-fees trap**: threshold test uses gross-rent-collected by agent, NOT net-paid-to-landlord (§19.13 Wave 4 lock).
10. What are the common drift / misconception items (§19.9 Do not write list) — "£10,000 is the MTD threshold" (abandoned 2022; §19.8); "Late payment penalties are 2%/2%/4%" (correct for non-MTD only; MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026 is 3%/3%/10% at 15/30/31 days); "MTD applies to LtdCos from April 2026" (false; LtdCos outside); "Joint owners test against the property's total gross" (false; each tests their share — cross-link A19); "Late submission produces an immediate £200 penalty" (false; points-based at 4-point threshold).
11. What about the new SI 2026/336 vs the old SI 2021/1076 — the operative MTD instrument from 1 April 2026 is **SI 2026/336**, which **revoked SI 2021/1076**; substantive mechanics carry over but regulation numbers have migrated (F-3 drift catch logged). Sessions writing should cite SI 2026/336 going forward; the prior SI is appropriate only in historical / migration-context discussion.

### Example 2 — Points-threshold worked example walked

Anonymised case: Alistair Brennan (anonymised — no real name), London-based landlord with £80,000 gross from a 4-property buy-to-let portfolio. In MTD ITSA scope from 6 April 2026 under the April 2026 mandate; £80,000 share-of-gross (sole-owner, no joint-owner overlay).

- **Quarter 1 (5 August 2026 deadline):** missed. **1 point. No £200 yet.**
- **Quarter 2 (5 November 2026 deadline):** missed. **2 points. No £200 yet.**
- **Quarter 3 (5 February 2027 deadline):** missed. **3 points. No £200 yet.**
- **Quarter 4 (5 May 2027 deadline):** missed. **4 points — AT THRESHOLD. No £200 yet on the threshold-trigger event** (per FA 2021 Sch 24 para 15(4), the £200 triggers for failure-at-threshold-and-subsequent).
- **2027/28 Q1 (5 August 2027 deadline):** missed. **£200 penalty triggered — first failure at threshold.**
- **2027/28 Q2 (5 November 2027 deadline):** missed. **Further £200 penalty.**
- **2027/28 Q3 (5 February 2028 deadline):** filed on time. **No further penalty. Points still at threshold.**
- **2027/28 Q4 + 2028/29 Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 + 2029/30 Q1, Q2 (8 consecutive on-time submissions):** continued compliance. **Points reset NOT yet triggered** — Sch 24 para 8(1) dual-condition test requires BOTH (a) 12-month period of full compliance (paragraph 8(4)(b) for quarterly filers) AND (b) all preceding-24-month submissions made. The 12-month compliance period runs from the first on-time submission after reaching threshold; the preceding-24-month submissions condition tests the broader historical record.
- **2029/30 Q3 (5 February 2030):** dual-condition test met (12 months full compliance from 2028/29 Q3 onward AND all preceding-24-month submissions made — the only missed submissions are in the 2026-2027 window which is now outside the rolling 24-month preceding period). **HMRC notifies points-expiry; all 4 points expire simultaneously.**
- **Total cost over the cycle:** 2 × £200 = £400 in fixed penalties + interest on any late-paid tax + the operational cost of HMRC enquiry correspondence + professional fees.
- **Operational lesson:** the points-based regime is FORGIVING on first missed quarter (1 point, no immediate £200) but COMPOUNDING when missed quarters accumulate. The dual-condition reset (12-month full compliance AND all-preceding-24-month-submissions) means the reset clock takes 24 months to fully clear; accountants should counsel clients on the cliff-edge nature of the 4-point threshold AND the slow reset cycle.

### Example 3 — Late-payment accelerated worked example

Anonymised case: Margaret Holloway (anonymised). MTD ITSA in-scope landlord; final declaration for 2025/26 submitted on time 31 January 2027; balancing payment £8,000 of income tax due same day; not paid by due date.

- **Day 0 (31 January 2027 due date):** balancing payment £8,000 unpaid.
- **Day 15 (15 February 2027):** £4,000 partial payment made. **First trigger fires per FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended (Spring Statement 2025).** 3% of the unpaid tax at day 15 = 3% × £8,000 = **£240 penalty** (the £4,000 payment on day 15 itself does not avoid the day-15 trigger which fires at the START of day 15).
- **Day 30 (2 March 2027):** balance £4,000 still unpaid. **Second trigger fires.** Additional 3% of the unpaid tax at day 30 = 3% × £4,000 = **£120 penalty**.
- **Day 31 onwards (3 March 2027):** balance £4,000 still unpaid. **Third trigger active.** 10% per annum on unpaid balance from day 31.
- **Day 45 (17 March 2027):** balance £4,000 paid in full. Day 31-45 = 15 days at 10% per annum on £4,000 = c. £16 (10% × £4,000 × 15/365). **Third-trigger accrual: c. £16.**
- **Total late-payment penalties:** £240 + £120 + £16 = **£376 plus FA 2009 ss.101-102 interest** (currently c. 7-8% annualised; verify gov.uk at write).
- **Cross-comparison with legacy non-MTD schedule** (continues to apply to non-MTD income tax + VAT): under the LEGACY 31/46/91 day-2%/2%/4% schedule, the same fact pattern would have triggered 2% × £8,000 = £160 at day 31 — but the legacy schedule does NOT apply to MTD ITSA. Sessions writing must use the 15/30/31 day-3%/3%/10% accelerated schedule for MTD ITSA content; the legacy schedule applies only to A13's general SA penalty regime.
- **Operational lesson:** the MTD-specific late-payment regime is MATERIALLY HARSHER than the legacy schedule. Day-15 trigger means a payment delay of just two weeks attracts a 3% penalty (vs the legacy 31-day window for the first 2% trigger). Landlords with cashflow gaps around the 31 January final-declaration deadline should arrange Time-to-Pay (TTP) arrangements PROACTIVELY rather than allow the day-15 trigger to fire.

### Example 4 — Digital-exclusion exemption applied

Anonymised case: Eleanor Whitfield (anonymised), age 82, recently widowed, single-property landlord with £55,000 share-of-gross (in scope April 2026 mandate). Terminal illness diagnosis; no broadband at her retirement-home address; mental capacity concerns following recent stroke.

### Example 5 — Income-exemption exit (three-year cumulative test)

Anonymised case: David Hartwell (anonymised), in MTD ITSA from April 2026 mandate (qualifying income £52,000 from 2024/25 SA return). Subsequent income falls:

- 2025/26: qualifying income £28,000 (qualifying amount that tax year £30,000 — BELOW threshold).
- 2026/27: qualifying income £26,000 (qualifying amount £20,000 — ABOVE threshold).
- 2027/28: qualifying income £22,000 (qualifying amount £20,000 — ABOVE threshold).
- 2028/29: qualifying income £18,000 (qualifying amount £20,000 — BELOW threshold).
- 2029/30: qualifying income £15,000 (qualifying amount £20,000 — BELOW threshold).
- 2030/31: qualifying income £14,000 (qualifying amount £20,000 — BELOW threshold).
- **Cumulative three-year test under SI 2026/336 reg 24:** the test for tax year Y requires that qualifying income did not exceed the qualifying amount for EACH of Y-3, Y-2, Y-1. For tax year 2031/32 (Y), Y-3 = 2028/29 (£18k vs £20k below ✓); Y-2 = 2029/30 (£15k vs £20k below ✓); Y-1 = 2030/31 (£14k vs £20k below ✓). **Cumulative test MET for 2031/32.** David exits MTD ITSA from 6 April 2031 onward; reverts to regular SA.
- **Why earlier years did NOT trigger exit:** for tax year 2029/30 (Y), Y-3 = 2026/27 (£26k vs £20k ABOVE threshold ✗). Single failed year breaks the cumulative three-year test. The earliest tax year where the three-consecutive-years-below test can be met is 2031/32 in this fact pattern.
- **Note the post-2028/29 timing constraint:** SI 2026/336 reg 24 verbatim text states the rule "also applies to a person for a digital obligation tax year ('the tax year Y') AFTER the tax year 2028-29" — exit can apply from 2029/30 onward (the first qualifying Y under reg 24), subject to the cumulative three-year test.
- **Operational mechanics on exit:** David notifies HMRC of the exit via the MTD ITSA portal; HMRC confirms exit; David ceases quarterly updates from the exit-effective tax year onward; resumes regular SA. Cross-link A7 (`heres-how-you-can-exit-mtd-if-your-income-falls`) for the deep-dive on transition mechanics, notification process, and re-entry triggers.
- **Operational lesson:** the income-exit route is SLOW. A single year above threshold resets the three-year cumulative test. Landlords whose income oscillates around the threshold may NEVER trigger exit; the rule favours sustained-decline scenarios over volatility.

### Example 7 — Reasonable-excuse appeal walked

Anonymised case: Michael Cartwright (anonymised). Single-property landlord in MTD ITSA scope from April 2026. Q3 (5 February 2027) quarterly update missed; HMRC issues penalty point. Michael's defence: serious illness (hospitalisation 1 January 2027 to 15 February 2027 with documented sepsis; remained convalescent through 28 February 2027).

- **Test (FA 2021 Sch 24 para 22 + Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 (TCC) four-stage framework):**
  1. **What facts of the case caused the failure?** Sepsis hospitalisation from 1 January 2027; quarterly update due 5 February 2027 fell during hospitalisation period.
  2. **Was there a reasonable excuse for the failure?** Yes — incapacitating illness preventing the taxpayer from filing falls squarely within the established reasonable-excuse line (Perrin para 71 + supporting FTT decisions).
  3. **Did the excuse persist for the entire period of the failure?** Yes — hospitalisation 1 January to 15 February (10 days post-deadline); convalescence to 28 February. The excuse persisted through to 28 February.
  4. **Was the failure remedied without unreasonable delay after the excuse ended?** Michael filed the Q3 quarterly update on 3 March 2027 — within 3 days of returning to functional capacity. Reasonable delay.
- **Outcome:** reasonable-excuse defence succeeds; point discharged; no £200 penalty triggered (point would only contribute to a future 4-point threshold; discharge resets that risk).
- **What if Michael had delayed filing until 30 April 2027?** The fourth Perrin limb (remedied without unreasonable delay) would have failed; HMRC would have argued the excuse ended on 28 February and 2 months delay is unreasonable. Reasonable-excuse defence likely fails on the remedy-delay limb.
- **What if Michael's illness was minor flu rather than hospitalisation?** The second Perrin limb (was there a reasonable excuse) becomes fact-sensitive. Brief illness without hospitalisation does not necessarily reach the reasonable-excuse threshold under the established line; HMRC typically requires either incapacitating severity OR substantive supporting evidence (e.g., GP letter) for shorter illness defences.
- **Operational lesson:** reasonable-excuse defences against MTD penalties are FACT-SENSITIVE. The Perrin four-stage framework requires (a) factual triggering event, (b) reasonable-excuse threshold, (c) excuse persistence through failure period, (d) remedy without unreasonable delay. Documentation discipline up front (hospital records, GP letters, employer absence records) is essential.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: What is the late submission penalty regime under MTD ITSA?**
   A: Points-based regime under FA 2021 Sch 24. Per paragraph 15(2), HMRC awards 1 point for each missed quarterly update (or other digital obligation submission). Per paragraph 5(9)(b), the threshold for Column B quarterly returns (which includes MTD ITSA quarterly updates) is 4 points. Per paragraph 15(4), once at threshold, a £200 fixed penalty triggers for the failure-at-threshold and for each subsequent failure while at threshold. Points-reset per paragraph 8(1) is a dual-condition test: 12-month compliance period (paragraph 8(4)(b) for quarterly filers) AND all preceding-24-month submissions made.

2. **Q: How does the points-reset mechanism actually work?**
   A: Per FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 8(1), all accumulated points expire simultaneously when both conditions are met: (a) the person makes all required returns on time for the relevant period (12 months for quarterly submissions per paragraph 8(4)(b)); AND (b) the person has submitted all returns due in the preceding 24 months. The dual-condition test means a missed submission in the past 24 months can delay points-reset even after 12 months of on-time future compliance. HMRC notifies the taxpayer on points-expiry. The reset operates point-cluster-wise: all current points expire together once the test is met.

3. **Q: What is the late payment penalty regime for MTD ITSA?**
   A: Accelerated regime under FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended by FA 2025 / Spring Statement 2025. Operative figures: 3% of unpaid tax at day 15; additional 3% at day 30; 10% per annum from day 31. The day-15 trigger is the materially accelerated point vs the legacy non-MTD schedule (31 days for first 2% trigger). FA 2009 ss.101-102 interest applies in parallel from the original due date. The legacy 31/46/91 day-2%/2%/4% schedule continues to apply to non-MTD income tax + VAT — it does NOT apply to MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026 onward.

4. **Q: What is the digital-exclusion exemption?**
   A: SI 2026/336 reg 18 + reg 19 + reg 20 establishes an exemption from MTD ITSA digital obligations where an exclusion notice is in place. Per reg 20, a person is "excluded" if (a) digitally excluded (TMA 1970 Sch A1 paragraph 14(2) definition — age, disability, religious belief, remote location, mental incapacity grounds) or (b) unable to meet identity-verification conditions under reg 46 by reason of circumstance beyond their control. Application made to HMRC; notice durable until circumstances change. Person continues regular SA reporting outside MTD ITSA while notice operates.

5. **Q: What is the income-exemption exit route?**
   A: SI 2026/336 reg 24. Applies for a digital obligation tax year Y AFTER the tax year 2028-29 where (a) a digital obligation applied for Y-3, Y-2, and Y-1; AND (b) qualifying income did not exceed the qualifying amount for each of those three tax years. Cumulative three-year test — single year above threshold breaks the test. Cross-link A7 (`heres-how-you-can-exit-mtd-if-your-income-falls`) for the deep-dive on transition mechanics and re-entry triggers.

6. **Q: What categorical exclusions apply (entities outside MTD ITSA entirely)?**
   A: Per §19.3 Wave 3 lock: (a) limited companies (CT600 regime continues; MTD for CT is a future cycle); (b) general partnerships (deferred to TBC; originally April 2027); (c) LLPs (treated as partnerships for MTD purposes); (d) trustees (SA900 trust return continues); (e) pension trustees for SIPP / SSAS-held property per §19.12 Wave 4 lock. Partners with separate sole-trader / landlord income outside the partnership remain in MTD ITSA via that other income. Non-UK resident individuals with UK property income are in scope where threshold met; the NRL scheme operates alongside MTD, not in place of it.

7. **Q: What is the reasonable-excuse defence for MTD penalties?**
   A: FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 22. A point or £200 penalty does not arise if the taxpayer satisfies HMRC (or on appeal the First-tier Tribunal) that there is a reasonable excuse for the failure. The Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 (TCC) four-stage framework applies: (1) what facts caused the failure; (2) was there a reasonable excuse; (3) did the excuse persist through the failure period; (4) was the failure remedied without unreasonable delay after the excuse ended. Statutory exclusions mirror the established line: insufficient funds is NOT reasonable excuse unless beyond control; reliance on another person is NOT reasonable excuse unless reasonable steps taken.

8. **Q: What is the special-circumstances reduction?**
   A: FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 17. HMRC may reduce a penalty under any paragraph of the Schedule because of special circumstances. Statutory exclusions: inability to pay; cross-taxpayer revenue balancing. FTT review is SUPERVISORY ONLY (Wednesbury-equivalent review of HMRC discretion) — FTT cannot substitute its own view on the merits, only review the decision-making process for irrationality / failure to consider relevant factors.

9. **Q: What is the 30-day appeal window?**
   A: TMA 1970 s.31A — 30 days from the date of the penalty notice. Within the window: (a) appeal directly to the First-tier Tribunal under s.31; (b) request HMRC statutory review under s.49 (stops the FTT clock; fresh 30-day window opens on review conclusion); (c) pay without prejudice and lodge a protective appeal. Late appeals require the Martland v HMRC [2018] UKUT 178 (TCC) three-stage framework FTT application — HMRC cannot administratively extend the statutory 30-day window.

10. **Q: I'm a landlord with a £45,000 gross portfolio. Am I in scope?**
    A: Depends on tax year. Per §19.1 Wave 3 lock + SI 2026/336 reg 27: 6 April 2026 mandate triggers at qualifying income above £50,000 (tested against 2024/25 SA return); 6 April 2027 at above £30,000 (tested against 2025/26); 6 April 2028 at above £20,000 (tested against 2026/27). At £45,000 you are out of scope April 2026; in scope April 2027 (above £30k threshold). For joint owners the share-of-gross rule applies per A19. For LtdCo holdings the categorical exclusion applies per §19.3.

11. **Q: What is the cumulative penalty exposure for a multi-quarter delinquent landlord?**
    A: Material. A 4-quarter delinquent landlord reaches 4 points but does NOT trigger the first £200 fixed penalty until the 5th missed submission (the failure-at-threshold + each subsequent). 8 consecutive missed quarters: 4 × £200 = £800 in fixed penalties (assuming 4 at threshold + 4 subsequent) + Sch 26 late-payment penalties on any unpaid balancing payments + interest under FA 2009 ss.101-102 + the operational cost of HMRC enquiry correspondence + professional fees. Compounding effect through Sch 26 accelerated triggers at day 15 / 30 / 31 makes the late-payment exposure dominate the late-submission exposure for taxpayers with large balancing payments.

12. **Q: I received an HMRC penalty notice — what should I do first?**
    A: Within the 30-day window (TMA 1970 s.31A): (a) consider reasonable-excuse defence under FA 2021 Sch 24 para 22 (use Perrin four-stage framework to assess viability); (b) consider statutory review request under TMA 1970 s.49 (cheaper-first-step than direct FTT appeal; pauses the FTT clock; fresh 30-day window on review conclusion); (c) consider special-circumstances reduction request under FA 2021 Sch 24 para 17 (HMRC discretion; supervisory FTT review only). Engage specialist tax representation early if the penalty exposure is material; document the facts contemporaneously; do NOT miss the 30-day window — late appeals require Martland framework which sets a high bar.

13. **Q: What is the difference between the MTD ITSA penalty regime and the general SA penalty regime?**
    A: Material differences. Late submission: general SA uses FA 2009 Sch 55 escalator (£100 immediate + £200 at 3 months + £300 at 6 months / 12 months + tax-geared paras 5-6 uplifts); MTD ITSA uses FA 2021 Sch 24 points-based regime (1 point per missed quarterly; £200 at 4-point threshold). Late payment: general SA uses FA 2009 Sch 56 (5%/5%/5% at 30 days / 5 months / 11 months) for non-MTD; MTD ITSA uses FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended by Spring Statement 2025 (3%/3%/10% at 15/30/31 days). Cross-link A13 (`late-filing-and-late-payment-penalties`) for the general SA regime; A20 (this page) for MTD-specific.

14. **Q: What is SI 2026/336 and why does it matter?**
    A: SI 2026/336 (Income Tax (Digital Obligations) Regulations 2026) is the operative MTD ITSA instrument from 1 April 2026. It REVOKED SI 2021/1076 (the prior Digital Requirements Regulations) on that date. Substantive mechanics carry over but regulation numbers migrated: qualifying income at reg 25 (was reg 20); qualifying-amount threshold at reg 27; exclusion-notice exemption at reg 18 + reg 20; three-year income-exit at reg 24 (was reg 22). F-3 drift catch logged 2026-05-27; sessions writing must cite SI 2026/336 going forward.
