# MTD Penalties, Exemptions, and What to Watch: A Practitioner-Grade Catalogue for UK Landlords (April 2026 Onwards)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — points-based late submission penalty regime (FA 2021 Sch 24):** Verified at write time 2026-05-27 against the enacted Schedule 24 of Finance Act 2021. The operative provisions:
  - **Paragraph 5(9)(b)** sets the **4-point penalty threshold** for quarterly returns (Column B group; MTD ITSA quarterly updates fall within this group).
  - **Paragraph 15(2)** awards a penalty point for each failure; when the person already has the maximum number of points, paragraph 15(4) triggers a fixed **£200 penalty** for the failure-at-threshold and for each subsequent failure while at threshold.
  - **Paragraph 8(1)** sets the points-reset mechanism: all accumulated points expire simultaneously when **both** conditions are met — (a) the person makes all required returns on time for the relevant period (paragraph 8(4)(b) specifies **12 months** for quarterly submissions), AND (b) the person has submitted all returns due in the **preceding 24 months**. The reset is a **dual-condition test**, not a single 12-month or 24-month rolling window. HMRC notifies on points-expiry.
- **Primary — late payment penalty regime under FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended by Spring Statement 2025 (§19.7 lock):** §19.7 Wave 3 lock 2026-05-22 with Correction logged 2026-05-22 (F-6 in prior wave architecture). Verbatim: *"The new rates will be 3% of the tax outstanding where tax is overdue by 15 days, plus 3% where tax is overdue by 30 days, plus 10% per annum where tax is overdue by 31 days or more."* — Spring Statement 2025 HTML document. This **15 / 30 / 31 day-trigger schedule at 3% / 3% / 10% percentages** applies to **MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026**. The legacy FA 2021 Sch 26 schedule of 31 / 46 / 91 day-triggers at 2% / 2% / 4% percentages continues to apply to **VAT and non-MTD income tax** (taxpayers below MTD threshold). Sessions writing on MTD ITSA penalty content MUST use 15 / 30 / 31 with 3% / 3% / 10%; the legacy schedule is a recurring §19.9 "Do not write" item.
- **Primary — comprehensive exemption catalogue under SI 2026/336 (Income Tax (Digital Obligations) Regulations 2026) — verified at write time 2026-05-27 against legislation.gov.uk:** SI 2026/336 came into force **1 April 2026**, replacing **SI 2021/1076 (Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021)** which was revoked on that date. F-3 logged on the §19 architecture migration. The exemption regime spans:
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 18** — exemption where an exclusion notice is or has been in place in relation to a relevant person on the relevant day. Verbatim partial: *"This regulation applies where an exclusion notice is or has been in place in relation to a relevant person on a day ('the relevant day')."* The digital obligations do not apply for that day or the tax year containing that day.
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 19** — exclusion notice mechanism (definition, application, "in place" timing — Stage 2 must fetch verbatim).
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 20** — meaning of "excluded". Verbatim: *"In regulation 19, a person is 'excluded' if the person is— (a) digitally excluded, or (b) unable to meet any condition relating to the verification of identity arising under regulation 46, by reason of any circumstance beyond the person's control."* "Digitally excluded" is defined at **TMA 1970 Sch A1 paragraph 14(2)**.
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 24** — three-tax-year income-exemption exit. Verbatim: *"This Chapter also applies to a person for a digital obligation tax year ('the tax year Y') after the tax year 2028-29 if— (a) a digital obligation applied to the person in relation to the tax year Y-3, the tax year Y-2 and the tax year Y-1, and (b) the amount of the person's qualifying income for each of those three tax years was not more than the qualifying amount for that tax year."* Cumulative three-year test — all three preceding years must satisfy the income threshold for the exemption to apply in year Y. Replaces SI 2021/1076 reg 22 cited at §19.5 lock.
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 25** — qualifying income definition (the gross-self-employment-turnover-plus-gross-property-rental-aggregated test per §19.2 Wave 3 lock).
  - **SI 2026/336 reg 27** — qualifying amount for a tax year (the £50k / £30k / £20k threshold figures per §19.1 Wave 3 lock).
- **Primary — excluded categories beyond the individual-exemption regime (§19.3 lock):** §19.3 Wave 3 lock 2026-05-22 captures the categorical exclusions that operate independently of the qualifying-income test:
  - **Limited companies** are outside MTD ITSA entirely (file annual CT600s under separate digital-record rules; MTD for CT is a future cycle with no confirmed date).
  - **General partnerships** are deferred (originally April 2027, now deferred to TBC). Treat as "MTD for partnerships expected in a later phase, no confirmed date as of May 2026". Partners with separate sole-trader / landlord income outside the partnership remain in MTD ITSA via that other income.
  - **LLPs** are partnerships for MTD purposes (deferred).
  - **Trustees** are outside MTD ITSA; trust property income reported via the SA900 trust return continues unchanged.
  - **Non-UK resident individuals** with UK property income are in scope where threshold met; the NRL scheme (§17.5) operates alongside MTD, not in place of it.
- **Primary — TMA 1970 Sch A1 framework + para 14(2) digital-exclusion definition:** Sch A1 (inserted by F(No.2)A 2017 Sch 14) establishes the "relevant persons" framework; paragraph 14(2) defines "digitally excluded" cross-referenced by SI 2026/336 reg 20. Sessions writing should NOT rely on TMA 1970 Sch A1 for the qualifying-income definition itself (that is at SI 2026/336 reg 25); Sch A1 sets the framework, the operative substantive rules sit in the new SI.
- **Supporting — appeals route under FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 22 (reasonable excuse) and TMA 1970:** a person is not liable to a points penalty where they have a reasonable excuse for the failure, the excuse persists, and the failure is remedied without unreasonable delay after the excuse ends. Stage 2 + RUN must verify para 22 verbatim at write time per §16.35; the reasonable-excuse threshold mirrors the established TMA 1970 s.118(2) line developed at FTT (the *Perrin* test) for the reasonable-objective-honest-taxpayer standard.
- **House position reference:** §19.7 (primary anchor — penalty regime, Wave 3 lock with Spring Statement 2025 correction 2026-05-22), §19.3 (excluded categories), §19.5 (exit / income-drop rule, cross-link to A7 deep-dive), §19.8 (abandoned £10k threshold — historical context), §19.9 (Do not write list — pin the canonical penalty figures), §19.10 (Wave 4 — ASA / agent transition discipline for the agent-change watch-item), §19.13 (Wave 4 — letting-agent net-of-fees trap for the threshold-test boundary watch-item). NEW HP LOCK NEEDED **only at the SI-citation-migration level** (F-3 logged); the substantive penalty + exemption mechanics are fully covered.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What are the late submission penalties under MTD ITSA — under FA 2021 Sch 24 the regime is points-based: 1 point per missed quarterly update; 4-point threshold for quarterly filers (paragraph 5(9)(b)); £200 fixed penalty at threshold and for each subsequent failure while at threshold (paragraph 15(4))?
2. How does the points-reset mechanism work — paragraph 8(1) dual-condition test: (a) all required returns made on time for the relevant period (12 months for quarterly filers per paragraph 8(4)(b)) AND (b) all returns due in the preceding 24 months submitted. Both conditions must be met; HMRC notifies on points-expiry.
3. What are the late payment penalties under MTD ITSA — under FA 2021 Sch 26 as amended by Spring Statement 2025: 3% of tax outstanding at day 15, additional 3% at day 30, 10% per annum from day 31 onwards. This is the doubled accelerated schedule applying to MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026; the legacy 31/46/91 day-2%/2%/4% schedule continues for VAT and non-MTD income tax.
4. What is the digital-exclusion exemption — SI 2026/336 reg 18 + reg 20 + TMA 1970 Sch A1 para 14(2): an exclusion notice can be issued where the person is (a) digitally excluded (age / disability / religious belief / remote-location / mental-incapacity grounds per the Sch A1 definition) OR (b) unable to meet identity-verification conditions under reg 46 by reason of circumstance beyond their control. Application is made to HMRC; notice is durable until withdrawn.
5. What is the income-exemption exit route — SI 2026/336 reg 24 verbatim: applies for tax year Y after 2028-29 where (a) a digital obligation applied for Y-3, Y-2, and Y-1, AND (b) qualifying income did not exceed the qualifying amount for each of those three tax years. Cumulative three-year test. Cross-link to A7 for the deep-dive on transition mechanics and notification to HMRC.
6. What categorical exclusions apply (§19.3) — limited companies (outside MTD ITSA entirely), general partnerships (deferred phase commencement, no confirmed date), LLPs (treated as partnerships), trustees (SA900 trust return continues), pension trustees (SIPP / SSAS-held property — §19.12 Wave 4 lock).
7. What about voluntary opt-in / opt-out — taxpayers below the qualifying-amount threshold can join MTD voluntarily from 6 April 2025 (pilot) or 6 April 2026 (general). Voluntary participants are bound by the quarterly cycle and penalties. They can also elect not to be exempt (where they meet an exemption condition) — verify exact reg number under SI 2026/336 (likely reg 23 successor; Stage 2 to verify).
8. What is the appeals route — under FA 2021 Sch 24 paragraph 22 (reasonable excuse) and the established *Perrin* line at FTT (reasonable-objective-honest-taxpayer standard): a penalty point or £200 penalty can be appealed where the failure had a reasonable excuse, the excuse persisted through the failure period, and the failure was remedied without unreasonable delay after the excuse ended. Special-circumstances reduction available under Sch 24 paragraph 17 where appropriate.
9. What operational scenarios should a landlord actively watch for — (a) **mid-year sale of last property**: final quarterly update covers partial quarter; EoPS + final declaration cover full year to cessation; cessation notification to HMRC (§19.15 Wave 4 lock); (b) **income drop year**: single year below threshold does NOT trigger exemption — needs three consecutive years per reg 24; in-scope status persists until cumulative three-year test met; (c) **spouse-asymmetric scope** (one spouse in MTD, one not): separate ASA authorisation, separate quarterly cycles, household bookkeeping must split per share (cross-link A19); (d) **agent transition mid-year**: ASA authorisations do NOT transfer; new agent must re-request authorisation; old agent's access revoked via gov.uk authorisation portal (§19.10 Wave 4 lock); (e) **letting-agent net-of-fees trap**: threshold test uses gross-rent-collected by agent, NOT net-paid-to-landlord (§19.13 Wave 4 lock).
10. What are the common drift / misconception items (§19.9 Do not write list) — "£10,000 is the MTD threshold" (abandoned 2022; §19.8); "Late payment penalties are 2%/2%/4%" (correct for non-MTD only; MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026 is 3%/3%/10% at 15/30/31 days); "MTD applies to LtdCos from April 2026" (false; LtdCos outside); "Joint owners test against the property's total gross" (false; each tests their share — cross-link A19); "Late submission produces an immediate £200 penalty" (false; points-based at 4-point threshold).
11. What about the new SI 2026/336 vs the old SI 2021/1076 — the operative MTD instrument from 1 April 2026 is **SI 2026/336**, which **revoked SI 2021/1076**; substantive mechanics carry over but regulation numbers have migrated (F-3 drift catch logged). Sessions writing should cite SI 2026/336 going forward; the prior SI is appropriate only in historical / migration-context discussion.
