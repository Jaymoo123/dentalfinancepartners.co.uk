# Plymouth vs Cardiff: The After-Tax BTL Yield Comparison Most "Hotspot" Pages Skip

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (England SDLT — Plymouth side): FA 2003 Pt 4 + Sch 4ZA (additional dwellings 5% surcharge) + Sch 9A (2% non-resident surcharge) — §1, §1.I, §1.O architecture pre-locked. Verbatim verification deferred to Stage 2 per §16.27 rate-by-reference.
- Primary (Welsh LTT — Cardiff side): Land Transaction Tax and Anti-avoidance of Devolved Taxes (Wales) Act 2017 (anaw 1) — main rates Schedule 1; higher residential rates (HRP, the Welsh ADS-equivalent) at Schedule 5. §23.1 + §23.2 + §23.3 architecture pre-locked. Welsh rates are devolved and **diverge** from English SDLT — no nil-rate band on HRP transactions; different thresholds; no first-time-buyers' relief equivalent (post-Welsh-Govt review). Verbatim re-verification at Stage 2 per §16.27 + §16.35.
- Primary (Northern Ireland comparison — not in this page but cross-reference per §23.7): SDLT applies (not devolved). Scotland is LBTT + ADS per §23.4 + §23.5; out-of-scope for this Plymouth/Cardiff page but mention briefly in a paragraph on "why the city you pick matters for tax".
- Primary (Section 24 — applies uniformly across England + Wales): ITTOIA 2005 s.272A. §4 lock.
- Primary (CGT residential bands — apply uniformly): TCGA 1992 + FA 2024 Part 1. §5 lock — 18%/24% from 30 October 2024.
- Primary (council-tax second-home premium — both Plymouth City Council and Cardiff Council have exercised LURA 2023 / equivalent Welsh powers): LGFA 1992 s.11B (England, as amended by Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023 Part 4) + Welsh equivalent (Local Government Finance Act 1992 as amended by Local Government Finance (Wales) Act 2024 — the Welsh second-home premium reform). §30 architecture pre-locked. **Critical:** Plymouth applies the England framework (LURA 2023 Part 4); Cardiff applies the Welsh framework (different statutory basis, different rate ceiling, different exemption set). Sessions verify current councils' published premium levels + exemptions for tax year at write per §16.27.
- Primary (Welsh licensing — Rent Smart Wales): Housing (Wales) Act 2014 Part 1 ss.1-7. **All Welsh landlords (including English landlords with a single Welsh property) must register the property + obtain a licence or use a Rent-Smart-Wales-licensed agent.** No equivalent mandatory all-landlords scheme in England — Plymouth operates selective licensing in specific wards under Housing Act 2004 Part 3.
- Primary (English selective licensing — Plymouth side): Housing Act 2004 Part 3 ss.79-100 + designation orders. Plymouth City Council has run selective licensing schemes in specified wards; sessions verify current designation order at Plymouth City Council at write.
- Primary (HMO licensing — applies in both): Housing Act 2004 Part 2 ss.55-78 (mandatory HMO licensing where 5+ unrelated occupants forming 2+ households + shared facilities). §26.9 pre-locked.
- Supporting (Renters' Rights Act 2025 — applies in England only): Section 21 abolition, periodic-tenancy default, PRS database, Landlord Redress Scheme. §20 architecture pre-locked. **Wales is on a separate regime**: Renting Homes (Wales) Act 2016, in force since 1 December 2022 (occupation contracts, standard / secure contracts, no-fault notice mechanics differ from the RRA 2025 England framework). Page must NOT conflate the two regimes.
- Supporting (Cardiff-specific) — Cardiff Council Article 4 directions affecting C4-to-C3 (HMO-to-dwelling-house) conversions in specific wards (Plasnewydd, Cathays, Roath area for student-let conversions). Article 4 removes permitted-development rights — a planning consequence relevant to BTL conversions in those wards. §32 pre-locked for planning-side architecture.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **The SDLT-vs-LTT purchase cost differential — worked example on a £200,000 second BTL** — England (Plymouth): SDLT additional-dwellings calculation under FA 2003 + Sch 4ZA. Wales (Cardiff): LTT Higher Residential Property rates calculation under LTTADA 2017 Sch 5. Stage 2 verifies current Welsh HRP rate stack against Welsh Revenue Authority + legislation.gov.uk per §16.27. **The Welsh HRP regime has NO nil-rate band** — every pound of consideration is charged from £1 upward; English SDLT additional-dwellings has the 5% surcharge ON TOP of the standard residential bands, so the bottom of the band-stack interacts with the Sch 4ZA surcharge differently. Net result: at lower price points, Welsh LTT HRP can be more expensive than English SDLT additional-dwellings in absolute terms despite the headline Welsh standard-rate bands being more taxpayer-friendly than English SDLT at the same price point. The page MUST run the actual numbers at a representative price-point (Stage 2 picks the price-point from current Plymouth / Cardiff median BTL price data and verifies with HMRC SDLT calculator + Welsh Revenue Authority LTT calculator at write).
2. **The Welsh higher-residential 4-year refund window vs English 3-year window** — England (FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 3 + para 8): 3-year replacement-of-main-residence window for the 5% surcharge refund. Wales (LTTADA 2017 Sch 5): 4-year window per Welsh-side reform. The longer Welsh window can be material for sequenced purchases. §1.I pre-locked for the English side; Welsh side requires Stage 2 verbatim verification of the current Welsh Government policy text (the 4-year-vs-3-year divergence has been a Welsh Government deliberate policy choice).
3. **Council-tax second-home premium — Plymouth-side LURA 2023 vs Cardiff-side Welsh framework** — Both councils have actively used the second-home premium powers. Plymouth applies the **LURA 2023 Part 4** framework (England — 100% premium ceiling, 12-month exemption-on-purchase, specific exemption list). Cardiff applies the **Local Government Finance (Wales) Act 2024** framework (Wales — up to **300% premium** ceiling per Welsh Government powers, with different exemption set). Stage 2 verifies Plymouth City Council's actual published premium rate for current tax year + Cardiff Council's actual published premium rate for current tax year per §16.27. The cash-on-hold overhead difference can be material for a landlord holding the property vacant between tenancies or for second-home use. **Critical:** the property must NOT be classified as a "second home" by the council if let to tenants — the premium hits empty + second-home properties, not let properties. The trap is short voids between tenancies + the relisting threshold for re-classification.
4. **Rent Smart Wales (Cardiff) vs Plymouth's selective licensing (specific wards only)** — Wales: Housing (Wales) Act 2014 Pt 1 — **all Welsh let properties** require landlord registration + licence (or use of a licensed agent). Single-property landlords of a single Welsh property MUST register. Annual cost: registration + licence. Plymouth: Housing Act 2004 Part 3 selective licensing — applies only in **designated wards** under designation orders. Many Plymouth wards are NOT covered. Cost difference: Welsh registration is uniform-mandatory; Plymouth selective licensing only triggers if property is in a designated ward. Stage 2 verifies current Plymouth designation orders + Rent Smart Wales registration fee at write.
5. **HMO licensing — uniform mandatory threshold but different overlays** — Housing Act 2004 Part 2 mandatory HMO licensing applies in both jurisdictions where 5+ unrelated occupants in 2+ households share basic amenities — the §26.9 floor. **Plymouth additional layer:** specific wards under Housing Act 2004 s.56 additional HMO licensing (smaller HMO threshold). **Cardiff additional layer:** Article 4 directions in Plasnewydd / Cathays / Roath restricting C4-to-C3 sui-generis conversions WITHOUT planning permission — a planning consequence on top of the licensing layer. The Cardiff student-let market is structurally affected by these Article 4 directions; landlords planning HMO conversions in those wards must factor in planning-permission cost + risk of refusal.
6. **Section 24 and CGT — uniform, no jurisdictional benefit** — ITTOIA 2005 s.272A finance cost restriction applies UK-wide. TCGA 1992 + §5 CGT residential bands (18%/24% from 30 October 2024) apply UK-wide. **No tax-planning lever exists** by choosing one city over the other on the income-tax side or capital-gains side. The cross-jurisdictional difference is concentrated at: SDLT/LTT (purchase), council tax premium (hold), licensing fee (hold). Sessions must NOT imply any income-tax or CGT differential between jurisdictions.
7. **Tenancy regime divergence — RRA 2025 (England, in force per §20.12 commencement timeline) vs RHWA 2016 (Wales, in force since 1 December 2022)** — England: Renters' Rights Act 2025 — Section 21 abolition; periodic-tenancy default; PRS database; Landlord Redress Scheme. Wales: Renting Homes (Wales) Act 2016 — occupation contracts (standard / secure / introductory); reduced no-fault-notice grounds; landlord registration via Rent Smart Wales. The two regimes are STRUCTURALLY DIFFERENT — sessions must NOT conflate them. A landlord crossing the border faces a different statutory tenancy regime, not just regulatory variation. §20 pre-locked for the RRA 2025 framework; sessions verify current RHWA 2016 commencement state at Welsh Government statutory anchor at write.
8. **The after-tax yield calculation — putting it all together** — Worked example: £200,000 BTL purchased, gross rental yield (Stage 2 sources from Rightmove + Zoopla city data at write), then deduct: SDLT or LTT on purchase (year 1 cash impact); annual licensing cost (Rent Smart Wales annual / Plymouth selective if applicable); annual council tax during voids (premium variation); Section 24-restricted finance costs at the 20% basic-rate credit; standard property-business deductible expenses; income tax at marginal rate. Two side-by-side P&L summaries — Plymouth vs Cardiff — over a 5-year hold. Sessions must label every figure as Stage 2 rate-by-reference per §16.27 and verify each at write.
9. **Plymouth-specific opportunity zones (regeneration corridor + student-let demand)** — Plymouth Council regeneration framework around the city centre / waterfront / Plymouth University catchment area drives student-let + young-professional demand. **Tax-relevant overlays:** capital allowances on plant & machinery within commercial-portion of mixed-use redevelopments (§25); SBA on qualifying non-residential structures (§25.4 pre-locked); Land Remediation Relief on previously-contaminated regeneration plots (§25.12 pre-locked). Page mentions these as forward-link opportunities rather than re-walking the statutory architecture.
10. **Cardiff-specific opportunity zones (Cardiff Bay + Plasnewydd / Cathays / Roath student catchment + Welsh Government investment incentives)** — Cardiff University catchment area in Plasnewydd / Cathays / Roath drives student-let demand — but offset by Article 4 restrictions on C4-to-C3 conversions and Rent Smart Wales mandatory licensing. **Welsh Government investment-incentive overlays:** Welsh Government grants for energy-efficiency retrofits (verify current scheme + eligibility at write); EPC C 2030 watchpoint — note that EPC C 2030 is NOT YET ENACTED per §26.3 do-not-write — sessions verify against Welsh Government regulatory state at write rather than asserting English EPC reform applies. The Cardiff regulatory layer is denser than Plymouth's; the offset is the Welsh Government's policy environment around BTL investment which sessions characterise factually at write.
