# How to Change Landlord Accountants — The Operational Walk-Through

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (record retention obligations — load-bearing for the switching process): TMA 1970 s.12B (individual self-assessment record retention — 5 years from 31 January following tax year for non-business; 5 years 10 months for business including landlord); CA 2006 s.388 (company accounting records — 6 years for private companies, 3 for unaudited small co — Stage 2 verifies); TMA 1970 s.12AA + Sch 18 (partnership SA800 + CT600 retention). §27.7 pre-locked.
- Primary (HMRC agent authorisation — 64-8 mechanics): SI 2003/2682 (PAYE Regulations) + HMRC Agent Authorisation procedures. The 64-8 form authorises the new agent + cancels the previous if so directed. Sessions verify current 64-8 process state at HMRC Agent Maintainer publications at write per §16.27 (the digital 64-8 / Online Agent Authorisation process has evolved; current state at write).
- Primary (Agent Services Account — ASA mechanics — load-bearing for MTD continuity): HMRC ASA framework (operational, not statutory) + SI 2021/1076 (MTD-ITSA regulations) — MTD for ITSA agent linkage is via ASA, distinct from legacy 64-8 paper authorisation. §19.10 pre-locked. **Critical:** an accountant change during a MTD-for-ITSA tax year requires affirmative ASA digital handover; failure of the digital handover during a quarterly window creates submission gaps the landlord (not the agent) is responsible for.
- Primary (Making Tax Digital quarterly continuity): §19 architecture pre-locked (mandate timeline; qualifying income; digital records; bridging software "digital link" rule per §19.14; mid-year cessation per §19.15; digital-records evidence discipline per §19.16). Sessions verify current MTD mandate state at write per §19.1.
- Primary (professional clearance protocol — ICAEW / ACCA / ATT / CIOT / CTA): the outgoing accountant releases clearance to the incoming accountant under professional-body standards. ICAEW Professional Standards Department + ACCA Rulebook + ATT / CIOT Professional Conduct in Relation to Taxation (PCRT) — operative guidance, not statutory. Sessions verify current ICAEW Code of Ethics / ACCA Rulebook chapter on agent change at write.
- Primary (GDPR data transfer obligations): UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018 — controller-to-controller transfer mechanics. Outgoing accountant must transfer the landlord's records securely; landlord (as data subject) has subject-access-request right to obtain own data. ICO guidance on agent change procedures applies.
- Primary (anti-money-laundering reverification on engagement): Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/692) — the new accountant must conduct fresh customer due diligence (CDD) at engagement; the outgoing accountant's CDD does not transfer. Sessions characterise this honestly — landlord faces fresh AML at every accountant change, not a one-off lifetime AML.
- Primary (ECCTA 2023 ACSP framework for designated members / officers of LLP / LtdCo landlords): ECCTA 2023 Part 1 (Companies House reforms) + ECCTA 2023 Part 3 (Authorised Corporate Service Provider framework). §11.A + §11.A.1 pre-locked. Sessions verify current ACSP rollout state at Companies House publications at write per §16.27. **Critical for landlords trading through LtdCo or LLP:** ID verification handover is a load-bearing step at agent change for the company-side compliance role.
- Primary (HMRC in-flight enquiry obligations — load-bearing pre-switch check): TMA 1970 s.9A (enquiry into self-assessment); s.28A (closure notice). Where an HMRC enquiry is open at the date of accountant change, the landlord MUST ensure continuity of representation; the outgoing accountant cannot abandon mid-enquiry, and the incoming accountant must affirmatively pick up the enquiry. Sessions surface this as a critical pre-switch check, not optional.
- Supporting (SDLT / CGT / IHT in-flight obligations): TCGA 1992 Sch 2 paras 6-12 (60-day in-year CGT reporting on residential disposals — §5 anchor); IHTA 1984 s.216 + s.226 (IHT account-and-payment obligations); FA 2003 Sch 10 (SDLT return obligations). Where a residential CGT disposal is mid-60-day-window, the landlord-side reporting obligation continues regardless of agent change.
- House position reference: §27.7 (record retention — TMA s.12B); §19 + §19.10 (MTD-for-ITSA + ASA mechanics); §11.A + §11.A.1 (ECCTA + ACSP framework); §5 (CGT residential + 60-day reporting); §27.1 (TMA s.9A enquiry framework); §27.4 (TMA s.28A closure-notice); §22 (IHT obligations). **No new HP lock needed** — page integrates across existing compliance infrastructure pre-locks.

New accountant must conduct fresh ID + address + source-of-funds (where relevant) + source-of-wealth (where relevant) CDD at engagement. Sessions characterise honestly — landlord cannot bypass this by saying "the previous firm did AML"; transfer of CDD is not permitted. The new firm's AML file is built fresh.
8. **The retention obligation — what records the landlord must keep regardless of accountant change** — TMA 1970 s.12B (5 years from 31 January following tax year for landlords + business records); CA 2006 s.388 (LtdCo accounting records — 6 years for private companies + Stage 2 verifies); HMRC partnership records retention; SDLT / CGT 60-day records. §27.7 pre-locked. **Critical:** the landlord's retention duty is INDEPENDENT of the accountant — the landlord must retain own records even where the accountant has retained working copies. Sessions surface this so the landlord cannot rely on the firm holding records during the transition.
9. **Cost / fee mechanics — engagement fee + handover fee + outstanding fees** — Sessions characterise the typical cost stack: new firm engagement fee (one-off + ongoing); outgoing firm may charge a handover / closure fee per their engagement letter; outstanding fees with the outgoing firm remain payable (professional clearance does not require them to be paid first, but contractual payment terms apply). ## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The pre-switch checklist applied to a landlord with multiple in-flight obligations

Mr Patel holds 4 BTL properties + 1 LtdCo holding a 5th property. He is considering switching from his current accountant (Firm A) to a specialist property accountant (Firm B). Pre-switch position as of October 2026:

- **MTD-for-ITSA quarterly cycle:** Mr Patel's combined property income is £58,000/year — above the £50,000 mandate threshold from April 2026. He is mid-Q3 2026/27 (October-December quarterly window).
- **Open HMRC enquiry:** Firm A is mid-defence of an HMRC enquiry into Mr Patel's 2024/25 SA return under TMA s.9A — opened June 2026, no closure notice issued yet.
- **In-flight 60-day CGT disposal:** Mr Patel completed a residential disposal on 25 September 2026 — 60-day reporting window closes 24 November 2026. Computation prepared by Firm A but not yet submitted.
- **LtdCo position:** Firm A acts as ACSP for the LtdCo per ECCTA 2023 Part 3; confirmation statement due 31 December 2026.

**Pre-switch checklist applied:**

- **MTD timing:** switch BETWEEN quarterly windows where possible. Current Q3 window closes 5 February 2027; switching after that window's quarterly submission is cleaner than mid-window. **Recommended:** delay switch to early February 2027 to clear Q3 submission with Firm A first.
- **Open enquiry continuity:** Firm A cannot abandon the enquiry mid-process; Firm B must affirmatively pick up the enquiry on engagement. Sessions emphasise both firms must formally agree the handover; HMRC's compliance officer should be notified of the agent change with continuity confirmed.
- **60-day CGT window:** the 24 November 2026 deadline falls during the potential switch window. **Recommended:** Firm A completes the 60-day submission before any agent-change action; Firm B picks up the year-end SA reconciliation post-switch.
- **LtdCo ACSP handover:** ECCTA Part 3 requires Firm B to register as ACSP for the LtdCo + complete ID verification on designated members. The corporate-filings agent change is DISTINCT from the personal-tax agent change — Mr Patel may delay the LtdCo handover until post-31-December-2026 confirmation statement to avoid mid-filing complication.

**Outcome:** Mr Patel agrees a switch sequence with both firms — Firm A completes Q3 submission + 60-day CGT submission + LtdCo confirmation statement before any 64-8/ASA cancellation; Firm B onboards with engagement letter + MLR 2017 reg 27 fresh CDD + 64-8/ASA application; the enquiry continuity is documented with HMRC; the switch effective date is mid-February 2027. No submission gaps; no penalty exposure.

### Example 2 — The professional clearance protocol in practice

Ms Singh has appointed Firm B as her new landlord accountant. Firm B initiates the professional clearance request to Firm A under ICAEW PCRT.

- **Step 1 — Firm B's clearance request to Firm A:** standard letter requesting (a) any professional reason against accepting the engagement; (b) outstanding client matters; (c) unpaid fees position; (d) any AML concerns; (e) any HMRC enquiry / dispute state.
- **Step 2 — Firm A's response:** Firm A confirms no professional reason against the engagement; lists outstanding matters (none — Ms Singh's affairs are up to date); notes unpaid fee of £450 from the latest invoice; confirms no AML concerns; confirms no open HMRC enquiry.
- **Step 3 — Unpaid fee position:** **Critical:** ICAEW PCRT distinguishes "reason to delay" (a real concern that the new engagement needs to consider) from "reason to refuse" (a professional bar to engaging). An unpaid fee alone is NOT a professional reason to refuse engagement; Firm A cannot withhold clearance solely on the basis of the unpaid fee. Firm A can (and should) raise the unpaid fee with Ms Singh for contractual settlement, but cannot block the engagement.
- **Step 4 — Records transfer:** Firm A transfers to Firm B (on Ms Singh's instruction): Ms Singh's tax returns + computations + HMRC correspondence + supporting schedules. Firm A retains: internal working notes; firm-side standing audit / engagement files; MLR 2017 CDD file (Firm A's own record + duty, does not transfer).
- **Step 5 — Ms Singh's GDPR fall-back:** if Firm A is uncooperative on records release, Ms Singh can submit a UK GDPR subject-access-request (free, within 1 month) for her own data direct from the firm. ICO guidance supports the SAR route.

Inline aside CTA: "If you are considering switching accountants and have any in-flight obligations (open enquiry, 60-day CGT window, pending submissions, MTD-for-ITSA quarterly cycle), the switching sequence matters as much as the firm choice. Talk to us about a clean transition."

### Example 3 — The MTD-for-ITSA quarterly handover sequence

Mr Patel's combined property income is £62,000/year — above the £50,000 mandate threshold from April 2026. He is currently mid-Q2 2026/27 (July-September quarterly window) and switching from Firm A to Firm B.

- **Pre-switch (Firm A):** Q1 submission (April-June) completed and submitted 5 August 2026. Q2 currently in progress; digital records being maintained by Firm A's MTD-compliant software.
- **Switch window:** Mr Patel notifies Firm A end-August 2026; engagement letter signed with Firm B 1 September 2026.
- **Operational handover sequence:**
  - Firm B submits 64-8 / digital agent authorisation to HMRC 2 September 2026.
  - Firm B requests Agent Services Account linkage for Mr Patel's MTD-for-ITSA registration 2 September 2026.
  - HMRC confirms agent linkage within ~3-5 working days; Mr Patel's HMRC Online Services shows Firm B as agent from ~7 September 2026.
  - Firm A's authorisation removed by HMRC automatically OR on Firm B's affirmative request (current behaviour evolving — Stage 2 verifies at write per §16.27).
  - Firm B reconciles inherited digital records against Mr Patel's source documents.
  - Q2 submission (due 5 November 2026) made by Firm B as agent of record.
- **Risk if handover bungled:** if HMRC's system gap leaves both firms as agents during the Q2 window, both firms may attempt to submit; HMRC may reject both; the landlord (not the firm) is responsible for the Q2 quarterly submission penalty under FA 2009 Sch 55 framework. Sessions emphasise: Mr Patel retains 64-8 / ASA confirmation copies + date-stamped acknowledgements; if uncertainty arises, contact HMRC directly to confirm agent of record before submission.

### Example 4 — The LtdCo + LLP ECCTA cascade at agent change

Patel Holdings Ltd holds 3 BTL properties; designated members are Mr Patel + Mrs Patel. Firm A is the ACSP for the LtdCo per ECCTA 2023 Part 3.

- **Pre-switch ACSP position:** Firm A has completed ID verification on Mr Patel and Mrs Patel under ECCTA's company-side framework; verification reference is held by Firm A and reported to Companies House.
- **At switch:** Firm B must register as ACSP for Patel Holdings Ltd; Firm B's ID verification on Mr Patel and Mrs Patel is REQUIRED FRESH — the ACSP verification does NOT transfer between firms. Firm A's ACSP role is cancelled.
- **Timing:** Firm B's ACSP registration + fresh ID verification can take 2-4 weeks depending on Companies House queue. **Critical:** any Companies House filing during the gap (confirmation statement, accounts, PSC update) must be made through the ACSP of record. If Firm A is being cancelled but Firm B is not yet onboarded as ACSP, the filing window may be missed.
- **Operative sequence:** verify the next Companies House filing deadline; ensure either (a) Firm A completes the filing before cancellation, OR (b) Firm B is registered as ACSP before the filing deadline.
- **The corporate-filings agent change is DISTINCT from the personal-tax agent change.** A landlord may change personal-tax accountant without changing corporate-filings ACSP, or vice versa. The two layers operate independently and require separate handover protocols.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target)

1. **Q: Can my outgoing accountant refuse to release my records over unpaid fees?**
   A: Generally no. ICAEW / ACCA / ATT / CIOT professional standards distinguish "reason to delay" (a real concern that the new engagement needs to consider) from "reason to refuse" (a professional bar to engaging). An unpaid fee alone is NOT a professional reason to refuse engagement; the outgoing accountant cannot withhold professional clearance solely on the basis of unpaid fees. The outgoing firm can (and should) raise the unpaid fee with you for contractual settlement, but cannot block the new engagement or block release of YOUR own records. UK GDPR subject-access-request is the fall-back: you can request your own data direct from the firm under DPA 2018 + UK GDPR (free, within 1 month).

2. **Q: Do I need a new AML check at the new accountant?**
   A: Yes. MLR 2017 SI 2017/692 reg 27 requires fresh customer due diligence (CDD) at engagement. The outgoing accountant's CDD file is the outgoing firm's own record + duty and does NOT transfer to the new firm. The new firm must conduct fresh ID + address + source-of-funds (where relevant) + source-of-wealth (where relevant) CDD. This applies at every agent change; you cannot bypass by saying "the previous firm did AML". Expect the new firm to request photographic ID + proof of address + portfolio overview at engagement.

3. **Q: What happens to my open HMRC enquiry if I switch accountants?**
   A: The HMRC enquiry continues; only the agent of record changes. Critical: the outgoing accountant CANNOT abandon mid-enquiry, and the incoming accountant MUST affirmatively pick up the enquiry. Both firms should formally agree the handover; HMRC's compliance officer should be notified of the agent change with continuity confirmed. The enquiry deadlines, correspondence, and substantive position do not reset — the new accountant inherits the state of play. Switch timing recommendation: NOT mid-enquiry unless necessary; if necessary, ensure both firms agree the handover formally.

4. **Q: Can I switch mid-MTD-for-ITSA quarter?**
   A: Yes, but switch BETWEEN quarterly windows where possible. Mid-quarter switching creates operational handover risk: the digital records continuity per §19.14 + §19.16 (bridging software / digital-link discipline) must be preserved; the ASA agent-linkage handover must complete before the next submission deadline; the new accountant must reconcile inherited submissions before filing. If a Q2 submission falls during the switch window, the landlord (not the firm) is ultimately responsible for the submission penalty if both firms' attempts fail or miss the deadline. Recommendation: complete the current quarter's submission with the outgoing firm; switch in the days following submission acknowledgement.

5. **Q: Do I need to update Companies House if I change accountants for my LtdCo?**
   A: Yes, if your accountant acts as ACSP for the LtdCo under ECCTA 2023 Part 3. ACSPs have specific ID verification obligations for company-side filings + designated-member / officer roles. At agent change, the new ACSP must register + conduct fresh ID verification on designated members; the outgoing ACSP's role is cancelled. The personal-tax agent change is DISTINCT from the ACSP change — you may change one without the other. Timing recommendation: ensure either the outgoing ACSP completes the next Companies House filing before cancellation OR the new ACSP is registered before the filing deadline.

6. **Q: How long does the 64-8 / digital agent authorisation handover take?**
   A: Typically 3-5 working days from submission of the 64-8 / digital agent authorisation by the new accountant to HMRC's confirmation of agent linkage. ASA (Agent Services Account) linkage for MTD-for-ITSA clients is separate and may take 5-10 working days. The outgoing accountant's authorisation is removed by HMRC automatically OR on affirmative request — current behaviour at agent change is evolving (Stage 2 verifies at write per §16.27); retain copies of the 64-8 / ASA confirmation + date-stamped acknowledgement.

7. **Q: What records am I entitled to receive from my outgoing accountant?**
   A: You are entitled to your own documents (originals or copies); tax returns + computations prepared on your behalf; HMRC correspondence; workings + supporting schedules where they are part of YOUR record. The outgoing accountant may RETAIN (and is not obliged to release): internal working notes; firm-side standing audit / engagement files; opinions provided to other clients; the MLR 2017 CDD file (the firm's own duty record). If the outgoing accountant is uncooperative on release of YOUR records, UK GDPR subject-access-request is the fall-back.

8. **Q: Are there records I must keep myself regardless of which accountant has the copies?**
   A: Yes. Your record-retention duty under TMA 1970 s.12B is INDEPENDENT of your accountant. Individual + landlord business records must be kept for 5 years from 31 January following the tax year (5 years 10 months for business records); LtdCo accounting records must be kept for 6 years (CA 2006 s.388, 3 for unaudited small co — verify post-uplift); partnership records per TMA s.12AA. SDLT / CGT 60-day records also subject to retention. Your accountant holds copies; you hold the originals (or at minimum, certified copies). §27.7 lock for record retention discipline.

9. **Q: Can I switch accountants if I have an in-flight 60-day CGT residential disposal?**
   A: Yes, but timing matters. The 60-day window from completion (TCGA 1992 Sch 2 paras 6-12) does not stop because you change accountants. If the disposal is mid-window, ensure either (a) the outgoing accountant completes the 60-day submission before any agent-change action OR (b) the new accountant's onboarding is fast enough to handle the submission before the deadline (typically not possible if onboarding is mid-process). The 60-day reporting + payment continues to be the landlord's obligation; agent change does not extend the window.

10. **Q: Will my new accountant inherit my MTD digital records?**
    A: Yes, on inheritance + reconciliation. The new accountant must verify the quarterly submission history they are inheriting (download from HMRC Online Services or MTD-compliant software) and reconcile against your records. Digital-records continuity per §19.14 + §19.16 requires the bridging software / digital-link discipline to remain unbroken. If the outgoing accountant used different MTD-compliant software, migration of records to the new accountant's software may require a one-off data transfer (typically achievable; some software bundles include migration tools). The reconciliation is the new firm's onboarding work.

11. **Q: Are there any reasons I should NOT switch accountants right now?**
    A: Yes — several timing-dependent reasons: (a) mid-enquiry under TMA s.9A (continuity risk); (b) mid-60-day CGT residential disposal window (deadline risk); (c) mid-quarterly MTD submission window (handover risk); (d) days before a SA or CT filing deadline (handover risk); (e) mid-ECCTA ACSP cascade for LtdCo (corporate-filings handover risk); (f) any time the switch would create a substantive submission gap. Recommended switch windows: immediately after a tax-year-end submission (cleanest break); BEFORE the new tax year's quarterly cycle starts; for LtdCo, around the accounting reference period end where possible.

12. **Q: How much should I budget for the transition?**
    A: Variable — depends on firm + portfolio size + complexity. Typical components: new firm engagement fee (one-off + ongoing); outgoing firm may charge a handover / closure fee per their engagement letter; outstanding fees with the outgoing firm remain payable (professional clearance does not require them to be paid first, but contractual payment terms apply). For a typical 4-5 property BTL portfolio, expect a one-off transition cost of £500-£1,500 + ongoing fee differentials. For LtdCo + LLP structures with ACSP cascade, add £200-£500 for the ECCTA ID verification step. Talk to us about a quote specific to your portfolio.
