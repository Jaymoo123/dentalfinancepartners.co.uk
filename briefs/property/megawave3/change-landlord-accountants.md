# How to Change Landlord Accountants — The Operational Walk-Through

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (record retention obligations — load-bearing for the switching process): TMA 1970 s.12B (individual self-assessment record retention — 5 years from 31 January following tax year for non-business; 5 years 10 months for business including landlord); CA 2006 s.388 (company accounting records — 6 years for private companies, 3 for unaudited small co — Stage 2 verifies); TMA 1970 s.12AA + Sch 18 (partnership SA800 + CT600 retention). §27.7 pre-locked.
- Primary (HMRC agent authorisation — 64-8 mechanics): SI 2003/2682 (PAYE Regulations) + HMRC Agent Authorisation procedures. The 64-8 form authorises the new agent + cancels the previous if so directed. Sessions verify current 64-8 process state at HMRC Agent Maintainer publications at write per §16.27 (the digital 64-8 / Online Agent Authorisation process has evolved; current state at write).
- Primary (Agent Services Account — ASA mechanics — load-bearing for MTD continuity): HMRC ASA framework (operational, not statutory) + SI 2021/1076 (MTD-ITSA regulations) — MTD for ITSA agent linkage is via ASA, distinct from legacy 64-8 paper authorisation. §19.10 pre-locked. **Critical:** an accountant change during a MTD-for-ITSA tax year requires affirmative ASA digital handover; failure of the digital handover during a quarterly window creates submission gaps the landlord (not the agent) is responsible for.
- Primary (Making Tax Digital quarterly continuity): §19 architecture pre-locked (mandate timeline; qualifying income; digital records; bridging software "digital link" rule per §19.14; mid-year cessation per §19.15; digital-records evidence discipline per §19.16). Sessions verify current MTD mandate state at write per §19.1.
- Primary (professional clearance protocol — ICAEW / ACCA / ATT / CIOT / CTA): the outgoing accountant releases clearance to the incoming accountant under professional-body standards. ICAEW Professional Standards Department + ACCA Rulebook + ATT / CIOT Professional Conduct in Relation to Taxation (PCRT) — operative guidance, not statutory. Sessions verify current ICAEW Code of Ethics / ACCA Rulebook chapter on agent change at write.
- Primary (GDPR data transfer obligations): UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018 — controller-to-controller transfer mechanics. Outgoing accountant must transfer the landlord's records securely; landlord (as data subject) has subject-access-request right to obtain own data. ICO guidance on agent change procedures applies.
- Primary (anti-money-laundering reverification on engagement): Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/692) — the new accountant must conduct fresh customer due diligence (CDD) at engagement; the outgoing accountant's CDD does not transfer. Sessions characterise this honestly — landlord faces fresh AML at every accountant change, not a one-off lifetime AML.
- Primary (ECCTA 2023 ACSP framework for designated members / officers of LLP / LtdCo landlords): ECCTA 2023 Part 1 (Companies House reforms) + ECCTA 2023 Part 3 (Authorised Corporate Service Provider framework). §11.A + §11.A.1 pre-locked. Sessions verify current ACSP rollout state at Companies House publications at write per §16.27. **Critical for landlords trading through LtdCo or LLP:** ID verification handover is a load-bearing step at agent change for the company-side compliance role.
- Primary (HMRC in-flight enquiry obligations — load-bearing pre-switch check): TMA 1970 s.9A (enquiry into self-assessment); s.28A (closure notice). Where an HMRC enquiry is open at the date of accountant change, the landlord MUST ensure continuity of representation; the outgoing accountant cannot abandon mid-enquiry, and the incoming accountant must affirmatively pick up the enquiry. Sessions surface this as a critical pre-switch check, not optional.
- Supporting (SDLT / CGT / IHT in-flight obligations): TCGA 1992 Sch 2 paras 6-12 (60-day in-year CGT reporting on residential disposals — §5 anchor); IHTA 1984 s.216 + s.226 (IHT account-and-payment obligations); FA 2003 Sch 10 (SDLT return obligations). Where a residential CGT disposal is mid-60-day-window, the landlord-side reporting obligation continues regardless of agent change.
- House position reference: §27.7 (record retention — TMA s.12B); §19 + §19.10 (MTD-for-ITSA + ASA mechanics); §11.A + §11.A.1 (ECCTA + ACSP framework); §5 (CGT residential + 60-day reporting); §27.1 (TMA s.9A enquiry framework); §27.4 (TMA s.28A closure-notice); §22 (IHT obligations). **No new HP lock needed** — page integrates across existing compliance infrastructure pre-locks.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **The pre-switch tax-side checks the landlord must run BEFORE notifying the outgoing accountant** — (a) MTD-for-ITSA quarterly cycle state — am I in-quarter / between quarters? (the digital ASA handover sequence differs); (b) any open HMRC enquiry under TMA s.9A — § cannot switch agents mid-enquiry without affirmative continuity; (c) any in-flight 60-day CGT residential disposal — §5 + TCGA 1992 Sch 2 paras 6-12; (d) any pending SDLT obligation — FA 2003 Sch 10; (e) any IHT account-and-payment obligation — IHTA 1984 s.216 + s.226; (f) for LtdCo / LLP landlords, any in-flight ECCTA-side ID verification cascading through. Sessions emphasise these are PRE-SWITCH checks, not post-switch surprises.
2. **The professional clearance protocol — what the new accountant must request from the outgoing accountant** — Standard ICAEW / ACCA / ATT / CIOT professional clearance request: outgoing firm confirms (a) any professional reason against accepting the engagement; (b) outstanding client matters; (c) unpaid fees position; (d) any AML concerns; (e) any HMRC enquiry / dispute state. **Critical:** outgoing accountant CANNOT withhold clearance based on unpaid fees alone — professional standards distinguish "reason to delay" from "reason to refuse"; sessions characterise this without legal-advice framing. Landlord can request own copy of clearance correspondence.
3. **The 64-8 + ASA + digital-handles sequence** — Sessions walk: (a) sign the new accountant's engagement letter; (b) new accountant submits 64-8 / digital agent authorisation to HMRC (the modern process is largely digital via HMRC Online Services); (c) new accountant requests Agent Services Account linkage for MTD-for-ITSA clients (separate from 64-8); (d) HMRC confirms agent linkage (typically within days); (e) the outgoing accountant's authorisation is removed by HMRC automatically OR on affirmative request — sessions verify current HMRC handover behaviour at write per §16.27 (the digital authorisation process behaviour at agent-change has evolved). **Critical:** the landlord should retain copies of the 64-8 / ASA confirmation + the date-stamped acknowledgement; HMRC system gaps have caused submission-routing issues in the past.
4. **Record transfer — what the landlord is entitled to receive and what the outgoing accountant is entitled to retain** — Outgoing accountant must transfer to the landlord (or to the new accountant on the landlord's instruction): (a) the landlord's own documents (originals or copies); (b) tax returns + computations prepared on the landlord's behalf; (c) HMRC correspondence; (d) workings + supporting schedules where they are part of the landlord's record. **Outgoing accountant may retain (and is not obliged to release):** internal working notes; firm-side standing audit / engagement files; opinions provided to other clients. The MLR 2017 CDD file is the outgoing accountant's own record + duty — sessions clarify it does NOT transfer. **Critical:** GDPR subject-access-request is the landlord's fall-back if the outgoing accountant is uncooperative — the landlord can request own data direct from the firm under UK GDPR.
5. **The MTD-for-ITSA quarterly handover — the operational sequence for landlords in-scope** — §19 pre-locked. For an in-scope landlord (qualifying income above mandate threshold per §19.1; current thresholds verified at write), the quarterly cycle must continue uninterrupted across an accountant change: (a) the digital-records continuity per §19.14 + §19.16 (bridging software / digital-link discipline); (b) ASA agent-linkage handover; (c) the new accountant must verify the quarterly submission history they are inheriting and reconcile against the landlord's records; (d) any unfiled quarterly submission becomes the landlord's responsibility immediately on change (not the outgoing accountant's). Sessions surface a specific timing recommendation: switch BETWEEN quarterly windows where possible, not mid-window.
6. **The LtdCo / LLP-specific cascades — ECCTA ID verification + designated-member / officer continuity** — §11.A + §11.A.1 pre-locked. ECCTA 2023 Part 3 ACSP framework — agents acting as Authorised Corporate Service Providers have specific ID verification obligations for company-side filings + designated-member / officer roles in LLP. Sessions verify current ACSP rollout state at Companies House publications at write per §16.27. **Critical for landlords with LtdCo / LLP structures:** the agent change at the corporate filings layer is distinct from the agent change at the personal-tax layer. Sessions surface both layers; landlord may be changing one without the other.
7. **The AML reverification at the new firm — fresh CDD on engagement** — MLR 2017 SI 2017/692 reg 27 (CDD requirements). New accountant must conduct fresh ID + address + source-of-funds (where relevant) + source-of-wealth (where relevant) CDD at engagement. Sessions characterise honestly — landlord cannot bypass this by saying "the previous firm did AML"; transfer of CDD is not permitted. The new firm's AML file is built fresh.
8. **The retention obligation — what records the landlord must keep regardless of accountant change** — TMA 1970 s.12B (5 years from 31 January following tax year for landlords + business records); CA 2006 s.388 (LtdCo accounting records — 6 years for private companies + Stage 2 verifies); HMRC partnership records retention; SDLT / CGT 60-day records. §27.7 pre-locked. **Critical:** the landlord's retention duty is INDEPENDENT of the accountant — the landlord must retain own records even where the accountant has retained working copies. Sessions surface this so the landlord cannot rely on the firm holding records during the transition.
9. **Cost / fee mechanics — engagement fee + handover fee + outstanding fees** — Sessions characterise the typical cost stack: new firm engagement fee (one-off + ongoing); outgoing firm may charge a handover / closure fee per their engagement letter; outstanding fees with the outgoing firm remain payable (professional clearance does not require them to be paid first, but contractual payment terms apply). Sessions DO NOT quote specific fee figures (varies enormously by firm + portfolio size + complexity); the discipline is "expect a transition cost; budget for it".
10. **The right time to switch — timing recommendations** — Sessions recommend: (a) immediately after a tax-year-end submission completion (cleanest break — new accountant inherits a closed year); (b) BEFORE the new tax year's quarterly cycle starts (avoids mid-cycle handover); (c) NOT mid-enquiry, NOT mid-60-day-CGT-window, NOT in the run-up to a submission deadline; (d) for LtdCo landlords, around the accounting reference period end where possible (clean financial-year break). Sessions emphasise timing is a load-bearing variable — the same switch executed at a bad time creates operational chaos a well-timed switch avoids.
