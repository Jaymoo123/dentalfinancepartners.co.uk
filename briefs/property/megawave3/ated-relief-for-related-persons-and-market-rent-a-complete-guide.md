# ATED relief for related persons and market rent: when does paying market rent unlock the rental relief and when does s.136 block it regardless

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (the relief whose conditions related-persons + market-rent must satisfy):**
  - **FA 2013 s.133** — "Property rental businesses" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). A day qualifies for relief if the single-dwelling interest is "being exploited as a source of rents or other receipts (other than excluded rents) in the course of a qualifying property rental business." A "qualifying property rental business" is one "run on a commercial basis and with a view to profit." Relief is **DENIED** if "a non-qualifying individual is permitted to occupy the dwelling" on that day.
  - The s.133 architecture has **two independent conditions** that BOTH must be satisfied for relief on a given day:
    1. The "commercial basis and with a view to profit" test — this is the market-rent gateway.
    2. The "no non-qualifying individual permitted to occupy" test — this is the s.136 catalogue gateway.
- **Primary (definition of non-qualifying individual — the operative catalogue):**
  - **FA 2013 s.136** — "Meaning of 'non-qualifying individual'" (verified at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Eight-category catalogue covering: (a) individuals entitled to the interest (otherwise than as partnership member); connected persons; partnership members; settlors of trusts holding the interest; relatives of connected persons / settlors; spouses / civil partners of relatives; participants in collective investment schemes; persons connected to those participants. The catalogue extends to **multiple-hop family relationships** — spouse of a relative of a connected person is caught.
- **Primary (clawback if s.136 occupation supervenes):**
  - **FA 2013 s.135** — "Non-qualifying occupation: look-forward and look-back" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Look-forward up to 3 subsequent chargeable periods; look-back to earlier days held by the same "relevant person" unless a s.133(1)(a) qualifying-use day intervenes. Cross-reference B11 for the clawback mechanic deep-dive.
- **Primary (the genuine market-rent + related-person reliefs):**
  - **FA 2013 s.145** — "Provision of dwelling to certain employees etc." (employee accommodation relief). Qualifying employee must be provided the dwelling "for performance of duties" + tight definition of qualifying employee (s.146 + s.147 controlling-interest carve-outs).
  - **FA 2013 s.138** — Property developer trade relief (dwelling held as trading stock by a property developer for at least 2 years).
  - **FA 2013 s.139** — Property-developer exchange-of-dwellings variant (sub-section (2): same non-qualifying-individual exclusion as s.133).
  - **FA 2013 s.141** — Property trader relief (held as trading stock by a property trader).
  - **FA 2013 s.137** — Dwellings open to the public (commercially run + open to public for at least 28 days per year).
  - **FA 2013 s.149** — Social housing relief (registered provider).
  - **FA 2013 s.150** — Charitable use relief.
  - **FA 2013 ss.134-135 (subset)** — demolition / conversion intention.
- **Primary (the CIHC parallel test at company level):**
  - **CTA 2010 s.18N(3)** — close investment-holding company test. The CIHC qualifying-purpose carve-out fails for connected occupancy at the company-tax level (separate from but overlapping with ATED s.136). HP §21.5 carries the full CIHC connected-occupant catalogue: connected person + spouse + relative + relative's spouse. Sessions writing related-persons pages must address both the ATED s.136 effect AND the CT s.18N(3) effect — they are independent and both bite.
- **Primary (market rent + connected party — wider tax architecture):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 / CTA 2009 Part 4** — UK property income charge regardless of connected-party status; income tax / corporation tax applies on the rental income at the prevailing rate (with §24 finance-cost restriction for individuals, full CT rate for companies).
  - **TCGA 1992 s.18** — connected-party CGT mark-to-market on disposal (relevant for eventual de-enveloping or sale to family).
  - **CTA 2010 s.444** — connected-party transactions for CT.
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 — verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full)**, §18.3 (relief catalogue — full table of s.133 / s.137 / s.138 / s.141 / s.144 / s.145 / s.149 / s.150 / ss.134-135), §18.4 (return mechanics + relief clawback architectural note), §18.8 (do-not-write list including "ATED reliefs are automatic where the dwelling is let" anti-pattern), §21.A (CT three-figure framework — CIHC), §21.5 (CIHC depth — qualifying-purpose carve-out + connected-occupant catalogue).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)
This page is the **related-persons-and-market-rent decision page** — the page that answers the question "I want to let our company-owned £1.5m flat to my daughter; if I charge market rent, can I claim the s.133 relief?" The popular answer (yes-with-documentation) is **wrong**. The correct answer is **almost always no** because the s.136 catalogue blocks regardless of rent level.

The framing differentiator is the **structural-correctness refusal of the market-rent myth**:

1. **Market rent is necessary but NOT sufficient.** s.133 has two independent conditions: (a) commercial basis + view to profit (market-rent gateway), AND (b) no non-qualifying individual occupation (s.136 catalogue gateway). Both must hold. A market-rent let to a director's daughter satisfies (a) but fails (b) — relief denied; the £4,600-£303,450 ATED charge applies in full; s.135 look-forward + look-back bites surrounding periods.
2. **The s.136 catalogue is wider than owners assume.** Eight categories including multi-hop family (spouse of a relative of a connected person), partnership members, settlors, CIS participants. Sessions writing related-persons copy must walk the catalogue explicitly — the popular "relative" mental shorthand misses the half of the catalogue (settlor / partnership / CIS).
3. **The genuine market-rent + related-person reliefs are narrow.** s.145 employee accommodation (with the controlling-interest carve-outs at s.146 + s.147 that exclude many family-employee patterns). s.138 / s.139 property-developer trade (transient, narrow). The relief catalogue does NOT contain a "let to family at market rent" door.
4. **The CIHC parallel hit at company level.** Even if (per impossibilam) the ATED relief survived, the company-level CT consequence kicks in via s.18N(3): connected occupancy defeats the qualifying-purpose carve-out, the company becomes a CIHC, and the 25% CT main rate applies regardless of profit level. Sessions must walk both effects.
5. **The honest decision-page conclusion.** For a company / partnership / CIS owning a £500k+ residential dwelling and wishing to provide it to family, the answer is usually: (a) de-envelope to personal name, OR (b) accept the full ATED charge and CIHC-rate CT, with both quantified. The "let at market rent and claim the relief" path almost never works for connected occupancy.
6. **Where market rent DOES matter.** For arm's-length lets to **unconnected** tenants who happen to be friends or distant acquaintances — sessions must address the "where does the s.136 catalogue stop" question. Acquaintances who are NOT connected persons / relatives / settlors / partnership members / CIS participants ARE outside the catalogue; market-rent commercial-terms lets to them DO qualify for s.133 relief.
7. **The s.135 clawback overlay.** Even one day of non-qualifying occupation triggers look-forward up to 3 chargeable periods + look-back unwinding earlier periods. Sessions writing related-persons copy must cross-link to B11 for the clawback mechanic.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. **Does paying market rent to a related person unlock the s.133 rental relief?** (Almost never. s.133 has two independent conditions: market-rent (commercial basis + view to profit) AND no non-qualifying-individual occupation per s.136. Market rent satisfies the first; the s.136 catalogue blocks the second for connected occupancy regardless of rent level.)
2. **Who is a "non-qualifying individual" under s.136?** (Eight-category catalogue per HP §18.3 + the s.136 verbatim text: (a) individuals entitled to the interest, connected persons, partnership members, settlors, relatives of connected persons / settlors, spouses of relatives, CIS participants, persons connected to CIS participants. Stage 2 verbatim-verifies each category against legislation.gov.uk.)
3. **What does "connected person" mean operationally for ATED?** (CTA 2010 s.1122 + s.1123 connection rules — the standard cross-Act connection catalogue. Director + their spouse + their relatives + the spouses of those relatives + companies controlled by them + trusts they settled. Cross-applied through s.136 to ATED.)
4. **Are there ANY market-rent + related-person ATED reliefs?** (Narrow: s.145 employee accommodation (with s.146 + s.147 controlling-interest carve-outs that exclude controlling shareholders, their spouses, and connected persons — so a typical family-employee pattern fails). s.138 / s.139 property-developer trade (dwelling held as trading stock; transient). s.137 dwellings open to the public (commercial entertainment use, not residential family). No relief covers a vanilla "let at market rent to family member" structure.)
5. **What about the s.145 employee accommodation door — does it work for a family employee?** (Tight definition: qualifying employee must be provided the dwelling "for performance of duties"; s.146 + s.147 controlling-interest carve-outs exclude controlling shareholders + their spouses + their connected persons + employees with material interest in the employer. A director's spouse / child employed as company secretary typically does NOT qualify under s.145 because of the controlling-interest carve-out.)
6. **The CIHC parallel hit at company level (CTA 2010 s.18N(3)).** (Connected occupancy defeats the qualifying-purpose carve-out from CIHC treatment. CIHC = 25% main rate, no small-profits relief regardless of profit level. The s.18N(3) catalogue is parallel to but NOT identical to s.136; sessions must address both. HP §21.5 carries the s.18N(3) connected-occupant detail.)
7. **The s.135 clawback overlay.** (Cross-link to B11. A single day of s.136 occupation triggers look-forward up to 3 chargeable periods + look-back. Owners considering brief family visits must understand the period-extension architecture.)
8. **What documentation is required to support a genuine arm's-length commercial let to an UNCONNECTED person?** (Tenancy agreement at market rent; AST (or RRA equivalent post-commencement); contemporaneous market evidence for rent (comparable lettings); tenant outside the s.136 catalogue — no connected-person status, no settlor / partnership / CIS link. The let must run on a commercial basis with a view to profit — short voids do not invalidate.)
9. **The de-envelope alternative for family-use intentions.** (Where the owner's underlying intention is family occupation, the structurally honest answer is usually de-envelope: distribute the dwelling in specie or sell to the family member at market value. SDLT-on-distribution + CGT-on-deemed-disposal mechanics apply; the personal-name post-de-envelope tax landscape includes §24 finance-cost restriction. Stage 2 cross-links to existing incorporation + de-envelope pillars.)
10. **The accept-the-charge alternative.** (Where keeping the dwelling enveloped is strategically right (succession, anonymity, multi-property structure), the owner accepts the full ATED charge + CIHC-rate CT for periods of connected occupancy. £1.5m dwelling at 2026/27 £9,450 ATED + 25% CT on rental income.)
11. **Penalty exposure if connected occupancy is unreported.** (Sch 24 inaccuracy on the original-period return for unreported non-qualifying occupation; Sch 55 escalator on omitted s.163 ACA returns; offshore Category 2 / 3 uplift for overseas-corporate filers. Cross-link B8 + B9.)
12. **Pre-decision discipline for owners.** Before allowing any connected-person occupancy: (a) walk the s.136 catalogue with the prospective occupant; (b) walk the s.18N(3) parallel catalogue at company-tax level; (c) take advice on the s.135 look-forward / look-back exposure; (d) decide between de-envelope, accept-the-charge, or rebuild around s.145 employee structure with controlling-interest analysis.
