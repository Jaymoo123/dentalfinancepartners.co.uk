# UK-Isle of Man Double Taxation Agreement: what Manx-resident landlords of UK property actually face

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (treaty):** UK-Isle of Man Double Taxation Agreement signed 2 July 2018, in force 19 December 2018, effective for UK income tax from 6 April 2019 and UK corporation tax from 1 April 2019. Published at gov.uk "Isle of Man: tax treaties". Sits in OECD Model 2017 form. Replaced the 1955 limited arrangement.
- **Primary (UK domestic — overrides treaty for UK situs property):**
  - **ITTOIA 2005 Part 3** (UK property income, individuals).
  - **CTA 2009 Part 4** (UK property income, companies).
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** (NRCGT — Finance Act 2019 rewrite; older ss.14B-14H structure repealed).
  - **FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902** (Non-Resident Landlord scheme — statutory withholding).
  - **TIOPA 2010 s.18 + s.130** (foreign tax credit relief framework).
- **Supporting:** 2024 UK-Isle of Man Memorandum of Understanding (7 February 2024); 2021 exchange of letters on assistance in collection of taxes (effective 1 January 2022).
- **House position reference:** §10 (DTAs general), §16 (DTAs Wave 2 extension — Art 4 / 6 / 13 anchors), §16.5 (Crown Dependencies: modern 2018+ treaties in OECD form, Art 13(4) indirect-disposal applies), §16.6 (NRL is statutory, not treaty), §17.4 (NRCGT regime), §17.5 (NRL operational mechanics).

## Key questions this page must answer
1. Is a Manx-resident landlord exempt from UK tax on UK rental income under the 2018 UK-IoM DTA? (No — Article 6 allocates source-state taxing rights to the UK; UK domestic ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 / CTA 2009 Part 4 charges apply.)
2. Does the treaty displace the Non-Resident Landlord scheme withholding? (No — NRL is statutory under FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902, not treaty-based. NRL1 application still required for gross rent.)
3. How does NRCGT apply on disposal of UK property by a Manx-resident? (Full NRCGT regime under TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A applies; 60-day return required regardless of whether tax is due.)
4. Where does Article 4 tie-breaker apply? (Where the individual is dual-resident under UK SRT and Manx domestic law — cascade: permanent home, centre of vital interests, habitual abode, nationality, mutual agreement.)
5. Does Article 13(4) indirect-disposal apply under the UK-IoM treaty? (Yes — 2018 treaty is modern OECD form; indirect disposals of property-rich entity shares fall to the source state.)
6. How does the foreign tax credit work in practice for Manx-resident landlords claiming UK tax paid? (Claimed via the Manx personal / corporate income tax return; UK has no symmetric FTC because UK is the source state with primary taxing rights.)
7. What does the 2024 Memorandum of Understanding add operationally? (Stage 2 verifies scope — typically clarifies competent-authority procedure and exchange-of-information arrangements; not a substantive treaty rewrite.)
8. Does the 2021 exchange of letters on collection assistance affect UK enforcement against Manx landlords? (Stage 2 verifies — broadly enables HMRC to seek IoM authority assistance recovering UK tax debts from Manx residents.)
9. Are Manx companies inside or outside ATED? (ATED applies to non-natural persons including non-UK companies — a Manx company holding a UK dwelling worth more than £500,000 is in scope. Cross-reference §18 + Bucket B ATED picks.)
10. How does the FIG regime (ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J, inserted by FA 2025 s.37) apply to a Manx-resident who returns to live in the UK after 10+ tax years non-UK-resident? (Standard FIG eligibility test — Manx residence counts as non-UK residence; 10-of-prior-10-years gateway applies.)

### Example 2 — Mr Quayle, Manx-resident individual landlord (the most common scenario)

Mr Quayle (Douglas, Isle of Man resident throughout the 2026/27 tax year; UK-non-resident under SRT; no permanent home in UK) owns a Manchester city-centre flat let to a tenant. Annual gross rent £18,000; agent's fees £1,800; mortgage interest £4,200; service charge + repairs £2,100. Position:
- **Treaty:** UK has primary taxing rights under Art 6. The treaty does NOT exempt.
- **NRL:** The letting agent must withhold 20% of rent paid to Mr Quayle unless he holds NRL1 approval. He applies for NRL1 on the day he first instructs the agent; HMRC typically approves within 6 weeks. Until approval, the agent withholds £3,600 per year (20% of £18,000) and accounts quarterly via NRL2.
- **UK Self-Assessment:** Mr Quayle must register for SA + file an SA100 + SA105 (UK property pages) each year. Rental income £18,000 minus deductible expenses (excluding mortgage interest, which is restricted to a 20% basic-rate tax credit under ITA 2007 s.274A per §4) = taxable property profit £14,100; mortgage-interest tax credit £840 (20% of £4,200) reduces UK income tax. At basic-rate (20%) UK tax on £14,100 = £2,820; minus £840 credit = £1,980 UK income tax due.
- **NRL withholding offset:** The £3,600 already withheld by the agent appears on his NRL6 + is credited against UK self-assessment liability. He is therefore due a UK refund of £1,620.
- **Manx side:** Mr Quayle reports the UK rental income on his Manx personal income tax return. Manx FTC under the 2018 treaty + Manx domestic law gives credit for £1,980 UK income tax paid against any Manx tax on the same income. The Manx tax rate framework (currently 10% / 20% with personal allowance — RUN session verifies at write) determines whether there is residual Manx tax to pay.
- **Common misframing:** "I'm a Manx resident, the DTA means I don't pay UK tax." FALSE — UK has primary taxing rights under Art 6; the treaty only prevents double taxation by giving Manx side the FTC obligation, not by exempting UK source income.

### Example 3 — Manx-incorporated property company holding UK BTL (the corporate-holding scenario)

Crosby Properties Limited (Isle of Man incorporated; resident in IoM for Manx tax purposes; UK-non-resident company; sole shareholder a Manx-resident individual) acquires a Liverpool BTL flat in 2026 for £620,000 (above the £500,000 ATED threshold). Annual rental £24,000. Position:
- **NRL:** Letting agent withholds 20% unless Crosby holds NRL2 approval; Crosby applies on incorporation as the UK property holder.
- **UK CT on rental:** Crosby Properties is within the charge to UK corporation tax on its UK property income under CTA 2009 Part 4 — non-UK companies with UK property income have been within UK CT (rather than non-resident income tax) since 6 April 2020. Rental profit (after deductible expenses) taxed at UK CT rates per §21.A — 19% small profits rate if augmented profits ≤£50,000 (likely here); 25% main rate above £250,000; 26.5% effective marginal between. If Crosby is the only company in the group, no associated-companies dilution. **CIHC trap (§21.A + §21.5):** Crosby's business is investment in land let commercially to an unconnected tenant — qualifies for the s.18N(2)(b) "let commercially" carve-out from CIHC — small profits rate available. Letting to a connected person (e.g. the shareholder's family member) would defeat the carve-out and push Crosby to the 25% main rate.
- **ATED:** UK dwelling worth >£500,000 held by a non-natural person → ATED in scope under FA 2013 Part 3 per §18. At £620,000 valuation, the property sits in the £500,001-£1m band: 2026/27 charge £4,600 per §18.1 (verify at write). Crosby files ATED return by 30 April 2026 + pays £4,600. Property-rental-business relief under s.133 FA 2013 is available (let commercially to unconnected tenant) BUT must be claimed on the return — failing to file even with relief available triggers Sch 55 FA 2009 penalty points (£100 immediate; £200 / £300 / £300 escalation per §27.2).
- **NRCGT on disposal:** When Crosby sells the flat in (say) 2032 for £780,000 (gain £160,000 before allowances), the 60-day NRCGT return is required regardless of whether tax is due. UK CT on the chargeable gain at the prevailing CT rate applies; ATED-CGT was abolished from 6 April 2019 per §18.5.
- **Manx-side CT:** Manx 0% corporate income tax rate typically applies to Manx companies (verify at write — Manx CT framework distinguishes by activity type; standard 0% / 10% / 20% structure). The UK CT paid is NOT generally creditable against Manx 0% — there is nothing to credit against. The Manx-incorporation route therefore typically yields no Manx-side tax saving on UK-source rental + gain; the structural reasons for Manx incorporation are usually IHT / succession / anonymity / fiduciary-management driven, not UK-tax driven.

### Example 4 — Art 4 tie-breaker for a dual-resident individual

Ms Kissack (UK-born; works partly in London partly in Douglas; spent 95 UK days in 2026/27; has a flat in Douglas + a flat-share in Battersea; works overseas in Douglas for a Manx employer 35 hours/week most weeks but has UK clients she sees in person ~30 days/year). SRT analysis: no automatic overseas test met (>16 UK days; not <46 days; UK workdays >30); not 183-day automatic UK resident; sufficient-ties test applies — 4 ties present (accommodation, work, 90-day, country) + 95 UK days → UK resident under SRT. Manx domestic law also makes her Manx-resident (home + economic centre in Douglas). She is dual-resident. Art 4 tie-breaker cascade:
- **(a) Permanent home:** Available in both UK (Battersea flat-share) and IoM (Douglas flat) → does not resolve.
- **(b) Centre of vital interests:** Family + economic + personal closer to Douglas (Manx employer; partner in Douglas; main bank accounts; social ties in IoM) → resolves to IoM.
- → For Art 4 treaty purposes, Ms Kissack is treated as Manx-resident; UK-resident status for SRT does not displace this. She is taxable in UK on UK source under Art 6 + Art 13; Manx side has primary taxing rights on her worldwide income.
- **Important:** Art 4 tie-breaker resolution affects treaty allocation only. It does NOT change her domestic-law UK residence (she remains UK-resident under SRT — relevant for any non-treaty UK matter, e.g. registration obligations). For NRL purposes she is treated as resident in IoM (treaty-resident in IoM = "non-UK resident landlord" for NRL practical purposes) — verify at write per §16.6 framing.

### Example 5 — Art 13(4) indirect disposal of UK property-rich entity shares

The 2018 UK-IoM treaty is modern OECD form and DOES contain an Art 13(4) indirect-disposal extension. Worked example: Mr Cregeen (Manx-resident; UK-non-resident under SRT) owns 30% of the shares in a Jersey-incorporated holding company ("UK Property HoldCo Ltd") whose sole asset is a London commercial property worth £14m. In 2026 he sells his 30% stake to an unrelated buyer for £4.2m (base cost £2m; gain £2.2m). Position:
- **Property-rich entity test:** UK Property HoldCo's value >75% derived from UK land → property-rich under Sch 1A para 6.
- **Threshold-holder test:** Mr Cregeen held ≥25% of the entity at any time in the 2 years before disposal (he held 30% throughout) → qualifies as a substantial holder under Sch 1A para 8.
- **NRCGT applies** under TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A Part 4 (indirect disposals). 60-day return required.
- **Treaty position:** Art 13(4) of the 2018 UK-IoM treaty allocates taxing rights on alienation of shares in property-rich entities to the source state (UK). UK domestic NRCGT and the treaty allocation are aligned — no treaty / statute conflict.
- **Compare UK-India (B3):** The 1993 UK-India treaty does NOT contain Art 13(4); were Mr Cregeen Indian-resident, the treaty would not allocate taxing rights to UK on this indirect disposal — but UK NRCGT under Sch 1A would still apply, and HMRC's position is that exercising NRCGT in the absence of treaty allocation is consistent with the treaty (treaty silence is not exemption). For 2018 UK-IoM there is no such conflict — Art 13(4) and Sch 1A align.

### Example 6 — Returning Manx-resident accessing the FIG regime (the reverse-direction worked example)

Mr Faragher (born and raised in IoM; Manx-resident throughout the 10 tax years 2016/17 to 2025/26; UK-non-resident under SRT throughout that period; never previously UK-resident; never previously claimed FIG / remittance basis) moves to London in May 2026 to take up a UK employment. He becomes UK-resident under SRT from the 2026/27 tax year. Position under FIG (per §17.8):
- **Qualifying new resident test (ITTOIA 2005 s.845B(1)):** (a) UK-resident 2026/27 → yes; (b) NOT UK-resident in any of the 10 preceding tax years (2016/17 to 2025/26) → yes; (c) not previously disqualified → yes; (d) over age 10 at start of 2026/27 → yes. **He qualifies for the FIG regime.**
- **Duration:** Up to 4 tax years: 2026/27 + 2027/28 + 2028/29 + 2029/30. Per-year claim.
- **Coverage:** Foreign income within ITTOIA 2005 s.845H categories (foreign property income, foreign interest, foreign dividends, etc.) — but Mr Faragher's UK employment income from his new London role is UK source and NOT covered by FIG (UK-source income is excluded per s.845I disqualified income).
- **Manx-side residual income:** Suppose he retains a Manx-source consulting revenue stream of £45,000 in 2026/27. Under FIG (if he claims for 2026/27), this Manx-source foreign income is UK-tax-exempt for the year. But claiming FIG costs him his personal allowance + dividend allowance + CGT AEA for 2026/27 — same architecture as historic remittance basis per §17.8. He must do the arithmetic: if Manx-source foreign income is small, the lost personal allowance / AEA outweighs the FIG benefit; if Manx-source foreign income is large, FIG saves him meaningful tax.
- **Claim deadline:** 31 January 2028 for the 2026/27 claim (12 months after 31 January 2027 self-assessment deadline) per s.845A + §17.8 lock. Late claims forfeit.
- **Common misframing:** "FIG is automatic for the first 4 years if you qualify." FALSE — per-year claim required; failure to claim forfeits the year per §17.8.

### Example 7 — Misframings the page must correct (verbatim do-not-write list)

- **Misframe 1:** "The 2018 UK-IoM treaty exempts Manx-residents from UK tax on UK rental income." FALSE — Art 6 allocates source-state taxing rights to UK; UK domestic charge under ITTOIA 2005 / CTA 2009 applies; treaty provides FTC route on the Manx side, not UK exemption.
- **Misframe 2:** "Manx-resident landlords don't need to operate NRL withholding because the treaty disapplies it." - **Misframe 4:** "The 1955 UK-IoM arrangement is the operative treaty." FALSE — replaced by the 2018 DTA in force 19 December 2018; competitor pages still citing the 1955 arrangement are stale.
- **Misframe 5:** "The 2018 treaty does not cover indirect disposals." FALSE — 2018 treaty is modern OECD form; Art 13(4) extends to property-rich entity shares (the IoM treaty aligns with NRCGT here).
- **Misframe 6:** "Manx incorporation saves UK tax on UK rental." FALSE / OVER-SIMPLE — Manx companies are within UK CT under CTA 2009 Part 4 on UK property income; the structural reasons for Manx incorporation are typically IHT / succession / anonymity / fiduciary-management, not UK-tax saving on rental + gain.
- **Misframe 7:** "Manx companies are outside ATED." FALSE — ATED applies to non-natural persons including non-UK companies per §18.2; a Manx company holding a UK dwelling worth >£500,000 is in scope.

- **Misframe 10:** "Manx-resident with UK rental income has no UK Self-Assessment obligation if NRL withholding has covered the tax." FALSE — non-resident landlords with UK property income must register for SA and file annually per §17.7; NRL withholding is offset on the SA return, not a substitute for it.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: Does the 2018 UK-Isle of Man Double Taxation Agreement mean I don't pay UK tax on my Manchester rental flat?**
   A: No. Article 6 of the 2018 treaty allocates source-state taxing rights to the UK on income from UK immovable property. UK domestic charge under ITTOIA 2005 Part 3 (individuals) or CTA 2009 Part 4 (companies) applies as if no treaty existed. The treaty's role is to prevent double taxation by obliging the Isle of Man (as residence state) to give a foreign tax credit for UK tax paid, not to exempt the UK source.

2. **Q: I'm Manx-resident — do my UK tenants or letting agent still have to withhold 20% under the NRL scheme?**
   A: Yes. The Non-Resident Landlord scheme is statutory under FA 1995 Schedule 23 and SI 1995/2902. It is a UK domestic obligation that operates independently of any treaty. Tenants (where annual rent exceeds £100/week and no letting agent is involved) and letting agents must withhold 20% of UK rent paid to a non-resident landlord unless the landlord holds NRL1 / NRL2 / NRL3 approval to receive rent gross. Apply for NRL1 on the date you first instruct the agent; HMRC typically approves within six weeks.

3. **Q: How does the 60-day NRCGT return apply when I sell a UK property as a Manx-resident?**
   A: NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A and Schedules 1A / 1B / 4AA applies to every UK land disposal by a non-resident, including indirect disposals of shares in property-rich entities. The 60-day return must be filed regardless of whether tax is due. Residential rates are 18% basic / 24% higher band on the gain (post-30 October 2024); Annual Exempt Amount is £3,000 for individuals (nil for non-resident companies). Rebasing rules per §17.4: default 5 April 2015 rebasing for residential acquired before that date; 5 April 2019 for non-residential and indirect-disposal interests acquired before then.

4. **Q: I'm dual-resident under UK SRT and Manx domestic rules — how does the Article 4 tie-breaker work?**
   A: The Article 4 cascade applies in this order: (i) permanent home (resolved if available in only one state); (ii) centre of vital interests (family / economic / personal); (iii) habitual abode; (iv) nationality; (v) mutual agreement procedure between the competent authorities. Tie-breaker resolution affects treaty allocation only — it does NOT change your domestic UK residence under SRT for non-treaty UK matters. For NRL purposes, a treaty-resident-in-IoM individual is treated as the non-UK resident landlord and the NRL withholding applies on UK rental.

5. **Q: Does the 2018 treaty cover indirect disposals of shares in UK property-rich companies?**
   A: Yes. Article 13(4) of the 2018 UK-IoM treaty extends to alienation of shares deriving more than 75% of their value from immovable property in the source state. This aligns with UK NRCGT under TCGA 1992 Schedule 1A Part 4. A Manx-resident selling shares in a UK-property-rich entity (where the threshold-holder test is met — 25% or more at any time in the 2 years before disposal) is within both treaty allocation and UK statutory charge; the 60-day return is required.

6. **Q: I'm a Manx-incorporated company holding a UK BTL flat — what's my UK tax position?**
   A: Three-layer obligation. (i) UK corporation tax on rental profits under CTA 2009 Part 4 (non-resident companies have been within UK CT on UK property income since 6 April 2020). Per §21.A: 19% small-profits rate up to £50,000 augmented profits; 25% main rate above £250,000; 26.5% effective marginal between (with associated-companies divisor reducing thresholds where applicable). The CIHC carve-out at s.18N FA 2010 keeps you in the SPR provided the property is let commercially to unconnected tenants. (ii) NRL withholding by the agent unless NRL2/NRL3 approval is held. (iii) ATED under FA 2013 Part 3 if the dwelling is worth more than £500,000 — claim the s.133 property-rental-business relief on the annual return (relief is not automatic — file the return even when no tax is due to avoid Schedule 55 FA 2009 penalty points).

7. **Q: Are Manx companies in scope for ATED?**
   A: Yes. ATED applies to any non-natural person — UK or non-UK company, partnership with corporate member, collective investment scheme — holding a UK dwelling worth more than £500,000 per §18.2. A Manx company holding a £620,000 Liverpool BTL flat falls in the £500,001-£1m band: 2026/27 charge £4,600 (verify gov.uk at write per §16.27). The s.133 property-rental-business relief is typically available for commercial letting to unconnected tenants but MUST be claimed on the return; failing to file even where no tax is due triggers Schedule 55 FA 2009 penalty points and exposure to the OTM letters campaign per §18.7.

8. **Q: How does the foreign tax credit work? Do I claim it from HMRC or from the Isle of Man Income Tax Division?**
   A: The Isle of Man, as the residence state, gives the foreign tax credit. The UK retains its source-state taxing rights under Article 6 / 13 and does NOT give a UK-side credit for IoM tax (there typically is no IoM tax on the same income to credit). Mechanically: pay UK tax via SA or CT, then claim Manx FTC for UK tax paid on the Manx personal or corporate income tax return. The treaty's Article 23 (elimination of double taxation) sets the credit framework; Manx domestic law operates the credit calculation.

9. **Q: I'm a Manx resident planning to move back to the UK — does the FIG regime help me?**
   A: Possibly. Under ITTOIA 2005 ss.845A-845J (inserted by FA 2025 s.37) per §17.8, you qualify as a "new arrival" if you were not UK-resident in any of the 10 tax years preceding your arrival year. Manx residence counts as non-UK residence for this test. If you qualify, you can claim relief on foreign income and gains arising in the first 4 UK tax years — but per-year claim is required and claiming costs your personal allowance + dividend allowance + CGT AEA for that year (same architecture as historic remittance basis). The claim deadline is 31 January 12 months after the end of the tax year. Do the arithmetic before claiming.

10. **Q: What does the 2024 Memorandum of Understanding add?**
    A: The 7 February 2024 MoU clarifies competent-authority procedure and exchange-of-information arrangements between HMRC and the IoM Income Tax Division. It does not rewrite substantive treaty allocation. Operationally it tightens the framework for cross-border information requests and competent-authority resolution under Article 25 (Mutual Agreement Procedure). For most Manx-resident landlords the MoU is background context rather than active mechanic; it becomes relevant in a competent-authority dispute or a cross-border audit.

11. **Q: What does the 2021 exchange of letters on collection assistance mean for HMRC enforcement?**
    A: The exchange of letters (effective 1 January 2022) enables HMRC to seek IoM authority assistance recovering UK tax debts from Manx residents, and vice versa. In practical terms, a Manx-resident landlord who has not paid UK tax due on UK rental income can no longer rely on UK-IoM jurisdictional friction as an enforcement barrier. HMRC can pursue collection through Manx channels.

12. **Q: My UK tenant says the treaty means they shouldn't have to withhold 20% NRL on my Manchester flat — is that right?**
    A: No, and the tenant / agent is at risk if they don't withhold. NRL withholding is a statutory obligation on the tenant or agent under FA 1995 Sch 23 + SI 1995/2902. Treaty residence does not displace the obligation. The only mechanism to receive gross rent is HMRC NRL1 / NRL2 / NRL3 approval on the basis of the landlord's UK tax compliance. Until you hold that approval, the tenant or agent must withhold + account quarterly to HMRC; failure makes them liable for the unwithheld tax (no time limit for unprompted discovery).

13. **Q: What happens if I miss the 60-day NRCGT return after selling my UK flat?**
    A: Failure-to-file penalties under FA 2009 Schedule 55 apply per §27.2: £100 immediate, £200 at 3 months, £300 at 6 months and 12 months; tax-geared penalties for prompted / unprompted / deliberate / concealed bands above 6 months. Interest on unpaid tax runs from the original due date. The 60-day clock starts on the day of completion (legal completion, not exchange). Even if no tax is due (rebasing, AEA, or loss) the return MUST still be filed.
