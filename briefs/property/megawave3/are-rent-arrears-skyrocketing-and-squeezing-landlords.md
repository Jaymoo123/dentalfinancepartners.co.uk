# Rent Arrears: The Landlord's Tax + Possession Handbook (Post-RRA 2025)

## Statutory anchor

- Primary (possession ground — mandatory rent-arrears route, post-RRA-2025 reform): Housing Act 1988 Sch 2 Ground 8, as amended by Renters' Rights Act 2025 Sch 1 para 24 (verified verbatim 2026-05-27 against legislation.gov.uk). Threshold increased from **8 weeks** to **13 weeks** arrears; notice period under HA 1988 s.8 extended from **2 weeks** to **3 weeks**. Universal Credit housing-element delay carve-out added (disregarded when calculating arrears). RRA 2025 in force from 1 May 2026 per SI 2026/421 reg.2; §20.2 + §20 disambiguation table lock.
- Primary (discretionary arrears grounds — fallback where Ground 8 not met): HA 1988 Sch 2 Grounds 10 (some arrears at notice + possession date) and 11 (persistent delay in paying rent). Discretionary — court has discretion even if proved. Stage 2 verifies post-RRA-2025 wording at write time.
- Primary (notice and possession procedure): HA 1988 s.8 (notice of intention to seek possession). Form 3 prescribed notice. New 3-week notice period for Ground 8 per RRA 2025 Sch 1 para 24.
- Primary (irrecoverable-debt deduction — accruals-basis landlords): ITTOIA 2005 s.35 (bad and doubtful debts) — irrecoverable trade debts deductible when shown to be irrecoverable; trade-equivalent rule for property businesses under ITTOIA 2005 s.270 (deductions same basis as a trade). HMRC PIM2068 + BIM42700+.
- Primary (cash-basis default for individual landlords under £150,000): ITTOIA 2005 s.271A (cash basis is the default for individuals carrying on a property business with cash-basis receipts under the threshold). Cash-basis landlords do NOT recognise unpaid rent — arrears are simply not income until received. No bad-debt write-off needed under cash basis; conversely, no relief if the rent is received later.
- Primary (s.24 finance-cost restriction — the squeeze multiplier): ITTOIA 2005 ss.272A-272B (finance-cost restriction; mortgage interest converted to a 20% basic-rate tax reducer, not a deduction against profit). §4 lock.
- Primary (Universal Credit Alternative Payment Arrangement — direct landlord payment route): Universal Credit Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/376) reg 58 + Universal Credit guidance on Alternative Payment Arrangements. Landlord-direct payment available where tenant arrears reach 2 months' rent; ongoing payment of rent + arrears reduction.
- Supporting (PRS rent-arrears market data — for setting context only, not statutory): English Housing Survey + ONS rental data + NRLA quarterly landlord confidence index. Stage 2 verifies current figures at write time.
- Supporting (debt-management routes pre-eviction): pre-action protocol for possession claims by social landlords (CPR PD 55B, persuasive analogy for PRS landlords); breathing-space moratorium under Debt Respite Scheme Regulations 2020 (SI 2020/1311) — 60-day moratorium during which possession proceedings are paused for arrears within scope.
- House position reference: §20.2 + §20 disambiguation table (RRA 2025 s.3 + Sch 1 reform of Section 8 grounds); §4 (s.24 finance-cost restriction); §27.7 (record retention — bad-debt evidence trail); §19.16 (MTD-ITSA digital records). **No new HP lock needed** — all architecture already pre-locked. Possible §20 mini-extension if Stage 1b wants to pin the post-RRA-2025 Ground 8 threshold (13 weeks + 3 weeks notice) as a quotable verbatim line; informational, not blocking.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. **What the new Ground 8 threshold means in practice — the 13-week + 3-week clock** — Under the post-RRA-2025 reform (RRA 2025 Sch 1 para 24, in force 1 May 2026), the mandatory possession ground for rent arrears now requires **13 weeks** of unpaid rent (not 8 weeks) at both the notice date and the court hearing. The s.8 notice period is **3 weeks** (not 2 weeks). For a monthly-tenancy let, this means a tenant who pays no rent must be 3 calendar months in arrears before notice can be served, plus the 3-week notice period, plus court listing — realistic total to possession order in 5-6 months minimum. Under the UC carve-out (Sch 1 para 24), arrears attributable to delayed UC housing-element payments are disregarded — the landlord cannot use a UC-delay-arrears situation to satisfy Ground 8.
2. **Discretionary arrears grounds (Grounds 10 + 11) — the fallback route** — Where Ground 8 is not met (tenant pays just enough to drop below 13 weeks before hearing), Grounds 10 + 11 remain available. Ground 10 = some arrears unpaid at notice + hearing. Ground 11 = persistent delay in paying rent. Both DISCRETIONARY — court can refuse possession even if proved. Practical use: a tenant cycling through 8-12 weeks of arrears and then paying down before the hearing repeats the cycle indefinitely. RRA 2025 retained these grounds in modified form; Stage 2 verifies current wording at write time.
3. **The cash-basis-vs-accruals decision — and what it means for arrears recognition** — ITTOIA 2005 s.271A defaults individual landlords with property + trading turnover under £150,000 to cash basis. Under cash basis, arrears are **not income** until actually received. The landlord books only what hits the bank account; an unpaid tenant produces zero recognised rent (and therefore zero tax) until payment. **No bad-debt write-off is needed under cash basis** — the rent never entered the books in the first place. Larger landlords on accruals (s.25 election or above-threshold operations) book rent receivable, must then write off irrecoverable amounts under s.35 to back the income out.
4. **The bad-debt write-off — when and how to claim it on the SA105** — For accruals-basis landlords, ITTOIA 2005 s.35 (read through s.270's trade-equivalent rule) allows a deduction for rent debts shown to be **irrecoverable**. "Irrecoverable" is HMRC's working test (BIM42700+): the landlord must have taken reasonable steps to recover and the debt must be objectively bad — typical evidence is the tenant's untraceable departure, a county court judgment unsatisfied 6+ months, a bankruptcy or IVA. Doubtful debts (specific provision for unlikely-to-pay but not yet objectively bad) qualify but only for the specific amount; general provisions are not deductible. The deduction goes on **SA105 box 28** (other allowable property expenses) with a working schedule kept for evidence.
5. **The s.24 squeeze multiplier — why arrears hurt more for mortgage-funded landlords** — Under the §4 lock (ITTOIA 2005 s.272A-B), mortgage interest is no longer a deduction against rental profit. It is converted to a 20% basic-rate tax reducer applied against the tax bill. A landlord with £20,000 annual rent and £15,000 annual mortgage interest pre-2017 had £5,000 taxable profit and a £2,000 tax bill (40% band). Post-s.24 with full restriction, the same landlord has £20,000 taxable profit, £8,000 marginal tax, less £3,000 reducer = £5,000 tax bill — £3,000 worse. Now layer arrears: if the tenant pays only £15,000 (3 months arrears), the landlord still owes £20,000 mortgage interest, books £15,000 rent (cash basis), pays £6,000 marginal tax less £3,000 reducer = £3,000 tax bill on a property that produced **zero net cash** for the year. The s.24 + arrears interaction is the operational squeeze the page must surface in numbers.
6. **The UC Alternative Payment Arrangement (APA) — the landlord-direct payment route** — Universal Credit Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/376) reg 58 + UC guidance allow landlord-direct payment of the housing element where (a) the tenant is in 2+ months arrears AND (b) the landlord applies through the UC Landlord Portal. Approved APA = housing element paid direct to landlord, bypassing the tenant. Ongoing rent + a contribution toward arrears (typically 10-20% of the standard allowance) reduces the arrears over time. APA does NOT bypass the possession route — Ground 8 + Grounds 10/11 still available — but it is the operational tool that stabilises cash-flow without immediately moving to possession.
7. **Pre-eviction debt-management — breathing space + court pre-action protocol** — Under the Debt Respite Scheme Regulations 2020 (SI 2020/1311), a tenant entering a breathing-space moratorium (via an FCA-authorised debt adviser) triggers a **60-day pause** on possession proceedings, court fees, interest, and certain enforcement action. Landlords with a tenant in breathing space must respect the pause; serving a s.8 notice during the moratorium is enforcement action capable of being challenged. The Mental Health Crisis Moratorium variant (under the same Regulations) has no time limit while the crisis treatment is ongoing. Pre-action protocol practice (CPR PD 55B persuasive for PRS) recommends written engagement with the tenant + signposting to debt advice before formal possession.
8. **Records, evidence, audit trail for bad debts** — TMA 1970 s.12B 5-year retention for property-business records (cf. §27.7). Required documentation for a s.35 write-off: tenancy agreement; rent ledger showing the period of non-payment; copies of demand letters / WhatsApp / email correspondence chasing payment; s.8 notice or court papers (where possession sought); county court judgment (where obtained); evidence of attempted enforcement (instructed bailiffs, return of warrant); tracing-agent invoice (where tenant absconded); insolvency filings (where tenant is bankrupt or in IVA). The write-off claim must be backed by this evidence on enquiry — HMRC PIM2068 + BIM42700+ define the working test.
9. **The 60-day CGT route + arrears — what happens on sale of an arrears-burdened BTL** — Where a landlord sells a BTL with a sitting non-paying tenant (a "tenanted sale to discount"), the disposal triggers CGT under TCGA 1992 Sch 2 paras 6-12 (60-day in-year reporting) at the residential rate (24% from 30 October 2024, §5 lock). Sale price typically depressed 15-25% for sitting tenant + arrears. The arrears outstanding at sale are NOT capital — they remain an income-tax matter and if irrecoverable can be written off in the year of disposal. The 12-month landlord-sale-grounds re-letting restriction under RRA 2025 s.15 (inserting HA 1988 s.16E) is the operative gate where possession is sought first then sale; otherwise the tenanted-sale route avoids that restriction.
10. **The PRS context — when arrears are the symptom of regulatory squeeze** — Rent arrears in 2026 sit at the intersection of the cost-of-living squeeze + s.24 mortgage cost compounding + the RRA 2025 tenancy-security shift. The macro context is for the page's framing, not for legal claims — Stage 2 verifies current arrears statistics at write time via NRLA quarterly landlord confidence + ONS rental data + English Housing Survey. The page should NOT lead with macro numbers; lead with the landlord's situation today and frame the macro context as the WHY at the end.

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The 13-week + 3-week possession timeline for monthly rent

Tenant rent £1,500/month, paid in advance on the 1st. Tenant pays March 2026 rent then stops. Landlord's options under the post-RRA-2025 regime:

- **30 April:** 2 months arrears (£3,000) — under the OLD Ground 8 threshold (8 weeks), notice would now be possible; under POST-RRA-2025 Ground 8 (13 weeks), still insufficient. Landlord cannot yet serve a mandatory-ground notice.
- **30 May:** 3 months arrears (£4,500) — Ground 8 threshold of 13 weeks NOW reached. Landlord may serve s.8 notice with 3-week notice period (notice expires ~21 June).
- **21 June:** notice expires; landlord applies to court for possession order. Court listing typically 6-10 weeks from application; possession order likely August or September 2026.
- **End-September 2026:** possession order, with 14-day enforcement-vacate window. Bailiff appointment 4-8 weeks further.
- **Total elapsed time:** approximately 7-9 months from first missed payment to vacant possession. Total arrears at handover: approximately £10,500-13,500.

### Example 2 — The s.24 + arrears squeeze worked end-to-end

Landlord: higher-rate (40%) taxpayer; single BTL property; £20,000 annual rent at full occupancy; £15,000 annual mortgage interest; £3,000 other allowable expenses; cash-basis taxpayer (gross under £150,000 threshold).

- **Year of full payment (baseline):** rent received £20,000; deductible expenses £3,000; taxable profit £17,000; income tax at 40% = £6,800; less 20% s.24 reducer on £15,000 mortgage interest = £3,000; **net tax bill £3,800; net cash after mortgage £2,000**.
- **Year tenant pays 8 months (£13,500 received, 4 months arrears):** rent received £13,500 (cash basis — arrears not income); deductible expenses £3,000; taxable profit £10,500; income tax at 40% = £4,200; less s.24 reducer £3,000; **net tax bill £1,200; net cash after mortgage £-2,700** (loss).
- **Year tenant pays 4 months (£6,000 received, 8 months arrears):** rent received £6,000; deductible expenses £3,000; taxable profit £3,000; income tax at 40% = £1,200; less s.24 reducer £3,000 (capped at the tax otherwise due) = nil reducer-net; **net tax bill nil; net cash after mortgage £-12,000** (substantial loss).

Inline aside CTA: "The s.24 reducer cap on a low-income year is a trap most landlord-tax pages miss. Talk to us about modelling the multi-year tax-and-cash impact of your specific arrears situation."

### Example 3 — Accruals-basis landlord: the s.35 bad-debt write-off

Landlord: above £150,000 threshold (portfolio landlord on accruals); single BTL property £20,000 annual rent recognised as receivable; tenant pays £6,000 then disappears in month 4; subsequent county court judgment £14,000 unsatisfied 12 months later; bailiff returns warrant unsatisfied; tenant untraceable.

- **Accruals book at year-end:** rent receivable £20,000; cash received £6,000; debtor balance £14,000.
- **s.35 + s.270 write-off:** the £14,000 is objectively irrecoverable on the evidence (CCJ unsatisfied 6+ months, bailiff warrant returned unsatisfied, tenant untraceable). Write off £14,000 to bad-debt expense.
- **Tax computation:** taxable rental profit = £20,000 receivable − £14,000 bad debt − £3,000 other expenses = £3,000. Income tax + s.24 calculation applied to £3,000 base.
- **Evidence pack retained for HMRC enquiry:** tenancy agreement, rent ledger, demand letters, s.8 notice, court papers, CCJ, bailiff return, tracing-agent invoice. TMA 1970 s.12B 5-year retention discipline.

Inline aside CTA: "We can review your bad-debt evidence pack before submission to make sure the s.35 write-off survives an HMRC enquiry. Particularly important if the arrears exceed £10,000."

### Example 4 — The UC Alternative Payment Arrangement application

Tenant in receipt of Universal Credit; falls 2 months in arrears (£3,000 on monthly £1,500 rent). Tenant has not engaged with landlord since week 4. Landlord application route:

1. Landlord registers on the UC Landlord Portal (gov.uk/landlord-portal).
2. Landlord submits the APA application: tenant NI number; tenancy address; current arrears amount; rent amount; bank details for direct payment.
3. UC assesses (typically 4-6 weeks): if approved, housing-element paid direct to landlord going forward + ongoing contribution to arrears (typically 10-20% of standard allowance).
4. APA continues until either (a) tenant moves to rent + arrears stability for 6+ months and applies for reinstatement of tenant-direct, or (b) tenancy terminates.

Critically: APA does NOT pause possession proceedings. The landlord may continue with the s.8 Ground 8 / Grounds 10-11 route in parallel where appropriate. The two operate independently.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target)

1. **Q: How many weeks of arrears do I need for a mandatory possession ground after RRA 2025?**
   A: Under the post-RRA-2025 reform (Renters' Rights Act 2025 Sch 1 para 24, in force 1 May 2026), the mandatory possession ground for rent arrears (HA 1988 Sch 2 Ground 8) now requires **13 weeks** of unpaid rent at both the notice date and the court hearing, up from 8 weeks pre-RRA-2025. The s.8 notice period itself has also extended from 2 weeks to **3 weeks**. For a monthly tenancy, this means the tenant must be a full 3 calendar months in arrears before notice can be served. Universal Credit housing-element-delayed arrears are disregarded for the 13-week count under the new carve-out.

2. **Q: Can I still serve a Section 8 notice for arrears under the older 8-week rule if my tenant fell into arrears before May 2026?**
   A: RRA 2025 commencement on 1 May 2026 applies to notices SERVED on or after that date. Pre-existing arrears built up before commencement count toward the 13-week threshold (the threshold is about arrears state at notice + hearing, not arrears accrual date). RUN session verifies the commencement-and-transitional position at write.

3. **Q: What about Grounds 10 and 11 if the tenant pays down to below 13 weeks before the hearing?**
   A: Grounds 10 (some arrears unpaid at notice + hearing) and 11 (persistent delay in paying rent) remain available as DISCRETIONARY grounds. The court can refuse possession even if proved. Tenants strategically cycling 8-12 weeks of arrears and then partially paying down ahead of the hearing exploit the discretionary nature. The RRA 2025 retained these grounds in modified form; RUN session verifies the current consolidated wording at write.

4. **Q: I'm on the cash basis — do I have to write off arrears at all?**
   A: No. Cash-basis landlords under the ITTOIA 2005 s.271A default (individual property businesses under £150,000 turnover) only recognise rent when received. Unpaid arrears are simply not income; no bad-debt write-off is needed because the rent never entered the books. Conversely, if a previously-uncollected arrear is paid years later, that payment IS income in the year of receipt under the cash basis.

5. **Q: I'm on accruals — when can I claim a bad-debt write-off for rent arrears?**
   A: Under ITTOIA 2005 s.35 (read through s.270's trade-equivalent rule), accruals-basis landlords can deduct rent debts shown to be IRRECOVERABLE. HMRC's working test (BIM42700+) requires reasonable steps taken to recover the debt + objective bad-debt evidence: typical examples include an untraceable tenant departure, a county court judgment unsatisfied 6+ months, a bankruptcy or IVA. Doubtful debts (specific provision for unlikely-to-pay but not yet objectively bad) qualify for the specific amount; general provisions are not deductible.

6. **Q: Which box on my SA105 does the bad-debt write-off go in?**
   A: For self-assessment individual landlords, the bad-debt write-off goes in box 28 (other allowable property expenses) on SA105 with a working schedule kept for evidence. The arrears are NOT shown as a reduction in box 5 (rent received); box 5 carries the gross rent receivable on accruals, and the bad-debt write-off appears as a deduction.

7. **Q: How does Section 24 interact with rent arrears?**
   A: ITTOIA 2005 ss.272A-272B convert mortgage interest from a deduction-against-profit into a 20% basic-rate tax reducer applied against the tax bill. This means that when a tenant pays partial rent, the landlord's gross taxable profit drops (good) but the s.24 reducer is capped at the tax otherwise due (bad — the reducer cannot create a refund). For a higher-rate landlord with high mortgage interest and substantial arrears, the s.24 + arrears combination can produce a tax bill on a property that delivers ZERO net cash for the year.

8. **Q: Can I claim Universal Credit's Alternative Payment Arrangement to receive rent direct from the DWP?**
   A: Yes, under Universal Credit Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/376) reg 58 + UC guidance, landlord-direct payment of the housing element is available where the tenant is 2+ months in arrears AND the landlord applies via the UC Landlord Portal. Approved APA = housing element paid direct to the landlord, bypassing the tenant, plus ongoing contribution toward arrears (typically 10-20% of the standard allowance). APA does NOT pause possession proceedings — Ground 8 + Grounds 10/11 remain available in parallel.

9. **Q: What is "breathing space" and how does it affect my possession proceedings?**
   A: Under the Debt Respite Scheme Regulations 2020 (SI 2020/1311), a tenant entering a breathing-space moratorium (via an FCA-authorised debt adviser) triggers a **60-day pause** on possession proceedings, court fees, interest, and certain enforcement action. Serving a s.8 notice during the moratorium is enforcement action capable of being challenged. The Mental Health Crisis Moratorium variant has no time limit while crisis treatment is ongoing. Landlords with a tenant in breathing space must respect the pause and re-serve any notice once the moratorium ends.

10. **Q: What records do I need to support a bad-debt write-off?**
    A: Required documentation under HMRC PIM2068 + BIM42700+: tenancy agreement; rent ledger showing the period of non-payment; copies of demand letters / WhatsApp / email correspondence chasing payment; s.8 notice or court papers; county court judgment (where obtained); evidence of attempted enforcement (bailiff instructions, return of warrant); tracing-agent invoice (where tenant absconded); insolvency filings (where tenant is bankrupt or in IVA). Retain for 5 years per TMA 1970 s.12B per §27.7.

11. **Q: What happens to the arrears if I sell the property with the tenant in situ?**
    A: The disposal triggers CGT under TCGA 1992 Sch 2 paras 6-12 (60-day in-year reporting) at the residential rate (24% from 30 October 2024 per §5). Sale price is typically depressed 15-25% to reflect sitting-tenant-plus-arrears. The arrears outstanding at sale remain an income-tax matter — they are NOT capital. If irrecoverable post-sale (the tenant remains in occupation but the new owner now collects), the original landlord can write off the pre-sale arrears under s.35 in the year of disposal. The 12-month landlord-sale-grounds re-letting restriction under RRA 2025 s.15 (inserting HA 1988 s.16E) does not apply to tenanted sales.

12. **Q: Does the rent arrears figure on my SA return need to match the figure in my tenancy ledger?**
    A: For accruals-basis taxpayers, yes — the gross rent receivable in box 5 should reconcile to the contractual rent due for the year per the tenancy ledger. Variances arise legitimately (rent reviews mid-year, void periods, periodic tenancy renewals); these are documented in the working schedule. For cash-basis taxpayers, box 5 reflects RECEIVED rent only; ledger and SA figures will diverge by the arrears amount.
