# How Making Tax Digital Affects Limited Companies: A Landlord-LtdCo Guide to MTD ITSA Exclusion, MTD VAT, and the Future MTD for CT Cycle

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — MTD ITSA limited-company exclusion:** MTD for Income Tax Self-Assessment applies to individuals (sole traders and landlords) under TMA 1970 **Schedule A1** (inserted by FA (No.2) 2017 Schedule 14) read with the Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1076). **Limited companies are outside the scope of MTD ITSA entirely** — they file annual CT600 returns under the corporation-tax regime governed by FA 1998 Sch 18 and CTA 2009 / CTA 2010. The exclusion is structural, not exemption-based: a LtdCo is a separate legal person taxed under CT not IT, so MTD ITSA (which sits within Schedule A1 to TMA 1970, the income-tax management Act) does not engage. House position §19.3 ("Excluded categories") expressly locks this.
- **Primary — MTD VAT (where it engages):** Value Added Tax (Amendment) Regulations 2018 (SI 2018/261) — the operative regulations inserting Part 7A "Digital reporting and record-keeping for VAT" into the VAT Regulations 1995 (SI 1995/2518). **MTD VAT became mandatory for VAT-registered businesses above the VAT-registration threshold from 1 April 2019**, and **extended to all VAT-registered businesses (regardless of turnover) from 1 April 2022**. A LtdCo with VAT-registered activity — typically commercial-property let with an option to tax under VATA 1994 Sch 10, or serviced-accommodation / FHL business pre-2025 abolition, or a TOGC sale under VATA 1994 s.49 + SI 1995/1268 reg 5 — is within MTD VAT scope.
- **Primary — corporation-tax digital records (the parallel CT framework):** CTA 2009 s.386 + Companies Act 2006 s.388 — the statutory accounting-records obligation for limited companies (6-year retention from end of accounting period). FA 1998 Sch 18 paragraph 17 sets the late-filing penalty regime (£200 / £400 with £1000 / £2000 third-successive-failure uplift — verified per F-1 lock from MW3 Bucket A Sub-Agent A on 2026-05-27; sessions must NOT use the historic £100 / £200 / £500 / £1000 figures). This is the **existing** CT digital-records floor — distinct from a future "MTD for Corporation Tax" cycle.
- **Primary — "MTD for Corporation Tax" future cycle (no go-live date):** HMRC published a consultation on MTD for CT in November 2020 with proposed go-live "from 2026 at the earliest." Subsequent updates have deferred indefinitely; as of May 2026 there is **no confirmed go-live date**. Sessions writing must NOT cite a specific date for MTD for CT engagement; the published position is "future cycle, no date confirmed".
- **Supporting — close-company loan + DLA mechanics (cross-stream cluster):** §21.1 (DLA) + the s.455 close-company loan charge 2026/27 rate of **35.75%** (verified per F-9 Wave 7 Bucket B lock 2026-05-24 at gov.uk; FA 2026 s.4(1)(b) substitution to 35.75% effective 2026/27 onwards). The MTD-VAT digital-records requirement intersects with DLA bookkeeping for VAT-registered close-companies; sessions writing on LtdCo MTD must NOT collapse this into MTD ITSA.
- **House position reference:** §19.3 (Excluded categories — Wave 3 MTD lock) primary; §19 (MTD ITSA Wave 3 extension) for the broader MTD architecture; §21 (Ltd Co + FIC Wave 4 lock) for the LtdCo + FIC operational mechanics; §29 (VAT for property — architectural anchor cluster Wave 8 mini-lock) for the VAT-side framework. No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — the architecture is locked across §19 + §21 + §29.

Sessions writing must NOT frame this as "LtdCos are exempt from MTD" — that is wrong; LtdCos are subject to MTD VAT and will be subject to MTD for CT in due course.

The angle this page takes: a landlord considering or operating a property LtdCo (e.g. FIC structure under §21.5, single-director property SPV, family-LtdCo BTL incorporation post-Section-24, or commercial-property LtdCo with VAT-registered option-to-tax let) needs the **three-layer MTD picture** for the corporate wrapper: (1) MTD ITSA does NOT apply (the most common LLM-drift point); (2) MTD VAT DOES apply where VAT-registered; (3) MTD for CT will eventually engage but no current date. The page operates as a planning-decision input — for some landlords, the MTD-ITSA-exclusion is a meaningful incorporation-favouring factor (especially landlords near or above the £50k / £30k / £20k mandate thresholds whose admin appetite is the binding constraint).
