# How Making Tax Digital Affects Limited Companies: A Landlord-LtdCo Guide to MTD ITSA Exclusion, MTD VAT, and the Future MTD for CT Cycle

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — MTD ITSA limited-company exclusion:** MTD for Income Tax Self-Assessment applies to individuals (sole traders and landlords) under TMA 1970 **Schedule A1** (inserted by FA (No.2) 2017 Schedule 14) read with the Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1076). **Limited companies are outside the scope of MTD ITSA entirely** — they file annual CT600 returns under the corporation-tax regime governed by FA 1998 Sch 18 and CTA 2009 / CTA 2010. The exclusion is structural, not exemption-based: a LtdCo is a separate legal person taxed under CT not IT, so MTD ITSA (which sits within Schedule A1 to TMA 1970, the income-tax management Act) does not engage. House position §19.3 ("Excluded categories") expressly locks this.
- **Primary — MTD VAT (where it engages):** Value Added Tax (Amendment) Regulations 2018 (SI 2018/261) — the operative regulations inserting Part 7A "Digital reporting and record-keeping for VAT" into the VAT Regulations 1995 (SI 1995/2518). **MTD VAT became mandatory for VAT-registered businesses above the VAT-registration threshold from 1 April 2019**, and **extended to all VAT-registered businesses (regardless of turnover) from 1 April 2022**. A LtdCo with VAT-registered activity — typically commercial-property let with an option to tax under VATA 1994 Sch 10, or serviced-accommodation / FHL business pre-2025 abolition, or a TOGC sale under VATA 1994 s.49 + SI 1995/1268 reg 5 — is within MTD VAT scope.
- **Primary — corporation-tax digital records (the parallel CT framework):** CTA 2009 s.386 + Companies Act 2006 s.388 — the statutory accounting-records obligation for limited companies (6-year retention from end of accounting period). FA 1998 Sch 18 paragraph 17 sets the late-filing penalty regime (£200 / £400 with £1000 / £2000 third-successive-failure uplift — verified per F-1 lock from MW3 Bucket A Sub-Agent A on 2026-05-27; sessions must NOT use the historic £100 / £200 / £500 / £1000 figures). This is the **existing** CT digital-records floor — distinct from a future "MTD for Corporation Tax" cycle.
- **Primary — "MTD for Corporation Tax" future cycle (no go-live date):** HMRC published a consultation on MTD for CT in November 2020 with proposed go-live "from 2026 at the earliest." Subsequent updates have deferred indefinitely; as of May 2026 there is **no confirmed go-live date**. Sessions writing must NOT cite a specific date for MTD for CT engagement; the published position is "future cycle, no date confirmed".
- **Supporting — close-company loan + DLA mechanics (cross-stream cluster):** §21.1 (DLA) + the s.455 close-company loan charge 2026/27 rate of **35.75%** (verified per F-9 Wave 7 Bucket B lock 2026-05-24 at gov.uk; FA 2026 s.4(1)(b) substitution to 35.75% effective 2026/27 onwards). The MTD-VAT digital-records requirement intersects with DLA bookkeeping for VAT-registered close-companies; sessions writing on LtdCo MTD must NOT collapse this into MTD ITSA.
- **House position reference:** §19.3 (Excluded categories — Wave 3 MTD lock) primary; §19 (MTD ITSA Wave 3 extension) for the broader MTD architecture; §21 (Ltd Co + FIC Wave 4 lock) for the LtdCo + FIC operational mechanics; §29 (VAT for property — architectural anchor cluster Wave 8 mini-lock) for the VAT-side framework. No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — the architecture is locked across §19 + §21 + §29.

### Example 1 — Single-director BTL SPV considering MTD scope

- **Aldridge BTL Ltd, the director-shareholder:** holds 4 residential rentals via a single-director SPV. Gross rental income £62,000 in 2026/27. The director also has £15,000 personal rental income (1 property held outside the SPV) and £8,000 self-employment income.
- **MTD ITSA scope:** the LtdCo is OUTSIDE MTD ITSA entirely (§19.3 / Wave 3 lock). The director's PERSONAL position is at £23,000 qualifying income (rental + self-employment) — below the April 2026 £50,000 phase but above the April 2028 £20,000 phase. The director enters MTD ITSA personally from April 2028 for their own income; the LtdCo's £62,000 rental remains entirely outside the digital regime.
- **MTD VAT scope:** the LtdCo is NOT VAT-registered (residential lettings are exempt under VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 1). MTD VAT does not engage.
- **Future MTD for CT:** the LtdCo will fall within scope when HMRC confirms a go-live date; as of May 2026 no date has been confirmed.
- **Operational point:** the director can use the LtdCo wrapper as a MTD-ITSA-admin escape for the rental activity. The personal-side quarterly cycle covers personal-rental + self-employment only.

### Example 2 — LtdCo with VAT-registered commercial-property let

- **Mawell Commercial Properties Ltd, the company:** owns 3 commercial properties let on FRI leases. The company has opted to tax under VATA 1994 Sch 10 to recover input VAT on refurbishment costs. Annual rental £180,000, all VAT-standard-rated. Annual input VAT recoverable on operating costs.
- **MTD ITSA scope:** the LtdCo is outside MTD ITSA (§19.3 lock).
- **MTD VAT scope:** the LtdCo IS within MTD VAT. Since 1 April 2019 (above-threshold businesses) and 1 April 2022 (all VAT-registered businesses regardless of turnover), MTD VAT applies. The LtdCo files quarterly VAT returns via MTD-compatible software, keeps digital records under reg 7A-7C of the VAT Regs 1995 Part 7A, and submits returns through HMRC's MTD VAT API.
- **Future MTD for CT:** awaiting confirmed go-live date.
- **Operational point:** the MTD VAT obligation has been in force for this company since 2019; it is not new. The page should NOT frame MTD VAT as a future obligation for property LtdCos — it is current.

### Example 3 — Mid-tax-year incorporation transition

- **Singh-Estate, the landlord:** runs a 6-property BTL portfolio personally generating £85,000 gross rental in 2026/27. Mandated into MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026 at the £50,000 phase. Decides to incorporate on 1 October 2026, transferring the properties to Singh-Estate Property Ltd.
- **Effect on personal-side MTD ITSA:** from 1 October 2026 the personal rental activity ceases. The landlord still files quarterly updates for Q1 (April-June) and Q2 (July-September). After incorporation the personal-side qualifying income drops below threshold. Whether the landlord can exit MTD ITSA depends on the §19.15 mid-year cessation framework + reg 22 three-tax-year-income exit route (cross-link to A7 sibling pick).

- **Operational point:** incorporation can simplify the MTD admin burden for landlords at or above the personal mandate thresholds whose admin appetite is the binding constraint.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target)

1. **Q: Does MTD for Income Tax Self-Assessment apply to limited companies?**
   A: No. MTD ITSA applies to individuals (sole traders and landlords) under TMA 1970 Schedule A1 and the Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1076). Limited companies are structurally outside MTD ITSA because they are taxed under corporation tax, not income tax. The exclusion is structural, not an exemption.

2. **Q: Does MTD for VAT apply to a property LtdCo?**
   A: Yes, where the LtdCo is VAT-registered. MTD VAT has been mandatory for above-threshold VAT-registered businesses since 1 April 2019 and for ALL VAT-registered businesses (regardless of turnover) since 1 April 2022. A property LtdCo with VAT-registered commercial-property activity (typically via an option to tax under VATA 1994 Sch 10) is within MTD VAT scope.

3. **Q: When does a property LtdCo become VAT-registered?**
   A: Three common triggers: (i) an option to tax on commercial property under VATA 1994 Sch 10 (the LtdCo elects to charge VAT on otherwise-exempt commercial rental to recover input VAT on refurbishment); (ii) serviced-accommodation / short-term-let activity above the VAT registration threshold (currently £90,000 from 1 April 2024 per the rate-by-reference discipline); (iii) trading activity associated with the property business (e.g. property-development trade) above the registration threshold.

4. **Q: Are residential lettings within MTD VAT?**
   A: No. Residential lettings are exempt from VAT under VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 1. A residential-only BTL LtdCo is not VAT-registered and is not within MTD VAT scope. The MTD VAT engagement question for property LtdCos turns on the commercial or short-let element of the portfolio.

5. **Q: What is "MTD for Corporation Tax" and when does it start?**
   A: HMRC published a consultation on Making Tax Digital for Corporation Tax in November 2020 with a proposed go-live date "from 2026 at the earliest". Subsequent updates have deferred indefinitely; as of May 2026 there is no confirmed go-live date. Sessions writing must NOT cite a specific date for MTD for CT — the published HMRC position is "future cycle, no date confirmed".

6. **Q: What CT-side filing obligations does the LtdCo already have?**
   A: CTA 2009 section 386 and Companies Act 2006 section 388 impose statutory accounting-record obligations on every limited company. Records must be retained for 6 years from the end of the accounting period. CT600 returns are filed annually under FA 1998 Schedule 18; late-filing penalties under Schedule 18 paragraph 17 are £200 / £400 (initial) escalating to £1,000 / £2,000 (third successive failure). Sessions writing must NOT use the historic £100 / £200 / £500 / £1000 figures (corrected per F-1 / Wave 3 lock).

7. **Q: Can incorporation help me avoid MTD ITSA?**
   A: For landlords near or above the £50,000 / £30,000 / £20,000 personal mandate thresholds whose admin burden is the binding constraint, yes. Incorporating into a LtdCo shifts the rental activity into a structure outside MTD ITSA. The trade-off is the broader incorporation-vs-personal calculus (CT rates, S24 mortgage-interest restriction, dividend tax on profit extraction, ATED on residential properties valued above £500,000). MTD admin reduction is one factor, not the dominant one.

8. **Q: How does VAT MTD interact with the CT-side accounting cycle?**
   A: They run in parallel. MTD VAT is a quarterly digital reporting cycle for VAT only; the CT600 is an annual return for corporation tax. A VAT-registered LtdCo files four MTD VAT returns and one CT600 per accounting period. The records supporting both can sit in the same accounting software, but the regulatory cycles are distinct.

9. **Q: What happens if I run a portfolio personally AND through a LtdCo?**
   A: The two streams run in parallel. Personal-side rental + self-employment falls within MTD ITSA if qualifying income crosses the cohort threshold (£50,000 / £30,000 / £20,000 phased schedule). LtdCo-side rental sits outside MTD ITSA but engages MTD VAT if VAT-registered and will engage MTD for CT in due course. The two regimes do not statutorily overlap; sessions must NOT collapse one into the other.

10. **Q: Is a Family Investment Company within MTD ITSA?**
    A: No. A Family Investment Company (FIC) is structurally a limited company and falls within the LtdCo exclusion from MTD ITSA. Sessions writing on FIC + MTD must NOT introduce quarterly-update obligations on the FIC itself. The §21.5 / Wave 4 FIC investment-only lock confirms this.

11. **Q: What is the position when I incorporate mid-tax-year?**
    A: Personal-side rental activity ceases at incorporation. Quarterly MTD ITSA updates continue for the periods up to cessation. The §19.15 mid-year cessation framework (Wave 4 lock) governs the personal-side close-out. The LtdCo's CT-side digital obligations begin with the company's first accounting period. The transition is administrative; both regimes apply to their respective parts of the tax year.

12. **Q: Will MTD for CT change how property LtdCos file?**
    A: Pending HMRC's confirmation. The 2020 consultation proposed quarterly-update obligations for LtdCos similar to MTD ITSA, plus an end-of-period statement and a final declaration. The published position is now indefinitely deferred. Property LtdCos should monitor HMRC updates but should NOT plan for imminent engagement; the current CT600 annual cycle continues unchanged.
