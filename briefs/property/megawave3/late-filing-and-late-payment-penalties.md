# Late Filing and Late Payment Penalties: The Self-Assessment Cascade for Individual Landlords (Pre-MTD + MTD ITSA from April 2026)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — late filing of SA return (legacy regime, still operative for non-MTD landlords):** Finance Act 2009 Schedule 55 — item 1 (Income Tax / CGT under TMA 1970). Sch 55 para 3 imposes the **£100 fixed initial penalty** when the return is not filed by the filing date (31 January following the tax year for electronic returns; 31 October for paper). Sch 55 para 4 imposes **£10-per-day** for up to 90 days where the return remains outstanding after 3 months. Sch 55 para 5 imposes a further **5% of tax liability or £300, whichever is greater** at 6 months. Sch 55 para 6 imposes a further **5% or £300** at 12 months (with a higher 70%/100% uplift where the failure is deliberate / deliberate-and-concealed per para 6(3A)-(5)). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2009/10/schedule/55`.
- **Primary — late payment of SA tax (legacy regime):** Finance Act 2009 Schedule 56 — item 1 (Income Tax / CGT under TMA 1970 s.59B). Sch 56 para 3(2) imposes **5% of unpaid tax at the 30-day point**; sch 56 para 3(3) imposes a further **5% at the 6-month point** (5 months after the 30-day trigger); sch 56 para 3(4) imposes a further **5% at the 12-month point**. Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2009/10/schedule/56`. Cross-check: TMA 1970 s.59B sets the **31 January following the tax year** SA payment due date; s.59A sets the 31 January + 31 July payments-on-account architecture (which is its own late-payment trigger, not Sch 56 — payments-on-account interest only, no Sch 56 surcharge).
- **Primary — MTD ITSA points-based late-submission regime (supersedes Sch 55 item 1 for in-scope landlords from 6 April 2026):** Finance Act 2021 Schedule 24 (the new points-based regime — distinct from FA 2007 Sch 24 inaccuracy penalties; sessions must NOT conflate the two Sch 24 instruments — context distinguishes them). 1 point per missed quarterly update; threshold **4 points** for quarterly filers; at threshold **£200** per missed submission while at threshold; points reset after 24 months of full compliance. House-position-locked at §19.7 with verification against gov.uk 2026-05-22.
- **Primary — MTD ITSA accelerated late-payment cascade (supersedes Sch 56 item 1 for in-scope landlords from 6 April 2026):** Spring Statement 2025 reform doubling the percentages AND accelerating the trigger days. **3% from day 15, +3% from day 30, +10% per annum from day 31** — anchored verbatim in the gov.uk Spring Statement 2025 HTML document (`https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/spring-statement-2025-document/spring-statement-2025-html`) verified at the Wave 3 §19.7 lock. The legacy 31/46/91 day-triggers at 2%/2%/4% remain ONLY for VAT and for non-MTD income tax filers below the threshold. House-position-locked at §19.7 with the F-1-adjacent correction logged 2026-05-22 (earlier site copy carried the legacy 31/46/91 schedule against the new percentages — sessions writing must use the 15/30/31 schedule WITH the 3%/3%/10% percentages, not crossover).
- **Primary — reasonable-excuse defence:** Schedule 55 paragraph 23 + Schedule 56 paragraph 16 — statutory exclusion from penalty where the taxpayer had a reasonable excuse for the failure and remedied the failure without unreasonable delay once the excuse ended. Controlling case law: **Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156** (four-stage test: what facts were the actual reason; whether those facts give rise to a reasonable excuse; whether the excuse persisted throughout the period of failure; whether the failure was remedied without unreasonable delay once the excuse ended). Stale guidance pre-Perrin should be flagged. House-position-locked at §27.3 + §27.4 lock 2026-05-24.
- **Primary — interest on unpaid tax (not penalty, but accrues separately):** TMA 1970 s.86 (interest on unpaid income tax / CGT) + FA 2009 s.101 (HMRC interest framework). Interest accrues from the due date and is not mitigated by any reasonable-excuse defence; the only way to stop interest is to pay the tax. **As of 2026, HMRC late-payment interest rate is the Bank of England base rate + 4 percentage points** (verify against gov.uk at write time per §16.27 rate-by-reference discipline — interest rates change with each MPC base-rate move). Distinct from penalty regime.
- **Supporting — appeal route:** TMA 1970 s.31A — 30-day appeal window against any penalty assessment. Late appeals require reasonable-excuse application under **Martland v HMRC [2018] UKUT 178** (three-stage test for late-appeal admission). Appeals go to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber) under TCEA 2007 + SI 2009/273 (Tribunal Procedure Rules).
- **House position reference:** §19.7 (MTD ITSA penalty regime — Wave 3 lock, primary for in-scope landlords from 6 April 2026); §27.3 (Sch 41 reasonable-excuse mechanics — read-across to Sch 55 / Sch 56 reasonable-excuse defence); §27.4 (FTT appeal route — TMA s.31A + TCEA 2007). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — the bifurcation is fully locked across §19.7 (MTD ITSA) and §27 (legacy SA).
