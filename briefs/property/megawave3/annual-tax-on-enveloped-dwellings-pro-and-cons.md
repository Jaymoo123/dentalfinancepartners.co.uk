# Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings: a balanced pros and cons walkthrough for landlords

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (charge):** **FA 2013 Part 3 (ss.94-174)** — ATED charge, reliefs, returns, valuation regime. Sections 94-99 establish the basic charge mechanism; sections 132-150 provide the relief catalogue (property-rental-business at s.133, property-developer at s.138, property-trader at s.141, farmhouse at s.144, employee accommodation at s.145, etc.).
- **Primary (SDLT 15% schedule for new enveloped acquisitions):** **Schedule 4A FA 2003** — the 15% SDLT rate on residential property over £500,000 acquired by a non-natural person, with corresponding reliefs mirroring the ATED relief catalogue.
- **Primary (CT on enveloped-rental profits):**
  - **CTA 2009 Part 4** (property income of a UK or non-UK company).
  - **CTA 2010 Part 3** (CT charge — 25% main rate / 19% small-profits rate / 26.5% effective marginal slice; CIHC carve-out at s.18N — see HP §21.A).
- **Primary (CGT on disposal):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** (NRCGT — captures disposals by non-resident companies of UK land; ATED-CGT abolished from 6 April 2019 per FA 2019).
- **Primary (IHT residence-based regime):** **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C + ss.267ZC-267ZF** (FA 2025 architecture — 10-of-20 long-term residence test; replaces deemed-domicile framework).
- **Primary (RoE):** ECTEA 2022 c. 10 — Register of Overseas Entities (parallel obligation for overseas enveloped structures).
- **Supporting:** Companies House ECCTA 2023 ID-verification regime (PSC + director); FA 2009 Sch 55 + Sch 56 (penalties); FA 2007 Sch 24 (inaccuracy penalties).
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 — verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (full Wave 3 ATED extension)**, §18.1 (band table), §18.2 (chargeable persons), §18.3 (relief catalogue), §18.4 (return mechanics + 5-yearly revaluation; 1 April 2027 next revaluation), §18.5 (ATED-CGT abolished from 6 April 2019; NRCGT now applies), §18.6 (RoE parallel obligation), §18.7 (HMRC OTM compliance campaign), §21.A (CT three-figure framework — relevant where company holds enveloped property), §22.X (IHT LTR — relevant where individual settlor is non-UK-resident long-term).

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What is ATED in 60 seconds? (Annual tax on UK residential property worth more than £500,000 held by a non-natural person. 2026/27 bands £4,600 / £9,450 / £32,200 / £75,450 / £151,450 / £303,450 per §18.1.)
2. What are the genuine advantages of holding via a company that ATED's annual cost might be worth bearing? (Anonymity, succession, limited liability, potential CT-vs-IT differential on rental profits post-§24, structural neutrality across portfolios, IHT positioning options.)
3. What are the genuine disadvantages beyond the ATED charge itself? (15% SDLT on new acquisitions; double-tax extraction; CIHC trap; admin + revaluation cycle; RoE + ECCTA overheads; CGT asymmetry.)
4. When does the **s.133 property-rental-business relief** save the day, and when does it not? (BTL ltd-co letting to unconnected tenants on commercial terms qualifies; family-occupant arrangements do not. Crucially the relief must be CLAIMED on a return; failing to file even when relief applies still triggers penalties.)
5. What is the impact of the **CIHC carve-out** on enveloped property? (HP §21.A: a close investment-holding company is always taxed at 25% CT, no small-profits rate. The expansive "let commercially" exclusion at s.18N(3) means letting to a connected person OR their spouse OR a relative of a connected person OR a relative's spouse defeats the qualifying-purpose carve-out.)
6. When does the FA 2025 IHT residence-based regime change the enveloping calculation? (For non-UK-resident long-term owners considering UK property holding, the 10-of-20 LTR test under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C reshapes the IHT-exposure timing. Cross-reference HP §22.X for the tail-period table.)
7. How does ATED interact with NRCGT on disposal? (Per §18.5: ATED-CGT abolished 6 April 2019; non-resident company disposals fall under TCGA 1992 s.1A NRCGT with the standard 60-day return obligation. UK companies pay CT on chargeable gains at the prevailing CT rate.)
8. What is the RoE / ECCTA load on an overseas-enveloped structure? (RoE annual update within 14 days of update period under ECTEA 2022 s.7; ECCTA director + PSC ID verification per HP §11.A — material admin burden.)
9. What is the de-enveloping pathway, and when is it net-positive? (Stage 2 walks the distribution-in-specie route, the SDLT-on-distribution analysis under FA 2003, the CGT recognition on the company's deemed disposal, and the personal-name post-de-envelope tax landscape including §24 restriction. The decision turns on the owner's marginal IT rate, the property's likely appreciation horizon, and the family-occupation profile.)
10. What is the **owner archetype** that ATED clearly favours? (High-value BTL with arm's-length corporate-tenant letting; multi-property portfolio with succession planning needs; overseas owner whose home jurisdiction makes corporate-holding tax-efficient on the source side.)
11. What is the **owner archetype** that ATED clearly disfavours? (Single property occupied by owner or family; low-yield property where £4,600+ annual charge erodes net return; owner with low marginal IT rate who would benefit more from personal-name §24-free treatment if eligible.)
