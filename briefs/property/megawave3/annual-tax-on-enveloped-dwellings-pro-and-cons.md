# Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings: a balanced pros and cons walkthrough for landlords

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (charge):** **FA 2013 Part 3 (ss.94-174)** — ATED charge, reliefs, returns, valuation regime. Sections 94-99 establish the basic charge mechanism; sections 132-150 provide the relief catalogue (property-rental-business at s.133, property-developer at s.138, property-trader at s.141, farmhouse at s.144, employee accommodation at s.145, etc.).
- **Primary (SDLT 15% schedule for new enveloped acquisitions):** **Schedule 4A FA 2003** — the 15% SDLT rate on residential property over £500,000 acquired by a non-natural person, with corresponding reliefs mirroring the ATED relief catalogue.
- **Primary (CT on enveloped-rental profits):**
  - **CTA 2009 Part 4** (property income of a UK or non-UK company).
  - **CTA 2010 Part 3** (CT charge — 25% main rate / 19% small-profits rate / 26.5% effective marginal slice; CIHC carve-out at s.18N — see HP §21.A).
- **Primary (CGT on disposal):**
  - **TCGA 1992 s.1A + Sch 1A / 1B / 4AA** (NRCGT — captures disposals by non-resident companies of UK land; ATED-CGT abolished from 6 April 2019 per FA 2019).
- **Primary (IHT residence-based regime):** **IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C + ss.267ZC-267ZF** (FA 2025 architecture — 10-of-20 long-term residence test; replaces deemed-domicile framework).
- **Primary (RoE):** ECTEA 2022 c. 10 — Register of Overseas Entities (parallel obligation for overseas enveloped structures).
- **Supporting:** Companies House ECCTA 2023 ID-verification regime (PSC + director); FA 2009 Sch 55 + Sch 56 (penalties); FA 2007 Sch 24 (inaccuracy penalties).
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 — verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (full Wave 3 ATED extension)**, §18.1 (band table), §18.2 (chargeable persons), §18.3 (relief catalogue), §18.4 (return mechanics + 5-yearly revaluation; 1 April 2027 next revaluation), §18.5 (ATED-CGT abolished from 6 April 2019; NRCGT now applies), §18.6 (RoE parallel obligation), §18.7 (HMRC OTM compliance campaign), §21.A (CT three-figure framework — relevant where company holds enveloped property), §22.X (IHT LTR — relevant where individual settlor is non-UK-resident long-term).

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What is ATED in 60 seconds? (Annual tax on UK residential property worth more than £500,000 held by a non-natural person. 2026/27 bands £4,600 / £9,450 / £32,200 / £75,450 / £151,450 / £303,450 per §18.1.)
2. What are the genuine advantages of holding via a company that ATED's annual cost might be worth bearing? (Anonymity, succession, limited liability, potential CT-vs-IT differential on rental profits post-§24, structural neutrality across portfolios, IHT positioning options.)
3. What are the genuine disadvantages beyond the ATED charge itself? (15% SDLT on new acquisitions; double-tax extraction; CIHC trap; admin + revaluation cycle; RoE + ECCTA overheads; CGT asymmetry.)
4. When does the **s.133 property-rental-business relief** save the day, and when does it not? (BTL ltd-co letting to unconnected tenants on commercial terms qualifies; family-occupant arrangements do not. Crucially the relief must be CLAIMED on a return; failing to file even when relief applies still triggers penalties.)
5. What is the impact of the **CIHC carve-out** on enveloped property? (HP §21.A: a close investment-holding company is always taxed at 25% CT, no small-profits rate. The expansive "let commercially" exclusion at s.18N(3) means letting to a connected person OR their spouse OR a relative of a connected person OR a relative's spouse defeats the qualifying-purpose carve-out.)
6. When does the FA 2025 IHT residence-based regime change the enveloping calculation? (For non-UK-resident long-term owners considering UK property holding, the 10-of-20 LTR test under IHTA 1984 ss.6A-6C reshapes the IHT-exposure timing. Cross-reference HP §22.X for the tail-period table.)
7. How does ATED interact with NRCGT on disposal? (Per §18.5: ATED-CGT abolished 6 April 2019; non-resident company disposals fall under TCGA 1992 s.1A NRCGT with the standard 60-day return obligation. UK companies pay CT on chargeable gains at the prevailing CT rate.)
8. What is the RoE / ECCTA load on an overseas-enveloped structure? (RoE annual update within 14 days of update period under ECTEA 2022 s.7; ECCTA director + PSC ID verification per HP §11.A — material admin burden.)
9. What is the de-enveloping pathway, and when is it net-positive? (Stage 2 walks the distribution-in-specie route, the SDLT-on-distribution analysis under FA 2003, the CGT recognition on the company's deemed disposal, and the personal-name post-de-envelope tax landscape including §24 restriction. The decision turns on the owner's marginal IT rate, the property's likely appreciation horizon, and the family-occupation profile.)
10. What is the **owner archetype** that ATED clearly favours? (High-value BTL with arm's-length corporate-tenant letting; multi-property portfolio with succession planning needs; overseas owner whose home jurisdiction makes corporate-holding tax-efficient on the source side.)
11. What is the **owner archetype** that ATED clearly disfavours? (Single property occupied by owner or family; low-yield property where £4,600+ annual charge erodes net return; owner with low marginal IT rate who would benefit more from personal-name §24-free treatment if eligible.)

### Example 2 — Owner archetype 1: ATED clearly favours (the multi-property BTL with succession needs)

Dr Singh-Patel (UK-resident; additional-rate IT payer; substantial mortgage borrowings; 6-property London BTL portfolio acquired 2014-2022; total portfolio value £8.5m; 3 adult children; succession-planning conversation underway with private-client team).
- **Pre-envelope position:** Personal-name holding → §24 restriction bites HARD on her additional-rate mortgage interest; effective tax on net rental profits roughly 45% on profit + reduced credit-for-mortgage-interest equivalent (basic-rate only). High-marginal-IT additional-rate exposure. Annual rental ~£280k; mortgage interest ~£120k; net pre-tax profit ~£160k; effective tax ~£72k + restricted credit treatment.
- **Post-envelope position via HoldCo + multi-SPV (UK-incorporated):** Each SPV taxed at CT rates per §21.A. With 6 associated SPVs the lower-limit divisor is £50k / 6 = £8,333 per SPV; upper-limit £250k / 6 = £41,667 per SPV. Each SPV typically below the upper limit on standalone profits — marginal-relief range applies; effective rate per SPV roughly 19-26.5% depending on profits position. Each SPV's commercial letting to unconnected tenant qualifies for s.18N(2)(b) carve-out → SPR / marginal-relief preserved (no CIHC).
- **ATED cost:** 6 dwellings × varying band charges. Suppose 3 dwellings in £1m-£2m band (£9,450 each) + 2 in £2m-£5m band (£32,200 each) + 1 in £500k-£1m band (£4,600). Total ATED 2026/27 = £28,350 + £64,400 + £4,600 = £97,350 annually (verify per §18.1 at write).
- **ATED RELIEF:** Each SPV lets commercially to unconnected tenants → s.133 property-rental-business relief CLAIMED on each return → ATED tax = £0 across all 6 dwellings. ADMIN OVERHEAD: 6 claim-only returns by 30 April each year + 5-yearly revaluations 1 April 2027 + ECCTA director/PSC verification + annual confirmation statements.
- **SDLT 15% on new acquisitions:** Any future SPV acquisition above £500k is subject to FA 2003 Sch 4A 15% rate UNLESS reliefs apply. Mirrored s.133 relief brings the rate back to the standard residential rates (FA 2003 SDLT scale + 3%/5% surcharges per §1.A) on acquisition.
- **CT-vs-IT differential:** at additional-rate IT (45%) vs CT (19-26.5%) on net rental profits, the enveloped route saves materially per year for Dr Singh-Patel even with extraction costs.
- **Succession:** at her eventual death, her shareholding (rather than 6 separate property titles) passes to her 3 adult children — operationally cleaner; potential for prospective gifting + 7-year PET planning on share value; potential FIC restructuring options.
- **IHT:** the underlying UK property is in UK IHT regardless (UK situs per §22.X.4) — enveloping does NOT shield from UK IHT on UK property. But for SUCCESSION operational simplicity + lifetime gifting structures, enveloping is materially helpful.
- **Verdict:** ATED favours. The annual £97,350 worst-case-before-relief is £0 after relief; CT savings on rental profits are material; succession architecture is materially cleaner.

### Example 3 — Owner archetype 2: ATED clearly disfavours (single-property owner-occupier with family use)

Mr Walker (UK-resident; basic-rate IT payer; bought a £1.6m Surrey home in 2018 in a UK Ltd company "for IHT reasons" on advice that has not aged well; owner-occupies with spouse + 2 children; the ltd-co holds no other property; ltd-co takes no rental income).
- **ATED position:** £1.6m dwelling → £1m-£2m band → 2026/27 charge £9,450 annually per §18.1. NO RELIEF applies (s.133 requires commercial letting to unconnected tenant — Walker family occupies; family-occupant arrangements do not qualify; no other relief on these facts).
- **CIHC risk:** the ltd-co's business is investment in land let to connected persons (the Walker family are connected to the company). The s.18N(2)(b) "let commercially" carve-out is DEFEATED per s.18N(3) → CIHC → 25% CT main rate locked in (no SPR even if profits low; no marginal-relief band).
- **Annual exposure:** £9,450 ATED + ECCTA director/PSC verification + ECTEA RoE if overseas-incorporated (not here; UK ltd-co) + Companies House filings + corporate accounting fees ~£1,500-£3,000/year.
- **Sale exposure:** if Walker eventually disposes (sells house), the company's chargeable gain attracts CT at 25% main rate (CIHC) — vs the personal-name route which would have given Private Residence Relief (PRR) under TCGA 1992 ss.222-226 → likely ZERO CGT on a fully-PRR-qualifying main residence.
- **IHT positioning:** UK property is in UK IHT regardless; enveloping has saved NOTHING on IHT.
- **De-envelope option:** distribution in specie of the £1.6m dwelling to Walker triggers (i) SDLT on the deemed market-value consideration (potentially 0% if the distribution is structured as a return of capital — verify SDLT analysis at write per FA 2003 + Schedule 4A interaction); (ii) CGT on the company's deemed disposal at market value (the company's chargeable gain is taxable at 25% CT main rate); (iii) on Walker's side, a distribution at market value may be a distribution under CTA 2010 s.1000 → income tax at dividend rates (basic 8.75% / higher 33.75% / additional 39.35% on the excess over share capital + share premium) OR a capital distribution if dissolution-routed. Specialist advice needed.
- **Verdict:** ATED disfavours strongly. Walker should consider de-enveloping despite the transaction costs — the £9,450 ATED + ongoing admin + lost-PRR-on-future-disposal aggregate cost outweighs the IHT advice that prompted enveloping in the first place. Pre-de-envelope analysis + specialist tax + legal representation mandatory.

### Example 4 — The CIHC carve-out trap (per §21.A + §21.5)

Sapling Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co; owns a single £950k London flat let to Mr Sapling's adult son Jake on a commercial-rent basis — Jake pays open-market rent of £42k/year; AST in place; deposit protected; all formalities observed). Sapling Holdings assumed the s.133 ATED relief applies because the letting is "commercial" + "to an unconnected tenant" + "with a view to profit". WRONG.
- **The CIHC test:** per §21.A + §21.5, "let commercially" per s.18N(2)(b) EXCLUDES letting to connected persons + their spouses + their relatives + their relatives' spouses per s.18N(3) statutory exclusion list. Jake is Mr Sapling's son → connected (relative of a connected person = Mr Sapling). The carve-out is DEFEATED. Sapling Holdings is a CIHC → 25% main rate CT on rental profits + investment income; no SPR; no marginal relief.
- **ATED side parallel:** s.133 property-rental-business relief for ATED also turns on the "unconnected" test (verify exact statutory language at write — the ATED relief tracks the CT carve-out closely but is not strictly identical). If Jake is treated as a connected person for s.133 purposes too, ATED relief fails → £4,600 (2026/27 £500k-£1m band — £950k flat just under £1m boundary) annual tax due.
- **Sapling's exposure across the structure:** ATED £4,600/year + CT at 25% on the net rental profit (no SPR) — material annual cost.
- **Even a single-property single-family-tenant arrangement triggers BOTH cliffs simultaneously.** The s.18N(3) exclusion is the most-misunderstood single point in UK property tax for family-property structuring.

### Example 5 — De-enveloping pathway worked example

Mr Ferguson (UK-resident; bought a £750k London BTL flat in 2019 via a UK ltd-co "Ferguson Property Holdings Limited" on cookie-cutter advice; let to unconnected tenant throughout; sole shareholder + director). 2026: tenant gives notice; Ferguson considers whether to keep enveloped or de-envelope before re-letting.
- **Current enveloped position:** £4,600 ATED annually (s.133 relief claimed); CT at SPR 19% on net rental profits (small SPV with augmented profits ≤£50k; CIHC carve-out preserved with unconnected commercial letting); ongoing ECCTA verification + Companies House admin + accounting fees ~£1,800/year. Total ongoing carry ~£2,000-£4,000/year (ATED nil due to relief but the admin is real).
- **De-envelope option:** distribute the flat in specie to Ferguson.
  - **SDLT analysis:** distribution in specie of a residential property to a shareholder — SDLT generally NOT chargeable IF the distribution is structured as a return of capital with no debt assumed by the shareholder; if Ferguson assumes existing mortgage debt as part of the distribution, the debt assumption is consideration → SDLT chargeable. Specialist SDLT advice required.
  - **CT on company's deemed disposal:** company deemed to dispose at market value; if 2019 cost £750k + 2026 market value £820k → £70k chargeable gain in the company → CT at SPR 19% = £13,300.
  - **Income tax / capital treatment on Ferguson's side:** distribution at market value → may be a distribution under CTA 2010 s.1000 → IT at dividend rates. At basic-rate (8.75%) on £820k - share capital ~£100 = £820k effective dividend → £71,750 IT bill. If structured via solvent dissolution (MVL — Member's Voluntary Liquidation) the distribution may instead be a capital distribution → CGT on Ferguson at 18%/24% residential rates with £3,000 AEA → £820k - share capital ~£100 - £3k AEA = £816,900 chargeable gain. At 24% higher-band that's £196,056 CGT — materially worse than the dividend route for Ferguson at basic-rate IT.
  - **TAAR / s.396B ITTOIA 2005 phoenix anti-avoidance:** if Ferguson resumes a substantially similar property activity within 2 years of MVL → TAAR potentially recharacterises the capital distribution as income → IT at dividend rates instead of CGT. Specialist counsel essential.
  - **Total de-envelope cost:** SDLT (variable — potentially nil if cleanly structured as capital return without debt assumption); CT £13,300 on the company; IT or CGT on Ferguson depending on route (£71,750 IT at basic-rate dividend route; substantially more at additional-rate dividend OR via MVL capital route + TAAR risk).
- **Post-de-envelope position:** Ferguson holds the £820k flat in his own name. Personal income tax at his marginal rate on rental profits (with §24 mortgage interest restriction). PRR available if he moves in (or partial PRR if owner-occupied for part of ownership). Standard NRCGT / CGT rules on disposal. NO ATED. NO Companies House admin.
- **Verdict:** for Ferguson's single-property SPV with commercial-letting, de-enveloping is COSTLY upfront (~£85k-£100k at basic-rate dividend route) but eliminates the ongoing £2-4k/year carry + restores PRR availability + simplifies structure. The cost-benefit turns on Ferguson's holding horizon (10+ years to recoup; <10 years suggests stay enveloped); his expected appreciation profile; his eventual disposal-vs-occupation intention. Specialist advice mandatory — this is not a do-it-yourself decision.

### Example 6 — Non-resident-overseas-owner archetype + new IHT LTR positioning

Sra Cordero (Spanish-resident throughout; not LTR per IHTA 1984 s.6A; never UK-resident; owns a £6.5m central London apartment held via a Cayman-incorporated company; uses the apartment ~30 days/year for family visits; remainder vacant). Position:
- **ATED:** £6.5m → £5m-£10m band → 2026/27 charge £75,450 annually per §18.1. NO RELIEF (no qualifying use — owner-occupied + vacant; not commercial letting; not redevelopment; not employee accommodation).
- **CIHC:** Cayman company is a non-natural person holding investment property; if let to a connected person (Sra Cordero / family), CIHC analysis applies for UK CT purposes BUT the company is non-resident → not within UK CT charge under CTA 2009 Part 4 on rental income unless it has UK rental income (here: no rental — vacant). UK CT not engaged on rental.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: What is ATED in 60 seconds?**
   A: ATED is an annual tax on UK residential property worth more than £500,000 held by a non-natural person (UK or non-UK company, partnership with corporate member, collective investment scheme). For 2026/27 the band charges per §18.1 are £4,600 (£500k-£1m), £9,450 (£1m-£2m), £32,200 (£2m-£5m), £75,450 (£5m-£10m), £151,450 (£10m-£20m), £303,450 (above £20m) — verified gov.uk 2026-05-22. Bands index by CPI for year ended September.

2. **Q: I'm thinking of buying a London BTL flat through a ltd co — what's the honest case for and against?**
   A: For: anonymity vs Land Registry public record; succession via share transfer; limited-liability ring-fencing; potential CT-vs-IT differential on rental profits after §24 finance-cost restriction (favours company for additional-rate owners with substantial mortgage interest); structural neutrality for multi-property scaling; lifetime-gifting + 7-year PET planning on share value. Against: annual ATED charge if dwelling > £500k; 15% SDLT on acquisition under FA 2003 Sch 4A unless reliefs apply; double-tax extraction (CT + dividend IT); CIHC trap if family occupy; ECCTA + RoE admin (overseas only); 5-yearly revaluation + annual return overhead; CGT-on-disposal asymmetry. The answer depends on YOUR profile — marginal IT rate, mortgage interest, family-occupation intention, portfolio horizon, succession plans, residence status.

3. **Q: My BTL ltd co lets commercially to unconnected tenants — does the s.133 property-rental-business relief eliminate my ATED exposure?**
   A: Yes, if you properly claim it on the annual return. The s.133 relief is for letting on commercial terms to an unconnected tenant with a view to profit. If you qualify throughout the chargeable period, ATED tax = £0 — BUT the return must still be filed (claim-only return). Failing to file even where relief applies triggers Schedule 55 FA 2009 penalty points + exposure to the HMRC OTM letters campaign per §18.7.

4. **Q: What is the CIHC carve-out and why does family occupation kill it?**
   A: Per §21.A + §21.5, a close investment-holding company is taxed at the 25% main CT rate regardless of profit level — no small-profits rate, no marginal relief. The s.18N(2)(b) "let commercially" carve-out from CIHC requires letting to a person NOT connected with the candidate company per s.18N(3) statutory exclusion list — which reaches: (a) connected persons; (b) their spouses or civil partners; (c) their relatives (siblings, parents, children, etc.); (d) those relatives' spouses or civil partners. Letting to your adult son, your sibling, your parent, or any of their spouses defeats the carve-out → CIHC → 25% main rate. The s.133 ATED relief tracks a similar "unconnected" test (verify at write — closely parallel but check exact statutory wording).

5. **Q: I bought a £1.6m London home through a ltd co years ago for "IHT reasons" — should I de-envelope?**
   A: Likely yes if you owner-occupy with family. The £9,450 annual ATED + ongoing Companies House + ECCTA admin + loss of PRR availability on eventual disposal (CT at 25% main rate on the company's gain vs typically £0 CGT under PRR for personal-name) typically outweigh the IHT positioning that prompted enveloping. UK property is in UK IHT regardless of structure per §22.X.4 — enveloping does NOT shield UK property from UK IHT (the historic "envelope = IHT shield" framing pre-dates Sch A1 enveloped-residential look-through effective 6 April 2017). De-enveloping triggers SDLT, CT on company's deemed disposal, and IT or CGT on the shareholder side — costly upfront; specialist representation mandatory.

6. **Q: How does the new FA 2025 IHT residence-based regime change the enveloping calculation for non-UK-resident owners?**
   A: Per §22.X — the long-term-resident (LTR) test (UK-resident 10 of the previous 20 tax years per IHTA 1984 s.6A) replaced the historic deemed-domicile framework from 6 April 2025. For an OVERSEAS-resident not-LTR owner, UK situs property remains in UK IHT regardless of LTR status (Sch A1 look-through has been operative since 6 April 2017; nothing in FA 2025 changes this). Worldwide non-UK assets are NOT in UK IHT for not-LTR individuals. For a UK-resident-departing-emigrant LTR holder, the §22.X.2 tail-period table extends IHT exposure post-departure for 3-10 years depending on prior UK-resident years. The decision-tree on enveloping for IHT purposes shifted MATERIALLY in 2025 — pre-2025 advice anchored on deemed-domicile timing is obsolete.

7. **Q: What happens to my CT bill on disposal of an enveloped UK dwelling?**
   A: ATED-CGT was abolished from 6 April 2019 per §18.5 — historic 28% rate gone. Disposals now fall under: (a) for UK companies, standard CT on chargeable gains via TCGA / CTA framework at the prevailing CT rate per §21.A (19% / 25% / 26.5% effective marginal); (b) for non-resident companies, NRCGT under TCGA 1992 s.1A — chargeable gain taxed at the CT rate; 60-day NRCGT return required. CGT asymmetry vs personal-name (18%/24% residential with £3,000 AEA for individuals) cuts both ways depending on rate position.

8. **Q: I'm an overseas owner — what is the RoE + ECCTA burden on top of ATED?**
   A: Per §18.6 + §11.A, RoE under ECTEA 2022 requires the overseas entity holding UK property to register + file annual update statements within 14 days of update period (non-compliance blocks Land Registry dispositions). ECCTA 2023 requires director + PSC identity verification — natural-person directors + PSCs verify ONCE per person (one permanent personal code covers all UK roles per ECCTA s.68). For an overseas-PSC corporate structure, the corporate-PSC officer verification phase has a later commencement (verify at write — overseas-PSC scope phases differently). Aggregate annual admin overhead for an overseas-enveloped structure is material — RoE update + ECCTA verification renewal review + Companies House confirmation statements + corporate accounting + ATED return.

9. **Q: Each of my SPVs in a multi-property portfolio gets its own £50k small profits CT band, right?**
   A: No. Per §21.A, associated companies share the lower limit (£50k) and upper limit (£250k) via the s.18D divisor — both limits divided by (1 + N) where N = number of associated companies. A 6-SPV portfolio under common control has SPR band per SPV = £50k / 6 = £8,333; marginal-relief band per SPV = £8,333 - £41,667; main rate above £41,667. Multi-SPV portfolios designed for IHT or operational segregation lose most of the SPR benefit unless the SPVs are genuinely independent (different unrelated shareholders, no common-control test met under s.18E + s.450).

10. **Q: Is the 15% SDLT on enveloped acquisitions still in force?**
    A: Yes — FA 2003 Schedule 4A imposes the 15% SDLT rate on residential property over £500,000 acquired by a non-natural person. Reliefs mirror the ATED catalogue (s.133 equivalent property-rental-business relief brings the rate back to the standard residential rates + 3%/5% surcharges if you qualify for the relief from acquisition). Where no relief applies (e.g. owner-occupier-via-company acquisition), the 15% rate is operative — making single-family-property enveloping economically very poor at the acquisition stage.

11. **Q: What's the next ATED revaluation date and what do I need to do?**
    A: Per §18.4 (verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), the next 5-yearly revaluation date is **1 April 2027**. The chargeable value for the 2027/28 chargeable period onward will be the property's open-market value at 1 April 2027 (or the acquisition value if acquired between revaluation dates). Owners holding properties near band boundaries (just under £1m, £2m, £5m, £10m, £20m) should consider engaging a RICS-qualified valuation before 1 April 2027 + use the pre-return banding check (PRBC) facility for values within 10% of a band boundary. Property values that have appreciated significantly since 1 April 2022 may push into a higher band for the 2027/28+ periods.

12. **Q: I let my London ltd-co-held flat to my adult son at market rent on a formal AST — is the ATED relief available?**
    A: No. Per the §21.A + §21.5 s.18N(3) expansive "let commercially" exclusion, letting to a connected person OR a relative of a connected person defeats the carve-out. Your adult son is a relative of you (a connected person of the company via your director/shareholder relationship) → s.18N(3) bites. Both the CIHC carve-out for CT purposes AND the s.133 ATED relief (which tracks a similar unconnected-letting test) typically fail. The £4,600+ annual ATED applies + CT on rental profits at 25% main rate (CIHC) + no SPR. Family-occupant structures are operationally the worst case for enveloped property tax outcomes.

13. **Q: If I de-envelope my single-property ltd-co, what does the actual sequence cost?**
    A: Three cost layers: (i) SDLT on the in-specie distribution — generally NIL if structured as a clean return of capital with no debt assumption by the shareholder; CHARGEABLE on any debt assumed; specialist SDLT analysis essential. (ii) CT on the company's deemed disposal at market value (chargeable gain at company's CT rate — 19% SPR if augmented profits ≤£50k; 25% main rate above £250k). (iii) Either income tax on the shareholder at dividend rates (basic 8.75% / higher 33.75% / additional 39.35%) OR — if structured via solvent dissolution (MVL — Member's Voluntary Liquidation) — capital treatment with CGT at 18%/24% residential rates with £3,000 AEA. MVL route may attract TAAR / ITTOIA 2005 s.396B phoenix anti-avoidance if you resume a substantially similar property activity within 2 years of dissolution → recharacterised as income → IT at dividend rates. Specialist tax + legal representation mandatory.
