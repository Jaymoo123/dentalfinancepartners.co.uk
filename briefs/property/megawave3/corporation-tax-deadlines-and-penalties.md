# Corporation Tax Deadlines and Penalties: The CTSA Cycle for Property LtdCos and Landlord SPVs

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — return filing obligation + filing date:** Finance Act 1998 Schedule 18 paragraph 14 (Company Tax Returns). "The filing date for a company tax return is the last day of whichever of the following periods is the last to end—(a) twelve months from the end of the period for which the return is made..." Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1998/36/schedule/18/paragraph/14`. The 12-months-from-end-of-accounting-period filing date is the operational deadline; subsequent (b)/(c)/(d) subparagraphs add narrow extensions for accounts-delivery + late-notice scenarios.
- **Primary — flat-rate late-filing penalty:** Finance Act 1998 Schedule 18 paragraph 17. Verbatim-verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1998/36/schedule/18/paragraph/17`. Subparagraph 17(2): the penalty is (a) **£200** if the return is delivered within three months after the filing date, and (b) **£400** in any other case. Subparagraph 17(3): increased to **£1000 / £2000** for a third successive failure (three consecutive accounting periods where the company is within the charge to CT, return required for each, and penalty incurred under para 17 for the first two then again for the third). **NB:** these figures differ from the long-historically-cited £100 / £200 / £500 / £1000 carried in some older guides. F-1 raised this batch to require Stage 1b back-patch sweep across the site for stale figures.
- **Primary — tax-geared late-filing penalty:** Finance Act 1998 Schedule 18 paragraph 18 (subject to Stage 2 verification at write time). Operates at 10% of unpaid CT where return is more than 18 months late, increasing to 20% where return is more than 24 months late — anchored to the unpaid tax at the relevant due date, not the filing date.
- **Primary — CT due date for small companies:** Taxes Management Act 1970 section 59D (under CTSA; cross-reference CTA 2010 s.6) — CT due **9 months and 1 day after end of accounting period** for companies not within the quarterly-instalment regime (CTA 2010 s.6(2) sets the architecture; the operational date is fixed by SI). Sessions cite this as "9 months 1 day after end of accounting period" with anchor to the underlying statute at write time.
- **Primary — CT due date for large companies (quarterly instalments):** Corporation Tax (Instalment Payments) Regulations 1998 (SI 1998/3175 as amended). Applies where augmented profits exceed £1.5 million in the AP (divided by associated companies under §21.A divisor — CTA 2010 s.18E). Four instalments: months 7, 10, 13, 16 from start of AP (each one-quarter of the estimated full-year liability). Very-large-company regime (augmented profits > £20m divided by associated companies): instalments accelerated to months 3, 6, 9, 12 from start of AP (SI 1998/3175 reg 3 as amended by FA 2017).
- **Primary — late-payment penalty:** Finance Act 2009 Schedule 56 (item 5 in the Table: Corporation Tax). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2009/10/schedule/56`. Standard 5% / 5% / 5% escalator at penalty-date triggers (paragraph 4 of Sch 56 read with item 5). Sch 56 also imposes interest under the FA 2009 s.101 framework; interest is not penalty but accrues continuously.
- **Primary — inaccuracy in the CT600:** Schedule 24 FA 2007 (cross-statute, same as for SA). Behaviour categories + mitigation matrix under §27.2 lock.
- **House position reference:** §21.A (FA 2021 CT three-figure framework + associated-companies divisor — primary anchor for instalment-threshold testing); §21.1 (DLA mechanics — relevant where a director loan is repaid late and s.455 / s.458 charges interact with the AP filing); §27 (penalty + enquiry framework — for the Sch 24 inaccuracy + s.29 discovery overlay); §27.2 (Sch 24 mechanics); §35 (payroll mechanics — for the LtdCo employer-PAYE crossover into A1 paye-penalties seed). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED but **F-1 recommends opening a §27.10 micro-lock** to pin verified Sch 18 para 17 figures.

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — Single SPV with year-end alignment, normal-cycle CT timeline

- **Marwood Properties Ltd** is a single BTL SPV (no associated companies) with year-end 31 March. AP for the year ending 31 March 2026 → CT600 filing date 31 March 2027 (12 months from end of AP per Sch 18 para 14). CT payment date 1 January 2027 (9 months 1 day after end of AP per s.59D — for small companies not within instalment regime).
- **Augmented profits assumption:** £80,000 (well below £1.5m instalment threshold). Single-company position so divisor is 1. SPR 19% applies on first £50k; marginal relief on £30k. CT liability ~£15,200 (rough; RUN re-computes exactly).
- **Filing-and-payment discipline:** payment due 1 January 2027; CT600 due 31 March 2027. Both must hit in sequence. Missing the 1 January 2027 payment triggers Sch 56 item 5 escalator from 30 days late (5% at day 31); missing the 31 March 2027 filing date triggers Sch 18 para 17 flat-rate (£200 if delivered within 3 months / £400 thereafter / £1,000 / £2,000 third-successive).
- **NB:** F-1 confirmed corrected verbatim figures (£200 / £400 / £1,000 / £2,000) verified live 2026-05-27. The legacy £100 / £200 / £500 / £1,000 figures carried in some older guides are stale.

### Example 2 — Multi-SPV portfolio operator pushed into quarterly instalments by associated-companies divisor

- **Heatherden Group** owns 5 SPVs (Heatherden Property 1-5 Ltd) under a common parent. Combined augmented profits across the group £6m / year (£1.2m per SPV).
- **Without associated-companies divisor:** each SPV's £1.2m would be just below the £1.5m instalment-payment threshold and outside quarterly instalments.
- **With associated-companies divisor (5 companies → divisor = 5):** the £1.5m threshold becomes £300k per SPV. EACH SPV now exceeds the threshold and must pay CT in quarterly instalments under SI 1998/3175.
- **Operational impact:** each SPV pays CT in four instalments at months 7, 10, 13, 16 from start of AP (each one-quarter of the estimated full-year liability). For a 31 March year-end SPV, instalments are 14 October Y1 / 14 January Y2 / 14 April Y2 / 14 July Y2.
- **Strategic note:** the associated-companies divisor catches portfolio operators by surprise more often than the headline thresholds suggest. The page must lead with this trap; multi-SPV operators need to model their instalment position from incorporation, not just at filing time.

### Example 3 — Late filing 3 months / 6 months / 18 months — penalty progression

- **Stoneleigh Investments Ltd** (single SPV, no associates) has CT600 due 31 March 2027 for AP ending 31 March 2026. Augmented profits £150,000; CT liability £37,500 (at 25% main rate, then less marginal relief £1,500 = £36,000).
- **Scenario A — filed 30 June 2027 (3 months late):** Sch 18 para 17 subpara 2(a) flat-rate £200 (within 3 months of filing date). Sch 18 para 18 tax-geared not yet triggered (18-month threshold). Total penalty: **£200**.
- **Scenario B — filed 30 September 2027 (6 months late):** Sch 18 para 17 subpara 2(b) flat-rate £400. Sch 18 para 18 tax-geared still not triggered (within 18 months). Total penalty: **£400**.
- **Scenario C — filed 30 September 2028 (18 months late):** Sch 18 para 17 flat-rate £400. Sch 18 para 18 tax-geared 10% × unpaid CT at filing date = 10% × £36,000 = £3,600. Total: **£4,000**.
- **Scenario D — filed 30 March 2029 (24 months late):** Sch 18 para 17 flat-rate £400. Sch 18 para 18 tax-geared rises to 20% × £36,000 = £7,200. Total: **£7,600**.
- **Plus Sch 56 late-payment penalties** stacking independently from the late-payment due date 1 January 2027: 5% at 31 January 2027, further 5% at 30 June 2027, further 5% at 31 December 2027. Total late-payment penalty: 15% × £36,000 = £5,400 by the time the return is filed under Scenario D. Plus interest under FA 2009 s.101 throughout.

### Example 4 — CIHC trap denying SPR and forcing 25% main rate

- **Singh Lettings Ltd** owns 3 BTL flats. Augmented profits £40,000 — comfortably within the SPR band (assuming single-company SPV). The flats are let: 2 to unconnected tenants at market rent (£10,800 each year); 1 to the founder's adult son at £3,600/year (market rent £9,600 by comparable evidence).
- **CIHC analysis under CTA 2010 s.18N:** the connected-tenant let at sub-market rent risks failing the s.18N(2)(b) qualifying-purpose carve-out + s.18N(3) connected-tenant exclusion. HMRC's expansive reading treats the company as a Close Investment-Holding Company → 25% main rate denies SPR.
- **CT cost of CIHC classification:** £40,000 × 25% = £10,000 vs SPR position £40,000 × 19% = £7,600. **CIHC trap costs £2,400 per year.**
- **Remediation:** charge the son market rent £9,600 (or formalised arm's-length terms). The cost of doing so is real-world (son's affordability) but the CT saving (£2,400/year) typically outweighs the increase in rent income to be brought to charge.
- **Cross-reference to §21.A drift catch:** the s.18N(3) connected-tenant exclusion is interpreted expansively by HMRC. Any below-market connected let risks pulling the whole company into CIHC, not just the connected-let portion.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target)

1. **Q: When is my CT600 due to be filed?**
   A: 12 months after the end of the accounting period under FA 1998 Schedule 18 paragraph 14. For a year-end 31 March 2026, the CT600 is due 31 March 2027. Narrow extensions apply where the company's accounts are delivered later than the filing date (para 14(b)) or where the notice to deliver was given late (para 14(c) and (d)), but these are exceptional cases.

2. **Q: When is Corporation Tax due to be paid for a small company?**
   A: 9 months and 1 day after the end of the accounting period for companies not within the quarterly-instalment regime, under TMA 1970 s.59D read with CTA 2010 s.6. For a year-end 31 March 2026, CT is due 1 January 2027. The payment date is independent of the filing date and falls 3 months before the filing date in the standard cycle.

3. **Q: When do quarterly instalment payments apply?**
   A: Where augmented profits exceed £1.5 million in the AP under the Corporation Tax (Instalment Payments) Regulations 1998 (SI 1998/3175), the company pays in four instalments at months 7, 10, 13, 16 from the start of the AP. Very-large companies (augmented profits over £20 million) pay in four accelerated instalments at months 3, 6, 9, 12 from the start of the AP under SI 1998/3175 as amended by FA 2017. The £1.5m and £20m thresholds are DIVIDED by 1 plus the number of associated companies under CTA 2010 s.18E.

4. **Q: How does the associated-companies divisor affect my instalment-threshold testing?**
   A: A multi-SPV property operator with 4 associated companies has the £1.5m and £20m thresholds divided by 5 (1 + 4 associates). Each SPV's effective threshold becomes £300k / £4m. A multi-SPV operator with combined £6m profit (£1.2m per SPV) is below the headline £1.5m but ABOVE the £300k per-SPV effective threshold and must pay each SPV in quarterly instalments. The associated-companies divisor is the most-missed mechanic for portfolio operators.

5. **Q: What is the flat-rate late-filing penalty under Schedule 18 paragraph 17?**
   A: Subparagraph 17(2): £200 if the CT600 is delivered within 3 months after the filing date; £400 in any other case. Subparagraph 17(3): increased to £1,000 / £2,000 for a third successive failure where the company has been in charge to CT in three consecutive accounting periods, has been required to deliver a return for each, and has incurred a paragraph 17 penalty for the first two. These figures were verified live 2026-05-27 and differ from the long-historically-cited £100 / £200 / £500 / £1,000 figures still carried in some older guides.

6. **Q: What is the tax-geared late-filing penalty under Schedule 18 paragraph 18?**
   A: 10% of unpaid CT where the return is more than 18 months late; 20% where the return is more than 24 months late. The tax-geared penalty STACKS on top of the flat-rate penalty under paragraph 17, and operates on the unpaid CT at the relevant due date, not the filing date.

7. **Q: What is the Schedule 56 FA 2009 late-payment escalator for Corporation Tax?**
   A: 5% of unpaid tax at the penalty date (typically 30 days after the due date), further 5% at 6 months after the due date, further 5% at 12 months after the due date. Interest under FA 2009 s.101 accrues continuously alongside; interest is not penalty but stacks economically. For a CT liability of £36,000 left unpaid for 12 months past due date, the cumulative late-payment penalty is 15% × £36,000 = £5,400, plus interest.

8. **Q: How does the inaccuracy-in-return penalty under Schedule 24 FA 2007 interact with the CT late-filing regime?**
   A: Sch 24 FA 2007 penalises an INACCURACY within the CT600 that has been filed. Sch 18 para 17 + 18 penalises a FAILURE TO FILE the CT600 on time. They can apply concurrently: a late-filed CT600 containing inaccuracies attracts both. The Sch 24 inaccuracy-in-return penalty uses the standard behaviour matrix (careless 30% / deliberate 70% / deliberate-and-concealed 100% maxima) with offshore Category uplift under Sch 24 para 4A.

9. **Q: How long must I keep my company accounting records for CT purposes?**
   A: Companies Act 2006 section 388 requires accounting records to be kept for 6 years from the end of the accounting period for private companies (3 years for public companies). The 6-year retention floor under CA 2006 is distinct from the 5-year-from-31-January floor for income tax under TMA 1970 s.12B. For HMRC discovery purposes the relevant time-limits under FA 1998 Sch 18 paras 41-43 substitute for TMA 1970 s.29 but the substantive limits (4 / 6 / 20 / 12 years) read across.

10. **Q: What happens to my CT cycle if I incorporate a new SPV mid-tax-year?**
    A: A newly-incorporated SPV has a short first AP from incorporation date to its first ARD (typically end of the calendar month 12 months after formation). The CT600 filing date and the CT payment date shift accordingly — both run from the END of that short AP. Additionally, the new SPV becomes an associated company of any existing group companies from incorporation, which can affect instalment-threshold testing in the AP of incorporation.

11. **Q: How does the CIHC classification under CTA 2010 s.18N affect my CT cost?**
    A: A close investment-holding company is denied access to the small profits rate and marginal relief and pays 25% main rate on ALL profit. The s.18N(2)(b) "let commercially" qualifying-purpose carve-out excludes companies whose business is wholly or mainly letting land to unconnected persons on commercial terms. The s.18N(3) connected-tenant exclusion is interpreted expansively by HMRC — any below-market connected let risks pulling the whole company into CIHC. Cost of CIHC: typically (25% − 19%) × profits = 6 percentage points more CT, more at higher profit levels where marginal relief would otherwise give an even better effective rate.

12. **Q: What property-LtdCo-specific scenarios should I watch for at the CT cycle?**
    A: Three traps. (a) AP-alignment: new SPV incorporated mid-year creates a short first AP and ARD that may misalign with personal tax year-end, shifting CT filing / payment dates. (b) Associated-companies-divisor: each additional SPV shrinks the SPR / marginal-relief band AND the instalment-threshold band; multi-SPV operators can be pushed into quarterly instalments unexpectedly. (c) CIHC overlay: a single below-market connected-party let can flip the whole company into CIHC and lose SPR access entirely.
