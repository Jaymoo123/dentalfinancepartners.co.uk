# ATED return amendment guide: when to amend, how the Sch 33 window actually works, and the tax-tips practitioners use to minimise penalty exposure

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (the amendment power and the operative deadline):**
  - **FA 2013 Schedule 33 paragraph 3** "Amendment of return by chargeable person" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Sub-paragraph (1): "A person who has delivered a return may amend the return by notice to an officer of Revenue and Customs." Sub-paragraph (3): the amendment deadline is "the end of the next chargeable period after the chargeable period to which the return relates." Sub-paragraph (4) extension: where the return is delivered on or after 1 January in that following chargeable period, the amendment window extends to "the end of the period of 3 months after the day on which the return is delivered."
  - The Sch 33 para 3(3) operative period is NOT a flat 12-months-from-filing window analogous to TMA 1970 s.9ZA self-assessment amendment. It is anchored to the chargeable-period boundary (1 April to 31 March), with a sub-(4) safety extension for late filers. Practitioners reading across from SA practice routinely get this wrong; the F-numbered correction lock at HP §18.4 must reflect the period-boundary anchor rather than the SA-analogue myth.
- **Primary (HMRC's parallel correction power):**
  - **FA 2013 Schedule 33 paragraph 4** "Correction of return by HMRC" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). Sub-paragraph (1): "An officer of Revenue and Customs may correct any obvious error or omission in a return." Sub-paragraph (4): corrections must be made within "the 9 months beginning with" either the delivery date or amendment date. Sub-paragraphs (5)-(7): the chargeable person may reject the correction within a "special period" — after the amendment deadline but within 3 months of the correction notice — by giving notice. This is the bilateral architecture: chargeable-person amendment under para 3 + HMRC obvious-error correction under para 4, with the rejection-of-correction mechanic preserving the chargeable person's autonomy.
- **Primary (the declaration that gives an inaccurate amendment teeth):**
  - **FA 2013 Schedule 33 paragraph 1(3)** "Every return must include a declaration by the person making it to the effect that the return is correct and complete to the best of the person's knowledge" (verified verbatim at legislation.gov.uk 2026-05-27). An amendment carries the same declaration discipline; a careless or deliberately-understated amendment falls into the Sch 24 inaccuracy regime exactly as the original return.
- **Primary (penalty interaction with amendment):**
  - **FA 2007 Schedule 24** inaccuracy penalty regime — applies to original returns AND to amendments. Behaviour categories: careless (15-30% prompted, 0-30% unprompted), deliberate (35-70% prompted, 20-70% unprompted), deliberate-and-concealed (50-100% prompted, 30-100% unprompted). The amendment itself counts as a disclosure if it pre-empts HMRC's enquiry or letter, and unprompted treatment is available where the chargeable person had no reason to believe HMRC was about to discover the inaccuracy. Sessions writing tax-tips copy must walk the prompted/unprompted distinction in the amendment context.
  - **FA 2015 Schedule 21** offshore-asset penalty uplift (Category 2 / 3) applies where the inaccuracy concerns an offshore matter or offshore transfer. Overseas-company ATED filers correcting historical inaccuracies face the Cat-2 / Cat-3 escalator on top of Sch 24 percentages.
- **Primary (the beyond-window route):**
  - Once the Sch 33 para 3(3)/(4) amendment window has closed, the route is **HMRC correction request under enquiry / discovery** (FA 2013 Sch 33 para 8 enquiries; Sch 33 para 21 discovery assessments) or **voluntary disclosure** outside the formal return-amendment machinery. The HMRC One-to-Many (OTM) letters campaign (HP §18.7) is the practical inbound trigger for many post-window corrections; sessions must address the OTM-letter response architecture as the operational beyond-window path.
- **Primary (the relief-claim correction pattern that the amendment route specifically enables):**
  - Reliefs under FA 2013 ss.133-150 (HP §18.3) must be **claimed on the return**. Where a relief was overlooked at original-filing time but the qualifying conditions were met throughout the chargeable period, amendment under Sch 33 para 3 is the route to restore the relief and recover overpaid tax. This is the most common practitioner amendment scenario and the highest-tax-value tip in the playbook.
- **House position reference:** §2 (ATED bands 2026/27 — verified gov.uk 2026-05-22), **§18 (Wave 3 ATED extension full)**, §18.3 (relief catalogue — full table of s.133 / s.137 / s.138 / s.141 / s.144 / s.145 / s.149 / s.150 / ss.134-135), §18.4 (return mechanics + 30 April deadline + £100/£200/£300 late-filing penalties + five-yearly revaluation), §18.7 (HMRC OTM letters campaign), §18.8 (do-not-write list).

## Framing differentiator (anti-templating, anti-cannibalisation)

This page is the **decision-and-planning page** on ATED return amendment, NOT the procedure mechanic page. The existing site page `ated-return-amendment-corrections-procedure` walks the procedural how — log into the ATED online service, navigate to the historical return, click amend, re-submit. This page walks the **decision-level questions**:

1. **When SHOULD a chargeable person amend** versus accept the original return, request HMRC correction, or move to voluntary disclosure. Different errors take different routes; the choice is not procedural but strategic.
2. **How the Sch 33 para 3 window actually works.** Many practitioner-facing competitor pages assert a "12 months from filing" amendment window borrowed from TMA 1970 s.9ZA SA logic. That is wrong for ATED. The operative deadline is **end of the next chargeable period** after the period to which the return relates (Sch 33 para 3(3)) with a **3-months-from-delivery extension** where the return is filed on or after 1 January in that following chargeable period (Sch 33 para 3(4)). Sessions must walk the period-boundary anchor explicitly; the SA-analogue mental shorthand misleads.
3. **The penalty-mitigation tax-tips angle.** Amendment can be a disclosure event under Sch 24 — pre-empting HMRC unlocks unprompted-disclosure treatment, lower behaviour-floor percentages, and a stronger reasonable-excuse posture if penalty correspondence follows. Sessions walk the timing levers: amend BEFORE the OTM letter arrives; amend BEFORE the discovery-assessment time-limit clock starts running on HMRC's side; amend with a contemporaneous explanation letter that locks in the unprompted posture.
4. **The relief-restoration angle.** The amendment route is uniquely effective for restoring missed FA 2013 s.133 / s.137 / s.138 / s.141 / s.144 / s.145 / s.149 / s.150 reliefs where the qualifying conditions were met throughout the period but the relief was not claimed on the original return. Tax-tips copy must walk the strongest-tax-value scenarios: forgotten s.133 rental relief on a let dwelling, missed s.145 employee accommodation relief, missed s.138 / s.141 trading-stock relief during a redevelopment year.
5. **The beyond-window architecture.** Where Sch 33 para 3 has expired, the routes are: (a) await HMRC enquiry under Sch 33 para 8 and run the corrections through that machinery; (b) respond to an OTM letter under HP §18.7 with voluntary-disclosure framing; (c) seek HMRC's obvious-error correction under Sch 33 para 4 (taxpayer-initiated request via correspondence — HMRC's 9-month window from delivery). The Sch 33 para 4 route is under-used by competitor pages and is a tax-tips lever for genuine HMRC-side mistakes.
6. **The amendment-versus-new-return distinction.** A common practitioner-facing confusion: where the original return was a claim-only relief return and the dwelling shifted to non-relief mid-period, the correct route is amendment under Sch 33 para 3 (one return per chargeable person per chargeable period — there is no second-return route for the same period). Sessions must walk the one-return-per-period architecture explicitly.

## Key questions this page must answer
1. **When does the Sch 33 para 3 amendment window close?** (End of the next chargeable period after the period to which the return relates — i.e. for a 2025/26 return relating to 1 April 2025 - 31 March 2026, the amendment deadline is 31 March 2027. Sub-(4) extension: if the return was filed on/after 1 January 2027, the window extends to 3 months from filing — so a return delivered on 15 February 2027 gets until 15 May 2027 to amend.)
2. **How is this different from the self-assessment amendment window?** (TMA 1970 s.9ZA gives SA filers 12 months from the 31 January filing deadline. ATED runs on a different schedule: 30 April return deadline, end-of-next-chargeable-period amendment window. The two regimes look superficially similar but the operative anchors differ.)
3. **Can HMRC amend an ATED return?** (Yes, under Sch 33 para 4 — "obvious error or omission" correction within 9 months of delivery. The chargeable person can reject the correction within 3 months of the correction notice — Sch 33 para 4(5)-(7). This is bilateral architecture: chargeable-person amendment + HMRC obvious-error correction.)
4. **What if the amendment window has closed?** (Routes: (a) HMRC enquiry under Sch 33 para 8 — opens enquiry window for HMRC; (b) discovery assessment under Sch 33 para 21 — HMRC's beyond-window assessment power, subject to time-limits at FA 2008 Sch 39 / FA 2009 Sch 56 (4 / 6 / 12 / 20 years depending on behaviour); (c) voluntary disclosure outside the return-amendment machinery, typically in response to an HMRC OTM letter — HP §18.7.)
5. **How is the penalty calculated on an amendment that increases tax due?** (FA 2007 Sch 24 inaccuracy penalty — behaviour-based: careless 15-30% prompted / 0-30% unprompted; deliberate 35-70% / 20-70%; deliberate-and-concealed 50-100% / 30-100%. The amendment carries the same Sch 33 para 1(3) "correct and complete" declaration — careless or deliberately-understated amendment is itself an inaccuracy. Unprompted treatment available where HMRC has not signalled awareness — the amendment-timing tax-tip is to amend BEFORE the OTM letter arrives.)
6. **Does the Cat-2 / Cat-3 offshore uplift apply to ATED amendments?** (Yes for overseas-company filers where the inaccuracy concerns an offshore matter or offshore transfer under FA 2015 Sch 21. The uplift sits on top of Sch 24 percentages — Cat 2 = 1.5x; Cat 3 = 2x.)
7. **What is the tax-tips angle for restoring a missed s.133 rental relief?** (Amendment under Sch 33 para 3 within window. Evidence required: tenancy agreement throughout the chargeable period; rent at commercial terms; tenant outside the s.136 non-qualifying-individual catalogue (HP §18.3, B11, B12); commercial-basis-and-with-a-view-to-profit evidence per the s.133 two-conditions architecture. The amendment refiles the period's return with the relief claimed; tax overpaid is repaid by HMRC with statutory interest under FA 2009 s.102.)
8. **What is the tax-tips angle for re-banding via amendment?** (Where a PRBC (Pre-Return Banding Check) post-filing reveals the band was wrong, amendment under Sch 33 para 3 corrects the band. The PRBC is non-binding pre-return guidance from HMRC; a contemporaneous RICS Red Book valuation supports the amendment. Banding-boundary cases — flats within 10% of a band edge — are the strongest re-banding targets.)
9. **What documentation discipline supports a defensible amendment?** (Contemporaneous explanation letter accompanying the amendment; underlying evidence of the corrected position (rental tenancy agreements; valuation reports; relief-eligibility documentation); a "best of knowledge" declaration at Sch 33 para 1(3); preservation of the original return alongside the amended version for audit-trail purposes.)
10. **When does HMRC's discovery assessment power displace the amendment route?** (HMRC's Sch 33 para 21 discovery sits outside the chargeable-person amendment window. If HMRC discovers an inaccuracy after Sch 33 para 3 has expired, they can assess under para 21 within the FA 2008 Sch 39 time limits: 4 years (no fault); 6 years (careless); 20 years (deliberate). The discovery route is HMRC-driven; the chargeable person's amendment route is taxpayer-driven and time-bound.)
11. **What is the strategic choice between amendment and voluntary disclosure?** (Where the amendment window is open AND no HMRC OTM letter has arrived AND the matter is genuinely correctable on the face of the return: amendment is the cleaner route. Where the OTM letter has arrived, voluntary-disclosure framing under the OTM response track may be preferable because it sits within HMRC's published mitigation framework. Where the inaccuracy is deliberate and historical, the Contractual Disclosure Facility / Code of Practice 9 route may displace both.)
12. **The penalty-suspension angle on amendment-driven carelessness penalties.** (FA 2007 Sch 24 para 14 — HMRC's discretion to suspend a careless-behaviour penalty subject to SMART conditions for up to 24 months. Amendment-driven carelessness with a contemporaneous remediation plan (improved record-keeping; agent oversight; specialist review) is a strong suspension case. Sessions tax-tips copy must walk the suspension architecture.)

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The Sch 33 para 3 amendment-window decision tree (the page's first concrete artefact)

Render as semantic HTML `<ol>`. This is the period-boundary architecture that resolves the 12-months-from-filing myth:

1. **Identify the chargeable period to which the return relates.** ATED chargeable periods run 1 April to 31 March. A "2025/26 return" relates to the 1 April 2025 - 31 March 2026 period.
2. **Apply Sch 33 para 3(3) primary deadline.** The amendment deadline is the END of the NEXT chargeable period after the period to which the return relates. For a 2025/26 return, the amendment deadline is **31 March 2027**.
3. **Check Sch 33 para 3(4) extension trigger.** Was the return delivered on or AFTER 1 January in the following chargeable period (i.e. on or after 1 January 2027 for a 2025/26 return)? If YES, the amendment window EXTENDS to 3 months from the delivery date.
4. **Apply the extension if triggered.** A 2025/26 return delivered on 15 February 2027 gets until **15 May 2027** to amend (3 months from delivery). A 2025/26 return delivered on 30 April 2025 (normal timing) gets only the primary deadline — **31 March 2027**.
5. **If amendment window is OPEN:** proceed via Sch 33 para 3(1) "notice to an officer of Revenue and Customs". The amendment refiles the period's return with the corrected position.
6. **If amendment window is CLOSED:** proceed to one of three beyond-window routes — (a) request HMRC obvious-error correction under Sch 33 para 4 (if within HMRC's 9-month window from delivery); (b) voluntary disclosure under the OTM letters track (HP §18.7); (c) await HMRC enquiry under Sch 33 para 8 or discovery assessment under Sch 33 para 21. Cross-link Example 4 for the beyond-window architecture.

### Example 2 — The relief-restoration amendment (highest-tax-value scenario)

Topaz Holdings Limited (UK ltd-co; sole director Mr Topaz; owns a £1.6m central London flat acquired 2019 via ltd-co; let throughout 2025/26 to Susan Hartnell, an unconnected professional contact, on a standard 12-month AST at market rent of £52k/year). Mr Topaz's accountant filed the 2025/26 ATED return on 30 April 2025 declaring full charge of **£9,450** (band 2 £1m-£2m) — but failed to claim FA 2013 s.133 property rental business relief because the accountant treated the let-to-an-acquaintance pattern as "potentially connected" rather than running the s.136 catalogue analysis.

**Amendment-window analysis:**

- Return relates to 2025/26 chargeable period. Sch 33 para 3(3) primary deadline: 31 March 2027.
- Return delivered 30 April 2025 (well before 1 January 2027 trigger) → Sch 33 para 3(4) extension does NOT apply.
- Operative amendment deadline: **31 March 2027**.

**Relief-eligibility analysis:**

- s.133 condition (a) commercial basis + view to profit: PASSED (market rent £52k/year, comparable evidence, standard AST, profit-oriented).
- s.133 condition (b) no non-qualifying individual occupation per s.136: Susan Hartnell is unconnected (not in s.1122 connected-persons list; not a relative under s.994; not a settlor; not a partnership member; not a CIS participant). PASSED.
- Both conditions hold → s.133 relief AVAILABLE.

**Amendment mechanic:**

- Mr Topaz's accountant lodges amendment with HMRC ATED team via the online service on (say) 15 October 2026 — well within Sch 33 para 3(3) window.
- Amended return claims s.133 relief; ATED charge reduces from £9,450 to £0 (claim-only return).
- £9,450 already paid is repayable; HMRC repays under FA 2009 s.102 with statutory interest from the original payment date.
- Amendment carries the Sch 33 para 1(3) "correct and complete to the best of knowledge" declaration. Contemporaneous explanation letter attached (tenancy agreement; market-rent comparables; s.136 catalogue walk; no penalty exposure because amendment reduces tax, not increases it).

**Result:** £9,450 ATED recovered + statutory interest (c. £350 at 4% × 18 months). No penalty exposure (no inaccuracy in the sense of under-declared tax — the original return overpaid). This is the highest-tax-value amendment scenario; the prevention discipline is to walk the s.136 catalogue at original filing, but the amendment route catches the missed case.

### Example 3 — The PRBC re-banding amendment

Cordoba Holdings Cayman Limited (Cayman-incorporated; ATED filer for a £2.05m Knightsbridge flat acquired 2022). Original 2024/25 ATED return (filed 30 April 2024) declared band 3 (£2m-£5m) at £31,050 — the agent erred on the cautious side because the dwelling was within 10% of the £2m boundary and no PRBC had been obtained at original-filing time.

**Post-filing PRBC submission:**

In November 2025, Cordoba's tax adviser commissions a contemporaneous RICS Red Book valuation as at 1 April 2022 (the operative valuation date per FA 2013 s.102). RICS valuation: **£1.95m** (within 10% of the £2m boundary but UNDER it). Adviser submits a Pre-Return Banding Check to HMRC ATED team in February 2026 attaching the RICS valuation; HMRC's non-binding response confirms band 2 (£1m-£2m).

**Amendment-window analysis (for the 2024/25 return):**

- Return relates to 2024/25 chargeable period. Sch 33 para 3(3) primary deadline: **31 March 2026**.
- Return delivered 30 April 2024 → Sch 33 para 3(4) extension does NOT apply.
- Operative amendment deadline: **31 March 2026**.

**Amendment mechanic:**

- Adviser lodges amendment on 15 March 2026 (within Sch 33 para 3(3) window).
- Amended return reclassifies to band 2 (£1m-£2m); ATED charge reduces from £31,050 to £9,150 (2024/25 band 2).
- Refund of £21,900 + statutory interest under FA 2009 s.102 (c. £620 at 4% × 22 months).
- PRBC + RICS valuation appended to amendment as evidence; contemporaneous explanation letter cites Sch 33 para 1(3) declaration discipline.

**Result:** £21,900 recovered + interest. The amendment-window timing matters: had the adviser delayed until April 2026, the Sch 33 para 3(3) window would have closed and the route would have been the OTM-letters / voluntary-disclosure track at HP §18.7, with significant additional friction.

### Example 4 — Beyond-window correction architecture (when Sch 33 para 3 has closed)

Acer Property Partners LLP (UK LLP with a corporate member; ATED filer for a £3.2m Hampstead Garden Suburb property held since 2018). 2022/23 ATED return filed 30 April 2022 declared full charge £31,050 (band 3 £2m-£5m), no relief claimed. Acer's structure-restructure project in early 2026 surfaces that the property had been let to an unconnected commercial tenant throughout 2022/23 — relief was missed.

**Amendment-window analysis (for the 2022/23 return):**

- Return relates to 2022/23 chargeable period. Sch 33 para 3(3) primary deadline: **31 March 2024**.
- Return delivered 30 April 2022 → Sch 33 para 3(4) extension does NOT apply.
- Operative amendment deadline: **31 March 2024** — **WINDOW CLOSED** (current date 2026-05-27).

**Beyond-window route analysis:**

- **Route 1 — Sch 33 para 4 HMRC obvious-error correction:** HMRC's correction window is 9 months from delivery (or amendment date). Delivery was 30 April 2022 → HMRC's para 4 window expired 30 January 2023. **Route 1 unavailable.**
- **Route 2 — Sch 33 para 8 enquiry:** HMRC's enquiry window expired (typically 12 months from delivery for an in-time return = 30 April 2023). **Route 2 unavailable unless HMRC opens a fresh enquiry, which is unlikely without trigger.**
- **Route 3 — Sch 33 para 21 discovery:** HMRC has discovery power within FA 2008 Sch 39 time limits (4 years no-fault; 6 years careless; 20 years deliberate). For the 2022/23 period: 4-year window runs to 5 April 2027 (the standard 4-year discovery for income-tax assessments cross-applies through Sch 33 para 21). **Route 3 available to HMRC but not to the chargeable person.**
- **Route 4 — OTM letters / voluntary disclosure (HP §18.7):** Acer's adviser can write to HMRC's ATED team requesting voluntary disclosure of overpaid tax. HMRC's published practice accepts late-claim refund requests under TMA 1970 s.43 (4 years from the chargeable period end) for overpaid tax — Acer's 2022/23 overpayment can be claimed back via this route until 5 April 2027. The route is not formal amendment but achieves the same economic result.

**Operational mechanic for Route 4:**

- Adviser drafts a TMA 1970 s.43 overpaid tax claim (or equivalent ATED-specific repayment claim) to HMRC's ATED team, attaching: (a) original 2022/23 return; (b) evidence of qualifying s.133 rental relief throughout the period (tenancy agreement + market rent + commercial-basis evidence + s.136 catalogue walk); (c) explanation letter framing the original error as honest mistake; (d) Sch 33 para 1(3)-equivalent declaration.
- HMRC reviews and (where satisfied) repays under FA 2009 s.102 with statutory interest from the original payment date.
- No penalty exposure (the claim is for overpaid tax, not under-declared tax).

**Result:** £31,050 potentially recoverable via TMA 1970 s.43 route + interest (c. £4,000 at 4% × 4 years). The Sch 33 para 3 window-closed scenario is recoverable but requires the secondary architecture; the lesson is to action amendments BEFORE the period-boundary deadline.

### Example 5 — Penalty-mitigation amendment (under-declared tax scenario)

Sapphire Estates Cayman Limited (Cayman-incorporated; ATED filer for a £6.8m Belgravia town house held since 2020). 2024/25 ATED return filed 30 April 2024 declared band 4 (£5m-£10m) at £72,700, claiming s.134 transitional relief for the full period on the basis of "demolition intention". HMRC's One-to-Many letter arrives in March 2026 asking for evidence of the demolition programme. The directors realise the demolition-intention claim was overstated — there were preliminary discussions but no planning application, no contractor engagement, no programme dates. The honest position: no s.134 relief should have been claimed; full £72,700 ATED was due.

**Amendment-window analysis (for the 2024/25 return):**

- Return relates to 2024/25 chargeable period. Sch 33 para 3(3) primary deadline: **31 March 2026**.
- Return delivered 30 April 2024 → Sch 33 para 3(4) extension does NOT apply.
- Operative amendment deadline: **31 March 2026** — **WINDOW JUST CLOSED at OTM letter receipt March 2026**.

**Tax-tips angle — the amendment-vs-OTM-response decision:**

- Even with the Sch 33 para 3 window closed, voluntary correction via the OTM-letters response track is the right move BEFORE HMRC issues a formal assessment.
- Sch 24 penalty exposure: the original claim was "overstated" — careless behaviour (insufficient evidence at filing time). Prompted disclosure (HMRC's OTM letter is a prompt) → careless prompted floor 15%-30%. Cat-2 / Cat-3 offshore uplift potentially applies (Cayman incorporation; offshore-matter inquiry needed per FA 2015 Sch 21 territory schedule). Assume Cat-2 = 1.5x.
- Voluntary response with full disclosure within HMRC's response window unlocks lower-end of prompted band (15% floor). Without disclosure → HMRC assessment likely lands at upper end (30% prompted ceiling).
- Cat-2 uplift on 15% prompted floor: 15% × 1.5 = **22.5% penalty**. On £72,700: penalty £16,358.
- Without disclosure (HMRC assessment): 30% × 1.5 = **45%**. On £72,700: penalty £32,715.
- **Voluntary correction saves c. £16,357 in penalty** (plus reduces investigation duration + protects against deliberate-behaviour escalation to 35%-70%).

**Operational mechanic:**

- Sapphire's adviser drafts an OTM-response letter acknowledging the overclaim, providing the true position, paying the £72,700 ATED + interest, and proposing the 22.5% Cat-2 prompted-floor penalty subject to HMRC review.
- Contemporaneous internal memo prepared documenting the cause of the original error (advisor turnover; no second-pair-of-eyes review; reliance on director's verbal indication that demolition would proceed) — supports the careless-not-deliberate behaviour grading.
- Optionally, request penalty suspension under Sch 24 para 14 if HMRC grades as careless: SMART conditions for the next 24 months (specialist review of all ATED returns; demonstrable record-keeping discipline; agent oversight).

**Result:** £72,700 ATED + interest + c. £16,358 penalty (subject to HMRC review). Total c. £92,000-£95,000. Alternative (no disclosure, HMRC assessment) likely c. £105,000-£115,000. Voluntary correction saves £10-20k AND avoids escalation to deliberate behaviour grading which would trigger 35%-70% percentages.

### Example 6 — Misframings the page must correct (verbatim do-not-write list)

- **Misframe 1:** "The ATED amendment window is 12 months from filing, like self-assessment." FALSE per FA 2013 Sch 33 para 3(3) — the operative deadline is end of the next chargeable period after the period to which the return relates (a period-boundary anchor, not a filing-date anchor). The 12-months-from-filing analogy is borrowed from TMA 1970 s.9ZA SA practice and is wrong for ATED.
- **Misframe 2:** "If you miss the amendment window, you've lost the relief forever." FALSE — beyond-window routes exist: TMA 1970 s.43 overpaid-tax claims (4 years from period end); voluntary disclosure via OTM-letters track; HMRC obvious-error correction under Sch 33 para 4 if within HMRC's 9-month window. The window matters for the cleanest route but doesn't kill the underlying refund right.
- **Misframe 3:** "HMRC can only amend a return through enquiry." FALSE — Sch 33 para 4 gives HMRC an "obvious error or omission" correction power within 9 months of delivery, separate from enquiry. The chargeable person can REJECT the correction within 3 months by notice under Sch 33 para 4(5)-(7).
- **Misframe 4:** "Amending a return that increases tax due always triggers a Sch 24 penalty." FALSE — unprompted disclosure (where HMRC has not signalled awareness and no OTM letter has arrived) unlocks the 0% careless-unprompted floor under Sch 24 para 10. Timing matters: amend BEFORE HMRC contact lands.
- **Misframe 5:** "Cat-2 / Cat-3 uplift only applies to deliberate behaviour." FALSE per FA 2015 Sch 21 — the offshore uplift applies across all behaviour categories (careless 0-30% → ×1.5 = 0-45% Cat-2 / ×2 = 0-60% Cat-3; deliberate 20-70% → ×1.5 = 30-105% Cat-2 / ×2 = 40-140% Cat-3 (capped at 200%); deliberate-concealed 30-100% → ×1.5 = 45-150% Cat-2 / ×2 = 60-200% Cat-3).
- **Misframe 6:** "Penalty suspension is unavailable for ATED." FALSE per FA 2007 Sch 24 para 14 — suspension is available for careless-behaviour penalties subject to SMART conditions for up to 24 months across the inaccuracy regime, INCLUDING ATED amendments. Suspension is not available for deliberate-behaviour penalties.
- **Misframe 7:** "Once HMRC opens an enquiry, you can't amend." TECHNICALLY CORRECT but misleading — once HMRC's Sch 33 para 8 enquiry is open, formal amendment is suspended, but corrections can still be communicated to HMRC through the enquiry response and will be reflected in the closure notice. The substantive correction is achievable; just not through the para 3 amendment route.
- **Misframe 8:** "If the original return overpaid tax, you can amend it any time within 4 years." MISLEADING — the Sch 33 para 3 amendment window is bounded by the period-boundary anchor, NOT 4 years. The 4-year window is the TMA 1970 s.43 overpaid-tax claim route, which is the BEYOND-window architecture. Sessions must walk both routes distinctly.
- **Misframe 9:** "Amendment is always cheaper than waiting for HMRC discovery." NOT NECESSARILY — where the original return was correct as filed and HMRC's queries are unfounded, amendment can introduce inaccuracy where none existed. Don't amend defensively without specialist review. Sessions must walk the "is the amendment correct" question separately from the "is it timely" question.
- **Misframe 10:** "A claim-only return cannot be amended." FALSE — a claim-only relief return is still a return under Sch 33 para 1 and can be amended under para 3. Common amendment scenario: original return claimed s.133 relief; mid-period non-qualifying-individual occupation discovered post-filing; amendment to remove relief + declare ATED due. This is a critical scenario for s.135 clawback compliance.
- **Misframe 11:** "Amending under Sch 33 para 3 protects against discovery assessment." FALSE — amendment within window does not extinguish HMRC's Sch 33 para 21 discovery power. HMRC can discover further inaccuracies in the amended return AND can discover inaccuracies in the original return not corrected by the amendment. The amendment is corrective, not protective.
- **Misframe 12:** "The 'best of knowledge' declaration only applies to original returns." FALSE per Sch 33 para 1(3) — every return must carry the declaration, and an amendment is a return. A careless amendment is itself a Sch 24 inaccuracy.
- **Misframe 13:** "Amendment is the same as resubmission." FALSE — amendment is a formal Sch 33 para 3 process via notice to an officer. Resubmission of the original return without the para 3 notice is not effective; HMRC may treat the second filing as a duplicate and ignore it. Use the online service amend flow or write formally to HMRC's ATED team.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: I filed my 2025/26 ATED return on 30 April 2025 and just realised in March 2026 that I missed claiming s.133 rental relief. Can I still amend?**
   A: YES. FA 2013 Sch 33 para 3(3) gives you until the end of the NEXT chargeable period (i.e. 31 March 2027 for a 2025/26 return) to amend. You're well within the window. The amendment refiles the period's return with the s.133 relief claimed; ATED charge reduces to £0; overpaid tax repayable with statutory interest under FA 2009 s.102. The amendment-vs-12-months-from-filing confusion comes from self-assessment practice — ATED uses a different (period-boundary) anchor.

2. **Q: My accountant says the ATED amendment window is 12 months from when I filed the return. Is that right?**
   A: NO. That is the self-assessment amendment window under TMA 1970 s.9ZA — a borrowed analogy that doesn't apply to ATED. FA 2013 Sch 33 para 3(3) anchors the ATED amendment deadline to the END OF THE NEXT CHARGEABLE PERIOD after the period to which the return relates (chargeable periods run 1 April to 31 March). For a 2025/26 return, the amendment deadline is 31 March 2027. There is a sub-(4) extension for returns delivered on or after 1 January in the following chargeable period: the window extends to 3 months from the delivery date.

3. **Q: Can HMRC amend my ATED return without telling me?**
   A: HMRC has an obvious-error-or-omission correction power under FA 2013 Sch 33 para 4 within 9 months of delivery. HMRC must NOTIFY you of any correction. You can REJECT the correction within 3 months of the correction notice (Sch 33 para 4(5)-(7)) — rejection preserves your autonomy and the original return stands. Beyond Sch 33 para 4, HMRC's only routes to alter a return are enquiry (Sch 33 para 8) or discovery (Sch 33 para 21).

4. **Q: What if the amendment window has closed and I want to recover overpaid tax?**
   A: The beyond-window route for overpaid tax is a TMA 1970 s.43 overpayment claim (4 years from the chargeable period end). Adviser writes to HMRC's ATED team with: original return, evidence of the qualifying position (e.g. s.133 relief eligibility), explanation letter framing original error as honest mistake. HMRC reviews and (where satisfied) repays with statutory interest under FA 2009 s.102. No penalty exposure — the claim is for overpaid tax, not under-declared tax.

5. **Q: What if the amendment window has closed and I want to disclose UNDER-declared tax?**
   A: Voluntary disclosure via the OTM-letters response track (HP §18.7) or direct correspondence to HMRC's ATED team. Pre-empts HMRC discovery assessment under Sch 33 para 21. Unprompted disclosure (no prior OTM letter; no HMRC signal of awareness) unlocks lower Sch 24 mitigation floors (0% careless / 20% deliberate). Prompted disclosure (after OTM letter) still unlocks lower end of prompted band. Always cheaper than waiting for HMRC assessment.

6. **Q: I claimed s.134 demolition relief but the demolition was abandoned. Do I have to amend?**
   A: YES if within Sch 33 para 3 window (cleanest route). The s.134 transitional relief required demonstrable demolition intention; if intention failed, relief was not properly available and the return understates tax. Amendment under Sch 33 para 3 corrects to full ATED; voluntary disclosure mitigates Sch 24 penalty exposure (potentially careless not deliberate where the intention was genuine at filing time but later abandoned for documented reasons).

7. **Q: Does the offshore Cat-2 / Cat-3 uplift apply to my amendment?**
   A: Only if the inaccuracy concerns an "offshore matter" or "offshore transfer" under FA 2015 Sch 21. For overseas-corporate ATED filers (Cayman / BVI / Jersey / Luxembourg / Delaware / etc.), the matter is typically offshore (corporate registers, accounting records, decision-makers offshore) — Cat-2 or Cat-3 likely applies. Uplift sits on top of Sch 24: Cat-2 = 1.5x; Cat-3 = 2x. Verify the current territory schedule against gov.uk at filing time.

8. **Q: Can I get the careless penalty suspended after an amendment?**
   A: Penalty suspension under FA 2007 Sch 24 para 14 is available for careless-behaviour penalties only, subject to SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time-bound) conditions for up to 24 months. Strong suspension cases: amendment-driven careless behaviour + contemporaneous remediation plan (specialist review of all future ATED returns; improved record-keeping discipline; agent oversight; documented controls). Deliberate-behaviour penalties are NOT suspendable.

9. **Q: I filed late on 15 February 2027 (10 months late for the 2025/26 return). Does the amendment window shift?**
   A: YES per Sch 33 para 3(4). Where a return is delivered on or after 1 January in the following chargeable period, the amendment window extends to 3 months from the delivery date. Your 2025/26 return delivered 15 February 2027 gets until 15 May 2027 to amend — narrower than the normal 31 March 2027 (sub-(3)) deadline, BUT the sub-(4) extension means you don't lose the amendment route entirely just because filing was late.

10. **Q: How do I time the amendment to maximise penalty mitigation if the amendment INCREASES tax due?**
    A: AMEND EARLY. Sch 24 unprompted-disclosure mitigation requires that HMRC has not signalled awareness of the inaccuracy. Amend before any OTM letter arrives; amend before any enquiry opens; amend before any informal HMRC correspondence touches the matter. Each of these events shifts the mitigation matrix from unprompted (0-30% careless floor) to prompted (15-30% careless floor). The amend-early discipline can save 15 percentage points on a careless-behaviour penalty.

11. **Q: Can I amend a claim-only return (where original ATED was £0 due to full relief)?**
    A: YES under Sch 33 para 3. A claim-only return is still a return under Sch 33 para 1. Common scenario: original claim-only return with s.133 rental relief; mid-period non-qualifying-individual occupation surfaces (a relative visited for a fortnight); amendment to add the days of non-qualifying occupation + recalculate the chargeable amount + pay tax due. Critical for s.135 clawback compliance.

12. **Q: I think HMRC made a mistake on my return — can I get them to correct it under Sch 33 para 4?**
    A: Sch 33 para 4 is HMRC's power to correct YOUR return, not your power to compel HMRC to correct theirs. However, an adviser can write to HMRC's ATED team formally requesting an obvious-error correction be issued within HMRC's 9-month window from delivery. If HMRC accepts the request, they issue a correction notice; you may then accept (silent acceptance after 3 months) or reject within 3 months. The route is under-used and is a tax-tips lever for genuine HMRC-side mistakes.

13. **Q: Does amendment protect me from a discovery assessment under Sch 33 para 21?**
    A: NO. Amendment within window does not extinguish HMRC's discovery power. HMRC can discover further inaccuracies in the amended return AND can discover original-return inaccuracies not corrected by the amendment. Amendment is corrective, not protective. The protection comes from FULL DISCLOSURE — if everything material is disclosed in the amendment + explanation letter, HMRC's discovery power is constrained because the disclosure satisfies the "no reason to believe HMRC was about to discover" standard for unprompted treatment, AND constrains HMRC's argument that discovery was reasonable.

14. **Q: What's the difference between amending and resubmitting?**
    A: Amendment under Sch 33 para 3 is a FORMAL process via notice to an officer of Revenue and Customs — typically through the ATED online service "amend" flow or by written correspondence to HMRC's ATED team. Resubmission of the original return without the para 3 notice is NOT effective amendment; HMRC may treat the second filing as a duplicate, ignore it, and the original return stands. Use the formal amend route; document the para 3 notice; preserve the audit trail.
