# The Benefits of Participating in HMRC's Let Property Campaign: A Decision-Layer Guide for Residential Landlords

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — failure-to-notify framework (the alternative path if LPC is NOT used):** Schedule 41 FA 2008 — penalties for failure to notify chargeability. Sch 41 para 5 (behaviour categories), Sch 41 para 13 (disclosure-mitigation matrix), Sch 41 para 20 (reasonable-excuse defence). House-position-locked at §27.3 with verification 2026-05-24. Without LPC the landlord faces these maxima: non-deliberate 30% Cat 1 / 45% Cat 2 / 60% Cat 3; deliberate-not-concealed 70% / 105% / 140%; deliberate-and-concealed 100% / 150% / 200%.
- **Primary — disclosure obligation underlying the failure:** Taxes Management Act 1970 section 7 — notice of liability to income tax + CGT within 6 months of the end of the tax year. Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1970/9/section/7`. Missing this is the failure that Sch 41 penalises.
- **Primary — discovery + extended-time-limit assessment (what HMRC does if you don't use LPC):** TMA 1970 s.29 (discovery threshold) + s.34 (ordinary 4 years) + s.36(1) (careless 6 years) + s.36(1A) (deliberate 20 years). House-position-locked at §27.1 with verification 2026-05-24 against the legislation.gov.uk current-as-amended text. For offshore matters s.36A FA 2019 gives HMRC 12 years even without behaviour element.
- **Supporting — inaccuracy penalties (where SA returns were filed but inaccurate):** Schedule 24 FA 2007 (where the landlord filed SA returns but understated rental income — different statutory hook from a never-notified scenario). Sch 24 para 4A offshore Category uplift.
- **Supporting — interest on unpaid tax:** TMA 1970 s.86 (interest on income tax + CGT); FA 2009 s.101 (general HMRC interest framework). Interest is not penalty but accrues continuously and is NOT mitigated by LPC.
- **Supporting — LPC operational anchor:** HMRC published guidance at gov.uk/guidance/let-property-campaign. Campaign open since 9 September 2013 with no announced end date (per §27.6 lock).
- **House position reference:** §27.6 (LPC + WDF + DDS + CoP9 route choice — primary anchor); §27.3 (Sch 41 unprompted-disclosure mechanics — the mitigation engine LPC unlocks); §27.1 (TMA 1970 s.29 / s.36 / s.36A — the counterfactual HMRC-discovery pathway); §27.5 (CoP9 boundary — LPC is NOT appropriate where deliberate-conduct criminal-prosecution exposure exists). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What is the headline mitigation floor benefit — Sch 41 para 13 unprompted-disclosure floor: 0% (non-deliberate, within 12 months of when liability arose) / 10% (non-deliberate, after 12 months) / 20% (deliberate not concealed) / 30% (deliberate and concealed) — versus the prompted-disclosure equivalents of 10% / 35% / 50%?
2. What is the certainty-of-closure benefit — once a complete LPC disclosure is accepted and paid, the matter is closed for s.29 discovery purposes (subject to the s.29(4)/(5) re-opening conditions where the disclosure was incomplete or deliberately inaccurate)?
3. What is the offshore uplift avoidance benefit — for landlords with offshore rental income, LPC mitigation under Sch 41 para 13 still attracts the Category 1/2/3 uplift, but at the unprompted-disclosure floor rather than prompted; and WDF + FtC overlay may apply alongside (§27.6 lock).
4. What does the LPC operational cycle look like — the three steps: (i) notify via gov.uk landlord disclosure form (no penalty consequence at notification); (ii) disclose full liability within 90 days of HMRC's acknowledgment; (iii) pay liability + interest + penalty on the disclosure?
5. What is the cost-of-NOT-using-LPC counterfactual — HMRC discovery under TMA 1970 s.29 brings the prompted-disclosure floor (10% / 35% / 50%); s.36 extends the assessment reach to 6 years (careless) or 20 years (deliberate) or 12 years (offshore innocent-error under s.36A); s.86 interest continues to accrue at the official rate; nudge-letter pathways under §27 (HMRC OTM / nudge letter campaign — multiple types covered in sibling pages A9 + A10) frequently precede formal s.29 assessment, eroding the unprompted-disclosure floor before the landlord acts?
6. What is the reasonable-excuse defence sitting alongside LPC — Sch 41 para 20 + Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 four-stage test? Where reasonable excuse applies the penalty falls to nil entirely; LPC mitigation is the floor in the absence of reasonable excuse.
7. What is the boundary of LPC — residential rental income only (NOT commercial, NOT mixed-use, NOT serviced-accommodation in some configurations); UK + non-UK resident landlords both eligible; companies NOT eligible (LtdCo route differs and uses Corporation Tax disclosure routes); trustees NOT eligible (TRS route under §22.16); deliberate-fraud cases with criminal-prosecution exposure NOT suitable (use CoP9 / CDF instead per §27.5).
8. What records support an LPC disclosure (cross-reference §27.7 record retention — TMA 1970 s.12B 5-years-from-31-January floor; firm's 7-year practical recommendation per §27.8)?
9. What time-to-pay options exist where the disclosed liability cannot be paid in full within 90 days — TMA 1970 s.108 + CRCA 2005 time-to-pay; HMRC will typically agree a structured plan subject to evidence of inability to pay in full; interest continues to accrue under s.86 during any time-to-pay arrangement?
10. What is the post-LPC landscape — closure for s.29 purposes (subject to s.29(4)/(5)); HMRC retains discretion on criminal investigation (LPC does NOT confer criminal-prosecution immunity, distinguish from CoP9 / CDF); the landlord's record-keeping obligations continue under s.12B; any continuing rental income from the disclosed period forward must be brought into the regular self-assessment cycle (or MTD ITSA from 6 April 2026 if threshold met under §19.1 lock).
