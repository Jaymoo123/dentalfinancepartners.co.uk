# Missed Taxes? The Let Property Campaign Is to the Rescue: An Action Framework for Landlords Who Have Just Realised

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — the rescue mechanism is Sch 41 FA 2008 para 13:** the unprompted-disclosure mitigation matrix is what makes LPC the gentle entry-point HMRC designed for the realised-late landlord population. The 0% non-deliberate-unprompted-within-12-months floor is the headline rescue line; the 20% / 30% unprompted-deliberate floors (versus 35% / 50% prompted) are the substantive rescue for landlords with longer-running awareness. Verified per §27.3 Wave 7 lock 2026-05-24 against legislation.gov.uk.
- **Primary — the time-pivot point for the 0% floor:** the 12-month qualifier in Sch 41 para 13 runs from the date liability arose (end of the tax year in which the rental income was received). For a landlord realising in 2026 they have 2024/25 + earlier rental that was never disclosed: the 2024/25 tax year liability arose 5 April 2025; the 12-month qualifier window for the 0% unprompted-non-deliberate floor on 2024/25 closes 5 April 2026. Earlier years are already past the 12-month window — the floor is 10%, still substantially better than prompted-disclosure 10% (which is the same for that band) but importantly the unprompted-status itself is the rescue (any HMRC indication of awareness flips floor to prompted bands across all years).
- **Primary — the 3-step LPC operational cycle (the rescue mechanism in operation):** (i) **notify** intent via gov.uk LPC notification form — no penalty consequence at this stage, HMRC issues a Disclosure Reference Number (DRN); (ii) **disclose** full liability within 90 days of HMRC's acknowledgement — tax + interest + proposed penalty calculation by tax year covered, with supporting evidence; (iii) **pay** the full liability on or before submission of the disclosure (time-to-pay arrangements may be agreed in advance for material liabilities — operational exception not the rule). House-position-locked at §27.6 Wave 7 lock 2026-05-24.
- **Primary — what does NOT count as the rescue route (boundary statements):**
  - **CoP9 / CDF** — for **deliberate-fraud cases with criminal-prosecution exposure**, not for careless / accidental landlords. Sessions writing must NOT route panicking-but-careless landlords into CoP9; the route is calibrated to deliberate-concealed-with-criminal-interest cases (false invoices, third-party-conduit bank accounts, document destruction). §27.5 Wave 7 lock.
  - **WDF (Worldwide Disclosure Facility)** — for **offshore** rental income (Cat 2 / Cat 3 territory rental property), not UK-only. UK-only undisclosed rental income uses LPC. Cross-jurisdictional landlords (UK rental + offshore rental) use BOTH routes in parallel (each route for its respective tax-year-source). §27.6 Wave 7 lock.
  - **DDS general voluntary disclosure** — for **non-residential / commercial** rental property, or **non-rental UK tax** matters. Residential rental income uses LPC specifically. §27.6 Wave 7 lock.
  - **SA-return amendment** — landlords sometimes try to "fix" the problem by filing an amended SA return adding the previously-omitted rental income. **DO NOT.** This route is procedurally wrong (it does not engage the LPC mitigation matrix; HMRC treats it as a prompted disclosure if they were already aware, or as a routine amendment if not — neither outcome is the calibrated LPC unprompted-disclosure mitigation). Sessions writing must explicitly warn against this misstep.
- **Primary — what a nudge letter changes:** HMRC's nudge-letter programme issues targeted letters to taxpayers HMRC has identified via Connect-system data-matching as potentially having undisclosed rental income. **A nudge letter does NOT itself crystallise prompted-disclosure status** — but it puts the landlord on notice that HMRC has indicated awareness, and landlords who respond with disclosure after a nudge letter are typically treated as prompted-disclosure for Sch 41 para 13 purposes (10% floor non-deliberate / 35% deliberate-not-concealed / 50% deliberate-concealed). The strategic point: if a landlord receives a nudge letter, the unprompted-status window for the years HMRC has identified is closed; remaining years (if any) where the nudge letter did not indicate awareness may retain unprompted status — Stage 2 must clarify this nuance with care.
- **Primary — the interest cascade (NOT mitigated by LPC):** TMA 1970 s.86 (interest on income tax + CGT) + FA 2009 s.101 (HMRC interest framework). Interest accrues continuously from each year's due date, is NOT mitigated by LPC, and continues to accrue until full payment. For a landlord with 6 years of undisclosed rental tax, interest accrued can run into substantial sums; this is the **operative cost component** that the LPC penalty mitigation does NOT touch. House-position-locked at §27 framework.
- **Primary — the closure-of-exposure benefit on acceptance:** TMA 1970 s.29 (discovery threshold) + s.28A (closure-notice framework) — once an LPC disclosure is accepted and paid in full, the covered years are **closed for s.29 discovery purposes** (subject to the s.29(4)/(5) re-opening conditions where the disclosure was incomplete or deliberately understated). This is the structural reassurance that LPC is the rescue — it produces a finished outcome, not a perpetually-open exposure. House-position-locked at §27.4 Wave 7 lock.
- **House position reference:** §27.6 (LPC + 3-step cycle — primary anchor); §27.3 (Sch 41 mitigation matrix — operative for the headline rescue floors); §27.1 (discovery time limits — what HMRC could reach if discovery comes first); §27.4 (s.28A closure-notice architecture — closure-of-exposure mechanism); §27.5 (CoP9 boundary — for the specialist-territory escalation). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED — fully covered by Wave 7 §27 lock.
