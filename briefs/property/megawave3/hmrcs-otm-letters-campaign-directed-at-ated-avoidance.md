# HMRC's OTM letters campaign on ATED non-compliance: what a director should do in the first 30 days

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (ATED charge):** **FA 2013 Part 3 (ss.94-174)** introduces and operates ATED. Charge mechanism ss.94-99; chargeable interest definitions ss.107-119; reliefs ss.132-150; returns + administration ss.151-174.
- **Primary (penalties):**
  - **FA 2009 Schedule 55** — failure to file penalties (£100 immediate, £200 at 3 months, £300 at 6 months and 12 months; tax-geared penalties for prompted / unprompted / deliberate / concealed bands).
  - **FA 2009 Schedule 56** — failure to pay penalties (5% / 5% / 5% at 30 days / 5 months / 11 months past the due date).
  - **FA 2007 Schedule 24** — penalties for inaccuracy in a return (careless / deliberate / deliberate-and-concealed bands; suspension where careless and conditions met).
- **Primary (HMRC powers):**
  - **TMA 1970 s.29** — discovery assessment time limits (4 / 6 / 20 year windows depending on behaviour).
  - **TMA 1970 s.36** — extended time limit for offshore matters / careless / deliberate behaviour.
  - **Schedule 36 FA 2008** — information notice powers.
- **Supporting:** Companies Act 2006 (director duties); RoE under ECTEA 2022 (overseas entity registration parallel obligation per §11.A + §18.6); HMRC ATED return form ATED1 + electronic filing platform.
- **House position reference:** **§18.7** (HMRC OTM letters campaign — "letter is not an assessment; it invites voluntary disclosure" + "known practical compliance pressure"), §18 (full ATED Wave 3 extension), §2 (ATED bands 2026/27), §18.3 (relief catalogue), §18.4 (return mechanics + penalties), §18.6 (RoE interaction), §27.6 (voluntary disclosure routes — LPC + WDF + general voluntary).

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What is an HMRC OTM (One-to-Many) letter? (HMRC compliance nudge — not a formal assessment under TMA 1970 s.29; identifies suspected non-compliance based on data-warehouse matching, typically Land Registry holding + no ATED return.)
2. Why might a company receive one? (Land Registry shows the company holds a UK dwelling that HMRC's data suggests is worth more than £500,000, and no ATED return has been filed for one or more of the most recent chargeable periods.)
3. Is the letter itself an assessment? (No — it invites a voluntary response; ignoring it triggers HMRC discovery assessment under TMA 1970 s.29 with the unprompted-prompted penalty differential at FA 2007 Sch 24.)
4. What is the first triage step? (Confirm whether the property is in ATED scope: non-natural-person holder per s.94; chargeable value above £500,000 at most recent valuation date (1 April 2017 or 1 April 2022); reliefs available per ss.132-150.)
5. If a relief is available (most commonly the **s.133 property-rental-business relief**), does that mean nothing needs filing? (NO — relief MUST be claimed on a return; a claim-only return with all-relief boxes ticked is still mandatory. Failing to file even when no tax is due triggers penalty points under FA 2009 Sch 55.)
6. What is the disclosure pathway? (Typically the Digital Disclosure Service via HMRC online; ATED-specific disclosures generally do NOT go through the Let Property Campaign (which is income-tax focused). Confirm with HMRC's voluntary disclosure routing.)
7. How are penalties calculated? (Failure-to-file: FA 2009 Sch 55 graduated tariff. Failure-to-pay: FA 2009 Sch 56. Inaccuracy: FA 2007 Sch 24 with the unprompted (30%-70%) vs prompted (35%-100%) bands depending on behaviour category.)
8. How does the RoE / ECTEA 2022 obligation interact? (RoE and ATED run in parallel — see §18.6. A non-compliant overseas entity cannot complete Land Registry dispositions, but the ATED return obligation continues regardless of RoE status.)
9. What is the role of Schedule 36 FA 2008 information notices? (HMRC may follow up with a formal information notice if voluntary engagement is refused or inadequate; non-compliance with the notice triggers further penalties.)
10. How does the **5-year discovery window** apply for ATED non-compliance? (Standard 4 years; 6 years for careless; 20 years for deliberate. ATED-specific cases involving overseas elements may attract the offshore-matters extended time limits under TMA 1970 s.36.)
11. Should the director use CoP9 (Contractual Disclosure Facility) instead of DDS? (CoP9 is for suspected deliberate tax fraud — appropriate where dishonesty allegations are foreseeable. Most ATED-OTM cases are honest oversight or relief-mis-classification; DDS is the proportionate route. Cross-reference §27.5.)
