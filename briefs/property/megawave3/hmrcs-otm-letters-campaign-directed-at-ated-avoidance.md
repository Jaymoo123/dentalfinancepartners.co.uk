# HMRC's OTM letters campaign on ATED non-compliance: what a director should do in the first 30 days

## Statutory anchor
- **Primary (ATED charge):** **FA 2013 Part 3 (ss.94-174)** introduces and operates ATED. Charge mechanism ss.94-99; chargeable interest definitions ss.107-119; reliefs ss.132-150; returns + administration ss.151-174.
- **Primary (penalties):**
  - **FA 2009 Schedule 55** — failure to file penalties (£100 immediate, £200 at 3 months, £300 at 6 months and 12 months; tax-geared penalties for prompted / unprompted / deliberate / concealed bands).
  - **FA 2009 Schedule 56** — failure to pay penalties (5% / 5% / 5% at 30 days / 5 months / 11 months past the due date).
  - **FA 2007 Schedule 24** — penalties for inaccuracy in a return (careless / deliberate / deliberate-and-concealed bands; suspension where careless and conditions met).
- **Primary (HMRC powers):**
  - **TMA 1970 s.29** — discovery assessment time limits (4 / 6 / 20 year windows depending on behaviour).
  - **TMA 1970 s.36** — extended time limit for offshore matters / careless / deliberate behaviour.
  - **Schedule 36 FA 2008** — information notice powers.
- **Supporting:** Companies Act 2006 (director duties); RoE under ECTEA 2022 (overseas entity registration parallel obligation per §11.A + §18.6); HMRC ATED return form ATED1 + electronic filing platform.
- **House position reference:** **§18.7** (HMRC OTM letters campaign — "letter is not an assessment; it invites voluntary disclosure" + "known practical compliance pressure"), §18 (full ATED Wave 3 extension), §2 (ATED bands 2026/27), §18.3 (relief catalogue), §18.4 (return mechanics + penalties), §18.6 (RoE interaction), §27.6 (voluntary disclosure routes — LPC + WDF + general voluntary).

## Key questions this page must answer
1. What is an HMRC OTM (One-to-Many) letter? (HMRC compliance nudge — not a formal assessment under TMA 1970 s.29; identifies suspected non-compliance based on data-warehouse matching, typically Land Registry holding + no ATED return.)
2. Why might a company receive one? (Land Registry shows the company holds a UK dwelling that HMRC's data suggests is worth more than £500,000, and no ATED return has been filed for one or more of the most recent chargeable periods.)
3. Is the letter itself an assessment? (No — it invites a voluntary response; ignoring it triggers HMRC discovery assessment under TMA 1970 s.29 with the unprompted-prompted penalty differential at FA 2007 Sch 24.)
4. What is the first triage step? (Confirm whether the property is in ATED scope: non-natural-person holder per s.94; chargeable value above £500,000 at most recent valuation date (1 April 2017 or 1 April 2022); reliefs available per ss.132-150.)
5. If a relief is available (most commonly the **s.133 property-rental-business relief**), does that mean nothing needs filing? (NO — relief MUST be claimed on a return; a claim-only return with all-relief boxes ticked is still mandatory. Failing to file even when no tax is due triggers penalty points under FA 2009 Sch 55.)
6. What is the disclosure pathway? (Typically the Digital Disclosure Service via HMRC online; ATED-specific disclosures generally do NOT go through the Let Property Campaign (which is income-tax focused). Confirm with HMRC's voluntary disclosure routing.)
7. How are penalties calculated? (Failure-to-file: FA 2009 Sch 55 graduated tariff. Failure-to-pay: FA 2009 Sch 56. Inaccuracy: FA 2007 Sch 24 with the unprompted (30%-70%) vs prompted (35%-100%) bands depending on behaviour category.)
8. How does the RoE / ECTEA 2022 obligation interact? (RoE and ATED run in parallel — see §18.6. A non-compliant overseas entity cannot complete Land Registry dispositions, but the ATED return obligation continues regardless of RoE status.)
9. What is the role of Schedule 36 FA 2008 information notices? (HMRC may follow up with a formal information notice if voluntary engagement is refused or inadequate; non-compliance with the notice triggers further penalties.)
10. How does the **5-year discovery window** apply for ATED non-compliance? (Standard 4 years; 6 years for careless; 20 years for deliberate. ATED-specific cases involving overseas elements may attract the offshore-matters extended time limits under TMA 1970 s.36.)
11. Should the director use CoP9 (Contractual Disclosure Facility) instead of DDS? (CoP9 is for suspected deliberate tax fraud — appropriate where dishonesty allegations are foreseeable. Most ATED-OTM cases are honest oversight or relief-mis-classification; DDS is the proportionate route. Cross-reference §27.5.)

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — The 30-day triage decision tree (the page's first concrete artefact)

Render as semantic HTML ordered list — this is the operational anchor a director needs in the first hour after the letter arrives:

1. **Identify the chargeable period(s) in question.** ATED chargeable period runs 1 April to 31 March. HMRC's OTM letter typically cites the period(s) where no return has been filed. Note the cited period(s) explicitly + the property they relate to.
2. **Confirm scope per FA 2013 s.94.** Is the holder a non-natural person (UK company, non-UK company, partnership with corporate member, collective investment scheme)? If yes → ATED-applicable in principle.
3. **Confirm value per the most recent valuation date.** ATED chargeable value is the property's open-market value at the most recent 5-yearly valuation date (1 April 2012 / 1 April 2017 / 1 April 2022 / 1 April 2027) OR the acquisition value if acquired between revaluation dates. If chargeable value > £500,000 → ATED applies.
4. **Identify any available reliefs per FA 2013 ss.132-150.** The most common is the s.133 property-rental-business relief for commercial letting to unconnected tenants. Other reliefs: s.138 property-developer, s.141 property-trader, s.144 farmhouse, s.145 employee accommodation, s.137 dwellings open to public, s.150 charitable use, s.149 social-housing registered provider, ss.134-135 demolition / conversion.
5. **Calculate the worst-case tax exposure per period.** Use the §18.1 band table for the chargeable value bracket. Annual charge × number of years unfiled.
6. **Calculate the worst-case penalty exposure.** Sch 55 FA 2009 failure-to-file (£100 / £200 / £300 escalation per period unfiled); Sch 56 FA 2009 failure-to-pay (5% / 5% / 5%); Sch 24 FA 2007 inaccuracy if the return is filed with errors (30%-200% depending on behaviour band + offshore Category — per §27.2).
7. **Determine the disclosure route.** Digital Disclosure Service (DDS) is the standard route for ATED-side compliance. The Let Property Campaign is income-tax-side and is NOT the ATED route. Where deliberate behaviour with criminal-prosecution exposure is suspected, the appropriate route is CoP9 (Contractual Disclosure Facility) per §27.5.
8. **Engage specialist representation within the first 7 days.** ATED-OTM cases have a narrow window for the unprompted-disclosure floor; representation matters.
9. **Notify HMRC of intent to disclose via DDS within the first 14 days.** Notification triggers the 90-day disclosure window.
10. **File outstanding returns + disclosure + pay tax + penalty within the 90-day window.** Each missed return is a separate filing under FA 2009 Sch 55.

### Example 2 — Worked penalty exposure: BTL ltd co receives OTM letter for 3 unfiled years

Magnolia Investments Limited (UK company; sole director Mr Bridge; sole asset a £900k London BTL flat let to an unconnected tenant since 2022 for £36k/year on commercial terms). Mr Bridge incorporated the company in February 2022 to insulate the property from his personal estate + IHT planning. He missed the ATED requirement entirely — Magnolia has never filed an ATED return. HMRC's OTM letter arrives in May 2026 citing chargeable periods 2023/24, 2024/25, 2025/26 (three years unfiled).
- **Scope check:** Magnolia is a UK company (non-natural person per s.94 FA 2013 → in scope).
- **Value check:** £900k acquisition value (2022, between 2022 and 2027 valuation dates) → falls in the £500,001-£1m band → 2023/24 charge ~£4,150; 2024/25 ~£4,400; 2025/26 ~£4,450; 2026/27 charge £4,600 per §18.1 (verify each year at write per §16.27 against gov.uk ATED-specific archived band tables).
- **Relief availability:** Magnolia lets commercially to an unconnected tenant → qualifies for s.133 property-rental-business relief throughout. Worst-case TAX exposure if relief is properly claimed = £0 (claim-only return with all-relief boxes ticked).
- **Penalty exposure even with relief:** the obligation to FILE the return is independent of the relief calculation. Failure to file triggers Sch 55 FA 2009 per period unfiled — £100 immediate + £200 at 3 months + £300 at 6 months + £300 at 12 months per year = up to £900 per year × 3 years = £2,700 (this is the worst-case before tax-geared penalty top-up + mitigation).
- **Sch 24 inaccuracy:** does NOT bite if the return is filed correctly with the relief claimed — no inaccuracy. If the relief is wrongly claimed (e.g. letting to a connected person while claiming s.133), Sch 24 applies on the tax-geared exposure (30%-100% unprompted band depending on behaviour per §27.2 per F-5 lock).
- **Disclosure route:** Mr Bridge engages an ATED specialist within 7 days; notifies HMRC of DDS intent within 14 days; files all three claim-only returns + a brief disclosure narrative explaining the oversight within the 90-day DDS window. Sch 55 unprompted-disclosure floors apply.
- **Realistic outcome:** with prompt engagement + unprompted disclosure, the Sch 55 penalty load is materially reduced; HMRC typically accepts that genuine oversight on relief-only filings warrants the lower mitigation band. Mr Bridge's exposure crystallises at a few hundred pounds in penalties + the time + professional fees. Ignoring the letter would have moved the case to a prompted-disclosure footing + potential follow-up under FA 2008 Sch 36 information notices + escalation to formal s.29 assessment.

### Example 3 — Worked penalty exposure: overseas company with deliberate non-disclosure

Glentworth Holdings BVI (BVI-incorporated company; UK-non-resident; UK-Land-Registry-registered owner of a £4.2m Knightsbridge dwelling occupied by the company's beneficial owner Sr Ortiz + his family throughout 2017-2026). The director (a nominee) has never filed an ATED return; the structure was deliberately set up to avoid UK tax visibility. HMRC's OTM letter arrives in May 2026 referencing periods 2017/18 through 2026/27 (10 years unfiled).
- **Scope check:** Glentworth Holdings BVI is a non-UK company (non-natural person per s.94 → in scope).
- **Value check:** £4.2m → £2m-£5m band → 2026/27 annual charge £32,200 per §18.1; back-years per archived band tables (verify each year at write per §16.27).
- **Relief availability:** Sr Ortiz + his family OCCUPY the dwelling — the s.133 property-rental-business relief does NOT apply (no commercial letting); no other relief obviously applies on these facts. Worst-case TAX exposure: ~£250,000-£300,000+ across 10 years of unfiled annual charges (verify aggregate at write).
- **Penalty exposure:** Sch 55 failure-to-file (£900/year × 10 years = £9,000 before tax-geared); Sch 56 failure-to-pay (15% of unpaid tax across 10 years); Sch 24 inaccuracy — but there are no returns to be inaccurate; the operative penalty machinery is Sch 41 failure-to-notify (per §27.3) bringing the deliberate-concealed offshore Category 3 200% bracket potentially into play if HMRC views the structure as deliberately concealed offshore arrangement. Aggregate exposure could exceed £600,000-£700,000 + interest.
- **Discovery time limit per §27.1:** s.36A (12 years for offshore innocent-error) is unlikely to apply here (this looks deliberate, not innocent). s.36(1A) (20 years for deliberate) is more likely → HMRC can reach back to the 2007/08 period (the original 2013 ATED commencement is the practical floor).
- **Disclosure route — CoP9 NOT DDS:** the deliberate-behaviour + criminal-prosecution-exposure footprint here is consistent with the CoP9 / Contractual Disclosure Facility framework per §27.5 (offers criminal-prosecution immunity for matters disclosed). Mr Ortiz / Glentworth's director should engage specialist tax-investigations counsel within 7 days; the CoP9 route is the proportionate response, not DDS.
- **RoE interaction:** Glentworth Holdings BVI is also subject to the Register of Overseas Entities under ECTEA 2022 per §18.6. A non-RoE-compliant overseas entity cannot complete UK Land Registry dispositions — the OTM letter may trigger parallel RoE investigation. Sr Ortiz's structure faces ATED + RoE + potential follow-up under MLR 2017 + ECCTA 2023 ID-verification.
- **Realistic outcome:** specialist representation, CoP9 + full disclosure + tax + tax-geared penalty (with mitigation toward the unprompted-disclosure band) + immunity from criminal prosecution for the matters disclosed. Total cash exposure substantial but bounded. Ignoring the letter would convert this to a criminal-investigation track with potential prosecution risk.

### Example 4 — When DDS is the route (the most common scenario)

DDS (Digital Disclosure Service at `https://www.gov.uk/guidance/hmrc-disclosure-service-and-time-to-pay-arrangements`) is the standard voluntary-disclosure mechanism for ATED-side compliance failures that do NOT involve deliberate fraud. Three-step structure per §27.6:
1. **Notify** intent to disclose via the gov.uk DDS form. No penalty consequence at this stage.
2. **Disclose** full liability within 90 days of HMRC's acknowledgment — calculating tax + interest + penalty. ATED-specific: each unfiled year is a separate return obligation; calculate per-year tax + Sch 55 penalty + Sch 56 if any tax was due + unpaid; Sch 24 if filed-with-errors.
3. **Pay** the full liability on disclosure.
- **Penalty band achieved:** typically careless 0% (unprompted within 12 months — per F-5 lock, this is Sch 41 not Sch 24; Sch 24 unprompted floor is 0% but has no 12-month qualifier) to 15% (prompted), occasionally up to 30% — DDS unlocks the unprompted-disclosure floors of FA 2007 Sch 24 / FA 2008 Sch 41 frameworks per §27.2 + §27.3.
- **When DDS is NOT appropriate:** where HMRC has criminal-prosecution interest (CoP9 / CDF is the route per §27.5); where the ATED-side failures are inseparable from income-tax-side rental failures (consider whether Let Property Campaign route is parallel-needed for income-tax-side); where the matter is purely on appeal-side of an existing assessment (use ARTG appeals process, not DDS).

### Example 5 — When CoP9 is the route (deliberate behaviour suspected)

CoP9 (HMRC Code of Practice 9) is HMRC's civil-fraud-investigation track per §27.5. Where HMRC suspects tax fraud + offers CDF (Contractual Disclosure Facility), the taxpayer has 60 days to accept by signing the CDF acceptance + providing an outline disclosure. Acceptance crystallises immunity from criminal prosecution for the matters disclosed.
- **CoP9 in ATED context:** rare for ordinary BTL ltd-co oversight (where DDS is proportionate). Operative for: (i) deliberately-concealed overseas-entity structures intended to evade ATED; (ii) Sch 24 deliberate-concealed inaccuracies on filed returns; (iii) where the ATED matter is one of several deliberate non-disclosures bundled together (income tax + ATED + IHT + CGT).
- **HMRC-initiated CoP9** is where HMRC issues a CoP9 letter offering CDF — distinguish from **taxpayer-initiated CDF request** where the taxpayer asks HMRC to put them under CoP9 (often as the prophylactic step before HMRC opens an enquiry).
- **Specialist representation essential.** CoP9 is not a do-it-yourself route; tax-investigations counsel + specialist representation is the operative standard.

### Example 6 — The "I filed an ATED return but it was wrong" case (Sch 24 inaccuracy)

Yew Tree Estates Limited (UK company; £1.8m London dwelling; let on commercial terms to an unconnected tenant for the 2022/23 to 2024/25 periods → s.133 relief applied; for 2025/26 the company let to the director's adult son for a below-market rent). Yew Tree filed the 2025/26 return claiming s.133 relief — but the s.133 relief does NOT apply where the letting is not to an unconnected tenant on commercial terms. HMRC's OTM letter (a different OTM iteration targeting relief-claim accuracy via Land Registry + Companies House cross-match against PSC + director records) arrives flagging the relief-claim accuracy.
- **Position:** s.133 is unavailable for 2025/26 → £9,450 tax due under £1m-£2m band per §18.1. The 2025/26 return was inaccurate.
- **Sch 24 penalty per §27.2:** behaviour band depends on the facts. Standard maximums: careless 30%; deliberate not concealed 70%; deliberate-and-concealed 100%. Mitigation: unprompted disclosure floor 0% / 20% / 30%; prompted floor 15% / 35% / 50%. Since HMRC's OTM letter has prompted disclosure → prompted floor applies. If treated as careless (overlooked the connected-person test) → 15% mitigation; penalty on £9,450 = £1,418 (verify quantum at write per §16.27).
- **Suspension under Sch 24 para 14:** careless penalties may be suspended for up to 2 years subject to conditions — possible here if the director commits to specific governance / return-review steps for future ATED filings. *Anderson v HMRC* [2018] UKUT 159 provides the framework for suspension challenges if HMRC declines.
- **Practical: amend the 2025/26 return + pay the £9,450 + £1,418 penalty (or argue suspension) within the DDS 90-day window.**

### Example 7 — Misframings the page must correct (verbatim do-not-write list)

- **Misframe 1:** "The OTM letter is a formal tax assessment." FALSE — OTM is HMRC's data-warehouse-driven compliance nudge; formal assessment comes later under TMA 1970 s.29.
- **Misframe 2:** "Ignoring the OTM letter is a valid strategy if you think HMRC has no real evidence." FALSE — ignoring materially worsens the penalty position (unprompted → prompted disclosure floor differential per §27.2); HMRC's data-warehouse evidence (Land Registry + Companies House + bank reporting) is typically sufficient to ground a formal s.29 assessment.
- **Misframe 3:** "If the s.133 property-rental-business relief applies, no return needs to be filed." FALSE — the relief MUST be claimed on a return; a claim-only return with all-relief boxes ticked is mandatory; failing to file triggers Sch 55 FA 2009 penalty points.
- **Misframe 4:** "The Let Property Campaign is the disclosure route for ATED matters." FALSE — LPC is income-tax-side (residential landlord undisclosed rental income); ATED-side disclosure uses DDS or CoP9 (where deliberate fraud is suspected) per §27.6.
- **Misframe 5:** "ATED-CGT applies on disposal of an enveloped dwelling." FALSE — ATED-CGT abolished from 6 April 2019 per §18.5; gains now fall under NRCGT (non-resident company) or standard CT-on-chargeable-gains (UK company).
- **Misframe 6:** "The discovery window for ATED is always 4 years." FALSE — standard 4 years; 6 years careless; 20 years deliberate; 12 years offshore innocent-error under s.36A per §27.1.
- **Misframe 7:** "RoE compliance means ATED is automatically satisfied." FALSE — RoE and ATED are parallel regimes per §18.6; an RoE-compliant overseas entity still must file ATED returns annually.
- **Misframe 8:** "CoP9 is the appropriate route for ordinary BTL ltd-co ATED oversight." FALSE — CoP9 is for suspected deliberate fraud per §27.5; ordinary oversight is DDS.
- **Misframe 9:** "Sch 24 unprompted disclosure floor of 0% requires the disclosure to be within 12 months." FALSE per F-5 lock at §27.2 — the 12-month cliff exists in Sch 41 (failure-to-notify), NOT Sch 24 (inaccuracy). Sch 24 careless-unprompted floor is 0% with no 12-month qualifier.
- **Misframe 10:** "Sch 24 careless penalties cannot be suspended." FALSE per Sch 24 para 14 — careless penalties may be suspended for up to 2 years subject to suspension conditions; *Anderson* framework for HMRC refusal challenges.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 12-14 FAQs target)

1. **Q: I'm the director of a UK company that owns a £900k London BTL flat. An HMRC OTM letter just arrived saying we haven't filed ATED returns. What is this and what do I do first?**
   A: An HMRC OTM (One-to-Many) letter is a compliance nudge sent on the basis of data-warehouse matching — HMRC has cross-referenced Land Registry holdings against ATED filings and identified that your company appears to be in scope but no return has been filed for the chargeable periods cited. It is NOT a formal assessment under TMA 1970 s.29. Your first 30-day triage steps: (i) identify the chargeable period(s) HMRC is citing; (ii) confirm your company is a non-natural person and the dwelling is worth more than £500,000 at the most recent valuation date; (iii) identify any reliefs that apply (most commonly the s.133 property-rental-business relief for commercial letting to unconnected tenants); (iv) engage a specialist within 7 days; (v) notify HMRC of intent to disclose via the Digital Disclosure Service within 14 days; (vi) file outstanding returns + disclosure within the 90-day DDS window.

2. **Q: Does an OTM letter mean HMRC has already decided we owe tax?**
   A: No. The OTM letter invites a voluntary response. It does NOT carry the legal force of a formal assessment. A formal assessment under TMA 1970 s.29 comes later if voluntary engagement is refused or inadequate. Engaging promptly via the Digital Disclosure Service unlocks the unprompted-disclosure penalty floors under FA 2007 Sch 24 — ignoring the letter moves the case to the prompted-disclosure floor (15%-50% higher mitigation floors per §27.2 + F-5 lock).

3. **Q: My BTL ltd co lets commercially to unconnected tenants — doesn't the s.133 property-rental-business relief mean I don't need to file?**
   A: No. The s.133 relief MUST be claimed on a return. A claim-only return with all-relief boxes ticked is still a mandatory ATED filing. Failing to file even where no tax is due triggers Sch 55 FA 2009 penalty points (£100 immediate + £200 at 3 months + £300 at 6 months + £300 at 12 months per period unfiled). The relief eliminates the tax exposure, not the filing obligation.

4. **Q: How are ATED penalties calculated?**
   A: Three penalty regimes can apply: (i) **Sch 55 FA 2009 failure-to-file** — graduated tariff per period unfiled, £100 immediate then £200 / £300 / £300 escalation; (ii) **Sch 56 FA 2009 failure-to-pay** — 5% at 30 days late + 5% at 5 months + 5% at 11 months; (iii) **Sch 24 FA 2007 inaccuracy** — standard maxima 30% careless / 70% deliberate / 100% deliberate-and-concealed, with offshore Category 2/3 uplifts to 45%/60% / 105%/140% / 150%/200% per §27.2. Unprompted-disclosure floors (0% / 20% / 30%) materially below prompted floors (15% / 35% / 50%) — engaging promptly via DDS is the operative lever.

5. **Q: Should I use the Let Property Campaign for the disclosure?**
   A: No, for the ATED-side failure. The Let Property Campaign is income-tax-side — for residential landlords with undisclosed rental income on the personal tax side. ATED-specific disclosure uses the Digital Disclosure Service. If you have parallel rental-income-side undisclosed obligations (e.g. the ltd co's rental profits weren't properly self-assessed via CT600), you may need both routes — LPC route doesn't exist for ltd-co rental either; CT600 inaccuracy disclosure also goes through DDS or appropriate Compliance Handbook routing.

6. **Q: What if we filed an ATED return but claimed a relief that doesn't apply?**
   A: This is an inaccuracy under Sch 24 FA 2007. The tax-geared penalty applies on the corrected tax due, with mitigation under the careless / deliberate / deliberate-and-concealed framework per §27.2. Careless penalties may be suspended for up to 2 years per Sch 24 para 14 (*Anderson v HMRC* [2018] UKUT 159 provides the appeal framework if HMRC declines suspension). Amend the return + pay the corrected tax + penalty (or argue for suspension) within the DDS 90-day window.

7. **Q: When should I consider CoP9 / Contractual Disclosure Facility instead of DDS?**
   A: When HMRC has criminal-prosecution interest in the matters — typically where deliberate behaviour is foreseeable AND the conduct could be characterised as tax fraud. CoP9 is HMRC's civil-fraud-investigation track per §27.5; CDF acceptance crystallises immunity from criminal prosecution for matters disclosed. For ordinary BTL ltd-co ATED oversight (honest mistake; relief mis-classification; missed annual filing) DDS is the proportionate route. For deliberately-concealed overseas-entity structures where the ATED non-disclosure is part of a broader pattern of evasion, CoP9 / CDF is the safer course — engage specialist tax-investigations counsel.

8. **Q: How far back can HMRC go for ATED discovery?**
   A: Standard 4 years from end of chargeable period under TMA 1970 s.34; 6 years where careless behaviour (s.36(1)); 20 years where deliberate (s.36(1A)); 12 years for offshore innocent-error matters (s.36A FA 2019 reform). For ATED specifically, the 2013 commencement of the regime is the practical floor — HMRC cannot go behind the regime's start date.

9. **Q: We are an overseas entity holding a UK dwelling — does RoE compliance mean ATED is satisfied?**
   A: No. Per §18.6, RoE under ECTEA 2022 + ATED under FA 2013 Part 3 are parallel regimes that operate independently. A non-RoE-compliant overseas entity cannot complete UK Land Registry dispositions (e.g. sale, mortgage charge), but the ATED return obligation continues regardless of RoE status. Receiving an OTM letter on ATED non-compliance may trigger parallel RoE review by HMRC and Companies House — engage specialists across both regimes if the overseas-entity structure is in scope.

10. **Q: What is the role of FA 2008 Sch 36 information notices in OTM-letter follow-up?**
    A: If voluntary engagement (via DDS or otherwise) is refused or inadequate, HMRC may issue a formal information notice under FA 2008 Sch 36 requiring production of specific documents and information. Non-compliance with the notice triggers further penalties + the case escalates toward a formal s.29 assessment. The notice is appealable on limited grounds (reasonably required test); the operationally critical point is that ignoring an information notice is materially worse than engaging — at this stage the unprompted-disclosure mitigation floors are gone.

11. **Q: We've had no income from the dwelling (it's been vacant). Are we still in ATED?**
    A: Yes if the dwelling is worth more than £500,000 at the relevant valuation date and the holder is a non-natural person. ATED is an annual ownership charge — it does NOT depend on income generation. Vacant dwellings held by non-natural persons are in ATED. Reliefs (s.133, s.137, s.138 etc.) require active qualifying use; pure vacancy without redevelopment intent typically does NOT qualify for any relief. The s.134-135 demolition / conversion reliefs apply only where genuine demolition or conversion to non-residential is underway.

12. **Q: My ATED return is just a few weeks late. Should I just file now and hope HMRC doesn't notice?**
    A: File now — but do not assume HMRC won't notice. ATED returns are tracked via the HMRC electronic filing platform; a late filing surfaces. Sch 55 FA 2009 penalties apply automatically (£100 immediate). For a few-weeks-late single filing without underlying tax due (relief properly claimed), exposure is limited to the £100 + any Sch 24 penalty if the return contains errors. For a multi-period failure, the proper route is DDS to access unprompted-disclosure mitigation floors.

13. **Q: Will responding to an OTM letter trigger broader investigation across other tax heads (income tax, CGT, IHT)?**
    A: Possible. HMRC's compliance functions are increasingly joined-up; a disclosure on the ATED side may surface adjacent compliance gaps (income-tax-side rental disclosure under LPC; CGT on past disposals; IHT on enveloping arrangements). Specialist representation should assess the full compliance footprint before disclosure — scope the DDS or CoP9 disclosure to capture all material matters in one engagement rather than crystallise a sequence of separate disclosures over months.
