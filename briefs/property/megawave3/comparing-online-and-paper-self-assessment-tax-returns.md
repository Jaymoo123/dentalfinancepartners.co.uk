# Comparing Online and Paper Self-Assessment Tax Returns: Filing Modes for UK Landlords in the MTD Transition Era

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — return-filing obligation + deadline asymmetry:** Taxes Management Act 1970 section 8 — personal return. Subsection (1D) sets the standard deadlines: non-electronic ("paper") returns must be delivered "on or before 31st October in Year 2"; electronic returns must be delivered "on or before 31st January in Year 2". Subsection (1F) applies Exception 1 (notice given after 31 July but by 31 October: 3 months for paper / 31 January for electronic). Subsection (1G) applies Exception 2 (notice given after 31 October: 3 months regardless of format). Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1970/9/section/8`.
- **Primary — chargeability + notification (pre-condition to filing):** TMA 1970 section 7 — notice of liability within 6 months of end of tax year for persons not already in SA. Verified live 2026-05-27 at `https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1970/9/section/7`. §27.3-aligned where notification is missed.
- **Primary — MTD ITSA mandate (the phase-out context for paper):** Finance (No.2) Act 2017 Schedule A1 + Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 (SI 2021/1076 as amended). MTD ITSA mandate from 6 April 2026 for qualifying-income > £50,000; from 6 April 2027 for > £30,000; from 6 April 2028 for > £20,000. House-position-locked at §19.1 with verification 2026-05-22. From 6 April 2026, mandated MTD ITSA filers cannot use paper SA100 in any year they remain in scope.
- **Supporting — late-filing penalties (asymmetric impact of missing each deadline):** Schedule 55 FA 2009 — automatic £100 initial penalty for any return submitted after the relevant deadline; daily £10 penalties from 3 months late (up to 90 days); 5% or £300 (whichever higher) at 6 months and again at 12 months. House-position-locked at §27 framework.
- **Supporting — late-payment penalties (independent of filing mode but tied to 31 January payment date):** Schedule 56 FA 2009 — 5% of unpaid tax at 30 days late + further 5% at 6 months + further 5% at 12 months (legacy 5/5/5 schedule for non-MTD self-assessment; the accelerated 3%/3%/10% Spring Statement 2025 schedule applies to MTD ITSA filers from 6 April 2026 per §19.7 lock).
- **Supporting — paper-return distribution + digital-exclusion policy:** since the 2022/23 cycle HMRC has not routinely posted SA100 paper packs; taxpayers must request a paper pack via the gov.uk SA100 request page. HMRC's "digital exclusion" policy permits paper filing for filers with reasonable-excuse grounds (no internet access, religious exemption, disability impairing online use) — this is HMRC operational policy applied within s.8 not a separate statutory route.
- **House position reference:** §19 (MTD for ITSA — the phase-out context) primary; §27 (penalties + record-keeping) for the late-filing-penalty consequence layer; §27.7 (TMA 1970 s.12B record retention 5-year-from-31-January floor) for the post-filing record discipline. No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED.

The page closes the gap between the still-live statutory option and the increasingly-narrow operational reality.

## Key questions this page must answer

1. What does TMA 1970 s.8 actually say about deadlines — paper 31 October / online 31 January in the standard subsection (1D) case; 3-months-from-notice paper / 31 January online under (1F) for late-notice cases; 3-months-from-notice both under (1G) for very-late-notice cases?
2. What is the operational reality of paper SA100 in 2026 — HMRC no longer routinely posts paper packs; filer must request via gov.uk; paper-pack constraints applied since the 2022/23 cycle?
3. What is the MTD ITSA phase-out timeline for paper filing — mandated MTD filers from 6 April 2026 cannot use the SA100 paper route for tax years in which they are in scope (their reporting cycle is the four quarterly updates + EoPS + final declaration via MTD-recognised software)?
4. What scenarios still warrant paper — the HMRC "digital exclusion" policy (reasonable-excuse grounds: no internet access, religious exemption, disability), trustees + partnerships with non-MTD-supported return forms (SA900 trust, SA800 partnership), the brand-new-UTR cohort during their first filing season before online activation, in-year migration cases where taxpayer cannot get a Government Gateway credential in time?
5. What are the practical filing-mode benefits — error-checking (online portal pre-validates arithmetic + flags missing fields, paper does not); refund speed (online tax-refund typically 14-21 working days vs paper 6-8 weeks); deadline cushion (online buys 3 extra months at 31 January vs paper 31 October); agent flow (online filing supports ASA + 64-8 dual-route flows, paper requires paper 64-8 + manual processing); MTD continuity (online filing today builds the credential and software fluency needed for MTD ITSA mandate from 6 April 2026)?
6. What are the practical filing-mode trade-offs — paper offers the simplest workflow for a single-property landlord with no other income, no need for a Government Gateway credential, no software cost, and no transaction-trail anxiety; online requires Government Gateway login + activation code + identity verification, can be intimidating for first-time filers?
7. How does the Sch 55 FA 2009 late-filing penalty apply to a landlord who plans to file paper but misses the 31 October deadline — can they still file electronically by 31 January without penalty? Yes — the £100 initial penalty under Sch 55 attaches only if neither route is satisfied (paper deadline missed AND no electronic submission by 31 January). The reverse is not symmetric: a paper return arriving after 31 October triggers immediate £100 even if HMRC physically receives it on, say, 5 November.
8. What is the HMRC digital exclusion policy operational mechanic — informal application via HMRC; granted in genuine cases; no formal statutory exemption process beyond the reasonable-excuse defence under Sch 55 para 23 (which is appellable to FTT under TMA 1970 s.31A + TCEA 2007 + Perrin v HMRC [2018] UKUT 156 four-stage test where HMRC refuses exclusion)?
9. What forms does each route involve — paper: SA100 (main return) + SA105 (UK property) + SA108 (capital gains) + SA106 (foreign income / property) + SA110 (tax calculation summary) + computation page if the filer is preparing their own calculation; online: a single integrated guided flow through the gov.uk portal that surfaces the same fields as the supplementary forms but does not produce them as standalone documents?
10. What is the forward path — for a landlord currently below the MTD ITSA threshold but anticipating crossing it within the next 2-3 years, what filing-mode choice today best positions them for mandated MTD transition (build Government Gateway credential, build agent ASA flow, build software fluency now rather than at mandate)?
