# What Is Qualifying Income for MTD? The Technical Threshold-Test Definition for UK Landlords (April 2026 Onwards)

## Statutory anchor

- **Primary — qualifying income definition under SI 2021/1076 reg 20:** Income Tax (Digital Requirements) Regulations 2021 regulation 20 defines "qualifying income" for the MTD ITSA threshold test. The operative definition is **gross self-employment turnover + gross property rental income, before deductions**, aggregated across the two streams in the test year. Verified per §19.2 Wave 3 lock 2026-05-22 against gov.uk + legislation.gov.uk. Stage 2 must re-verify verbatim citation of reg 20 wording at write time per §16.35.
- **Primary — mandate-threshold timeline (the trigger points qualifying income is tested against):**
  - **6 April 2026** — mandatory for those with qualifying income **above £50,000**, tested against the **2024/25 SA return**.
  - **6 April 2027** — mandatory for those above **£30,000**, tested against the **2025/26 SA return**.
  - **6 April 2028** — mandatory for those above **£20,000**, tested against the **2026/27 SA return**.
  - House-position-locked at §19.1 Wave 3 lock 2026-05-22.
- **Primary — gross-not-net operational point:** §19.2 explicitly: "A landlord with £52,000 rental income and £40,000 of allowable deductions (net £12,000 profit) is **in scope** at the April 2026 mandate. Net-low / gross-high landlords are the largest population HMRC's outreach targets." The gross-not-net point is the operationally-most-important misconception — most landlords intuitively think of their tax return as net-profit-based and assume the threshold test is net-based. It is not. Wave 3 §19.2 lock 2026-05-22.
- **Primary — aggregation rule:** §19.2: "The two streams are aggregated for the threshold test; £30,000 of trade and £25,000 of rent (£55,000 combined) brings the taxpayer in." A landlord-trader with multiple income streams must add gross trade turnover + gross rental income; if either stream alone is below threshold but the combined total exceeds, the landlord is in scope. Wave 3 §19.2 lock.
- **Primary — excluded from qualifying income:** §19.2: employment income (PAYE), pensions, dividends, savings interest, partnership profit shares (until the partnership phase commences — currently deferred with no confirmed date per §19.3 lock), and overseas property income reported in non-MTD ways. **Foreign property income counts as property income for MTD where reported on the UK return** — the foreign-property exclusion is narrow and applies only to specific non-MTD reporting routes (e.g. NRL scheme reporting). Wave 3 §19.2 + §19.11 Wave 4 extension lock.
- **Primary — joint-property share-of-gross rule:** §19.4: joint owners (spouses, civil partners, joint tenants, tenants in common) test the threshold against their **share of gross**, not the property's total gross. Spouses owning jointly with £100,000 gross rental income test £50,000 each (default 50/50 split absent Form 17 election). A Form 17 election that splits 75/25 brings the 75% spouse into scope earlier than their partner. HMRC's published view: joint-owner threshold testing follows the income-split rule applied for SA, not a default joint-test rule. Wave 3 §19.4 lock + Wave 4 cross-reference to §24 (Wave 5 lock) for Form 17 + spouse-mechanics framework.
- **Primary — letting-agent net-of-fees trap:** §19.13: the landlord is the MTD filer (NOT the letting agent); the landlord receives a monthly statement from the letting agent showing gross rent collected, agent commission deducted, management fees, other deductions, net paid to landlord. **Trap:** a landlord reporting "net of agent fees" as gross income understates qualifying income for the threshold test AND understates expenses; both errors largely cancel for net profit reporting, but for the threshold test (§19.2 gross-test) the landlord may incorrectly conclude below mandate threshold. The threshold test must use gross-rent-collected by the agent (the gross-before-deductions figure), not net-paid-to-landlord. Wave 4 §19.13 lock.
- **Primary — limited-company exclusion (the cross-stream boundary):** §19.3: limited companies are outside MTD ITSA entirely. A landlord with property in personal name (within MTD ITSA scope) AND property in a LtdCo (outside MTD ITSA scope) must NOT aggregate the two streams for qualifying-income testing — the LtdCo rental income is the company's income for CT purposes, not the landlord's income for MTD ITSA threshold testing. Wave 3 §19.3 lock + Wave 4 §19.12 SIPP/SSAS extension.
- **Primary — pension-fund-held property exclusion:** §19.12: trustees of pension funds are excluded from MTD ITSA. SIPP / SSAS holding commercial property: the rental income is the scheme's income, not the landlord's; sessions writing must NOT combine SIPP / SSAS rental income with personal-portfolio rental income for the threshold test. The landlord with both personal portfolio + SIPP-held property tests only the personal-portfolio gross against the mandate threshold. Wave 4 §19.12 lock.
- **Supporting — voluntary opt-in below threshold:** taxpayers below threshold can join MTD voluntarily from 6 April 2025 (pilot) or 6 April 2026 (general voluntary). Voluntary participants are bound by the quarterly cycle and penalties. Wave 3 §19.5 + §19.7 lock.
- **Supporting — the threshold-test-year cross-reference:** the threshold is tested against the **SA return submitted by 31 January after the test year**. For the April 2026 mandate, the test year is 2024/25 — SA return filed by 31 January 2026. HMRC writes to taxpayers who appear in scope based on the SA return; the obligation is the taxpayer's regardless of whether HMRC's letter arrives. Wave 3 §19.1 lock.
- **House position reference:** §19.2 (qualifying income definition — Wave 3 lock, primary anchor) read with §19.1 (mandate timeline + threshold test year), §19.3 (excluded categories — companies, partnerships, trustees), §19.4 (joint-property share-of-gross), §19.11 (foreign-property treatment — Wave 4), §19.12 (pension funds — Wave 4), §19.13 (letting-agent net-of-fees trap — Wave 4). No NEW HP LOCK NEEDED.

## Worked-example data (RUN session uses these as canvas)

### Example 1 — Gross-not-net headline: the most common misconception

- **Pevensey-Estate, the gross-vs-net landlord:** holds 3 BTL properties personally; 2024/25 gross rental income £52,000; allowable deductions (agent fees £4,800, repairs £8,200, insurance £1,400, council tax voids £2,100, finance costs £18,500 cross-rated under S24, other £5,000) = £40,000; net profit £12,000. The landlord intuitively thinks "I only made £12k profit so I'm below the £50k threshold".
- **Threshold-test analysis:** SI 2021/1076 reg 20 tests GROSS rental income, NOT net profit. Pevensey-Estate's gross figure is £52,000; this is ABOVE the £50,000 April 2026 mandate threshold. Pevensey-Estate is IN scope from 6 April 2026.
- **Operational point:** the gross-not-net point is the most common misconception. RUN session must lead the page with this headline example. The Wave 3 §19.2 lock teaches "Net-low / gross-high landlords are the largest population HMRC's outreach targets" — Pevensey-Estate is exactly that profile. The S24 finance-cost restriction makes this misconception worse because S24-restricted finance costs reduce net profit substantially while gross rental income is unchanged.

### Example 2 — Aggregation rule: trade + rental crossover

- **Quenington-Estate, the dual-stream landlord:** runs a small consultancy with 2024/25 gross self-employment turnover £30,000 (well-below any standalone threshold); also holds 1 BTL property with 2024/25 gross rental income £25,000 (well-below any standalone threshold).
- **Threshold-test analysis:** SI 2021/1076 reg 20 AGGREGATES the two streams for the threshold test. £30,000 trade + £25,000 rental = £55,000 combined qualifying income; this is ABOVE the £50,000 April 2026 mandate threshold. Quenington-Estate is IN scope from 6 April 2026, despite EITHER stream alone being below threshold.
- **Operational point:** the aggregation rule trips landlords who think "neither my trade nor my rental alone is above £50k, so I am out of scope". RUN session must teach the aggregation explicitly. The combined-stream cohort is materially smaller than the gross-not-net cohort but still operationally significant for sole-trader-plus-landlord populations.

### Example 3 — Joint-property share-of-gross with Form 17 election

- **Rochester-Estate, the spouse-joint-owner:** joint owners with spouse on a 4-property residential portfolio; 2024/25 gross rental income £100,000 total across the portfolio. Default 50/50 split (no Form 17 election). Then they consider a Form 17 election to split 75/25 in favour of the lower-rate spouse for income-tax efficiency.
- **Default 50/50 threshold-test analysis:** each spouse tests £50,000 against the £50,000 April 2026 threshold. BOTH spouses are at the boundary (technically £50,000 is NOT above £50,000; sessions writing must check the precise reg 20 wording at write time — Wave 3 lock teaches "above £50,000" as the operational phrasing, so neither spouse is in scope on default 50/50; both come in at April 2027 £30k threshold tested against 2025/26).
- **Form 17 75/25 threshold-test analysis:** the 75% spouse tests £75,000 — ABOVE £50k threshold — IN scope April 2026. The 25% spouse tests £25,000 — BELOW £50k AND BELOW £30k — IN scope April 2028 (£20k threshold) only.
- **Operational point:** the Form 17 election is a tax-planning instrument with the dominant purpose of income-tax-efficient split (using both spouses' Personal Allowances + lower-rate bands). The MTD ITSA threshold-testing consequence is a SECONDARY consideration that can affect timing of MTD adoption. Sessions writing must teach that the share-of-gross rule follows the SA income-split rule (Form 17 if elected, default 50/50 if not), not a separate MTD-specific joint-test rule.

### Example 4 — Letting-agent net-of-fees trap

- **Stapleford-Estate, the agent-managed landlord:** all 5 BTL properties are managed by letting agents. Each month, agents collect gross rent from tenants, deduct agent commission + management fees + sometimes maintenance contractor invoices, and pay the net to the landlord's bank account. 2024/25 agent statements: gross rent collected £75,000; agent fees and management £9,000; maintenance + repairs handled by agent £4,000; net paid to Stapleford-Estate £62,000.
- **Trap:** Stapleford-Estate sees £62,000 hitting his bank account; treats this as "rental income"; tests £62,000 against the £50,000 threshold; concludes IN scope April 2026 with a margin (correct outcome BY COINCIDENCE).
- **Correct threshold-test analysis:** SI 2021/1076 reg 20 + §19.13 Wave 4 lock requires the GROSS rent collected by the agent (£75,000), not the net paid to the landlord (£62,000). Both reported figures exceed the threshold here but for a borderline landlord (e.g. £52k gross collected + £8k agent fees = £44k net paid), the agent-fee deduction can flip the apparent answer from IN-scope to OUT-of-scope incorrectly.
- **Operational point:** the letting-agent net-of-fees trap is a recurring drift watchpoint per §19.13 Wave 4 lock. RUN session must teach the trap with a specific worked example where the misframing flips the answer. Sessions writing must NOT use "net paid to landlord" as the threshold-test input; the gross-rent-collected by the agent is the correct figure.

### Example 5 — LtdCo cross-stream boundary

- **Thirling-Estate, the LtdCo-and-personal landlord:** holds 2 BTL properties personally (gross rental £45,000 in 2024/25); also sole director / shareholder of a property LtdCo holding 3 further properties (LtdCo's rental income £62,000 in the accounting period ending 31 March 2025).
- **Threshold-test analysis:** the PERSONAL stream is the only stream tested against the MTD ITSA threshold. £45,000 personal-stream gross is BELOW the £50,000 April 2026 threshold; Thirling-Estate is NOT in scope April 2026. The personal-stream is BELOW the £30,000 April 2027 threshold? No — £45,000 IS above £30,000, so Thirling-Estate comes in scope from 6 April 2027 (assuming 2025/26 personal-stream gross stays around £45k).
- **LtdCo-stream treatment:** the LtdCo's £62,000 rental income is the COMPANY's income for CT purposes — Corporation Tax, not Income Tax. It is filed on a CT600, not an SA return. Cross-link to A11 (LtdCo MTD page) for the LtdCo-side picture (LtdCo is outside MTD ITSA entirely; LtdCo's MTD obligations are MTD VAT if registered, plus a future MTD for CT cycle).
- **Operational point:** the LtdCo cross-stream boundary trips landlords who think "I have £107k of total property income, so I am in scope" (aggregating personal + LtdCo wrongly). Sessions writing must teach the boundary — the LtdCo's income is the company's income, NOT the landlord's, for MTD ITSA threshold purposes.

### Example 6 — SIPP / SSAS pension-held property exclusion

- **Underbrook-Estate, the SIPP-and-personal landlord:** holds 1 BTL property personally (gross rental £28,000 in 2024/25); also has a self-administered SIPP that holds 1 commercial property let to a connected business (gross rental to the SIPP £35,000 in the same period).
- **Threshold-test analysis:** the PERSONAL stream is the only stream tested. £28,000 personal-stream gross is BELOW the £50,000 April 2026 threshold AND BELOW the £30,000 April 2027 threshold; Underbrook-Estate is NOT in scope April 2026 or April 2027. AT the £20,000 April 2028 threshold, the £28,000 personal-stream IS above; Underbrook-Estate comes in scope from 6 April 2028.
- **SIPP-stream treatment:** the SIPP's £35,000 rental is the SCHEME's income, not Underbrook-Estate's. Pension-fund trustees are excluded from MTD ITSA per §19.12 Wave 4 lock. The SIPP files via the scheme's own reporting framework (annual SIPP / SSAS administrator filings, not the landlord's SA return).
- **Operational point:** the SIPP / SSAS cross-stream boundary is a less common but operationally similar pattern to the LtdCo cross-stream boundary. The pension fund is a separate taxpayer for tax purposes; its rental income is not part of the landlord's qualifying-income test.

## FAQ expansion (RUN session polishes prose; 10-12 FAQs target)

1. **Q: What is qualifying income for MTD ITSA?**
   A: Qualifying income under SI 2021/1076 regulation 20 is gross self-employment turnover plus gross property rental income, BEFORE deductions, aggregated across the two streams in the test year. The threshold figure (£50,000 from April 2026, £30,000 from April 2027, £20,000 from April 2028) is the GROSS figure, not the net profit after expenses.

2. **Q: Why is the gross-not-net point so important?**
   A: It is the single most common misconception about the MTD ITSA threshold test. A landlord with £52,000 gross rental income and £40,000 of allowable deductions (£12,000 net profit) is IN scope at the April 2026 mandate, despite the £12,000 net profit being well below the £50,000 figure. The S24 mortgage-interest restriction makes this worse because S24-restricted finance costs reduce net profit substantially while leaving gross rental income unchanged.

3. **Q: How does aggregation work if I have both trade and rental income?**
   A: SI 2021/1076 regulation 20 aggregates the two streams for the threshold test. £30,000 of self-employment trade turnover + £25,000 of rental gross = £55,000 combined qualifying income. Either stream alone may be below the threshold but the combined total triggers the mandate. The aggregation rule trips landlords who think "neither my trade nor my rental alone is above the threshold, so I am out of scope".

4. **Q: What is excluded from qualifying income?**
   A: Employment income (PAYE), pensions, dividends, savings interest, partnership profit shares (until the partnership phase commences — currently deferred with no confirmed date), and overseas property income reported via specific non-MTD routes (e.g. via NRL scheme reporting). Foreign property income generally counts as property income for MTD where reported on the UK return; the foreign-property exclusion is narrow.

5. **Q: What is the threshold timeline?**
   A: From 6 April 2026, mandatory for those with qualifying income above £50,000, tested against the 2024/25 SA return. From 6 April 2027, mandatory for those above £30,000, tested against the 2025/26 SA return. From 6 April 2028, mandatory for those above £20,000, tested against the 2026/27 SA return.

6. **Q: How does joint-property ownership affect the threshold test?**
   A: Each owner tests their SHARE of gross rental income against the threshold, not the property's total gross. Default 50/50 split applies absent a Form 17 election; a Form 17 election that splits 75/25 (for example) brings the 75% spouse into scope earlier than the 25% spouse. HMRC's published view is that joint-owner threshold testing follows the SA income-split rule, not a separate joint-test rule.

7. **Q: What is the letting-agent net-of-fees trap?**
   A: A landlord using a letting agent receives monthly statements showing gross rent collected, agent commission and management fees deducted, and net paid to the landlord. The MTD ITSA threshold test must use the GROSS rent collected by the agent, not the net paid to the landlord. Reporting "net of agent fees" as gross income understates qualifying income and may incorrectly conclude below the threshold for borderline landlords.

8. **Q: What if I have personal-name property AND a property limited company?**
   A: The personal-stream rental is tested against the MTD ITSA threshold. The LtdCo's rental income is the COMPANY's income for Corporation Tax purposes (filed on a CT600) and is NOT aggregated with the personal stream for MTD ITSA threshold testing. Cross-link our LtdCo MTD page for the company-side picture.

9. **Q: What if I have a SIPP / SSAS holding commercial property?**
   A: Pension fund trustees are excluded from MTD ITSA. The SIPP / SSAS rental income is the SCHEME's income, not the landlord's. It is not aggregated with personal-portfolio rental for the threshold test. The landlord with both personal portfolio + SIPP-held property tests only the personal-portfolio gross against the mandate threshold.

10. **Q: How does foreign property income count?**
    A: Foreign property income counts as property income for MTD where reported on the UK return (which is the most common scenario for UK-tax-resident landlords with overseas property). The narrow exclusion is for specific non-MTD reporting routes (e.g. where the income is reported via the NRL scheme for non-resident landlords) — verify the specific reporting route at write time per §19.11 Wave 4 lock.

11. **Q: Can I opt into MTD ITSA voluntarily if I am below the threshold?**
    A: Yes. Voluntary participation is available from 6 April 2025 (pilot) or 6 April 2026 (general voluntary). Voluntary participants are bound by the quarterly cycle and the FA 2021 Schedule 24 + Spring Statement 2025 penalty regimes. Voluntary opt-in is most useful for landlords who anticipate crossing the threshold soon and want to test the cycle in a planning window before the mandate bites.

12. **Q: I have heard about a £10,000 threshold — what is that?**
    A: The £10,000 figure was the ORIGINAL 2018 MTD ITSA design threshold. It was abandoned in late 2022 / early 2023 under the previous government; the phased £50,000 / £30,000 / £20,000 schedule was announced 19 December 2022. The £10,000 was never implemented. Any source citing the £10,000 threshold is stale.
