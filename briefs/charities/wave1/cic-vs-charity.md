## House positions touched

- **HP 22 (a CIC is not a charity), copied exactly:** "CICs get no charity tax reliefs (no Gift Aid on income, no charity rate relief) and pay corporation tax normally. They are regulated by the Office of the Regulator of Community Interest Companies (based at Companies House), not the Charity Commission." Citation: https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 23 (asset lock and dividend caps), copied exactly:** "CICs have a statutory asset lock; CICs limited by shares face dividend/performance caps. The CIC guidance pack chapters ('Asset Locked Body', 'Shares & Dividend Cap') are the citation anchor." Citation: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-how-to-form-a-cic (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 25 (structure choice), copied exactly:** "A CIO registers only with the Charity Commission (no Companies House), which is its headline advantage over a charitable company, which must register with both." Citation: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charity-types-how-to-choose-a-structure (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 14 (the relief a CIC forgoes), copied exactly:** "A charity or CASC claims 25p for every £1 donated." Citation: https://www.gov.uk/claim-gift-aid (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 21 (the other relief a CIC forgoes):** charitable rate relief gives up to 80% off the business rates bill, discretionary top-up to 100%. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/apply-for-business-rate-relief/charitable-rate-relief (verified 2026-07-11).
- Consistency rule that binds this page: "Never imply a CIC can claim Gift Aid or charity rate relief (positions 22-23 vs 14/21)." This page exists to state that difference; get it exactly right.
