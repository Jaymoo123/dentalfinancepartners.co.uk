## House positions touched

- HP 22: "CICs get no charity tax reliefs (no Gift Aid on income, no charity rate relief) and pay corporation tax normally. They are regulated by the Office of the Regulator of Community Interest Companies (based at Companies House), not the Charity Commission." https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies
- HP 23: "CICs have a statutory asset lock; CICs limited by shares face dividend/performance caps." Citation anchor: CIC guidance pack chapters ("Asset Locked Body", "Shares & Dividend Cap"), https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-how-to-form-a-cic
- HP 24: "Every CIC files a CIC34 community interest company report alongside its accounts at Companies House (online or by post), with a filing fee." FLAG: fee amount NOT verified; do not state a fee figure on-site until confirmed. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-business-activities
- HP 12 (where trading touches): small trading exemption is a CHARITY relief; three-tier limits "£8,000 / 25% of income / £80,000 cap, with the £32,000 and £320,000 tier boundaries". https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading (use only to contrast: CICs get no such exemption)
- HP 13 (where structures touch): "when the subsidiary donates its profits to the parent charity there is no corporation tax due on those payments." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading

Consistency rules: NEVER imply a CIC can claim Gift Aid or charity rate relief (HP 22-23 vs 14/21; explicit writer rule).
