## House positions touched

- HP 22: "CICs get no charity tax reliefs (no Gift Aid on income, no charity rate relief) and pay corporation tax normally." https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies
- HP 23: "CICs have a statutory asset lock; CICs limited by shares face dividend/performance caps." https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-how-to-form-a-cic
- HP 25: "A CIO registers only with the Charity Commission (no Companies House), which is its headline advantage over a charitable company, which must register with both." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charity-types-how-to-choose-a-structure
- HP 12: small trading exemption (charities only): "charity gross annual income under £32,000 → max £8,000 trading turnover; £32,000 to £320,000 → max 25% of total income; over £320,000 → max £80,000. Exceed the limit and tax is due on ALL profits of that trade, not just the excess." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading
- HP 13: "when the subsidiary donates its profits to the parent charity there is no corporation tax due on those payments (corporate Gift Aid; the company donates without deducting tax)." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading

Consistency rules: never imply a CIC (or non-charity social enterprise) can claim Gift Aid or charity rate relief; small trading exemption always stated with the £32,000/£320,000 boundaries.
