## House positions touched

- HP 22: "CICs get no charity tax reliefs (no Gift Aid on income, no charity rate relief) and pay corporation tax normally. They are regulated by the Office of the Regulator of Community Interest Companies (based at Companies House), not the Charity Commission." — https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies
- HP 23: "CICs have a statutory asset lock; CICs limited by shares face dividend/performance caps. The CIC guidance pack chapters ('Asset Locked Body', 'Shares & Dividend Cap') are the citation anchor." — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-how-to-form-a-cic
- HP 24: "Every CIC files a CIC34 community interest company report alongside its accounts at Companies House (online or by post), with a filing fee." FLAG: do not state the fee amount. — https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-business-activities
- HP 25: "A CIO registers only with the Charity Commission (no Companies House), which is its headline advantage over a charitable company, which must register with both." — https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charity-types-how-to-choose-a-structure
- Consistency rule: never imply a CIC can claim Gift Aid or charity rate relief (positions 22-23 vs 14/21).
