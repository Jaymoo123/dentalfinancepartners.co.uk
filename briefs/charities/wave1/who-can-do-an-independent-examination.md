## House positions touched

- **HP 3:** "Once gross income exceeds £25,000, trustees must arrange external scrutiny: an independent examination or an audit. At or below £25,000 the Charities Act requires no external scrutiny (governing document can still impose one)." Citation: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/independent-examination-of-charity-accounts-trustees-cc31 (verified 2026-07-11: "Where a charity's annual income is over £25,000...").
- **HP 5:** "Where gross income exceeds £250,000, the independent examiner must be a member of a body listed in the Charities Act: ICAEW, ICAS, ICAI, ACCA, AAPA, AAT, AIA, CIMA, the Chartered Governance Institute, CIPFA, ACIE, IFA, CPAA. At or below £250,000, any independent person with the requisite ability and practical experience may examine." Citation: CC31 full guidance (verified 2026-07-11; full list of 13 bodies confirmed).
- **HP 4** (audit boundary, brief mention): "A statutory audit is mandatory above these gates; independent examination is not permitted (save exceptional Commission-approved cases)." Income over £1m, OR income over £250,000 AND gross assets over £3.26m. Citation: CC31 full guidance (verified 2026-07-11).
- **HP 26:** "Scottish charities are regulated by OSCR... Site behaviour regardless: E&W default, Scotland flagged, never silently mixed." Citation: https://www.oscr.org.uk/ (verified 2026-07-11).
- Consistency rule: thresholds in positions 1-6 are the single source of truth; every page quoting a scrutiny figure links its gov.uk page.
