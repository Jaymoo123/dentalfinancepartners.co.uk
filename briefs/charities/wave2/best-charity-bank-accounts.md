Do you need a charity bank account, and when (BLUF 40-60w): a charity should hold funds in an account in the charity's name, usually opened before or soon after registration; trustees are responsible for safeguarding funds, and most banks require the governing document, the charity number where registered, and ID for signatories.
2. What makes a charity account different from a personal or business account (dual authorisation, multiple signatories, non-profit terms, trustee accountability).

5. CICs and unincorporated groups (CICs open a business account, not a charity account, as they are not charities; small unincorporated groups may face extra checks) — flag.
