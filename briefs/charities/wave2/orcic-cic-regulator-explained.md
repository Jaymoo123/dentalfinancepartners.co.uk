## Required structure (H2 skeleton)

1. The short answer (BLUF 40-60w): CICs are regulated by the Office of the Regulator of Community Interest Companies, an independent office based at Companies House. It approves CIC formation, applies the community-interest test, and enforces the asset lock. It is NOT the Charity Commission and a CIC is not a charity.
2. Where the Regulator sits and what it is (light-touch, based at Companies House).

4. The community-interest test (what it means; the Regulator must be satisfied a reasonable person would consider the activities benefit the community).
5. The CIC Regulator vs the Charity Commission (the disambiguation that drives most searches) — table.
6. When and how the Regulator can intervene (complaints, enforcement powers at a high level).
