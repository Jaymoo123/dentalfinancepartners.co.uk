## House positions touched (figures map)

- **HP 22** (CIC gets no charity tax reliefs — no Gift Aid, no charity rate relief; pays Corporation Tax normally; regulated by ORCIC): https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies — §2, §5.
- **HP 23** (asset lock + dividend cap — the funder-reassurance and community-shares context): https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-how-to-form-a-cic — §2, §3.
- **HP 24** (CIC34 community interest report — the funder-facing accountability document; fee UNVERIFIED): https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/community-interest-companies-business-activities — §4.
- **HP 13** (trading-subsidiary donation to a parent charity escapes Corporation Tax — only where a connected charity structure exists; describe as an option, do not imply a bare CIC gets it): https://www.gov.uk/guidance/charities-and-trading — §3.
- **HP 14** (Gift Aid 25p/£1 — reference for why CICs are at a fundraising disadvantage vs charities): https://www.gov.uk/claim-gift-aid — §2.

## HP GAPS (FLAG, do not invent)

- **Taxability of specific grants to a CIC**: HP does not lock grant-tax treatment. §5 must stay principle-level (grants that are genuine gifts vs grants that are consideration for a supply are treated differently; specifics depend on the grant terms). Do NOT assert a blanket "grants are tax-free" or "grants are taxable". Steer to advice; cite HP 22 for the general Corporation-tax-normally position.
- **Named grant funds / eligibility of specific funders**: do NOT name or endorse specific grant schemes (they change and eligibility varies). Describe categories only.
- **CIC34 fee (HP open flag 2)**: no fee figure.
- **Community-shares regulatory detail**: describe as an option for CICs limited by shares; do NOT assert offer thresholds or FCA specifics from memory.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Never imply a CIC can claim Gift Aid or receive Gift Aid on donations (HP 22 — the core fundraising distinction).
- Never imply a bare CIC gets the trading-subsidiary Corporation-tax exemption; that applies to a subsidiary donating UP to a parent charity (HP 13).
- Do not name specific live grant programmes or state their amounts.
- Do not assert grant income is always/never taxable.
