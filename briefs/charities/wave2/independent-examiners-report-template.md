## House positions touched (figures map)

- **HP 3** (independent examination required once gross income exceeds £25,000; at/below, no external scrutiny required by the Act): https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/independent-examination-of-charity-accounts-trustees-cc31 — §1-2.
- **HP 4** (audit mandatory over £1m income, OR over £250,000 income AND gross assets over £3.26m — the ceiling above which IE is not permitted): CC31 full guidance — §2, §6.
- **HP 5** (over £250,000 the examiner must belong to one of the 13 listed bodies): CC31 — §5.
- **HP 26** (Scotland = OSCR, different scrutiny regime; the report content differs — flag, do not detail): https://www.oscr.org.uk/

## HP GAPS / OPEN FLAGS (FLAG, do not invent)

- **The prescribed statements' exact wording**: HP does not lock the verbatim statutory report content. The report's required elements come from the Charities (Accounts and Reports) Regulations and the Commission's CC32 examiners' directions. Describe the required sections from CC31's framing; do NOT fabricate a verbatim legal template. Verify the exact prescribed statements at CC32 (PDF, HP 5 notes it was not re-parsed): https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/independent-examination-of-charity-accounts-examiners-cc32 — the worked example must be labelled illustrative and cross-checked against CC32 before publishing.
- **OSCR report differences (HP open flag 3)**: do not assert Scottish report content; flag + link OSCR.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do NOT present the worked example as a legally sufficient fill-in template; label it illustrative and steer users to CC32/their examiner. The prescribed statements must be verified against CC32, not written from memory.
- Do not state that the report is filed separately with the Commission — it forms part of the accounts (verify filing mechanics via HP 2 / annual-return blog before asserting).
- Over £250,000, do not suggest any competent person can examine (HP 5 — listed bodies only).
