## 5. Structural Ideas Worth Stealing

### 5.1 TTCA: Split VAT Reverse Charge Into Three Targeted Pages

TTCA's approach of creating three distinct pages for VAT domestic reverse charge — (1) overview, (2) contractor/recipient version, (3) subcontractor/issuer version — is the right content architecture. Each serves a different user with different questions. Our current single reverse-charge post should be expanded to at least two: one for subcontractors receiving contractors' invoices and being paid without VAT, one for contractors receiving sub invoices.

**Steal:** Create `cis-vat-reverse-charge-for-contractors` and `cis-vat-reverse-charge-for-subcontractors` as distinct posts alongside the overview, with audience-specific worked examples and software guidance.

---

### 5.2 Accurox: Trade Page Architecture With Sub-Contractor Hub

Accurox has `/construction-property-accountants/trades/` as a hub linking to individual trade pages. However, their trade page content is thin — the trade-specific intro is minimal and the body is mostly generic. **Our 15 trade pages have a structural advantage if we write genuinely trade-specific content** (trade-specific expenses, typical income structures, GPS eligibility by trade turnover, common mistakes by trade).

**Steal:** Add a `/trades/` hub page linking all 15 trade pages. This mirrors Accurox's architecture but at 2x the trades coverage.

---

### 5.3 3E's Accountants: Transparent Pricing as a Lead Magnet

3E's Accountants publish a fixed monthly price of £100+VAT/mo on their CIS page. This is unusual in the market — most firms say "contact us for a quote." constructionaccountants.uk publishes a price menu (£180-£450 per service). Both use pricing transparency as a trust and conversion mechanism.

**Steal:** If our model allows it, a "How much does a CIS accountant cost?" guide page (even if we don't publish our own prices) will rank for commercial-intent queries and generate leads. Address the pricing question head-on. This is a genuine SERP gap — no one has a dedicated, honest pricing guide.

---

### 5.4 TTCA: ICAEW + Trading Standards = Dual Trust Credentialling

TTCA displays both ICAEW membership AND Trading Standards Approved — a pairing that signals both professional and consumer-protection authority. Combined with 379 verified Google reviews, their trust stack is strong.

**Steal:** Identify which professional credentials our partner firm(s) hold and display them prominently on the homepage and CIS service page. Aim for at least one recognisable professional body badge. For a site not yet live, building Google reviews from day one is a priority.

---

### 5.5 Reflex Accounting: Deep Technical Niche Posts Build Authority

Reflex Accounting's 2,100-word retentions article (not a basic CIS overview — a specific technical niche within CIS) demonstrates how going deep on a narrow sub-topic differentiates from the commodity CIS overview content that fills the SERPs.

**Steal:** Identify 5-8 niche technical sub-topics within CIS that no one covers adequately — retentions, GPS post-2026 fraud rules, deemed contractors, CIS supply chain compliance, plant hire deduction rules — and write genuinely authoritative long-form posts. These are unlikely to be directly searched for but will earn links and establish expertise signals.

---

### 5.6 Construction Accountants UK: Published Pricing Menu

constructionaccountants.uk publishes a clear price menu with ranges for each service type. This reduces friction for prospective clients who are comparison-shopping. Even as a network/directory (not a single firm), the pricing transparency is converting visitors.

**Steal:** A "CIS accountant fees explained" page or section that demystifies what different services typically cost (even without quoting our own fees) positions us as transparent and helpful. Use it to pre-qualify leads who are budget-conscious.

---

### 5.7 Lanop: 100-Minute Free Advisory Session via Calendly

Lanop's lead magnet is a 100-minute free advisory meeting (not just a quick call), booked via Calendly. This sets a high anchored value expectation and filters for serious prospects. It also signals expertise depth.

**Steal:** Consider positioning the free consultation with a specific time (e.g., "30-minute CIS health check") and a structured scope ("we'll review your registration status, deduction rate, and whether you're owed a refund"). Specificity converts better than generic "free consultation."

## 6. Key Findings Summary

**Structural competitive gap:** No pure-play CIS specialist in the top SERP results has both (a) a comprehensive trade-page library and (b) a real tool set. Lanop and TTCA have neither. Accurox has trade pages but no tools. We enter with both from day one.

**Content gap to close before launch:**
1. Retentions in construction — CIS treatment (no competitor does this well)
2. CIS for limited companies (EPS / Corp Tax offset) — distinct from sole trader refund
3. Deemed contractors — high-value business audience, underserved
4. Sole trader vs limited company for CIS — highest-searched structural question we currently lack

**Tool advantage to amplify:** CIS-vs-PAYE tool and invoice splitter appear to be unique to us across all competitors surveyed. Market these explicitly in trade page copy and on the homepage.

**April 2026 GPS change is a time-limited content opportunity:** New instant GPS cancellation + 5-year re-application ban for fraud-connected transactions. Only BDO, TaxAssist and one or two others have published on this. A dedicated post ("Gross Payment Status: What the April 2026 Changes Mean for You") could earn early rankings before the topic is saturated.

**SERP competitive intensity by query:**
- "cis accountant" — competitive, Lanop holds a strong position; beatable with tool + trade-page signals
- "cis tax return accountants" — less competitive, My Tax Accountant, Swift, Hayes are beatable
- "accountant for subcontractors" — moderately competitive, Construct Accountants and directory sites dominate; good opportunity
- "cis tax refund" — DNS Accountants dominates (20,000 clients, 40 offices); hard to outrank on authority but can win on intent-specific landing pages
- "cis contractors accountant" — CoreAdviz holds #1; all others are beatable on content depth and tool signals
