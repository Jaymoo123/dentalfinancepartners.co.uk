## 3. Location Page Blueprint

### What the ranking pages prove

Based on direct analysis of constructionaccountants.uk/Leeds (ranked, ~75% custom), Northern Accountants' Leeds CIS page (ranked, genuine local), and CDM Accountancy Leeds (ranked, strong local signals), this is the evidence-based specification for a Trade Tax Specialists city page to rank:

---

### Blueprint: CIS Accountants in [City] - ~2,000-2,500 words

**URL pattern:** `/cis-accountants/[city]/` or `/cis-accountant-[city]/`

**H1:** "CIS Accountants in [City] | Construction Tax Specialists"

#### Section 1: Local market context (200-300 words, must be custom per city)
- Name 3-5 major live construction projects in the city (with scale: floor area, investment value, completion date where known)
- Name the Tier 1 contractors active on those projects (BAM, Vinci, Wates, Caddick, GMI etc. - varies by city)
- Name the supply chain geography (which motorway corridor / industrial zones local subbies come from)
- State what trades are dominant in that market (e.g. Sheffield = steel/structural + South Yorkshire supply chain; Manchester = fit-out heavy due to MediaCityUK + hotel pipeline)

This section CANNOT be templated. constructionaccountants.uk ranks partly because of this specificity. The accountantforconstruction.co.uk Birmingham page has 85-90% template and ranks lower despite 40+ city name insertions.

#### Section 2: CIS services in [City] (400-500 words)
- Subcontractor self-assessment + CIS refund claim
- CIS registration and verification
- Monthly CIS return preparation
- VAT reverse charge compliance
- Gross Payment Status application
- Year-end accounts for construction sole traders and limited companies
- CIS for limited companies (different treatment to sole traders)

One sentence of local flavour in each service bullet (e.g. "For subcontractors working on the [Project X] pipeline, managing CIS statements from multiple contractors is a common pain point we handle.").

#### Section 3: CIS refund worked example for [City] (200-250 words)
- Use a realistic trade (e.g. "Leeds groundworker earning £42,000 gross" or "Manchester electrician, 2 years working for 3 contractors")
- Show: gross income, 20% deducted (£X), allowable expenses (travel/tools/PPE), taxable profit, actual tax owed, refund due
- Cite the back-years angle: "If you haven't claimed for 2022/23, 2023/24, 2024/25 AND 2025/26, your combined refund could be £X-£X"
- Link to refund estimator calculator

**Why this section matters:** No ranking competitor has this. RIFT has a calculator but no worked city-specific example. Northern Accountants has no calculator or example. This is a differentiator.

#### Section 4: Trade-specific notes for [City] market (150-200 words)
- What trades are most active locally (groundworkers, groundwork + civils, M&E, roofing, fit-out)
- Any local compliance patterns (e.g. high proportion of unverified 30% deductions in informal supply chains)
- Mention of CITB levy threshold (£135k turnover) for larger subbies moving into contractor role

#### Section 5: FAQ (8-10 questions, ~300 words)
Must-answer questions for this intent:
1. Do I need to be based in [City] to use you?
2. How much is the average CIS refund in [City]?
3. Can I claim back 4 years of CIS?
4. What if I have multiple contractors on different sites?
5. How do CIS and self-assessment interact?
6. What's different about CIS for limited company vs sole trader in [City]?
7. What is gross payment status and do I qualify?
8. How long does a CIS refund take?

#### Section 6: Local proof signals (essential for GBP pack competition)
- Physical address (or nearest office) in or near the city
- Named accountant/adviser (photo + short bio)
- Google reviews count + rating (ideally with review text from construction clients)
- Any named local client testimonial (trade + city + outcome: "John, roofer, Sheffield, claimed £4,200 over 3 years")

**Trust schema to implement:** LocalBusiness, FAQPage, Review aggregate markup

**Length target:** 2,000-2,500 words. Ranking pages are all in this range; shorter pages (Northern Accountants' 1,300 words) rank but are beatable.

**Key differentiator vs template pages:** The local construction project knowledge and the worked refund example. These cannot be AI-filled from a template. Research each city's 3-5 major live projects before writing. Sources: Glenigan, Barbour ABI press releases, local council planning portals.

---

### Do dedicated local pages rank, or do national pages + GBP win?

Evidence from the 4 city SERPs:
- **Dedicated local pages rank** in organic positions 2-6 for every query examined
- **GBP/local pack** takes positions 1-3 for pure "near me" queries but the organic results immediately below are all city-specific landing pages
- **National firms without local pages** (e.g. RIFT, DNS Accountants) do NOT rank for "[city] CIS accountant" despite massive domain authority - they have no city CIS pages
- **Directories** (businessfinancing.co.uk, ciscontractors.uk) rank #1-2 for some queries but capture aggregated traffic, not direct leads
- **The GBP is necessary but not sufficient** - firms with both a GBP and a substantive local landing page dominate
- Conclusion: Build both GBP listings AND substantive local landing pages. GBP without a local page means losing organic slots 2-6 to competitors with pages.

## 4. Refund Funnel Analysis: RIFT

### Funnel architecture (5 stages)

**Stage 1: Discovery / SEO entry**
- Multiple entry points: CIS hub, blog posts, Yorkshire/London/Manchester location pages, calculator
- Each entry point is functionally standalone but all CTAs route to the same signup flow
- Average claim figures displayed prominently on every page (£1,867/yr; £3,000 average; "£7,000+ for 4 years")

**Stage 2: Instant estimate (calculator)**
- 5 inputs: tax year dropdown, self-employed/PAYE toggle, annual pay (£), annual expenses (£), CIS registered (yes/no)
- 3 outputs: estimated taxes paid, CIS deductions, approximate refund
- No email gate - results shown immediately
- Post-results: single "Start now" CTA to full signup; trust signals displayed (96% recommendation rate)
- No lead capture at this stage - RIFT deliberately removes friction here

**Stage 3: Signup questionnaire**
- Employment/work location details
- Expense categories (mileage, tools, PPE, accommodation, meals, parking, insurance)
- Low-friction: RIFT gathers data before asking for payment

**Stage 4: Expert review + submission**
- Personal tax specialist assigned
- RIFT handles all HMRC correspondence (using 64-8 authorisation form)
- 4-10 week processing window
- Flat fee: £354 inc VAT (or £300 for CIS-only without PAYE element - per location page data)

**Stage 5: Retention + referral**
- "All aftercare included" year-round
- RIFT Rapid Refund: 50% paid upfront (premium option)
- Refer a Friend: £50 per referral + VIP club (£150 bonus per 5 referrals, winners gallery)
- MyRIFT app/portal for repeat customers

### Trust devices deployed (in priority order by page prominence)

| Device | Data point |
|---|---|
| Volume social proof | "150,000+ people helped since 1999, £400m+ claimed back" |
| Recommendation rate | "96% of our CIS customers recommend us" |
| Success rate | "95% of CIS customers get a refund" |
| Professional credentials | AAT + ACCA logos; Institute of Customer Service ServiceMark (first in tax sector) |
| Guarantee | RIFT Guarantee: if HMRC reclaims, RIFT pays - removes financial downside entirely |
| Named reviews | Named testimonials with trade/location, refund amounts |
| Trustpilot | Integrated link/badge |
| Pricing transparency | Flat fee stated upfront (£354); no hourly billing; "no charge if not due a refund" |
| Speed claim | "Instant estimate"; "rapid refund 50% upfront" |
| Named expert | "Reviewed by Helen Lambkin, Asst Ops Mgr, 12+ years at RIFT" on blog content |

### What Trade Tax Specialists should adopt from RIFT

1. **Friction-free calculator first** - results visible without email gate. Gate comes after (consultation booking, not email harvest). RIFT's conversion insight: trust the product to sell itself.
2. **The guarantee** - RIFT's guarantee is the single most powerful converter. We need an equivalent: "If we file it and HMRC queries it, we handle it at no extra cost."
3. **Named testimonials with numbers** - not generic praise. Format: Trade + city + refund amount + time frame. E.g. "Tom, plasterer, Leeds - £3,400 back over 3 years."
4. **Average claim figure on every page** - £2,000-£3,000/year is the SERP-wide anchor. Beat it by being specific: show worked examples that justify a higher figure (expenses RIFT typically misses: professional subscriptions, phone bills, home-office-as-office costs for subbies working from home between sites).
5. **Rapid refund option** - the "50% upfront" premium upsell deserves a version here. Even framing it as "most refunds paid within 3-4 weeks" addresses the same anxiety.
6. **Referral programme** - RIFT's referral engine drives significant volume in the CIS trade community (word of mouth is very high in construction). A simpler version (e.g. £50 Amazon voucher per referred client) should be built into the funnel from launch.
7. **FAQ depth** - RIFT's 20-24 FAQ questions per page addresses virtually every objection. Our FAQ sections should be equally exhaustive.

### Where we can beat RIFT

- **City-level specificity**: RIFT has no city CIS pages. Their Yorkshire page is general tax rebate (not CIS-specific). Trade Tax Specialists will own "[city] CIS accountant" organically.
- **Trade-specific pages**: RIFT has no dedicated plumber/electrician/groundworker pages. We have 15 planned.
- **Business-audience content**: RIFT is entirely subcontractor-facing. Main contractors, property developers, housebuilders, labour agencies - zero RIFT content for these.
- **Ongoing accountancy vs refund-only**: RIFT is a refund agent. We can offer year-round accountancy, making us better value for ongoing clients.
- **Technical depth**: RIFT's blog is introductory-level (1,200-1,500 words, no worked numbers). Our planned blog of 35 posts should include authoritative technical content (GPS application mechanics, VAT reverse charge edge cases, CITB levy thresholds, Ltd company CIS treatment) that RIFT doesn't approach.
