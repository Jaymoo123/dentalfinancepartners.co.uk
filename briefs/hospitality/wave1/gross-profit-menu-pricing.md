## House positions touched

- **HP 1 to 3 (VAT catering rules), for the VAT-and-pricing section:** hot takeaway food standard-rated (20%), cold takeaway mostly zero-rated; anything consumed on the premises is catering and standard-rated; carve-outs (confectionery, crisps/savoury snacks, soft drinks, alcohol always 20%). Cite https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091 and https://www.gov.uk/guidance/food-products-and-vat-notice-70114 (verified 2026-07-12). Use only to explain that menu prices must be costed ex-VAT and the VAT you retain differs by supply; do NOT turn this into a full VAT explainer (that is the VAT checker page's job).
- **HP 16 (draught relief), for the wet-vs-dry section:** draught products 3.5% to below 8.5% ABV attract £19.45/litre pure alcohol (beer/spirits/wine) or £8.95/litre (still cider), vs £22.58/litre packaged beer 3.5-8.4% ABV; products below 1.2% ABV zero duty. Cite https://www.gov.uk/guidance/alcohol-duty-rates (verified 2026-07-12). Use to make the point that drinks GP maths must use the correct duty rate; keep the detailed duty walkthrough on the draught-relief post and link to it.
- No GP%/food-cost% benchmark is a house position. Target margins are NOT a locked figure. Present them as an illustrative range grounded in stated assumptions, never as an HMRC or authoritative number.

## Hallucination danger zones

- **No locked GP%/food-cost benchmark exists in HP.** Do not state "restaurants should hit 70% GP" or "food cost should be 30%" as fact. Frame every target as a worked illustration with explicit assumptions, or as "operators commonly aim for", never as a rule or an official figure.
- Draught duty and packaged duty rates (HP 16) are year-sensitive and must be copied exactly from HP or re-verified at write time; do not round or paraphrase the pence-per-litre figures.
- VAT rate on drinks and hot food: alcohol is always 20% (HP 3); do not imply any drinks zero-rating.
- Worked-example arithmetic must be internally consistent and re-checked; a wrong sum on the flagship numeracy page is maximally damaging to authority.
- Do not conflate GP% with net/operating margin; the P&L section must keep the distinction explicit.
- No em-dashes in user-facing copy.
