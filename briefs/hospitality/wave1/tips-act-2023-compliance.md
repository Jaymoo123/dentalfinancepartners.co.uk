## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md — ONLY figures source)

- **HP 8 (Tips Act 2023), figures/framings locked:** "Since the Employment (Allocation of Tips) Act 2023, 100% of qualifying tips must reach workers." The Act (2023 c. 13) is **in force from 1 October 2024**, requires employers to pass on all tips without deductions and to allocate fairly per the statutory Code of Practice, with a **written tips policy** and **records** of allocation. Agency workers are covered. Workers can bring employment-tribunal claims. The Act does not prescribe the distribution method but requires it to be fair and transparent. Citations: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/13/contents , https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/distributing-tips-fairly-statutory-code-of-practice , https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work
- **HP 7 (tronc NIC, for the tronc section), locked:** tips paid through an independently run tronc are free of employer AND employee NIC (PAYE income tax still due); the troncmaster must genuinely control allocation, and any employer involvement in allocation destroys the exemption. Citations: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/national-insurance-manual/nim02922 , https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/e24-tips-gratuities-service-charges-and-troncs
- **HP 9 (context):** cash tips kept directly by staff are the worker's own tax responsibility; employer-distributed tips go through payroll. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work

## Hallucination danger zones

- The Act has been in force since **1 October 2024** (consistency rule, HP 8): do NOT frame it as forthcoming or "coming in 2026".
- The tronc NIC exemption (HP 7) is NOT automatic: it requires genuine troncmaster independence. Never imply "run a tronc and NIC disappears" without the independence condition.
- The Act does not mandate a specific split method; do NOT invent a required percentage or formula for allocation. "Fair" is judged against the Code's factors, described not computed.
- Do not state a specific record-retention period or penalty amount unless a Stage 2 fetch confirms one on the gov.uk/legislation source; flag for HP extension otherwise.
- Keep the NMW rule (tips can never count toward NMW, HP 10) out of scope here beyond a one-line link; it has its own coverage.
