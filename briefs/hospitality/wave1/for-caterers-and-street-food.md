## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 1: Hot takeaway standard-rated; most cold takeaway zero-rated (five hot tests) — context for the catering contrast. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- HP 2: Anything consumed on the supplier's premises is catering and standard-rated (and catering supplies generally). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- HP 3: Zero-rating carve-outs. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/food-products-and-vat-notice-70114
- HP 4: VAT registration compulsory past £90,000 rolling turnover. https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration/when-to-register
- HP 5: FRS catering rate 12.5% (includes mobile stands). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-flat-rate-scheme/frs7300
- HP 22: AIA £1m; FA 2026 WDA 14% + 40% FYA for vans/trailers/kitchen kit; sequence AIA then FYA then WDA. Year-sensitive. https://www.gov.uk/capital-allowances/annual-investment-allowance
- HP 11 / HP 12 / HP 13: NLW 2026 / employer NIC 15% / Employment Allowance £10,500. Year-sensitive. https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates ; https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions ; https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance
- HP 14: Casual/seasonal staff almost always employees for PAYE. https://www.gov.uk/employment-status/employee
- HP 25: Food business registration at least 28 days before trading (free; unregistered trading is an offence). https://www.gov.uk/food-business-registration
- HP 23 (context): Cash basis default for unincorporated from April 2024; accruals better for stock-heavy — active choice. https://www.gov.uk/simpler-income-tax-cash-basis

HP GAP FLAG: event-margin / catering GP% benchmarks are NOT house positions. Present as illustrative operator guidance only. Whether a specific vehicle qualifies for a particular capital-allowance treatment (van vs car classification) is a write-time factual check, not a locked figure.

Consistency rules: catering supplies are standard-rated (HP 2) regardless of the food type; do not conflate with retail cold-food zero-rating. Capital allowance figures are year-sensitive (HP 22). England/Wales/NI for food registration (28-day rule per HP 25). Cash basis default only since April 2024 (HP 23 rule).
