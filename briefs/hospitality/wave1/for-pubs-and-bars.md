## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 5: FRS pub sector rate 6.5% (limited-cost-trader 16.5% trap catches wet-led). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-flat-rate-scheme/frs7300
- HP 15: AWRS buyer due diligence, verify wholesaler URN, keep records. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/the-alcohol-wholesaler-registration-scheme-awrs
- HP 16: Draught relief lower duty on qualifying draught (containers 20L+); draught beer/wine/spirits £19.45/litre pure alcohol, still cider £8.95, vs packaged beer £22.58. Year-sensitive duty table. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/alcohol-duty-rates
- HP 17: Premises licence + DPS holding personal licence required (England and Wales); first-application licence costs NOT deductible (BIM61405, Kneeshaw v Albertolli), renewal costs deductible. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/alcohol-licensing and https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim61405
- HP 18: Machine Games Duty registration required before machines are made available to play. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/machine-games-duty
- HP 7: Tronc via independent tronc free of employer/employee NIC. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/national-insurance-manual/nim02922
- HP 8: Tips Act 2023 in force 1 Oct 2024. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/13/contents
- HP 11 / HP 12: NLW 2026 / employer NIC 15%. Year-sensitive. https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates ; https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions

HP GAP FLAG: wet/dry GP% benchmarks are NOT house positions. Present as illustrative operator guidance only.

Consistency rules: draught relief maths must use the CURRENT duty table (HP 16 is year-sensitive). Licensing cost treatment must distinguish first-application (capital, not deductible) from renewal (deductible), HP 17 corrects the common blanket-deductibility error. England and Wales default for licensing; Scotland has a different regime, flag it. Tronc NIC exemption not automatic.
