## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 7: "Tips paid through an independent tronc are free of employer and employee NIC; PAYE income tax still applies." The troncmaster must genuinely control allocation; any employer involvement in allocation destroys the NIC exemption. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/national-insurance-manual/nim02922 and https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/e24-tips-gratuities-service-charges-and-troncs (E24). Employer top-up payments are contractual earnings and NIC-liable: https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/national-insurance-manual/nim02935
- HP 8: Employment (Allocation of Tips) Act 2023, in force 1 October 2024, requires 100% of qualifying tips to reach workers, allocated fairly per the statutory Code of Practice, with a written policy and records. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/13/contents and https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/distributing-tips-fairly-statutory-code-of-practice and https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work
- HP 9: cash tips kept directly by staff are the worker's own tax responsibility; employer-distributed tips must go through payroll. https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work
- HP 10: tips can never count toward NMW/NLW. https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work
- HP 12: employer NIC is 15% above a £5,000 secondary threshold from April 2025 (context for the saving quantum; do not restate as the tronc rate). https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions

Consistency rules: the tronc NIC exemption (HP 7) is NOT automatic; frame it as conditional on genuine troncmaster independence every time. The Tips Act 2023 (HP 8) is in force, not forthcoming.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do not frame the NIC exemption as automatic or guaranteed; it depends on genuine independence (HP 7, consistency rule). Employer involvement in allocation destroys it.
- Do not state a specific NIC saving figure or percentage saved beyond the mechanics; the 15%/£5,000 employer-NIC figures (HP 12) are the only rate anchors, and the calculator does the quantum.
- Do not imply tips can top up wages to minimum wage (HP 10). This is the recurring misconception.
- Do not describe the Tips Act 2023 as upcoming or optional (in force 1 October 2024, HP 8).
- No fee or pricing figures anywhere.
- Do not quote E24 or NIM Direction-level detail from memory beyond the anchored HP 7 statement; the E24 HTML body was not fully parsed in the producer pass.
