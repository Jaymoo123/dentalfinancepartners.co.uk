## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 1: hot takeaway food is standard-rated (20%); most cold takeaway food is zero-rated; the five "hot" tests decide edge cases (heated for consumption, heated to order, kept hot, heat-retentive packaging, marketed as hot). Freshly baked goods incidentally warm but meeting none of the five tests can remain zero-rated. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- HP 2: anything consumed on the supplier's premises is catering and standard-rated, hot or cold, including food courts and designated seating. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- HP 3: zero-rating carve-outs: confectionery, crisps and savoury snacks, soft drinks, and alcohol (always standard-rated). Ice cream is standard-rated. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/food-products-and-vat-notice-70114
- HP 4: VAT registration compulsory once rolling 12-month taxable turnover exceeds £90,000; register within 30 days of crossing; monitor monthly. https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration/when-to-register
- HP 5: FRS sector rates: catering (restaurants, cafes, takeaways) 12.5%; pubs 6.5%; hotels and accommodation 10.5%; limited-cost-trader rate 16.5% (goods less than 2% of FRS turnover, or less than £1,000/year). The tradescode determines the rate, not the business's own description. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-flat-rate-scheme/frs7300 and https://www.gov.uk/vat-flat-rate-scheme and https://www.gov.uk/vat-flat-rate-scheme/how-much-you-pay
- HP 22 (adjacency, only if the fit-out FAQ is answered): AIA up to £1m of qualifying plant; input VAT recovery on capital spend is a separate VAT point, describe qualitatively. https://www.gov.uk/capital-allowances/annual-investment-allowance

Consistency rules: VAT catering rules (HP 1 to 3) require careful hot/cold and eat-in/takeaway distinction; never reduce to "hot food is taxed". The FRS rate is set by the tradescode, not self-description.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Never reduce the food-VAT position to "hot food is taxed, cold food is not" (HP 1 to 3 consistency rule). Always carry the five hot tests and the eat-in/premises override.
- Do not state or imply a VAT registration threshold other than £90,000 (HP 4). Do not confuse it with the deregistration threshold (not a locked position; if mentioned, flag as HP gap).
- Use only the HP 5 FRS rates (12.5 / 6.5 / 10.5 / 16.5); do not invent other sector percentages. State clearly the tradescode governs, not the trader's label.
- Alcohol is always standard-rated in every context (HP 3); do not carve any exception.
- Input VAT recovery on the FRS is restricted; do not imply FRS users recover input VAT normally. If the page covers input VAT recovery on capital assets under FRS, describe qualitatively and flag; the detailed FRS capital-goods rule is not a locked HP.
- No fee or pricing figures anywhere.
- HP GAP: VAT deregistration threshold, and the reduced-rate hospitality period that ended in 2022, are NOT locked positions. Do not assert either; if historical context is needed, flag for Stage 2.
