## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md — ONLY figures source)

- **HP 1 (hot vs cold), figures locked:** "Hot takeaway food is standard-rated (20%); most cold takeaway food is zero-rated." Five hot tests: intentionally heated for consumption, heated to order, kept hot after cooking, supplied in heat-retentive packaging, or advertised/marketed as a hot supply. Any one test met = standard-rated. Freshly baked goods incidentally warm but meeting none of the five may stay zero-rated. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- **HP 2 (eat-in / on-premises):** "Anything consumed on the supplier's premises is catering and standard-rated", regardless of hot/cold, including food courts and designated seating. Cold sandwiches eaten in a seating area = standard rate. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- **HP 3 (carve-outs):** four standing carve-outs from zero-rating: confectionery (incl. chocolate-covered biscuits), crisps/savoury snacks, soft drinks, alcohol (always 20%). Ice cream also standard-rated. Most other food for human consumption is zero-rated. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/food-products-and-vat-notice-70114
- **HP 4 (context only, keep short):** VAT registration compulsory once rolling 12-month taxable turnover exceeds £90,000; register within 30 days of crossing; monitor monthly. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration/when-to-register — link out to the VAT returns service, do not expand here.
