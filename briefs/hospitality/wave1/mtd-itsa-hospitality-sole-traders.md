## House positions touched

- **HP 24 (MTD for Income Tax), the spine of this page:** MTD for Income Tax applies to sole-trader operators with combined self-employment and property income over £50,000 from 6 April 2026; over £30,000 (2025-26 qualifying income) from 6 April 2027; over £20,000 (2026-27 qualifying income) from 6 April 2028. Affected individuals must use MTD-compatible software for digital records and quarterly updates. Cite https://www.gov.uk/guidance/sign-up-your-client-for-making-tax-digital-for-income-tax and, for the lower-threshold staircase, https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-if-youre-eligible-for-making-tax-digital-for-income-tax (both checker-verified 2026-07-12). Thresholds and dates LOCKED, not a gap.
- **HP 25 (cash basis default), one-line link only:** cash basis is the default for unincorporated businesses from 6 April 2024; MTD does not change the accounting-method choice. Cite https://www.gov.uk/simpler-income-tax-cash-basis (verified 2026-07-12). Keep the method discussion on the cash-basis-vs-accruals post; link, do not duplicate.
- **HP 23 (corporation tax) for the limited-company section:** companies are outside MTD for Income Tax; do not state a CT-MTD date (MTD for Corporation Tax has no confirmed mandation date in HP). Only use HP 23 to confirm companies pay CT (19%/25% with marginal relief) and are a separate regime. Cite https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates (verified 2026-07-12).
