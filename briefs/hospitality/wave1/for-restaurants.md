## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md; gov.uk URLs below)

- HP 1: Hot takeaway food standard-rated (20%); most cold takeaway food zero-rated (five hot tests). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- HP 2: Anything consumed on the premises is catering and standard-rated (incl. designated seating). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/catering-takeaway-food-and-vat-notice-7091
- HP 3: Zero-rating carve-outs (confectionery, savoury snacks, soft drinks, alcohol always 20%; ice cream standard-rated). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/food-products-and-vat-notice-70114
- HP 5: FRS catering sector rate 12.5% (limited-cost-trader 16.5% trap). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-flat-rate-scheme/frs7300
- HP 7: Tronc via independent tronc free of employer/employee NIC (PAYE still due). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/national-insurance-manual/nim02922
- HP 8: Tips Act 2023, 100% of qualifying tips to workers, in force 1 Oct 2024. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2023/13/contents
- HP 11: NLW/NMW April 2026 (£12.71/£10.85/£8.00). Year-sensitive. https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates
- HP 12: Employer NIC 15% above £5,000 secondary threshold. Year-sensitive. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions
- HP 14: Casual/zero-hours workers almost always employees for PAYE. https://www.gov.uk/employment-status/employee
- HP 22: AIA £1m; FA 2026 main-pool WDA 14% + 40% FYA for kitchen fit-out. Year-sensitive. https://www.gov.uk/capital-allowances/annual-investment-allowance

HP GAP FLAG: GP% / food-cost target benchmarks are NOT house positions (no gov.uk source). Present any GP% figure as illustrative operator guidance only, never as an authoritative threshold. Do not invent a "standard" GP% and cite it.

Consistency rules: never reduce VAT to "hot food is taxed"; distinguish hot/cold and eat-in/takeaway (HP 1-3). Tronc NIC exemption not automatic. England default.
