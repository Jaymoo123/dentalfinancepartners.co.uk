## House positions touched (docs/hospitality/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 9: employer-distributed tips must go through payroll; cash kept by staff is the worker's own responsibility. https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work
- HP 10: tips can never count toward NMW/NLW. https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work
- HP 11: NLW and NMW from 1 April 2026: £12.71 (21+), £10.85 (18 to 20), £8.00 (under-18 and apprentice). Apprentice rate applies to apprentices under 19 and to those aged 19+ in the first year of their apprenticeship. https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates (year-sensitive; re-verify at write time per fresh-data rule)
- HP 12: employer NIC is 15% above the £5,000 secondary threshold (£96/week, £417/month) from April 2025; same in 2026-27. https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions (year-sensitive; re-verify at write time)
- HP 13: Employment Allowance of £10,500 per year offsets employer NIC for eligible businesses. https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance and https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2026-to-2027
- HP 14: casual and zero-hours workers are almost always employees for PAYE, not self-employed; misclassification is a hospitality audit staple. https://www.gov.uk/employment-status/employee
