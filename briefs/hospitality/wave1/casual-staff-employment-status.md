## House positions touched

- **HP 14 (casual/zero-hours status), the spine:** casual and zero-hours workers are almost always employees for PAYE, not self-employed; the tests (required to work regularly, told how/when to work, cannot freely substitute, work under control and direction) point to employee; zero-hours alone is not a disqualifier; misclassification is a common HMRC hospitality audit focus carrying back-dated NIC and income tax. Cite https://www.gov.uk/employment-status/employee (verified 2026-07-12). LOCKED.
- **HP 11 (NMW/NLW rates), copied exactly:** from 1 April 2026, £12.71 (21 and over), £10.85 (18 to 20), £8.00 (under-18 and apprentice); apprentice rate applies to under-19 apprentices and first-year 19+ apprentices, then age-appropriate rate. Cite https://www.gov.uk/national-minimum-wage-rates (verified 2026-07-12). Year-sensitive: re-verify at write time.
- **HP 10 (tips never count toward NMW/NLW), copied exactly:** tips can never count toward NMW/NLW; relying on tips to reach minimum wage is unlawful. Cite https://www.gov.uk/tips-at-work (verified 2026-07-12). This is a locked consistency rule; state it explicitly.
- **HP 12 (employer NIC), copied exactly:** employer NIC is 15% above the secondary threshold of £5,000/year (£96/week, £417/month) from April 2025, same in 2026-27. Cite https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions/rates-and-allowances-national-insurance-contributions (verified 2026-07-12). Year-sensitive.
- **HP 13 (Employment Allowance), copied exactly:** Employment Allowance of £10,500/year offsets employer NIC for eligible businesses. Cite https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance (verified 2026-07-12).
