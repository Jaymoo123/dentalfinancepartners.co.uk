# Wealth Management and Investment Planning for Dentists (UK)

## Statutory anchor

Primary education anchors (tax treatment, not product):

- **ISA:** Income Tax Act 2007 s.694 and Individual Savings Account Regulations
  1998 (SI 1998/1870, as amended); confirmed annual subscription limit
  **£20,000 for 2026/27** (gov.uk/individual-savings-accounts, verified
  2026-07-09). Income and gains inside an ISA are exempt from UK income tax
  and CGT.
- **CGT annual exempt amount:** **£3,000 per individual** (verified at
  gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/allowances, 2026-07-09). No year-specific figure
  found for 2026/27 in the fetched page; gov.uk currently states £3,000 as the
  current allowance. Stage 2 must confirm whether £3,000 holds for 2026/27 or
  whether a further cut has been legislated.
- **Personal Savings Allowance:** **£1,000 basic rate / £500 higher rate**
  (gov.uk/apply-tax-free-interest-on-savings, verified 2026-07-09; no 2026/27
  change found; additional rate taxpayers receive nil). Statutory basis:
  Finance Act 2016 ss.4-5, ITA 2007 ss.12A-12B.
- **Dividend allowance + rates 2026/27:** £500 per individual; rates
  10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35% from 6 Apr 2026 (gov.uk/tax-on-dividends, verified
  2026-07-09; consistent with house position §2 verification log and FA 2026
  s.4 amending ITA 2007 s.8, Royal Assent 18 March 2026).
- **SIPP / private pension:** Finance Act 2004 Part 4 (annual allowance,
  relief at source). Pension annual allowance figure deferred entirely to
  house position §2.B (£60,000 / taper / £10,000 floor, 2025/26). This page
  does NOT re-state pension mechanics.
- **GIA (general investment account):** no statutory wrapper; gains subject to
  CGT at standard rates (18% / 24% on residential property, 18% / 24% for
  other gains from 6 Apr 2024; verify current rates at Stage 2 before locking).
  NOTE: CGT rates on other-than-residential-property assets changed from
  10%/20% to 18%/24% in the October 2024 Budget. Stage 2 must verify whether
  the 18%/24% (non-residential) rates apply from 30 Oct 2024 or 6 Apr 2025
  and confirm the current position for 2026/27. **[NEW HP LOCK NEEDED:
  HOUSE_POSITION_EXTENSION — GIA CGT rates for non-residential/non-BPR gains
  2026/27]**
- **Company surplus investing:** corporation tax on investment income within a
  dental company falls under corporation tax rules (CTA 2010); profits on
  disposal of company-held investments subject to main CT rates (19%/25%,
  house position §2 verification log); close company rules may apply on
  distributions. No personal CGT AEA or PSA inside a company.

## Framing differentiator (anti-cannibalisation)

The four existing pension pages own pension mechanics, annual allowance,
taper, carry-forward, Scheme Pays, employer contributions, and the
locum-SIPP route. This page must not re-explain any of those.

The framing is: "you have done the pension correctly. What happens next?"

Unique territory:
1. The order-of-wrappers decision (ISA first, SIPP top-up, GIA last) and why
   a dentist's tax profile changes that ordering compared with a generic
   earner.
2. Non-pension tax-sheltering for a dental company surplus (investing inside
   vs outside the company; the close-company distribution trap; timing of
   extraction for ISA use).
3. Spouse and family allowances (ISA bed-and-ISA, second dividend allowance,
   second CGT AEA, spousal transfers).
4. The investment-company structure angle (dental company as a passive holding
   vehicle post-retirement or alongside practice income).
5. FCA boundary: education stops at tax treatment of the wrapper. No
   recommendations on funds, platforms, asset allocation, or product selection.
   Regulated-adviser signpost mirrors the framing on locum-dentist-pension-
   options-outside-nhs-scheme.

This page does NOT cover: SIPP mechanics, annual allowance, NHS pension
accrual, employer contribution deductibility, or pension relief at source (all
deferred to the four linked pension pages via a "Start here" section).

   what are the CT and dividend tax consequences of eventually extracting that

   contributes to the NHS pension?
