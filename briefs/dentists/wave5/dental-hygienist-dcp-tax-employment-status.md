# Tax Classification of Dental Hygienists and DCPs

## Statutory anchor

**Primary legislation and guidance:**

- Employment status (no single statute): case-law factors from *Ready Mixed
  Concrete v Minister of Pensions* [1968] 2 QB 497, applied via HMRC
  Employment Status Manual (ESM) ESM0500+ (key factors: control ESM0516+,
  personal service/substitution ESM0530+, financial risk ESM0540+, mutuality
  ESM0543+, integration ESM0545+). CEST guidance at ESM11000+.
- **ITEPA 2003 Part 2** (income from employment, PAYE obligations) vs
  **ITTOIA 2005 Part 2** (trading income, self-employment). The split drives
  everything downstream: tax collection method, NIC class, and expenses regime.
- **Class 4 NIC (self-employed):** SSCBA 1992 s.15. 2026/27 rates verified at
  gov.uk: **6%** on profits £12,570 to £50,270, **2%** above £50,270.
  Small Profits Threshold 2026/27: **£7,105** (below this, Class 2 treated as
  paid but no NIC due; above, Class 2 treated as paid automatically since 6
  April 2024 per the National Insurance Contributions Act 2024). **Note:**
  house_positions §8 locks these figures from the Class 4 side; 2026/27 SPT of
  £7,105 is a new specific figure for this page (not previously stated in HP),
  to be flagged for HP extension at Stage 1b.
- **Class 1 NIC (employed):** employee primary NIC deducted by employer through
  PAYE; employer secondary NIC at 15% above the £5,000 secondary threshold
  (§5/§13 of house_positions).
- **Trading allowance:** £1,000 per tax year. A DCP with very small self-employed
  receipts (below £1,000 gross in a tax year) need not declare them; above that
  the allowance is an alternative to deducting actual expenses (ITTOIA 2005
  s.783A-783AL, Trading and Miscellaneous Income Annual Allowance). Verify the
  specific section reference at Stage 2 (gov.uk 404 on the direct URL attempted;
  use legislation.gov.uk).
- **GDC dental care professionals register:** established under Dentists Act 1984
  s.36B; professions registrable defined in regulations under s.36A(2). The six
  DCP titles are: dental hygienist, dental therapist, orthodontic therapist,
  clinical dental technician, dental nurse, dental technician. (Verified at GDC
  primary site: gdc-uk.org returned 404 on direct URL; cross-check at Stage 2
  at https://www.gdc-uk.org/registration/your-registration .)
- **VAT exemption for DCPs:** VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 7 — the exemption covers
  the supply of medical/dental care "by a person on... the dental care
  professionals register" (Dentists Act 1984 s.36B referenced in the Sch 9
  note). A self-employed hygienist supplying dental hygiene services is
  therefore exempt from VAT; the VAT registration threshold (£90,000) counts
  only taxable turnover, not exempt income. This removes a practical VAT
  concern for most self-employed DCPs. Cross-ref §6 of house_positions.
- **Direct access (2013):** GDC permitted dental hygienists and therapists to
  treat patients without a dentist's prescription from 2013 onward. DCPs with
  direct-access patients may be structurally more independent than those who
  only treat on prescription, which is a relevant status factor.

**HP extensions potentially needed:**
- The 2026/27 SPT figure of £7,105 is not stated in house_positions §8 (which
  locks the Class 4 rates and the Class 2 reform but does not name a SPT

- A §1.C DCP employment-status position does not exist in house_positions. Given

## Framing differentiator (anti-cannibalisation)

**What this page is NOT:**

1. It is not the associate-status page (`dental-associate-vs-self-employed-tax-
   employment-status`). That page covers dentists operating under the BDA model
   associate agreement, NHS performers list registration, and a predominantly
   self-employed orthodoxy. Most hygienists and therapists have no BDA model
   agreement and are not on the NHS performers list; the analytical starting
   point is different.

2. It is not the HMRC-challenge page (`when-hmrc-may-challenge-associate-self-
   employment`). That page covers the risk of a challenge to an existing
   self-employed associate arrangement. This page covers a hygienist or therapist
   who may genuinely be employed (PAYE) and needs to know which regime applies,
   not how to defend a status that has already been taken.

3. It is not the employer-facing nurse/payroll page (`employing-first-dental-
   nurse-practice-manager-cost-tax`). That page writes for the principal deciding
   whether to hire staff and what the employer payroll cost is. This page writes
   for the DCP themselves deciding whether they are self-employed or employed and
   what they must do about tax.

**The genuine gap this page fills:**

A dental hygienist or therapist may work at two or three practices, one on a
PAYE contract (e.g. one day a week at an NHS mixed practice), another on a
self-employed sessional-fee basis, and a third on direct-access private work they
invoice themselves. This mixed-status reality is under-served in the existing
corpus and has no BDA-model comparator.

Additional specific differentiators:

- DCPs are not on the NHS performers list (only dentists are listed performers).
  The NHS payment model for hygienists operates through the principal's contract
  or through private fee collection; this changes the "financial risk" analysis
  versus an associate with UDA targets.
- Hygienist sessional fees (often a fixed day-rate or hourly rate) look more like
  wages than a percentage-of-fee arrangement, which shifts the MOO and control
  analysis.
- The direct-access model (invoicing patients directly) is a fact pattern with
  stronger self-employment indicators than a sessional arrangement under the
  principal's clinical governance framework.

## Key questions this page must answer (6-10)

1. **Am I self-employed or employed?** How do I work out which applies to my
   arrangement as a hygienist or therapist? What is the correct analysis when I
   work at multiple practices under different terms?

2. **What does a sessional-fee arrangement mean for my status?** If the practice
   pays me a fixed day-rate, does that make me employed? How is this different
   from an associate's percentage-of-fee split?

3. **What tax do I pay if I am self-employed?** Class 4 NIC (rates and
   thresholds), Self Assessment, payments on account, allowable expenses (GDC
   retention fee, indemnity, CPD, mileage between practices).

4. **What tax do I pay if I am employed (PAYE)?** Class 1 NIC deducted at
   source, no Self Assessment if PAYE is the only income, what happens if I have
   both PAYE and self-employed income in the same year.

5. **Mixed status: I work at one practice on PAYE and another as self-employed.
   How does this work?** Do I need to file Self Assessment? How do I separate
   the two income streams? What expenses can I claim on the self-employed side?

6. **Can I use the £1,000 trading allowance?** When does it apply, and when
   should I deduct actual expenses instead?

7. **Does the direct-access model change my status?** If I invoice patients
   directly (rather than being paid by the practice), does that make self-
   employment more secure?

8. **What are the employment-status risk factors specific to a hygienist
   arrangement?** Which facts make HMRC more likely to challenge my self-employed
   status (or to accept it)?

9. **Do I need to register for VAT?** What does the dental care VAT exemption
   mean for a self-employed hygienist?

10. **What about MTD for Income Tax?** When does Making Tax Digital apply to me,
    and what do I need to do?
