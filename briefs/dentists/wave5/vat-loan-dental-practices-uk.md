# VAT Loan for Dental Practices: When You Actually Need One

---

## Statutory anchor

**VATA 1994 Schedule 9 Group 7** (health and welfare exemption) is the governing
statute for the dental VAT position. Verified at primary source in house_positions
§6 (2026-06-03): the supply of medical care or dental prostheses by a person
registered in the dentists' register or the dental care professionals register is
exempt from VAT. The exemption turns on "principal purpose: protecting, maintaining
or restoring the health of the person concerned" (VATHLT2450, last updated
19 Dec 2025). Purely cosmetic or aesthetic treatment with no therapeutic purpose is
standard-rated (VATHLT2480: each case is facts-specific; the BDA notes appearance
is rarely the only factor, but facial aesthetics such as Botox and fillers for
cosmetic purposes, and tooth whitening assessed as purely aesthetic, are the
watch-items HMRC scrutinises).

**Partial exemption** where a practice has both exempt and taxable supplies:
VAT Regulations 1995 (SI 1995/2518) regs 99 to 110 (standard method, de minimis
£625/month AND no more than 50% of total input VAT, annual adjustment). Locked at
HP §6.A.

**Capital Goods Scheme (CGS)**: where a partially exempt practice spends £250,000+
(VAT-exclusive) on land or a building, input VAT is adjusted over 10 annual
intervals (regs 112-116). Locked at HP §6.B.

**VAT registration threshold**: £90,000 of taxable (non-exempt) turnover (raised
from £85,000 on 1 April 2024). Verified at gov.uk/vat-registration/when-to-register
(2026-07-09 fetch). Only non-exempt turnover counts; NHS and private dental care
income is exempt and does not accumulate toward the threshold.

**Deregistration threshold**: £88,000 (HP §6.C, VATA 1994 Sch 1).

**VAT payment deadline**: one calendar month and 7 days after the end of the
accounting period (verified at gov.uk/vat-returns/pay-your-vat-bill, 2026-07-09).
So a quarter ending 31 March is due 7 May.

**HMRC Time to Pay (TTP)**: HMRC may agree to pay a VAT bill in instalments where
the taxpayer cannot pay in full. Application via HMRC's payment plan service.
> VERIFY: gov.uk/difficulties-paying-hmrc did not return specific VAT TTP mechanics
> in the 2026-07-09 fetch (page is a general overview). Stage 2 must fetch the
> dedicated VAT TTP guidance (gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/debt-management-and-banking
> or VAT Notice 700/58) and confirm whether TTP pauses late-payment surcharges and
> what HMRC's eligibility criteria are for a VAT TTP agreement.
> NEW HP LOCK NEEDED: VAT Time to Pay mechanics (eligibility, surcharge pause, HMRC
> contact route, VAT-specific vs generic TTP). Flag: HOUSE_POSITION_EXTENSION.

**Interest deductibility**: interest on a VAT loan is deductible per HP §5.B
(ITTOIA 2005 trading-expense rules for unincorporated; CTA 2009 Part 5
loan-relationship rules for companies; CIR de minimis £2m so a normal dental
company is unaffected). Principal repayments not deductible.

## Key questions this page must answer (8)

1. Do most dental practices actually pay VAT? (Open with: no, because dental care
   is VAT-exempt under VATA 1994 Sch 9 Group 7; set out which practices do have a
   VAT liability and why.)

2. Which dental practices are VAT-registered and what generates their VAT bill?
   (Mixed practices with cosmetic/facial-aesthetics turnover above £90,000 taxable
   threshold; practices doing standard-rated lab supplies or retail; partial
   exemption means only the taxable fraction creates a net VAT payable position after
   input-VAT recovery.)

3. How big is the VAT bill likely to be and when is it due? (Quarterly: 1 month and
   7 days after period end. Example: a practice with £150,000 per year in taxable
   cosmetic turnover at 20% VAT less recoverable input VAT on costs.)

4. What is a VAT loan and how does it work for a dental practice? (Short-term
   facility drawn when the quarter's VAT payment is due and repaid from the next
   quarter's income or from a cash buffer. Distinguish from a long-term working
   capital facility.)

5. When does a VAT loan make sense versus just using a practice overdraft or
   revolving facility? (Compare: VAT loan is a specific short-term product; the
   existing working-capital page covers generic overdraft/revolving facilities; here
   focus on the fit for the quarterly VAT payment cycle.)

6. What is HMRC Time to Pay and is it a better alternative than borrowing? (TTP lets
   the practice pay in instalments; no new lender involved; but interest and
   surcharges may apply; approval is not guaranteed; uses HMRC goodwill.)
   > VERIFY at Stage 2: TTP VAT mechanics per statutory anchor note above.

7. Is the interest on a VAT loan tax-deductible? (Yes, per HP §5.B: interest
   deductible for the trade; principal not. Short answer only; link to the
   working-capital page for full treatment.)

8. What happens if a partially exempt practice runs a major CGS refurbishment
   and faces a large VAT cashflow event? (CGS input-VAT initially recovered may be
   subject to annual adjustments; also a practice may need to fund the VAT on a
   large construction invoice before partial recovery is confirmed. This is the CGS
   angle: link to `capital-goods-scheme-dental-practice-refurbishment-vat` for
   mechanics, explain here why the cashflow event is unusual and may warrant
   short-term finance.)
