## C1 — Employing your first dental nurse or practice manager: cost, tax and employer NIC
Intent: the loaded cost of a first EMPLOYEE (contrast the existing associate-hire page, which is a self-employed contractor). Build the true cost: salary + employer NIC (§5: 15% above £5,000) + employer pension (§13: 3% of qualifying earnings) + payroll + on-costs (holiday, SSP/SMP exposure, employers' liability insurance compulsory). Employment Allowance £10,500 available to a practice with genuine non-director staff (§5). PAYE/RTI registration (§13). Wages deductible (§8). Employee vs self-employed line (§1) — getting a nurse classified right. Cross-link C2 (payroll/AE deep-dive), hiring-first-associate (the contractor case).

## C2 — Payroll and auto-enrolment for dental practice staff
Intent: the operational payroll + workplace-pension page. PAYE/RTI on-or-before payday, monthly remittance (§13). Employer pension deductible, no NIC (§5/§13). Getting it RIGHT (the compliance angle in the slug). Cross-link C1 (the cost), C3 (systems), dentist-pension-contributions for the relief mechanics.

## C4 — Trivial benefits and tax-efficient perks for a dental team
Intent: what you can give staff tax-free. Trivial benefits (§13.A): £50/item cliff edge, not cash/voucher, not for services, not contractual; £300 annual cap for the owner-director of the close company (s.323B); no annual limit for ordinary staff. Plus annual staff event £150/head (s.264), mobile phone (s.319), and the REAL lever = employer pension contributions (§5/§2.B). Frame as modest but genuine. Cross-link C1/C2 (the staff context), how-to-pay-yourself / incorporation (the director extraction context), employer-pension-contributions.

## C5 — The cost of locum cover for a dental practice: budgeting + tax treatment
Intent: when a practice pays for locum cover (holiday, sickness, maternity, gaps). The locum is usually SELF-EMPLOYED (§1) or via their own company (§1.A IR35) — the practice does NOT run PAYE on a genuine self-employed locum, but MUST consider status (a controlled, integrated locum could be an employee/deemed employee). Cost of cover is a deductible business expense (§8) — wholly & exclusively for the trade. Budgeting it (cash reserve, §8). Contrast with employing staff (C1/C2): a locum is a contractor cost, not a payroll cost, IF the status holds. IR35/off-payroll only where the practice is medium/large and the locum uses a PSC (§1.A). Cross-link C1 (employee route), locum-vs-associate, ir35-locum-dentists, day-rate-vs-percentage.
