## B1 — UK dentist returning from abroad: temporary non-residence + FIG
Intent: a dentist coming back to the UK after a spell abroad. Lead with the two traps a returner does not expect: (1) temporary non-residence (§11.A) clawing income/gains realised during a SHORT absence (4-of-7 prior-year residence AND ≤5 years => charged in the year of return); (2) whether they qualify for the 4-year FIG regime (§11.B, needs 10+ years non-resident — so most short-stint returners do NOT qualify, the long-emigrant returner might). Re-establishing UK residence via the SRT on the way back (§11), split-year on arrival (§11 / B5 deep-dive), restarting Self Assessment/payments on account (§8). Distinct from B2 (leaving) and B4 (treaty while abroad).

## B2 — Dentist leaving the UK or emigrating: the tax position
Intent: the pre-departure checklist. Becoming non-resident is mechanical not chosen (§11): single year abroad rarely works; full-time-work-overseas automatic test; ties. Split-year in the year of departure (Cases 1-3). What to settle FIRST: final SA return + payments on account (§8), the temporary-non-residence warning if they might return within 5 years (§11.A — do not bank a pre-departure dividend/gain as cleansed), NHS pension accrual pausing, GDC registration, capital gains exit points. Distinct from B1 (returning).

## B3 — DFT to DCT and specialty training: pay and tax progression
Intent: career-stage pay/tax map. DFT salaried first year (§1.B — £42,408 from 1 Apr 2025, PAYE, NHS-officer-pensionable). Then forks: hospital DCT/StR (salaried PAYE, officer-pensionable, pay band uplifts annually — qualitative + date-tagged, NOT a locked figure); OR self-employed associate (status §1, Class 4 §8, payments-on-account shock §8). Transition triggers: SA registration, the payments-on-account double bill, switch Class 1->Class 4 NIC, practitioner vs officer pension. Mobility tie-in: many trainees do an overseas fellowship -> cross-link B1/B2/§11. Distinct from the existing DFT-first-year page (this is the PROGRESSION across stages).

## B4 — Double-tax-treaty relief for a UK dentist working abroad
Intent: the "am I taxed twice?" page. Dual residence (§11.C): the treaty tie-breaker cascade (permanent home -> centre of vital interests -> habitual abode -> nationality -> mutual agreement). Then credit relief (§11.C / TIOPA 2010 Part 2): foreign tax credit capped at UK tax on the same income; net = higher of the two rates, not the sum. Worldwide-vs-UK-source fork (§11). Keep treaty specifics general/country-dependent. Distinct from B1/B2 (residence status) — this is the relief mechanics once dual taxation arises.

## B5 — Split-year treatment for a dentist arriving in or leaving the UK mid-year
Intent: the deep-dive on the 8 split-year cases (§11). Cases 1-3 leavers (1 full-time work overseas; 2 partner of same; 3 ceasing UK home), Cases 4-8 arrivers (4 starting UK-home-only; 5 full-time UK work; 6 ceasing full-time work overseas; 7 partner of same; 8 starting to have a UK home). Only ONE year can be split; it must be a residence year; priority ordering where more than one case applies. Worked dentist scenarios (Aug departure to an overseas post; mid-year return to a UK associate role). Distinct from B1/B2 which use split-year as one ingredient; B5 is the mechanism page.
