3. **Why the 2026/27 rise specifically narrows the gap** — the saving was always the spread between the self-employed combined marginal rate and the CT-plus-dividend cost; adding 2 points to dividends shrinks that spread (§5).

## 5. Worked examples (actual numbers, tagged to §, dated)

**Example 1 — £80,000 profit, 2026/27 (§5, §8).**
- *Sole trader (2026/27):* Income tax on £80,000: PA £12,570 free; £12,571 to £50,270 at 20% = £7,540; £50,271 to £80,000 at 40% = £11,892. Income tax ≈ £19,432. Class 4 NIC: 6% on £12,570 to £50,270 (£37,700) = £2,262; 2% on £29,730 = £595. NIC ≈ £2,857. Total ≈ £22,289. Net ≈ £57,711.
- *Limited company (2026/27):* Salary £12,570 (no employee/employer NIC at this level for a sole director using the personal allowance level; flag employer-NIC nuance and cross-ref B2). Profit after salary £67,430. CT at 19% (under £50k? No, £67,430 is above £50k, so marginal relief applies between £50k and £250k) ≈ effective ~20.3% on this band; CT ≈ £13,680. Distributable ≈ £53,750. Dividends: £500 allowance free; remainder taxed mostly at 10.75% with a slice at 35.75% once total income passes £50,270. Net to owner roughly comparable to sole trader, with the limited company edge measured in low hundreds, not thousands.
- *Takeaway:* at £80k the 2026/27 tax case for incorporation is marginal. Tag: dividend rates 10.75%/35.75% effective 6 Apr 2026 (FA 2026 s.4, §5); CT 19%/marginal effective FY2026 (§5).

**Example 2 — £120,000 profit, 2026/27 (§5, §8).**
- *Sole trader (2026/27):* income tax (note PA taper begins above £100,000, so part of PA withdrawn: at £120,000 adjusted net income, PA reduced by £10,000 to £2,570) plus higher-rate band; Class 4 at 2% on the slice above £50,270. Writer to compute full figure; flag the PA taper explicitly (§5).
- *Limited company (2026/27):* salary £12,570; CT with marginal relief on ~£107,430; dividends at 10.75% then 35.75%. Net saving vs sole trader at this level is real but modest (illustrative low-thousands), and visibly smaller than it would have been at 2025/26 dividend rates.
- Tag: 2026/27 dividend 10.75%/35.75% (§5); CT marginal FY2026 (§5); PA taper from £100,000 (§5).

**Example 3 — £180,000 profit, 2026/27 (§5).**
- *Sole trader (2026/27):* full higher-rate exposure, PA fully withdrawn (income above £125,140 means PA = £0), 45% additional rate begins above £125,140; Class 4 at 2%.
- *Limited company (2026/27):* CT with marginal relief; the case strengthens where the owner does NOT need to extract all profit (retained earnings taxed only at CT, deferring the upper-rate dividend cost). Where the owner extracts everything, the upper-rate 35.75% dividend cost compresses the saving.
- *Takeaway:* the incorporation advantage at £180k is driven by retention and reinvestment, not by extraction. Tag: dividend upper 35.75% effective 6 Apr 2026 (§5); CT FY2026 (§5).
