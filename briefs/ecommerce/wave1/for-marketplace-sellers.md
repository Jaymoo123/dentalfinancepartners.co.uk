## House positions touched (quoted HP text + citation URL)

- HP 12: "Digital platforms must report seller income to HMRC under the OECD model rules; the rules came into force on 1 January 2024, with first reports due in January 2025." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms and https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms
- HP 13: "The platform reporting threshold (fewer than 30 sales and €2,000 or less) is a report-exclusion trigger, not a tax threshold." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms
- HP 14: "The £1,000 trading allowance: gross trading income of £1,000 or less needs no return; above it, the allowance can replace actual expenses." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tax-free-allowances-on-property-and-trading-income
- HP 15: "Whether decluttering or a side hustle is 'trading' is a badges-of-trade question, not a threshold question." FLAG: cite HMRC BIM badges-of-trade pages (BIM20205 onward) at build time; no fixed URL in the ledger.
- HP 1: VAT threshold £90,000 gross taxable sales, not marketplace payout. https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration
- HP 2: overseas-established marketplace sellers, deemed supplier; UK-established sellers liable. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vat-and-overseas-goods-sold-to-customers-in-the-uk-using-online-marketplaces
- HP 6: VAT on overseas-billed marketplace fees is reverse charge; value counts toward the £90k threshold. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vat-place-of-supply-of-services-notice-741a
- HP 21 (where registration touches): Self Assessment notification deadline 5 October following the tax year. https://www.gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment

Consistency rules: the 30-sales/€2,000 trigger is a reporting EXCLUSION, not a tax threshold (HP 13); "is my decluttering trading" is a badges-of-trade question, cite BIM at build, never a sales count (HP 15); VAT threshold gross not payout (HP 1); no named-expert authority; no em-dashes.
