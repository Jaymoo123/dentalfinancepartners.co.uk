## House positions touched

- HP 1: "VAT registration is compulsory once taxable turnover exceeds GBP 90,000 in any rolling 12-month period; for marketplace sellers 'turnover' is gross sales, not the platform payout." Forward-look test: register if turnover expected to exceed GBP 90,000 in the next 30 days alone. https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration
- HP 2: "Overseas-established sellers have no registration threshold: on an online marketplace the marketplace is the deemed supplier and collects UK VAT; UK-established sellers on the same marketplace remain liable themselves." Establishment status is the single most consequential VAT fact. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vat-and-overseas-goods-sold-to-customers-in-the-uk-using-online-marketplaces
- HP 3: "Imports of GBP 135 or less sold directly to UK consumers: supply VAT is due at the point of sale, not import VAT at the border." Direct-to-consumer only; via a marketplace the marketplace accounts for it (HP 2). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vat-and-overseas-goods-sold-directly-to-customers-in-the-uk
- HP 4: "Postponed import VAT accounting lets VAT-registered importers declare and recover import VAT on the same return." Cash-flow default for FBA stock imports. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-when-you-can-account-for-import-vat-on-your-vat-return
- HP 6: "Marketplace and software fees billed from abroad are reverse-charge services; the seller self-accounts for UK VAT and that reverse-charge value counts toward the GBP 90,000 registration threshold." (Notice 741A.) The classic surprise-registration trigger. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/vat-place-of-supply-of-services-notice-741a
- HP 7: "The Flat Rate Scheme is usually the wrong choice for goods sellers: the limited cost business rate is 16.5%, and FRS forfeits input VAT recovery on stock." Limited cost business = relevant goods spend under 2% of turnover or under GBP 1,000 a year. https://www.gov.uk/vat-flat-rate-scheme/how-much-you-pay
- HP 8: "The second-hand margin scheme lets resellers pay VAT on the margin, not the full sale price, with record-keeping conditions." Not all goods qualify. https://www.gov.uk/vat-margin-schemes

Consistency rules: registration turnover is always gross taxable sales, never the platform payout (HP 1). Establishment status governs the whole marketplace-VAT analysis; never blur UK vs overseas (HP 2). GBP 135 rule is direct-to-consumer only (HP 3). FRS default framing is "usually wrong for goods sellers", limited-cost rate 16.5% (HP 7).
