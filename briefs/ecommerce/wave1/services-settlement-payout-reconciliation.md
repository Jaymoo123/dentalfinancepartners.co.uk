## Search-intent class + play

SELLER-PROBLEM class (LAUNCH_CORE class 2) but on the bookkeeping-pain edge rather than the tax edge. The pain is real and specific: a seller's bank deposit from Amazon/Shopify/eBay is a net figure after fees, refunds, reserves, advertising, FBA charges and currency conversion, so the deposit never matches gross sales and never matches taxable profit. This is the source of the "why is my taxable profit higher than my bank balance" confusion.

Positioning is SaaS-NEUTRAL and this is the whole differentiator: "with or without A2X / Link My Books". The dedicated rivals are largely SaaS-partner firms who assume you run their preferred tool. This page says we reconcile your settlements and payouts either way: we set up and run A2X/Link My Books if that is right for you, or we reconcile directly from raw settlement reports if it is not. That neutrality is the wedge against the SaaS-partner firms (DEDUP_AUDIT verdict UNIQUE, no estate overlap).

## House positions touched

This is a bookkeeping-service page and touches few locked figures directly; the tax facts it leans on are owned by other pages and should be signposted, not restated in depth.

- HP 1 (signpost only): turnover for the GBP 90,000 VAT threshold is gross sales, not the platform payout, which is exactly why payout reconciliation matters. Full treatment on services-ecommerce-vat-compliance. https://www.gov.uk/vat-registration
- HP 17 (signpost): "Cash basis is the default for unincorporated businesses, but stock-heavy sellers usually get better data from accruals plus proper inventory accounting." Reconciliation feeds this choice. https://www.gov.uk/simpler-income-tax-cash-basis
- HP 18 (signpost): "Stock is valued at the lower of cost and net realisable value at year end (accruals)." This is the COGS/inventory position behind "why is my taxable profit higher than my bank balance". https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33115

Consistency rules: never imply the payout is turnover (HP 1) or profit; the whole page depends on that distinction.
