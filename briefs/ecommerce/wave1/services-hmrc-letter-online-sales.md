## House positions touched

- HP 12: "Digital platforms must report seller income to HMRC under the OECD model rules; the rules came into force on 1 January 2024, with first reports due in January 2025." Platforms named: Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Vinted, Airbnb. Statutory annual deadline 31 January. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms and https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms
- HP 13: "The platform reporting threshold (fewer than 30 sales and EUR 2,000 / about GBP 1,700 or less) is a report-exclusion trigger, not a tax threshold... tax liability follows trading status, not whether the platform reported you." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/reporting-rules-for-digital-platforms
- HP 14: "The GBP 1,000 trading allowance: gross trading income of GBP 1,000 or less needs no return; above it, the allowance can replace actual expenses (not both). For goods sellers with real COGS, using the allowance instead of actual COGS is usually the worse choice." https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tax-free-allowances-on-property-and-trading-income
- HP 15: "Whether decluttering or a side hustle is 'trading' is a badges-of-trade question, not a threshold question. Selling personal possessions generally not trading; buying or making goods to sell at a profit generally is." Turns on badges of trade (profit motive, frequency, nature of asset, how acquired). FLAGGED: cite HMRC Business Income Manual badges pages (BIM20205 onward) at build; no fixed URL yet.
- HP 21: "Self Assessment registration deadline: 5 October following the end of the tax year in which the income arose." https://www.gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment

Consistency rules: the 30-sales / EUR 2,000 trigger is a reporting exclusion, NEVER a tax-free allowance (HP 13 verbatim rule). "Is my decluttering trading?" is a badges-of-trade question, never a sales count (HP 15 rule).
