## Required structure

Each guide H2 opens with a plain BLUF definition (this cluster is accounting-standard-led, not HMRC-rate-led, so few HP figures apply; where a tax figure touches, cite the HP).

H2 skeleton:
1. BLUF: what revenue recognition means for SaaS (revenue recognition is matching income to the period in which the service is delivered, not when cash is received; for a SaaS business selling subscriptions this means recognising the fee over the subscription term and holding the not-yet-earned portion as deferred revenue; 40-60 words).
2. Cash received vs revenue earned (the core distinction; why an annual upfront payment is not all this month's revenue).
3. Deferred revenue on the balance sheet (BLUF: deferred revenue (deferred income) is a liability for services you have been paid for but not yet delivered; it unwinds into revenue over the subscription period; a worked example over 12 months).
4. Recognising an annual subscription, worked example (a £12,000 annual plan paid upfront recognised at £1,000/month, £11,000 deferred at month one; clearly labelled illustrative).
5. The UK standard: IFRS 15 / FRS 102 (which standard applies to which company; the 5-step model at a high level; do NOT state US ASC 606 governs a UK company; keep standard-references accurate and qualitative where unsure, FLAG).
6. How rev-rec feeds MRR/ARR and investor reporting (define MRR/ARR; note they are operating metrics distinct from statutory revenue; do NOT assert benchmark thresholds).
7. Where rev-rec meets tax (revenue recognition affects the accounting profit that flows into the corporation-tax computation; VAT is due by its own tax-point rules, separate from rev-rec; VAT registration at £90k rolling turnover as ARR scales, HP26; SaaS place-of-supply can keep overseas B2B revenue out of the threshold, link the VAT-for-SaaS post, HP26). Cite HP26 only for the VAT figure.
8. Getting SaaS accounting right (route to fractional-CFO + core-compliance services).
