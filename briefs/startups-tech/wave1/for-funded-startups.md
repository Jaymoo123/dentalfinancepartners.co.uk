## House positions touched

- HP 1: merged R&D scheme, 20% above-the-line taxable credit. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-large-companies
- HP 2: loss-making R&D-intensive SMEs keep the SME route under ERIS (86% deduction, 14.5% payable credit, R&D spend at least 30% of total expenditure). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-small-and-medium-sized-enterprises
- HP 3: first-time claimants must notify HMRC within 6 months of the period end. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tell-hmrc-that-youre-planning-to-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief
- HP 12: EMI up to £250,000 unexercised value per employee (rolling 3-year), £3m per company; gross assets no more than £30m, fewer than 250 FTE, no excluded activities, working-time test. https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/enterprise-management-incentives-emis
- HP 13: ERS returns (including nil returns) and EMI grant notifications are due by 6 July after the tax year end. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-your-employment-related-securities-ers-return
- HP 16: section 431 election, 14-day joint election on restricted securities (option grants to new hires). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-related-securities/ersm30450
- HP 21: corporation tax 19% up to £50,000 and 25% at £250,000+, marginal relief between; limits divided by associated companies. https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates (marginal relief: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-marginal-relief)
- HP 23: employer NIC 15% above the £5,000 secondary threshold; Employment Allowance up to £10,500 but solo-director company excluded (flag on founder-salary content). https://www.gov.uk/national-insurance-rates-letters (Employment Allowance: https://www.gov.uk/claim-employment-allowance)

## Hallucination danger zones

- ERIS requires the company to be loss-making AND R&D spend at least 30% of total expenditure (HP2); do not present ERIS as a default for all funded startups.
- Claim-notification is 6 months from period end for first-time claimants (or nobody-in-prior-3-years), HP3; do not conflate with the AIF (HP4) or the pre-trading 7-year rule.
- EMI limits: £250,000 per employee is unexercised value over a rolling 3-year window; £3m per company; gross assets no more than £30m and fewer than 250 FTE (HP12). Do not misstate.
- CT limits divide by associated companies (HP21); flag this for multi-entity founder groups, do not state a flat threshold.
- Employer NIC / Employment Allowance solo-director exclusion is a founder-salary flag (HP23); Scottish bands flag only on founder-salary content, never on CGT/CT.
- No IR35 depth (HP27), no agency audience (HP28), no named experts, no pricing.
