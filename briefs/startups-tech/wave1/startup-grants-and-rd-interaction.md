FLAG: the precise subsidised-expenditure/state-aid mechanics are NOT in an HP (see danger zones).

Keep this qualitative and orientation-only; do NOT list specific scheme amounts, deadlines or eligibility figures unless verified at build, and FLAG.
3. Why grants and R&D relief interact at all: R&D relief is a tax relief on the company's own qualifying R&D spend, so where a grant subsidises that spend, the subsidised portion may be treated differently. Explain the principle qualitatively.
4. The merged-scheme context: since the merged R&D scheme applies for periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024 (one 20% above-the-line taxable credit, HP1) and ERIS keeps the SME route for loss-making R&D-intensive SMEs (86% deduction + 14.5% payable credit, HP2), the historic "grant pushes SME spend into RDEC" framing has changed. State the current-regime position qualitatively and FLAG that the exact subsidised-expenditure treatment under the merged scheme must be verified at build.
5. Does the underlying work still qualify? Whatever the funding, the work must still seek an advance in science or technology and resolve technological uncertainty; routine development does not qualify (HP6, the honesty moat). A grant does not make non-qualifying work qualify.
6. Practical sequence: check the grant terms before you claim, keep the grant and R&D records aligned, and get advice where a grant part-funds a claimable project. Pointers to claim-notification (6-month, HP3) and AIF (HP4) as the downstream compliance steps.
7. What to do next: route the grant-complicated claim to the R&D claims service.

## House positions touched

- HP 1: merged R&D scheme, one 20% above-the-line taxable expenditure credit, periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-large-companies
- HP 2: ERIS keeps the SME route for loss-making SMEs ≥30% R&D-intensive (86% deduction + 14.5% payable credit). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-small-and-medium-sized-enterprises
- HP 6: qualifying R&D must seek an advance in science or technology; routine development does not qualify (a grant does not change this). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird100000
- HP 3: claim-notification 6-month deadline (downstream pointer). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tell-hmrc-that-youre-planning-to-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief
- HP 4: AIF mandatory before CT600 (downstream pointer). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-detailed-information-before-you-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief
