## Required structure / H2 skeleton

Each money/guide H2 opens with a citable BLUF answer of 40-60 words.

1. Hero + BLUF: the test. BLUF: software work qualifies for R&D relief only where it seeks an advance in science or technology and resolves genuine technological uncertainty. Routine software development, using existing techniques in the normal way, does not qualify, however hard or costly it was (HP6).
2. The positive test in plain English: advance in science or technology + technological uncertainty + a competent professional could not readily deduce the solution
3. What genuinely qualifies (worked software examples: novel algorithmic work, performance/scale problems with no known solution, integration where the approach was genuinely uncertain)
4. What does NOT qualify (the honest negative list: routine feature build, standard CRUD apps, configuring known frameworks, cosmetic/UI work, bug fixing and testing as routine maintenance, using an off-the-shelf approach even if new to your team)
5. Where the boundary falls inside one project (a single project can contain qualifying and non-qualifying work; you claim the qualifying slice, not the whole payroll)
6. Worked example: a scaling software team's roadmap, mark which workstreams qualify and which do not, and why
7. Getting it right and why honesty pays (weak claims get removed via the AIF HP4 and can trigger enquiry; then merged/ERIS route (HP1/HP2 pointers) and the R&D claims service CTA)

Worked example / table to include:
- Two-column table: QUALIFIES vs DOES NOT QUALIFY, with concrete software examples in each column (this is the flagship visual). Ground the negative column in HP6.
- Worked project breakdown: an illustrative scaling-team roadmap (say, several workstreams), tag each as qualifying / not, with a one-line reason citing the advance + uncertainty test. Label illustrative.
- Decision aid: a short "is this R&D?" checklist (advance sought? uncertainty a competent professional could not readily resolve? not routine? then potentially qualifying).

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do NOT overstate what qualifies. The negative list is the whole point; keep it honest and grounded in HP6. Do not coach readers to reframe routine work as R&D.
- "Advance in the company's own knowledge" is not enough; the advance must be in the overall field of science or technology. Do not blur this. If a nuance is unclear, flag "confirm with us".
- Do not claim UI/cosmetic/standard-CRUD/configuration work qualifies. HP6 governs.
- Part-project qualification: you claim the qualifying workstreams, not total engineering payroll. Do not imply whole-team claims.
- Do not state qualifying-cost categories or apportionment percentages beyond a high level; that is not this page's scope (link the AIF guide) and no HP gives the detail.
- Worked roadmap and any figures are illustrative and must be labelled.
- No pricing, no named experts, no fabricated client examples, no success-rate claims.
- IR35/off-payroll and agency-project framing are out of scope (HP27/HP28); do not introduce them.
