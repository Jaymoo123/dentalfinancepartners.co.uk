## Required structure / H2 skeleton

Each money/guide H2 opens with a citable BLUF answer of 40-60 words.

1. Hero + BLUF: what the AIF is and why it is not optional. BLUF: the Additional Information Form is a detailed submission that must reach HMRC before the R&D claim on the CT600. Claims filed without a valid AIF are removed by HMRC, so the form is the gate to any relief (HP4).
2. Where the AIF sits in the process (notification HP3 → AIF → CT600 claim); link the notification guide
3. What HMRC asks for, section by section (project descriptions, the science/technology advance sought, the uncertainties, qualifying-cost breakdown, contact/agent details). Walk each, plainly.
4. How to describe a software project so it reads as genuine R&D (HP6 applied: the advance in science or technology, the technological uncertainty, why a competent professional could not readily deduce the solution; and honestly, what to leave OUT because it is routine dev)
5. Worked example: an AIF project narrative for a software company (illustrative, showing the advance + uncertainty framing)
6. Cost apportionment in the AIF (staff time, subcontractors, cloud/consumables at a high level; flag detailed apportionment rules as "confirm with us")
7. Common AIF mistakes that get claims removed; then the R&D claims service CTA

Worked example / table to include:
- Table: AIF section-by-section checklist (section · what HMRC wants · common pitfall).
- Worked AIF project narrative: an illustrative software R&D project written to HMRC's structure (advance sought, baseline/state of knowledge, uncertainty, resolution attempt). Clearly labelled illustrative.

## House positions touched

- HP4: the AIF is mandatory before the R&D claim on the CT600; claims without a valid AIF are removed by HMRC. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-detailed-information-before-you-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief
- HP6: the advance-in-science/tech + technological-uncertainty test; routine software dev does not qualify (drives how the project narrative is written and what to leave out). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird100000
- HP3: claim notification precedes the AIF for first-time claimants (distinguish, pointer). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tell-hmrc-that-youre-planning-to-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do NOT list AIF form fields as hard requirements beyond what HP4's guidance supports. Describe the sections HMRC's own AIF guidance sets out; if unsure a field is required, flag "confirm current AIF fields at build" rather than inventing.
- The AIF must precede the CT600 claim, and a missing/invalid AIF means the claim is REMOVED. Do not soften.
- The project narrative must reflect HP6 honestly. Do not coach readers to dress up routine development as R&D; the honesty framing is the differentiator.
- Do not state detailed qualifying-cost apportionment rules (staff-time percentages, subcontractor caps) beyond a high level unless an HP supports them; flag as "confirm with us".
- Worked project narrative and any numbers are illustrative and must be labelled.
- No pricing, no named experts, no fabricated claim counts or success rates.
