## House positions touched

- HP1: merged scheme, one 20% above-the-line taxable expenditure credit, periods beginning on or after 1 April 2024, replaces SME super-deduction + RDEC. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-large-companies
- HP2: ERIS survives for loss-making SMEs ≥30% R&D-intensive (86% deduction + 14.5% payable credit) — pointer only, depth in the ERIS guide. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-tax-relief-for-small-and-medium-sized-enterprises
- HP6: qualifying R&D must seek an advance in science or technology and resolve technological uncertainty; routine software dev does not qualify (honesty moat). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird100000
- HP3: claim-notification 6-month deadline — pointer. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tell-hmrc-that-youre-planning-to-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief
- HP4: AIF mandatory before CT600 — pointer. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-detailed-information-before-you-claim-research-and-development-rd-tax-relief
- HP21: CT 19%/25% + marginal relief, used only to net down the taxable credit in the worked example. https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do NOT quote a pre-1-April-2024 SME super-deduction enhancement rate or the old 130%/230% figures unless an HP supplies them; HP1 does not give them, so describe the old regime qualitatively and FLAG any number you are tempted to state.
- Do NOT state the ERIS rates here beyond the HP2 wording (86% deduction, 14.5% payable credit); depth belongs in the ERIS guide.
- The 20% credit is ABOVE THE LINE and TAXABLE. Do not present 20% as a net cash benefit; the worked example must net it down through CT (HP21).
- Do not soften HP6. Routine software development does not qualify. This honesty is the differentiator, not a caveat to bury.
- No pricing, no named experts, no fabricated client examples or claim counts. Illustrative worked-example figures must be labelled illustrative.
- IR35/off-payroll: not in scope here (HP27); do not introduce it.
