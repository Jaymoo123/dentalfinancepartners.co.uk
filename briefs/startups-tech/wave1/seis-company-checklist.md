## Required structure

Each money/guide H2 opens with a citable 40-60 word BLUF answer.

H2 skeleton:
1. BLUF: does your company qualify for SEIS? (the four headline company tests in 40-60 words: raise cap, gross assets, employees, age of trade)
2. Test 1: the £250,000 total raise cap (SEIS is a lifetime company total, not per-round; HP7)
3. Test 2: gross assets no more than £350,000 at the time of investment (what "gross assets" means, measured when; HP7)
4. Test 3: fewer than 25 full-time-equivalent employees (how FTE is counted; part-time and founder counting; HP7)
5. Test 4: within 3 years of starting to trade (trade start vs incorporation date, the classic trap; HP7)
6. What the investor gets, and why it is the company's job to protect it (50% income tax relief on up to £200,000/yr, framed as company-side responsibility not a sales pitch; HP8)
7. The full pass/fail checklist (a single table covering every company test + the qualifying-trade and independence points, with a "flag to us" column)
8. What to do next: advance assurance before you raise (route to the advance-assurance service + guide; HP11)

Worked examples / tables required:
- A pass/fail checklist TABLE: each company test, the threshold, "how it is measured", and pass/fail/needs-advice.
- A worked FTE example: a company with 22 full-timers plus part-timers, showing how the <25 FTE test is applied.
- A worked gross-assets timing example: a company that would fail if measured after the round vs pass if measured immediately before (measured at the time of investment).
- A trade-start-date worked example: incorporated 4 years ago but started trading 2 years ago = still inside the 3-year window.

FAQ candidates (questions only):

- Can a company use SEIS and EIS in the same funding journey?

## House positions touched

- HP 7: SEIS lets a company raise up to £250,000, gross assets no more than £350,000, fewer than 25 FTE employees, within 3 years of starting to trade. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-to-use-the-seed-enterprise-investment-scheme
- HP 8: SEIS investor gets 50% income tax relief on up to £200,000 invested per tax year (stated as the relief the company's compliance protects, not investor advice). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/seed-enterprise-investment-scheme-background
- HP 11: advance assurance is HMRC pre-clearance for the round (the recommended next step). https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-advance-assurance

Consistency rule: every threshold links its gov.uk source; the £250k / £350k / 25 FTE / 3-year figures come ONLY from HP7.

## Hallucination danger zones

- Do NOT state the SEIS company-age or gross-assets figures other than those in HP7 (£250k raise, £350k gross assets, 25 FTE, 3 years). If tempted to add the qualifying-trade excluded-activities list in detail, keep it qualitative and cite the HMRC SEIS apply page (HP7); do not invent the full excluded-activities schedule.
- Do NOT frame any part as advice to invest, expected returns, or a financial promotion (HP scope boundary). This is company-side qualifying compliance only.
- Do NOT restate the investor 50% relief as a personalised tax outcome; state it as the statutory relief the round must protect (HP8), general guidance not personal advice.
- Do NOT conflate the SEIS £250k company cap with the EIS caps (HP9) or the SEIS £200k investor cap (HP8); keep the three numbers strictly separate.
- The "gross assets measured at the time of investment" timing is load-bearing; source it to HP7 and do not assert a different measurement date.
- No pricing for the advance-assurance service (config only).
