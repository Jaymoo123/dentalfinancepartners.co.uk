## Required structure / H2 skeleton

Each money/guide H2 opens with a citable 40-60 word BLUF answer.

1. BLUF: what a disqualifying event is and why it matters. State in 40-60 words that a disqualifying event is a change that stops an existing EMI option meeting the qualifying conditions, and that the tax advantage on any growth in value after the event can be lost unless the option is exercised inside the statutory window (HP12; window depth flagged below).
2. Company-level events: loss of independence (the company comes under the control of another company), the company ceasing to meet the gross-assets (£30m or less) or the <250 FTE tests, or the company starting an excluded activity (HP12).
3. Employee-level events: the employee ceasing to meet the working-time requirement, or ceasing to be employed by the qualifying company (HP12).
4. Option-level and structural events: variations to the option terms and certain share reorganisations or exchanges of the shares under option; note that a properly structured share-for-share exchange of options can preserve EMI status, and that a section 135 TCGA share-for-share is not itself a disposal but is subject to the s.137 anti-avoidance test (HP20) · keep this pointer short and link the growth-shares / s.431 context where relevant.
5. The tax-consequence window: what happens after a disqualifying event, and the deadline to exercise before the relief on post-event growth is lost. FLAG (see danger zones): the specific number-of-days window is NOT in an HP; describe it qualitatively (there is a statutory window after the event within which exercise preserves the advantage on value up to the event) and instruct the writer to source the exact figure to HMRC ETASSUM at build, or state it qualitatively if unverified.
6. What it means for BADR and CGT on eventual sale: EMI shares qualify for BADR at 18% under the two-year rule without the 5% personal-company test (HP18), and CGT main rates on shares are 18% within the basic band / 24% above (HP19). Note only that a disqualifying event can affect the income-tax-vs-CGT treatment on exercise; do not over-claim.
7. What to do next: reporting the event on the ERS return (nil returns included, 6 July deadline, HP13) and routing to the EMI setup service for a live case.

Worked examples / tables required:
- An EVENT MAP TABLE: each disqualifying event · the level it sits at (company / employee / option) · the immediate effect on the option · the action to consider. Use only HP12 wording for the qualifying tests; do not add tests HP12 does not list.
- A worked timeline example: company grants EMI, later comes under the control of an acquirer (loss of independence), and the option holder exercises inside the window. Show, qualitatively where the day-count is unverified, that value up to the event keeps the EMI advantage and growth after the event may be taxed as employment income. Label figures illustrative.

## House positions touched

- HP 12: EMI qualifying conditions (≤£250k unexercised value per employee rolling 3-yr, £3m per company, gross assets ≤£30m, <250 FTE, no excluded activities, working-time requirement), a disqualifying event is the loss of one of these. https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/enterprise-management-incentives-emis · gross-assets test https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employee-tax-advantaged-share-scheme-user-manual/etassum52030
- HP 13: ERS return and EMI grant notification due 6 July after tax-year end, nil returns included, used for the "report the event" pointer. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/submit-your-employment-related-securities-ers-return
- HP 14: EMI valuation agreed with HMRC SAV, pointer only; do NOT publish a VAL231 link (re-verify at build). https://www.gov.uk/tax-employee-share-schemes/enterprise-management-incentives-emis
- HP 18: BADR 18% from 6 Apr 2026, £1m lifetime limit, EMI shares qualify on the 2-year rule without the 5% personal-company test. https://www.gov.uk/business-asset-disposal-relief
- HP 19: CGT on shares 18% basic band / 24% above. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates
- HP 20: s.135 TCGA share-for-share not a disposal, subject to s.137 (only the exchange-of-options pointer). https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/capital-gains-manual/cg52521
