## Search-intent class + play

HIRE / structural page for the multiple-store owner (intent class 1 + operator-problem). The group owner's distinctive exposure the single-store frame misses: with separate companies, the corporation-tax marginal-band limits (£50,000 and £250,000) divide by the number of associated companies, so a multi-store owner with several companies loses lower-rate headroom and can push the whole group toward the 25% main rate (HP 27). The play: expose that trap, then frame the group-structure and consolidation work that manages it. Answer-first, convert to a structure-review engagement. Owner frame.

## Hallucination danger zones

- The associated-companies rule divides the £50,000 and £250,000 limits by the number of associated companies (HP 27); do not misstate the limits or imply each company gets a full separate band.
- Employment Allowance across connected companies is restricted (only one claim across the group broadly, plus the single-director bar); do not imply every company claims £10,500 independently (HP 26).
- Employer NIC 15% above £5,000 from 6 April 2025 (HP 25), not 13.8%/£9,100. Dividends 10.75%/35.75%/39.35% for 2026/27 (HP 28).
- Do not assert a valuation multiple for intra-group deals (HP 16 method-level; route to /services/pharmacy-valuation-goodwill).
- Nothing clinical; group business mechanics only (positioning wall).
- No credential claims, no named expert. No em-dashes.
