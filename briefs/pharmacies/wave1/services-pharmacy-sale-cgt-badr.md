## House positions touched (docs/pharmacies/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 14: on sale, Business Asset Disposal Relief charges CGT at 18% for 2026/27 on qualifying disposals up to the £1m lifetime limit; the BADR rate was 14% in 2025/26 and 10% before that, rising to 18% from 6 April 2026; the £1m lifetime limit is per person; timing a sale around the rate steps is real money. https://www.gov.uk/business-asset-disposal-relief
- HP 15: standard CGT applies beyond BADR at 18% within the basic-rate band and 24% above it, with the annual exempt amount frozen at £3,000; deal structuring (earn-outs, deferred consideration) changes when the tax falls due; a pharmacy is a non-residential asset for the rate split. https://www.gov.uk/capital-gains-tax/rates
- HP 13 (support): goodwill dominates pharmacy pricing; context for what the gain is largely composed of. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-relief-on-goodwill-and-relevant-assets

Consistency rules: the standard CGT rate (HP 15) is ALWAYS presented with the basic-band split, never a flat 18% or flat 24%. BADR (HP 14) is 18% for 2026/27 up to £1m per person; state the rate history exactly. The £3,000 AEA (HP 15) is frozen, never £6,000 or £12,300.

## Hallucination danger zones

- NEVER state a flat CGT rate. Standard CGT beyond BADR is 18% within the remaining basic-rate band, 24% above (HP 15). BADR is 18% for 2026/27 (HP 14). Do not conflate the two rates.
- Use £3,000 for the annual exempt amount (HP 15, 2026/27). Do not use £6,000 or £12,300 (both stale).
- State the BADR rate history exactly (10% before, 14% in 2025/26, 18% from 6 April 2026; HP 14). Do NOT imply it is still 10% or 14%.
- The £1m BADR lifetime limit is per person, not per business or per disposal (HP 14). Do not imply a couple gets one £1m; each qualifying person has their own.
- Do NOT invent a valuation multiple or pence-per-item figure to illustrate a gain (HP 16). Use the client's own proceeds and base cost, or a clearly labelled illustrative placeholder.
- Do NOT promise BADR eligibility; it depends on qualifying conditions (route to us). Do NOT promise HMRC will accept a given deal structure.
