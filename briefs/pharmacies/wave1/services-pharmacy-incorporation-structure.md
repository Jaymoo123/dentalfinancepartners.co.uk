## House positions touched (docs/pharmacies/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 27: corporation tax is 25% main rate on profits over £250,000 and a 19% small-profits rate on profits up to £50,000, with Marginal Relief between; the £50,000 and £250,000 limits are divided by the number of associated companies, so a multi-store owner with separate companies loses the lower-rate headroom; most single-store pharmacies sit in the marginal band. https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-rates
- HP 28: pharmacy owners typically extract profit as a salary/dividend mix; dividends are taxed at 10.75% (basic), 35.75% (higher) and 39.35% (additional) for 2026/27, with a £500 dividend allowance; these are the FA 2026 rates and are load-bearing; extraction is a two-layer calculation because dividends are paid from post-tax profit. https://www.gov.uk/tax-on-dividends
- HP 11: premises must be GPhC-registered and ownership changes have regulatory steps, including superintendent-pharmacist requirements for companies; cover ownership and registration mechanics only, never clinical professional matters. https://www.pharmacyregulation.org/pharmacies

Consistency rules: dividend rates are 10.75% / 35.75% / 39.35% with a £500 allowance for 2026/27 (HP 28); do not use older 8.75%/33.75%/39.35% or a £1,000/£2,000 allowance. CT limits divide by associated companies (HP 27). Extraction is always presented as two layers (CT then dividend tax) (HP 28).
