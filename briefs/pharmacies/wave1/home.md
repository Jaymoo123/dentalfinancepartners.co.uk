## Search-intent class + play

Accountant-seeking lead page (HIRE, intent class 1). Head of the money funnel: the visitor is choosing a firm, not learning a rule. Establish specialist depth fast (NHS-contract-driven income not till takings, the VAT-mixed structure, the buying/selling moment) and route each audience and service intent to its own page. Frame outputs as general tax and accounting guidance, never clinical, prescribing, or patient-safety content (regulated-advice boundary and medical-site adjacency trap, house_positions preamble).

## The three things that make this a specialist page (the wedge)

1. Income is contract-driven, not till-driven. Pharmacy revenue is Drug Tariff reimbursement plus remuneration under the Community Pharmacy Contractual Framework, and the FP34 submission cycle drives cash flow with a roughly two-month payment lag (HP 6, HP 7). No generalist models this.
2. It is a VAT-mixed business. NHS-dispensed prescription drugs are zero-rated while most OTC retail is standard-rated, so a pharmacy almost always reclaims more input VAT than a pure retailer expects (HP 1). This is the strongest differentiation wedge in the niche and the SKU-by-SKU / retail-scheme mechanic (HP 4) is the proof of depth.
3. The buying and selling moment. Market entry is regulated and the NHS contract, not the shop, is the asset (HP 10); the purchase/sale event carries the highest lead value and the share-vs-asset, goodwill, and BADR questions no generalist frames correctly (HP 12, HP 13, HP 14).

## House positions touched (docs/pharmacies/house_positions.md; gov.uk / NHS URLs below)

- HP 1: NHS-dispensed prescription drugs zero-rated, most OTC retail standard-rated; a pharmacy is a VAT-mixed business. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/health-professionals-pharmaceutical-products-and-vat-notice-70157
- HP 4: retail schemes are the mechanic for splitting zero-rated and standard-rated takings; the wrong scheme systematically overpays VAT. https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/vat-retail-schemes
- HP 6: income is contract-driven (reimbursement + remuneration under the CPCF), not till-driven. https://www.england.nhs.uk/community-pharmacy-contractual-framework/ and https://www.nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/drug-tariff
- HP 7: FP34 submission cycle drives cash flow; monthly submission, payment roughly two months later. https://www.nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/submitting-prescriptions
- HP 8: Drug Tariff and Category M clawbacks make gross margin centrally set and retrospectively adjusted; margin variance analysis is the core monthly job. https://www.nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/drug-tariff
- HP 10: market entry is regulated (NHS 2013 Regulations); the NHS contract, not the shop, is the asset. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/349/contents
- HP 12: share purchase vs asset purchase (SDLT up to 5% on property in asset deals vs 0.5% stamp duty on shares). https://www.gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax/nonresidential-and-mixed-use-rates and https://www.gov.uk/tax-buy-shares
- HP 13: goodwill dominates pharmacy pricing; CT relief on goodwill is restricted. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-relief-on-goodwill-and-relevant-assets
- HP 14: BADR charges CGT at 18% for 2026/27 up to the £1m lifetime limit. https://www.gov.uk/business-asset-disposal-relief
