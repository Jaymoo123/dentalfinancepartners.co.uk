## House positions touched (docs/pharmacies/house_positions.md; every figure maps to a position + gov.uk URL)

- HP 10: market entry is regulated; opening, relocating or buying a pharmacy engages the NHS (Pharmaceutical and Local Pharmaceutical Services) Regulations 2013; the NHS contract, not the shop, is the asset. https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/349/contents
- HP 12: share purchase versus asset purchase is the first structuring decision; asset deals attract SDLT on any property at non-residential and mixed-use rates (up to 5%); share deals attract 0.5% stamp duty on shares but inherit the company's history. https://www.gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax/nonresidential-and-mixed-use-rates and https://www.gov.uk/tax-buy-shares
- HP 13: goodwill dominates pharmacy pricing (driven by the NHS contract and item volume); on a company purchase, corporation tax relief on goodwill is restricted and only available in limited cases at fixed rates. https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-relief-on-goodwill-and-relevant-assets
- HP 11 (support): premises must be GPhC-registered and ownership changes have regulatory steps, including superintendent-pharmacist requirements for companies; cover ownership and registration mechanics only, never clinical professional matters. https://www.pharmacyregulation.org/pharmacies
- HP 7 (support): the FP34 submission cycle drives cash flow (payment roughly two months later plus an advance); context for the finance-ready working-capital model. https://www.nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/submitting-prescriptions

Consistency rules: the NHS contract is the asset (HP 10) is the spine of the page; state SDLT on property as "non-residential rates, up to 5%" (HP 12) and re-verify the current band table at build time; never assert goodwill relief is available generally (HP 13, it is restricted).
