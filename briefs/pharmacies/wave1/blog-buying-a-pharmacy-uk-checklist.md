## House positions touched (docs/pharmacies/house_positions.md, ONLY figures source)

- **HP 10 (regulated market entry; the contract is the asset):** buying/relocating/opening engages the NHS (Pharmaceutical and LPS) Regulations 2013; the NHS contract, not the shop, is the asset. Citation: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/349/contents
- **HP 11 (GPhC registration + superintendent):** premises must be GPhC-registered and ownership changes have regulatory steps including the superintendent pharmacist requirement for companies; ownership and registration mechanics only, never clinical matters. Citation: https://www.pharmacyregulation.org/pharmacies
- **HP 12 (share vs asset, first structuring decision):** asset deals attract SDLT on any property at non-residential/mixed-use rates up to 5%; share deals attract 0.5% stamp duty on shares but inherit company history. Citations: https://www.gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax/nonresidential-and-mixed-use-rates and https://www.gov.uk/tax-buy-shares
- **HP 13 (goodwill dominates; CT relief restricted on company purchase):** goodwill is driven by the NHS contract and item volume; on a company purchase, CT relief on goodwill is restricted and only available in limited cases at fixed rates. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-relief-on-goodwill-and-relevant-assets
- **HP 7 (FP34 payment cycle):** monthly submission, payment ~two months later with advance on account; due-diligence must read the NHSBSA payment history. Citation: https://www.nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/submitting-prescriptions
- **HP 8 (Drug Tariff / Category M margin):** margin is set centrally and adjusted retrospectively; the margin story is a due-diligence item, not just the P&L. Citation: https://www.nhsbsa.nhs.uk/pharmacies-gp-practices-and-appliance-contractors/drug-tariff
- **HP 1 (VAT-mixed business):** NHS-dispensed drugs zero-rated, most OTC standard-rated; the VAT mix is a due-diligence and finance-ready-accounts item. Citation: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/health-professionals-pharmaceutical-products-and-vat-notice-70157

## Hallucination danger zones (enforce)

- SDLT vs stamp duty on shares: assets/property attract SDLT at non-residential rates up to 5% (HP 12); share deals attract 0.5% stamp duty on shares. Do not conflate the two taxes or quote residential SDLT rates. A pharmacy is non-residential for the SDLT split.
- Goodwill CT relief on a company purchase is RESTRICTED and only in limited cases at fixed rates (HP 13). Do not imply full/automatic goodwill amortisation relief.
- The NHS contract does NOT transfer automatically on a change of ownership; the 2013 Regulations govern entry (HP 10). Do not imply "buy the shop, keep the contract" is automatic.
- Superintendent pharmacist is a company-ownership/registration requirement (HP 11), described at ownership-mechanics level only. Do NOT stray into clinical/professional-conduct content (positioning wall).
- No invented cost-of-purchase figures, multiples or lender rates. "Cost of buying a pharmacy" is answered at method/driver level (goodwill dominance, item volume, structure), never with an invented headline price.
- No credential claims, no named individuals. No em-dashes.
