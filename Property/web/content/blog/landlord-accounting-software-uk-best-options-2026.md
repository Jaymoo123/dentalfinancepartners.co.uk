<h2>What "landlord accounting software" should actually do</h2>

<p>A well-chosen package should handle the following operational areas in addition to MTD submissions:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Per-property profit and loss.</strong> Each property is a separate income source for tax and a separate management unit. The software should let you tag every transaction to a property and produce profit reports by property and across the portfolio.</li>
<li><strong>Bank feed integration.</strong> Auto-import transactions from your business bank account. Rules should auto-categorise recurring rent receipts and expense payments.</li>
<li><strong>Section 24 finance cost separation.</strong> Mortgage interest needs to be tagged separately because it does NOT reduce taxable profit for personally-held BTL, it generates the 20% basic-rate tax reducer at year end. Software that lumps interest with other expenses creates extra work for your accountant.</li>
<li><strong>Capital expenditure tracking.</strong> Improvements (extension, new kitchen with upgrade element, structural works) add to CGT base cost on eventual disposal, not to current year expense. Good software keeps a separate capital expenditure ledger per property.</li>
<li><strong>Replacement of Domestic Items Relief logging.</strong> Furniture and white goods replacements need to be flagged for RDIR. The relief is the lower of cost or like-for-like replacement value.</li>
<li><strong>Receipt capture.</strong> Mobile apps that photograph and store receipts against transactions. Helps with HMRC enquiries and MTD digital linking requirements.</li>
<li><strong>Quarterly MTD submission.</strong> HMRC-recognised quarterly submission of cumulative income and expense category totals.</li>
<li><strong>Accountant collaboration.</strong> A free accountant seat so your adviser can review and adjust without data exports.</li>
<li><strong>Tenant management (optional).</strong> Some landlords prefer separate property management tools (Arthur, Lettings Hub) for tenant communication, viewings, deposits. Others want it integrated.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The eight verified UK options for 2026</h2>

<h3>1. Hammock</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> Landlord-specific UK product. Built for individual and portfolio landlords.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes, HMRC-recognised.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Per-property profit tracking, Section 24 handling, rent collection reconciliation, mortgage interest tagging, capital allowances, accountant access.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Personal-name UK landlords with 1 to 10 properties.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Tiered by portfolio size, typically £15 to £45 a month.</p>

<h3>2. Landlord Studio</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> Landlord-specific, US-origin but UK-localised.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Bookkeeping plus property management, tenant screening, online rent collection, document storage, receipt scanning.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Self-managing landlords wanting accounting plus tenant tools in one place.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free for one unit, £12 to £35 a month for portfolios.</p>

<h3>3. FreeAgent</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> General small business accounting, popular with sole traders.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Self assessment integration, tax timeline, mobile receipt capture, bank feeds. Not landlord-specific so per-property reporting needs manual tagging.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords already using FreeAgent for other self-employed activity, or who get it free with NatWest, RBS, Mettle, or Ulster Bank business banking.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> £19 a month standard, FREE for NatWest Group business customers.</p>

<h3>4. Xero</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> General cloud accounting, widely used by limited companies and larger sole traders.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes for VAT directly; MTD for ITSA requires a bridging app (HammockHQ and others publish Xero MTD bridges).</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Full double-entry, multi-user, accountant-friendly, marketplace of property add-ons (RentSurv, RentRedi UK, etc.).</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Limited company landlords, mixed business owners, larger portfolios needing full accounting.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> £15 to £55 a month depending on tier.</p>

<h3>5. QuickBooks</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> General accounting software, multiple tiers.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Bank feeds, mileage tracking, mobile app, accountant collaboration. Property tracking via class codes or tags rather than native per-property reporting.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords running QuickBooks for other business activity, or those with mixed self-employment.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> £12 to £40 a month.</p>

<h3>6. Sage Accounting</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> General accounting, popular with limited companies.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Multi-user, full reporting suite, payroll integration, VAT handling, large accountant network familiar with Sage.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Limited company landlords with VAT-registered commercial property or holiday let operations.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> £15 to £40 a month.</p>

<h3>7. Provestor</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> Landlord-specific accountancy provider, combining software and managed service.</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Bundled accounting software plus annual tax return preparation, Section 24 handling, limited company support, dedicated client manager.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords who want software and accountant in one fixed-fee package.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Tiered by portfolio size and structure, typically £50 to £200 a month total.</p>

<h3>8. Arthur Online</h3>

<p><strong>Type:</strong> Property management software (with accounting features).</p>
<p><strong>MTD-compatible:</strong> Via integration partners; not natively HMRC-recognised on its own.</p>
<p><strong>Key features:</strong> Strong tenant management, maintenance ticketing, leasing pipeline, owner statements. Accounting features are functional but not the primary draw.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Portfolio landlords with 15+ properties needing serious tenant management. Pair with separate accounting software (Xero, FreeAgent) for MTD.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> £15 to £20 per unit per month.</p>

<h2>What to look for during the trial period</h2>

<p>Most products offer 30-day free trials. During the trial:</p>

<ul>
<li>Set up at least one property and reconcile a full month of transactions</li>
<li>Test the bank feed (does your bank connect cleanly?)</li>
<li>Try the receipt capture mobile app</li>
<li>Run a Section 24 calculation (or verify mortgage interest is correctly separated)</li>
<li>Generate a profit and loss report per property</li>
<li>Test the MTD quarterly submission flow (most products have a sandbox mode)</li>
<li>Invite your accountant to confirm they can access cleanly</li>
<li>Try logging a capital improvement (does the software keep it out of revenue expenses?)</li>
</ul>

<p>If any of those steps feel awkward, the product is not right for your portfolio. Workflow friction at trial stage compounds over years of use.</p>

<h2>Penalties for non-compliance</h2>

<p>HMRC operates a points-based penalty regime for late MTD quarterly updates. For quarterly filers, four points trigger a £200 fixed penalty, then another £200 for each further missed deadline. Points reset after 24 months of full compliance. Late payment penalties on the underlying tax are separate: 2% of unpaid tax at 15 days, another 2% at 30 days, daily interest at Bank of England base rate plus 2.5% from the original due date.</p>

<p>Software cost is trivial against penalty exposure for any landlord above the MTD threshold.</p>

<h2>Common mistakes when choosing landlord software</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>Going too cheap and missing per-property tracking.</strong> A £5-a-month spreadsheet-style tool will not give you the per-property profit reports you need for portfolio decisions.</li>
<li><strong>Going too expensive for what you actually need.</strong> A single-property landlord does not need Sage or Xero.</li>
<li><strong>Forgetting the limited company case.</strong> Hammock and Landlord Studio are personal-name landlord tools. They do not produce CT600 returns. If you incorporate, switch to Xero or QuickBooks.</li>
<li><strong>Not involving your accountant in the choice.</strong> Some accountants standardise on one product per client base. Using a non-standard tool costs you their efficiency.</li>
<li><strong>Picking software your bank does not connect to.</strong> Check the bank feed list before committing.</li>
<li><strong>Importing historical data badly.</strong> Opening balances at a clean date (1 April or company year-end) avoid mid-year reconciliation nightmares.</li>
</ul>
