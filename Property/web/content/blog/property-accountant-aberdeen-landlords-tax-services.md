<h2>Key Services Aberdeen Landlords Need</h2>

<h3>Tax Return Preparation and Compliance</h3>

<p>Aberdeen landlords need accurate property income reporting that captures all allowable expenses. This includes understanding how oil industry downturns affect void period claims and ensuring student property expenses are correctly allocated between rental and personal use.</p>

<p>With <a href="/blog/making-tax-digital-mtd/making-tax-digital-landlords-april-2026-deadline">Making Tax Digital for Income Tax</a> becoming mandatory from April 2026, quarterly digital submissions will be required for landlords with gross property income over £10,000.</p>

<h3>Section 24 Tax Relief Optimisation</h3>

<p>The <a href="/blog/section-24-and-tax-relief/section-24-tax-relief-complete-guide">Section 24 mortgage interest restriction</a> caps tax relief at 20%, regardless of your marginal tax rate. Aberdeen landlords with high mortgages need sophisticated planning to minimise the impact.</p>

<p>Specialist <strong>landlord tax advice in Aberdeen</strong> includes timing mortgage refinancing, structuring portfolio purchases, and considering incorporation where beneficial.</p>

<h3>Capital Gains Tax Planning</h3>

<p>Aberdeen's property market volatility creates both CGT planning opportunities and risks. <a href="/blog/capital-gains-tax/capital-gains-tax-property-complete-guide-uk">Capital gains tax on property</a> is charged at 18% for basic rate taxpayers and 24% for higher rate taxpayers.</p>

<p>Timing disposals around market cycles and personal income fluctuations can significantly reduce CGT liability. Aberdeen landlords may also benefit from principal private residence relief if they've lived in rental properties.</p>

<h2>Incorporation Considerations for Aberdeen Landlords</h2>

<p>Many Aberdeen landlords consider <a href="/blog/incorporation-and-company-structures/buy-to-let-limited-company-complete-guide-uk">buy-to-let limited companies</a> to mitigate Section 24's impact. However, incorporation isn't always beneficial and depends on individual circumstances.</p>

<p><strong>Benefits of incorporation include:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Full mortgage interest deductibility against rental profits</li>
<li>Corporation tax rates (19% on profits up to £250,000, 25% above)</li>
<li>Greater flexibility in profit extraction timing</li>
<li>Easier expansion financing through company borrowing</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Drawbacks include:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>3% SDLT surcharge on company property purchases (increased to 5% in October 2024)</li>
<li>Capital gains within the company structure</li>
<li>Additional compliance costs and complexity</li>
<li>Restrictions on profit extraction without tax charges</li>
</ul>
