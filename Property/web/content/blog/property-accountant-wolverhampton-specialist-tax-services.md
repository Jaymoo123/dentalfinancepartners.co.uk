<p>Under the current system, that landlord receives only basic rate (20%) tax relief on mortgage interest, regardless of their overall tax bracket. <h2>Making Tax Digital Compliance for Wolverhampton Landlords</h2>

<p>From April 2026, <strong>landlord tax advice Wolverhampton</strong> increasingly focuses on Making Tax Digital (MTD) compliance. Any landlord with gross property income over £10,000 must keep digital records and submit quarterly updates to HMRC.</p>

<p>For Wolverhampton landlords, this typically means:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Software implementation:</strong> Choosing MTD-compatible accounting software that handles property-specific transactions</li>
<li><strong>Quarterly reporting:</strong> Submitting income and expenses data every three months, not just annually</li>
<li><strong>Digital record-keeping:</strong> Maintaining digital receipts and invoices for all property-related expenses</li>
<li><strong>Annual reconciliation:</strong> Completing a final annual submission with any adjustments</li>
</ul>
