<h2>Navigating Section 24 Tax Relief Restrictions</h2>
<p>Section 24 restrictions hit Swindon landlords particularly hard due to the area's higher mortgage-to-value ratios on buy-to-let properties. With Swindon's average buy-to-let property price around £280,000 and typical mortgage rates requiring significant interest payments, the restriction of mortgage interest relief to basic rate only creates substantial additional tax liabilities.</p>
<p>For example, a Swindon landlord with three properties generating £45,000 annual rental income and £18,000 in mortgage interest now faces:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pre-Section 24:</strong> Tax on £27,000 net profit (£45,000 - £18,000)</li>
<li><strong>Post-Section 24:</strong> Tax on full £45,000 income, with only 20% credit on interest</li>
<li><strong>Additional tax cost:</strong> Potentially £3,600+ annually for higher-rate taxpayers</li>
