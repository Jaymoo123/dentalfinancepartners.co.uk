<h2>Bournemouth Property Market: Unique Tax Considerations</h2>

<p>Bournemouth's property landscape creates specific tax scenarios that general accountants often miss. The town's mix of traditional buy-to-let properties, student accommodation, and holiday lets (following the abolition of Furnished Holiday Lettings relief in April 2025) requires nuanced tax planning.</p>

<p>Properties near Bournemouth University typically achieve higher rental yields but involve different expense patterns and tenant turnover rates. Meanwhile, seafront properties command premium rents but may have higher maintenance costs due to coastal conditions. A specialist <strong>buy to let accountant in Bournemouth</strong> understands these local variations and their tax implications.</p>

<h3>Student Property Considerations</h3>

<p>Bournemouth's large student population creates opportunities for HMO investments and purpose-built student accommodation. These properties often involve:</p>

<ul>
<li>Higher licensing and compliance costs that qualify as deductible expenses</li>
<li>Different council tax arrangements (students are exempt)</li>
<li>Potential for higher wear and tear requiring careful depreciation planning</li>

<h2>Section 24 Impact on Bournemouth Landlords</h2>

<p>With mortgage interest relief now fully restricted to basic rate tax relief, many Bournemouth landlords are reassessing their investment strategies. Properties purchased during the coastal property boom of recent years often carry substantial mortgages, making Section 24 particularly painful.</p>

<p>A typical Bournemouth landlord with a £250,000 mortgage at 5.5% interest faces annual interest costs of £13,750. Under the old system, a higher-rate taxpayer could deduct this fully against rental income. Now, they receive only 20% tax relief (£2,750), creating a significant tax burden increase.</p>

<p><strong>Landlord tax advice in Bournemouth</strong> increasingly focuses on <a href="/incorporation">incorporation strategies</a>, portfolio restructuring, and alternative financing arrangements to mitigate Section 24's impact. Local specialists understand which Bournemouth properties might benefit from incorporation and which should remain in personal ownership.</p>

<h3>Incorporation Considerations for Coastal Properties</h3>

<p>Bournemouth's property values mean that incorporation decisions often involve substantial Stamp Duty Land Tax implications. With the 5% surcharge on additional properties (increased from 3% in October 2024), transferring a £400,000 property to a company involves £20,000 in SDLT plus the underlying transaction costs.</p>

<p>However, the <a href="/blog/incorporation-and-company-structures/buy-to-let-limited-company-complete-guide-uk">buy-to-let limited company structure</a> can provide significant tax advantages for larger portfolios, particularly with the upcoming separate property income tax rates from April 2027 (22% basic, 42% higher, 47% additional rate).</p>

<h2>Making Tax Digital: Bournemouth Landlords' Deadline Approaching</h2>

<p>From 6 April 2026, landlords with gross property income over £10,000 must comply with Making Tax Digital for Income Tax. For many Bournemouth landlords, particularly those with multiple properties or higher rental yields, this threshold is easily exceeded.</p>

<p>MTD compliance requires:</p>

<ul>
<li>Digital record-keeping using approved software</li>
<li>Quarterly submissions to HMRC</li>
<li>Annual summary submission by 31 January following the tax year</li>
<li>Potential penalties for non-compliance from day one</li>
</ul>

<p>A specialist property accountant ensures systems are in place well before the <a href="/blog/making-tax-digital-mtd/making-tax-digital-landlords-april-2026-deadline">April 2026 deadline</a>, avoiding last-minute implementation issues that could trigger penalties.</p>

<h2>Capital Gains Tax Planning for Bournemouth Properties</h2>

<p>Bournemouth's property appreciation over recent years means many landlords face substantial capital gains tax liabilities when selling. With CGT rates of 18% for basic rate taxpayers and 24% for higher rate taxpayers, plus the reduced annual exempt amount of £3,000, tax planning is crucial.</p>

<p>Specialist property accountants help Bournemouth landlords with:</p>

<ul>
<li>Principal Private Residence relief claims for properties that were once main residences</li>
<li>Timing disposals to manage tax rates and utilize annual exemptions</li>
<li>Incorporation relief strategies for portfolio transfers</li>
<li>Renovation and improvement cost claims to reduce taxable gains</li>
</ul>

<p>A £500,000 property purchased for £300,000 creates a £200,000 gain. After the £3,000 annual exempt amount, a higher-rate taxpayer faces £47,280 in CGT. Proper planning might reduce this significantly through legitimate reliefs and timing strategies.</p>
