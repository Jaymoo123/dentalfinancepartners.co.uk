<p>Whether you own a single buy-to-let property or manage a substantial portfolio across Northamptonshire, working with a specialist who understands both UK property tax law and the local market dynamics can help you navigate these challenges effectively.</p>

<p>Northampton's property market offers distinct opportunities and challenges that require local knowledge. The town's position as a major logistics hub, with excellent transport links to London, Birmingham, and the Midlands, has created strong rental demand from professionals and transport workers.</p>

<ul>

<h2>Key Tax Challenges Facing Northampton Landlords</h2>

<h3>Section 24 Impact on Local Investors</h3>

<p>Section 24 restrictions have particularly affected landlords in Northampton's mid-range property market. With average property prices between £150,000-£300,000, many investors rely on mortgage financing where interest costs previously provided substantial tax relief.</p>

<p>For example, a landlord with a £200,000 Northampton property earning £1,200 monthly rent and paying £600 mortgage interest now faces tax on the full £14,400 annual rental income, with only a 20% tax credit on the £7,200 interest. This can increase their effective tax rate significantly, especially for higher-rate taxpayers.</p>

<p>A specialist property accountant can help you understand these calculations and explore alternatives like <a href="/incorporation">incorporation strategies</a> that might reduce the impact.</p>

<h3>Making Tax Digital Requirements from April 2026</h3>

<p>Landlords with gross property income over £10,000 must comply with <a href="/blog/making-tax-digital-mtd/making-tax-digital-landlords-april-2026-deadline">Making Tax Digital requirements</a> from 6 April 2026. This affects most Northampton buy-to-let investors and requires:</p>

<ul>
<li>Quarterly digital submissions to HMRC</li>
<li>Compatible record-keeping software</li>
<li>Digital links between records and submissions</li>
</ul>

<p>Local landlords managing properties across different Northamptonshire postcodes need systems that can track income and expenses by property while meeting these digital requirements.</p>
