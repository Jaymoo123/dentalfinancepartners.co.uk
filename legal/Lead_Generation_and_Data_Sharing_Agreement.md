## BACKGROUND

(A) The Supplier owns and operates the website at propertytaxpartners.co.uk and related digital properties under the brand "Property Tax Partners" (the **"Site"**), which attracts inbound enquiries from landlords, property investors and other persons seeking property-tax advice.

(B) Persons visiting the Site may submit an enquiry through an enquiry form, providing their contact details and their question, so that a specialist firm may respond to their enquiry (each such enquiry, a **"Lead"**).

(C) The Supplier wishes to make the exclusive output of qualifying Leads from the Site available to a single specialist firm, and the Client, a firm of accountants and tax advisers, wishes to receive that exclusive Lead flow, on the terms of this Agreement.

(D) The Supplier collects the Lead data as a controller. On delivery of a Lead to the Client, the Client becomes a controller of that data in its own right, and decides independently whether and how to respond to and advise each enquirer. The parties therefore share personal data as independent controllers, each determining its own purposes and means of processing. This Agreement records a controller-to-controller data sharing arrangement; it is not a controller-to-processor arrangement and neither party acts as the other's processor.

<!-- LAW: Recital (D) fixes the relationship as INDEPENDENT controller-to-controller (UK GDPR Art 4(7); ICO Data Sharing Code under s.121 DPA 2018), and now recites the factual basis for independence (each party determines its own purposes; the Client decides whether/how to advise) to insulate against a residual joint-controller argument. (E) The Supplier does not guarantee that any Lead will respond, engage or become a client of the Client. The Supplier warrants the exclusive flow of Leads meeting the agreed definition, and nothing more, as set out in this Agreement.
