# Lead Generation and Data Sharing Agreement - Plain-English Summary

*One-page summary of the key terms. This is a plain-English guide only; the wording of the Agreement itself governs. Not legal advice.*

**Who**
- **Supplier (you):** **Ashfield Trading Limited** (company no. 16358723), trading as **Property Tax Partners** (propertytaxpartners.co.uk).
- **Client:** **DJH Business Advisers Limited** (company no. 03451690), the accountancy firm that receives the leads.

**What the deal is**
- You own a property-tax website that attracts enquiries from landlords and investors. You forward those enquiries ("Leads") to DJH, who advises the enquirer directly.
- DJH gets the **exclusive** flow of qualifying Leads from your site. No one else gets them, as long as DJH keeps paying. You can run other businesses, but not a confusingly-similar property-tax site feeding a competing firm.

**The money**
- **£85 per qualifying lead**, flat (you decided to drop the lower tier, so leads without a message are charged the full £85 too).
- Billable the moment it is delivered. You are **not** paid on results: it does not matter whether the enquirer replies, is contactable, or becomes a client.
- **VAT:** none added (you are not currently VAT-registered); if that changes, VAT is added on top.

**What counts as a billable (qualifying) Lead**
- A genuine property-tax enquiry submitted through your site, with a name plus a working email and/or phone (a message is **not** required), delivered to DJH's designated inbox, with the lawful basis for sharing in place.

**Rejections and credits (the main ways a Lead is not paid for)**
- Within **3 working days**, DJH can flag a Lead as (a) spam/bot, (b) a duplicate of one sent in the prior 30 days, (c) clearly not about property tax, or (d) contact details that don't work (email bounces, phone dead), with brief evidence.
- Valid rejections are credited against the next invoice. DJH can't *unilaterally* withhold payment for any other reason, but it is **not barred from raising a fair concern** with you (your call whether to agree a credit).
- **No-message leads, fairness review (clause 5.8):** a lead with no written message is still billable at £85, but if DJH thinks the fee isn't fair value for it, it can raise that within 5 working days and **you both discuss a reasonable fee or credit in good faith**. The fee stays payable while you discuss (you can choose to defer it instead if you'd rather). This is the "let's be fair about no-message leads" mechanism you asked for.
- DJH can audit your delivery log (at most twice a year, at its cost unless it finds a material error).

**Billing and payment**
- Invoiced **monthly** in arrears: one invoice at the start of each month for the previous month's delivered Leads.
- **You bill against your own log.** You keep the Delivery Log (your record of every lead sent) and bill from it. With each invoice you send DJH an **itemised "Lead Statement"** listing the leads being billed (this is "I'll send them my list on the 1st").
- **DJH keeps its own log and checks yours against it.** DJH has 10 working days to flag any mismatch (a lead it never got, or one counted twice); you reconcile in good faith. If they don't flag anything, your Delivery Log is the agreed record for that month.
- Payment due within **14 days** of the invoice (as you proposed).
- Late payment carries **8% interest over the Bank of England base rate** plus statutory fixed compensation and recovery costs (Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998).

**If DJH doesn't pay**
- After notice and a 10-working-day grace, you can **suspend** deliveries and/or exclusivity, with no liability, and place the Lead flow elsewhere until you are paid. Suspension is not a breach by you.

**Data protection (what DJH's compliance team will focus on)**
- You and DJH are **independent data controllers** sharing data controller-to-controller (not a processor arrangement).
- **Clean hand-off (clause 8.5):** once a Lead lands in DJH's designated inbox, DJH is responsible as controller for storing, securing and handling that data, and you are not liable for what DJH does with it afterwards. (You stay responsible only for your own conduct up to the point of delivery, e.g. having a valid basis to share.)
- **Legal basis - decided for you: "legitimate interests"** (the person asked to be put in touch with a specialist, so passing their details to DJH is allowed). You don't decide this; your solicitor confirms it. **One small form change:** because the basis is legitimate interests (not consent), your form should show this as a short *acknowledgement* ("by submitting, your details will be shared with DJH...") rather than a tick-the-box-to-agree. It's a one-line change; Annex B.1 has the exact wording. (If you'd rather keep the tick-box, we just call the basis "consent" instead - simpler, slightly more exposed. Either is fine.) Either way you flip the partner config before go-live and add DJH's privacy-policy link (https://www.djh.co.uk/privacy-policy/).
- **DJH's own privacy notice is DJH's job, not yours.** You can't (and don't need to) edit their policy. The contract makes DJH responsible for telling enquirers it received their details and that DJH Business Advisers is the controller. Your only part is the form wording above.
- **Newsletter caution:** your email-only **newsletter** sign-ups only agreed to *your* updates, **not** to be shared with DJH, so they must not be sent to DJH as leads.
- Both sides keep their own UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 (as amended by the **Data (Use and Access) Act 2025**) obligations: privacy notices, security, data-subject rights, retention, breach notification, the new complaints duty, and ICO registration.
- **Retention:** you delete or anonymise your copy of each lead's details (name/phone/email/message) within **3 months** of sending it to DJH; you keep only the minimal log + consent evidence longer (for billing and the audit window). DJH holds the data after that as controller. A retention limit is legally required, but for you it's light - a manual quarterly clear-out is enough.
- DJH can answer the enquiry (that is not "marketing"); separate consent is needed before marketing anything else.

**Who is liable for what**
- Each side is responsible for its **own** data breaches. You are not liable for what DJH does with the data after delivery.
- Liability is **capped** (mutual cap: greater of a £floor or 12 months' fees), with a higher, separate cap for data-protection claims, backed by insurance. A mutual cap is both fairer and safer for you (a one-sided cap is more likely to be struck down, which would leave you uncapped).
- Indemnities are **mutual, fault-based and capped**. There are no uncapped indemnities. The data-protection indemnities cover a fine imposed on the *other* party because of *your* breach (not your own fines). You cannot exclude liability for fraud or death/personal injury, and neither side can contract out of its GDPR duties to individuals or the regulator.

**Your protections built in**
- You keep all **ownership**: the site, brand, content, systems and the lead database.
- Exclusivity is **conditional on payment**; on default or after the deal ends, you can place the Leads elsewhere.
- A **non-circumvention** clause stops DJH dodging the £85 fee by re-routing your enquirers.
- No guarantees of volume, conversion or revenue.
- Short payment terms, statutory interest, and suspension rights.

**Term and exit**
- Rolling, no minimum term. Either side can end it on **5 days'** notice (as you asked - note it lets DJH leave on 5 days too). You can suspend or terminate immediately for non-payment, and either side can terminate for serious breach or insolvency.

**Governing law:** England and Wales.
