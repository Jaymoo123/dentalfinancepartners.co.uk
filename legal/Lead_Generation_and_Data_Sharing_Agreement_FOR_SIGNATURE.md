## BACKGROUND

(A) The Supplier operates the website at propertytaxpartners.co.uk and related digital properties under the brand "Property Tax Partners" (the **"Site"**), and wishes to supply the exclusive flow of qualifying enquiries (**"Leads"**) from the Site to the Client, a firm of accountants and tax advisers, which wishes to receive them on these terms.

(B) The Supplier collects each Lead as a controller and, on Delivery, the Client becomes a controller in its own right and decides independently whether and how to respond to and advise the enquirer. The parties share the Lead Data as independent controllers. This is a controller-to-controller data sharing arrangement, not a processor arrangement, and neither party acts as the other's processor.

(C) The Supplier does not guarantee that any Lead will respond, engage or become a client. It warrants the exclusive flow of Leads meeting the agreed definition, and nothing more.

## 4. EXCLUSIVITY

4.1 During the Term the Supplier will Deliver each Qualifying Lead generated by the Site to the Client only, and will not sell, license or make the same Leads available to anyone else.

4.2 Exclusivity relates to the Site's output. It does not prevent the Supplier owning or operating any other website, brand or business (including other lead-generation sites), except that during the Term the Supplier will not operate another property-tax enquiry website under a brand confusingly similar to "Property Tax Partners" whose leads are supplied to a firm competing with the Client.

4.3 Exclusivity is conditional on payment. If any undisputed sum is overdue, the Supplier may suspend Exclusivity and/or Delivery under clause 7 and, while the suspension continues, supply Qualifying Leads to any other person without breach of this Agreement and without liability to the Client.

4.4 On expiry or termination, Exclusivity ends immediately and the Supplier is free to supply the Site's Lead flow to anyone.

## 5. QUALIFYING LEADS AND CREDITS

5.1 A Lead is a **Qualifying Lead** if, at the time of Delivery, it: (a) is a genuine property-tax enquiry; (b) was submitted or captured through the Site by the enquirer; (c) carries the enquirer's name together with an email address and/or telephone number in valid format (a written message is not required); and (d) is Delivered to the Nominated Address with the lawful basis for sharing required by Schedule 2. Whether the contact details prove functional is not a condition of qualification; non-functional details give rise only to the credit in clause 5.4(d).

5.2 **A Qualifying Lead is billable on Delivery.** The Lead Fee is earned and payable on Delivery and is not contingent on whether the enquirer responds, is contactable, engages, attends a meeting, accepts a proposal, becomes a client, or generates revenue.

5.3 The Supplier may apply filtering to reduce obvious spam and out-of-scope submissions, but does not verify the truth, accuracy or completeness of what an enquirer provides.

5.4 Within **3 Working Days** of Delivery, the Client may flag a Lead as a **Rejected Lead** on these grounds only: (a) it is spam or bot-generated; (b) it duplicates a Lead Delivered in the previous 30 days (same enquirer, substantially the same enquiry); (c) it is clearly outside property tax; or (d) the contact details are non-functional, in that the email hard-bounces and/or the telephone number is unobtainable or invalid, so the enquirer cannot be contacted by any supplied channel.

5.5 A flag must be made in writing to the Supplier's contact in Schedule 1, identify the Lead by its Delivery Log reference, state the ground relied on and include brief supporting evidence. A late, unsupported or otherwise-grounded flag is not valid.

5.6 The Supplier will review each valid flag reasonably and in good faith. If the Supplier accepts a flag (or does not respond within 5 Working Days of a valid, evidenced flag, in which case it is treated as accepted), the Lead Fee is credited against the next invoice (or, if none is due, refunded within 30 days). On a final invoice, where the Client has validly flagged a Lead not yet reviewed, the Client may withhold that Lead Fee pending review, any amount then found payable being due within 5 Working Days.

5.7 Except as provided in this clause 5, the Client has **no right to unilaterally withhold, set off or reduce** payment of the Lead Fee for any Delivered Qualifying Lead, and no ground other than clause 5.4 entitles it to a credit. The Client may still raise a genuine, good-faith query under clause 5.8 or clause 7.3. Even so, each Lead Fee stays payable on its due date while a query is being discussed. The only exception is an amount that is validly disputed under clause 7.3.

5.8 **No-message Leads.** A Lead Delivered without an enquiry message is still a Qualifying Lead billable under clause 5.2. However, if the Client considers in good faith that the Lead Fee is not fair value for a particular no-message Lead (or an identified group), it may raise this in writing within 5 Working Days of Delivery, identifying the Leads, and the parties will discuss in good faith and acting reasonably whether a reduced fee or a credit is appropriate. Any reduction takes effect only as agreed in writing; pending agreement the Lead Fee remains payable.

# SCHEDULE 2 - DATA SHARING AND DATA PROTECTION

This Schedule is an independent controller-to-controller data sharing arrangement, aligned with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 (as amended by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025) and the Commissioner's data sharing guidance.

**1. Status and purpose.** The parties share the Lead Data as independent Controllers, each responsible for its own compliance; the Client decides independently whether and how to respond to and advise each enquirer; neither is the other's Processor and this is not an Article 28 contract. The **"Permitted Purpose"** is enabling the Client, as a specialist firm, to receive and respond to the enquirer's property-tax enquiry and provide the requested information or advice. Each party processes the Lead Data only for the Permitted Purpose or a compatible purpose. All processing takes place in the United Kingdom; neither party will make a restricted transfer outside the UK without a valid mechanism under Articles 44 to 49. Neither party will take a decision based solely on automated processing that has legal or similarly significant effects on an enquirer, except in compliance with the UK GDPR. The Client is part of the DJH Group and may, as receiving Controller, share the Lead Data within the DJH Group for the Permitted Purpose; any such onward sharing is the Client's own responsibility under paragraph 11 and clauses 8.5 and 8.6, and does not make the Supplier responsible for it.

**2. Roles and registration.** The Supplier is the disclosing Controller; the Client is the receiving Controller (from Delivery). Each is, or will be before the first Delivery, registered with the Commissioner and has paid or will pay the data protection fee (to the extent required), and will maintain that registration for the Term. Contacts are in Schedule 1, paragraph 15; each party's data protection contact there is its single point of contact for matters under this Schedule (including breach notification under paragraph 9, data subject rights under paragraph 10 and cooperation on complaints), and each party will keep that contact current and notify the other in writing of any change.
